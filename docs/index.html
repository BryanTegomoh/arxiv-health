This paper argues for a robust safety evaluation framework for Large Language Models (LLMs) used in safety-critical roles, particularly when LLMs act as judges (LaJ). Authors: Hannah Smith, Abhirup Banerjee, Leto Luana Riebel et al.
This AI-enabled modeling and simulation study quantifies how torso-heart anatomical factors contribute to sex-differences in ECG biomarkers following myocardial infarction. It found that female anatomies, characterized by smaller ventricles, a more superior cardiac position, and different ventricular orientation, lead to reduced ST-elevation, shorter QRS duration, and shortened T-peak-to-end interval due to increased distances and altered angles between infarcts and ECG electrodes.
