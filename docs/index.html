FlowLet: Conditional 3D Brain MRI Synthesis using Wavelet Flow Matching

üìÖ 2026-01-08 ‚≠ê 0.95 üìÇ cs.CV
Authors: Danilo Danese, Angela Lombardi, Matteo Attimonelli et al.
FlowLet addresses the scarcity and demographic imbalance of 3D brain MRI datasets crucial for Brain Age Prediction (BAP) by proposing a novel conditional generative framework. It synthesizes age-conditioned 3D MRIs efficiently using flow matching within an invertible 3D wavelet domain, thereby avoiding latent compression artifacts and reducing computational demands. This approach generates high-fidelity volumes, significantly improving BAP performance for underrepresented age groups while preserving anatomical accuracy.

üìÖ 2026-01-08 ‚≠ê 0.90 üìÇ stat.ME
Authors: Baolin Chen, Mengfei Ran
This paper introduces Adaptive Joint Learning (AJL), a novel regularization framework designed to address the limitations of standard functional data analysis in capturing abrupt structural shifts and complex time-varying associations within high-dimensional longitudinal biomedical data. AJL simultaneously performs functional variable selection and structural changepoint detection in multivariate time-varying coefficient models. The method offers strong theoretical guarantees, including the oracle property in ultra-high-dimensional settings, and effectively identifies synchronized disease progression phases and prognostic markers in Primary Biliary Cirrhosis (PBC) data.
