Feasibility of Radio Frequency Based Wireless Sensing of Lead Contamination in Soil

üìÖ 2025-12-18 ‚≠ê 0.90 üìÇ cs.ET
Authors: Yixuan Gao, Tanvir Ahmed, Mikhail Mohammed et al.
This paper introduces SoilScanner, a novel radio frequency (RF)-based wireless system designed for the rapid detection of lead (Pb) contamination in soil, addressing the limitations of current labor-intensive and costly methods. The system leverages the discovery that different salts, including lead compounds, distinctively affect radio signal propagation patterns across various frequencies. SoilScanner demonstrated 72% accuracy in classifying soil samples into low-Pb and high-Pb categories (with a 200 ppm threshold), crucially avoiding misclassification for any samples exceeding 500 ppm Pb, thereby proving the feasibility of developing portable and affordable Pb detection devices.
