üìÖ 2025-12-30 ‚≠ê 0.95 üìÇ cs.AI
Authors: Ioanna Gemou, Evangelos Lamprou
This paper introduces McCoy, a novel proof-of-concept framework that addresses the challenge of creating high-quality knowledge bases for symbolic AI in healthcare by integrating Large Language Models (LLMs) with Answer Set Programming (ASP). McCoy leverages LLMs to translate medical literature into ASP code, combines it with patient data, and uses an ASP solver to produce robust and interpretable disease diagnoses. Preliminary results indicate strong performance on small-scale diagnostic tasks, demonstrating the potential for explainable AI in medicine.

üìÖ 2025-12-30 ‚≠ê 0.95 üìÇ cs.CV
Authors: Yipei Wang, Qianye Yang, Lior Drukker et al.
This paper introduces a novel bi-level optimization framework for automated and objective fetal ultrasound skill assessment, addressing the subjectivity and time-intensity of traditional methods. It assesses skills by quantifying how well a clinical task is performed on acquired ultrasound images, utilizing jointly optimized clinical task and skill predictor networks without relying on manually predefined skill ratings. Validation on real-world fetal head scan videos demonstrates the framework's feasibility in predicting and indicating ultrasound skills through optimized task performance.

Authors: Amal Alqahtani, Efsun Kayi, Mona Diab
StressRoBERTa is a novel cross-condition transfer learning approach for the automatic detection of self-reported chronic stress in English tweets. By continually training a RoBERTa model on data from conditions highly comorbid with stress (depression, anxiety, PTSD), it achieved an 82% F1-score, outperforming the best prior system by 3 percentage points and demonstrating superior performance compared to general mental health models.
