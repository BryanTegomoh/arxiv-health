Saddle-to-Saddle Dynamics Explains A Simplicity Bias Across Neural Network Architectures

üìÖ 2025-12-23 ‚≠ê 0.70 üìÇ cs.LG
Authors: Yedi Zhang, Andrew Saxe, Peter E. Latham
This paper introduces a unifying theoretical framework, based on 'saddle-to-saddle dynamics,' to explain the widely observed phenomenon of 'simplicity bias' in neural networks, where models trained with gradient descent learn increasingly complex solutions over time. It demonstrates how this dynamic leads to an incremental increase in complexity metrics like rank, kinks, convolutional kernels, or attention heads across diverse architectures, including linear, ReLU, convolutional, and attention networks. The research also elucidates how data distribution and weight initialization influence the learning process, specifically affecting the duration and number of learning plateaus.

ScoreMatchingRiesz: Auto-DML with Infinitesimal Classification

üìÖ 2025-12-23 ‚≠ê 0.90 üìÇ econ.EM
Authors: Masahiro Kato
This study proposes ScoreMatchingRiesz, a novel method for Riesz representer estimation based on score matching, extending techniques from direct density ratio estimation (DRE) and diffusion models. The primary goal is to address the issue of overfitting commonly encountered with flexible models in Riesz representer estimation, which is crucial for constructing $\sqrt{n}$-consistent and efficient estimators in debiased machine learning (DML) for causal inference and structural parameter estimation. The proposed method not only mitigates overfitting but also offers new insights into causal inference by bridging marginal effects and average policy effects through time score functions.

Authors: Chehak Malhotra, Mehak Gopal, Akshaya Devadiga et al.
This study benchmarks the performance of Large Language Models (LLMs) like GatorTron-Base, Llama 8B, and Mistral 7B against Smaller Language Models (SLMs) such as BioBERT and Word2Vec for predicting shock in critically ill ICU patients using text data from the MIMIC III database. The findings indicate that while GatorTron-Base achieved the highest weighted recall, the overall predictive performance metrics were comparable between LLMs and SLMs, suggesting that LLMs are not inherently superior for predicting future clinical events in this context.
