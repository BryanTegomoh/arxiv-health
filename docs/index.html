Authors: Mohammad Rubayet Rahman, Chanaka Kottegoda, Lucas M. Stolerman
This paper introduces a game-theoretic model to analyze how spontaneous household decisions regarding breeding site control influence mosquito population dynamics. It reveals a feedback loop where perceived risks drive household control actions, which in turn regulate the mosquito carrying capacity. A key finding is that when perceived risk depends on mosquito prevalence, the system can exhibit sustained oscillations in both mosquito abundance and household engagement, arising intrinsically from their dynamic interaction via a Hopf bifurcation.

This paper introduces FlowLet, a novel conditional generative framework designed to synthesize age-conditioned 3D Brain MRIs. An interpretable data-driven approach to optimizing clinical fall risk assessment

üìÖ 2026-01-08 ‚≠ê 1.00 üìÇ cs.LG
Authors: Fardin Ganjkhanloo, Emmett Springer, Erik H. Hoyer et al.
This study developed an interpretable data-driven approach, Constrained Score Optimization (CSO), to recalibrate the Johns Hopkins Fall Risk Assessment Tool (JHFRAT) for improved fall risk prediction. By reweighting JHFRAT items while preserving its structure and clinical thresholds, the CSO model significantly outperformed the original tool, demonstrating improved predictive performance (AUC-ROC 0.91 vs. 0.86). This enhancement translates to protecting an additional 35 high-risk patients weekly across the health system and provides a robust, interpretable solution for optimizing inpatient fall prevention protocols.

üìÖ 2026-01-08 ‚≠ê 1.00 üìÇ cs.CV
Authors: Qiu Guan, Zhiqiang Yang, Dezhang Ye et al.
This paper introduces DB-MSMUNet, a novel dual-branch multi-scale Mamba UNet architecture, for accurate segmentation of the pancreas and its lesions in CT scans. The model effectively addresses challenges like low contrast and blurry boundaries by integrating multi-scale state space modeling, deformable convolutions, a dual-decoder with edge and area enhancement paths, and auxiliary deep supervision. Experiments on three datasets demonstrate that DB-MSMUNet achieves state-of-the-art Dice Similarity Coefficients, enhancing segmentation accuracy, edge preservation, and robustness for pancreatic cancer diagnosis and treatment.
