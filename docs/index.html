Covariance-Driven Regression Trees: Reducing Overfitting in CART

üìÖ 2026-01-12 ‚≠ê 0.85 üìÇ stat.ML
Authors: Likun Zhang, Wei Ma
This paper introduces Covariance-Driven Regression Trees (CovRT) as a novel approach to mitigate overfitting in decision trees like CART, particularly in deep trees or with small sample sizes. By using a covariance-driven splitting criterion instead of empirical risk minimization, CovRT achieves more balanced and stable splits and identifies covariates with true signals more effectively. The method demonstrates superior prediction accuracy compared to CART in both theoretical proofs and empirical validations, enhancing model robustness and interpretability.

üìÖ 2026-01-12 ‚≠ê 0.70 üìÇ cs.LG
Authors: Ijun Jang, Jewon Yeom, Juan Yeo et al.
This paper introduces Veto, a novel objective-level reformulation for stable on-policy knowledge distillation (KD) that addresses the training instability and distributional gaps inherent in current methods. Veto constructs a geometric bridge in the logit space using an intermediate target distribution, effectively stabilizing optimization by adaptively suppressing harmful gradients and balancing output diversity with performance. Experiments show Veto consistently outperforms supervised fine-tuning and other on-policy baselines across various reasoning and generation tasks.

Authors: Sophie Sigfstead, River Jiang, Brianna Davies et al.
This paper developed and validated a novel lead-aware deep learning framework, Lead-Aware Spatial Attention Networks (LASAN), for the automated multi-class classification of inherited arrhythmia syndromes (Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy (ARVC), Long QT Syndrome (LQTS), and control) from ECGs.
