Authors: Jonathan Simkin, Lovedeep Gondara, Zeeshan Rizvi et al.

Modeling Epidemic Dynamics of Mutant Strains with Evolutionary Game-based Vaccination Behavior

üìÖ 2026-01-02 ‚≠ê 1.00 üìÇ q-bio.PE
Authors: Wenjie Zhang, Yusheng Li, Qin Li et al.
This paper introduces an extended SIRS model that distinguishes between original and mutant strain infections and includes a vaccinated compartment. It integrates evolutionary game theory to model individual vaccination decisions influenced by social interactions and the epidemiological situation, coupling these dynamics via the Microscopic Markov Chain Approach (MMCA). The framework effectively mitigates outbreaks and highlights vaccine cost, efficacy, and perceived side effect risk as critical factors influencing vaccination uptake, providing theoretical support for public health policy design.

Detecting Performance Degradation under Data Shift in Pathology Vision-Language Model

üìÖ 2026-01-02 ‚≠ê 1.00 üìÇ cs.CV
Authors: Hao Guan, Li Zhou
This paper addresses the critical issue of performance degradation in pathology Vision-Language Models (VLMs) after deployment, which often occurs due to data distribution shifts and is challenging to detect without labeled data. The authors propose a novel framework that combines input-level data shift detection using a developed toolbox (DomainSAT) with a label-free, confidence-based output indicator. Their findings demonstrate that this combined approach provides a more reliable and interpretable method for identifying actual performance degradation in VLMs, enhancing clinical reliability.
