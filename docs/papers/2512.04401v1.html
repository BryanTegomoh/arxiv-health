Learnt Microwave Image Reconstruction with A Conformal Antenna Array

arXiv ID: 2512.04401v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Wenyi Shao, Beibei Zhou
Categories: physics.med-ph
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a deep learning model for reconstructing 2D dielectric breast images from time-domain signals, distinguishing itself by integrating a conformal antenna array. Unlike fixed array systems, this approach incorporates antenna positioning into the processing pipeline, allowing the array to adapt to various breast sizes for optimal data collection. Numerical results indicate that this method, by enabling the neural network to focus on a pre-estimated breast surface within the region of interest, can reconstruct breast images with good quality.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly advances microwave breast imaging technology, potentially offering a safer, more comfortable, and highly personalized method for breast cancer screening and diagnosis. By adapting to individual patient anatomies, it can improve imaging accuracy and reduce artifacts, leading to more reliable detection.

AI Health Application

The paper proposes a deep learning model for medical image reconstruction, specifically for 2D dielectric breast images. This is a direct application of AI to improve the quality and adaptiveness of medical diagnostic imaging techniques for patient care.

Key Points

  • A deep learning model is proposed for 2D dielectric breast image reconstruction utilizing time-domain signals.
  • The system employs a novel conformal antenna array that dynamically adapts to different breast sizes, a departure from traditional fixed antenna arrays.
  • Antenna positioning data is integrated directly into the image reconstruction pipeline, enabling this adaptivity.
  • This adaptive approach aims to optimize signal collection and eliminate signal attenuation issues often encountered with coupling liquids used in fixed array setups.
  • Leveraging antenna positions allows for pre-estimation of the breast surface, which helps the neural network to concentrate its reconstruction efforts on the specific region of interest.
  • Numerical simulations demonstrate the model's capability to reconstruct breast images with good quality.

Methodology

The methodology involves a deep learning model trained to reconstruct 2D dielectric breast images from time-domain microwave signals. A key innovation is the integration of real-time or pre-determined antenna positioning data to configure a conformal antenna array. This positioning information is then used to pre-estimate the breast surface, guiding the neural network to focus its reconstruction efforts on the region of interest. The model's performance was evaluated through numerical simulations.

Key Findings

The primary finding is that the proposed deep learning model, when combined with an integrated conformal antenna array and leveraging antenna position information, can achieve good quality 2D dielectric breast image reconstruction based on numerical results. This approach successfully addresses limitations of fixed arrays by adapting to varying breast anatomies and minimizing signal attenuation from coupling liquids.

Clinical Impact

This technology holds the potential to significantly improve the clinical utility of microwave breast imaging. By offering patient-specific antenna configurations, it could lead to more accurate and reliable detection of breast abnormalities, particularly in diverse patient populations. It may also enhance patient comfort by potentially eliminating the need for uncomfortable coupling liquids and could serve as a valuable supplementary or alternative screening tool for breast cancer.

Limitations

The abstract indicates that the results are based on "numerical results," implying a lack of experimental or in vivo validation. Furthermore, the abstract does not provide specific quantitative metrics for what constitutes "good quality" image reconstruction, nor does it offer a comparative analysis against established imaging modalities or other deep learning approaches.

Abstract

A deep learning model is proposed for reconstructing 2D dielectric breast images from time-domain signals. Unlike existing learning models that employ a fixed antenna array, where input data consists solely of measurements, the proposed system integrates antenna positioning into the processing pipeline. This allows for a conformal antenna array that adapts to different breast sizes for optimal data collection across various patients, which eliminates undesired signal attenuation in coupling liquid when implemented for the fixed array. By leveraging antenna positions, the breast surface can be pre-estimated, enabling the neural network to focus on image reconstruction within the region of interest. Numerical results demonstrate that the proposed model may reconstruct breast images with good quality.
