Dynamic Weight Adjustment for Knowledge Distillation: Leveraging Vision Transformer for High-Accuracy Lung Cancer Detection and Real-Time Deployment

arXiv ID: 2510.20438v1
Published: 2025-10-23
Authors: Saif Ur Rehman Khan, Muhammad Nabeel Asim, Sebastian Vollmer, Andreas Dengel
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00

Summary

This paper introduces FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet, a novel model for high-accuracy lung cancer (LC) detection that utilizes dynamic fuzzy logic-driven knowledge distillation (KD) to adaptively adjust distillation weights, focusing on high-confidence regions in medical images. By employing a Vision Transformer (ViT-B32) as the instructor and MobileNet as the student, coupled with advanced image fusion and genetic algorithm-based optimization, the model achieves robust performance with 99.16% accuracy on histopathological images and 99.54% on CT-scan images, addressing the complexity and uncertainty in disease diagnosis for real-time deployment.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly advances the field of lung cancer diagnostics by offering a highly accurate and computationally efficient AI model capable of robustly classifying LC from both histopathological and CT images, directly addressing critical challenges of uncertainty and complexity in clinical settings.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of a sophisticated deep learning model (FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet) for automated, high-accuracy detection and classification of lung cancer from various medical images (histopathological slides and CT scans). It leverages advanced AI concepts like Knowledge Distillation, Vision Transformers, and fuzzy logic for robust disease diagnosis, with an emphasis on real-time deployment in clinical environments to assist healthcare professionals.

Key Points

  • Introduces FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet, a novel architecture for lung cancer classification leveraging dynamic knowledge distillation.
  • Employs dynamic fuzzy logic to adjust distillation weights, enabling the student model to prioritize high-confidence regions and reduce attention to ambiguous areas in LC images, thereby handling varying uncertainty levels.
  • Utilizes a Vision Transformer (ViT-B32) as the robust instructor model to transfer knowledge effectively to the more lightweight MobileNet student model, enhancing its generalization capabilities.
  • Integrates a multi-stage image preprocessing pipeline including Gamma correction, Histogram Equalization, and wavelet-based fusion (using `wavedec2` to standardize to 224x224 and fuse multi-scale frequency components) to improve image resolution and feature preservation.
  • Optimizes computational efficiency by using a Genetic Algorithm (GA) to select the most suitable pre-trained student model from a pool of 12 candidates, balancing performance and cost.
  • Achieves high classification accuracies: 99.16% on LC25000 histopathological images and 99.54% on IQOTH/NCCD CT-scan images, demonstrating robustness across different imaging modalities.
  • Incorporates a dynamic wait adjustment mechanism during training to optimize convergence and overall performance.

Methodology

The FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet model uses a ViT-B32 teacher and MobileNet student within a dynamic fuzzy logic-driven Knowledge Distillation framework. This framework adaptively adjusts distillation weights based on fuzzy logic to manage uncertainty. Images undergo preprocessing including Gamma correction, Histogram Equalization, and wavelet-based fusion (`wavedec2`) to a 224x224 resolution. A Genetic Algorithm selects the optimal MobileNet student, and training is further optimized with a dynamic wait adjustment mechanism.

Key Findings

The model achieved 99.16% accuracy on the LC25000 histopathological image dataset and 99.54% accuracy on the IQOTH/NCCD CT-scan image dataset, demonstrating high accuracy and robustness across different lung cancer imaging modalities. The dynamic fuzzy logic and image fusion techniques successfully enhance the model's ability to handle image uncertainty and improve feature representation.

Clinical Impact

The high accuracy and efficiency of FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet could significantly aid clinicians in the early and accurate diagnosis of lung cancer, potentially reducing misdiagnosis rates and improving patient outcomes. Its real-time deployment potential suggests faster diagnostic turnaround times, making it a valuable tool in busy clinical environments for both pathological and radiological evaluations.

Abstract

This paper presents the FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet model, a novel approach for lung cancer (LC) classification, leveraging dynamic fuzzy logic-driven knowledge distillation (KD) to address uncertainty and complexity in disease diagnosis. Unlike traditional models that rely on static KD with fixed weights, our method dynamically adjusts the distillation weight using fuzzy logic, enabling the student model to focus on high-confidence regions while reducing attention to ambiguous areas. This dynamic adjustment improves the model ability to handle varying uncertainty levels across different regions of LC images. We employ the Vision Transformer (ViT-B32) as the instructor model, which effectively transfers knowledge to the student model, MobileNet, enhancing the student generalization capabilities. The training process is further optimized using a dynamic wait adjustment mechanism that adapts the training procedure for improved convergence and performance. To enhance image quality, we introduce pixel-level image fusion improvement techniques such as Gamma correction and Histogram Equalization. The processed images (Pix1 and Pix2) are fused using a wavelet-based fusion method to improve image resolution and feature preservation. This fusion method uses the wavedec2 function to standardize images to a 224x224 resolution, decompose them into multi-scale frequency components, and recursively average coefficients at each level for better feature representation. To address computational efficiency, Genetic Algorithm (GA) is used to select the most suitable pre-trained student model from a pool of 12 candidates, balancing model performance with computational cost. The model is evaluated on two datasets, including LC25000 histopathological images (99.16% accuracy) and IQOTH/NCCD CT-scan images (99.54% accuracy), demonstrating robustness across different imaging domains.
