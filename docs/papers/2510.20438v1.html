Dynamic Weight Adjustment for Knowledge Distillation: Leveraging Vision Transformer for High-Accuracy Lung Cancer Detection and Real-Time Deployment

Summary

This paper introduces the FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet model, a novel approach for high-accuracy lung cancer (LC) classification. It employs dynamic fuzzy logic-driven knowledge distillation, where a Vision Transformer (ViT-B32) instructor transfers knowledge to a MobileNet student, allowing for adaptive weight adjustment based on regional uncertainty in medical images. This method achieves high diagnostic accuracy on both histopathological and CT-scan datasets while being optimized for real-time deployment.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly advances lung cancer diagnosis by providing a highly accurate, robust, and computationally efficient AI model. Its ability to handle diverse imaging modalities and uncertainty in medical images can lead to earlier and more precise detection, ultimately improving patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

The AI model (FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet) is designed for automated, high-accuracy detection and classification of lung cancer from medical images (histopathology slides and CT scans). This directly applies to medical AI for diagnostic support, aiming to assist pathologists and radiologists in identifying cancerous lesions more efficiently and accurately, potentially reducing diagnostic errors and improving patient outcomes. The focus on 'real-time deployment' suggests its utility in clinical decision support systems or integrated diagnostic platforms.

Key Points

  • **FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet Model:** A new AI architecture designed for lung cancer detection, leveraging dynamic fuzzy logic and knowledge distillation.
  • **Dynamic Fuzzy Logic-driven Knowledge Distillation (KD):** Unlike traditional static KD, fuzzy logic dynamically adjusts distillation weights, enabling the student model to focus on high-confidence regions and reduce attention to ambiguous areas in LC images, thereby improving uncertainty handling.
  • **ViT-B32 Instructor & MobileNet Student:** Utilizes a Vision Transformer (ViT-B32) as a robust instructor model to effectively transfer knowledge to a lightweight MobileNet student, enhancing the student's generalization capabilities for efficient real-time deployment.
  • **Advanced Image Pre-processing & Fusion:** Incorporates pixel-level image improvement techniques (Gamma correction, Histogram Equalization) and a wavelet-based fusion method (using `wavedec2`) to standardize image resolution (224x224), decompose into multi-scale components, and enhance feature preservation.
  • **Genetic Algorithm (GA) for Student Model Selection:** Employs a Genetic Algorithm to select the most suitable pre-trained MobileNet student model from 12 candidates, optimizing for a balance between model performance and computational cost.
  • **Dynamic Wait Adjustment Mechanism:** The training process is further optimized with a dynamic wait adjustment mechanism to improve convergence and overall model performance.
  • **High-Accuracy Performance:** Achieved 99.16% accuracy on the LC25000 histopathological image dataset and 99.54% accuracy on the IQOTH/NCCD CT-scan image dataset, demonstrating robust performance across different imaging domains.

Methodology

The FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet model integrates a ViT-B32 instructor with a MobileNet student, employing dynamic fuzzy logic to adjust knowledge distillation weights based on image region confidence. Image processing includes Gamma correction, Histogram Equalization, and wavelet-based fusion (wavedec2) for resolution standardization and feature enhancement. A Genetic Algorithm selects the optimal MobileNet student model for computational efficiency, and a dynamic wait adjustment mechanism optimizes training convergence.

Key Findings

The model achieved exceptionally high accuracy rates: 99.16% on the LC25000 histopathological image dataset and 99.54% on the IQOTH/NCCD CT-scan image dataset, demonstrating its robustness and effectiveness across different types of lung cancer imaging.

Its optimized design for real-time deployment indicates it could be integrated into clinical settings to assist radiologists and pathologists, potentially streamlining diagnostic workflows, reducing human error, and facilitating quicker treatment decisions.

Abstract

This paper presents the FuzzyDistillViT-MobileNet model, a novel approach for lung cancer (LC) classification, leveraging dynamic fuzzy logic-driven knowledge distillation (KD) to address uncertainty and complexity in disease diagnosis. Unlike traditional models that rely on static KD with fixed weights, our method dynamically adjusts the distillation weight using fuzzy logic, enabling the student model to focus on high-confidence regions while reducing attention to ambiguous areas. This dynamic adjustment improves the model ability to handle varying uncertainty levels across different regions of LC images. We employ the Vision Transformer (ViT-B32) as the instructor model, which effectively transfers knowledge to the student model, MobileNet, enhancing the student generalization capabilities. The training process is further optimized using a dynamic wait adjustment mechanism that adapts the training procedure for improved convergence and performance. To enhance image quality, we introduce pixel-level image fusion improvement techniques such as Gamma correction and Histogram Equalization. The processed images (Pix1 and Pix2) are fused using a wavelet-based fusion method to improve image resolution and feature preservation. This fusion method uses the wavedec2 function to standardize images to a 224x224 resolution, decompose them into multi-scale frequency components, and recursively average coefficients at each level for better feature representation. To address computational efficiency, Genetic Algorithm (GA) is used to select the most suitable pre-trained student model from a pool of 12 candidates, balancing model performance with computational cost. The model is evaluated on two datasets, including LC25000 histopathological images (99.16% accuracy) and IQOTH/NCCD CT-scan images (99.54% accuracy), demonstrating robustness across different imaging domains.
