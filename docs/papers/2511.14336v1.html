ArchMap: Arch-Flattening and Knowledge-Guided Vision Language Model for Tooth Counting and Structured Dental Understanding

Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Bohan Zhang, Yiyi Miao, Taoyu Wu, Tong Chen, Ji Jiang, Zhuoxiao Li, Zhe Tang, Limin Yu, Jionglong Su

Summary

ArchMap is a novel training-free, knowledge-guided framework designed to provide robust structured understanding of 3D intraoral scans by addressing limitations of existing deep-learning approaches. It achieves this by standardizing raw meshes through a geometry-aware arch-flattening module and leveraging a comprehensive Dental Knowledge Base for semantic reasoning. The system demonstrates superior accuracy and stability across various dental analysis tasks compared to supervised and VLM baselines.

Medical Relevance

This framework is profoundly relevant for digital orthodontics and general dentistry by enabling highly accurate, automated, and consistent analysis of 3D intraoral scans, critical for diagnosis, treatment planning, and progress monitoring without the need for extensive manual annotation or device-specific training, thus improving efficiency and accessibility of advanced dental care.

AI Health Application

The paper describes an AI-driven framework (Vision Language Model based) designed to automate and standardize the analysis of 3D intraoral scans. This application assists dental professionals in tasks such as identifying dental conditions (e.g., caries, missing teeth, crowding), classifying dentition stages, and anatomical partitioning, thereby supporting diagnostic processes and treatment planning, particularly in digital orthodontics. It aims to make AI-powered dental analysis more practical and scalable in clinical settings.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: Overcomes challenges in 3D intraoral scan analysis, including reliance on large annotated datasets, modality-specific training, and variations in arch pose or incomplete geometry inherent in raw meshes.
  • **Arch-Flattening Module**: Introduces a geometry-aware module that transforms raw 3D meshes into standardized, spatially aligned, and continuity-preserving multi-view projections, improving consistency for downstream analysis.
  • **Dental Knowledge Base (DKB)**: Incorporates a knowledge base encoding hierarchical tooth ontology, dentition-stage policies, and clinical semantics to guide and constrain the symbolic reasoning process, enhancing interpretability and accuracy.
  • **Training-Free Approach**: ArchMap operates as a fully training-free system, eliminating the need for extensive annotated datasets and complex, time-consuming model training, making it highly practical for clinical deployment.
  • **Comprehensive Validation**: Validated on 1060 pre-/post-orthodontic cases, demonstrating robust performance across a range of tasks including tooth counting, anatomical partitioning, dentition-stage classification, and identification of clinical conditions (e.g., crowding, missing teeth, prosthetics, caries).
  • **Superior Performance**: Achieves higher accuracy, reduced semantic drift, and superior stability compared to both supervised pipelines and prompted Vision Language Model (VLM) baselines, particularly under sparse or artifact-prone conditions.
  • **Scalability and Practicality**: Combines geometric normalization with ontology-guided multimodal reasoning, offering a practical, scalable, and generalized solution for the structured analysis of 3D intraoral scans in modern digital orthodontics.

Methodology

ArchMap employs a two-stage, training-free methodology. First, a geometry-aware arch-flattening module processes raw 3D intraoral meshes to create standardized, spatially aligned, and continuity-preserving multi-view 2D projections. Second, a meticulously constructed Dental Knowledge Base (DKB) is utilized, which encapsulates hierarchical tooth ontology, dentition-stage policies, and clinical semantics. This DKB guides and constrains the symbolic reasoning process applied to the flattened projections for robust semantic interpretation and understanding.

Key Findings

ArchMap delivered robust performance across 1060 pre-/post-orthodontic cases, achieving high accuracy in fundamental tasks such as tooth counting, anatomical partitioning, and dentition-stage classification. Furthermore, it reliably identified various clinical conditions including crowding, missing teeth, prosthetics, and caries. Critically, ArchMap demonstrated superior accuracy, reduced semantic drift, and enhanced stability when compared to traditional supervised pipelines and state-of-the-art prompted Vision Language Model (VLM) baselines, especially under challenging conditions like sparse or artifact-prone data.

Clinical Impact

The training-free, robust, and scalable nature of ArchMap has the potential to significantly impact clinical workflows in digital orthodontics and dentistry. It can lead to more consistent and accurate automated diagnostics, precise treatment planning, and effective monitoring of orthodontic progress by overcoming current limitations in processing 3D intraoral scans. This system reduces reliance on labor-intensive manual analysis and extensive data annotation, thereby streamlining clinical processes and making advanced dental analysis more accessible and efficient.

Abstract

A structured understanding of intraoral 3D scans is essential for digital orthodontics. However, existing deep-learning approaches rely heavily on modality-specific training, large annotated datasets, and controlled scanning conditions, which limit generalization across devices and hinder deployment in real clinical workflows. Moreover, raw intraoral meshes exhibit substantial variation in arch pose, incomplete geometry caused by occlusion or tooth contact, and a lack of texture cues, making unified semantic interpretation highly challenging. To address these limitations, we propose ArchMap, a training-free and knowledge-guided framework for robust structured dental understanding. ArchMap first introduces a geometry-aware arch-flattening module that standardizes raw 3D meshes into spatially aligned, continuity-preserving multi-view projections. We then construct a Dental Knowledge Base (DKB) encoding hierarchical tooth ontology, dentition-stage policies, and clinical semantics to constrain the symbolic reasoning space. We validate ArchMap on 1060 pre-/post-orthodontic cases, demonstrating robust performance in tooth counting, anatomical partitioning, dentition-stage classification, and the identification of clinical conditions such as crowding, missing teeth, prosthetics, and caries. Compared with supervised pipelines and prompted VLM baselines, ArchMap achieves higher accuracy, reduced semantic drift, and superior stability under sparse or artifact-prone conditions. As a fully training-free system, ArchMap demonstrates that combining geometric normalization with ontology-guided multimodal reasoning offers a practical and scalable solution for the structured analysis of 3D intraoral scans in modern digital orthodontics.
