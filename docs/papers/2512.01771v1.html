Robust Rigid and Non-Rigid Medical Image Registration Using Learnable Edge Kernels

Summary

This paper introduces a novel approach for robust rigid and non-rigid medical image registration by integrating learnable edge kernels into learning-based techniques. The method adaptively optimizes edge features from perturbed predefined kernels, enabling superior capture of diverse structural information. Across extensive evaluations on various datasets and setups, it consistently outperformed state-of-the-art methods, signifying significant advancements in multi-modal image alignment.

Medical Relevance

Accurate medical image registration is foundational for precise disease diagnosis, effective treatment planning (e.g., radiation therapy, surgery), and monitoring of disease progression or treatment response. This method offers a more robust solution to align images from different modalities or time points, directly enhancing the utility and reliability of medical imaging in clinical practice.

Methodology

The proposed method integrates learnable edge kernels into learning-based rigid and non-rigid registration frameworks. These kernels are initialized from standard edge detection kernels, perturbed with random noise, and then optimized end-to-end during training to adaptively extract optimal, task-specific edge features. This adaptive feature extraction enhances the capture of diverse structural details critical for robust image alignment. The approach was evaluated on a local Medical University dataset (comprising setups for rigid registration with and without skull removal, and non-rigid registration) and two publicly available datasets, with performance assessed against state-of-the-art techniques using eight distinct model variants (four rigid, four non-rigid) to analyze component contributions.

Key Findings

The primary finding is that the proposed method, leveraging learnable edge kernels, consistently achieved superior performance across all evaluation setups and datasets when compared to existing state-of-the-art medical image registration techniques. This demonstrates its enhanced robustness and accuracy in handling the complexities of medical image alignment, including variations in contrast and spatial distortions.

Abstract

Medical image registration is crucial for various clinical and research applications including disease diagnosis or treatment planning which require alignment of images from different modalities, time points, or subjects. Traditional registration techniques often struggle with challenges such as contrast differences, spatial distortions, and modality-specific variations. To address these limitations, we propose a method that integrates learnable edge kernels with learning-based rigid and non-rigid registration techniques. Unlike conventional layers that learn all features without specific bias, our approach begins with a predefined edge detection kernel, which is then perturbed with random noise. These kernels are learned during training to extract optimal edge features tailored to the task. This adaptive edge detection enhances the registration process by capturing diverse structural features critical in medical imaging. To provide clearer insight into the contribution of each component in our design, we introduce four variant models for rigid registration and four variant models for non-rigid registration. We evaluated our approach using a dataset provided by the Medical University across three setups: rigid registration without skull removal, with skull removal, and non-rigid registration. Additionally, we assessed performance on two publicly available datasets. Across all experiments, our method consistently outperformed state-of-the-art techniques, demonstrating its potential to improve multi-modal image alignment and anatomical structure analysis.
