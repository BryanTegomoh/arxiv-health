Seeing Beyond the Image: ECG and Anatomical Knowledge-Guided Myocardial Scar Segmentation from Late Gadolinium-Enhanced Images

Published: 2025-11-18

Summary

This paper introduces a novel multimodal deep learning framework for accurate myocardial scar segmentation from late gadolinium-enhanced (LGE) cardiac MRI. It integrates ECG-derived electrophysiological information and anatomical priors with LGE-MRI data, utilizing a Temporal Aware Feature Fusion (TAFF) mechanism to handle non-simultaneous data acquisition. The method achieved substantial gains over image-only baselines, significantly improving Dice scores for scar segmentation.

Medical Relevance

Accurate segmentation of myocardial scar from LGE-MRI is critical for evaluating tissue viability, stratifying patient risk, and guiding treatment decisions in cardiology, impacting patients with conditions like ischemic heart disease and cardiomyopathies.

AI Health Application

This paper presents a novel AI framework that applies multimodal deep learning (computer vision and signal processing) to enhance medical image analysis. Specifically, it uses AI to fuse information from Late Gadolinium-Enhanced (LGE) MRI and Electrocardiogram (ECG) signals, along with anatomical priors, to accurately segment myocardial scars. This application aims to improve the diagnostic precision for heart conditions, aiding clinicians in evaluating tissue viability and guiding treatment decisions for patients.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** Improves accuracy of myocardial scar segmentation from LGE-MRI, which is challenging due to variable contrast and artifacts.
  • **Multimodal Approach:** Proposes a novel framework integrating LGE-MRI with ECG-derived electrophysiological information and anatomical priors (AHA-17 atlas).
  • **Physiological Consistency:** Leverages ECG signals, which can indicate conduction abnormalities suggestive of scarred myocardial regions, to guide physiologically consistent segmentation.
  • **Temporal Data Fusion:** Introduces a Temporal Aware Feature Fusion (TAFF) mechanism to dynamically weight and fuse features, specifically addressing the non-simultaneous acquisition of ECG and LGE-MRI data.
  • **Significant Performance Gain:** Demonstrated substantial improvement, increasing the average Dice score for scar segmentation from 0.6149 (image-only baseline) to 0.8463 on a clinical dataset.
  • **High Accuracy Metrics:** Achieved high precision (0.9115) and sensitivity (0.9043) in scar segmentation, indicating robust and reliable performance.
  • **New Research Direction:** Establishes a new direction for cardiac image analysis by enabling models to "see beyond the image" through integrated physiological and anatomical knowledge.

Methodology

The methodology involves a novel multimodal deep learning framework that fuses information from LGE-cardiac MRI, ECG-derived electrophysiological signals, and anatomical priors from the AHA-17 atlas. A critical component is the Temporal Aware Feature Fusion (TAFF) mechanism, which dynamically weights and combines features based on the acquisition time difference between ECG and LGE-MRI to overcome their non-simultaneous nature. The framework is designed to achieve physiologically consistent myocardial scar segmentation.

Key Findings

The proposed multimodal framework significantly outperformed the state-of-the-art image-only baseline (nnU-Net), achieving a substantial increase in the average Dice score for myocardial scar segmentation from 0.6149 to 0.8463. Additionally, the method demonstrated high accuracy with a precision of 0.9115 and a sensitivity of 0.9043 on a clinical dataset.

Clinical Impact

This advancement can lead to more precise and physiologically consistent identification of myocardial scars, which is crucial for improved patient diagnosis, risk stratification, and guiding interventions (e.g., revascularization, ablation, device therapy). It has the potential to enhance clinical decision-making and personalize treatment strategies for patients with heart disease.

Abstract

Accurate segmentation of myocardial scar from late gadolinium enhanced (LGE) cardiac MRI is essential for evaluating tissue viability, yet remains challenging due to variable contrast and imaging artifacts. Electrocardiogram (ECG) signals provide complementary physiological information, as conduction abnormalities can help localize or suggest scarred myocardial regions. In this work, we propose a novel multimodal framework that integrates ECG-derived electrophysiological information with anatomical priors from the AHA-17 atlas for physiologically consistent LGE-based scar segmentation. As ECGs and LGE-MRIs are not acquired simultaneously, we introduce a Temporal Aware Feature Fusion (TAFF) mechanism that dynamically weights and fuses features based on their acquisition time difference. Our method was evaluated on a clinical dataset and achieved substantial gains over the state-of-the-art image-only baseline (nnU-Net), increasing the average Dice score for scars from 0.6149 to 0.8463 and achieving high performance in both precision (0.9115) and sensitivity (0.9043). These results show that integrating physiological and anatomical knowledge allows the model to "see beyond the image", setting a new direction for robust and physiologically grounded cardiac scar segmentation.
