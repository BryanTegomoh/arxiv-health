Seeing Beyond the Image: ECG and Anatomical Knowledge-Guided Myocardial Scar Segmentation from Late Gadolinium-Enhanced Images

Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Farheen Ramzan, Yusuf Kiberu, Nikesh Jathanna, Meryem Jabrane, Vicente Grau, Shahnaz Jamil-Copley, Richard H. Clayton, Chen, Chen

Summary

This paper introduces a novel multimodal framework for myocardial scar segmentation from Late Gadolinium-Enhanced (LGE) MRI, integrating ECG-derived electrophysiological information and AHA-17 anatomical priors. It addresses the challenge of non-simultaneous data acquisition with a Temporal Aware Feature Fusion (TAFF) mechanism. The proposed method significantly outperforms state-of-the-art image-only baselines on a clinical dataset, achieving substantial gains in Dice score, precision, and sensitivity.

Medical Relevance

Accurate myocardial scar segmentation is paramount for assessing tissue viability in cardiac patients, guiding crucial treatment decisions such as revascularization or ablation, and predicting prognosis. This research provides a more robust and physiologically informed method for scar detection, which can significantly improve patient management and outcomes.

AI Health Application

This research describes an AI-powered diagnostic tool designed for advanced cardiac imaging analysis. It utilizes a multimodal deep learning framework to integrate ECG-derived electrophysiological information with Late Gadolinium-Enhanced (LGE) MRI scans and anatomical knowledge to achieve highly accurate myocardial scar segmentation. This application aims to assist cardiologists in precisely identifying scarred heart tissue, which is crucial for assessing tissue viability, guiding treatment strategies (e.g., revascularization, ablation), and improving risk stratification for patients with myocardial disease.

  • Addresses the critical challenge of accurate myocardial scar segmentation from LGE-MRI, essential for evaluating cardiac tissue viability.

Methodology

The study proposes a novel deep learning-based multimodal framework that integrates three distinct data sources: Late Gadolinium-Enhanced (LGE) cardiac MRI scans, electrophysiological information derived from ECG signals, and anatomical prior knowledge from the AHA-17 cardiac segmentation atlas. A key innovation is the Temporal Aware Feature Fusion (TAFF) mechanism, designed to dynamically weight and fuse features from ECG and LGE-MRI by considering their respective acquisition time differences, as these modalities are not acquired simultaneously. The model was trained and evaluated on a clinical dataset, with its performance compared against an image-only state-of-the-art baseline (nnU-Net).

Key Findings

The proposed multimodal framework significantly improved myocardial scar segmentation performance, boosting the average Dice score from 0.6149 (image-only baseline) to 0.8463. Furthermore, the method demonstrated high precision (0.9115) and sensitivity (0.9043), indicating superior ability to accurately detect and delineate myocardial scar regions. These results confirm that integrating physiological (ECG) and anatomical (AHA-17) knowledge substantially enhances the robustness and physiological consistency of scar segmentation.

Clinical Impact

This advanced scar segmentation technology has the potential to profoundly impact clinical cardiology by providing more precise and reliable assessments of myocardial tissue viability. Clinically, this could lead to better-informed decisions regarding revascularization procedures, more accurate planning for arrhythmia ablations, and improved risk stratification for patients with ischemic heart disease or other cardiomyopathies. Its robustness could facilitate broader adoption in clinical workflows.

Potential unmentioned considerations could include the availability of paired ECG and LGE-MRI data for training, the generalizability of the TAFF mechanism to other non-simultaneously acquired multimodal datasets, and the computational demands for real-time inference in clinical settings.

Future Directions

The authors suggest that this work sets a 'new direction for robust and physiologically grounded cardiac scar segmentation,' implying future research could focus on further integrating diverse physiological and anatomical knowledge sources. This could involve exploring additional cardiac biomarkers, developing more sophisticated multimodal fusion strategies, or extending the approach to other challenging cardiac pathologies and imaging tasks where complementary information is valuable.

Abstract

Accurate segmentation of myocardial scar from late gadolinium enhanced (LGE) cardiac MRI is essential for evaluating tissue viability, yet remains challenging due to variable contrast and imaging artifacts. Electrocardiogram (ECG) signals provide complementary physiological information, as conduction abnormalities can help localize or suggest scarred myocardial regions. In this work, we propose a novel multimodal framework that integrates ECG-derived electrophysiological information with anatomical priors from the AHA-17 atlas for physiologically consistent LGE-based scar segmentation. As ECGs and LGE-MRIs are not acquired simultaneously, we introduce a Temporal Aware Feature Fusion (TAFF) mechanism that dynamically weights and fuses features based on their acquisition time difference. Our method was evaluated on a clinical dataset and achieved substantial gains over the state-of-the-art image-only baseline (nnU-Net), increasing the average Dice score for scars from 0.6149 to 0.8463 and achieving high performance in both precision (0.9115) and sensitivity (0.9043). These results show that integrating physiological and anatomical knowledge allows the model to "see beyond the image", setting a new direction for robust and physiologically grounded cardiac scar segmentation.
