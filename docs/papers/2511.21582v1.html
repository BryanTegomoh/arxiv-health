Deep Learning-Based Multiclass Classification of Oral Lesions with Stratified Augmentation

arXiv ID: 2511.21582v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Joy Naoum, Revana Salama, Ali Hamdi
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This research introduces a deep learning-based multiclass classification system designed to differentiate sixteen types of oral lesions, aiming to facilitate early diagnosis of oral cancer. It effectively addresses the challenges of limited and imbalanced medical datasets by employing stratified data splitting combined with advanced data augmentation and oversampling techniques. The proposed model achieved superior performance compared to existing state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating the critical role of these data-handling strategies, especially for minority class recognition.

Medical Relevance

Early and accurate differentiation of oral lesions is paramount for improving patient prognoses in oral cancer, which is frequently diagnosed at advanced stages. This system offers a potential solution to enhance diagnostic precision and timeliness, thereby reducing morbidity and mortality associated with oral malignancies.

AI Health Application

Development of a deep learning-based computer-aided diagnosis (CAD) system for the multiclass classification of oral lesions to facilitate early detection of oral cancer in clinical settings.

Key Points

  • Addresses the global challenge of late oral cancer diagnosis due to the visual similarity between benign, precancerous, and malignant oral lesions.
  • Proposes a deep learning model for multiclass classification across sixteen distinct oral lesion categories.
  • Utilizes a robust methodology incorporating stratified data splitting, advanced data augmentation, and oversampling to mitigate issues from limited and imbalanced datasets.
  • Achieved strong experimental results: 83.33% accuracy, 89.12% precision, and 77.31% recall.
  • The model demonstrated superiority over current state-of-the-art methods in oral lesion classification.
  • Validated the significant effectiveness of oversampling and augmentation strategies, particularly in enhancing classification performance for minority classes.
  • Presents the framework as a promising initial step towards developing trustworthy computer-aided diagnostic systems for early oral cancer detection in clinical environments.

Methodology

The research implemented a deep learning architecture for multiclass classification, designed to distinguish between sixteen types of oral lesions. To counteract the common challenges of limited and imbalanced datasets, the methodology critically involved stratified data splitting to ensure representative distribution, alongside advanced data augmentation and oversampling techniques to artificially expand and balance the training data.

Key Findings

The developed deep learning model achieved an overall accuracy of 83.33%, a precision of 89.12%, and a recall of 77.31%. A crucial finding was the demonstrated superiority of the suggested model over existing state-of-the-art methods. Furthermore, the study effectively confirmed the substantial impact and effectiveness of the integrated oversampling and augmentation strategies, notably in improving classification performance, particularly for underrepresented (minority) classes.

Clinical Impact

This framework offers a significant advancement toward creating reliable computer-aided diagnostic systems for early oral cancer detection. Its potential integration into clinical settings could empower clinicians with a tool for more accurate and timely diagnosis, leading to earlier intervention, improved treatment outcomes, and enhanced patient care in oral oncology.

Limitations

The abstract primarily discusses the general challenges of 'limited and imbalanced datasets' in the field, which the proposed methodology aims to overcome. It does not explicitly state specific limitations or caveats of the developed model itself.

Future Directions

The suggested framework is positioned as a 'first step' towards developing trustworthy computer-aided diagnostic systems for early oral cancer detection in clinical settings. This implies ongoing research, validation, and development are needed to transition it from a promising research model to a fully deployable and reliable clinical tool.

Abstract

Oral cancer is highly common across the globe and is mostly diagnosed during the later stages due to the close visual similarity to benign, precancerous, and malignant lesions in the oral cavity. Implementing computer aided diagnosis systems early on has the potential to greatly improve clinical outcomes. This research intends to use deep learning to build a multiclass classifier for sixteen different oral lesions. To overcome the challenges of limited and imbalanced datasets, the proposed technique combines stratified data splitting and advanced data augmentation and oversampling to perform the classification. The experimental results, which achieved 83.33 percent accuracy, 89.12 percent precision, and 77.31 percent recall, demonstrate the superiority of the suggested model over state of the art methods now in use. The suggested model effectively conveys the effectiveness of oversampling and augmentation strategies in situations where the minority class classification performance is noteworthy. As a first step toward trustworthy computer aided diagnostic systems for the early detection of oral cancer in clinical settings, the suggested framework shows promise.
