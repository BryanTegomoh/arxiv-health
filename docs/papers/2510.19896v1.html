Enhancing Diagnostic Accuracy for Urinary Tract Disease through Explainable SHAP-Guided Feature Selection and Classification

Summary

This paper proposes a novel approach to improve diagnostic accuracy for urinary tract diseases, particularly bladder cancer, by integrating SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)-based feature selection into predictive models. The methodology successfully maintained or enhanced key performance metrics while concurrently increasing model transparency, offering a pathway to more reliable clinical decision support systems.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant for advancing the early and accurate diagnosis of bladder cancer and other urinary tract diseases. By enhancing model transparency and reliability through explainable AI, it increases clinician trust and facilitates the adoption of AI-driven tools in clinical practice, potentially leading to improved patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of an explainable clinical decision support system designed to improve the accuracy and transparency of diagnosing urinary tract diseases, particularly bladder cancer. It aims to optimize screening and early diagnosis by providing predictive models that identify key features contributing to a diagnosis, making the system more reliable for clinicians.

  • Six distinct binary classification scenarios were developed to differentiate bladder cancer from other urological and oncological conditions.
  • Advanced ensemble algorithms (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost) were utilized, with hyperparameter optimization performed via Optuna and class imbalance addressed using SMOTE.
  • The SHAP-guided feature selection strategy successfully maintained or even improved crucial performance metrics, including balanced accuracy, precision, and specificity.
  • The study demonstrates that using explainability techniques like SHAP for feature selection is an effective approach for developing more transparent and reliable predictive models.

Methodology

The study involved developing six binary classification scenarios for distinguishing bladder cancer. It employed state-of-the-art ensemble machine learning algorithms (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost), with hyperparameter optimization conducted using Optuna. Class imbalance was addressed through the SMOTE technique. Critically, feature selection was guided by SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) importance values to identify the most predictive and explainable features.

It successfully maintained or improved key performance metrics such as balanced accuracy, precision, and specificity, while simultaneously increasing model transparency. This confirms the utility of explainability techniques for guiding variable selection in diagnostic tasks.

Abstract

In this paper, we propose an approach to support the diagnosis of urinary tract diseases, with a focus on bladder cancer, using SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)-based feature selection to enhance the transparency and effectiveness of predictive models. Six binary classification scenarios were developed to distinguish bladder cancer from other urological and oncological conditions. The algorithms XGBoost, LightGBM, and CatBoost were employed, with hyperparameter optimization performed using Optuna and class balancing with the SMOTE technique. The selection of predictive variables was guided by importance values through SHAP-based feature selection while maintaining or even improving performance metrics such as balanced accuracy, precision, and specificity. The use of explainability techniques (SHAP) for feature selection proved to be an effective approach. The proposed methodology may contribute to the development of more transparent, reliable, and efficient clinical decision support systems, optimizing screening and early diagnosis of urinary tract diseases.
