Stage Aware Diagnosis of Diabetic Retinopathy via Ordinal Regression

arXiv ID: 2511.14398v1
Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Saksham Kumar, D Sridhar Aditya, T Likhil Kumar, Thulasi Bikku, Srinivasarao Thota, Chandan Kumar
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a novel, state-of-the-art Ordinal Regression-based framework for the stage-aware diagnosis of Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) using fundus images. Employing a sophisticated preprocessing pipeline on the APTOS-2019 dataset, the method achieved a Quadratic Weighted Kappa (QWK) score of 0.8992, establishing a new benchmark for accurate DR classification that aligns closely with clinical grading.

Medical Relevance

Timely and accurate diagnosis of Diabetic Retinopathy is paramount for preventing irreversible vision loss and blindness in diabetic patients. This research offers an automated, high-performing tool that can significantly enhance screening programs, enabling earlier intervention and better patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

The AI application is an automated diagnostic tool that analyzes fundus images to detect and stage Diabetic Retinopathy. This can significantly aid in mass screening efforts, assist ophthalmologists and healthcare providers in early diagnosis, and facilitate timely intervention to prevent blindness caused by DR.

Key Points

  • Addresses Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) as a major cause of preventable blindness, highlighting the critical need for timely screening and intervention.
  • Proposes a novel DR detection framework utilizing Ordinal Regression, which is inherently suited for classifying ordered disease stages.
  • Leverages the APTOS-2019 fundus image dataset for model development and evaluation.
  • Implements a robust preprocessing pipeline comprising Green Channel (GC) Extraction, Noise Masking, and CLAHE to optimize feature isolation for DR classification.
  • Evaluates model performance using the Quadratic Weighted Kappa (QWK) metric, specifically chosen for its ability to assess agreement in ordinal classification tasks with clinical grading.
  • Achieved a QWK score of 0.8992, setting a new, high-performance benchmark on the APTOS-2019 dataset.
  • The approach is 'stage aware,' implying the model can differentiate between various severity levels of DR, which is crucial for clinical decision-making.

Methodology

The methodology involves developing an Ordinal Regression-based framework for classifying stages of Diabetic Retinopathy from fundus images. Input images from the APTOS-2019 dataset undergo a specific preprocessing sequence: Green Channel (GC) Extraction, Noise Masking, and Contrast-Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization (CLAHE). Model performance is rigorously evaluated using the Quadratic Weighted Kappa (QWK) score, a metric designed to assess agreement for ordered categories, with a strong emphasis on aligning results with expert clinical grading.

Key Findings

The primary finding is that the Ordinal Regression-based DR detection framework achieved a Quadratic Weighted Kappa (QWK) score of 0.8992 on the APTOS-2019 dataset. This score represents a new state-of-the-art benchmark, indicating exceptionally high agreement with clinical grading for stage-aware diagnosis of Diabetic Retinopathy.

Clinical Impact

This high-performing, automated DR detection system has significant potential to revolutionize screening efforts, especially in regions with limited access to ophthalmologists. It could facilitate earlier and more efficient diagnosis, streamline patient triaging, and enable timely interventions, thereby preventing vision loss and reducing the burden of preventable blindness in diabetic populations. Its stage-aware capability also allows for more targeted clinical management.

Abstract

Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) has emerged as a major cause of preventable blindness in recent times. With timely screening and intervention, the condition can be prevented from causing irreversible damage. The work introduces a state-of-the-art Ordinal Regression-based DR Detection framework that uses the APTOS-2019 fundus image dataset. A widely accepted combination of preprocessing methods: Green Channel (GC) Extraction, Noise Masking, and CLAHE, was used to isolate the most relevant features for DR classification. Model performance was evaluated using the Quadratic Weighted Kappa, with a focus on agreement between results and clinical grading. Our Ordinal Regression approach attained a QWK score of 0.8992, setting a new benchmark on the APTOS dataset.
