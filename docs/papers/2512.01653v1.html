Cuffless Blood Pressure Estimation from Six Wearable Sensor Modalities in Multi-Motion-State Scenarios

arXiv ID: 2512.01653v1
Published: 2025-12-01
Authors: Yiqiao Chen, Fazheng Xu, Zijian Huang, Juchi He, Zhenghui Feng
Categories: eess.SP, cs.LG

Summary

This paper introduces a novel six-modal cuffless blood pressure (BP) estimation framework designed for robust accuracy across various motion states, addressing the limitations of existing PPG/ECG-based methods under dynamic conditions. By integrating diverse wearable sensor data and advanced machine learning techniques, the proposed system achieves high clinical-grade accuracy for SBP and DBP in multi-motion scenarios. This significantly advances continuous, non-invasive BP monitoring for early screening and long-term management of hypertension.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for advancing the fight against cardiovascular disease by enabling accurate, continuous, and non-invasive blood pressure monitoring even during daily physical activities. It offers a powerful tool for early detection of hypertension and improved long-term management, potentially reducing morbidity and mortality associated with this silent risk factor.

AI Health Application

The research develops an AI-powered framework for cuffless continuous blood pressure estimation using multi-modal wearable sensor data. This application enables real-time, non-invasive monitoring of a vital health parameter, which can be used for early detection of hypertension, chronic disease management, and personalized health insights, thereby enhancing preventive and therapeutic strategies in cardiology and general healthcare.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of maintaining accurate cuffless BP estimation in multi-motion-state scenarios, a known weakness of traditional PPG/ECG-only methods.
  • Proposes a novel six-modal framework leveraging ECG, multi-channel PPG, attachment pressure, sensor temperature, and triaxial acceleration/angular velocity for comprehensive data capture.
  • Employs a sophisticated methodology including lightweight branch encoders for individual modalities, contrastive learning for cross-modal semantic alignment, and a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) regression head for adaptive BP mapping across different motion states.

  • Meets the numerical criteria of the Association for the Advancement of Medical Instrumentation (AAMI) standard for mean error (ME) and standard deviation of error (SDE).
  • The framework's robustness in dynamic environments makes it highly promising for real-world continuous hypertension monitoring and management.

Methodology

The study proposes a six-modal BP estimation framework integrating data from ECG, multi-channel PPG, attachment pressure, sensor temperature, and triaxial acceleration and angular velocity sensors. Each modality is processed through a dedicated lightweight branch encoder. Contrastive learning is then applied to enforce cross-modal semantic alignment of the features. Finally, a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) regression head adaptively fuses these aligned features and maps them to SBP and DBP, specifically designed to maintain robust accuracy across multi-motion-state scenarios like sitting, walking, and running.

Key Findings

The proposed method achieved a mean absolute error (MAE) of 3.60 mmHg for systolic BP (SBP) and 3.01 mmHg for diastolic BP (DBP) across multi-motion-state scenarios. Clinically, it met Grade A for SBP, DBP, and mean arterial pressure (MAP) according to the British Hypertension Society (BHS) protocol. Furthermore, it satisfied the numerical criteria of the Association for the Advancement of Medical Instrumentation (AAMI) standard for both mean error (ME) and standard deviation of error (SDE).

Abstract

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) is a leading cause of morbidity and mortality worldwide, and sustained hypertension is an often silent risk factor, making cuffless continuous blood pressure (BP) monitoring with wearable devices important for early screening and long-term management. Most existing cuffless BP estimation methods use only photoplethysmography (PPG) and electrocardiography (ECG) signals, alone or in combination. These models are typically developed under resting or quasi-static conditions and struggle to maintain robust accuracy in multi-motion-state scenarios. In this study, we propose a six-modal BP estimation framework that jointly leverages ECG, multi-channel PPG, attachment pressure, sensor temperature, and triaxial acceleration and angular velocity. Each modality is processed by a lightweight branch encoder, contrastive learning enforces cross-modal semantic alignment, and a mixture-of-experts (MoE) regression head adaptively maps the fused features to BP across motion states. Comprehensive experiments on the public Pulse Transit Time PPG Dataset, which includes running, walking, and sitting data from 22 subjects, show that the proposed method achieves mean absolute errors (MAE) of 3.60 mmHg for systolic BP (SBP) and 3.01 mmHg for diastolic BP (DBP). From a clinical perspective, it attains Grade A for SBP, DBP, and mean arterial pressure (MAP) according to the British Hypertension Society (BHS) protocol and meets the numerical criteria of the Association for the Advancement of Medical Instrumentation (AAMI) standard for mean error (ME) and standard deviation of error (SDE).
