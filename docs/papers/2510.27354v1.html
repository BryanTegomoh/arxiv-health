Streptococcosis in aquaculture: Advances, challenges, and future directions in disease control and prevention

arXiv ID: 2510.27354v1
Published: 2025-10-31
Authors: Hussein Aliu Sule, Abdulwakil Olawale Saba, Choo Yee Yu
Categories: q-bio.PE
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This comprehensive review synthesizes current knowledge on streptococcal infections (Streptococcus iniae, S. agalactiae) in aquaculture, which pose significant threats to global food security due to high fish morbidity and mortality. It details advancements in disease control and prevention strategies, monitoring technologies, and advocates for a holistic One-Health approach to foster sustainable and productive aquaculture systems. The paper consolidates insights on epidemiology, pathogenesis, and clinical manifestations, offering a roadmap for future research and collaborative efforts.

Medical Relevance

This research is medically relevant because streptococcal infections in aquaculture directly impact global food security, which is foundational to human health and nutrition. Moreover, some fish pathogens, like *Streptococcus agalactiae*, have zoonotic potential, and effective disease control in aquaculture contributes to preventing antimicrobial resistance, thereby safeguarding public health within the broader One-Health framework.

AI Health Application

The paper describes the application of big data analytics, IoT technologies, real-time surveillance, and predictive modeling for disease monitoring and management. While the immediate application is for fish health, these are direct AI/data science methodologies relevant to medical AI for human disease surveillance, outbreak prediction, public health management, and real-time health monitoring.

Key Points

  • Streptococcosis, caused by pathogens like *Streptococcus iniae* and *S. agalactiae*, induces severe systemic infections in fish, leading to high morbidity and mortality in aquaculture.
  • Advancements in genetic engineering (e.g., CRISPR-Cas9) and genomic selection are highlighted for developing disease-resistant fish strains.
  • Improved farming practices, including optimal stocking density, feeding regimes, and stringent biosecurity measures, are critical for minimizing disease prevalence.
  • The integration of big data analytics and Internet of Things (IoT) technologies enables real-time disease surveillance, predictive modeling, and timely interventions in aquaculture systems.
  • Significant progress has been made in vaccine development, including subunit, DNA, and recombinant protein vaccines, offering tailored immunoprophylactic strategies for fish.
  • The review emphasizes the necessity of a One-Health approach, advocating for essential collaboration among industry, academia, and government to address the interconnected health of humans, animals, and the environment.
  • A holistic strategy, supported by advanced technologies and interdisciplinary partnerships, is crucial for enhancing the sustainability and productivity of global aquaculture.

Methodology

This paper is a comprehensive review that consolidates current scientific knowledge on the epidemiology, pathogenesis, clinical manifestations, and multifaceted control and prevention strategies for streptococcal infections in aquaculture. It involves an extensive analysis of existing literature on genetic engineering, farming practices, advanced technologies (big data, IoT), vaccine development, and the One-Health approach.

Key Findings

The review identifies crucial advancements and effective strategies for combating streptococcal infections: the potential of genetic engineering and selective breeding for disease resistance; the significant impact of optimized farming practices and biosecurity; the transformative role of big data and IoT in real-time disease management; the progress in developing diverse vaccine platforms; and the indispensable role of a One-Health approach for integrated health outcomes.

Clinical Impact

The practical application of these strategies promises to significantly reduce morbidity and mortality rates in farmed fish, leading to improved animal welfare, increased productivity, and enhanced sustainability of aquaculture systems. This directly contributes to global food security, potentially mitigates the spread of antimicrobial resistance, and safeguards human health by ensuring a safer and more stable food supply.

Limitations

As a comprehensive review, this paper synthesizes existing knowledge and does not present novel experimental data or specific quantitative results from new research. The practical implementation of the discussed strategies, particularly those involving advanced technologies and complex interdisciplinary collaborations, may face significant logistical, economic, and regulatory challenges in diverse real-world aquaculture settings, which are not detailed as limitations within the abstract.

Future Directions

Future research should focus on continued innovation in disease control technologies, the development of more effective and accessible vaccines, and strengthening interdisciplinary partnerships across industry, academia, and government. These efforts are crucial for overcoming the persistent challenges of streptococcal infections and ensuring the long-term sustainability of aquaculture.

Abstract

Aquaculture is pivotal for global food security but faces significant challenges from infectious diseases, particularly those caused by Streptococcus species such as Streptococcus iniae and Streptococcus agalactiae. These pathogens induce severe systemic infections in various fish species, resulting in high morbidity and mortality rates. This review consolidates current knowledge on the epidemiology, pathogenesis, and clinical manifestations of these infections in fish and provides a comprehensive analysis of multifaceted control and prebention strategies. Advancements in genetic engineering and selective breeding are highlighted, demonstrating significant potential in developing disease-resistant fish strains through technologies like CRISPR-Cas9 and genomic selection. We examine the impact of farming practices on disease prevalence, emphasizing the roles of stocking density, feeding regimes, and biosecurity measures. The integration of big data analytics and IoT technologies is shown to revolutionize disease monitoring and management, enabling real-time surveillance and predictive modeling for timely interventions. Progress in vaccine development, including subunit, DNA, and recombinant protein vaccines, highlights the importance of tailored immunoprophylactic strategies. Furthermore, this review emphasizes the One-Health approach and the essential collaboration among industry, academia, and government to address the interconnected health of humans, animals, and the environment. This holistic strategy, supported by advanced technologies and collaborative efforts, promises to enhance the sustainability and productivity of aquaculture systems. Future research directions advocate for continued innovation and interdisciplinary partnerships to overcome the persistent challenges of streptococcal infections in aquaculture.
