Advanced Unsupervised Learning: A Comprehensive Overview of Multi-View Clustering Techniques

Summary

This comprehensive survey provides an in-depth analysis of Multi-View Clustering (MVC), an unsupervised learning paradigm designed to overcome the limitations of single-view methods and computational challenges associated with complex, multi-source datasets. It systematically categorizes MVC techniques, evaluates their strengths and weaknesses, and discusses practical challenges, emerging trends, and future research directions. The paper integrates insights from over 140 publications, including comparative analyses of data fusion strategies and investigations into practical use cases, notably in healthcare.

Medical Relevance

Multi-View Clustering is highly relevant to medicine by enabling more robust and comprehensive analysis of complex, multi-source patient data, such as electronic health records, medical imaging, genomic profiles, and wearable sensor data. This integrated approach can lead to richer disease representations, improved patient stratification, and more effective diagnostic or prognostic models than those derived from single data modalities.

AI Health Application

Multi-view clustering can be applied in healthcare to integrate and analyze complex, heterogeneous datasets from various sources (e.g., patient demographics, lab results, medical images, genomic data, lifestyle data). This integration can lead to improved patient stratification, discovery of novel disease subtypes, more accurate diagnostic and prognostic models, and personalized treatment recommendations by leveraging the complementary information across different data 'views'.

Key Points

  • MVC addresses fundamental challenges in machine learning, including computational constraints, limitations of single-view learning, and the complexity of processing large, multi-domain datasets.
  • The survey systematically categorizes MVC methods into well-defined groups: co-training, co-regularization, subspace, deep learning, kernel-based, anchor-based, and graph-based strategies.
  • It provides an in-depth analysis of the strengths, weaknesses, and practical challenges inherent to various MVC methods, specifically mentioning scalability and the handling of incomplete data.
  • Comparative insights are offered on different data integration strategies, including early fusion, late fusion, and joint learning, for combining information from multiple views.
  • The work reviews over 140 foundational and recent publications, demonstrating an extensive coverage of the MVC landscape.
  • Practical use cases in areas such as healthcare, multimedia, and social network analysis are investigated to highlight MVC's real-world utility.

Methodology

This paper constitutes a comprehensive literature survey and review. Its methodology involved systematically reviewing over 140 foundational and recent publications related to Multi-View Clustering (MVC). The authors categorized diverse MVC methods into seven groups, analyzed their technical attributes (strengths, weaknesses), compared different data integration strategies (early, late, joint fusion), and identified practical challenges, emerging trends, and interdisciplinary applications within the field.

Key Findings

The survey's key findings include a robust, systematic categorization of diverse MVC methods, a detailed exposition of their respective technical merits and drawbacks, and the identification of critical practical challenges such as scalability and the robust handling of incomplete multi-view data. It also provides comparative insights into different data fusion approaches and highlights MVC's significant potential to extract semantically richer data representations by integrating heterogeneous data sources.

Clinical Impact

The application of Multi-View Clustering can lead to improved diagnostic accuracy by facilitating the integrated analysis of heterogeneous patient data, such as combining imaging scans with lab results and genetic markers. It can enhance patient stratification for personalized treatment plans, enabling more targeted therapies based on a holistic view of a patient's condition. Furthermore, MVC may enable earlier detection and better prognostication of diseases by uncovering subtle patterns across complex multi-modal clinical data that single-view analysis might overlook.

Limitations

The survey highlights several practical limitations inherent to Multi-View Clustering methods themselves, which researchers need to address. These include significant challenges related to scalability when processing extremely large, high-dimensional datasets and the complexities of effectively handling incomplete or missing data across different views, which is common in real-world clinical settings.

Future Directions

Future research in Multi-View Clustering should focus on addressing persistent practical challenges like improving scalability for massive datasets and developing more robust strategies for handling incomplete multi-view data. The paper also encourages exploring emerging trends within MVC, such as advanced deep learning architectures, and fostering interdisciplinary applications beyond current use cases, particularly in areas like complex disease modeling and personalized health analytics.

Abstract

Machine learning techniques face numerous challenges to achieve optimal performance. These include computational constraints, the limitations of single-view learning algorithms and the complexity of processing large datasets from different domains, sources or views. In this context, multi-view clustering (MVC), a class of unsupervised multi-view learning, emerges as a powerful approach to overcome these challenges. MVC compensates for the shortcomings of single-view methods and provides a richer data representation and effective solutions for a variety of unsupervised learning tasks. In contrast to traditional single-view approaches, the semantically rich nature of multi-view data increases its practical utility despite its inherent complexity. This survey makes a threefold contribution: (1) a systematic categorization of multi-view clustering methods into well-defined groups, including co-training, co-regularization, subspace, deep learning, kernel-based, anchor-based, and graph-based strategies; (2) an in-depth analysis of their respective strengths, weaknesses, and practical challenges, such as scalability and incomplete data; and (3) a forward-looking discussion of emerging trends, interdisciplinary applications, and future directions in MVC research. This study represents an extensive workload, encompassing the review of over 140 foundational and recent publications, the development of comparative insights on integration strategies such as early fusion, late fusion, and joint learning, and the structured investigation of practical use cases in the areas of healthcare, multimedia, and social network analysis. By integrating these efforts, this work aims to fill existing gaps in MVC research and provide actionable insights for the advancement of the field.
