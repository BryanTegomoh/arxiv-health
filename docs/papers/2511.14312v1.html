H-LDM: Hierarchical Latent Diffusion Models for Controllable and Interpretable PCG Synthesis from Clinical Metadata

arXiv ID: 2511.14312v1
Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Chenyang Xu, Siming Li, Hao Wang
Categories: cs.LG, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00

Summary

H-LDM introduces a Hierarchical Latent Diffusion Model for synthesizing clinically accurate and controllable Phonocardiogram (PCG) signals from structured clinical metadata. It addresses the scarcity of labeled pathological PCG data by generating synthetic data with a physiologically-disentangled latent space and fine-grained control over 17 distinct conditions. The model demonstrates state-of-the-art performance, achieving high clinical validity and significantly improving rare disease classification when used for data augmentation.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to cardiovascular disease diagnosis by providing a crucial solution to the scarcity of labeled pathological Phonocardiogram (PCG) data. By enabling the generation of clinically accurate, controllable, and interpretable synthetic PCGs, H-LDM facilitates robust AI system development, enhances model training, and holds the potential to significantly improve the early and accurate detection of rare and complex cardiac conditions.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the generation of synthetic, clinically accurate, and controllable phonocardiogram (PCG) signals. This synthetic data serves as a data augmentation technique for training and improving AI diagnostic models, especially for rare cardiovascular diseases where real labeled data is scarce. The goal is to enhance the capability and accuracy of AI systems in diagnosing heart conditions, providing interpretability, and addressing data limitations in medical AI.

Key Points

  • H-LDM is a Hierarchical Latent Diffusion Model designed to synthesize clinically accurate and controllable PCG signals from structured clinical metadata.
  • It employs a multi-scale Variational Autoencoder (VAE) to learn a physiologically-disentangled latent space, effectively separating rhythm, heart sounds, and murmurs.
  • The model features a hierarchical text-to-biosignal pipeline, enabling fine-grained control over PCG generation based on 17 distinct clinical conditions derived from metadata.
  • An interpretable diffusion process is guided by a novel Medical Attention module, enhancing the clinical utility and transparency of the generated signals.
  • Experiments on the PhysioNet CirCor dataset demonstrated state-of-the-art performance, including a Fr√©chet Audio Distance (FAD) of 9.7 and a 92% attribute disentanglement score.
  • The synthetic PCGs achieved 87.1% clinical validity, as confirmed by cardiologists, highlighting their diagnostic relevance.
  • Augmenting existing diagnostic AI models with H-LDM's synthetic data improved rare disease classification accuracy by 11.3%, addressing a critical challenge in cardiac diagnostics.

Methodology

H-LDM is a Hierarchical Latent Diffusion Model comprising three key components: (1) a multi-scale Variational Autoencoder (VAE) that learns a physiologically-disentangled latent space to separate rhythm, heart sounds, and murmurs; (2) a hierarchical text-to-biosignal pipeline that leverages rich clinical metadata for fine-grained control over 17 distinct PCG conditions; and (3) an interpretable diffusion process guided by a novel Medical Attention module to enhance the model's transparency and clinical utility.

Critically, augmenting diagnostic models with H-LDM's synthetic data resulted in an 11.3% improvement in the accuracy of rare disease classification.

Its ability to generate controllable and clinically valid PCG data can accelerate the development of more accurate and robust AI diagnostic tools through enhanced data augmentation. This could lead to earlier and more precise diagnoses, improved patient stratification, and better training resources for clinicians, ultimately improving patient outcomes.

Abstract

Phonocardiogram (PCG) analysis is vital for cardiovascular disease diagnosis, yet the scarcity of labeled pathological data hinders the capability of AI systems. To bridge this, we introduce H-LDM, a Hierarchical Latent Diffusion Model for generating clinically accurate and controllable PCG signals from structured metadata. Our approach features: (1) a multi-scale VAE that learns a physiologically-disentangled latent space, separating rhythm, heart sounds, and murmurs; (2) a hierarchical text-to-biosignal pipeline that leverages rich clinical metadata for fine-grained control over 17 distinct conditions; and (3) an interpretable diffusion process guided by a novel Medical Attention module. Experiments on the PhysioNet CirCor dataset demonstrate state-of-the-art performance, achieving a Fr√©chet Audio Distance of 9.7, a 92% attribute disentanglement score, and 87.1% clinical validity confirmed by cardiologists. Augmenting diagnostic models with our synthetic data improves the accuracy of rare disease classification by 11.3\%. H-LDM establishes a new direction for data augmentation in cardiac diagnostics, bridging data scarcity with interpretable clinical insights.
