Towards Scalable Meta-Learning of near-optimal Interpretable Models via Synthetic Model Generations

Summary

This paper introduces an efficient and scalable method for meta-learning interpretable decision trees by generating large-scale, realistic synthetic pre-training data composed of near-optimal decision trees. By pre-training a MetaTree transformer architecture with this synthetic data, the approach achieves performance comparable to using real-world data or computationally expensive optimal trees, significantly reducing computational costs and enhancing data generation flexibility. This strategy paves the way for scalable and efficient development of interpretable AI models, particularly relevant for high-stakes fields like healthcare.

Methodology

The methodology involves two primary components: (1) **Synthetic Model Generation**: Efficiently sampling and generating large-scale, realistic datasets of 'near-optimal' decision trees, designed to serve as pre-training data. (2) **Meta-learning with Transformer Architecture**: Utilizing a MetaTree transformer architecture, which is subsequently pre-trained on the generated synthetic data. Performance is then evaluated by comparing against models pre-trained on real-world data or derived from computationally expensive optimal decision trees.

Abstract

Decision trees are widely used in high-stakes fields like finance and healthcare due to their interpretability. This work introduces an efficient, scalable method for generating synthetic pre-training data to enable meta-learning of decision trees. Our approach samples near-optimal decision trees synthetically, creating large-scale, realistic datasets. Using the MetaTree transformer architecture, we demonstrate that this method achieves performance comparable to pre-training on real-world data or with computationally expensive optimal decision trees. This strategy significantly reduces computational costs, enhances data generation flexibility, and paves the way for scalable and efficient meta-learning of interpretable decision tree models.
