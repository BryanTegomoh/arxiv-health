3D-Guided Scalable Flow Matching for Generating Volumetric Tissue Spatial Transcriptomics from Serial Histology

Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Mohammad Vali Sanian, Arshia Hemmat, Amirhossein Vahidi, Jonas Maaskola, Jimmy Tsz Hang Lee, Stanislaw Makarchuk, Yeliz Demirci, Nana-Jane Chipampe, Omer Bayraktar, Lassi Paavolainen, Mohammad Lotfollahi

Summary

HoloTea is a novel 3D-aware flow-matching framework that accurately infers spot-level gene expression to generate volumetric tissue spatial transcriptomics from serial H&E histology. It overcomes limitations of existing methods by explicitly using cross-section information through a ControlNet and a 3D-consistent prior, demonstrating improved accuracy and generalization across diverse tissue types and resolutions.

Medical Relevance

This technology is critical for understanding human biology and disease by providing detailed 3D maps of gene expression within tissues, enabling a holistic view of cellular organization and molecular changes relevant to pathology and therapeutics.

Key Points

  • HoloTea is a 3D-aware flow-matching framework designed to impute spot-level gene expression from H&E histology, addressing the limitations of 2D methods that ignore 3D structure and existing 3D-aware methods that lack generativity and scalability.
  • A core innovation is the retrieval of morphologically corresponding spots on neighboring slides in a shared feature space, fusing this cross-section context into a lightweight ControlNet to ensure anatomical continuity during generation.
  • The framework introduces a 3D-consistent prior for flow matching, which combines a learned zero-inflated negative binomial (ZINB) prior with a spatial-empirical prior constructed from neighboring sections, specifically tailored to capture the count nature of gene expression data.
  • HoloTea incorporates a global attention block that enables its 3D H&E scaling linearly with the number of spots, making the framework scalable for training and inference on large volumetric spatial transcriptomics datasets.
  • The method consistently achieves improved 3D expression accuracy and generalization compared to both 2D and other 3D baseline approaches across three distinct spatial transcriptomics datasets spanning different tissue types and resolutions.
  • This technology provides a robust and scalable solution for creating accurate 3D virtual tissues, offering a holistic understanding of tissue organization and advancing biological and disease research.

Methodology

HoloTea utilizes a 3D-aware flow-matching framework that imputes spot-level gene expression. It identifies morphologically corresponding spots across serial H&E sections in a shared feature space. This cross-section contextual information is then fused into a lightweight ControlNet to guide the generative process and maintain anatomical continuity. To accurately model count data, it employs a 3D-consistent prior, combining a learned Zero-Inflated Negative Binomial (ZINB) prior with a spatial-empirical prior derived from adjacent sections. A global attention block ensures the method scales efficiently with large 3D datasets.

Key Findings

HoloTea consistently demonstrated superior 3D expression accuracy and enhanced generalization capabilities compared to both 2D and existing 3D baseline methods. This performance improvement was validated across diverse spatial transcriptomics datasets, encompassing various tissue types and resolutions.

Abstract

A scalable and robust 3D tissue transcriptomics profile can enable a holistic understanding of tissue organization and provide deeper insights into human biology and disease. Most predictive algorithms that infer ST directly from histology treat each section independently and ignore 3D structure, while existing 3D-aware approaches are not generative and do not scale well. We present Holographic Tissue Expression Inpainting and Analysis (HoloTea), a 3D-aware flow-matching framework that imputes spot-level gene expression from H&E while explicitly using information from adjacent sections. Our key idea is to retrieve morphologically corresponding spots on neighboring slides in a shared feature space and fuse this cross section context into a lightweight ControlNet, allowing conditioning to follow anatomical continuity. To better capture the count nature of the data, we introduce a 3D-consistent prior for flow matching that combines a learned zero-inflated negative binomial (ZINB) prior with a spatial-empirical prior constructed from neighboring sections. A global attention block introduces 3D H&E scaling linearly with the number of spots in the slide, enabling training and inference on large 3D ST datasets. Across three spatial transcriptomics datasets spanning different tissue types and resolutions, HoloTea consistently improves 3D expression accuracy and generalization compared to 2D and 3D baselines. We envision HoloTea advancing the creation of accurate 3D virtual tissues, ultimately accelerating biomarker discovery and deepening our understanding of disease.
