3D-Guided Scalable Flow Matching for Generating Volumetric Tissue Spatial Transcriptomics from Serial Histology

Summary

HoloTea is a novel 3D-aware flow-matching framework designed to generate volumetric tissue spatial transcriptomics by imputing spot-level gene expression from serial H&E histology. It leverages information from adjacent sections using a ControlNet and a 3D-consistent prior, overcoming limitations of existing 2D and 3D methods to achieve scalable and accurate 3D expression profiles. This approach significantly improves 3D expression accuracy and generalization across diverse tissue types and resolutions.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine as it enables the creation of detailed, scalable, and accurate 3D maps of gene expression within tissues from standard histology slides, which is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of human biology, disease progression, and therapeutic responses.

AI Health Application

HoloTea is an AI (flow-matching framework with ControlNet) application that imputes spot-level gene expression from H&E histology images. This enables the creation of 3D virtual tissues with detailed molecular information, which can be used to accelerate biomarker discovery, improve disease understanding, and potentially aid in diagnostic or prognostic predictions by integrating genomic and morphological data in a 3D context.

Key Points

  • HoloTea is a 3D-aware flow-matching framework that imputes spot-level gene expression from H&E histology, explicitly using contextual information from adjacent sections.
  • It retrieves morphologically corresponding spots on neighboring slides in a shared feature space and fuses this cross-section context into a lightweight ControlNet to ensure anatomical continuity.
  • A novel 3D-consistent prior for flow matching is introduced, combining a learned zero-inflated negative binomial (ZINB) prior with a spatial-empirical prior constructed from neighboring sections, specifically designed to capture the count nature of gene expression data.
  • The framework incorporates a global attention block that enables 3D H&E scaling linearly with the number of spots, facilitating training and inference on large-scale 3D spatial transcriptomics datasets.
  • HoloTea consistently demonstrated improved 3D expression accuracy and generalization across three different spatial transcriptomics datasets spanning various tissue types and resolutions, outperforming conventional 2D and existing 3D baseline methods.
  • The scalability and robustness of HoloTea facilitate the creation of accurate 3D virtual tissues, offering a holistic understanding of tissue organization and disease mechanisms.

Methodology

HoloTea employs a 3D-aware flow-matching framework to infer gene expression. It uses a shared feature space to identify and retrieve morphologically corresponding spots between adjacent H&E sections. This cross-section context is then integrated into a lightweight ControlNet for conditioning, ensuring anatomical continuity. To model the count nature of gene expression data, it incorporates a 3D-consistent prior combining a learned Zero-Inflated Negative Binomial (ZINB) prior with a spatial-empirical prior derived from neighboring sections. Scalability for large 3D datasets is achieved through a global attention block that linearly scales with the number of spots.

Key Findings

HoloTea consistently improved 3D expression accuracy and generalization across three diverse spatial transcriptomics datasets, which included different tissue types and resolutions. It demonstrably outperformed both 2D and other 3D baseline methods.

Abstract

A scalable and robust 3D tissue transcriptomics profile can enable a holistic understanding of tissue organization and provide deeper insights into human biology and disease. Most predictive algorithms that infer ST directly from histology treat each section independently and ignore 3D structure, while existing 3D-aware approaches are not generative and do not scale well. We present Holographic Tissue Expression Inpainting and Analysis (HoloTea), a 3D-aware flow-matching framework that imputes spot-level gene expression from H&E while explicitly using information from adjacent sections. Our key idea is to retrieve morphologically corresponding spots on neighboring slides in a shared feature space and fuse this cross section context into a lightweight ControlNet, allowing conditioning to follow anatomical continuity. To better capture the count nature of the data, we introduce a 3D-consistent prior for flow matching that combines a learned zero-inflated negative binomial (ZINB) prior with a spatial-empirical prior constructed from neighboring sections. A global attention block introduces 3D H&E scaling linearly with the number of spots in the slide, enabling training and inference on large 3D ST datasets. Across three spatial transcriptomics datasets spanning different tissue types and resolutions, HoloTea consistently improves 3D expression accuracy and generalization compared to 2D and 3D baselines. We envision HoloTea advancing the creation of accurate 3D virtual tissues, ultimately accelerating biomarker discovery and deepening our understanding of disease.
