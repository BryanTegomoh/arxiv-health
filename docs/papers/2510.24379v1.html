A Luminance-Aware Multi-Scale Network for Polarization Image Fusion with a Multi-Scene Dataset

arXiv ID: 2510.24379v1
Published: 2025-10-28
Authors: Zhuangfan Huang, Xiaosong Li, Gao Wang, Tao Ye, Haishu Tan, Huafeng Li
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a Luminance-aware Multi-Scale Network (MLSN) for polarization image fusion, effectively combining S0 and DOLP images to reveal detailed surface roughness and material properties. The network innovatively addresses challenges like complex luminance environments and inherent contrast differences through specialized encoder, bottleneck, and decoder modules. Alongside a new diverse multi-scene polarization dataset (MSP), MLSN achieves state-of-the-art performance, with significant implications for applications such as tissue pathology analysis.

Medical Relevance

This research is directly relevant to medicine, specifically 'tissue pathology analysis.' By providing enhanced visualization of tissue microstructure, surface roughness, and material properties through improved polarization image fusion, it offers a powerful tool for more accurate and early disease diagnosis and characterization.

AI Health Application

The proposed luminance-aware multi-scale network is an AI model designed for polarization image fusion. When applied to tissue pathology analysis, this AI can enhance the visualization and interpretation of tissue characteristics, such as surface roughness and material properties, which are crucial for disease diagnosis and understanding at a microscopic level. This could lead to improved accuracy and efficiency in medical diagnostics by providing richer, more discriminative image features to clinicians or other AI systems.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** Integrates complementary information from S0 and DOLP polarization images in complex luminance environments, overcoming inherent contrast differences and data scarcity.
  • **Novel Network Architecture (MLSN):** Proposes a Luminance-aware Multi-Scale Network with a U-Net-like structure, featuring specialized modules for robust fusion.
  • **Encoder Innovation:** Incorporates a 'brightness-branch' to generate a multi-scale spatial weight matrix, dynamically injecting luminance information and solving inherent contrast differences in polarized images.
  • **Bottleneck Layer Design:** Implements a global-local feature fusion mechanism using windowed self-attention and residual linking to balance global context and local details effectively.
  • **Decoder Enhancement:** Includes a Brightness-Enhancement module that performs nonlinear luminance correction, mapping luminance distribution to texture features for improved adaptability to diverse lighting conditions.
  • **New Dataset (MSP):** Introduces MSP, a high-quality dataset of 1000 polarized image pairs covering 17 indoor and outdoor complex lighting scenes, including four-direction polarization raw maps, addressing the scarcity of such data.
  • **Superior Performance:** MLSN significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods on MSP, PIF, and GAND datasets, with MS-SSIM and SD metrics showing substantial improvements (e.g., 8.57% to 63.53% higher than average values of other methods).

Methodology

The methodology involves a Luminance-aware Multi-Scale Network (MLSN) with an encoder-bottleneck-decoder architecture. The encoder features a brightness-branch that generates a multi-scale spatial weight matrix, dynamically injecting luminance into feature maps to handle contrast. The bottleneck layer employs a global-local feature fusion mechanism using windowed self-attention with residual linking to balance context and details. The decoder includes a Brightness-Enhancement module for nonlinear luminance correction by mapping luminance distribution to texture features. The network was trained and evaluated on a newly introduced Multi-Scene Polarization (MSP) dataset, comprising 1000 pairs of polarized images (S0, DOLP, and four-direction raw maps) captured in 17 diverse lighting conditions, as well as existing PIF and GAND datasets.

Key Findings

The MLSN demonstrated superior performance over existing state-of-the-art methods in polarization image fusion, evidenced by both subjective and objective evaluations on the MSP, PIF, and GAND datasets. Quantitatively, the network achieved significant improvements in MS-SSIM and SD metrics, with values higher than the average of other methods by 8.57%, 60.64%, 10.26%, 63.53%, 22.21%, and 54.31% respectively. These findings indicate that MLSN effectively integrates complementary information from S0 and DOLP images, robustly adapts to complex lighting conditions, and successfully addresses inherent contrast differences, leading to more detailed and accurate fusion results.

Abstract

Polarization image fusion combines S0 and DOLP images to reveal surface roughness and material properties through complementary texture features, which has important applications in camouflage recognition, tissue pathology analysis, surface defect detection and other fields. To intergrate coL-Splementary information from different polarized images in complex luminance environment, we propose a luminance-aware multi-scale network (MLSN). In the encoder stage, we propose a multi-scale spatial weight matrix through a brightness-branch , which dynamically weighted inject the luminance into the feature maps, solving the problem of inherent contrast difference in polarized images. The global-local feature fusion mechanism is designed at the bottleneck layer to perform windowed self-attention computation, to balance the global context and local details through residual linking in the feature dimension restructuring stage. In the decoder stage, to further improve the adaptability to complex lighting, we propose a Brightness-Enhancement module, establishing the mapping relationship between luminance distribution and texture features, realizing the nonlinear luminance correction of the fusion result. We also present MSP, an 1000 pairs of polarized images that covers 17 types of indoor and outdoor complex lighting scenes. MSP provides four-direction polarization raw maps, solving the scarcity of high-quality datasets in polarization image fusion. Extensive experiment on MSP, PIF and GAND datasets verify that the proposed MLSN outperms the state-of-the-art methods in subjective and objective evaluations, and the MS-SSIM and SD metircs are higher than the average values of other methods by 8.57%, 60.64%, 10.26%, 63.53%, 22.21%, and 54.31%, respectively. The source code and dataset is avalable at https://github.com/1hzf/MLS-UNet.
