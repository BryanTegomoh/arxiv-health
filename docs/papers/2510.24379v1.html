A Luminance-Aware Multi-Scale Network for Polarization Image Fusion with a Multi-Scene Dataset

arXiv ID: 2510.24379v1
Published: 2025-10-28
Authors: Zhuangfan Huang, Xiaosong Li, Gao Wang, Tao Ye, Haishu Tan, Huafeng Li
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces MLSN, a luminance-aware multi-scale network designed for polarization image fusion, effectively combining S0 and DOLP images to reveal enhanced texture features. The network addresses challenges of complex luminance environments and inherent contrast differences through novel encoder, bottleneck, and decoder modules. It also presents MSP, a new high-quality dataset, and demonstrates that MLSN significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods in both subjective and objective evaluations across multiple datasets.

By providing clearer, more robust fused images, it can improve the visualization of subtle cellular and architectural changes, crucial for accurate disease diagnosis and characterization.

Key Points

  • **Problem Solved**: Addresses the challenge of fusing S0 and DOLP polarization images to extract complementary texture features (surface roughness, material properties) in complex luminance environments for applications like tissue pathology analysis.
  • **Novel Architecture (MLSN)**: Proposes a Luminance-aware Multi-Scale Network (MLSN) with specialized components to handle luminance variations and contrast differences in polarized images.
  • **Encoder Innovation**: Features a 'brightness-branch' in the encoder to generate a multi-scale spatial weight matrix, dynamically injecting luminance into feature maps to resolve inherent contrast discrepancies.
  • **Bottleneck Innovation**: Incorporates a global-local feature fusion mechanism using windowed self-attention and residual linking, balancing global context with local details.
  • **Decoder Innovation**: Includes a Brightness-Enhancement module in the decoder, performing nonlinear luminance correction by mapping luminance distribution to texture features, improving adaptability to diverse lighting.
  • **New Dataset (MSP)**: Introduces MSP, a dataset of 1000 pairs of polarized images covering 17 complex indoor/outdoor lighting scenes, providing four-direction polarization raw maps, addressing data scarcity in the field.
  • **Superior Performance**: MLSN significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods across MSP, PIF, and GAND datasets, with MS-SSIM and SD metrics showing substantial improvements (e.g., MS-SSIM up to 22.21% higher, SD up to 63.53% higher than average comparator values).

Methodology

The authors developed a Luminance-aware Multi-Scale Network (MLSN). The encoder stage integrates a 'brightness-branch' to create a multi-scale spatial weight matrix, which dynamically injects luminance information into feature maps to mitigate inherent contrast differences. The bottleneck layer utilizes a global-local feature fusion mechanism, employing windowed self-attention and residual connections for balancing global context and local details. The decoder stage features a Brightness-Enhancement module for nonlinear luminance correction by establishing a mapping between luminance distribution and texture features, thereby improving adaptability to varied lighting. They also constructed MSP, a dataset of 1000 polarized image pairs across 17 diverse lighting scenarios, used for training and extensive evaluation.

Key Findings

The MLSN demonstrates superior performance in polarization image fusion, effectively integrating S0 and DOLP information under complex luminance conditions. It significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods in both subjective visual quality and objective metrics (MS-SSIM and SD), achieving improvements of up to 22.21% for MS-SSIM and 63.53% for SD compared to the average of other methods. The proposed novel modules within MLSN, especially the luminance-aware components, are critical for robust fusion. The MSP dataset is also identified as a valuable contribution, addressing the scarcity of high-quality polarization datasets.

Abstract

Polarization image fusion combines S0 and DOLP images to reveal surface roughness and material properties through complementary texture features, which has important applications in camouflage recognition, tissue pathology analysis, surface defect detection and other fields. To intergrate coL-Splementary information from different polarized images in complex luminance environment, we propose a luminance-aware multi-scale network (MLSN). In the encoder stage, we propose a multi-scale spatial weight matrix through a brightness-branch , which dynamically weighted inject the luminance into the feature maps, solving the problem of inherent contrast difference in polarized images. The global-local feature fusion mechanism is designed at the bottleneck layer to perform windowed self-attention computation, to balance the global context and local details through residual linking in the feature dimension restructuring stage. In the decoder stage, to further improve the adaptability to complex lighting, we propose a Brightness-Enhancement module, establishing the mapping relationship between luminance distribution and texture features, realizing the nonlinear luminance correction of the fusion result. We also present MSP, an 1000 pairs of polarized images that covers 17 types of indoor and outdoor complex lighting scenes. MSP provides four-direction polarization raw maps, solving the scarcity of high-quality datasets in polarization image fusion. Extensive experiment on MSP, PIF and GAND datasets verify that the proposed MLSN outperms the state-of-the-art methods in subjective and objective evaluations, and the MS-SSIM and SD metircs are higher than the average values of other methods by 8.57%, 60.64%, 10.26%, 63.53%, 22.21%, and 54.31%, respectively. The source code and dataset is avalable at https://github.com/1hzf/MLS-UNet.
