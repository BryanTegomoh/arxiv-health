Summary

KOM is a novel multi-agent AI system designed for the precision management of Knee Osteoarthritis (KOA), aiming to automate evaluation, risk prediction, and personalized treatment prescription. It demonstrated superior performance over general-purpose large language models in imaging analysis and prescription generation, and in a simulation study, reduced diagnostic and planning time by 38.5% while improving treatment quality when collaborating with clinicians.

Medical Relevance

This system directly addresses the significant global burden of KOA by offering a scalable, efficient, and personalized approach to disease management, potentially enhancing access to high-quality care, improving patient outcomes, and alleviating pressure on medical resources, especially in underserved areas.

AI Health Application

KOM is a multi-agent AI system developed to automate the evaluation, risk prediction, and treatment prescription for Knee Osteoarthritis (KOA). It assists clinicians by generating tailored management plans based on patient profiles, disease status, and risk factors, performs imaging analysis, and aims to improve diagnostic/planning efficiency and treatment quality in the management of this chronic condition. Its modular architecture is also proposed for application to other chronic conditions.

Key Points

  • Knee Osteoarthritis (KOA) is a prevalent chronic condition affecting over 600 million globally, requiring resource-intensive, personalized interventions often challenging to implement in resource-limited settings.
  • KOM is a multi-agent artificial intelligence system developed to automate KOA evaluation, risk prediction, and the generation of tailored treatment prescriptions.
  • The system supports clinicians by creating personalized management plans based on individual patient profiles, disease status, risk factors, and contraindications.
  • In benchmark experiments, KOM exhibited superior performance compared to several general-purpose large language models in both imaging analysis and prescription generation tasks.
  • A randomized three-arm simulation study showed that collaboration between KOM and clinicians reduced total diagnostic and planning time by 38.5% relative to independent approaches.
  • This clinician-KOM collaborative model also resulted in improved treatment quality when compared to clinicians or KOM acting independently.
  • The modular architecture of KOM offers insights for developing similar AI-assisted management systems for other chronic conditions beyond KOA.

Methodology

The development of KOM as a multi-agent AI system involved two primary evaluation methodologies: 1) **Benchmark Experiments:** Comparing KOM's proficiency in imaging analysis and prescription generation against several general-purpose large language models. 2) **Randomized Three-Arm Simulation Study:** Assessing diagnostic and planning time, as well as treatment quality, across three groups: clinicians collaborating with KOM, KOM operating independently, and clinicians operating independently.

Key Findings

KOM demonstrated superior performance in imaging analysis and prescription generation compared to general-purpose large language models. Critically, a collaborative workflow between KOM and clinicians resulted in a 38.5% reduction in total diagnostic and planning time and led to improved treatment quality compared to either entity working in isolation.

Clinical Impact

KOM has the potential to significantly enhance clinical workflows by automating complex tasks like KOA evaluation, risk prediction, and treatment plan generation, leading to more efficient and consistent care. Its ability to provide personalized plans and reduce clinician workload could improve patient access to high-quality care, standardize management, and ultimately improve patient functional outcomes and quality of life, particularly beneficial in resource-constrained settings.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly detail limitations. However, potential considerations could include the findings being derived from a 'simulation study' rather than real-world clinical deployment, a lack of data on long-term patient outcomes or satisfaction, and the specific context of the general-purpose LLMs used for comparison which may not have been specialized for medical tasks.

Future Directions

The modular architecture of KOM suggests its adaptability for developing AI-assisted management systems for other chronic conditions. Future research will likely focus on real-world clinical validation, integration into existing Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems, and evaluating long-term patient outcomes and cost-effectiveness in diverse healthcare settings.

Abstract

Knee osteoarthritis (KOA) affects more than 600 million individuals globally and is associated with significant pain, functional impairment, and disability. While personalized multidisciplinary interventions have the potential to slow disease progression and enhance quality of life, they typically require substantial medical resources and expertise, making them difficult to implement in resource-limited settings. To address this challenge, we developed KOM, a multi-agent system designed to automate KOA evaluation, risk prediction, and treatment prescription. This system assists clinicians in performing essential tasks across the KOA care pathway and supports the generation of tailored management plans based on individual patient profiles, disease status, risk factors, and contraindications. In benchmark experiments, KOM demonstrated superior performance compared to several general-purpose large language models in imaging analysis and prescription generation. A randomized three-arm simulation study further revealed that collaboration between KOM and clinicians reduced total diagnostic and planning time by 38.5% and resulted in improved treatment quality compared to each approach used independently. These findings indicate that KOM could help facilitate automated KOA management and, when integrated into clinical workflows, has the potential to enhance care efficiency. The modular architecture of KOM may also offer valuable insights for developing AI-assisted management systems for other chronic conditions.
