SegRap2025: A Benchmark of Gross Tumor Volume and Lymph Node Clinical Target Volume Segmentation for Radiotherapy Planning of Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma

Summary

SegRap2025 establishes a comprehensive multi-center, multi-modality benchmark for the automated segmentation of Gross Tumor Volume (GTV) and Lymph Node Clinical Target Volume (LN CTV) in Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) for radiotherapy planning. This challenge evaluates the generalizability and robustness of AI segmentation models across diverse imaging centers and CT modalities. The results from ten participating teams provide valuable insights into the current capabilities and challenges of developing clinically applicable automated radiotherapy planning systems.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and consistent delineation of GTV and LN CTV on CT scans is a cornerstone of precise radiotherapy planning for Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma, directly influencing treatment efficacy and minimizing damage to healthy tissues. This research is crucial for developing automated tools that can improve the consistency, efficiency, and quality of this critical step in cancer treatment, ultimately benefiting patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

The paper describes a benchmark challenge for developing AI models (segmentation models) that can automatically and accurately delineate Gross Tumor Volume (GTV) and Lymph Node Clinical Target Volume (LN CTV) from CT scans. This automation is crucial for precise radiotherapy planning in Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC), aiming to enhance the efficiency, consistency, and accuracy of clinical radiotherapy workflows and improve patient care.

Key Points

  • SegRap2025 expands upon SegRap2023, focusing on enhancing the generalizability and robustness of segmentation models for NPC radiotherapy targets across different imaging centers and CT modalities.
  • The benchmark consists of two tasks: Task01 addresses GTV segmentation, utilizing paired non-contrast (ncCT) and contrast-enhanced CT (ceCT) scans from SegRap2023, with an external testing set to evaluate cross-center generalization.
  • Task02 focuses on LN CTV segmentation, employing multi-center training data and an unseen external testing set comprising paired CT, ceCT-only, or ncCT-only cases to assess both cross-center and cross-modality robustness.
  • For GTV segmentation (Task01), top-performing models achieved an average Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC) of 74.61% on the internal testing cohort and 56.79% on the external testing cohort.
  • For LN CTV segmentation (Task02), the highest average DSC values reached 60.24% on paired CT, 60.50% on ceCT-only, and 57.23% on ncCT-only subsets of the external testing data.
  • The challenge involved comprehensive analysis of solutions submitted by ten participating teams, contributing to a large-scale dataset for research.
  • SegRap2025 serves as a critical benchmark to drive advancements towards automated, reliable, and generalized radiotherapy target segmentation systems for NPC.

Methodology

The SegRap2025 challenge is structured into two segmentation tasks evaluated on a multi-center, multi-modality CT dataset. Task01 focuses on GTV segmentation using paired ncCT/ceCT from the SegRap2023 dataset, with an additional external testing set specifically designed for cross-center generalization. Task02 addresses LN CTV segmentation, leveraging multi-center training data and an unseen external testing set that includes cases with paired CT scans or single modalities (ceCT-only or ncCT-only) to evaluate both cross-center and cross-modality robustness. Performance of the ten participating teams' solutions was primarily measured using the Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC).

Key Findings

Top models in GTV segmentation (Task01) achieved average DSCs of 74.61% on internal data and notably dropped to 56.79% on the external testing set, highlighting generalization challenges. For LN CTV segmentation (Task02), the highest average DSCs obtained were 60.24% for paired CT, 60.50% for ceCT-only, and 57.23% for ncCT-only subsets, indicating similar performance across different modalities for this target volume.

Clinical Impact

The insights gained from SegRap2025 are instrumental for developing robust and generalized automated segmentation tools that can be reliably deployed across various clinical settings and imaging protocols. Such automation can significantly reduce the manual workload of clinicians, decrease inter-operator variability in contouring, and enhance the precision and consistency of radiotherapy planning for NPC, potentially leading to improved treatment outcomes and patient quality of life.

Limitations

While not explicitly stated as limitations, the observed performance drop for GTV segmentation from internal (74.61%) to external (56.79%) cohorts, and the overall DSC values (~60%) for LN CTV segmentation, implicitly suggest that achieving high generalizability and robustness across diverse centers and imaging modalities remains a significant challenge for current automated models, indicating areas for further research and improvement before widespread clinical applicability.

Future Directions

The benchmark aims to provide valuable insights "toward clinically applicable automated radiotherapy planning systems." This indicates future research will focus on improving the generalizability and robustness of segmentation models to achieve the high level of accuracy and reliability necessary for routine clinical deployment. Addressing the performance gap observed on external and multi-modality datasets will be a key area for future development.

Abstract

Accurate delineation of Gross Tumor Volume (GTV), Lymph Node Clinical Target Volume (LN CTV), and Organ-at-Risk (OAR) from Computed Tomography (CT) scans is essential for precise radiotherapy planning in Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC). Building upon SegRap2023, which focused on OAR and GTV segmentation using single-center paired non-contrast CT (ncCT) and contrast-enhanced CT (ceCT) scans, the SegRap2025 challenge aims to enhance the generalizability and robustness of segmentation models across imaging centers and modalities. SegRap2025 comprises two tasks: Task01 addresses GTV segmentation using paired CT from the SegRap2023 dataset, with an additional external testing set to evaluate cross-center generalization, and Task02 focuses on LN CTV segmentation using multi-center training data and an unseen external testing set, where each case contains paired CT scans or a single modality, emphasizing both cross-center and cross-modality robustness. This paper presents the challenge setup and provides a comprehensive analysis of the solutions submitted by ten participating teams. For GTV segmentation task, the top-performing models achieved average Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC) of 74.61% and 56.79% on the internal and external testing cohorts, respectively. For LN CTV segmentation task, the highest average DSC values reached 60.24%, 60.50%, and 57.23% on paired CT, ceCT-only, and ncCT-only subsets, respectively. SegRap2025 establishes a large-scale multi-center, multi-modality benchmark for evaluating the generalization and robustness in radiotherapy target segmentation, providing valuable insights toward clinically applicable automated radiotherapy planning systems. The benchmark is available at: https://hilab-git.github.io/SegRap2025_Challenge.
