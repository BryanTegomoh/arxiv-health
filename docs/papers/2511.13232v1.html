MRIQT: Physics-Aware Diffusion Model for Image Quality Transfer in Neonatal Ultra-Low-Field MRI

Summary

This paper introduces MRIQT, a 3D conditional diffusion model designed for image quality transfer (IQT) to enhance ultra-low-field (uLF) neonatal MRI scans to the quality of high-field (HF) MRI. By employing physics-aware K-space degradation, v-prediction with classifier-free guidance, and an SNR-weighted perceptual loss, MRIQT significantly improves image quality. The model outperforms existing baselines and enables reliable brain assessment in neonates, as validated by both quantitative metrics and physician ratings.

Medical Relevance

Improving uLF-MRI quality is crucial for accessible and safe neuroimaging in neonatal care, especially for premature or unstable infants who cannot be transported to high-field MRI scanners. This technology enhances diagnostic capabilities at the point-of-care, potentially leading to earlier and more accurate interventions for neurological conditions.

Key Points

  • Addresses the challenge of low signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) and poor diagnostic quality in portable ultra-low-field MRI (uLF-MRI) for neonatal care, contrasting it with high-field (HF) MRI.
  • Proposes MRIQT, a novel 3D conditional diffusion framework for robust image quality transfer (IQT) from uLF to HF MRI, specifically tailored for neonatal brain imaging.
  • Incorporates realistic K-space degradation for physics-consistent uLF simulation, enhancing the model's ability to learn accurate mappings by mimicking real-world data acquisition physics.
  • Utilizes v-prediction with classifier-free guidance for stable image-to-image generation and an SNR-weighted 3D perceptual loss to ensure high anatomical fidelity and preserve critical features.
  • Employs a volumetric attention-UNet architecture that denoises a noised uLF input, conditioned on the original scan, to achieve structure-preserving translation.
  • Demonstrates superior performance, achieving a 15.3% improvement in PSNR over recent GAN and CNN baselines and a 1.78% gain over the state-of-the-art methods.
  • Clinically validated by physicians, who rated 85% of MRIQT's enhanced outputs as 'good quality' with clear pathology present, underscoring its potential for practical diagnostic utility.

Methodology

MRIQT is a 3D conditional diffusion model built on a volumetric attention-UNet architecture. Its methodology includes generating training data via realistic K-space degradation for physics-consistent uLF simulation, employing v-prediction with classifier-free guidance for stable image-to-image generation, and using an SNR-weighted 3D perceptual loss to ensure anatomical fidelity. The model denoises a noised uLF input, conditioned on the original uLF scan, to translate it to HF-like quality.

Key Findings

MRIQT significantly enhances uLF-MRI quality, yielding a 15.3% increase in PSNR over baseline GAN and CNN models, and a 1.78% improvement beyond current state-of-the-art methods. Crucially, physician assessment confirmed the high diagnostic utility of the enhanced images, with 85% rated as good quality and clear pathology present, validating its clinical applicability.

Clinical Impact

This research has the potential to revolutionize neonatal neuroimaging by providing high-fidelity, diagnostic-quality brain scans directly at the bedside using portable uLF-MRI. This could significantly reduce the need for risky infant transport to specialized HF-MRI facilities, enabling earlier diagnosis, monitoring, and potentially more timely interventions for vulnerable neonates with neurological conditions, thereby improving patient outcomes and accessibility to advanced care.

Abstract

Portable ultra-low-field MRI (uLF-MRI, 0.064 T) offers accessible neuroimaging for neonatal care but suffers from low signal-to-noise ratio and poor diagnostic quality compared to high-field (HF) MRI. We propose MRIQT, a 3D conditional diffusion framework for image quality transfer (IQT) from uLF to HF MRI. MRIQT combines realistic K-space degradation for physics-consistent uLF simulation, v-prediction with classifier-free guidance for stable image-to-image generation, and an SNR-weighted 3D perceptual loss for anatomical fidelity. The model denoises from a noised uLF input conditioned on the same scan, leveraging volumetric attention-UNet architecture for structure-preserving translation. Trained on a neonatal cohort with diverse pathologies, MRIQT surpasses recent GAN and CNN baselines in PSNR 15.3% with 1.78% over the state of the art, while physicians rated 85% of its outputs as good quality with clear pathology present. MRIQT enables high-fidelity, diffusion-based enhancement of portable ultra-low-field (uLF) MRI for deliable neonatal brain assessment.
