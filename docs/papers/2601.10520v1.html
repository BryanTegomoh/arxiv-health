Breaking Up with Normatively Monolithic Agency with GRACE: A Reason-Based Neuro-Symbolic Architecture for Safe and Ethical AI Alignment

Summary

This paper introduces GRACE (Governor for Reason-Aligned ContainmEnt), a novel neuro-symbolic reason-based containment architecture designed to ensure ethical alignment of autonomous AI agents. It decouples normative reasoning from instrumental decision-making, allowing for the integration and control of diverse AI systems to ensure their actions are not only effective but also morally permissible, interpretable, and justifiable.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine and health by proposing a robust framework for developing ethically aligned and safe AI agents, especially crucial for applications like therapy assistants where patient well-being, trust, and adherence to ethical guidelines are paramount. It offers a mechanism to ensure AI decisions in healthcare are morally sound, interpretable by clinicians, and accountable to patients.

AI Health Application

The paper proposes a neuro-symbolic architecture (GRACE) for safe and ethical AI alignment, demonstrating its utility on an LLM therapy assistant. This application aims to ensure that AI-driven therapeutic interventions are not only effective but also adhere to ethical norms, are interpretable, and justifiable, thereby making AI safer for use in mental health and patient care.

  • It decouples normative reasoning from instrumental decision-making, capable of containing virtually any AI agent design.
  • The architecture comprises three modules: a Moral Module (MM) for deontic logic-based reasoning on permissible macro actions; a Decision-Making Module (DMM) encapsulating the target agent for instrumental action selection; and a Guard for monitoring and enforcing moral compliance.
  • The Moral Module employs a reason-based formalism for deontic logic, providing semantic foundations for interpretability, contestability, and justifiability of decisions.
  • Symbolic representation from the MM enriches the DMM's context and supports formal verification and statistical guarantees of alignment enforced by the Guard.

Abstract

As AI agents become increasingly autonomous, widely deployed in consequential contexts, and efficacious in bringing about real-world impacts, ensuring that their decisions are not only instrumentally effective but also normatively aligned has become critical. We introduce a neuro-symbolic reason-based containment architecture, Governor for Reason-Aligned ContainmEnt (GRACE), that decouples normative reasoning from instrumental decision-making and can contain AI agents of virtually any design. GRACE restructures decision-making into three modules: a Moral Module (MM) that determines permissible macro actions via deontic logic-based reasoning; a Decision-Making Module (DMM) that encapsulates the target agent while selecting instrumentally optimal primitive actions in accordance with derived macro actions; and a Guard that monitors and enforces moral compliance. The MM uses a reason-based formalism providing a semantic foundation for deontic logic, enabling interpretability, contestability, and justifiability. Its symbolic representation enriches the DMM's informational context and supports formal verification and statistical guarantees of alignment enforced by the Guard. We demonstrate GRACE on an example of a LLM therapy assistant, showing how it enables stakeholders to understand, contest, and refine agent behavior.
