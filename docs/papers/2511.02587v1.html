The Analysis of Lexical Errors in Machine Translation from English into Romanian

arXiv ID: 2511.02587v1
Published: 2025-11-04
Authors: Angela Stamatie
Categories: cs.CL
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This research meticulously analyzes lexical errors encountered in Machine Translation (MT) from English into Romanian, specifically focusing on texts related to COVID-19 originating from critical health organizations like WHO, Gavi, and patient information leaflets. The study's primary objective is to enhance the quality of Google Translate by identifying and reducing these lexical errors, thereby improving the accuracy and fluency of automatically translated medical information.

Medical Relevance

Accurate translation of official health directives, vaccine information, and patient information leaflets is paramount for public health, patient safety, and effective communication during health crises. Lexical errors in such critical documents can lead to grave misunderstandings regarding dosages, side effects, contraindications, or public health advice, potentially resulting in adverse health outcomes.

AI Health Application

The research directly applies Machine Translation (an AI technology) to critical healthcare and medical texts. By focusing on improving lexical selection and reducing errors in the translation of medical information from sources like WHO and patient leaflets, it aims to enhance the accuracy and reliability of AI-powered translation tools for health communication, ultimately supporting medical information dissemination and patient understanding.

Key Points

  • The research specifically targets lexical errors within Machine Translation, differentiating them from other error types.
  • The study's corpus consists of 230 texts related to COVID-19, sourced from official health bodies (WHO, Gavi) and crucial patient information leaflets (PILs).
  • Google Translate, a widely used multilingual MT system, is the specific tool whose translation performance is being evaluated and targeted for improvement.
  • The overarching goal is to enhance the quality of MT systems by achieving better lexical selection and reducing translation errors in a medical context.
  • The methodology involves a comprehensive error analysis, systematically investigating the nature and frequency of lexical inaccuracies.
  • The research aims to contribute to more accurate and fluent automatic translation, which is particularly vital for health-related official and patient-facing documents.

Methodology

The study employs a comprehensive error analysis methodology, examining 230 texts that have been machine-translated from English into Romanian by Google Translate. The analysis specifically focuses on identifying, categorizing, and scrutinizing lexical errors within these translations of official COVID-19 related health information from sources like WHO, Gavi, and patient information leaflets.

Key Findings

While specific findings are not detailed in the abstract, the research aims to identify prevalent lexical error types and patterns in machine-translated medical texts. The anticipated outcome is to provide actionable insights for improving the lexical selection algorithms and overall error reduction strategies within Machine Translation systems like Google Translate, thereby enhancing the reliability of translated health information.

Clinical Impact

By improving the lexical accuracy of machine-translated medical content, this research has the potential to significantly enhance patient comprehension of vital health information, including medication instructions, side effects, and warning labels, thus mitigating risks and improving medication adherence. For healthcare providers and public health bodies, it facilitates the clearer and more consistent dissemination of critical health guidelines and updates, fostering better informed clinical decisions and public responses.

Abstract

The research explores error analysis in the performance of translating by Machine Translation from English into Romanian, and it focuses on lexical errors found in texts which include official information, provided by the World Health Organization (WHO), the Gavi Organization, by the patient information leaflet (the information about the active ingredients of the vaccines or the medication, the indications, the dosage instructions, the storage instructions, the side effects and warning, etc.). All of these texts are related to Covid-19 and have been translated by Google Translate, a multilingual Machine Translation that was created by Google. In the last decades, Google has actively worked to develop a more accurate and fluent automatic translation system. This research, specifically focused on improving Google Translate, aims to enhance the overall quality of Machine Translation by achieving better lexical selection and by reducing errors. The investigation involves a comprehensive analysis of 230 texts that have been translated from English into Romanian.
