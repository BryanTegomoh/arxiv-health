EHR-R1: A Reasoning-Enhanced Foundational Language Model for Electronic Health Record Analysis

Summary

This paper introduces EHR-R1, a series of reasoning-enhanced foundational language models specifically designed for robust Electronic Health Record (EHR) analysis. It leverages a novel, large-scale instruction dataset (EHR-Ins) generated via a thinking-graph-driven framework and a multi-stage training paradigm to imbue LLMs with extensive EHR domain knowledge and complex reasoning capabilities. EHR-R1 significantly outperforms state-of-the-art commercial and open-source LLMs, including GPT-4o, on a new comprehensive benchmark (EHR-Bench) for diverse EHR tasks.

Medical Relevance

Automated and accurate analysis of complex Electronic Health Records is paramount for improving clinical decision-making, personalizing treatment plans, and enhancing overall healthcare efficiency. This research provides a significantly more capable AI tool for extracting actionable, reasoning-based insights from patient data, directly impacting patient care and clinical workflows.

AI Health Application

The AI application is EHR-R1, a reasoning-enhanced foundational language model tailored for Electronic Health Record (EHR) analysis. It is designed to process complex EHR data to support and enhance clinical decision-making, improve clinical workflows, and enable accurate and robust analysis of patient information. This includes tasks such as information extraction, prediction, and generating insights from medical records, thereby serving as an advanced AI tool within healthcare settings.

Key Points

  • Addresses the limitation of current LLMs in EHR analysis, specifically their narrow task coverage and lack of EHR-oriented reasoning capabilities crucial for clinical decision-making.
  • Introduces EHR-Ins, a large-scale and comprehensive EHR reasoning instruction dataset comprising 300k high-quality reasoning cases and 4M non-reasoning cases across 42 distinct EHR tasks.
  • Proposes a novel thinking-graph-driven framework for generating high-quality EHR reasoning data at scale for the EHR-Ins dataset.
  • Develops EHR-R1, a series of reasoning-enhanced LLMs with up to 72B parameters, specifically tailored for accurate and robust EHR analysis.
  • Employs a multi-stage training paradigm for EHR-R1, including domain adaptation, reasoning enhancement (using EHR-Ins), and reinforcement learning, to systematically acquire EHR domain knowledge and diverse reasoning skills.
  • Establishes EHR-Bench, a new comprehensive benchmark curated from MIMIC-IV, spanning 42 tasks, to rigorously assess both reasoning and prediction capabilities across various EHR scenarios.
  • EHR-R1 consistently outperforms state-of-the-art LLMs (DeepSeek-V3, GPT-4o), surpassing GPT-4o by over 30 points on MIMIC-Bench and achieving a 10% higher zero-shot AUROC on EHRSHOT.

Methodology

The methodology centers on three pillars: first, the creation of EHR-Ins, a large-scale EHR instruction dataset generated using a novel "thinking-graph-driven framework" to ensure high-quality reasoning cases. Second, the development and training of EHR-R1, a series of LLMs (up to 72B parameters), utilizing a "multi-stage training paradigm" that integrates domain adaptation, reasoning enhancement via EHR-Ins, and reinforcement learning. Third, the establishment of EHR-Bench, a comprehensive evaluation benchmark derived from MIMIC-IV, covering 42 diverse EHR tasks to rigorously assess the model's reasoning and predictive performance.

Key Findings

EHR-R1 models demonstrate superior performance compared to leading commercial and open-source LLMs, including GPT-4o and DeepSeek-V3. Specifically, EHR-R1 achieved over 30 points higher than GPT-4o on the MIMIC-Bench evaluation and a 10% higher zero-shot AUROC on the EHRSHOT benchmark, indicating a significant advancement in EHR-oriented reasoning and analytical capabilities.

Clinical Impact

This research provides a more robust, accurate, and reasoning-enhanced AI system for EHR analysis, which can substantially improve clinical decision-making, facilitate more precise diagnoses, enable better patient risk stratification, and support personalized medicine initiatives. It has the potential to automate complex medical data interpretation, thereby reducing clinician workload and enhancing the efficiency and quality of care.

Abstract

Electronic Health Records (EHRs) contain rich yet complex information, and their automated analysis is critical for clinical decision-making. Despite recent advances of large language models (LLMs) in clinical workflows, their ability to analyze EHRs remains limited due to narrow task coverage and lack of EHR-oriented reasoning capabilities. This paper aims to bridge the gap, specifically, we present EHR-Ins, a large-scale, comprehensive EHR reasoning instruction dataset, comprising 300k high-quality reasoning cases and 4M non-reasoning cases across 42 distinct EHR tasks. Its core innovation is a thinking-graph-driven framework that enables to generate high-quality reasoning data at scale. Based on it, we develop EHR-R1, a series of reasoning-enhanced LLMs with up to 72B parameters tailored for EHR analysis. Through a multi-stage training paradigm, including domain adaptation, reasoning enhancement, and reinforcement learning, EHR-R1 systematically acquires domain knowledge and diverse reasoning capabilities, enabling accurate and robust EHR analysis. Lastly, we introduce EHR-Bench, a new benchmark curated from MIMIC-IV, spanning 42 tasks, to comprehensively assess reasoning and prediction across EHR scenarios. In experiments, we show that the resulting EHR-R1 consistently outperforms state-of-the-art commercial and open-source LLMs (including DeepSeek-V3 and GPT-4o), surpassing GPT-4o by over 30 points on MIMIC-Bench and achieving a 10\% higher zero-shot AUROC on EHRSHOT. Collectively, EHR-Ins, EHR-R1, and EHR-Bench have significantly advanced the development for more reliable and clinically relevant EHR analysis.
