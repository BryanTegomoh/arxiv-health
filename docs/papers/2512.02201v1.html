Swivuriso: The South African Next Voices Multilingual Speech Dataset

Summary

This paper introduces Swivuriso, a 3000-hour multilingual speech dataset specifically designed to support and benchmark Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) technologies across seven distinct South African languages. By covering critical domains like agriculture and healthcare, Swivuriso directly addresses significant gaps in existing ASR datasets, aiming to enhance the accessibility and utility of speech technology in these under-resourced languages.

Medical Relevance

This dataset is crucial for medicine/health as it includes a dedicated healthcare domain, enabling the development of ASR systems that can process and understand spoken medical information in multiple South African languages. This has direct implications for improving patient-provider communication, accessing health information, and supporting telemedicine services in linguistically diverse populations.

AI Health Application

The dataset supports the development of ASR models capable of transcribing speech related to healthcare in seven South African languages. This can lead to AI applications such as: automated medical dictation for doctors and nurses, transcription of patient consultations (in-person or telehealth), speech-enabled electronic health record (EHR) systems, voice interfaces for health information access, and assistive technologies for healthcare professionals in multilingual settings.

Key Points

  • Swivuriso is a substantial 3000-hour multilingual speech dataset developed for seven South African languages, targeting ASR development and benchmarking.
  • The dataset specifically includes topics related to healthcare, alongside agriculture and general domains, to ensure practical relevance for various applications.
  • The creation involved careful design principles, strict ethical considerations, and robust data collection procedures to ensure data quality and integrity.
  • It aims to bridge significant existing gaps in speech datasets for low-resource languages, fostering ASR innovation in a multilingual South African context.
  • The paper presents baseline ASR model training and finetuning results, offering initial performance metrics using Swivuriso data and comparing them to other datasets.
  • By including healthcare as a core domain, the dataset directly supports the potential development of ASR systems for medical information access, patient support, and telemedicine.
  • The project, part of the African Next Voices initiative, highlights a broader effort to advance AI capabilities in African languages.

Methodology

The methodology involved defining design principles and ethical considerations for data collection. Procedures included gathering speech data from diverse sources for seven South African languages, ensuring coverage of specific domains like agriculture, healthcare, and general topics. The collected data was then used to train and finetune ASR models to establish baseline performance benchmarks, which were subsequently compared against existing ASR datasets for the relevant languages.

Key Findings

The primary finding is the successful creation and introduction of Swivuriso, a 3000-hour multilingual speech dataset for seven South African languages, explicitly covering healthcare and other vital domains. The paper also presents initial baseline ASR model performance results when trained or finetuned with this new dataset, demonstrating its immediate utility for ASR research and development in these previously under-resourced linguistic contexts.

Clinical Impact

The dataset's clinical impact lies in enabling the creation of accurate ASR systems for medical dictation, patient history taking, health information dissemination, and mental health support in South African languages. This could facilitate better healthcare delivery, reduce language barriers in clinical settings, improve data collection for public health surveillance, and enhance the accessibility of telehealth services for millions of speakers.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly detail limitations, but common considerations for such datasets could include potential biases in speaker demographics (e.g., age, gender, regional accent distribution), the specificity and breadth of healthcare topics covered, and the challenges of maintaining data quality across a very large, multilingual corpus. Future work might need to address ongoing data maintenance, expansion, and validation for diverse real-world clinical scenarios.

Future Directions

Future directions include leveraging Swivuriso for further development and refinement of advanced ASR models specifically tailored for South African languages, especially within the healthcare domain. This dataset will serve as a critical benchmark for evaluating new ASR architectures and methodologies, potentially leading to deployable speech-to-text solutions for clinical documentation, patient education, and health service navigation, thereby improving digital health equity.

Abstract

This paper introduces Swivuriso, a 3000-hour multilingual speech dataset developed as part of the African Next Voices project, to support the development and benchmarking of automatic speech recognition (ASR) technologies in seven South African languages. Covering agriculture, healthcare, and general domain topics, Swivuriso addresses significant gaps in existing ASR datasets. We describe the design principles, ethical considerations, and data collection procedures that guided the dataset creation. We present baseline results of training/finetuning ASR models with this data and compare to other ASR datasets for the langauges concerned.
