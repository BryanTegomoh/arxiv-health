Real-Time Mobile Video Analytics for Pre-arrival Emergency Medical Services

Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Liuyi Jin, Amran Haroon, Radu Stoleru, Pasan Gunawardena, Michael Middleton, Jeeeun Kim

Summary

This paper introduces TeleEMS, a mobile live video analytics system designed to enhance pre-arrival emergency medical services (EMS) by integrating real-time audio and video streams. It addresses the limitations of current EMS infrastructure, which relies on manual interpretation of often overwhelming information, by providing multimodal inference to dispatchers and EMTs before arrival on scene. TeleEMS leverages domain-specialized AI models for symptom extraction, vital sign estimation, and protocol recommendations, demonstrating superior performance over general-purpose models.

Medical Relevance

This system provides crucial, timely, and accurate pre-arrival information to emergency responders, enabling earlier and more informed decision-making. This can lead to faster, more appropriate interventions, improved patient outcomes, and reduced stress and cognitive load for dispatchers and EMTs in critical situations.

AI Health Application

TeleEMS is a mobile live video analytics system that applies AI (specialized LLMs for symptom extraction - EMSLlama, computer vision for vital sign estimation - rPPG, and multimodal models for predicting medical protocols, medications, and procedures - PreNet) to enhance pre-arrival emergency medical services by providing real-time, data-driven insights to dispatchers and EMTs, ultimately aiming to improve patient outcomes in medical emergencies.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: Current EMS systems suffer from one-to-one video streaming and limited analytics, forcing manual, error-prone interpretation of noisy data in high-stress pre-arrival situations.
  • **TeleEMS Architecture**: Comprises a TeleEMS Client (for bystanders, EMTs en route, dispatchers across various devices) and an edge-deployed TeleEMS Server acting as a communication backbone (EMS-Stream) for multi-party video streaming.
  • **Multimodal Analytics Modules**: The server hosts three real-time modules: (1) audio-to-symptom analytics via EMSLlama (a domain-specialized LLM), (2) video-to-vital analytics using rPPG methods for heart rate, and (3) joint text-vital analytics via PreNet (a multimodal multitask model) for predicting EMS protocols, medications, and procedures.
  • **Specialized AI Models**: Utilizes EMSLlama for robust symptom extraction and normalization, state-of-the-art rPPG for non-contact heart rate estimation, and PreNet for comprehensive intervention recommendations.
  • **Performance Superiority**: EMSLlama significantly outperforms GPT-4o in exact-match symptom extraction (0.89 vs. 0.57), highlighting the value of domain-specialized large language models.
  • **Robust Inference**: Text-vital fusion within PreNet is shown to improve inference robustness, leading to more reliable pre-arrival intervention recommendations.
  • **Transformative Potential**: TeleEMS bridges communication and information gaps between bystanders, dispatchers, and EMTs, paving the way for advanced, intelligent EMS infrastructure.

Methodology

TeleEMS employs a client-server architecture with an edge-deployed server featuring a communication backbone (EMS-Stream) for multi-party video. Its analytical core uses three specialized modules: a domain-specialized Large Language Model (EMSLlama) for audio-to-symptom extraction, remote photoplethysmography (rPPG) for video-based heart rate estimation, and a multimodal, multitask deep learning model (PreNet) that fuses textual and vital sign data to predict EMS protocols, medication, and procedures. Evaluation involved comparing EMSLlama against GPT-4o and assessing the robustness of text-vital fusion.

Key Findings

EMSLlama achieved an exact-match score of 0.89 for symptom extraction, significantly outperforming GPT-4o's score of 0.57. The integration of text and vital sign data through multimodal fusion was found to enhance the robustness of inference, enabling the system to generate reliable pre-arrival intervention recommendations. These findings underscore the effectiveness of domain-specialized AI and multimodal data fusion in improving pre-arrival EMS intelligence.

Clinical Impact

TeleEMS has the potential to profoundly impact clinical care by providing dispatchers and EMTs with actionable, real-time insights before reaching the scene. This could facilitate earlier activation of specialized protocols, pre-staging of necessary equipment or medications, and more accurate triage, ultimately reducing time to definitive care and improving patient morbidity and mortality rates. It also promises to reduce the cognitive burden on EMS personnel by automating and structuring critical information analysis.

Abstract

Timely and accurate pre-arrival video streaming and analytics are critical for emergency medical services (EMS) to deliver life-saving interventions. Yet, current-generation EMS infrastructure remains constrained by one-to-one video streaming and limited analytics capabilities, leaving dispatchers and EMTs to manually interpret overwhelming, often noisy or redundant information in high-stress environments. We present TeleEMS, a mobile live video analytics system that enables pre-arrival multimodal inference by fusing audio and video into a unified decision-making pipeline before EMTs arrive on scene. TeleEMS comprises two key components: TeleEMS Client and TeleEMS Server. The TeleEMS Client runs across phones, smart glasses, and desktops to support bystanders, EMTs en route, and 911 dispatchers. The TeleEMS Server, deployed at the edge, integrates EMS-Stream, a communication backbone that enables smooth multi-party video streaming. On top of EMSStream, the server hosts three real-time analytics modules: (1) audio-to-symptom analytics via EMSLlama, a domain-specialized LLM for robust symptom extraction and normalization; (2) video-to-vital analytics using state-of-the-art rPPG methods for heart rate estimation; and (3) joint text-vital analytics via PreNet, a multimodal multitask model predicting EMS protocols, medication types, medication quantities, and procedures. Evaluation shows that EMSLlama outperforms GPT-4o (exact-match 0.89 vs. 0.57) and that text-vital fusion improves inference robustness, enabling reliable pre-arrival intervention recommendations. TeleEMS demonstrates the potential of mobile live video analytics to transform EMS operations, bridging the gap between bystanders, dispatchers, and EMTs, and paving the way for next-generation intelligent EMS infrastructure.
