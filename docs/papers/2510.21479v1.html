ITC-RWKV: Interactive Tissue-Cell Modeling with Recurrent Key-Value Aggregation for Histopathological Subtyping

Summary

This paper introduces ITC-RWKV, a novel dual-stream architecture designed for accurate histopathological subtyping by integrating multi-scale information. It addresses the limitation of existing foundation models by explicitly modeling cell-level features using a recurrent, linear-complexity key-value aggregation model and a bidirectional tissue-cell interaction module. Experiments on four benchmarks demonstrate superior performance, highlighting the critical role of cell-level aggregation and tissue-cell interaction in fine-grained computational pathology.

Methodology

The methodology centers on a dual-stream deep learning architecture. One stream processes macroscale tissue features, while the other aggregates fine-grained cell-level representations. A core component is the 'receptance-weighted key-value aggregation' (RWKV) model, which is a recurrent transformer designed for efficient, linear-complexity processing of large sets of cellular features and modeling inter-cell dependencies. Additionally, a bidirectional tissue-cell interaction module facilitates cross-attention and information exchange between cellular cues and their surrounding tissue context.

Key Findings

The ITC-RWKV model significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods on four different histopathological subtype classification benchmarks. This demonstrates that explicit modeling of cell-level feature aggregation and the interactive interplay between cellular and tissue-level information are fundamental for achieving high accuracy in fine-grained computational pathology tasks.

Abstract

Accurate interpretation of histopathological images demands integration of information across spatial and semantic scales, from nuclear morphology and cellular textures to global tissue organization and disease-specific patterns. Although recent foundation models in pathology have shown strong capabilities in capturing global tissue context, their omission of cell-level feature modeling remains a key limitation for fine-grained tasks such as cancer subtype classification. To address this, we propose a dual-stream architecture that models the interplay between macroscale tissue features and aggregated cellular representations. To efficiently aggregate information from large cell sets, we propose a receptance-weighted key-value aggregation model, a recurrent transformer that captures inter-cell dependencies with linear complexity. Furthermore, we introduce a bidirectional tissue-cell interaction module to enable mutual attention between localized cellular cues and their surrounding tissue environment. Experiments on four histopathological subtype classification benchmarks show that the proposed method outperforms existing models, demonstrating the critical role of cell-level aggregation and tissue-cell interaction in fine-grained computational pathology.
