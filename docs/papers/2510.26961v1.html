SYNAPSE-Net: A Unified Framework with Lesion-Aware Hierarchical Gating for Robust Segmentation of Heterogeneous Brain Lesions

arXiv ID: 2510.26961v1
Published: 2025-10-30
Authors: Md. Mehedi Hassan, Shafqat Alam, Shahriar Ahmed Seam, Maruf Ahmed
Categories: cs.CV, eess.IV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00

Summary

SYNAPSE-Net proposes a unified and adaptive deep learning framework to address the critical challenge of robustly segmenting heterogeneous brain lesions from multi-modal MRI, overcoming the generalization limitations of current specialized models. Leveraging a novel hybrid architecture and a variance reduction training strategy, it achieved state-of-the-art performance across diverse brain pathologies including WMH, stroke lesions, and brain tumors. This framework provides a robust and clinically feasible solution for automated lesion segmentation, demonstrating superior accuracy and generalization.

Medical Relevance

Automated and highly accurate segmentation of various brain lesions from multi-modal MRI is paramount for precise diagnosis, prognosis assessment, treatment planning, and monitoring disease progression in neurological conditions. This unified framework's ability to generalize across diverse pathologies offers a significant leap forward, potentially reducing diagnostic errors and improving clinical efficiency.

AI Health Application

The SYNAPSE-Net is a deep learning framework designed to automatically segment heterogeneous brain lesions (e.g., tumors, stroke lesions, white matter hyperintensities) from MRI scans. This AI application assists clinicians in diagnosis, disease monitoring, and treatment planning by providing precise lesion identification, reducing manual effort and improving consistency in clinical settings.

  • Introduces the Unified Multi-Stream SYNAPSE-Net, an adaptive framework designed for both high generalization and robustness.
  • Features a novel hybrid architecture combining multi-stream CNN encoders, a Swin Transformer bottleneck for global context, a dynamic cross-modal attention fusion (CMAF) mechanism, and a hierarchical gated decoder.

Methodology

The SYNAPSE-Net framework utilizes a novel hybrid deep learning architecture. It incorporates multi-stream CNN encoders for initial feature extraction from multi-modal MRI inputs, a Swin Transformer bottleneck to capture global contextual information, and a dynamic cross-modal attention fusion (CMAF) mechanism for effective integration of information across different modalities. A hierarchical gated decoder is then used for high-fidelity segmentation mask reconstruction. The model is trained with a variance reduction strategy, which includes pathology-specific data augmentation and a difficulty-aware sampling method, aimed at improving generalization and robustness.

Key Findings

The SYNAPSE-Net framework demonstrated state-of-the-art performance across multiple challenging datasets: - **MICCAI 2017 WMH Challenge**: Achieved a Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC) of 0.831 and a 95th percentile Hausdorff Distance (HD95) of 3.03. - **ISLES 2022 Challenge**: Achieved the best boundary accuracy, indicated by an HD95 value of 9.69, with a statistically significant difference compared to other methods. - **BraTS 2020 Challenge**: Reached the highest DSC value of 0.8651 for the critical tumor core region. These findings underscore the framework's superior accuracy and robustness for heterogeneous brain lesion segmentation.

Clinical Impact

This unified and robust framework has the potential to significantly enhance clinical workflows by providing a single, highly accurate tool for automated segmentation of diverse brain lesions from MRI, eliminating the need for multiple specialized models. Its strong generalization across pathologies can lead to improved diagnostic precision, more efficient and timely treatment planning for conditions like stroke and brain tumors, better monitoring of disease progression, and ultimately, improved patient outcomes in neurological and neuro-oncological care.

Abstract

Automated segmentation of heterogeneous brain lesions from multi-modal MRI remains a critical challenge in clinical neuroimaging. Current deep learning models are typically specialized `point solutions' that lack generalization and high performance variance, limiting their clinical reliability. To address these gaps, we propose the Unified Multi-Stream SYNAPSE-Net, an adaptive framework designed for both generalization and robustness. The framework is built on a novel hybrid architecture integrating multi-stream CNN encoders, a Swin Transformer bottleneck for global context, a dynamic cross-modal attention fusion (CMAF) mechanism, and a hierarchical gated decoder for high-fidelity mask reconstruction. The architecture is trained with a variance reduction strategy that combines pathology specific data augmentation and difficulty-aware sampling method. The model was evaluated on three different challenging public datasets: the MICCAI 2017 WMH Challenge, the ISLES 2022 Challenge, and the BraTS 2020 Challenge. Our framework attained a state-of-the-art DSC value of 0.831 with the HD95 value of 3.03 in the WMH dataset. For ISLES 2022, it achieved the best boundary accuracy with a statistically significant difference (HD95 value of 9.69). For BraTS 2020, it reached the highest DSC value for the tumor core region (0.8651). These experimental findings suggest that our unified adaptive framework achieves state-of-the-art performance across multiple brain pathologies, providing a robust and clinically feasible solution for automated segmentation. The source code and the pre-trained models are available at https://github.com/mubid-01/SYNAPSE-Net-pre.
