Mispronunciation Detection and Diagnosis Without Model Training: A Retrieval-Based Approach

Summary

This paper introduces a novel training-free framework for Mispronunciation Detection and Diagnosis (MDD), a critical task for language learning and speech therapy. Unlike conventional methods requiring complex model training, their approach leverages retrieval techniques with a pretrained Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) model. The method achieves accurate detection and diagnosis of pronunciation errors, demonstrating a superior F1 score of 69.60% on the L2-ARCTIC dataset without the computational complexity of model training.

Medical Relevance

This technology offers a streamlined, objective tool for speech-language pathologists to identify and diagnose articulation and phonological disorders, mispronunciations in second language learners, or accent issues. It can significantly enhance the efficiency and precision of speech therapy and rehabilitation programs.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using retrieval-based techniques with a pretrained Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) model to automatically detect and diagnose mispronunciations. This technology can serve as a diagnostic aid or a training tool within speech therapy contexts, helping therapists identify specific pronunciation errors more efficiently and provide targeted interventions for patients with speech impediments, dysarthria, apraxia, or other phonological disorders.

  • Eliminates the need for explicit phoneme-specific modeling, simplifying system development and reducing the substantial data and computational requirements often associated with traditional MDD systems.

Methodology

The proposed method is a training-free, retrieval-based framework for MDD. It leverages a pre-existing, pretrained Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) model. Instead of training phoneme-level models or scoring models, it uses retrieval techniques to compare target pronunciations with a speaker's actual pronunciation, allowing for the detection and diagnosis of errors without requiring task-specific fine-tuning or model training.

Key Findings

The study's main findings include the successful development of a training-free, retrieval-based approach for MDD that achieves accurate detection and diagnosis of mispronunciations. This method demonstrated a superior F1 score of 69.60% on the L2-ARCTIC dataset, crucially without requiring the traditional complexity and resource demands of model training or explicit phoneme-specific modeling.

It could enable more precise and individualized therapy planning, facilitate remote assessments, and offer quantifiable progress tracking for individuals with speech impediments, language learning difficulties, or those undergoing accent modification.

Abstract

Mispronunciation Detection and Diagnosis (MDD) is crucial for language learning and speech therapy. Unlike conventional methods that require scoring models or training phoneme-level models, we propose a novel training-free framework that leverages retrieval techniques with a pretrained Automatic Speech Recognition model. Our method avoids phoneme-specific modeling or additional task-specific training, while still achieving accurate detection and diagnosis of pronunciation errors. Experiments on the L2-ARCTIC dataset show that our method achieves a superior F1 score of 69.60% while avoiding the complexity of model training.
