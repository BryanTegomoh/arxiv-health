Machine Learning Approaches to Clinical Risk Prediction: Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment in Electronic Health Records

Summary

This study introduces the Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment Network (MSTAN) to address the challenges of temporal irregularity, varying sampling intervals, and multi-scale dynamic dependencies inherent in Electronic Health Records (EHR) for clinical risk prediction. MSTAN employs a learnable temporal alignment mechanism and a multi-scale convolutional structure to model both long-term trends and short-term fluctuations in EHR sequences. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed model significantly outperforms mainstream baselines across key performance metrics, offering a robust solution for complex medical time-series analysis.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine and health as it provides an advanced computational method for more accurate and robust clinical risk prediction from complex Electronic Health Records. Improved prediction can facilitate earlier disease detection, personalized interventions, and proactive patient management, ultimately enhancing patient outcomes and healthcare efficiency.

AI Health Application

This paper develops a machine learning model (MSTAN) to process complex, multi-scale temporal data from Electronic Health Records (EHR) to predict disease risk and assess individual health status. This AI application aims to provide clinical decision support by identifying patients at high risk, potentially enabling earlier interventions and more personalized care based on their longitudinal medical data.

Methodology

The Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment Network (MSTAN) is a deep learning model. It begins by mapping multi-source clinical features into a unified high-dimensional semantic space. A temporal embedding and a learnable temporal alignment module are then applied to dynamically weight irregularly sampled data and mitigate the impact of temporal distribution differences. Following this, a multi-scale convolutional feature extraction module, leveraging multi-layer convolutions and hierarchical fusion, captures key patterns across diverse temporal granularities. Finally, an attention-based aggregation mechanism integrates global temporal dependencies to produce individual-level risk representations for prediction.

The proposed MSTAN model demonstrated superior performance in clinical risk prediction on publicly available EHR datasets. It consistently outperformed mainstream baseline models across key evaluation metrics, including accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score, highlighting the effectiveness and robustness of its multi-scale temporal alignment approach in handling complex medical time-series data.

Abstract

This study proposes a risk prediction method based on a Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment Network (MSTAN) to address the challenges of temporal irregularity, sampling interval differences, and multi-scale dynamic dependencies in Electronic Health Records (EHR). The method focuses on temporal feature modeling by introducing a learnable temporal alignment mechanism and a multi-scale convolutional feature extraction structure to jointly model long-term trends and short-term fluctuations in EHR sequences. At the input level, the model maps multi-source clinical features into a unified high-dimensional semantic space and employs temporal embedding and alignment modules to dynamically weight irregularly sampled data, reducing the impact of temporal distribution differences on model performance. The multi-scale feature extraction module then captures key patterns across different temporal granularities through multi-layer convolution and hierarchical fusion, achieving a fine-grained representation of patient states. Finally, an attention-based aggregation mechanism integrates global temporal dependencies to generate individual-level risk representations for disease risk prediction and health status assessment. Experiments conducted on publicly available EHR datasets show that the proposed model outperforms mainstream baselines in accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score, demonstrating the effectiveness and robustness of multi-scale temporal alignment in complex medical time-series analysis. This study provides a new solution for intelligent representation of high-dimensional asynchronous medical sequences and offers important technical support for EHR-driven clinical risk prediction.
