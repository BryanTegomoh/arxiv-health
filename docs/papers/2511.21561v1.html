Machine Learning Approaches to Clinical Risk Prediction: Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment in Electronic Health Records

arXiv ID: 2511.21561v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Wei-Chen Chang, Lu Dai, Ting Xu
Categories: cs.LG

Summary

This study introduces the Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment Network (MSTAN) to address challenges in clinical risk prediction from Electronic Health Records (EHR) due to temporal irregularity, varying sampling intervals, and multi-scale dynamic dependencies. The MSTAN effectively models long-term trends and short-term fluctuations by integrating a learnable temporal alignment mechanism and a multi-scale convolutional feature extraction structure. Experiments conducted on publicly available EHR datasets demonstrate that the proposed model significantly outperforms mainstream baselines across key performance metrics such as accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly enhances the capability of predicting disease risk and assessing health status by more accurately modeling the complex, time-variant nature of patient data found in Electronic Health Records. This technological advancement could lead to earlier interventions, more personalized patient care, and improved clinical decision-making.

AI Health Application

This research develops an AI model (Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment Network) to analyze complex, irregularly sampled Electronic Health Record (EHR) data. Its application is to provide 'individual-level risk representations for disease risk prediction and health status assessment', offering 'important technical support for EHR-driven clinical risk prediction'. This serves as a foundational tool for developing intelligent systems that can assist clinicians in identifying patients at high risk, potentially enabling earlier interventions and personalized treatment strategies.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: The MSTAN is designed to overcome critical challenges in EHR-based clinical risk prediction, specifically temporal irregularity, differences in sampling intervals, and the need to model multi-scale dynamic dependencies within patient data.
  • **Learnable Temporal Alignment**: The model incorporates a novel learnable temporal alignment mechanism that dynamically weights irregularly sampled data, thereby minimizing the adverse impact of uneven temporal distribution on model performance.
  • **Multi-Scale Feature Extraction**: A multi-scale convolutional structure, employing multi-layer convolutions and hierarchical fusion, is utilized to capture both fine-grained short-term fluctuations and broader long-term trends within EHR sequences.
  • **Unified Semantic Space Input**: Multi-source clinical features are initially mapped into a unified high-dimensional semantic space, followed by temporal embedding, to prepare data for the alignment and feature extraction modules.
  • **Attention-based Aggregation**: An attention mechanism is applied to integrate global temporal dependencies across the processed features, generating comprehensive individual-level risk representations for prediction.
  • **Superior Predictive Performance**: The MSTAN model achieved superior performance compared to mainstream baseline models on publicly available EHR datasets, demonstrating higher accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score.
  • **Robustness in Complex Time-Series**: The study highlights the effectiveness and robustness of multi-scale temporal alignment for intelligent representation and analysis of complex, high-dimensional, and asynchronous medical time-series data.

Methodology

The study proposes a Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment Network (MSTAN). Its methodology involves first mapping multi-source clinical features into a unified high-dimensional semantic space. A temporal embedding module, combined with a learnable temporal alignment mechanism, dynamically weights irregularly sampled data. This is followed by a multi-scale convolutional feature extraction module that uses multi-layer convolution and hierarchical fusion to capture patterns across different temporal granularities. Finally, an attention-based aggregation mechanism integrates global temporal dependencies to generate an individual-level risk representation for disease risk prediction and health status assessment.

Key Findings

The MSTAN model demonstrated significant improvements in clinical risk prediction, outperforming mainstream baseline models across key metrics including accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score when evaluated on publicly available EHR datasets. This confirms the effectiveness and robustness of its multi-scale temporal alignment and feature extraction approach in handling complex, asynchronous medical time-series data.

Abstract

This study proposes a risk prediction method based on a Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment Network (MSTAN) to address the challenges of temporal irregularity, sampling interval differences, and multi-scale dynamic dependencies in Electronic Health Records (EHR). The method focuses on temporal feature modeling by introducing a learnable temporal alignment mechanism and a multi-scale convolutional feature extraction structure to jointly model long-term trends and short-term fluctuations in EHR sequences. At the input level, the model maps multi-source clinical features into a unified high-dimensional semantic space and employs temporal embedding and alignment modules to dynamically weight irregularly sampled data, reducing the impact of temporal distribution differences on model performance. The multi-scale feature extraction module then captures key patterns across different temporal granularities through multi-layer convolution and hierarchical fusion, achieving a fine-grained representation of patient states. Finally, an attention-based aggregation mechanism integrates global temporal dependencies to generate individual-level risk representations for disease risk prediction and health status assessment. Experiments conducted on publicly available EHR datasets show that the proposed model outperforms mainstream baselines in accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score, demonstrating the effectiveness and robustness of multi-scale temporal alignment in complex medical time-series analysis. This study provides a new solution for intelligent representation of high-dimensional asynchronous medical sequences and offers important technical support for EHR-driven clinical risk prediction.
