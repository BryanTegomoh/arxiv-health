Summary

This study introduces the Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment Network (MSTAN) to address critical challenges in Electronic Health Records (EHR) data, such as temporal irregularity and multi-scale dynamic dependencies, for enhanced clinical risk prediction. MSTAN employs a learnable temporal alignment mechanism and multi-scale convolutional feature extraction to effectively model both long-term trends and short-term fluctuations in patient data. Experimental results demonstrate its superior performance compared to mainstream baselines across multiple evaluation metrics on publicly available EHR datasets.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine and health as it provides a robust method to analyze complex, asynchronous Electronic Health Records, enabling more accurate and timely prediction of disease risk. This can significantly enhance proactive clinical decision-making, patient management, and the development of personalized intervention strategies.

This can aid clinicians in early intervention and personalized patient management.

Methodology

The study develops a Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment Network (MSTAN). It begins by mapping multi-source clinical features into a unified high-dimensional semantic space. A temporal embedding and alignment module dynamically weights irregularly sampled data, reducing the impact of temporal distribution differences. A multi-scale feature extraction module then uses multi-layer convolution and hierarchical fusion to capture key patterns across different temporal granularities for fine-grained patient state representation. Finally, an attention-based aggregation mechanism integrates global temporal dependencies to generate individual-level risk representations for disease risk prediction and health status assessment.

Abstract

This study proposes a risk prediction method based on a Multi-Scale Temporal Alignment Network (MSTAN) to address the challenges of temporal irregularity, sampling interval differences, and multi-scale dynamic dependencies in Electronic Health Records (EHR). The method focuses on temporal feature modeling by introducing a learnable temporal alignment mechanism and a multi-scale convolutional feature extraction structure to jointly model long-term trends and short-term fluctuations in EHR sequences. At the input level, the model maps multi-source clinical features into a unified high-dimensional semantic space and employs temporal embedding and alignment modules to dynamically weight irregularly sampled data, reducing the impact of temporal distribution differences on model performance. The multi-scale feature extraction module then captures key patterns across different temporal granularities through multi-layer convolution and hierarchical fusion, achieving a fine-grained representation of patient states. Finally, an attention-based aggregation mechanism integrates global temporal dependencies to generate individual-level risk representations for disease risk prediction and health status assessment. Experiments conducted on publicly available EHR datasets show that the proposed model outperforms mainstream baselines in accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score, demonstrating the effectiveness and robustness of multi-scale temporal alignment in complex medical time-series analysis. This study provides a new solution for intelligent representation of high-dimensional asynchronous medical sequences and offers important technical support for EHR-driven clinical risk prediction.
