Summary

VersaPants presents the first loose-fitting, textile-based capacitive sensing system for lower-body motion capture, integrating conductive patches and a compact acquisition unit into a pair of pants. Utilizing a lightweight Transformer-based deep learning model, the system accurately reconstructs hip, knee, and ankle joint angles with competitive performance. It offers a comfortable, privacy-preserving, and real-time embedded solution for motion analysis without requiring fitting adjustments.

Medical Relevance

This technology provides a comfortable, private, and scalable method for continuous lower-body motion tracking, which is crucial for objective assessment, monitoring, and rehabilitation in various medical and health contexts. Its ease of use and embedded nature make it suitable for long-term home-based monitoring.

AI Health Application

The AI component is a lightweight Transformer-based deep learning model that maps raw capacitance signals from textile sensors to lower-body joint angles (hip, knee, ankle). This AI model enables real-time, embedded processing of motion data on edge platforms (like smartwatches), facilitating objective quantitative assessment of movement for physical therapy progress tracking, gait pathology detection, activity monitoring for chronic disease management, fall risk prediction, and general wellness applications without compromising user comfort or privacy.

Key Points

  • VersaPants is a novel loose-fitting, textile-based capacitive sensing system designed for lower-body motion capture, featuring 6 capacitive channels per leg and built on the open-hardware VersaSens platform.
  • It overcomes limitations of IMU-based systems (no user-specific fitting required) and camera-based methods (preserves user privacy), prioritizing comfort due to its loose-fitting design.
  • A lightweight Transformer-based deep learning model maps raw capacitance signals directly to hip, knee, and ankle joint angles, optimized for embedded implementation on edge platforms.
  • The system's performance was validated using approximately 3.7 hours of motion data from 11 participants performing 16 diverse daily and exercise-based movements.
  • It achieved a mean per-joint position error (MPJPE) of 11.96 cm and a mean per-joint angle error (MPJAE) of 12.3 degrees, demonstrating its ability to generalize to unseen users and movements.
  • The deep learning model is highly efficient, achieving competitive reconstruction performance with up to 22 times fewer parameters and 18 times fewer FLOPs than other textile-based deep learning architectures, enabling real-time inference at 42 FPS on a commercial smartwatch without quantization.

Methodology

The VersaPants system integrates 6 conductive textile capacitive sensing channels per leg into a pair of pants, connected to a compact acquisition unit. Data was collected from 11 participants performing 16 diverse daily and exercise-based movements (approx. 3.7 hours). A lightweight Transformer-based deep learning model was trained to map these capacitance signals to hip, knee, and ankle joint angles. Performance was evaluated using Mean Per-Joint Position Error (MPJPE) and Mean Per-Joint Angle Error (MPJAE), and model efficiency was compared against other textile-based deep learning architectures.

Key Findings

The system accurately reconstructs lower-body pose with an MPJPE of 11.96 cm and an MPJAE of 12.3 degrees across hip, knee, and ankle joints, showing strong generalization. The deep learning model is highly efficient, requiring up to 22 times fewer parameters and 18 times fewer FLOPs than comparable textile-based architectures, allowing real-time inference at 42 FPS on a commercial smartwatch without quantization.

Clinical Impact

VersaPants offers a novel, non-intrusive solution for continuous, long-term monitoring of lower-body movement, critical for assessing gait, balance, and functional mobility in patients. Its comfort, privacy, and real-time edge processing capabilities could significantly advance remote rehabilitation, objective fitness tracking, early detection of mobility issues in older adults, and performance analysis in sports medicine, making personalized and data-driven interventions more accessible and patient-friendly.

Abstract

We present VersaPants, the first loose-fitting, textile-based capacitive sensing system for lower-body motion capture, built on the open-hardware VersaSens platform. By integrating conductive textile patches and a compact acquisition unit into a pair of pants, the system reconstructs lower-body pose without compromising comfort. Unlike IMU-based systems that require user-specific fitting or camera-based methods that compromise privacy, our approach operates without fitting adjustments and preserves user privacy. VersaPants is a custom-designed smart garment featuring 6 capacitive channels per leg. We employ a lightweight Transformer-based deep learning model that maps capacitance signals to joint angles, enabling embedded implementation on edge platforms. To test our system, we collected approximately 3.7 hours of motion data from 11 participants performing 16 daily and exercise-based movements. The model achieves a mean per-joint position error (MPJPE) of 11.96 cm and a mean per-joint angle error (MPJAE) of 12.3 degrees across the hip, knee, and ankle joints, indicating the model's ability to generalize to unseen users and movements. A comparative analysis of existing textile-based deep learning architectures reveals that our model achieves competitive reconstruction performance with up to 22 times fewer parameters and 18 times fewer FLOPs, enabling real-time inference at 42 FPS on a commercial smartwatch without quantization. These results position VersaPants as a promising step toward scalable, comfortable, and embedded motion-capture solutions for fitness, healthcare, and wellbeing applications.
