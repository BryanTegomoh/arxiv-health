Risk Prediction of Cardiovascular Disease for Diabetic Patients with Machine Learning and Deep Learning Techniques

Published: 2025-11-07
Authors: Esha Chowdhury
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00

Summary

This study develops efficient machine learning (ML) and hybrid deep learning (DL) models to accurately predict cardiovascular disease (CVD) risk in diabetic patients using the BRFSS dataset. After rigorous preprocessing including PCA, both XGBoost (ML) and LSTM (DL) models achieved the highest accuracy of 0.9050, with some DL models demonstrating perfect recall. The research highlights the potential of these models to automate and enhance clinical decision-making, improving personalized risk management and preventive strategies for diabetic individuals.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant for preventive cardiology and diabetes management, as it offers computational tools to proactively identify diabetic patients at high risk of cardiovascular disease, enabling earlier interventions and more personalized, data-driven care pathways. This can lead to reduced morbidity and mortality associated with CVD in diabetic populations.

AI Health Application

The research applies machine learning and deep learning models to predict cardiovascular disease risk specifically for diabetic patients. This AI application serves to enhance clinical decision-making, enable personalized risk management, and support the development of preventive strategies within healthcare settings.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical need for accurate CVD risk prediction in diabetic patients, given the strong epidemiological link between diabetes and heart disease.
  • Utilizes the BRFSS dataset, applying comprehensive preprocessing steps including duplicate removal, missing value handling, feature identification (categorical/numerical), and Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for feature extraction.
  • Evaluated a range of traditional Machine Learning models (Decision Trees, Random Forest, k-Nearest Neighbors, SVM, AdaBoost, XGBoost), with XGBoost achieving the highest accuracy of 0.9050.
  • Explored various Deep Learning architectures (ANN, DNN, RNN, CNN, LSTM, BiLSTM, GRU) and hybrid models combining CNN with LSTM, BiLSTM, and GRU.
  • The LSTM deep learning model achieved an accuracy of 0.9050, matching the top ML performer, while some DL models demonstrated perfect recall (1.00), indicating excellent sensitivity for identifying positive cases.
  • The research collectively underscores the effectiveness of both ML and DL approaches in predicting CVD risk, consistently achieving high accuracy and F1 scores.
  • Highlights the potential for these models to automate and enhance clinical decision-making, leading to improved personalized risk management and preventive strategies for diabetic patients.

Methodology

The study employed the BRFSS dataset, which underwent extensive preprocessing including duplicate removal, handling of missing values, identification of categorical and numerical features, and Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for dimensionality reduction and feature extraction. A diverse set of Machine Learning models (Decision Trees, Random Forest, k-Nearest Neighbors, Support Vector Machine, AdaBoost, XGBoost) and Deep Learning architectures (ANN, DNN, RNN, CNN, LSTM, BiLSTM, GRU), alongside hybrid models combining CNN with LSTM, BiLSTM, and GRU, were trained and evaluated for CVD risk prediction.

Key Findings

Both the XGBoost model (from ML techniques) and the LSTM model (from DL techniques) achieved the highest predictive accuracy of 0.9050. Notably, several deep learning models also demonstrated perfect recall (1.00), indicating their robust ability to correctly identify all actual positive cases of CVD risk among diabetic patients. Overall, the models exhibited high accuracy and F1 scores, confirming their strong predictive capability.

Clinical Impact

The developed ML and DL models offer a significant clinical impact by providing an automated, accurate, and data-driven approach for identifying diabetic patients at high risk of CVD. This allows healthcare providers to implement timely and personalized preventive strategies, tailor treatment plans, optimize resource allocation, and enhance overall patient management, potentially reducing the incidence and severity of cardiovascular events in this vulnerable population.

Abstract

Accurate prediction of cardiovascular disease (CVD) risk is crucial for healthcare institutions. This study addresses the growing prevalence of diabetes and its strong link to heart disease by proposing an efficient CVD risk prediction model for diabetic patients using machine learning (ML) and hybrid deep learning (DL) approaches. The BRFSS dataset was preprocessed by removing duplicates, handling missing values, identifying categorical and numerical features, and applying Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for feature extraction. Several ML models, including Decision Trees (DT), Random Forest (RF), k-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Support Vector Machine (SVM), AdaBoost, and XGBoost, were implemented, with XGBoost achieving the highest accuracy of 0.9050. Various DL models, such as Artificial Neural Networks (ANN), Deep Neural Networks (DNN), Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN), Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), Bidirectional LSTM (BiLSTM), and Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU), as well as hybrid models combining CNN with LSTM, BiLSTM, and GRU, were also explored. Some of these models achieved perfect recall (1.00), with the LSTM model achieving the highest accuracy of 0.9050. Our research highlights the effectiveness of ML and DL models in predicting CVD risk among diabetic patients, automating and enhancing clinical decision-making. High accuracy and F1 scores demonstrate these models' potential to improve personalized risk management and preventive strategies.
