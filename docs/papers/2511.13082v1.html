Real-time prediction of breast cancer sites using deformation-aware graph neural network

arXiv ID: 2511.13082v1
Published: 2025-11-17
Authors: Kyunghyun Lee, Yong-Min Shin, Minwoo Shin, Jihun Kim, Sunghwan Lim, Won-Yong Shin, Kyungho Yoon

Summary

This paper presents a novel graph neural network (GNN)-based model for real-time, accurate prediction of breast cancer tumor displacement during indirect MRI-guided biopsy procedures. By integrating individual-specific finite element (FE) models derived from MR images with a GNN trained on surface displacement and distance data, the model precisely predicts tissue and tumor deformation, significantly enhancing the precision and efficiency of breast cancer diagnosis.

Medical Relevance

Accurate, real-time prediction of breast tumor location during biopsy is critical for precise diagnosis and effective treatment planning. This research offers a method to make indirect MRI-guided biopsies more reliable and accessible, improving patient prognosis by facilitating earlier and more accurate detection of breast cancer.

AI Health Application

The paper develops a deformation-aware Graph Neural Network (GNN) model to provide real-time prediction of breast cancer site displacement during indirect MRI-guided biopsy procedures. This AI application aims to improve the accuracy and efficiency of tumor targeting, thereby enhancing breast cancer diagnosis by compensating for tissue deformation.

Key Points

  • Addresses the challenge of accurate real-time deformable breast models in indirect MRI-guided biopsy, which is crucial for precise lesion targeting.
  • Developed individual-specific finite element (FE) models using MR image-derived structural information of the breast and tumor to simulate realistic deformation behaviors.
  • Employed a graph neural network (GNN) designed to process surface displacement and distance-based graph data, enabling comprehensive prediction of tissue and tumor region displacement.
  • Achieved high accuracy, with a Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) of 0.2 mm for cancer node displacement and a Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC) of 0.977 for spatial overlap with actual cancerous regions.
  • Demonstrated real-time inference capability, achieving a computational speed-up of over 4,000 times compared to traditional FE simulations.
  • Validated the model using both phantom and real patient datasets, confirming its robustness and clinical applicability.
  • Proposes a promising solution that could significantly enhance the precision, efficiency, and potentially reduce costs of breast cancer diagnostic procedures by enabling accurate guidance outside the MRI room.

Methodology

The methodology involved two main steps: first, developing individual-specific finite element (FE) models using MR image-derived structural information of the breast and tumor to simulate deformation. Second, a graph neural network (GNN) was trained to process surface displacement and distance-based graph data from these FE simulations. This GNN then learned to predict overall tissue displacement, including the precise deformation of the tumor region, in real-time.

Key Findings

The GNN model achieved an RMSE of 0.2 mm for cancer node displacement and a DSC of 0.977 for spatial overlap with actual cancerous regions, demonstrating high accuracy. Crucially, it enabled real-time inference, exhibiting a computational speed-up of over 4,000 times compared to conventional FE simulations, making it suitable for clinical applications.

Clinical Impact

This deformation-aware GNN model has the potential to significantly enhance the precision and efficiency of breast cancer diagnosis. By enabling accurate real-time tumor displacement prediction in indirect MRI-guided biopsies, it could improve targeting accuracy, reduce procedure times, lower costs, and make advanced biopsy guidance more widely available, ultimately leading to earlier and more effective treatment for patients.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations of the proposed model. However, it implicitly suggests that further work on its integration into clinical procedures is still needed, indicating it's not yet fully implemented in practice.

Future Directions

The primary future direction highlighted is the integration of the proposed deformation-aware GNN model with existing clinical procedures to significantly enhance the precision and efficiency of breast cancer diagnosis, implying further validation and implementation studies in real-world clinical settings.

Abstract

Early diagnosis of breast cancer is crucial, enabling the establishment of appropriate treatment plans and markedly enhancing patient prognosis. While direct magnetic resonance imaging-guided biopsy demonstrates promising performance in detecting cancer lesions, its practical application is limited by prolonged procedure times and high costs. To overcome these issues, an indirect MRI-guided biopsy that allows the procedure to be performed outside of the MRI room has been proposed, but it still faces challenges in creating an accurate real-time deformable breast model. In our study, we tackled this issue by developing a graph neural network (GNN)-based model capable of accurately predicting deformed breast cancer sites in real time during biopsy procedures. An individual-specific finite element (FE) model was developed by incorporating magnetic resonance (MR) image-derived structural information of the breast and tumor to simulate deformation behaviors. A GNN model was then employed, designed to process surface displacement and distance-based graph data, enabling accurate prediction of overall tissue displacement, including the deformation of the tumor region. The model was validated using phantom and real patient datasets, achieving an accuracy within 0.2 millimeters (mm) for cancer node displacement (RMSE) and a dice similarity coefficient (DSC) of 0.977 for spatial overlap with actual cancerous regions. Additionally, the model enabled real-time inference and achieved a speed-up of over 4,000 times in computational cost compared to conventional FE simulations. The proposed deformation-aware GNN model offers a promising solution for real-time tumor displacement prediction in breast biopsy, with high accuracy and real-time capability. Its integration with clinical procedures could significantly enhance the precision and efficiency of breast cancer diagnosis.
