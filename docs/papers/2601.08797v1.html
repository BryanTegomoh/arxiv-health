DentalX: Context-Aware Dental Disease Detection with Radiographs

arXiv ID: 2601.08797v1
Published: 2026-01-13
Authors: Zhi Qin Tan, Xiatian Zhu, Owen Addison, Yunpeng Li
Categories: cs.CV

Summary

This paper introduces DentalX, a novel context-aware deep learning model designed to improve the detection of subtle dental diseases from radiographs. By integrating a structural context extraction module that leverages an auxiliary task of semantic segmentation for dental anatomy, DentalX significantly outperforms existing object detection methods, demonstrating a mutual benefit between anatomical understanding and disease identification.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and timely diagnosis of dental diseases from radiographs is critical for effective treatment planning. This research aims to provide clinicians with an AI-powered tool to overcome the inherent challenges of subtle visual evidence in radiographs, potentially leading to earlier detection, reduced misdiagnosis, and improved patient care.

AI Health Application

The paper describes 'DentalX', an AI system that leverages computer vision and machine learning techniques (specifically context-aware object detection and semantic segmentation) to automatically detect subtle dental diseases from radiographic images. This AI acts as a diagnostic aid, aiming to improve the accuracy and reduce the time spent by dentists in diagnosing conditions from X-rays.

Key Points

  • Addresses the challenge of detecting subtle dental diseases from radiographs, where diagnostic evidence is often visually ambiguous and less distinct than patterns in natural images.
  • Proposes DentalX, a context-aware approach that explicitly incorporates oral structural information to mitigate visual ambiguity in radiographic interpretation.
  • Introduces a structural context extraction module that learns an auxiliary task: semantic segmentation of dental anatomy (e.g., teeth, roots, bone structure).
  • The extracted structural context is integrated into the primary disease detection task, enhancing the model's ability to identify subtle pathological findings.
  • Leverages existing object detection frameworks (implied YOLOX from code link) as its base, adapting them for the specific challenges of dental radiographs.
  • Achieves significant performance improvement over prior methods on a dedicated benchmark, excelling in both dental disease detection and dental anatomy segmentation.
  • Demonstrates a 'mutual benefit' where the two correlated tasks (anatomy segmentation and disease detection) enhance each other's performance during model optimization.

Methodology

DentalX is built upon a deep learning framework, likely an advanced object detection model. Its core innovation lies in a 'structural context extraction module' that learns to semantically segment dental anatomy (e.g., individual teeth, bone, soft tissue boundaries) as an auxiliary task. The rich, context-aware features derived from this segmentation task are then integrated into the feature pipeline of the primary disease detection task, enabling the model to utilize comprehensive oral structural information to better interpret subtle disease patterns in radiographs.

Key Findings

Extensive experiments on a dedicated benchmark dataset confirm that DentalX significantly surpasses prior state-of-the-art methods in both the primary dental disease detection task and the auxiliary dental anatomy semantic segmentation task. The research highlights that the model effectively captures the correlation between these two tasks, leading to a synergistic performance improvement for both.

Clinical Impact

This technology holds the potential to significantly enhance the efficiency and accuracy of dental diagnosis. By providing automated, context-aware detection of subtle diseases like early caries, periodontal bone loss, or periapical lesions, DentalX could serve as a valuable diagnostic aid for dentists, potentially reducing diagnostic errors, speeding up clinical workflows, and facilitating earlier intervention for better patient outcomes.

Abstract

Diagnosing dental diseases from radiographs is time-consuming and challenging due to the subtle nature of diagnostic evidence. Existing methods, which rely on object detection models designed for natural images with more distinct target patterns, struggle to detect dental diseases that present with far less visual support. To address this challenge, we propose {\bf DentalX}, a novel context-aware dental disease detection approach that leverages oral structure information to mitigate the visual ambiguity inherent in radiographs. Specifically, we introduce a structural context extraction module that learns an auxiliary task: semantic segmentation of dental anatomy. The module extracts meaningful structural context and integrates it into the primary disease detection task to enhance the detection of subtle dental diseases. Extensive experiments on a dedicated benchmark demonstrate that DentalX significantly outperforms prior methods in both tasks. This mutual benefit arises naturally during model optimization, as the correlation between the two tasks is effectively captured. Our code is available at https://github.com/zhiqin1998/DentYOLOX.
