Personalized Treatment Outcome Prediction from Scarce Data via Dual-Channel Knowledge Distillation and Adaptive Fusion

arXiv ID: 2510.26444v1
Published: 2025-10-30
Authors: Wenjie Chen, Li Zhuang, Ziying Luo, Yu Liu, Jiahao Wu, Shengcai Liu
Categories: cs.LG, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces the Cross-Fidelity Knowledge Distillation and Adaptive Fusion Network (CFKD-AFN) to improve personalized treatment outcome prediction, particularly for small-sample and rare patient groups. CFKD-AFN addresses the scarcity of high-fidelity trial data by leveraging abundant low-fidelity simulation data through a dual-channel knowledge distillation and attention-guided fusion approach. The method demonstrates significant improvements in prediction accuracy and robustness, exemplified in chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) treatment outcomes.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant for precision medicine as it enables accurate personalized treatment outcome prediction for rare diseases and patient subgroups where clinical trial data is inherently limited, potentially leading to more targeted and effective therapeutic strategies.

AI Health Application

The paper proposes a novel AI/machine learning model (CFKD-AFN) to predict personalized treatment outcomes, especially in scenarios with scarce patient trial data. It uses dual-channel knowledge distillation and adaptive fusion to combine simulation and trial data. This AI application aims to enhance prediction accuracy for small-sample and rare patient groups, providing 'latent medical semantics' to support 'clinical decision-making' in areas like precision medicine and chronic disease management.

Methodology

The CFKD-AFN framework is designed to integrate information from disparate data sources: scarce high-fidelity trial data and abundant low-fidelity simulation data. It employs a dual-channel knowledge distillation module to extract and transfer complementary knowledge from a model trained on the low-fidelity data to enhance the learning process for the high-fidelity data. An attention-guided fusion module then dynamically combines the features and insights derived from both data fidelities and distillation channels to produce robust personalized treatment outcome predictions. The method was validated using a dataset for chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) treatment outcome prediction.

Key Findings

CFKD-AFN achieved substantial improvements in prediction accuracy for personalized treatment outcomes, outperforming state-of-the-art methods by 6.67% to 74.55%. The model demonstrated strong robustness, maintaining high performance even with small and varying sizes of the high-fidelity training dataset. Furthermore, an interpretable variant of CFKD-AFN was developed, which facilitates the exploration of latent medical semantics, thereby providing insights that can support clinical decision-making.

It can aid clinicians in selecting optimal therapies, reducing trial-and-error, and supporting evidence-based, tailored interventions, ultimately leading to better patient outcomes and more efficient healthcare resource utilization. The interpretability feature further enhances its utility by offering transparent insights into prediction rationales.

Abstract

Personalized treatment outcome prediction based on trial data for small-sample and rare patient groups is critical in precision medicine. However, the costly trial data limit the prediction performance. To address this issue, we propose a cross-fidelity knowledge distillation and adaptive fusion network (CFKD-AFN), which leverages abundant but low-fidelity simulation data to enhance predictions on scarce but high-fidelity trial data. CFKD-AFN incorporates a dual-channel knowledge distillation module to extract complementary knowledge from the low-fidelity model, along with an attention-guided fusion module to dynamically integrate multi-source information. Experiments on treatment outcome prediction for the chronic obstructive pulmonary disease demonstrates significant improvements of CFKD-AFN over state-of-the-art methods in prediction accuracy, ranging from 6.67\% to 74.55\%, and strong robustness to varying high-fidelity dataset sizes. Furthermore, we extend CFKD-AFN to an interpretable variant, enabling the exploration of latent medical semantics to support clinical decision-making.
