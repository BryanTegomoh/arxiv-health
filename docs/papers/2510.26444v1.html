Personalized Treatment Outcome Prediction from Scarce Data via Dual-Channel Knowledge Distillation and Adaptive Fusion

Authors: Wenjie Chen, Li Zhuang, Ziying Luo, Yu Liu, Jiahao Wu, Shengcai Liu

Summary

This paper introduces the Cross-Fidelity Knowledge Distillation and Adaptive Fusion Network (CFKD-AFN) to improve personalized treatment outcome prediction when only scarce, high-fidelity trial data is available. By leveraging abundant low-fidelity simulation data via dual-channel knowledge distillation and attention-guided fusion, CFKD-AFN achieves significant prediction accuracy improvements (6.67-74.55%) and robustness for diseases like COPD. The research also presents an interpretable variant to aid clinical decision-making.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant for precision medicine, especially for patient groups with rare diseases or specific phenotypes where large-scale clinical trial data is prohibitive. It offers a method to derive more accurate personalized treatment outcome predictions from limited real-world data, directly impacting treatment optimization and patient management.

AI Health Application

The AI application is a personalized treatment outcome prediction model designed to enhance predictions for small-sample and rare patient groups using a combination of scarce high-fidelity trial data and abundant low-fidelity simulation data. It provides clinical decision support by offering interpretable insights into latent medical semantics, specifically demonstrated for chronic obstructive pulmonary disease.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical problem of personalized treatment outcome prediction for small-sample, rare patient groups, where high-fidelity trial data is scarce and costly.
  • Proposes CFKD-AFN, a novel framework that integrates knowledge from abundant low-fidelity simulation data to enhance predictions on scarce high-fidelity trial data.
  • Incorporates a dual-channel knowledge distillation module to extract complementary knowledge from models trained on low-fidelity data.
  • Utilizes an attention-guided fusion module to dynamically integrate multi-source information, optimizing the combination of different data fidelities.
  • Demonstrated significant improvements in prediction accuracy (ranging from 6.67% to 74.55%) over state-of-the-art methods in experiments on Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD).
  • Exhibits strong robustness to varying sizes of the high-fidelity dataset, confirming its practical utility in scenarios with truly limited data.
  • Extends the core methodology to an interpretable variant, enabling the exploration of latent medical semantics to support clinical decision-making.

Methodology

The core methodology revolves around the Cross-Fidelity Knowledge Distillation and Adaptive Fusion Network (CFKD-AFN). It leverages two types of data: abundant low-fidelity simulation data and scarce high-fidelity trial data. The CFKD-AFN comprises a dual-channel knowledge distillation module, which extracts diverse and complementary knowledge from a model trained on the low-fidelity data, acting as a 'teacher.' This distilled knowledge is then used to enhance a 'student' model trained on the scarce high-fidelity data. An attention-guided fusion module dynamically integrates information from both the high-fidelity data and the distilled knowledge channels, allowing for optimized multi-source information processing. The approach aims to transfer robust patterns from readily available (though less precise) data to improve predictions on precise but limited data.

Key Findings

The study found that CFKD-AFN significantly improves personalized treatment outcome prediction accuracy, showing improvements between 6.67% and 74.55% when compared to state-of-the-art methods. The network demonstrated strong robustness across varying high-fidelity dataset sizes, crucial for real-world scarce data scenarios. Additionally, an interpretable variant of CFKD-AFN was developed, allowing for the exploration of latent medical semantics, which can provide valuable insights for clinicians.

Clinical Impact

The proposed CFKD-AFN has substantial clinical impact potential by enabling more accurate personalized treatment outcome predictions, particularly for challenging patient populations with scarce trial data. This can lead to improved patient stratification, optimized treatment plans, and better-informed clinical decision-making. The interpretability feature further enhances its clinical utility by providing insights into the prediction rationale, fostering trust among clinicians and facilitating its integration into routine practice for conditions like COPD and other rare diseases.

Abstract

Personalized treatment outcome prediction based on trial data for small-sample and rare patient groups is critical in precision medicine. However, the costly trial data limit the prediction performance. To address this issue, we propose a cross-fidelity knowledge distillation and adaptive fusion network (CFKD-AFN), which leverages abundant but low-fidelity simulation data to enhance predictions on scarce but high-fidelity trial data. CFKD-AFN incorporates a dual-channel knowledge distillation module to extract complementary knowledge from the low-fidelity model, along with an attention-guided fusion module to dynamically integrate multi-source information. Experiments on treatment outcome prediction for the chronic obstructive pulmonary disease demonstrates significant improvements of CFKD-AFN over state-of-the-art methods in prediction accuracy, ranging from 6.67\% to 74.55\%, and strong robustness to varying high-fidelity dataset sizes. Furthermore, we extend CFKD-AFN to an interpretable variant, enabling the exploration of latent medical semantics to support clinical decision-making.
