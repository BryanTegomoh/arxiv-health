STReasoner: Empowering LLMs for Spatio-Temporal Reasoning in Time Series via Spatial-Aware Reinforcement Learning

Published: 2026-01-06
Authors: Juntong Ni, Shiyu Wang, Ming Jin, Qi He, Wei Jin

Summary

This paper addresses the critical but underdeveloped field of spatio-temporal reasoning in time series, crucial for high-stakes decision-making in domains like disease propagation, by introducing ST-Bench, a novel benchmark, and STReasoner, an LLM-powered framework. STReasoner effectively integrates time series, graph structures, and text for explicit reasoning, achieving substantial accuracy gains (17-135%) at a fraction of the cost of proprietary models while generalizing robustly to real-world data.

Medical Relevance

Spatio-temporal reasoning is highly relevant to medicine, particularly in understanding disease propagation, predicting outbreaks, and optimizing resource allocation in healthcare networks, enabling more precise and timely public health interventions.

AI Health Application

The proposed STReasoner model, which empowers LLMs with spatial-aware reinforcement learning, can be applied to analyze spatio-temporal data for tasks like predicting disease outbreaks and spread, understanding the causative factors (etiology) of diseases, identifying correlations in health data across populations and regions, and potentially aiding in rapid response to health crises by providing reasoning capabilities for complex disease propagation models.

Key Points

  • The research tackles the gap in spatio-temporal reasoning within time series analysis, which is vital for complex systems like traffic, power grids, and disease propagation, often overlooked in favor of predictive accuracy.
  • A new benchmark, ST-Bench, is introduced, comprising four core tasks: etiological reasoning, entity identification, correlation reasoning, and in-context forecasting, developed using a network SDE-based multi-agent data synthesis pipeline.
  • The proposed model, STReasoner, empowers Large Language Models (LLMs) to perform explicit spatio-temporal reasoning by integrating diverse data modalities: time series, graph structures representing spatial dependencies, and textual context.
  • STReasoner incorporates S-GRPO, a novel spatial-aware reinforcement learning algorithm, specifically designed to promote spatially grounded logic by rewarding performance improvements directly attributable to the utilization of spatial information.
  • Experimental results demonstrate that STReasoner achieves significant average accuracy gains ranging from 17% to an impressive 135% on the ST-Bench tasks.
  • The model is highly cost-effective, operating at only 0.004X the cost of proprietary models, making advanced spatio-temporal reasoning more accessible.
  • STReasoner exhibits robust generalization capabilities, performing effectively when applied to real-world spatio-temporal datasets.

Methodology

The methodology involves creating ST-Bench, a synthetic benchmark for spatio-temporal reasoning tasks, using a network SDE-based multi-agent data synthesis pipeline. Subsequently, STReasoner is proposed, which integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) with time series data, graph structures (representing spatial dependencies), and textual context. A key component is S-GRPO, a spatial-aware reinforcement learning algorithm that specifically rewards performance gains derived from spatial information to promote spatially grounded logic.

Crucially, it does so at an extremely low computational cost, estimated at 0.004X that of proprietary models, while demonstrating robust generalization to real-world data.

Abstract

Spatio-temporal reasoning in time series involves the explicit synthesis of temporal dynamics, spatial dependencies, and textual context. This capability is vital for high-stakes decision-making in systems such as traffic networks, power grids, and disease propagation. However, the field remains underdeveloped because most existing works prioritize predictive accuracy over reasoning. To address the gap, we introduce ST-Bench, a benchmark consisting of four core tasks, including etiological reasoning, entity identification, correlation reasoning, and in-context forecasting, developed via a network SDE-based multi-agent data synthesis pipeline. We then propose STReasoner, which empowers LLM to integrate time series, graph structure, and text for explicit reasoning. To promote spatially grounded logic, we introduce S-GRPO, a reinforcement learning algorithm that rewards performance gains specifically attributable to spatial information. Experiments show that STReasoner achieves average accuracy gains between 17% and 135% at only 0.004X the cost of proprietary models and generalizes robustly to real-world data.
