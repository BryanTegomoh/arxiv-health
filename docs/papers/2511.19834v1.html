Large Language Model Aided Birt-Hogg-Dube Syndrome Diagnosis with Multimodal Retrieval-Augmented Generation

arXiv ID: 2511.19834v1
Published: 2025-11-25
Authors: Haoqing Li, Jun Shi, Xianmeng Chen, Qiwei Jia, Rui Wang, Wei Wei, Hong An, Xiaowen Hu
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces BHD-RAG, a novel multimodal retrieval-augmented generation framework designed to improve the diagnostic accuracy of Birt-Hogg-Dube (BHD) syndrome from Computed Tomography (CT) images, particularly amidst challenges of limited clinical data and low inter-class differentiation among Diffuse Cystic Lung Diseases (DCLDs). By integrating DCLD-specific expertise and clinical precedents with Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), BHD-RAG successfully mitigates hallucination risks and achieves superior diagnostic accuracy, generating evidence-based descriptions aligned with expert insights.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and timely diagnosis of Birt-Hogg-Dube syndrome is critical for patient management, as it is a rare genetic disorder with potential for renal cell carcinoma and other comorbidities. This research offers a significant advancement by improving the reliability of BHD diagnosis from CT scans, which is often complicated by its resemblance to other benign DCLDs.

AI Health Application

The AI application is an LLM-aided diagnostic system designed to improve the accuracy of diagnosing Birt-Hogg-Dube Syndrome (BHD) and other Diffuse Cystic Lung Diseases (DCLDs) from CT images by integrating domain-specific knowledge and clinical precedents to reduce hallucination risks and provide evidence-based descriptions.

Key Points

  • Addresses the dual challenges in BHD diagnosis via CT: limited clinical samples and low inter-class differentiation among Diffuse Cystic Lung Diseases (DCLDs).
  • Recognizes the diagnostic potential of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) for rare diseases but highlights their susceptibility to hallucination due to lack of domain-specific knowledge and radiological features.
  • Proposes BHD-RAG, a multimodal retrieval-augmented generation framework, as a solution to integrate DCLD-specific expertise and clinical precedents with MLLMs.
  • BHD-RAG comprises three core components: (1) a specialized agent for generating imaging manifestation descriptions to build a multimodal DCLD corpus, (2) a cosine similarity-based retriever for pinpointing relevant image-description pairs, and (3) an MLLM to synthesize retrieved evidence with imaging data for diagnosis.
  • The framework aims to enhance BHD diagnostic accuracy by grounding MLLM outputs in factual, contextually relevant clinical data.
  • Validation on a dataset encompassing four types of DCLDs demonstrated superior diagnostic accuracy.
  • A key outcome is the generation of evidence-based diagnostic descriptions that closely align with expert radiological interpretations, suggesting improved explainability.

Methodology

The BHD-RAG framework employs a three-stage approach: (1) Corpus Construction: A specialized agent analyzes CT images to generate detailed imaging manifestation descriptions, forming a multimodal corpus of DCLD cases. (2) Retrieval: For a given query CT image, a cosine similarity-based retriever identifies the most relevant image-description pairs from the specialized corpus. (3) Generation: A Multimodal Large Language Model (MLLM) then synthesizes the retrieved clinical evidence and radiological features with the input imaging data to provide an evidence-based BHD diagnosis.

Key Findings

The BHD-RAG framework achieved superior diagnostic accuracy for Birt-Hogg-Dube syndrome when evaluated against other Diffuse Cystic Lung Diseases. Furthermore, it demonstrated the ability to generate evidence-based radiological descriptions that closely correspond to expert insights, enhancing the explainability of the diagnostic process.

Clinical Impact

This methodology has the potential to significantly improve the diagnostic pathway for BHD syndrome, leading to earlier and more accurate diagnoses. It could reduce misdiagnosis rates among DCLDs, guide more effective clinical decision-making, and provide explainable AI outputs that bolster clinician confidence, ultimately improving patient outcomes for this rare disease.

Limitations

The abstract highlights the inherent challenges of deep learning methods in BHD diagnosis, namely limited clinical samples and low inter-class differentiation among DCLDs, which BHD-RAG aims to address. It also notes that MLLMs inherently face hallucination risks due to a lack of domain-specific knowledge without augmentation. The abstract does not explicitly detail limitations or caveats pertaining to the BHD-RAG framework itself.

Abstract

Deep learning methods face dual challenges of limited clinical samples and low inter-class differentiation among Diffuse Cystic Lung Diseases (DCLDs) in advancing Birt-Hogg-Dube syndrome (BHD) diagnosis via Computed Tomography (CT) imaging. While Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) demonstrate diagnostic potential fo such rare diseases, the absence of domain-specific knowledge and referable radiological features intensify hallucination risks. To address this problem, we propose BHD-RAG, a multimodal retrieval-augmented generation framework that integrates DCLD-specific expertise and clinical precedents with MLLMs to improve BHD diagnostic accuracy. BHDRAG employs: (1) a specialized agent generating imaging manifestation descriptions of CT images to construct a multimodal corpus of DCLDs cases. (2) a cosine similarity-based retriever pinpointing relevant imagedescription pairs for query images, and (3) an MLLM synthesizing retrieved evidence with imaging data for diagnosis. BHD-RAG is validated on the dataset involving four types of DCLDs, achieving superior accuracy and generating evidence-based descriptions closely aligned with expert insights.
