Knowledge-Guided Large Language Model for Automatic Pediatric Dental Record Understanding and Safe Antibiotic Recommendation

arXiv ID: 2512.09127v1
Published: 2025-12-09
Authors: Zihan Han, Junyan Ge, Caifeng Li
Categories: cs.CL, cs.AI

Summary

This study introduces a Knowledge-Guided Large Language Model (KG-LLM) designed for automated understanding of pediatric dental records and safe antibiotic recommendation. By integrating a specialized knowledge graph, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and a multi-stage safety validation pipeline, the system significantly improves record interpretation, antibiotic prescription accuracy, and notably reduces unsafe antibiotic suggestions compared to a baseline LLM.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine and health by providing an advanced AI solution to enhance patient safety and treatment efficacy in pediatric dentistry. It directly addresses the problem of medication errors, particularly with antibiotics, in a vulnerable patient population, by ensuring evidence-grounded and safety-validated recommendations.

AI Health Application

The paper develops a Knowledge-Guided Large Language Model (KG-LLM) that utilizes a pediatric dental knowledge graph, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and a multi-stage safety validation pipeline. This AI system automatically interprets unstructured pediatric dental clinical records and radiology reports, extracts clinical entities, summarizes diagnoses, and provides evidence-grounded and safe antibiotic recommendations (drug, dose, duration). It also features a dual-layer validation mechanism to prevent unsafe suggestions related to allergies, contraindications, and dosing errors, thereby acting as an intelligent clinical decision support tool for safe medication prescription in dentistry.

Methodology

The KG-LLM framework operates through a multi-stage pipeline: (1) A clinical Named Entity Recognition (NER) and Relation Extraction (RE) module extracts structured entities and relations from unstructured dental notes and radiology reports. (2) Relevant pediatric dental guidelines, drug-safety rules, and analogous historical cases are retrieved from a custom knowledge graph using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). (3) This structured and retrieved knowledge is then fed into a Large Language Model (LLM) for diagnostic summarization and prediction of the appropriate drug, dose, and duration. (4) Finally, a dual-layer safety validation mechanism, combining deterministic rule-based checks with a learned classifier, scrutinizes the LLM's recommendations for potential allergies, contraindications, and dosing errors before outputting the final suggestion.

Key Findings

The proposed KG-LLM achieved significant improvements over a domain-adapted Llama-2 clinical baseline, demonstrating an F1-score of 0.914 for record understanding (vs. 0.867) and a Top-1 accuracy of 0.782 for drug-dose-duration prediction (vs. 0.716). Most critically, the system reduced unsafe antibiotic suggestions by 50%. Ablation analyses confirmed that the knowledge graph, RAG, and safety modules individually contributed substantially to the system's clinical reliability and interpretability.

Clinical Impact

This KG-LLM has the potential to significantly improve the accuracy and safety of antibiotic prescribing in pediatric dental settings. By automating evidence-grounded recommendations and implementing robust safety checks, it can reduce medication errors, minimize adverse drug reactions, and enhance the quality of patient care. It offers a powerful clinical decision support tool that can assist dentists in navigating complex patient data and clinical guidelines, ultimately leading to better patient outcomes and optimized antibiotic stewardship.

Abstract

Accurate interpretation of pediatric dental clinical records and safe antibiotic prescribing remain persistent challenges in dental informatics. Traditional rule-based clinical decision support systems struggle with unstructured dental narratives, incomplete radiographic descriptions, and complex safety constraints. To address these limitations, this study proposes a Knowledge-Guided Large Language Model (KG-LLM) that integrates a pediatric dental knowledge graph, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and a multi-stage safety validation pipeline for evidence-grounded antibiotic recommendation. The framework first employs a clinical NER/RE module to extract structured entities and relations from dental notes and radiology reports. Relevant guidelines, drug-safety rules, and analogous historical cases are subsequently retrieved from the knowledge graph and supplied to the LLM for diagnostic summarization and dose-drug-duration prediction. Safety assurance is achieved through a dual-layer validation mechanism combining deterministic rule checking with a learned classifier for detecting allergies, contraindications, and dosing errors. Experiments on 32,000 de-identified pediatric dental visit records demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. Compared with a domain-adapted Llama-2 clinical baseline, KG-LLM improves record-understanding performance (F1: 0.914 vs. 0.867), drug-dose-duration accuracy (Top-1: 0.782 vs. 0.716), and reduces unsafe antibiotic suggestions by 50%. Additional evaluation across summary quality, recommendation accuracy, and global safety scores further confirms the robustness of the system. Ablation analyses indicate that the knowledge graph, RAG, and safety modules each contribute substantially to clinical reliability and interpretability.
