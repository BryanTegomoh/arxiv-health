Oversampling techniques for predicting COVID-19 patient length of stay

Summary

This paper addresses the challenge of predicting COVID-19 patient length of stay (LOS) as a measure of disease severity, particularly focusing on the inherent data imbalance where short stays are more common. The proposed methodology leverages synthetic oversampling techniques to balance the training data, subsequently employing an Artificial Neural Network (ANN) whose hyperparameters are optimized using Bayesian optimization, with the final model selected based on its F1 score.

Medical Relevance

Predicting COVID-19 patient length of stay is critical for proactive hospital resource management, optimizing patient flow, and identifying high-risk individuals who may require more intensive care, ultimately improving clinical outcomes and healthcare system efficiency.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using machine learning (Artificial Neural Networks with Bayesian optimization) to analyze electronic health records (EHR) data. The goal is to predict the severity of COVID-19 infection, specifically measuring it by a patient's length of hospital stay (LOS). This serves as a predictive tool for clinical decision support, resource allocation, and patient risk stratification within healthcare settings.

Key Points

  • Predicts COVID-19 infection severity using patient Length of Stay (LOS) as a primary metric, utilizing Electronic Health Records (EHR).
  • Identifies the prediction task as an imbalanced classification problem due to a higher prevalence of shorter hospital stays.
  • Combats data imbalance by synthetically generating alternate oversampled training datasets to ensure adequate representation of longer LOS cases.
  • Employs an Artificial Neural Network (ANN) as the core machine learning model for classification.
  • Utilizes Bayesian optimization during the training phase to efficiently tune the ANN's hyperparameters for optimal performance.
  • Selects the best performing model based on the F1 score, an appropriate evaluation metric for imbalanced datasets.
  • The research aims to evaluate and discuss the developed model's performance in predicting COVID-19 patient LOS.

Methodology

The study analyzes COVID-19 patient Electronic Health Records (EHR) to predict Length of Stay (LOS). To address the imbalanced nature of the dataset, synthetic oversampling techniques are applied to the training data. An Artificial Neural Network (ANN) is then trained on this balanced dataset, with its hyperparameters optimized through Bayesian optimization. The model exhibiting the best F1 score is selected for evaluation and discussion.

Key Findings

The study describes the development and evaluation of a robust predictive model for COVID-19 patient Length of Stay (LOS). It successfully integrates synthetic oversampling with an Artificial Neural Network (ANN) and Bayesian optimization to effectively handle the challenge of imbalanced EHR data, aiming to provide an accurate measure of disease severity, with the best performing model identified by its F1 score.

Clinical Impact

This predictive model offers potential clinical utility by enabling healthcare providers to forecast hospital bed occupancy, anticipate demand for critical resources (e.g., ICU beds, ventilators, specialized staff), and implement early interventions for patients at higher risk of prolonged hospitalization, leading to improved operational planning and personalized patient care.

Abstract

COVID-19 is a respiratory disease that caused a global pandemic in 2019. It is highly infectious and has the following symptoms: fever or chills, cough, shortness of breath, fatigue, muscle or body aches, headache, the new loss of taste or smell, sore throat, congestion or runny nose, nausea or vomiting, and diarrhea. These symptoms vary in severity; some people with many risk factors have been known to have lengthy hospital stays or die from the disease. In this paper, we analyze patients' electronic health records (EHR) to predict the severity of their COVID-19 infection using the length of stay (LOS) as our measurement of severity. This is an imbalanced classification problem, as many people have a shorter LOS rather than a longer one. To combat this problem, we synthetically create alternate oversampled training data sets. Once we have this oversampled data, we run it through an Artificial Neural Network (ANN), which during training has its hyperparameters tuned using Bayesian optimization. We select the model with the best F1 score and then evaluate it and discuss it.
