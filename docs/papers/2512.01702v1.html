A unified framework for geometry-independent operator learning in cardiac electrophysiology simulations

Summary

This paper introduces a novel geometry-independent operator-learning framework that uses a neural operator with a vision-transformer backbone to predict local activation time (LAT) fields in cardiac electrophysiology. It achieves a high accuracy (5.1 ms mean error) and near-instantaneous inference (0.12 ms/sample) across diverse left atrial anatomies, outperforming established methods. The framework decouples electrophysiological patterns from mesh topology using a Universal Atrium Coordinate system, enabling real-time and population-scale computational electrophysiology.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and rapid mapping of atrial electrical activation is critical for personalized treatment of cardiac arrhythmias like atrial fibrillation. This framework transforms computationally intensive simulations into a near-instantaneous process, making advanced electrophysiology models practical for real-time clinical decision support and large-scale patient studies.

Methodology

The researchers generated a massive dataset of 308,700 electrophysiology simulations using a GPU-accelerated solver, incorporating diverse pacing sites, varied conduction properties, and 147 patient-specific left atrial geometries from clinical cohorts. A core innovation is the Universal Atrium Coordinate (UAC) system, which provides a consistent, geometry-independent representation of both anatomical and functional data. Within this UAC space, a neural operator model with a vision-transformer backbone was designed and trained to learn the mapping from structural and electrophysiological inputs to LAT fields.

Key Findings

The developed framework predicts local activation time (LAT) fields with a mean error of 5.1 ms over a 455 ms maximum simulation time. Crucially, it achieves an inference time of just 0.12 ms per sample, which is near-instantaneous and significantly faster than established operator-learning approaches. This demonstrates robust, geometry-independent, and highly efficient prediction capabilities for cardiac electrophysiology.

It also facilitates large-scale patient population analyses to uncover disease mechanisms and develop new treatments, overcoming current computational bottlenecks and advancing personalized medicine in cardiac electrophysiology.

Abstract

Accurate maps of atrial electrical activation are essential for personalised treatment of arrhythmias, yet biophysically detailed simulations remain computationally intensive for real-time clinical use or population-scale analyses. Here we introduce a geometry-independent operator-learning framework that predicts local activation time (LAT) fields across diverse left atrial anatomies with near-instantaneous inference. We generated a dataset of 308,700 simulations using a GPU-accelerated electrophysiology solver, systematically varying multiple pacing sites and physiologically varied conduction properties across 147 patient-specific geometries derived from two independent clinical cohorts. All anatomical and functional data are expressed in a Universal Atrium Coordinate system, providing a consistent representation that decouples electrophysiological patterns from mesh topology. Within this coordinate space, we designed a neural operator with a vision-transformer backbone to learn the mapping from structural and electrophysiological inputs to LAT fields. With a mean prediction error of 5.1 ms over a 455 ms maximum simulation time, the model outperforms established operator-learning approaches and performs inference in 0.12 ms per sample. Our framework establishes a general strategy for learning domain-invariant biophysical mappings across variable anatomical domains and enables integration of computational electrophysiology into real-time and large-scale clinical workflows.
