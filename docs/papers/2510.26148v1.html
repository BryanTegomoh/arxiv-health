STAR: A Privacy-Preserving, Energy-Efficient Edge AI Framework for Human Activity Recognition via Wi-Fi CSI in Mobile and Pervasive Computing Environments

arXiv ID: 2510.26148v1
Published: 2025-10-30
Authors: Kexing Liu
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces STAR, an edge-AI optimized framework for real-time, energy-efficient, and privacy-preserving Human Activity Recognition (HAR) using Wi-Fi Channel State Information (CSI) in mobile and pervasive computing environments. It achieves high recognition accuracy (93.52% for activities, 99.11% for presence) on low-power embedded devices through a lightweight GRU-based neural network, advanced signal processing, and NPU-accelerated inference. This framework offers a practical and scalable solution for applications like healthcare monitoring, overcoming the computational limitations of existing methods.

Medical Relevance

This technology provides a contactless, privacy-preserving, and energy-efficient solution for continuous monitoring of human activities and presence, which is critically important for remote healthcare monitoring, elderly care, and patient safety without requiring intrusive cameras or cumbersome wearables.

AI Health Application

The paper presents STAR, an edge-AI-optimized framework that leverages a lightweight recurrent neural network (GRU-based) and advanced signal processing for real-time, privacy-preserving, and energy-efficient Human Activity Recognition (HAR) using Wi-Fi CSI. This AI application is designed to function on low-power embedded devices, making it ideal for continuous, unobtrusive monitoring in healthcare settings such as smart homes for the elderly, where it can detect activities, presence, and potentially critical events like falls, while respecting privacy and conserving energy.

Key Points

  • STAR is an edge-AI optimized framework designed to overcome computational inefficiency and high latency of existing HAR methods on resource-constrained embedded devices using Wi-Fi CSI.
  • It integrates a lightweight Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)-based recurrent neural network, reducing model parameters by 33% compared to conventional LSTM models while maintaining effective temporal modeling.
  • A multi-stage pre-processing pipeline, including median filtering, 8th-order Butterworth low-pass filtering, and Empirical Mode Decomposition (EMD), is employed to denoise CSI amplitude data and extract spatial-temporal features.
  • The system is implemented on a Rockchip RV1126 processor equipped with an embedded Neural Processing Unit (NPU) for accelerated inference, interfaced with an ESP32-S3-based CSI acquisition module.
  • Experimental results demonstrate a mean recognition accuracy of 93.52% across seven activity classes and 99.11% for human presence detection, utilizing a compact 97.6k-parameter model.
  • INT8 quantized inference on the NPU achieves a processing speed of 33 MHz with just 8% CPU utilization, delivering sixfold speed improvements over CPU-based execution with sub-second latency.
  • The framework provides real-time, privacy-preserving HAR with low power consumption, making it suitable for practical, scalable deployment in healthcare monitoring and smart home applications.

Methodology

STAR employs a lightweight Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)-based recurrent neural network architecture with 97.6k parameters. Raw Wi-Fi CSI amplitude data undergoes a multi-stage pre-processing pipeline that includes median filtering, an 8th-order Butterworth low-pass filter, and Empirical Mode Decomposition (EMD) for denoising and spatial-temporal feature extraction. The inference engine is deployed on a Rockchip RV1126 processor utilizing its embedded Neural Processing Unit (NPU) for INT8 quantized model execution, enabling energy-efficient and high-speed processing. CSI data acquisition is performed using an ESP32-S3-based module.

Key Findings

The STAR framework achieved a mean activity recognition accuracy of 93.52% across seven distinct activity classes and 99.11% accuracy for human presence detection. Its compact 97.6k-parameter GRU model, when deployed with INT8 quantization on an NPU, demonstrated a processing speed of 33 MHz with only 8% CPU utilization, resulting in a sixfold speed improvement over CPU-based execution. The system delivered sub-second response latency and low power consumption, confirming its real-time and energy-efficient capabilities.

Clinical Impact

The STAR framework offers a significant clinical impact by enabling unobtrusive, continuous monitoring of patient activities and presence in diverse healthcare settings, such as smart homes and assisted living facilities. By providing real-time, privacy-preserving HAR without requiring cameras or wearables, it can facilitate early detection of changes in activity patterns, prevent falls, monitor adherence to rehabilitation exercises, and enhance overall patient safety and well-being, particularly for elderly or vulnerable populations.

Abstract

Human Activity Recognition (HAR) via Wi-Fi Channel State Information (CSI) presents a privacy-preserving, contactless sensing approach suitable for smart homes, healthcare monitoring, and mobile IoT systems. However, existing methods often encounter computational inefficiency, high latency, and limited feasibility within resource-constrained, embedded mobile edge environments. This paper proposes STAR (Sensing Technology for Activity Recognition), an edge-AI-optimized framework that integrates a lightweight neural architecture, adaptive signal processing, and hardware-aware co-optimization to enable real-time, energy-efficient HAR on low-power embedded devices. STAR incorporates a streamlined Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)-based recurrent neural network, reducing model parameters by 33% compared to conventional LSTM models while maintaining effective temporal modeling capability. A multi-stage pre-processing pipeline combining median filtering, 8th-order Butterworth low-pass filtering, and Empirical Mode Decomposition (EMD) is employed to denoise CSI amplitude data and extract spatial-temporal features. For on-device deployment, STAR is implemented on a Rockchip RV1126 processor equipped with an embedded Neural Processing Unit (NPU), interfaced with an ESP32-S3-based CSI acquisition module. Experimental results demonstrate a mean recognition accuracy of 93.52% across seven activity classes and 99.11% for human presence detection, utilizing a compact 97.6k-parameter model. INT8 quantized inference achieves a processing speed of 33 MHz with just 8% CPU utilization, delivering sixfold speed improvements over CPU-based execution. With sub-second response latency and low power consumption, the system ensures real-time, privacy-preserving HAR, offering a practical, scalable solution for mobile and pervasive computing environments.
