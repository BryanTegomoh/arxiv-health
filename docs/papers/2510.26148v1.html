STAR: A Privacy-Preserving, Energy-Efficient Edge AI Framework for Human Activity Recognition via Wi-Fi CSI in Mobile and Pervasive Computing Environments

arXiv ID: 2510.26148v1
Published: 2025-10-30
Authors: Kexing Liu
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

STAR proposes an energy-efficient, privacy-preserving edge-AI framework for Human Activity Recognition (HAR) using Wi-Fi Channel State Information (CSI) in resource-constrained environments. It integrates a lightweight GRU-based neural network with adaptive signal processing and hardware co-optimization, achieving high accuracy (93.52% for 7 activities) and real-time performance on embedded devices. This solution offers a practical and scalable approach for applications like healthcare monitoring, overcoming the computational inefficiency and high latency of previous methods.

Medical Relevance

This framework offers a privacy-preserving, contactless, and energy-efficient method for continuous monitoring of human activity and presence, which is vital for remote healthcare, geriatric care (e.g., fall detection), and smart hospital systems, enabling non-intrusive patient management without compromising personal data.

AI Health Application

STAR is an Edge AI framework for real-time, privacy-preserving, and energy-efficient human activity recognition (HAR) using Wi-Fi CSI. This AI application can be used for continuous monitoring in healthcare settings, such as detecting falls in elderly individuals, tracking activity levels for chronic disease management, or observing behavioral patterns in smart homes for health and safety purposes, all while preserving privacy due to its contactless nature.

Key Points

  • Addresses computational inefficiency, high latency, and limited feasibility of existing Wi-Fi CSI-based HAR on resource-constrained embedded mobile edge environments.
  • Proposes STAR, an edge-AI-optimized framework featuring a streamlined Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)-based recurrent neural network, reducing model parameters by 33% compared to conventional LSTM models to 97.6k.
  • Employs a multi-stage pre-processing pipeline for CSI amplitude data, including median filtering, 8th-order Butterworth low-pass filtering, and Empirical Mode Decomposition (EMD) for robust denoising and spatio-temporal feature extraction.
  • Implemented on a Rockchip RV1126 processor with an embedded Neural Processing Unit (NPU) and an ESP32-S3-based CSI acquisition module, demonstrating hardware-aware co-optimization.
  • Achieves a mean recognition accuracy of 93.52% across seven distinct activity classes and 99.11% for human presence detection.
  • Delivers significant performance gains with INT8 quantized inference, achieving a processing speed of 33 MHz with just 8% CPU utilization, translating to a sixfold speed improvement over CPU-based execution.
  • Ensures real-time, privacy-preserving HAR with sub-second response latency and low power consumption, making it suitable for continuous monitoring in sensitive environments like healthcare.

Methodology

The STAR framework utilizes a lightweight Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)-based recurrent neural network architecture for temporal modeling, designed for reduced computational overhead. Prior to neural network processing, raw Wi-Fi CSI amplitude data undergoes a multi-stage pre-processing pipeline: median filtering for initial noise suppression, an 8th-order Butterworth low-pass filter to remove high-frequency noise, and Empirical Mode Decomposition (EMD) for extracting robust spatial-temporal features. The system is deployed on a Rockchip RV1126 processor with an integrated Neural Processing Unit (NPU) for accelerated inference, receiving CSI data from an ESP32-S3-based acquisition module. Hardware-aware co-optimization, including INT8 quantization, is applied to achieve efficient on-device execution.

Key Findings

The system achieves a mean recognition accuracy of 93.52% for seven activity classes and 99.11% for human presence detection. It utilizes a compact 97.6k-parameter model, demonstrating a 33% reduction compared to conventional LSTM models. INT8 quantized inference on the NPU delivers a processing speed of 33 MHz with only 8% CPU utilization, resulting in a sixfold speed improvement over CPU-based execution. The framework ensures sub-second response latency and low power consumption, enabling real-time operation on embedded devices.

Clinical Impact

STAR provides a non-intrusive, real-time, and energy-efficient solution for continuous patient monitoring in clinical and home environments. Its privacy-preserving nature makes it highly suitable for sensitive applications like elderly fall detection, tracking patient mobility for rehabilitation assessment, and general activity monitoring in smart homes or assisted living facilities. The low latency and high accuracy can enable timely alerts and interventions, enhancing patient safety and facilitating proactive care management without the need for wearable devices or visually intrusive cameras.

Abstract

Human Activity Recognition (HAR) via Wi-Fi Channel State Information (CSI) presents a privacy-preserving, contactless sensing approach suitable for smart homes, healthcare monitoring, and mobile IoT systems. However, existing methods often encounter computational inefficiency, high latency, and limited feasibility within resource-constrained, embedded mobile edge environments. This paper proposes STAR (Sensing Technology for Activity Recognition), an edge-AI-optimized framework that integrates a lightweight neural architecture, adaptive signal processing, and hardware-aware co-optimization to enable real-time, energy-efficient HAR on low-power embedded devices. STAR incorporates a streamlined Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)-based recurrent neural network, reducing model parameters by 33% compared to conventional LSTM models while maintaining effective temporal modeling capability. A multi-stage pre-processing pipeline combining median filtering, 8th-order Butterworth low-pass filtering, and Empirical Mode Decomposition (EMD) is employed to denoise CSI amplitude data and extract spatial-temporal features. For on-device deployment, STAR is implemented on a Rockchip RV1126 processor equipped with an embedded Neural Processing Unit (NPU), interfaced with an ESP32-S3-based CSI acquisition module. Experimental results demonstrate a mean recognition accuracy of 93.52% across seven activity classes and 99.11% for human presence detection, utilizing a compact 97.6k-parameter model. INT8 quantized inference achieves a processing speed of 33 MHz with just 8% CPU utilization, delivering sixfold speed improvements over CPU-based execution. With sub-second response latency and low power consumption, the system ensures real-time, privacy-preserving HAR, offering a practical, scalable solution for mobile and pervasive computing environments.
