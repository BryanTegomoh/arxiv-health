Summary

This paper provides a comprehensive overview of biological engineering, an interdisciplinary field merging engineering and biology, and structures it into bioinspired, biological, and biohybrid approaches. It analyzes inherent challenges and opportunities, proposing data-driven discovery and artificial intelligence as crucial tools to overcome the lack of reductionist models. Furthermore, it addresses the necessary educational frameworks for a new generation of biological engineers.

Medical Relevance

Biological engineering is pivotal for significant advances in healthcare, driving innovations in diagnostics, therapeutics, and regenerative medicine by applying sophisticated engineering principles to biological systems and complex health challenges.

AI Health Application

Artificial intelligence is described as a tool for data-driven discovery and design in biological engineering. This directly applies to health by accelerating the development of new treatments, diagnostics, and bio-inspired solutions in healthcare.

  • Data-driven discovery and design, in conjunction with artificial intelligence, are proposed as essential strategies to mitigate the limitations posed by the lack of reductionist models.

Methodology

This paper employs a comprehensive conceptual review and analytical approach, structuring a broad interdisciplinary field into defined categories. It dissects challenges and opportunities within these categories and proposes theoretical frameworks and educational imperatives rather than presenting new experimental data or empirical findings.

Key Findings

The paper's key findings highlight that biological engineering's complexity necessitates a categorization into bioinspired, biological, and biohybrid approaches. A central finding is that the field's advancement is heavily reliant on leveraging data-driven discovery and artificial intelligence to circumvent the inherent absence of comprehensive reductionist biological models. Furthermore, it concludes that a redefined educational paradigm, emphasizing mathematical, technical, and AI competencies, is critical for the future success and innovation within biological engineering.

Clinical Impact

The advancements discussed in biological engineering, particularly through bioinspired and biohybrid approaches augmented by AI, hold profound potential to revolutionize clinical practice. This includes enabling novel therapeutic strategies, developing more precise and rapid diagnostics, engineering advanced drug delivery systems, and creating enhanced regenerative medicine solutions for a wide array of diseases and injuries, ultimately leading to improved patient outcomes.

Limitations

The paper notes the 'absence of reductionist models' as a fundamental and inherent limitation within the field of biological engineering itself, requiring alternative data-driven and AI-based solutions. As a conceptual review, it does not present new experimental data or empirical validation of specific biological engineering applications.

Future Directions

Future efforts in biological engineering should primarily focus on deepening the integration of data-driven discovery and artificial intelligence into research and development to effectively address the challenges posed by complex biological systems. Concurrently, there is an imperative to continuously update and advance educational curricula to equip emerging biological engineers with robust mathematical, technical, and AI proficiencies essential for future innovation.

Abstract

Biological engineering, the convergence between engineering and biology, is at the forefront of significant advances in healthcare, agriculture, and environmental sustainability, making it highly relevant to current scientific and societal challenges. We take a comprehensive look at this broad and interdisciplinary domain, structure it into three main areas - bioinspired, biological and biohybrid approaches - and dissect inherent and fundamental challenges along with opportunities, highlighting specific examples. We describe how data-driven discovery and design, in conjunction with artificial intelligence, can mitigate the absence of reductionist models in these areas. Additionally, we address the education of a new generation of biological engineers, emphasizing mathematical, technical, and artificial intelligence frameworks.
