QuPCG: Quantum Convolutional Neural Network for Detecting Abnormal Patterns in PCG Signals

arXiv ID: 2511.02140v1
Published: 2025-11-04
Authors: Yasaman Torabi, Shahram Shirani, James P. Reilly
Categories: cs.LG, quant-ph
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces QuPCG, a novel hybrid quantum-classical convolutional neural network (QCNN) designed to classify S3 and murmur abnormalities in heart sound signals. It transforms one-dimensional phonocardiogram (PCG) signals into compact 8-pixel two-dimensional images using wavelet feature extraction and adaptive threshold compression, requiring only 8 qubits. Preliminary results on the HLS-CMDS dataset demonstrate high classification accuracy, suggesting the efficiency of quantum models in capturing temporal-spectral correlations for bioacoustic signal processing.

Medical Relevance

Early and accurate detection of cardiac abnormalities like S3 sounds and murmurs from heart sound signals is critical for timely diagnosis and intervention in cardiac diseases. This technology offers a potentially more efficient and powerful diagnostic tool, especially in settings with limited resources, by leveraging quantum computing principles.

AI Health Application

The paper describes a Quantum Convolutional Neural Network (QCNN) that uses PCG signals to automatically detect and classify specific cardiac abnormalities (S3 and murmurs). This represents a medical AI application for automated diagnosis and decision support in cardiology.

Key Points

  • Introduces QuPCG, a hybrid quantum-classical convolutional neural network (QCNN) for cardiac abnormality detection.
  • Specifically targets the classification of S3 sounds and murmur abnormalities in phonocardiogram (PCG) signals.
  • Transforms 1D PCG signals into compact 8-pixel 2D images using wavelet feature extraction and adaptive threshold compression.
  • The compression strategy efficiently utilizes quantum resources, requiring only 8 qubits for the quantum stage.
  • Achieved 93.33% classification accuracy on the test set and 97.14% on the train set using the HLS-CMDS dataset.
  • Represents the first known application of a QCNN algorithm for bioacoustic signal processing.
  • Aims to contribute to quantum-enhanced diagnostic systems, particularly for resource-constrained healthcare environments.

Methodology

The proposed method utilizes a hybrid quantum-classical convolutional neural network (QCNN). It preprocesses one-dimensional phonocardiogram (PCG) signals by applying wavelet feature extraction to capture crucial temporal-spectral information. These features are then subjected to an adaptive threshold compression method, transforming them into highly compact 8-pixel two-dimensional images. This extreme compression allows the quantum stage of the network to operate with only 8 qubits, making it resource-efficient. The QCNN subsequently processes these compressed images to classify specific cardiac abnormalities such as S3 sounds and murmurs.

Key Findings

The QuPCG model demonstrated high efficacy on the HLS-CMDS dataset, achieving a classification accuracy of 93.33% on the test set and 97.14% on the training set. These preliminary results strongly suggest that quantum models possess a significant capability to efficiently capture and analyze complex temporal-spectral correlations inherent in biomedical signals, specifically heart sounds.

Clinical Impact

This technology holds potential to significantly enhance the early and accurate diagnosis of cardiac conditions like S3 sounds and murmurs, which could lead to earlier clinical intervention and improved patient outcomes. Its design for resource-constrained healthcare environments suggests applicability in settings where advanced computational infrastructure might be limited, potentially broadening access to sophisticated diagnostic capabilities. It represents a foundational step towards integrating quantum computing into medical diagnostics.

Limitations

The reported results are described as "preliminary," indicating a need for further validation on larger and more diverse datasets, as well as potentially more extensive clinical trials. The method is also characterized as an "early step," suggesting it is not yet a fully matured or clinically deployable system and may require further development and refinement before widespread practical application.

Future Directions

Future work involves further advancing quantum-enhanced diagnostic systems based on this approach. This includes exploring its application to a wider range of bioacoustic and other biomedical signal processing tasks. There is also potential to scale and refine the method for broader clinical implementation, particularly focusing on optimizing its utility and accessibility in resource-constrained healthcare settings.

Abstract

Early identification of abnormal physiological patterns is essential for the timely detection of cardiac disease. This work introduces a hybrid quantum-classical convolutional neural network (QCNN) designed to classify S3 and murmur abnormalities in heart sound signals. The approach transforms one-dimensional phonocardiogram (PCG) signals into compact two-dimensional images through a combination of wavelet feature extraction and adaptive threshold compression methods. We compress the cardiac-sound patterns into an 8-pixel image so that only 8 qubits are needed for the quantum stage. Preliminary results on the HLS-CMDS dataset demonstrate 93.33% classification accuracy on the test set and 97.14% on the train set, suggesting that quantum models can efficiently capture temporal-spectral correlations in biomedical signals. To our knowledge, this is the first application of a QCNN algorithm for bioacoustic signal processing. The proposed method represents an early step toward quantum-enhanced diagnostic systems for resource-constrained healthcare environments.
