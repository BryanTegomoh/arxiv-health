Collaborative Multi-Agent Test-Time Reinforcement Learning for Reasoning

Summary

Multi-Agent Test-Time Reinforcement Learning (MATTRL) is introduced to address the instability and resource intensiveness of traditional multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) for LLM-driven systems. This framework enhances multi-agent deliberation at inference time by forming specialist teams, integrating retrieved textual experiences, and utilizing credit assignment for robust, accurate decision-making. It demonstrates significant accuracy improvements across medical, math, and education benchmarks.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a more stable and accurate AI reasoning framework, which is crucial for sensitive medical applications where errors can have severe consequences. By enabling LLM-driven agents to learn and adapt during inference without resource-intensive retraining, it can lead to more reliable diagnostic tools, personalized treatment recommendations, and advanced medical education systems that can handle real-world variability.

AI Health Application

The MATTRL framework, when applied to medical benchmarks, enhances the accuracy of multi-agent LLM systems in their reasoning processes. This could be applied to improve AI collaborators for tasks such as assisting medical professionals in complex diagnoses, refining treatment plans through multi-perspective AI deliberation, or supporting medical education by providing more accurate reasoning in problem-solving scenarios. It offers a more robust and stable approach to building AI systems that can leverage diverse information and reach consensus in healthcare settings.

Key Points

  • MATTRL addresses core challenges of MARL, including resource intensity, instability due to non-stationarity, and issues with sparse/high-variance rewards.
  • The framework operates at inference time, injecting structured textual experience directly into multi-agent deliberation processes.
  • It involves forming a multi-expert team of specialists engaging in multi-turn discussions, followed by retrieval and integration of test-time experiences.
  • A crucial component is credit assignment, which constructs a turn-level experience pool that is then reinjected into the dialogue for refined reasoning.
  • MATTRL achieved an average accuracy improvement of 3.67% over multi-agent baselines and 8.67% over single-agent baselines across challenging benchmarks.
  • The system is designed to be stable, effective, efficient, and robust to distribution shifts, notably without requiring model tuning.
  • Ablation studies were conducted to analyze the impact of different credit-assignment schemes on training outcomes.

Methodology

MATTRL forms a multi-expert team of LLM agents designed for multi-turn discussions. During inference, it dynamically retrieves and integrates structured textual experiences into the agents' deliberation. A core methodological aspect is the implementation of credit assignment to create and then reinject a turn-level experience pool into the dialogue. This process allows agents to refine their reasoning and reach consensus for final decision-making, adapting to new information or scenarios without requiring full model retraining.

Key Findings

MATTRL significantly improves reasoning accuracy, achieving an average increase of 3.67% compared to existing multi-agent baselines and a substantial 8.67% over comparable single-agent baselines. These gains were demonstrated across demanding benchmarks, including those in the medical field. The framework is shown to be stable, efficient, and robust to distribution shifts, offering an effective solution for multi-agent reasoning without the need for explicit model tuning.

Abstract

Multi-agent systems have evolved into practical LLM-driven collaborators for many applications, gaining robustness from diversity and cross-checking. However, multi-agent RL (MARL) training is resource-intensive and unstable: co-adapting teammates induce non-stationarity, and rewards are often sparse and high-variance. Therefore, we introduce \textbf{Multi-Agent Test-Time Reinforcement Learning (MATTRL)}, a framework that injects structured textual experience into multi-agent deliberation at inference time. MATTRL forms a multi-expert team of specialists for multi-turn discussions, retrieves and integrates test-time experiences, and reaches consensus for final decision-making. We also study credit assignment for constructing a turn-level experience pool, then reinjecting it into the dialogue. Across challenging benchmarks in medicine, math, and education, MATTRL improves accuracy by an average of 3.67\% over a multi-agent baseline, and by 8.67\% over comparable single-agent baselines. Ablation studies examine different credit-assignment schemes and provide a detailed comparison of how they affect training outcomes. MATTRL offers a stable, effective and efficient path to distribution-shift-robust multi-agent reasoning without tuning.
