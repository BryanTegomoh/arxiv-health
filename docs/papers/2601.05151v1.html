Summary

ROOFS is a Python package designed to assist researchers in selecting optimal feature selection (FS) methods for biomarker discovery in complex biomedical datasets. It benchmarks various FS approaches using comprehensive metrics like predictive performance (with optimism correction), stability, and reliability. Applied to a lung cancer immunotherapy resistance dataset, ROOFS identified a specific p-value based filter as superior, demonstrating its capacity to enhance the robustness and translational value of clinical predictive models.

Medical Relevance

This tool directly tackles a major hurdle in precision medicine and clinical research by providing a robust framework to identify reliable biomarkers from complex patient data, crucial for developing effective diagnostic, prognostic, and predictive models, particularly in areas like cancer immunotherapy.

AI Health Application

ROOFS is a Python package that enables researchers to robustly apply and benchmark machine learning (specifically, feature selection) methods to high-dimensional biomedical datasets. This directly supports the development of AI-driven predictive models for clinical applications, such as identifying biomarkers for disease prognosis, treatment response prediction (e.g., resistance to immunotherapy in lung cancer), and ultimately enhancing precision medicine. It acts as a meta-tool for optimizing the machine learning pipeline in medical research.

Methodology

ROOFS provides a Python package for benchmarking diverse feature selection (FS) methods. It systematically evaluates up to 34 FS algorithms in combination with various classifiers, generating detailed reports based on multiple metrics. These metrics include downstream predictive performance (estimated with optimism correction), feature stability, reliability of individual features, and true/false positive rates assessed on semi-synthetic data with a simulated outcome. The PIONeeR clinical trial application involved an initial iterative Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) pre-filtering step, followed by the evaluation of 23 FS methods with 11 classifiers (253 models) on a dataset of 214 features.

Key Findings

For the PIONeeR clinical trial dataset, which sought predictors for anti-PD-(L)1 immunotherapy resistance in lung cancer, `roofs` identified a specific filter-based approach as optimal. This method involved taking the union of Benjamini-Hochberg False Discovery Rate (FDR)-adjusted p-values derived from t-tests and logistic regression. This combination demonstrated superior predictive performance and robustness, notably outperforming other widely used methods, including LASSO.

Clinical Impact

`roofs` has the potential to significantly enhance the reliability and reproducibility of biomarker identification in clinical studies. By enabling researchers to systematically select the most appropriate feature selection method, it can lead to more robust and accurate predictive models for patient stratification, prognosis, and treatment response (e.g., predicting immunotherapy resistance), thereby accelerating the translation of research findings into improved clinical practice and personalized healthcare.

Abstract

Feature selection (FS) is essential for biomarker discovery and in the analysis of biomedical datasets. However, challenges such as high-dimensional feature space, low sample size, multicollinearity, and missing values make FS non-trivial. Moreover, FS performances vary across datasets and predictive tasks. We propose roofs, a Python package available at https://gitlab.inria.fr/compo/roofs, designed to help researchers in the choice of FS method adapted to their problem. Roofs benchmarks multiple FS methods on the user's data and generates reports that summarize a comprehensive set of evaluation metrics, including downstream predictive performance estimated using optimism correction, stability, reliability of individual features, and true positive and false positive rates assessed on semi-synthetic data with a simulated outcome. We demonstrate the utility of roofs on data from the PIONeeR clinical trial, aimed at identifying predictors of resistance to anti-PD-(L)1 immunotherapy in lung cancer. The PIONeeR dataset contained 374 multi-source blood and tumor biomarkers from 435 patients. A reduced subset of 214 features was obtained through iterative variance inflation factor pre-filtering. Of the 34 FS methods gathered in roofs, we evaluated 23 in combination with 11 classifiers (253 models in total) and identified a filter based on the union of Benjamini-Hochberg false discovery rate-adjusted p-values from t-test and logistic regression as the optimal approach, outperforming other methods including the widely used LASSO. We conclude that comprehensive benchmarking with roofs has the potential to improve the robustness and reproducibility of FS discoveries and increase the translational value of clinical models.
