Dental3R: Geometry-Aware Pairing for Intraoral 3D Reconstruction from Sparse-View Photographs

arXiv ID: 2511.14315v1
Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Yiyi Miao, Taoyu Wu, Tong Chen, Ji Jiang, Zhe Tang, Zhengyong Jiang, Angelos Stefanidis, Limin Yu, Jionglong Su
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00

Summary

Dental3R is a novel pose-free, graph-guided pipeline designed for robust, high-fidelity 3D reconstruction of intraoral structures from sparse, unposed smartphone photographs. It addresses critical challenges in tele-orthodontics by integrating a Geometry-Aware Pairing Strategy for stable geometry initialization and wavelet-regularized 3D Gaussian Splatting to preserve fine anatomical details. Validation on large clinical datasets demonstrates its superior novel view synthesis quality for dental occlusion visualization compared to state-of-the-art methods.

Medical Relevance

This innovation is crucial for the advancement of tele-orthodontics and digital dentistry, enabling remote diagnosis, treatment planning, and monitoring by generating accurate 3D dental models from accessible smartphone images. It expands access to specialized orthodontic care, particularly in underserved or remote areas.

AI Health Application

The research develops an AI-powered (Computer Vision, 3D reconstruction, Gaussian Splatting) pipeline, Dental3R, for high-fidelity 3D reconstruction of intraoral structures from sparse smartphone photographs. This enables remote diagnostics, treatment planning, and monitoring in orthodontics and dentistry, overcoming accessibility barriers of conventional scanning for tele-orthodontics by preserving critical diagnostic details for dental occlusion visualization.

Key Points

  • Addresses the inaccessibility of conventional intraoral scanners for remote tele-orthodontics, leveraging sparse smartphone imagery as input.
  • Proposes a **Geometry-Aware Pairing Strategy (GAPS)** to intelligently select a compact subgraph of high-value image pairs, enhancing correspondence matching, stabilizing geometry initialization, and reducing memory usage.
  • Utilizes **3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS)** for novel view synthesis, building upon recovered camera poses and an initial point cloud.
  • Introduces a **wavelet-regularized objective** during 3DGS training, employing a discrete wavelet transform to enforce band-limited fidelity. This prevents over-smoothing and preserves fine details like enamel boundaries and interproximal edges.
  • Validated on a large-scale dataset of 950 clinical cases and an additional video-based test set of 195 cases, demonstrating robustness to sparse, unposed inputs, large view baselines, inconsistent illumination, and specular surfaces.
  • Achieves superior novel view synthesis quality for dental occlusion visualization compared to existing state-of-the-art methods, effectively handling challenging intraoral photographic conditions.

Methodology

Dental3R employs a pose-free, graph-guided pipeline. First, a Geometry-Aware Pairing Strategy (GAPS) identifies optimal image pairs for robust correspondence matching and initial geometry estimation. Based on the recovered camera poses and initial point cloud, a 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) model is trained. Crucially, the 3DGS training incorporates a wavelet-regularized objective, utilizing a discrete wavelet transform to ensure band-limited fidelity, thereby preserving fine details and preventing the over-smoothing common in sparse-view photometric supervision.

Key Findings

Dental3R robustly reconstructs high-fidelity 3D intraoral models from challenging sparse, unposed smartphone photographs, effectively overcoming issues like large view baselines and specular surfaces. The wavelet regularization successfully preserves critical diagnostic details such as fine enamel boundaries and interproximal edges. The method significantly outperforms state-of-the-art techniques in novel view synthesis quality, particularly for dental occlusion visualization.

Clinical Impact

This technology holds significant potential to democratize access to orthodontic care by facilitating accurate 3D model generation from everyday smartphone imagery, thereby supporting remote consultations, diagnosis, and treatment planning. It can reduce the logistical burden and cost associated with in-clinic intraoral scanning, making digital orthodontics more accessible and efficient for both practitioners and patients globally.

Limitations

The paper addresses significant limitations inherent in applying standard 3D Gaussian Splatting or other sparse-view reconstruction methods to intraoral photographs, specifically the instability caused by large view baselines, inconsistent illumination, and specular surfaces, as well as the 'frequency bias' leading to over-smoothed reconstructions and loss of diagnostic detail. The abstract does not explicitly list limitations of Dental3R itself, but rather positions Dental3R as a solution to these existing challenges.

Abstract

Intraoral 3D reconstruction is fundamental to digital orthodontics, yet conventional methods like intraoral scanning are inaccessible for remote tele-orthodontics, which typically relies on sparse smartphone imagery. While 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) shows promise for novel view synthesis, its application to the standard clinical triad of unposed anterior and bilateral buccal photographs is challenging. The large view baselines, inconsistent illumination, and specular surfaces common in intraoral settings can destabilize simultaneous pose and geometry estimation. Furthermore, sparse-view photometric supervision often induces a frequency bias, leading to over-smoothed reconstructions that lose critical diagnostic details. To address these limitations, we propose \textbf{Dental3R}, a pose-free, graph-guided pipeline for robust, high-fidelity reconstruction from sparse intraoral photographs. Our method first constructs a Geometry-Aware Pairing Strategy (GAPS) to intelligently select a compact subgraph of high-value image pairs. The GAPS focuses on correspondence matching, thereby improving the stability of the geometry initialization and reducing memory usage. Building on the recovered poses and point cloud, we train the 3DGS model with a wavelet-regularized objective. By enforcing band-limited fidelity using a discrete wavelet transform, our approach preserves fine enamel boundaries and interproximal edges while suppressing high-frequency artifacts. We validate our approach on a large-scale dataset of 950 clinical cases and an additional video-based test set of 195 cases. Experimental results demonstrate that Dental3R effectively handles sparse, unposed inputs and achieves superior novel view synthesis quality for dental occlusion visualization, outperforming state-of-the-art methods.
