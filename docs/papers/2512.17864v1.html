Interpretable Plant Leaf Disease Detection Using Attention-Enhanced CNN

arXiv ID: 2512.17864v1
Published: 2025-12-19
Authors: Balram Singh, Ram Prakash Sharma, Somnath Dey
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.70 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This study introduces CBAM-VGG16, an interpretable attention-guided Convolutional Neural Network, for highly accurate plant leaf disease detection. By integrating Convolution Block Attention Module (CBAM) at each convolutional stage, the model achieves enhanced feature extraction, disease localization, and robust generalization across five diverse plant disease datasets, demonstrating superior performance (up to 98.87% accuracy) compared to existing techniques. The approach also emphasizes explainability through various visualization methods, advancing transparent AI in agricultural diagnostics.

Methodology

The study proposes CBAM-VGG16, a modified VGG16 architecture integrated with Convolution Block Attention Modules (CBAM) at each convolutional stage. CBAM modules enhance both channel and spatial attention, improving the model's ability to extract salient features and localize disease regions. The model was trained and extensively evaluated on five distinct plant disease datasets. For interpretability, various methods including CBAM attention maps, Grad-CAM, Grad-CAM++, and Layer-wise Relevance Propagation (LRP) were employed to visualize and explain the model's decision-making process.

Key Findings

The CBAM-VGG16 model achieved state-of-the-art performance in plant leaf disease detection, with accuracies reaching up to 98.87%. The integration of CBAM significantly improved feature extraction and demonstrated robust generalization across diverse plant disease datasets. The comprehensive interpretability analysis successfully visualized the model's focus, validating its ability to accurately localize disease regions on plant leaves and providing transparency to its predictions.

Abstract

Plant diseases pose a significant threat to global food security, necessitating accurate and interpretable disease detection methods. This study introduces an interpretable attention-guided Convolutional Neural Network (CNN), CBAM-VGG16, for plant leaf disease detection. By integrating Convolution Block Attention Module (CBAM) at each convolutional stage, the model enhances feature extraction and disease localization. Trained on five diverse plant disease datasets, our approach outperforms recent techniques, achieving high accuracy (up to 98.87%) and demonstrating robust generalization. Here, we show the effectiveness of our method through comprehensive evaluation and interpretability analysis using CBAM attention maps, Grad-CAM, Grad-CAM++, and Layer-wise Relevance Propagation (LRP). This study advances the application of explainable AI in agricultural diagnostics, offering a transparent and reliable system for smart farming. The code of our proposed work is available at https://github.com/BS0111/PlantAttentionCBAM.
