Blood Pressure Prediction for Coronary Artery Disease Diagnosis using Coronary Computed Tomography Angiography

arXiv ID: 2512.10765v1
Published: 2025-12-11
Authors: Rene Lisasi, Michele Esposito, Chen Zhao
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces an end-to-end pipeline and a novel diffusion-based regression model designed to predict coronary blood pressure directly from Coronary Computed Tomography Angiography (CCTA) derived features. The objective is to overcome the computational expense and time constraints of traditional Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations, enabling rapid, non-invasive hemodynamic assessment for Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) diagnosis. Evaluated on simulated data, the model achieved state-of-the-art performance, demonstrating a scalable and accessible framework for generating crucial diagnostic markers.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly enhances the non-invasive diagnosis of Coronary Artery Disease by offering a fast and accessible method to predict critical coronary blood pressure metrics directly from CCTA scans. This approach could streamline diagnostic workflows, reduce the need for more invasive or computationally intensive procedures, and facilitate earlier, physiology-based assessment of CAD.

AI Health Application

The paper develops a diffusion-based regression AI model to predict coronary blood pressure directly from CCTA-derived features. This AI application aims to bypass computationally expensive CFD simulations, providing a rapid and non-invasive method for diagnosing Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) and integrating physiology-based assessment into clinical workflows, thereby improving diagnostic efficiency and patient care.

Methodology

The methodology involves two main components: first, an automated end-to-end pipeline extracts coronary geometry from CCTA and then streamlines the generation of simulated coronary hemodynamic data (e.g., pressure gradients) via CFD, creating a consistent and labeled dataset. Second, a novel diffusion-based regression model is trained on this generated dataset to learn the mapping from CCTA-derived features to coronary blood pressure distributions, thereby enabling direct prediction without computationally expensive CFD during clinical inference.

Key Findings

The proposed diffusion-based regression model demonstrated state-of-the-art performance in predicting coronary blood pressure. When evaluated on a dataset of simulated coronary hemodynamics, it achieved an R2 score of 64.42%, a Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) of 0.0974, and a normalized RMSE of 0.154, significantly outperforming several established baseline prediction methods.

Clinical Impact

This work has the potential to profoundly impact clinical practice by enabling rapid, non-invasive assessment of coronary hemodynamics, a key factor in CAD diagnosis, directly from widely available CCTA scans. This can lead to more efficient diagnostic pathways, reduced reliance on invasive angiography or cumbersome CFD simulations, and provide clinicians with crucial physiological information earlier to guide treatment decisions and improve patient management for CAD.

Abstract

Computational fluid dynamics (CFD) based simulation of coronary blood flow provides valuable hemodynamic markers, such as pressure gradients, for diagnosing coronary artery disease (CAD). However, CFD is computationally expensive, time-consuming, and difficult to integrate into large-scale clinical workflows. These limitations restrict the availability of labeled hemodynamic data for training AI models and hinder broad adoption of non-invasive, physiology based CAD assessment. To address these challenges, we develop an end to end pipeline that automates coronary geometry extraction from coronary computed tomography angiography (CCTA), streamlines simulation data generation, and enables efficient learning of coronary blood pressure distributions. The pipeline reduces the manual burden associated with traditional CFD workflows while producing consistent training data. We further introduce a diffusion-based regression model designed to predict coronary blood pressure directly from CCTA derived features, bypassing the need for slow CFD computation during inference. Evaluated on a dataset of simulated coronary hemodynamics, the proposed model achieves state of the art performance, with an R2 of 64.42%, a root mean squared error of 0.0974, and a normalized RMSE of 0.154, outperforming several baseline approaches. This work provides a scalable and accessible framework for rapid, non-invasive blood pressure prediction to support CAD diagnosis.
