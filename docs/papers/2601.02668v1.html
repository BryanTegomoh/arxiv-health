MAFS: Multi-head Attention Feature Selection for High-Dimensional Data via Deep Fusion of Filter Methods

Summary

MAFS (Multi-head Attention-based Feature Selection) is a novel hybrid framework that integrates statistical filter methods with deep learning's multi-head attention to perform robust and interpretable feature selection for high-dimensional biomedical data. It addresses the limitations of existing approaches by providing superior coverage and stability, particularly demonstrated in cancer gene expression and Alzheimer's disease datasets.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and interpretable feature selection in high-dimensional biomedical datasets is crucial for identifying reliable biomarkers, understanding disease mechanisms, and developing more effective personalized diagnostics and treatments in precision medicine.

AI Health Application

This paper develops an AI/ML methodology (Multi-head Attention Feature Selection) that enhances the capability to build more accurate, stable, and interpretable predictive models from high-dimensional biomedical data. These models are crucial for various health applications, including AI-driven disease diagnosis, prognosis, risk stratification, personalized treatment recommendations, and the identification of novel biomarkers in fields like cancer research and neurodegenerative diseases.

Methodology

MAFS is a hybrid deep learning framework composed of three main stages. It begins by leveraging filter-based methods to establish statistical priors, which serve for stable initialization and guide the learning process. Subsequently, a multi-head attention mechanism is utilized to analyze features concurrently from multiple perspectives, enabling the capture of intricate nonlinear relationships and interactions. Finally, a reordering module processes and consolidates the outputs from the different attention heads, resolving potential conflicts and minimizing information loss to produce robust and consistent feature importance rankings.

Key Findings

MAFS consistently demonstrates superior coverage and stability in feature selection when compared to both traditional filter-based methods and contemporary deep learning-based alternatives. It successfully integrates statistical guidance with deep modeling capacity, yielding interpretable importance scores while maximizing the retention of informative signals, particularly effective in ultra-high-dimensional biomedical datasets such as cancer gene expression and Alzheimer's disease data.

This could lead to improved diagnostic tools, more targeted therapeutic strategies for diseases like cancer and Alzheimer's, and a deeper understanding of complex biological processes, ultimately benefiting patient care and outcomes.

Abstract

Feature selection is essential for high-dimensional biomedical data, enabling stronger predictive performance, reduced computational cost, and improved interpretability in precision medicine applications. Existing approaches face notable challenges. Filter methods are highly scalable but cannot capture complex relationships or eliminate redundancy. Deep learning-based approaches can model nonlinear patterns but often lack stability, interpretability, and efficiency at scale. Single-head attention improves interpretability but is limited in capturing multi-level dependencies and remains sensitive to initialization, reducing reproducibility. Most existing methods rarely combine statistical interpretability with the representational power of deep learning, particularly in ultra-high-dimensional settings. Here, we introduce MAFS (Multi-head Attention-based Feature Selection), a hybrid framework that integrates statistical priors with deep learning capabilities. MAFS begins with filter-based priors for stable initialization and guide learning. It then uses multi-head attention to examine features from multiple perspectives in parallel, capturing complex nonlinear relationships and interactions. Finally, a reordering module consolidates outputs across attention heads, resolving conflicts and minimizing information loss to generate robust and consistent feature rankings. This design combines statistical guidance with deep modeling capacity, yielding interpretable importance scores while maximizing retention of informative signals. Across simulated and real-world datasets, including cancer gene expression and Alzheimer's disease data, MAFS consistently achieves superior coverage and stability compared with existing filter-based and deep learning-based alternatives, offering a scalable, interpretable, and robust solution for feature selection in high-dimensional biomedical data.
