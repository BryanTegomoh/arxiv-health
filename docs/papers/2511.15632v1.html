Summary

CODE-II is a large-scale, real-world dataset comprising 2,735,269 12-lead ECGs from over 2 million adult patients, meticulously annotated with 66 clinically meaningful diagnostic classes validated by cardiologists, specifically designed to advance AI in ECG analysis. A neural network pre-trained on CODE-II demonstrated superior transfer performance on external benchmarks, outperforming models trained on other larger datasets.

Medical Relevance

This dataset provides an invaluable, high-quality resource for developing and validating advanced artificial intelligence models for ECG interpretation, which is critical for improving the accuracy and efficiency of cardiac diagnosis, screening, and telehealth services globally.

AI Health Application

The AI application described is the development and improvement of artificial intelligence models for automated or assisted interpretation and diagnosis of electrocardiograms (ECGs). This can enhance diagnostic accuracy, efficiency, and access to cardiac care, particularly in telehealth settings, by providing AI-driven analysis of heart conditions.

Key Points

  • The CODE-II dataset contains 2,735,269 12-lead ECGs from 2,093,807 adult patients, collected by the Telehealth Network of Minas Gerais (TNMG), Brazil.
  • Each ECG exam was annotated using standardized diagnostic criteria and subsequently reviewed by cardiologists, ensuring high-quality and clinically reliable labels.
  • A defining feature is a set of 66 clinically meaningful diagnostic classes, developed in collaboration with cardiologists and routinely utilized in telehealth practice.
  • The dataset includes an open-access subset, CODE-II-open (15,000 patients), and a robust test set, CODE-II-test (8,475 non-overlapping exams), which was reviewed by multiple cardiologists for blinded evaluation.
  • Neural networks pre-trained on CODE-II achieved superior transfer performance when evaluated on external benchmark datasets (PTB-XL and CPSC 2018).
  • The performance of models trained on CODE-II surpassed that of alternative models trained on other, potentially larger, datasets, highlighting the quality and clinical value of CODE-II.

Methodology

The CODE-II dataset was assembled from real-world 12-lead ECGs collected via a telehealth network. Each ECG underwent annotation based on standardized diagnostic criteria and was subsequently reviewed by cardiologists. A unique set of 66 clinically relevant diagnostic classes was developed with cardiologist input. The utility of CODE-II was demonstrated by training a neural network on it and evaluating its transfer performance on well-known external ECG benchmarks (PTB-XL, CPSC 2018).

Key Findings

The CODE-II dataset addresses critical limitations in existing ECG datasets by offering a massive scale, real-world context, and high-quality, clinically validated annotations across 66 distinct diagnostic classes. Crucially, AI models pre-trained on CODE-II demonstrate superior generalizability and achieve better transfer performance on external benchmarks compared to models trained on other large datasets, indicating its effectiveness for robust AI development.

Clinical Impact

CODE-II has the potential to significantly accelerate the development of highly accurate and reliable AI-powered ECG interpretation systems. This can lead to earlier and more precise detection of a wide range of cardiac conditions, enhance clinical decision support, alleviate the workload on cardiologists, and broaden access to expert ECG analysis, particularly within telehealth environments and underserved regions.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations of the CODE-II dataset itself. Instead, it highlights that CODE-II was developed to address existing limitations (in annotation quality, size, and scope) prevalent in *other* large datasets used for AI-based ECG analysis.

Future Directions

While not explicitly detailed as future research, the paper implicitly suggests that CODE-II will serve as a foundational resource for advancing the development of more robust, generalizable, and clinically impactful AI models for ECG analysis, paving the way for improved diagnostic accuracy and broader implementation of AI in cardiology and telehealth practices.

Abstract

Data-driven methods for electrocardiogram (ECG) interpretation are rapidly progressing. Large datasets have enabled advances in artificial intelligence (AI) based ECG analysis, yet limitations in annotation quality, size, and scope remain major challenges. Here we present CODE-II, a large-scale real-world dataset of 2,735,269 12-lead ECGs from 2,093,807 adult patients collected by the Telehealth Network of Minas Gerais (TNMG), Brazil. Each exam was annotated using standardized diagnostic criteria and reviewed by cardiologists. A defining feature of CODE-II is a set of 66 clinically meaningful diagnostic classes, developed with cardiologist input and routinely used in telehealth practice. We additionally provide an open available subset: CODE-II-open, a public subset of 15,000 patients, and the CODE-II-test, a non-overlapping set of 8,475 exams reviewed by multiple cardiologists for blinded evaluation. A neural network pre-trained on CODE-II achieved superior transfer performance on external benchmarks (PTB-XL and CPSC 2018) and outperformed alternatives trained on larger datasets.
