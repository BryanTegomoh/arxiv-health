Summary

CODE-II is a groundbreaking large-scale dataset comprising over 2.7 million 12-lead ECGs from 2 million adult patients, featuring 66 clinically meaningful diagnostic classes meticulously annotated by cardiologists. A neural network pre-trained on CODE-II achieved superior transfer performance on external benchmarks, outperforming models trained on even larger datasets, thereby significantly advancing AI-based ECG analysis by addressing existing limitations in data quality and scope.

Medical Relevance

This dataset provides a critical resource for developing and validating highly accurate artificial intelligence models for ECG interpretation, which can lead to improved diagnostic precision, earlier detection of cardiac conditions, and enhanced clinical decision support, particularly in telehealth environments.

AI Health Application

The paper facilitates the development and improvement of Artificial Intelligence models for automated and accurate interpretation of Electrocardiograms (ECGs). These AI applications can assist clinicians in diagnosing cardiac conditions, enhance diagnostic efficiency, reduce the workload on cardiologists, and potentially improve access to specialized cardiac diagnostics, particularly within telehealth frameworks.

Key Points

  • CODE-II provides 2,735,269 12-lead ECGs from 2,093,807 adult patients, collected from the real-world Telehealth Network of Minas Gerais, Brazil.
  • The dataset is uniquely annotated with 66 clinically meaningful diagnostic classes, developed with cardiologist input, using standardized criteria and expert review.
  • Annotation quality is high, with each exam reviewed by cardiologists, addressing common limitations of other large datasets in terms of label accuracy and clinical relevance.
  • Two public subsets are available: CODE-II-open (15,000 patients) for general research and CODE-II-test (8,475 non-overlapping exams) with multi-cardiologist blinded evaluation for robust benchmarking.
  • A neural network pre-trained on CODE-II demonstrated superior transfer performance on external benchmark datasets (PTB-XL and CPSC 2018).
  • The CODE-II pre-trained model outperformed alternative AI models, even those trained on larger existing datasets, highlighting the dataset's efficacy for developing advanced ECG interpretation algorithms.

Methodology

The methodology involved the retrospective collection of 2,735,269 12-lead ECGs from over 2 million adult patients from the Telehealth Network of Minas Gerais. These ECGs were then annotated using standardized diagnostic criteria to derive 66 clinically meaningful diagnostic classes, with each annotation reviewed by cardiologists. The utility of CODE-II was demonstrated by pre-training a neural network on the dataset and evaluating its transfer performance on external, established benchmarks (PTB-XL and CPSC 2018), comparing its efficacy against models trained on other large datasets.

Key Findings

The key finding is the successful creation and validation of CODE-II as a high-quality, large-scale dataset for ECG AI. A neural network pre-trained on CODE-II exhibited superior transfer performance on external benchmarks (PTB-XL and CPSC 2018) compared to existing AI models, including those trained on other larger datasets. This underscores CODE-II's unique value in addressing critical data limitations for robust AI development in ECG analysis.

Clinical Impact

CODE-II's comprehensive and cardiologist-validated annotations, combined with its vast scale, will enable the development of more accurate and generalizable AI algorithms for automated ECG interpretation. This has the potential to streamline diagnostic workflows, improve the early detection of a wide range of cardiac abnormalities, reduce the burden on clinicians, particularly in resource-constrained or telehealth settings, and ultimately enhance patient management and outcomes.

Limitations

The abstract primarily highlights CODE-II's strengths in addressing existing limitations of other datasets (annotation quality, size, scope) and does not explicitly detail any inherent limitations of CODE-II itself. Potential implicit limitations (not mentioned in the abstract) could include specific demographic representation biases within the Minas Gerais patient population or the exact distribution and prevalence of the 66 diagnostic classes.

Future Directions

While not explicitly stated in the abstract, the demonstrated success in transfer learning suggests future research directions could involve leveraging CODE-II to develop more specialized AI models for various cardiac conditions, exploring its applicability in different healthcare systems, integrating it with other clinical data for multi-modal AI, and fostering collaborative efforts to further expand and validate the dataset across diverse populations.

Abstract

Data-driven methods for electrocardiogram (ECG) interpretation are rapidly progressing. Large datasets have enabled advances in artificial intelligence (AI) based ECG analysis, yet limitations in annotation quality, size, and scope remain major challenges. Here we present CODE-II, a large-scale real-world dataset of 2,735,269 12-lead ECGs from 2,093,807 adult patients collected by the Telehealth Network of Minas Gerais (TNMG), Brazil. Each exam was annotated using standardized diagnostic criteria and reviewed by cardiologists. A defining feature of CODE-II is a set of 66 clinically meaningful diagnostic classes, developed with cardiologist input and routinely used in telehealth practice. We additionally provide an open available subset: CODE-II-open, a public subset of 15,000 patients, and the CODE-II-test, a non-overlapping set of 8,475 exams reviewed by multiple cardiologists for blinded evaluation. A neural network pre-trained on CODE-II achieved superior transfer performance on external benchmarks (PTB-XL and CPSC 2018) and outperformed alternatives trained on larger datasets.
