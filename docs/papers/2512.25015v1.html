Summary

This paper introduces MAMAMemeia, a novel multi-agent multi-aspect discussion framework grounded in Cognitive Analytic Therapy (CAT) Competencies, for identifying depressive symptoms in memes shared on social media. It also presents RESTOREx, a vital resource of Large Language Model (LLM) generated and human-annotated explanations, demonstrating a substantial 7.55% improvement in macro-F1 over state-of-the-art methods and establishing a new benchmark.

Methodology

The methodology involves creating RESTOREx, a resource of Large Language Model (LLM) generated and human-annotated explanations for depressive symptoms in memes. This resource is utilized by MAMAMemeia, a collaborative multi-agent multi-aspect discussion framework built upon Cognitive Analytic Therapy (CAT) Competencies, to identify and classify depressive symptoms in social media memes. Performance is evaluated using macro-F1 against over 30 state-of-the-art methods.

Abstract

Over the past years, memes have evolved from being exclusively a medium of humorous exchanges to one that allows users to express a range of emotions freely and easily. With the ever-growing utilization of memes in expressing depressive sentiments, we conduct a study on identifying depressive symptoms exhibited by memes shared by users of online social media platforms. We introduce RESTOREx as a vital resource for detecting depressive symptoms in memes on social media through the Large Language Model (LLM) generated and human-annotated explanations. We introduce MAMAMemeia, a collaborative multi-agent multi-aspect discussion framework grounded in the clinical psychology method of Cognitive Analytic Therapy (CAT) Competencies. MAMAMemeia improves upon the current state-of-the-art by 7.55% in macro-F1 and is established as the new benchmark compared to over 30 methods.
