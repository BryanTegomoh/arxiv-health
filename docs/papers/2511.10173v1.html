CephRes-MHNet: A Multi-Head Residual Network for Accurate and Robust Cephalometric Landmark Detection

arXiv ID: 2511.10173v1
Published: 2025-11-13
Authors: Ahmed Jaheen, Islam Hassan, Mohanad Abouserie, Abdelaty Rehab, Adham Elasfar, Knzy Elmasry, Mostafa El-Dawlatly, Seif Eldawlatly

Summary

This paper introduces CephRes-MHNet, a multi-head residual convolutional network designed for accurate and robust cephalometric landmark detection from 2D lateral skull X-rays. It achieves state-of-the-art performance with a Mean Radial Error of 1.23 mm and a Success Detection Rate @ 2.0 mm of 85.5%, outperforming previous models like AFPF-Net with significantly fewer parameters, thus offering an efficient and practical solution for orthodontic diagnosis.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant for orthodontic and dental practices, as it provides an advanced, automated tool to accurately identify critical cephalometric landmarks, thereby streamlining diagnostic workflows, reducing human error, and improving the precision of treatment planning for patients.

AI Health Application

The AI application is an automated system (CephRes-MHNet) that uses a multi-head residual convolutional network to accurately detect and localize cephalometric landmarks on 2D lateral skull X-rays. This assists orthodontists in the diagnosis of craniofacial anomalies, treatment planning for malocclusions, and monitoring treatment progress, replacing or augmenting time-consuming and error-prone manual annotation.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of accurate and efficient automated cephalometric landmark detection from 2D lateral skull X-rays, aiming to overcome manual annotation's time-consuming and error-prone nature.
  • Introduces CephRes-MHNet, a novel multi-head residual convolutional network specifically engineered for robustness and efficiency in detecting cephalometric landmarks.
  • The architecture incorporates residual encoding for robust feature learning, dual-attention mechanisms to enhance contextual reasoning, and multi-head decoders for precise anatomical localization.
  • Trained and validated on the Aariz Cephalometric dataset, comprising 1,000 diverse radiographs.
  • Achieved a Mean Radial Error (MRE) of 1.23 mm and a Success Detection Rate (SDR) @ 2.0 mm of 85.5%, establishing new state-of-the-art accuracy.
  • Significantly outperformed the strongest baseline, the attention-driven AFPF-Net (MRE = 1.25 mm, SDR @ 2.0 mm = 84.1%), while demonstrating superior architectural efficiency by utilizing less than 25% of its parameters.
  • The model's high accuracy and efficiency make it a practical and valuable tool for real-world orthodontic diagnosis and treatment planning.

Methodology

The study proposes CephRes-MHNet, a multi-head residual convolutional network architecture. This network leverages residual encoding for robust feature extraction, integrates dual-attention mechanisms to refine feature maps and improve contextual understanding, and employs multi-head decoders for simultaneous and precise prediction of multiple cephalometric landmark coordinates. The model was trained and evaluated on the Aariz Cephalometric dataset, consisting of 1,000 2D lateral skull X-rays, using Mean Radial Error (MRE) and Success Detection Rate (SDR) @ 2.0 mm as key performance metrics for comparison against existing models.

Key Findings

CephRes-MHNet demonstrated superior performance, achieving a Mean Radial Error (MRE) of 1.23 mm and a Success Detection Rate (SDR) @ 2.0 mm of 85.5%. These results surpass all evaluated baseline models, including the strong AFPF-Net, which recorded an MRE of 1.25 mm and an SDR @ 2.0 mm of 84.1%. Crucially, CephRes-MHNet achieved this higher accuracy with significantly greater architectural efficiency, using less than 25% of the parameters required by AFPF-Net.

Clinical Impact

The development of CephRes-MHNet has substantial clinical impact by offering an automated, highly accurate, and efficient solution for cephalometric landmark detection in orthodontics. This can lead to faster diagnostic processes, reduced inter-observer variability among clinicians, and more consistent and precise treatment planning, ultimately benefiting patient care. Its computational efficiency makes it highly practical for integration into existing clinical software and hardware systems.

Abstract

Accurate localization of cephalometric landmarks from 2D lateral skull X-rays is vital for orthodontic diagnosis and treatment. Manual annotation is time-consuming and error-prone, whereas automated approaches often struggle with low contrast and anatomical complexity. This paper introduces CephRes-MHNet, a multi-head residual convolutional network for robust and efficient cephalometric landmark detection. The architecture integrates residual encoding, dual-attention mechanisms, and multi-head decoders to enhance contextual reasoning and anatomical precision. Trained on the Aariz Cephalometric dataset of 1,000 radiographs, CephRes-MHNet achieved a mean radial error (MRE) of 1.23 mm and a success detection rate (SDR) @ 2.0 mm of 85.5%, outperforming all evaluated models. In particular, it exceeded the strongest baseline, the attention-driven AFPF-Net (MRE = 1.25 mm, SDR @ 2.0 mm = 84.1%), while using less than 25% of its parameters. These results demonstrate that CephRes-MHNet attains state-of-the-art accuracy through architectural efficiency, providing a practical solution for real-world orthodontic analysis.
