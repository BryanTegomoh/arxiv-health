Simultaneous optimization of non-coplanar beam orientations and cumulative EQD2 distribution for high-dose reirradiation of locoregionally recurrent non-small cell lung cancer

arXiv ID: 2510.26272v1
Published: 2025-10-30
Authors: Nathan Torelli, Jonas Willmann, Katja Daehler, Madalyne Day, Nicolaus Andratschke, Jan Unkelbach
Categories: physics.med-ph
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This study developed and evaluated a novel algorithm for simultaneous optimization of non-coplanar beam orientations and cumulative EQD2 distribution for high-dose reirradiation of locoregionally recurrent non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). It demonstrated that non-coplanar plans can significantly reduce maximum cumulative EQD2 to critical organs-at-risk (OARs) compared to conventional coplanar techniques, while maintaining target coverage. This advancement aims to improve the therapeutic window for challenging reirradiation cases.

Medical Relevance

Reirradiation for recurrent NSCLC is a complex treatment with high risks of cumulative toxicity to vital thoracic organs. This research offers a computational method to potentially improve patient safety by reducing OAR doses, thereby expanding treatment options and allowing for potentially more aggressive or effective tumor control while mitigating side effects.

AI Health Application

The paper describes an 'EQD2-based direct aperture optimization algorithm' for 'simultaneous optimization of the dynamic gantry-couch path and the cumulative EQD2 distribution' and the 'automated selection of favorable non-coplanar beam orientations'. This constitutes an application of computational optimization (a subfield of AI) to medical treatment planning, specifically in radiation oncology. It automates and enhances the precision of radiotherapy delivery, aiming for improved patient-specific outcomes by leveraging algorithmic intelligence.

Key Points

  • Reirradiation for locoregionally recurrent NSCLC is commonly performed with coplanar techniques, prompting investigation into non-coplanar approaches to limit OAR doses.
  • An in-silico study was conducted on 15 challenging high-dose reirradiation cases of NSCLC.
  • Previous treatment dose distributions were rigidly registered to the reirradiation CT and converted to equivalent dose in 2 Gy fractions (EQD2) for cumulative dose assessment.
  • A novel 2-arc non-coplanar reirradiation planning algorithm was developed, allowing simultaneous optimization of dynamic gantry-couch paths and cumulative EQD2 distribution.
  • Non-coplanar reirradiation plans were benchmarked against state-of-the-art 2-arc coplanar VMAT plans.
  • Non-coplanar plans significantly reduced the maximum cumulative EQD2 to critical OARs (e.g., bronchial tree, esophagus, thoracic wall, trachea) by at least 5 Gy2 for 6 out of 15 patients (40%).
  • Target coverage and lung EQD2 metrics remained comparable between non-coplanar and coplanar methods.

Methodology

This was an in-silico study involving 15 cases of locoregionally recurrent NSCLC undergoing high-dose reirradiation. Previous treatment dose distributions were mapped to the reirradiation planning CT using rigid dose registration and subsequently converted to cumulative EQD2. A novel 2-arc non-coplanar reirradiation plan was generated using a direct aperture optimization algorithm that simultaneously optimizes the dynamic gantry-couch path and the cumulative EQD2 distribution. These non-coplanar plans were benchmarked against 2-arc coplanar VMAT plans, which represent current clinical practice.

Key Findings

The study's primary finding is that the automated selection of favorable non-coplanar beam orientations significantly reduced the maximum cumulative EQD2 to critical OARs such as the bronchial tree, esophagus, thoracic wall, and trachea by at least 5 Gy2 in 40% (6 out of 15) of the challenging reirradiation cases. Importantly, these OAR sparing benefits were achieved while maintaining comparable target coverage and lung EQD2 metrics relative to conventional coplanar VMAT plans.

Clinical Impact

This automated non-coplanar beam orientation optimization offers a promising strategy to significantly improve the therapeutic window for high-dose reirradiation of recurrent NSCLC. By effectively limiting cumulative doses to critical OARs, it has the potential to reduce treatment-related toxicities, thereby enhancing patient safety and quality of life. Furthermore, this enhanced OAR sparing could enable clinicians to explore dose escalation to the tumor, potentially improving local control outcomes without increasing toxicity.

Limitations

Not explicitly mentioned in the abstract, but as an in-silico (computational) study, clinical validation in prospective trials is essential. The reliance on rigid dose registration for previous treatment doses might not fully account for anatomical changes or organ motion between treatment courses.

Future Directions

The authors suggest that the demonstrated OAR sparing capability allows for exploring either further OAR sparing or dose-escalation strategies in future clinical studies, with the ultimate goal of translating these theoretical benefits into improved patient outcomes in recurrent NSCLC reirradiation.

Abstract

Background and Purpose: Reirradiation for non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) is commonly delivered using coplanar techniques. In this study, we developed a beam orientation optimization algorithm for reirradiation planning to investigate whether the selection of favorable non-coplanar beam orientations may limit cumulative doses to critical organs-at-risk (OARs) and thus improve the therapeutic window. Materials and Methods: Fifteen cases of challenging high-dose reirradiation for locoregionally recurrent NSCLC were included in this in-silico study. For each patient, the dose distribution from the previous treatment was first mapped to the reirradiation planning CT using rigid dose registration, and subsequently converted to equivalent dose in 2 Gy fractions (EQD2). A 2-arc non-coplanar reirradiation plan, combining dynamic gantry and couch rotation, was then generated using an EQD2-based direct aperture optimization algorithm, which allows for the simultaneous optimization of the dynamic gantry-couch path and the cumulative EQD2 distribution. Non-coplanar reirradiation plans were benchmarked against 2-arc coplanar VMAT plans, which mimic state-of-the-art practice for reirradiation of NSCLC. Results: Non-coplanar reirradiation plans could reduce the maximum cumulative EQD2 to critical OARs such as bronchial tree, esophagus, thoracic wall and trachea by at least 5 Gy2 for 6 out of 15 patients compared to coplanar reirradiation plans. At the same time, target coverage and lung EQD2 metrics were comparable for both methods. Conclusions: The automated selection of favorable non-coplanar beam orientations may reduce the maximum cumulative EQD2 to critical OARs in challenging thoracic reirradiation cases. This allows to explore either better OAR sparing or dose-escalation in future clinical studies.
