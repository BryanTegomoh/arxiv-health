Simultaneous optimization of non-coplanar beam orientations and cumulative EQD2 distribution for high-dose reirradiation of locoregionally recurrent non-small cell lung cancer

arXiv ID: 2510.26272v1
Published: 2025-10-30
Authors: Nathan Torelli, Jonas Willmann, Katja Daehler, Madalyne Day, Nicolaus Andratschke, Jan Unkelbach
Categories: physics.med-ph
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This study developed an EQD2-based direct aperture optimization algorithm for non-coplanar beam orientation in high-dose reirradiation of locoregionally recurrent non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). It showed that non-coplanar planning could reduce maximum cumulative equivalent dose in 2 Gy fractions (EQD2) to critical organs-at-risk (OARs) like the bronchial tree, esophagus, thoracic wall, and trachea by at least 5 Gy2 in challenging cases. This was achieved without compromising target coverage or lung EQD2, suggesting an improved therapeutic window for these complex treatments.

Medical Relevance

Reirradiation of recurrent NSCLC is often limited by the cumulative dose tolerance of critical OARs. This optimized non-coplanar approach offers a method to enhance OAR sparing, potentially allowing for safer delivery of high reirradiation doses or dose escalation, thereby improving treatment outcomes for patients with limited options.

AI Health Application

The paper describes an 'EQD2-based direct aperture optimization algorithm' for radiation therapy planning. This algorithm simultaneously optimizes the dynamic gantry-couch path and the cumulative equivalent dose distribution to achieve more favorable non-coplanar beam orientations. This is an application of computational optimization and AI techniques to automatically select and plan medical treatments (reirradiation for lung cancer) with the goal of improving patient safety by sparing organs-at-risk while maintaining target coverage.

Key Points

  • Reirradiation for NSCLC commonly utilizes coplanar radiotherapy techniques, which may be suboptimal for OAR sparing in complex, high-dose scenarios.
  • A novel EQD2-based direct aperture optimization algorithm was developed, enabling simultaneous optimization of dynamic gantry-couch paths and cumulative EQD2 distribution for non-coplanar reirradiation plans.
  • The study was an in-silico analysis involving 15 challenging high-dose reirradiation cases of locoregionally recurrent NSCLC.
  • Previous treatment dose distributions were rigidly registered to reirradiation planning CTs and converted to EQD2 for cumulative dose assessment.
  • Non-coplanar reirradiation plans were benchmarked against 2-arc coplanar VMAT plans, which represent state-of-the-art practice.
  • Non-coplanar plans significantly reduced the maximum cumulative EQD2 to critical OARs (bronchial tree, esophagus, thoracic wall, trachea) by at least 5 Gy2 for 6 out of 15 patients (40%) compared to coplanar plans.
  • Target coverage and overall lung EQD2 metrics were found to be comparable between the non-coplanar and coplanar planning approaches.

Methodology

An in-silico study was conducted on 15 locoregionally recurrent NSCLC reirradiation cases. Prior treatment dose was registered to the reirradiation planning CT and converted to EQD2. A novel EQD2-based direct aperture optimization algorithm was used to generate 2-arc non-coplanar plans, simultaneously optimizing dynamic gantry and couch rotation, and cumulative EQD2. These plans were then compared against conventional 2-arc coplanar VMAT plans.

Key Findings

Non-coplanar reirradiation plans demonstrated a reduction of at least 5 Gy2 in maximum cumulative EQD2 to critical OARs (bronchial tree, esophagus, thoracic wall, and trachea) for 6 out of 15 patients compared to coplanar plans. Importantly, target coverage and lung EQD2 metrics were maintained at comparable levels across both planning methods.

Clinical Impact

This automated selection of favorable non-coplanar beam orientations has the potential to significantly improve the therapeutic window in challenging thoracic reirradiation cases. It could enable better sparing of critical OARs, or facilitate dose escalation to the tumor, leading to potentially improved local control and reduced toxicity for patients undergoing reirradiation for recurrent NSCLC.

Limitations

The study is an in-silico investigation, and therefore requires prospective clinical validation to confirm its safety and efficacy in a real-world setting.

Future Directions

The findings suggest the potential to explore either enhanced OAR sparing or dose escalation strategies in future clinical studies utilizing this non-coplanar beam optimization approach for reirradiation.

Abstract

Background and Purpose: Reirradiation for non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) is commonly delivered using coplanar techniques. In this study, we developed a beam orientation optimization algorithm for reirradiation planning to investigate whether the selection of favorable non-coplanar beam orientations may limit cumulative doses to critical organs-at-risk (OARs) and thus improve the therapeutic window. Materials and Methods: Fifteen cases of challenging high-dose reirradiation for locoregionally recurrent NSCLC were included in this in-silico study. For each patient, the dose distribution from the previous treatment was first mapped to the reirradiation planning CT using rigid dose registration, and subsequently converted to equivalent dose in 2 Gy fractions (EQD2). A 2-arc non-coplanar reirradiation plan, combining dynamic gantry and couch rotation, was then generated using an EQD2-based direct aperture optimization algorithm, which allows for the simultaneous optimization of the dynamic gantry-couch path and the cumulative EQD2 distribution. Non-coplanar reirradiation plans were benchmarked against 2-arc coplanar VMAT plans, which mimic state-of-the-art practice for reirradiation of NSCLC. Results: Non-coplanar reirradiation plans could reduce the maximum cumulative EQD2 to critical OARs such as bronchial tree, esophagus, thoracic wall and trachea by at least 5 Gy2 for 6 out of 15 patients compared to coplanar reirradiation plans. At the same time, target coverage and lung EQD2 metrics were comparable for both methods. Conclusions: The automated selection of favorable non-coplanar beam orientations may reduce the maximum cumulative EQD2 to critical OARs in challenging thoracic reirradiation cases. This allows to explore either better OAR sparing or dose-escalation in future clinical studies.
