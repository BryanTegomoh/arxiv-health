Toward Secure and Compliant AI: Organizational Standards and Protocols for NLP Model Lifecycle Management

Summary

This paper introduces the Secure and Compliant NLP Lifecycle Management Framework (SC-NLP-LMF), a comprehensive six-phase model addressing the security, privacy, and regulatory compliance risks of NLP systems in sensitive domains like healthcare. Developed through a PRISMA-based review and aligned with leading AI governance standards, the framework provides a practical, lifecycle-wide structure for developing, deploying, and maintaining secure and accountable NLP systems.

Medical Relevance

This framework is critically important for healthcare, where NLP systems process vast amounts of sensitive patient data, clinical notes, and research information. Ensuring the security, privacy (e.g., HIPAA compliance), and unbiased, accurate performance of these systems is vital for patient safety, data integrity, and adherence to stringent medical regulations.

AI Health Application

The paper provides a framework for the secure and compliant lifecycle management of NLP models in healthcare. This applies to AI applications such as processing clinical notes and electronic health records for insights, aiding in clinical decision support, identifying emerging health trends (like new disease terminology or outbreaks), and ensuring that these AI systems handle sensitive patient data ethically, securely, and in compliance with regulations (e.g., HIPAA, EU AI Act for high-risk medical devices). The framework addresses critical aspects like bias detection, privacy protection (differential privacy, federated learning), explainability, and secure deployment relevant to medical AI.

Methodology

The Secure and Compliant NLP Lifecycle Management Framework (SC-NLP-LMF) was developed using a systematic PRISMA-based review methodology, analyzing 45 peer-reviewed articles and regulatory sources. This approach aimed to synthesize existing knowledge and standards into a comprehensive, multi-phase framework for secure and compliant NLP model management.

Key Findings

The primary finding is the successful development and articulation of the SC-NLP-LMF, a six-phase framework that effectively addresses the complex challenges of security, privacy, and compliance for NLP systems throughout their operational lifecycle. It demonstrates the ability to integrate leading global standards and practical methods for bias detection, advanced privacy protection techniques (differential privacy, federated learning), secure deployment protocols, model explainability, and secure decommissioning, as evidenced by its application in a healthcare case study on managing terminology drift.

Clinical Impact

The SC-NLP-LMF holds significant potential to enhance the trustworthiness and reliability of NLP applications in clinical settings. It provides a structured, actionable framework to ensure patient data privacy in accordance with regulations like HIPAA, mitigate algorithmic bias in diagnostic or treatment NLP models, and enable dynamic, compliant updates for NLP systems to adapt to evolving medical terminology (e.g., new diseases, drug classifications). This ultimately contributes to improved patient care accuracy, data security, and clinical accountability.

Abstract

Natural Language Processing (NLP) systems are increasingly used in sensitive domains such as healthcare, finance, and government, where they handle large volumes of personal and regulated data. However, these systems introduce distinct risks related to security, privacy, and regulatory compliance that are not fully addressed by existing AI governance frameworks. This paper introduces the Secure and Compliant NLP Lifecycle Management Framework (SC-NLP-LMF), a comprehensive six-phase model designed to ensure the secure operation of NLP systems from development to retirement. The framework, developed through a systematic PRISMA-based review of 45 peer-reviewed and regulatory sources, aligns with leading standards, including NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001:2023, the EU AI Act, and MITRE ATLAS. It integrates established methods for bias detection, privacy protection (differential privacy, federated learning), secure deployment, explainability, and secure model decommissioning. A healthcare case study illustrates how SC-NLP-LMF detects emerging terminology drift (e.g., COVID-related language) and guides compliant model updates. The framework offers organizations a practical, lifecycle-wide structure for developing, deploying, and maintaining secure and accountable NLP systems in high-risk environments.
