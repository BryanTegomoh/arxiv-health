Systematic Evaluation of Preprocessing Techniques for Accurate Image Registration in Digital Pathology

Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Fatemehzahra Darzi, Rodrigo Escobar Diaz Guerrero, Thomas Bocklitz

Summary

This study systematically evaluates various preprocessing techniques, focusing on color transformations, to enhance image registration accuracy between H&E stained and non-linear multimodal images in digital pathology. It conclusively demonstrates that CycleGAN color transformation significantly reduces registration errors, leading to improved spatial alignment for downstream applications like biomarker analysis and tissue reconstruction. The findings highlight the critical role of appropriate preprocessing in achieving reliable image integration across different imaging modalities.

Medical Relevance

Accurate image registration in digital pathology is fundamental for integrating diverse datasets from different stains or imaging modalities, which is essential for comprehensive biomarker analysis, tissue reconstruction, and enhancing diagnostic precision in clinical practice and research.

AI Health Application

This research improves a foundational preprocessing step (image registration and color transformation using AI techniques like CycleGAN) that is vital for various AI applications in digital pathology. These applications include automated disease diagnosis, quantitative biomarker analysis, treatment response prediction, and tissue phenotyping from multi-modal whole slide images, by ensuring accurate spatial alignment of different image types.

Methodology

The study utilized a dataset of 20 paired tissue samples, comprising H&E stained images and non-linear multimodal images. These pairs underwent several preprocessing steps, including four color transformation techniques (CycleGAN, Macenko, Reinhard, Vahadane), inversion, contrast adjustment, intensity normalization, and denoising. Image registration was performed using the VALIS method, which sequentially applies rigid registration and then a two-step non-rigid registration on both low and high-resolution images. Performance was quantitatively assessed using the relative Target Registration Error (rTRE), specifically the median of median rTRE (MMrTRE) and the average of median rTRE (AMrTRE), alongside a custom point-based evaluation using ten manually selected key points. The entire process was evaluated under two conditions: using original multimodal images and using inverted multimodal images.

Key Findings

The most significant finding was that CycleGAN color transformation consistently resulted in the lowest registration errors among all tested preprocessing methods, across both scenarios (original and inverted multimodal images). This indicates its superior ability to facilitate accurate alignment between H&E and non-linear multimodal images compared to Macenko, Reinhard, and Vahadane transformations.

Clinical Impact

By demonstrating a highly effective preprocessing method (CycleGAN color transformation) for improving image registration accuracy, this research directly contributes to more reliable integration of information from different imaging modalities in digital pathology. This enhanced alignment is crucial for precise biomarker analysis, accurate tissue reconstruction, and could ultimately support more confident diagnoses and personalized treatment planning in clinical and research settings.

Abstract

Image registration refers to the process of spatially aligning two or more images by mapping them into a common coordinate system, so that corresponding anatomical or tissue structures are matched across images. In digital pathology, registration enables direct comparison and integration of information from different stains or imaging modalities, sup-porting applications such as biomarker analysis and tissue reconstruction. Accurate registration of images from different modalities is an essential step in digital pathology. In this study, we investigated how various color transformation techniques affect image registration between hematoxylin and eosin (H&E) stained images and non-linear multimodal images. We used a dataset of 20 tissue sample pairs, with each pair undergoing several preprocessing steps, including different color transformation (CycleGAN, Macenko, Reinhard, Vahadane), inversion, contrast adjustment, intensity normalization, and denoising. All images were registered using the VALIS registration method, which first applies rigid registration and then performs non-rigid registration in two steps on both low and high-resolution images. Registration performance was evaluated using the relative Target Registration Error (rTRE). We reported the median of median rTRE values (MMrTRE) and the average of median rTRE values (AMrTRE) for each method. In addition, we performed a custom point-based evaluation using ten manually selected key points. Registration was done separately for two scenarios, using either the original or inverted multimodal images. In both scenarios, CycleGAN color transformation achieved the lowest registration errors, while the other methods showed higher errors. These findings show that applying color transformation before registration improves alignment between images from different modalities and supports more reliable analysis in digital pathology.
