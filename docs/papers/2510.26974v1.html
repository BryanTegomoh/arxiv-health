Overview of the MEDIQA-OE 2025 Shared Task on Medical Order Extraction from Doctor-Patient Consultations

Summary

This paper introduces the MEDIQA-OE 2025 shared task, the inaugural challenge focused on automatically extracting actionable medical orders from doctor-patient consultations for Electronic Health Records (EHRs). Addressing an unexplored area in clinical documentation, the task aims to significantly reduce clinician burden and enhance patient care. The paper overviews the task, dataset, participant solutions leveraging various LLMs, and the final leaderboard from six participating teams.

Medical Relevance

This research is profoundly relevant to medical informatics and healthcare operations by proposing an automated solution to streamline the creation of medical orders from spoken clinical encounters. Successfully implementing such a system could drastically cut down on clinician documentation time, reduce errors in order entry, and accelerate patient care pathways.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using Large Language Models (LLMs) to automatically extract specific medical orders from unstructured doctor-patient consultation dialogues. This aims to streamline clinical documentation, reduce the burden on clinicians, and improve the efficiency and accuracy of populating Electronic Health Records, thereby positively impacting patient care.

Key Points

  • Addresses a critical gap in clinical documentation: the automated extraction of actionable medical orders directly from doctor-patient conversations for EHRs.
  • Introduces MEDIQA-OE 2025, the first-of-its-kind shared task specifically designed for this medical order extraction challenge.
  • Aims to substantially alleviate the documentation burden on clinicians and directly improve downstream patient care processes.
  • Six teams participated, employing a broad spectrum of Natural Language Processing (NLP) approaches, including both closed-source and open-source Large Language Models (LLMs).
  • The paper provides a comprehensive overview of the MEDIQA-OE task's definition, the utilized dataset, the final performance rankings (leaderboard), and detailed descriptions of the participants' technical solutions.
  • This initiative represents a novel application of advanced NLP and LLM technologies to a high-impact, previously unexplored problem in medical informatics.

Methodology

The paper describes the MEDIQA-OE 2025 shared task, which challenged six participating teams to extract medical orders from doctor-patient conversations. Participants developed and experimented with a diverse range of Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques, including both proprietary (closed-weight) and publicly available (open-weight) Large Language Models (LLMs). The paper acts as an overview, detailing the task setup, the dataset used, and summarizing the methodologies and results (leaderboard) from the contributing teams.

Key Findings

The primary finding is the successful launch and execution of the inaugural MEDIQA-OE 2025 shared task, demonstrating significant interest and active research in automated medical order extraction. Six teams explored diverse and advanced NLP approaches, including LLMs, to tackle this novel challenge, highlighting the feasibility and potential of current AI technologies for this complex clinical task. The paper serves as a foundational overview of the task's initial outcomes and participant solutions.

Clinical Impact

The potential clinical impact is substantial: by automating the extraction of medical orders from doctor-patient consultations, clinicians' documentation burden could be significantly reduced, allowing more time for direct patient interaction. This automation could lead to faster, more accurate, and standardized medical order entry into EHRs, improving clinical workflow efficiency, reducing administrative costs, and ultimately enhancing patient safety and quality of care by expediting treatment initiation.

The abstract primarily serves as an overview of the shared task and does not explicitly detail specific limitations of the participant solutions or the task design. However, as a first-of-its-kind challenge, inherent complexities likely include the nuances of conversational data, the interpretability and generalizability of LLMs across diverse clinical scenarios, and the potential for errors in critical medical order extraction.

Directions would likely involve refining the task definition, expanding and diversifying the datasets, improving the robustness and accuracy of extraction models, exploring explainable AI for clinical decision support, and ultimately integrating these automated solutions into real-world Electronic Health Record systems for clinical validation and adoption.

Abstract

Clinical documentation increasingly uses automatic speech recognition and summarization, yet converting conversations into actionable medical orders for Electronic Health Records remains unexplored. A solution to this problem can significantly reduce the documentation burden of clinicians and directly impact downstream patient care. We introduce the MEDIQA-OE 2025 shared task, the first challenge on extracting medical orders from doctor-patient conversations. Six teams participated in the shared task and experimented with a broad range of approaches, and both closed- and open-weight large language models (LLMs). In this paper, we describe the MEDIQA-OE task, dataset, final leaderboard ranking, and participants' solutions.
