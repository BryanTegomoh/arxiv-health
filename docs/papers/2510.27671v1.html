MolChord: Structure-Sequence Alignment for Protein-Guided Drug Design

Summary

MolChord is a novel deep learning framework for structure-based drug design (SBDD) that aligns protein and molecule structures with their sequential and textual representations to generate drugs with desired pharmacological properties. It integrates the NatureLM autoregressive model as a molecule generator and a diffusion-based structure encoder, refining the alignment process using Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) on a property-aware dataset. The approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on the CrossDocked2020 benchmark.

  • It unifies protein structural representations with molecular representations, integrating their textual descriptions (e.g., FASTA for proteins, SMILES for molecules).
  • The system leverages NatureLM, an autoregressive model capable of processing and generating text, small molecules, and proteins, acting as the primary molecule generator.
  • A diffusion-based structure encoder is utilized to effectively process and align the structural representations of proteins.
  • To guide molecule generation towards desired properties, MolChord curates a specialized property-aware dataset by incorporating preference data.

Methodology

MolChord integrates an autoregressive model, NatureLM, which unifies text, small molecules, and proteins, functioning as the molecule generator. It employs a diffusion-based structure encoder for processing protein structural data. The alignment of protein and molecule structures with their sequential/textual representations is refined using Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) on a specially curated property-aware dataset that incorporates preference data to guide molecule generation towards desired pharmacological characteristics.

Abstract

Structure-based drug design (SBDD), which maps target proteins to candidate molecular ligands, is a fundamental task in drug discovery. Effectively aligning protein structural representations with molecular representations, and ensuring alignment between generated drugs and their pharmacological properties, remains a critical challenge. To address these challenges, we propose MolChord, which integrates two key techniques: (1) to align protein and molecule structures with their textual descriptions and sequential representations (e.g., FASTA for proteins and SMILES for molecules), we leverage NatureLM, an autoregressive model unifying text, small molecules, and proteins, as the molecule generator, alongside a diffusion-based structure encoder; and (2) to guide molecules toward desired properties, we curate a property-aware dataset by integrating preference data and refine the alignment process using Direct Preference Optimization (DPO). Experimental results on CrossDocked2020 demonstrate that our approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on key evaluation metrics, highlighting its potential as a practical tool for SBDD.
