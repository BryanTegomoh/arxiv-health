DB-KAUNet: An Adaptive Dual Branch Kolmogorov-Arnold UNet for Retinal Vessel Segmentation

Summary

This paper introduces DB-KAUNet, an Adaptive Dual Branch Kolmogorov-Arnold UNet, designed for precise retinal vessel segmentation. It addresses the limitations of traditional CNNs in capturing long-range dependencies and complex nonlinear relationships by employing a heterogeneous dual-branch encoder, enhanced with novel interaction and adaptive fusion modules, thereby achieving state-of-the-art performance and robustness across benchmark datasets.

Medical Relevance

Accurate retinal vessel segmentation is a cornerstone for the clinical diagnosis, monitoring, and management of numerous ophthalmic diseases like diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma, and macular degeneration, as well as systemic diseases such as hypertension, stroke, and cardiovascular conditions.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of DB-KAUNet, an advanced deep learning model, for automated and precise segmentation of retinal vessels from medical images. This segmentation aids in the clinical diagnosis of various ophthalmic and systemic diseases by providing enhanced visual information to clinicians.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** Traditional CNNs struggle with capturing long-range dependencies and complex nonlinear relationships crucial for accurate retinal vessel segmentation.
  • **Proposed Architecture:** DB-KAUNet features a Heterogeneous Dual-Branch Encoder (HDBE) with parallel CNN and Transformer pathways, integrating novel KANConv and KAT blocks for comprehensive feature representation.
  • **Feature Interaction:** A Cross-Branch Channel Interaction (CCI) module is embedded to facilitate efficient exchange of channel features between the parallel CNN and Transformer branches.
  • **Spatial Feature Enhancement:** An attention-based Spatial Feature Enhancement (SFE) module is utilized to enhance spatial features and fuse outputs from both branches.
  • **Adaptive Fusion:** An advanced Spatial Feature Enhancement with Geometrically Adaptive Fusion (SFE-GAF) module is developed, employing adaptive sampling to precisely focus on true vessel morphology.
  • **Efficiency & Robustness:** The SFE-GAF module strengthens salient vascular features, significantly reduces background noise, and lowers computational overhead.
  • **Performance:** Extensive experiments on DRIVE, STARE, and CHASE_DB1 datasets validate DB-KAUNet's leading segmentation performance and exceptional robustness.

Methodology

The DB-KAUNet utilizes a Heterogeneous Dual-Branch Encoder (HDBE) combining parallel CNN and Transformer pathways, interleaved with KANConv and KAT blocks. A Cross-Branch Channel Interaction (CCI) module manages feature exchange. Spatial Feature Enhancement (SFE) modules employ attention for spatial feature enhancement and branch fusion, culminating in an advanced SFE-GAF module that uses adaptive sampling for geometrically adaptive fusion, targeting precise vessel morphology and noise reduction.

Key Findings

The DB-KAUNet achieved leading segmentation performance on benchmark datasets (DRIVE, STARE, CHASE_DB1) for retinal vessel segmentation. It also demonstrated exceptional robustness, indicating superior generalization capability and reliability in accurately identifying vessel structures.

Clinical Impact

This research has the potential to significantly improve the accuracy and efficiency of automated diagnostic tools for ophthalmic and systemic diseases. More precise and robust retinal vessel segmentation can lead to earlier detection, better prognostication, and more tailored treatment strategies, ultimately enhancing patient care and potentially reducing the burden on clinicians.

Abstract

Accurate segmentation of retinal vessels is crucial for the clinical diagnosis of numerous ophthalmic and systemic diseases. However, traditional Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) methods exhibit inherent limitations, struggling to capture long-range dependencies and complex nonlinear relationships. To address the above limitations, an Adaptive Dual Branch Kolmogorov-Arnold UNet (DB-KAUNet) is proposed for retinal vessel segmentation. In DB-KAUNet, we design a Heterogeneous Dual-Branch Encoder (HDBE) that features parallel CNN and Transformer pathways. The HDBE strategically interleaves standard CNN and Transformer blocks with novel KANConv and KAT blocks, enabling the model to form a comprehensive feature representation. To optimize feature processing, we integrate several critical components into the HDBE. First, a Cross-Branch Channel Interaction (CCI) module is embedded to facilitate efficient interaction of channel features between the parallel pathways. Second, an attention-based Spatial Feature Enhancement (SFE) module is employed to enhance spatial features and fuse the outputs from both branches. Building upon the SFE module, an advanced Spatial Feature Enhancement with Geometrically Adaptive Fusion (SFE-GAF) module is subsequently developed. In the SFE-GAF module, adaptive sampling is utilized to focus on true vessel morphology precisely. The adaptive process strengthens salient vascular features while significantly reducing background noise and computational overhead. Extensive experiments on the DRIVE, STARE, and CHASE_DB1 datasets validate that DB-KAUNet achieves leading segmentation performance and demonstrates exceptional robustness.
