DB-KAUNet: An Adaptive Dual Branch Kolmogorov-Arnold UNet for Retinal Vessel Segmentation

arXiv ID: 2512.01657v1
Published: 2025-12-01
Authors: Hongyu Xu, Panpan Meng, Meng Wang, Dayu Hu, Liming Liang, Xiaoqi Sheng
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00

Summary

This paper introduces DB-KAUNet, an Adaptive Dual Branch Kolmogorov-Arnold UNet, designed for highly accurate retinal vessel segmentation. It addresses the limitations of traditional CNNs in capturing long-range dependencies by employing a novel heterogeneous dual-branch encoder that combines parallel CNN and Transformer pathways with specialized KANConv and KAT blocks, achieving leading performance and exceptional robustness across multiple datasets.

Medical Relevance

Accurate retinal vessel segmentation is critical for the early and precise clinical diagnosis and monitoring of numerous ophthalmic conditions (e.g., diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma) and systemic diseases (e.g., hypertension, stroke) that manifest changes in the retinal vasculature.

AI Health Application

The AI model (DB-KAUNet) is applied to retinal vessel segmentation, which is a critical task in medical imaging for assisting in the clinical diagnosis of various ophthalmic conditions (like glaucoma, retinopathy) and systemic diseases (like diabetes, hypertension, arteriosclerosis) by analyzing the vasculature of the retina.

Key Points

  • DB-KAUNet is proposed to overcome limitations of traditional CNNs in capturing long-range dependencies and complex nonlinear relationships for retinal vessel segmentation.
  • It features a Heterogeneous Dual-Branch Encoder (HDBE) with parallel CNN and Transformer pathways, incorporating novel KANConv and KAT blocks for comprehensive feature representation.
  • A Cross-Branch Channel Interaction (CCI) module facilitates efficient channel feature exchange between the parallel branches.
  • An attention-based Spatial Feature Enhancement (SFE) module is used for enhancing spatial features and fusing branch outputs.
  • An advanced Spatial Feature Enhancement with Geometrically Adaptive Fusion (SFE-GAF) module is developed, utilizing adaptive sampling to precisely focus on true vessel morphology, reducing noise and computational overhead.
  • The model achieves leading segmentation performance and demonstrates exceptional robustness on the DRIVE, STARE, and CHASE_DB1 datasets.
  • The adaptive sampling in SFE-GAF strengthens salient vascular features while significantly reducing background noise.

Methodology

The DB-KAUNet employs a dual-branch U-shaped architecture. Its core is a Heterogeneous Dual-Branch Encoder (HDBE) consisting of parallel CNN and Transformer pathways. This encoder strategically interleaves standard CNN/Transformer blocks with novel KANConv (Kolmogorov-Arnold Network Convolution) and KAT (Kolmogorov-Arnold Transformer) blocks to capture both local and global features. A Cross-Branch Channel Interaction (CCI) module enables efficient feature exchange between the parallel branches. Outputs from the branches are fused and enhanced by a Spatial Feature Enhancement (SFE) module. An advanced SFE with Geometrically Adaptive Fusion (SFE-GAF) module is subsequently used, which incorporates adaptive sampling to focus on vessel morphology, enhancing salient features and reducing noise and computational cost.

Key Findings

DB-KAUNet achieved leading segmentation performance and demonstrated exceptional robustness across the DRIVE, STARE, and CHASE_DB1 datasets. The integration of KAN-based blocks, the dual-branch architecture, and especially the adaptive sampling within SFE-GAF, proved effective in precisely identifying and enhancing vascular features while suppressing background noise.

This technology has the potential to significantly improve the accuracy and efficiency of diagnosing and monitoring various diseases by providing more precise and reliable retinal vessel segmentations. This could lead to earlier detection of ophthalmic conditions like diabetic retinopathy and glaucoma, as well as systemic diseases such as hypertension and stroke, allowing for timely intervention and better patient outcomes. The enhanced robustness suggests applicability across diverse patient populations and imaging conditions.

Abstract

Accurate segmentation of retinal vessels is crucial for the clinical diagnosis of numerous ophthalmic and systemic diseases. However, traditional Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) methods exhibit inherent limitations, struggling to capture long-range dependencies and complex nonlinear relationships. To address the above limitations, an Adaptive Dual Branch Kolmogorov-Arnold UNet (DB-KAUNet) is proposed for retinal vessel segmentation. In DB-KAUNet, we design a Heterogeneous Dual-Branch Encoder (HDBE) that features parallel CNN and Transformer pathways. The HDBE strategically interleaves standard CNN and Transformer blocks with novel KANConv and KAT blocks, enabling the model to form a comprehensive feature representation. To optimize feature processing, we integrate several critical components into the HDBE. First, a Cross-Branch Channel Interaction (CCI) module is embedded to facilitate efficient interaction of channel features between the parallel pathways. Second, an attention-based Spatial Feature Enhancement (SFE) module is employed to enhance spatial features and fuse the outputs from both branches. Building upon the SFE module, an advanced Spatial Feature Enhancement with Geometrically Adaptive Fusion (SFE-GAF) module is subsequently developed. In the SFE-GAF module, adaptive sampling is utilized to focus on true vessel morphology precisely. The adaptive process strengthens salient vascular features while significantly reducing background noise and computational overhead. Extensive experiments on the DRIVE, STARE, and CHASE_DB1 datasets validate that DB-KAUNet achieves leading segmentation performance and demonstrates exceptional robustness.
