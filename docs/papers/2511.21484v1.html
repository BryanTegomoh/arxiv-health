A Dynamic Anti-Equinus Orthosis with Electromyography Sensor for Neuromuscular Rehabilitation

Authors: Manuel Terradillos Perea, Olga Alonso Gonzalez, Cristina Soguero Ruiz, David Gutierrez

Summary

This study introduces EquiSay, a dynamic anti-equinus orthosis equipped with an anterior elastic tension system and an EMG sensor for real-time tibialis anterior muscle activation monitoring. It leverages AI, including a U-Net model for synthetic EMG data generation and a predictive framework for threshold calibration, addressing data limitations. Experimental results show EquiSay significantly improves dorsiflexion, enhances patient satisfaction, and provides valuable clinical insights for neuromuscular rehabilitation.

Medical Relevance

Equinus foot profoundly affects gait and quality of life. EquiSay offers a novel approach by combining dynamic mechanical support with real-time objective neuromuscular feedback and AI-driven data augmentation, enabling more precise rehabilitation, personalized treatment, and potentially superior long-term functional outcomes for patients.

Methodology

EquiSay is a dynamic anti-equinus orthosis featuring an anterior elastic tension system for mechanical support. It incorporates an electromyography (EMG) sensor to quantify real-time muscle activation, specifically targeting the tibialis anterior. To overcome the scarcity of real EMG data, the system integrates a U-Net based deep learning model for generating synthetic EMG signals. Additionally, a predictive framework is employed for the automatic calibration of minimum muscle activation thresholds. The system's efficacy was evaluated through experimental results focusing on improvements in dorsiflexion and patient satisfaction.

Key Findings

Experimental results demonstrated significant improvements in ankle dorsiflexion among users. Patients expressed increased satisfaction with the use of the EquiSay orthosis. The system provided valuable clinical insights that are directly applicable to the planning and optimization of neuromuscular rehabilitation programs.

Clinical Impact

EquiSay has the potential to significantly enhance neuromuscular rehabilitation by providing dynamic support that improves foot posture and natural movement while offering real-time, objective feedback on muscle activation. Clinicians can leverage the AI-enhanced data and automatic calibration to develop more precise, personalized, and data-driven rehabilitation plans, potentially leading to faster recovery, better functional outcomes, and improved patient quality of life. It serves as an advanced assistive device that also informs therapeutic strategies.
