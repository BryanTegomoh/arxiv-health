A Dynamic Anti-Equinus Orthosis with Electromyography Sensor for Neuromuscular Rehabilitation

Summary

This study introduces EquiSay, a dynamic anti-equinus orthosis incorporating an anterior elastic tension system and an electromyography (EMG) sensor for real-time tibialis anterior muscle activation monitoring. The system leverages a U-Net model for synthetic EMG signal generation and a predictive framework for automatic threshold calibration, demonstrating improved dorsiflexion, increased patient satisfaction, and providing valuable clinical insights for neuromuscular rehabilitation.

Medical Relevance

This innovation offers a dynamic and data-driven solution for equinus foot, a debilitating neuromuscular condition, directly impacting patient mobility, gait quality, and overall quality of life by providing real-time feedback and intelligent support.

AI Health Application

The paper describes the application of AI through a U-Net based model for generating synthetic EMG signals and a predictive framework for automatic calibration of minimum activation thresholds, all integrated into a medical orthosis to enhance neuromuscular monitoring and rehabilitation.

Methodology

The methodology involved the design and development of EquiSay, a dynamic orthosis with an anterior elastic tension system. It incorporated an EMG sensor for real-time muscle activation quantification. To overcome data scarcity, a U-Net based deep learning model was developed for synthetic EMG signal generation, complemented by a predictive framework for automatic calibration of minimum activation thresholds. Experimental validation was conducted to assess dorsiflexion improvement, patient satisfaction, and the generation of clinical insights.

Key Findings

The EquiSay orthosis successfully improved ankle dorsiflexion in patients with equinus foot. Patients reported increased satisfaction with the device. The system effectively provided real-time neuromuscular monitoring and generated valuable clinical insights crucial for personalized rehabilitation planning. The integrated AI models demonstrated feasibility in addressing practical data challenges for device calibration.

Clinical Impact

EquiSay has the potential to significantly enhance neuromuscular rehabilitation for equinus foot by offering a dynamic assistive tool that provides objective, real-time feedback. This enables clinicians to develop more precise and personalized rehabilitation plans, monitor patient progress effectively, and ultimately improve patient gait mechanics and quality of life. The AI components also reduce barriers to widespread implementation by automating calibration and mitigating data limitations.

Abstract

The equinus foot is a neuromuscular condition that affects ankle dorsiflexion, impairing gait and reducing quality of life. This study presents EquiSay, a dynamic anti-equinus orthosis equipped with an anterior elastic tension system and an electromyography (EMG) sensor to quantify muscle activation, particularly of the tibialis anterior. EquiSay provides dynamic support that improves foot posture and natural movement while enabling real-time neuromuscular monitoring. To address the limited availability of EMG data, the system incorporates a U-Net based model for generating synthetic EMG signals and a predictive framework for automatic calibration of minimum activation thresholds. Experimental results show improved dorsiflexion, increased patient satisfaction, and valuable clinical insights for rehabilitation planning. These findings highlight the potential of EquiSay as an assistive tool and as a platform for future AI-enhanced developments.
