A Dynamic Anti-Equinus Orthosis with Electromyography Sensor for Neuromuscular Rehabilitation

Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Manuel Terradillos Perea, Olga Alonso Gonzalez, Cristina Soguero Ruiz, David Gutierrez

Summary

EquiSay is a dynamic anti-equinus orthosis utilizing an anterior elastic tension system and an electromyography (EMG) sensor to improve ankle dorsiflexion and monitor neuromuscular activity in patients with equinus foot. The system also integrates AI models, including a U-Net for synthetic EMG data generation and a predictive framework for automatic threshold calibration, demonstrating improved dorsiflexion, increased patient satisfaction, and valuable clinical insights for rehabilitation.

Medical Relevance

Equinus foot severely affects gait and quality of life. This innovative orthosis offers a dynamic, sensor-driven, and AI-enhanced approach to rehabilitation, providing objective data for personalized treatment and potentially restoring crucial ankle function for patients.

AI Health Application

The paper applies AI (U-Net based model) to generate synthetic EMG signals, addressing data scarcity in medical sensing. It also uses a predictive framework for automatic calibration of neuromuscular activation thresholds, enhancing the precision and usability of the medical device. This demonstrates AI's role in improving medical device functionality, data analysis, and potentially personalized rehabilitation.

Key Points

  • EquiSay is a dynamic anti-equinus orthosis designed to provide support for patients with equinus foot, a condition impairing ankle dorsiflexion and gait.
  • The orthosis incorporates an anterior elastic tension system to offer dynamic support that improves foot posture and facilitates natural movement.
  • It is equipped with an electromyography (EMG) sensor for real-time quantification of muscle activation, specifically targeting the tibialis anterior.
  • To address challenges with limited EMG data availability, the system integrates a U-Net based model for generating synthetic EMG signals.
  • A predictive framework is included for automatic calibration of minimum muscle activation thresholds, enhancing the system's adaptability and usability.
  • Experimental results demonstrated improved ankle dorsiflexion and reported increased patient satisfaction among users.
  • The system provides valuable clinical insights derived from neuromuscular monitoring, which aids in personalized rehabilitation planning and serves as a platform for future AI-enhanced developments.

Methodology

The study presents EquiSay, a dynamic anti-equinus orthosis featuring an anterior elastic tension system for mechanical assistance and an integrated electromyography (EMG) sensor for real-time tibialis anterior muscle activation monitoring. To overcome data scarcity, the system employs a U-Net based deep learning model for synthetic EMG signal generation and a predictive framework for automated calibration of muscle activation thresholds. Experimental trials were conducted to evaluate dorsiflexion improvements and patient satisfaction.

Key Findings

Experimental results indicated significant improvements in ankle dorsiflexion for users of the EquiSay orthosis. Patients reported increased satisfaction, suggesting enhanced comfort and functional benefits. The system also generated valuable clinical insights into neuromuscular activity, crucial for tailoring effective rehabilitation programs.

Clinical Impact

EquiSay offers a promising assistive and rehabilitative tool for individuals with equinus foot, potentially leading to improved gait, enhanced natural movement, and a better quality of life. Its real-time neuromuscular monitoring and AI-driven calibration capabilities provide clinicians with objective, actionable data for developing highly personalized and adaptive rehabilitation strategies.

However, it highlights the 'limited availability of EMG data' as a fundamental challenge in the field, which the system's integrated AI components (U-Net for synthetic data, predictive framework for calibration) are specifically designed to address.

Future Directions

The authors propose EquiSay as a robust platform for future AI-enhanced developments, suggesting ongoing research into further integrating advanced artificial intelligence for optimizing neuromuscular rehabilitation outcomes.

Abstract

The equinus foot is a neuromuscular condition that affects ankle dorsiflexion, impairing gait and reducing quality of life. This study presents EquiSay, a dynamic anti-equinus orthosis equipped with an anterior elastic tension system and an electromyography (EMG) sensor to quantify muscle activation, particularly of the tibialis anterior. EquiSay provides dynamic support that improves foot posture and natural movement while enabling real-time neuromuscular monitoring. To address the limited availability of EMG data, the system incorporates a U-Net based model for generating synthetic EMG signals and a predictive framework for automatic calibration of minimum activation thresholds. Experimental results show improved dorsiflexion, increased patient satisfaction, and valuable clinical insights for rehabilitation planning. These findings highlight the potential of EquiSay as an assistive tool and as a platform for future AI-enhanced developments.
