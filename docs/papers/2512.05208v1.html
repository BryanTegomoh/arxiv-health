Peakspan: Defining, Quantifying and Extending the Boundaries of Peak Productive Lifespan

arXiv ID: 2512.05208v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Alex Zhavoronkov, Dominika Wilczok
Categories: q-bio.QM, econ.GN
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00

Summary

This paper introduces 'Peakspan,' a novel metric defining the age interval during which an individual maintains at least 90% of their peak functional performance in specific physiological or cognitive domains. It highlights that traditional health metrics, like Healthspan, overlook a critical functional decline, revealing that most biological systems peak early, resulting in a remarkably short Peakspan and a significant portion of adult life spent in a 'healthy but declined' state.

Medical Relevance

Peakspan provides a more granular and functional metric than Healthspan for assessing the quality of aging, identifying a critical period of functional decline that current medical interventions often overlook. It redefines the goals of rejuvenative medicine towards maintaining and restoring peak physiological and cognitive capacities, rather than solely focusing on disease prevention.

AI Health Application

Artificial intelligence and foundational models of biological aging are proposed as crucial tools for recognizing and tracking Peakspan, which will aid in developing strategies to compress functional morbidity and maximize human potential across the life course. This implies AI will be used for monitoring, data analysis, and potentially predicting functional decline and guiding interventions related to aging and health.

Key Points

  • **Introduction of Peakspan:** A new metric defined as the age interval where an individual sustains >= 90% of their peak functional performance in a specific physiological or cognitive domain.
  • **Critique of Traditional Metrics:** Traditional Healthspan (years free of disease) is deemed insufficient, as it overlooks gradual functional erosion leading to declines in productivity and work capacity, even without overt illness.
  • **Multi-System Analysis Finding:** Reveals that most biological systems reach maximal capacity in early adulthood, resulting in a remarkably short Peakspan relative to an individual's total lifespan.
  • **Identification of 'Healthy but Declined' State:** Highlights that humans now spend the majority of their adult lives in a state characterized by significant functional decline despite the absence of diagnosed diseases, termed the 'healthy but declined' state.
  • **Rejuvenative Biomedical Progress:** Argues that extending Peakspan and developing strategies to restore post-peak function is the functional manifestation of rejuvenative biomedical progress.
  • **Socioeconomic Importance:** Emphasizes that Peakspan extension is crucial for sustained economic growth in aging societies.
  • **Future Role of AI:** Proposes that recognizing and tracking Peakspan, increasingly facilitated by artificial intelligence and foundational models of biological aging, is essential for compressing functional morbidity and maximizing human potential.

The paper presents a conceptual framework for defining and quantifying Peakspan, based on insights from an unspecified 'multi-system analysis' indicating early adult peak capacities. It further proposes the future utilization of artificial intelligence and foundational models of biological aging as key tools for the precise recognition, tracking, and extension of Peakspan.

Clinical Impact

Clinically, Peakspan could enable earlier identification of individuals experiencing significant functional decline well before overt disease onset, prompting targeted preventative or rejuvenative interventions. It could serve as a quantifiable biomarker for assessing the efficacy of anti-aging therapies, precision medicine approaches aimed at preserving or restoring peak function, and for guiding personalized healthcare plans to maximize functional independence and quality of life in later years.

Abstract

The unprecedented extension of the human lifespan necessitates a parallel evolution in how we quantify the quality of aging and its socioeconomic impact. Traditional metrics focusing on Healthspan (years free of disease) overlook the gradual erosion of physiological capacity that occurs even in the absence of illness, leading to declines in productivity and eventual lack of capacity to work. To address this critical gap, we introduce Peakspan: the age interval during which an individual maintains at least 90% of their peak functional performance in a specific physiological or cognitive domain. Our multi-system analysis reveals a profound misalignment: most biological systems reach maximal capacity in early adulthood, resulting in a Peakspan that is remarkably short relative to the total lifespan. This dissociation means humans now spend the majority of their adult lives in a "healthy but declined" state, characterized by a significant functional gap. We argue that extending Peakspan and developing strategies to restore function in post-peak individuals is the functional manifestation of rejuvenative biomedical progress and is essential for sustained economic growth in aging societies. Recognizing and tracking Peakspan, increasingly facilitated by artificial intelligence and foundational models of biological aging, is crucial for developing strategies to compress functional morbidity and maximize human potential across the life course.
