ACE-ICD: Acronym Expansion As Data Augmentation For Automated ICD Coding

arXiv ID: 2511.07311v1
Published: 2025-11-10
Authors: Tuan-Dung Le, Shohreh Haddadan, Thanh Q. Thieu
Categories: cs.CL
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces ACE-ICD, a novel data augmentation technique for automated ICD coding that tackles the underexplored challenge of medical acronyms in clinical notes. It leverages large language models (LLMs) to expand acronyms into their full forms, training models on these richer representations, and incorporates consistency training to regularize predictions between original and augmented documents. The approach achieves new state-of-the-art performance on the MIMIC-III dataset across common, rare, and full-code assignments.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and efficient automated ICD coding is fundamental for healthcare operations, directly impacting clinical documentation quality, patient billing accuracy, epidemiological surveillance, and medical research data integrity. This work significantly improves the reliability of automated coding systems by addressing a pervasive linguistic challenge (acronyms) in clinical notes, thereby streamlining critical administrative and analytical processes in medicine.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the automated assignment of ICD (International Classification of Diseases) codes to electronic medical records. This involves leveraging large language models and data augmentation techniques (specifically acronym expansion and consistency training) to process clinical notes and accurately infer disease and procedure codes, thereby enhancing the efficiency and precision of clinical documentation and healthcare billing.

Key Points

  • Addresses a critical gap in existing automated ICD coding methods by explicitly focusing on the pervasive use of medical acronyms in clinical notes, which are vital for accurate code inference.
  • Proposes ACE-ICD, a novel data augmentation technique designed to improve model understanding by expanding medical acronyms to their full-form representations.
  • Utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) as the core mechanism for effectively performing the medical acronym expansion.
  • Incorporates consistency training as a regularization strategy, compelling the model to produce similar predictions for both original and acronym-expanded (augmented) clinical documents.
  • Establishes new state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance for automated ICD coding on the widely used MIMIC-III dataset.
  • Demonstrates robust improvements across various coding granularities, including common codes (frequently assigned), rare codes (less frequent but often complex), and comprehensive full-code assignments.
  • The research code is publicly available, promoting reproducibility and further advancements in the field.

Methodology

The ACE-ICD method employs a two-stage approach. First, it utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) to perform data augmentation by identifying and expanding medical acronyms within electronic medical records into their full textual forms. This creates an augmented dataset with explicit representations of previously abbreviated terms. Second, it incorporates consistency training during the model's learning phase, which regularizes predictions by enforcing agreement in ICD code assignments between the original clinical documents and their acronym-expanded, augmented counterparts.

Key Findings

The proposed ACE-ICD approach achieved new state-of-the-art performance in automated ICD coding. This superior performance was consistently demonstrated across diverse coding scenarios, including common codes, rare codes, and full-code assignments, as validated through extensive experiments on the MIMIC-III dataset.

Abstract

Automatic ICD coding, the task of assigning disease and procedure codes to electronic medical records, is crucial for clinical documentation and billing. While existing methods primarily enhance model understanding of code hierarchies and synonyms, they often overlook the pervasive use of medical acronyms in clinical notes, a key factor in ICD code inference. To address this gap, we propose a novel effective data augmentation technique that leverages large language models to expand medical acronyms, allowing models to be trained on their full form representations. Moreover, we incorporate consistency training to regularize predictions by enforcing agreement between the original and augmented documents. Extensive experiments on the MIMIC-III dataset demonstrate that our approach, ACE-ICD establishes new state-of-the-art performance across multiple settings, including common codes, rare codes, and full-code assignments. Our code is publicly available.
