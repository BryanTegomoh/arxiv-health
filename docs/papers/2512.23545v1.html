PathFound: An Agentic Multimodal Model Activating Evidence-seeking Pathological Diagnosis

Summary

PathFound introduces an agentic multimodal model for pathological diagnosis that emulates clinical workflows by performing iterative evidence-seeking and diagnosis refinement. Unlike static inference methods, this model proactively acquires information through initial diagnosis, evidence-seeking, and final decision stages, significantly improving diagnostic accuracy and achieving state-of-the-art performance across diverse clinical scenarios.

Medical Relevance

This model's ability to refine diagnoses through iterative evidence-seeking closely mimics human pathologist workflows, promising more accurate and reliable diagnostic outcomes, especially for complex or ambiguous cases. Its potential to identify subtle features can significantly improve early detection and precision in disease characterization, directly impacting patient care.

AI Health Application

PathFound is an AI application designed to act as an intelligent assistant for pathologists. It uses a multimodal AI model to process whole-slide images, acquire additional information proactively (evidence-seeking), and refine diagnoses, thereby improving the accuracy and efficiency of pathological diagnosis, particularly in ambiguous cases, and mimicking human clinical diagnostic workflows.

Key Points

  • Current pathological foundation models predominantly rely on static inference, which contrasts with the iterative, hypothesis-refining workflow of clinical diagnosis.
  • PathFound is designed as an agentic multimodal model to support evidence-seeking inference, integrating pathological visual foundation models, vision-language models, and reinforcement learning-trained reasoning models.
  • The model operates through a dynamic, multi-stage process comprising initial diagnosis, targeted evidence-seeking, and a final decision stage to refine predictions.
  • Adopting PathFound's evidence-seeking strategy consistently improves diagnostic accuracy across several large multimodal models, validating its effectiveness.
  • PathFound achieves state-of-the-art diagnostic performance across a variety of clinical scenarios, demonstrating superior diagnostic capabilities.
  • The model exhibits a strong potential to identify subtle yet crucial pathological details, such as nuclear features and local invasions.
  • The core innovation lies in shifting from a static inference paradigm to a dynamic, 'agentic' approach that mimics human diagnostic reasoning for improved reliability.

Methodology

PathFound is an agentic multimodal model that combines specialized pathological visual foundation models, general vision-language models, and reasoning models trained with reinforcement learning. It employs a dynamic inference paradigm involving distinct stages: an initial diagnosis, a subsequent evidence-seeking phase for proactive information acquisition, and a final decision stage to refine diagnoses. The effectiveness of this agentic strategy was evaluated by applying it across several existing large multimodal models and comparing their diagnostic accuracy.

Key Findings

The core finding is that an agentic, evidence-seeking workflow for pathological diagnosis consistently and significantly improves diagnostic accuracy compared to static inference paradigms. Specifically, PathFound achieves state-of-the-art diagnostic performance across various clinical scenarios, demonstrating a superior capability to identify subtle yet critical pathological details like nuclear features and local invasions.

Clinical Impact

PathFound has the potential to transform computational pathology by providing more accurate, reliable, and clinically aligned diagnostic support, particularly for challenging cases. It could assist pathologists by reducing diagnostic ambiguity, improving the detection of subtle disease features crucial for prognosis and treatment, and potentially streamlining diagnostic workflows to enhance efficiency and consistency, ultimately leading to better patient outcomes.

Abstract

Recent pathological foundation models have substantially advanced visual representation learning and multimodal interaction. However, most models still rely on a static inference paradigm in which whole-slide images are processed once to produce predictions, without reassessment or targeted evidence acquisition under ambiguous diagnoses. This contrasts with clinical diagnostic workflows that refine hypotheses through repeated slide observations and further examination requests. We propose PathFound, an agentic multimodal model designed to support evidence-seeking inference in pathological diagnosis. PathFound integrates the power of pathological visual foundation models, vision-language models, and reasoning models trained with reinforcement learning to perform proactive information acquisition and diagnosis refinement by progressing through the initial diagnosis, evidence-seeking, and final decision stages. Across several large multimodal models, adopting this strategy consistently improves diagnostic accuracy, indicating the effectiveness of evidence-seeking workflows in computational pathology. Among these models, PathFound achieves state-of-the-art diagnostic performance across diverse clinical scenarios and demonstrates strong potential to discover subtle details, such as nuclear features and local invasions.
