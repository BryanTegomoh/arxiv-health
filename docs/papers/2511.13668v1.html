Integrative Model for Interoception and Exteroception: predictive coding, points of modulation, and testable predictions

arXiv ID: 2511.13668v1
Published: 2025-11-17
Authors: Pranjal Balar, Sundeep Kapila
Categories: q-bio.NC
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper presents an integrative predictive coding model for interoceptive and exteroceptive integration, where relative precision weights arbitrate between the two streams in the AIC and ACC. Computational simulations replicated stable integration and modeled how dysregulated precision weighting, characteristic of anxiety (interoceptive overweighting) and PTSD (exteroceptive overweighting), creates rigid imbalances and slowed recalibration. Empirical validation with EEG-fMRI data supports the model, which offers a unifying framework for these psychiatric conditions and outlines testable predictions for targeted therapies.

Medical Relevance

This model offers a novel, mechanistic explanation for anxiety and PTSD based on dysregulated interoceptive-exteroceptive precision weighting, providing a theoretical foundation for developing targeted diagnostic biomarkers and therapeutic interventions aimed at recalibrating these fundamental brain processes.

AI Health Application

The paper develops an 'integrative predictive coding model' validated through 'computational simulations' and 'empirical application'. This computational model, which identifies mechanisms of mental health disorders (anxiety, PTSD) and proposes therapeutic targets, could serve as a foundational framework for AI-driven applications. These applications might include personalized diagnostics, predictive analytics for treatment response, or real-time adaptive therapeutic interventions (e.g., neurofeedback, vagal stimulation) that adjust based on an individual's interoceptive-exteroceptive balance and precision weighting.

Key Points

  • The model conceptualizes interoception and exteroception as parallel hierarchical predictive coding systems, exchanging precision-weighted prediction errors, with arbitration governed by relative precision weights (w).
  • Integration and arbitration are proposed to occur primarily within the anterior insula (AIC) and anterior cingulate cortex (ACC).
  • Computational simulations demonstrated stable system convergence and coherent integration (w=0.5) under normal conditions, with prediction errors decaying exponentially.
  • Simulated anxiety (characterized by interoceptive overweighting, w trending to 1) and PTSD (exteroceptive overweighting/underweighted interoception, w trending to 0) profiles led to rigid, self-sustaining imbalances and significantly slowed recalibration rates.
  • The model's arbitration equation was empirically validated by applying it to published EEG-fMRI datasets, supporting its biological plausibility.
  • The framework provides a unifying account wherein dysregulated precision weighting is a core neurocomputational mechanism underlying anxiety (overweighted interoception) and PTSD (underweighted interoception).
  • A detailed experimental paradigm is proposed to test model predictions in human anxiety, neutral, and PTSD groups, including neural markers (coherence, vagal modulation of heartbeat-evoked potentials) and precision-sensitive behavioral signatures in interoceptive-exteroceptive congruency tasks.

Methodology

The study employed a multi-pronged approach: (1) **Theoretical Model Development**: An integrative predictive coding framework was formulated, positing parallel interoceptive and exteroceptive systems with precision-weighted error exchange and arbitration in the AIC/ACC. (2) **Computational Simulations**: The model was computationally simulated to examine dynamic stability, integration, and dysregulation patterns resembling anxiety and PTSD. (3) **Empirical Validation**: The model's arbitration equation was applied to existing published EEG-fMRI datasets to validate its biological realism. (4) **Proposed Experimental Design**: An outline for future human experiments was developed, involving targeted therapies and assessment of neural and behavioral markers across clinical groups.

Key Findings

1. The integrative predictive coding model successfully reproduced biologically plausible dynamics, with prediction errors decaying exponentially and arbitration weights self-normalizing towards an equilibrium (w = 0.5), indicating stable, coherent integration. 2. Simulated anxiety (interoceptive overweighting) and PTSD (exteroceptive overweighting/underweighted interoception) conditions resulted in rigid, self-sustaining imbalances in precision weighting (w trending towards 1 or 0) and significantly slowed recalibration. 3. Empirical application of the model's arbitration equation to published EEG-fMRI datasets provided further validation, suggesting its relevance to actual brain activity. 4. The framework provides a unifying theoretical account where dysregulated precision weighting (e.g., interoceptive overweighting in anxiety, underweighted interoception in PTSD) serves as a core mechanism underlying these psychiatric disorders.

Clinical Impact

This research has the potential to significantly impact clinical practice by: (1) providing a robust neurocomputational framework to understand the underlying mechanisms of anxiety and PTSD, shifting focus from symptoms to foundational dysregulation of precision weighting. (2) Offering a theoretical basis for developing and evaluating targeted therapies aimed at recalibrating interoceptive and exteroceptive precision, such as neuromodulation (e.g., vagal stimulation) or specific cognitive-behavioral interventions. (3) Providing quantifiable neural (e.g., coherence markers, heartbeat-evoked potentials) and behavioral (e.g., congruency task performance) biomarkers to assess treatment efficacy and predict therapeutic response in these conditions.

Abstract

Interoception and exteroception provide continuous feedback about the body and the environment, yet how they are dynamically integrated within a unified predictive coding framework has remained under-specified. This paper develops and empirically validates an integrative predictive coding model that treats interoceptive and exteroceptive inference as parallel hierarchical systems exchanging precision-weighted prediction errors. Within this framework, arbitration between the two streams is governed by relative precision weights (w) and integrated within the anterior insula (AIC) and anterior cingulate cortex (ACC). Computational simulations of the model reproduced biologically plausible dynamics: prediction errors decayed exponentially while arbitration weights self-normalized toward equilibrium (w = 0.5), demonstrating stable convergence and coherent integration. Simulated anxiety and PTSD profiles, characterized respectively by interoceptive and exteroceptive overweighting, yielded rigid, self-sustaining imbalances (w to 1 or w to 0) and slowed recalibration. Empirical application of the arbitration equation to published EEG-fMRI datasets further validated the model. The framework contributes a unifying account of how dysregulated precision weighting may underlie anxiety (overweighted interoception) and PTSD (underweighted interoception). Building on this validation, a proposed experimental paradigm is outlined to test the model's predictions in humans. It examines recalibration across anxiety, neutral, and PTSD groups following targeted interoceptive or exteroceptive therapies. Key predictions include identifiable neural markers of coherence, modulation of heartbeat-evoked potentials by vagal stimulation, and precision-sensitive behavioral signatures in interoceptive-exteroceptive congruency tasks.
