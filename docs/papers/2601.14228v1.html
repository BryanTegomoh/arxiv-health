Summary

This paper presents an interpretable decision support framework for sepsis treatment in intensive care units (ICUs). The system integrates patient risk stratification, synthetic data augmentation, an offline reinforcement learning agent for treatment recommendations, and a multi-modal large language model (LLM) for generating clinical justifications. Evaluated on the MIMIC-III and eICU datasets, the approach achieves high treatment accuracy while providing robust and interpretable policy recommendations to clinicians.

Medical Relevance

Sepsis is a leading cause of mortality in ICUs, where timely and precise treatment decisions are paramount for patient survival and outcomes. This research offers an intelligent, interpretable decision support tool that can assist critical care clinicians in making more accurate, safer, and explainable treatment choices, potentially reducing sepsis-related morbidity and mortality.

AI Health Application

An AI-powered interpretable decision support system for personalized sepsis treatment. It uses clustering for patient risk stratification, variational autoencoders and diffusion models for augmenting medical trajectory data, offline reinforcement learning for providing safety-aware treatment recommendations, and large language models for generating clinical justifications and rationales for these recommendations.

Key Points

  • A comprehensive four-component framework is proposed, combining patient stratification, data augmentation, offline RL, and rationale generation, specifically for sepsis treatment in ICUs.
  • Patients are stratified into low, intermediate, and high-risk groups upon ICU admission using clustering with statistical validation to tailor treatment pathways.
  • A synthetic data augmentation pipeline leverages variational autoencoders (VAE) and diffusion models to address data sparsity for underrepresented but critical treatment trajectories, such as fluid or vasopressor administration.
  • An offline Reinforcement Learning (RL) agent, trained using Advantage Weighted Regression (AWR) with a lightweight attention encoder, provides conservative and safety-aware treatment recommendations, supported by ensemble models.
  • A rationale generation module, powered by a multi-modal Large Language Model (LLM), produces natural-language justifications for recommended treatments, grounded in clinical context and retrieved expert knowledge.
  • The framework was rigorously evaluated using two extensive real-world clinical datasets: MIMIC-III and eICU.
  • The proposed approach demonstrated high treatment accuracy and successfully provided clinicians with interpretable and robust policy recommendations.

Methodology

The methodology integrates four core components: (1) **Clustering-based stratification** for categorizing patients into low, intermediate, and high-risk groups using statistical validation upon ICU admission; (2) A **synthetic data augmentation pipeline** leveraging variational autoencoders (VAE) and diffusion models to enrich underrepresented treatment trajectories; (3) An **offline Reinforcement Learning (RL) agent** trained using Advantage Weighted Regression (AWR) with a lightweight attention encoder and ensemble models for conservative, safety-aware treatment recommendations; and (4) A **rationale generation module** utilizing a multi-modal large language model (LLM) to produce natural-language justifications grounded in clinical context and expert knowledge.

Key Findings

The proposed interpretable decision support framework achieved high treatment accuracy when evaluated on the MIMIC-III and eICU datasets. A pivotal finding is its capability to provide clinicians with not only effective treatment policy recommendations but also robust and interpretable rationales, fostering trust and aiding clinical decision-making.

Clinical Impact

This system has the potential to significantly enhance the quality and safety of sepsis treatment in ICUs by providing clinicians with data-driven, safety-aware, and timely recommendations. The interpretable natural-language rationales can improve clinician trust in AI-driven tools, support shared decision-making, and contribute to standardizing best practices, ultimately aiming to improve patient outcomes and reduce the high mortality associated with sepsis.

Abstract

Sepsis remains one of the leading causes of mortality in intensive care units, where timely and accurate treatment decisions can significantly impact patient outcomes. In this work, we propose an interpretable decision support framework. Our system integrates four core components: (1) a clustering-based stratification module that categorizes patients into low, intermediate, and high-risk groups upon ICU admission, using clustering with statistical validation; (2) a synthetic data augmentation pipeline leveraging variational autoencoders (VAE) and diffusion models to enrich underrepresented trajectories such as fluid or vasopressor administration; (3) an offline reinforcement learning (RL) agent trained using Advantage Weighted Regression (AWR) with a lightweight attention encoder and supported by an ensemble models for conservative, safety-aware treatment recommendations; and (4) a rationale generation module powered by a multi-modal large language model (LLM), which produces natural-language justifications grounded in clinical context and retrieved expert knowledge. Evaluated on the MIMIC-III and eICU datasets, our approach achieves high treatment accuracy while providing clinicians with interpretable and robust policy recommendations.
