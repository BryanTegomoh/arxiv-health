Summary

This paper introduces LungNoduleAgent, a collaborative multi-agent system designed for the precise diagnosis of lung nodules from CT scans. It addresses current limitations of multimodal large language models (LLMs) in accurately describing nodule morphology and integrating medical expertise, showcasing superior performance over existing models through its structured, modular approach for identification, reporting, and malignancy reasoning.

Medical Relevance

This system offers a substantial advancement in the field of lung cancer diagnosis by providing a more precise and reliable AI-driven tool for identifying, describing, and grading lung nodules. It aims to bridge the gap between general AI models and the detailed, expert-level analysis required in clinical radiology and oncology, potentially improving diagnostic accuracy and efficiency.

AI Health Application

LungNoduleAgent is an AI-driven collaborative multi-agent system designed to enhance the precision diagnosis of lung nodules from CT scans. It functions by automating and improving nodule identification, generating detailed CT reports, and conducting malignancy reasoning through the integration of clinical detection models, image description techniques, and a pathology knowledge base. Its primary application is to serve as a foundational tool supporting clinical analyses of lung nodules, thereby aiding physicians in the early and accurate detection of lung cancer.

  • The system's diagnostic process is streamlined into three sequential primary modules: the Nodule Spotter for accurate nodule identification using clinical detection models.
  • The second module, the Radiologist, integrates localized image description techniques to produce comprehensive CT reports detailing nodule features.
  • The final module, the Doctor Agent System, performs malignancy reasoning by combining images, CT reports, a pathology knowledge base, and a multi-agent system framework.
  • It directly addresses the challenges faced by existing multimodal LLMs, specifically their inaccuracies in describing nodule morphology and difficulties in fully incorporating medical expertise.
  • Extensive evaluation on two private datasets and the public LIDC-IDRI dataset demonstrated that LungNoduleAgent significantly surpasses mainstream vision-language models, other agent systems, and advanced expert models.

The 'Nodule Spotter' coordinates clinical detection models for identification; the 'Radiologist' integrates localized image description techniques to generate CT reports; and the 'Doctor Agent System' performs malignancy reasoning using images, CT reports, a pathology knowledge base, and a multi-agent framework for collaborative decision-making.

Abstract

Diagnosing lung cancer typically involves physicians identifying lung nodules in Computed tomography (CT) scans and generating diagnostic reports based on their morphological features and medical expertise. Although advancements have been made in using multimodal large language models for analyzing lung CT scans, challenges remain in accurately describing nodule morphology and incorporating medical expertise. These limitations affect the reliability and effectiveness of these models in clinical settings. Collaborative multi-agent systems offer a promising strategy for achieving a balance between generality and precision in medical applications, yet their potential in pathology has not been thoroughly explored. To bridge these gaps, we introduce LungNoduleAgent, an innovative collaborative multi-agent system specifically designed for analyzing lung CT scans. LungNoduleAgent streamlines the diagnostic process into sequential components, improving precision in describing nodules and grading malignancy through three primary modules. The first module, the Nodule Spotter, coordinates clinical detection models to accurately identify nodules. The second module, the Radiologist, integrates localized image description techniques to produce comprehensive CT reports. Finally, the Doctor Agent System performs malignancy reasoning by using images and CT reports, supported by a pathology knowledge base and a multi-agent system framework. Extensive testing on two private datasets and the public LIDC-IDRI dataset indicates that LungNoduleAgent surpasses mainstream vision-language models, agent systems, and advanced expert models. These results highlight the importance of region-level semantic alignment and multi-agent collaboration in diagnosing nodules. LungNoduleAgent stands out as a promising foundational tool for supporting clinical analyses of lung nodules.
