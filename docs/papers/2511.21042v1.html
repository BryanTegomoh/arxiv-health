Summary

LungNoduleAgent is an innovative collaborative multi-agent system designed for precise diagnosis of lung nodules from CT scans, addressing limitations in current multimodal large language models regarding morphological description and medical expertise integration. It streamlines the diagnostic process through three sequential modules, demonstrating superior performance against existing models and highlighting the critical role of region-level semantic alignment and multi-agent collaboration.

Medical Relevance

This system significantly enhances the precision and reliability of lung nodule diagnosis from CT scans, crucial for early detection and management of lung cancer. By effectively integrating detailed morphological descriptions with medical expertise, it offers a more robust AI tool for radiologists and oncologists.

AI Health Application

The paper introduces LungNoduleAgent, a collaborative multi-agent AI system that uses computer vision and natural language processing techniques to identify lung nodules, describe their morphology from CT scans, and perform malignancy reasoning. This system is designed to act as a foundational tool to support clinical diagnosis of lung cancer, thereby enhancing precision and reliability in medical decision-making for patient health.

Methodology

LungNoduleAgent employs a collaborative multi-agent system architecture that processes lung CT scans through three sequential modules. The 'Nodule Spotter' coordinates clinical detection models for precise nodule identification. The 'Radiologist' module then integrates localized image description techniques to generate comprehensive CT reports. Finally, the 'Doctor Agent System' performs malignancy reasoning, utilizing both the images and the generated CT reports, supported by a pathology knowledge base and a multi-agent system framework, focusing on region-level semantic alignment.

Key Findings

LungNoduleAgent demonstrably surpasses mainstream vision-language models, other agent systems, and advanced expert models in accurately diagnosing lung nodules. The study establishes that region-level semantic alignment and multi-agent collaboration are fundamental enablers for achieving high precision and reliability in nodule diagnostic systems.

Abstract

Diagnosing lung cancer typically involves physicians identifying lung nodules in Computed tomography (CT) scans and generating diagnostic reports based on their morphological features and medical expertise. Although advancements have been made in using multimodal large language models for analyzing lung CT scans, challenges remain in accurately describing nodule morphology and incorporating medical expertise. These limitations affect the reliability and effectiveness of these models in clinical settings. Collaborative multi-agent systems offer a promising strategy for achieving a balance between generality and precision in medical applications, yet their potential in pathology has not been thoroughly explored. To bridge these gaps, we introduce LungNoduleAgent, an innovative collaborative multi-agent system specifically designed for analyzing lung CT scans. LungNoduleAgent streamlines the diagnostic process into sequential components, improving precision in describing nodules and grading malignancy through three primary modules. The first module, the Nodule Spotter, coordinates clinical detection models to accurately identify nodules. The second module, the Radiologist, integrates localized image description techniques to produce comprehensive CT reports. Finally, the Doctor Agent System performs malignancy reasoning by using images and CT reports, supported by a pathology knowledge base and a multi-agent system framework. Extensive testing on two private datasets and the public LIDC-IDRI dataset indicates that LungNoduleAgent surpasses mainstream vision-language models, agent systems, and advanced expert models. These results highlight the importance of region-level semantic alignment and multi-agent collaboration in diagnosing nodules. LungNoduleAgent stands out as a promising foundational tool for supporting clinical analyses of lung nodules.
