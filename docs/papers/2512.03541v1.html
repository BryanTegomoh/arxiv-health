Summary

This paper addresses the critical need for standardized operating procedures (SOPs) in medical imaging data management to facilitate the creation of AI-ready datasets. Developed by the NIH Bridge2AI Standards Working Group, it describes novel SOPs for the DICOM format, covering both static and cutting-edge video modalities, with a strong emphasis on data aggregation, validation, and advanced de-identification techniques.

Medical Relevance

This work is vital for advancing medical AI, enabling more efficient and reliable use of vast medical imaging data for disease diagnosis, monitoring, and clinical research discovery, ultimately leading to improved patient care and scientific breakthroughs.

AI Health Application

The primary AI health application is enabling and improving the development and deployment of AI/ML models for medical image analysis, diagnosis, and monitoring. By providing robust SOPs for data management, validation, and de-identification, the research aims to create high-quality, 'FAIR' (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) and 'AI-ready datasets' crucial for training effective and ethical medical AI systems, particularly in areas like ophthalmology retinal scan analysis.

Key Points

  • Identifies a significant gap: while standard formats like DICOM exist for medical imaging, SOPs for effective data management are largely absent.
  • Emphasizes that robust data management SOPs are crucial for achieving Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable (FAIR) data, which is a prerequisite for AI-ready datasets.
  • Describes the development of novel SOPs for DICOM data management by the NIH Bridge2AI Standards Working Group and domain experts.
  • These SOPs apply to a wide spectrum of imaging, including traditional static modalities and emerging cutting-edge video imaging modalities.
  • Highlights key procedural steps: centralized data aggregation, rigorous data validation, and robust de-identification techniques.
  • Includes a review of new 'defacing' methods for facial DICOM scans, specifically designed to anticipate and counteract adversarial AI/ML re-identification methods, enhancing data privacy.
  • Illustrates the practical application of these SOPs through data management vignettes based on Bridge2AI datasets, including efficient capture parameters for new ophthalmology retinal scan DICOM modalities.

Methodology

The methodology involved collaborative development of data management SOPs by the NIH Bridge2AI Standards Working Group members and domain-expert stakeholders. This process focused on establishing standardized procedures for DICOM data, encompassing aggregation, validation, and de-identification, and was informed by the needs of various imaging modalities and real-world Bridge2AI datasets.

Key Findings

The primary finding is the successful development and description of novel, comprehensive SOPs for managing DICOM medical imaging data. These SOPs specifically address the requirements for making data FAIR and AI-ready, including advanced de-identification strategies like 'defacing' for facial scans, and practical application examples for diverse modalities, notably new ophthalmology retinal scans.

Clinical Impact

The clinical impact includes streamlining the often months-long process of operationalizing medical imaging data for research and AI development, improving data quality and reliability for diagnostic algorithms, enhancing patient privacy through advanced de-identification, and accelerating the discovery of new diagnostic and therapeutic insights from medical imaging.

Limitations

Not explicitly mentioned in the abstract, but the abstract itself highlights the *current* lack of such SOPs as a major bottleneck, which the paper aims to resolve with its proposed solutions.

Future Directions

While not explicitly stated as 'future directions' in the abstract, the developed SOPs inherently pave the way for widespread adoption across medical imaging institutions, facilitating the creation of large-scale, high-quality, AI-ready datasets that can power future AI/ML advancements in diagnostics, prognostics, and personalized medicine.

Abstract

Medical imaging data plays a vital role in disease diagnosis, monitoring, and clinical research discovery. Biomedical data managers and clinical researchers must navigate a complex landscape of medical imaging infrastructure, input/output tools and data reliability workflow configurations taking months to operationalize. While standard formats exist for medical imaging data, standard operating procedures (SOPs) for data management are lacking. These data management SOPs are key for developing Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable (FAIR) data, a prerequisite for AI-ready datasets. The National Institutes of Health (NIH) Bridge to Artificial Intelligence (Bridge2AI) Standards Working Group members and domain-expert stakeholders from the Bridge2AI Grand Challenges teams developed data management SOPs for the Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine (DICOM) format. We describe novel SOPs applying to both static and cutting edge video imaging modalities. We emphasize steps required for centralized data aggregation, validation, and de-identification, including a review of new defacing methods for facial DICOM scans, anticipating adversarial AI/ML data re-identification methods. Data management vignettes based on Bridge2AI datasets include example parameters for efficient capture of a wide modality spectrum, including datasets from new ophthalmology retinal scans DICOM modalities.
