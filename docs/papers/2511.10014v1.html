fastbmRAG: A Fast Graph-Based RAG Framework for Efficient Processing of Large-Scale Biomedical Literature

Authors: Guofeng Meng, Li Shen, Qiuyan Zhong, Wei Wang, Haizhou Zhang, Xiaozhen Wang

Summary

fastbmRAG is a novel, fast graph-based Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) framework designed for efficient processing of large-scale biomedical literature. It addresses the computational intensity of existing graph-RAGs by employing a two-stage knowledge graph construction process that leverages paper structure and vector-based entity linking. The framework demonstrates over 10x speed improvement while maintaining superior coverage and accuracy for understanding, summarizing, and querying medical texts.

Medical Relevance

This framework significantly accelerates the processing and comprehension of vast amounts of biomedical research, which is crucial for accelerating scientific discovery, drug development, evidence-based clinical decision-making, and rapid access to critical medical knowledge.

AI Health Application

fastbmRAG is an AI framework (graph-based RAG leveraging LLMs) designed to efficiently process and extract knowledge from large-scale biomedical literature. Its application is to accelerate scientific research, drug discovery, and medical understanding by providing a fast solution for summarizing and answering questions about complex biomedical information, thereby supporting medical professionals, researchers, and pharmaceutical development.

Key Points

  • Addresses the computational intensity bottleneck of existing graph-based RAG systems, which hinders their application to vast biomedical datasets.
  • Introduces fastbmRAG, a graph-based RAG specifically optimized for the well-organized structure of biomedical papers.
  • Utilizes a novel two-stage methodology for knowledge graph construction: initially drafting graphs from abstracts, then refining them using main texts.
  • Employs vector-based entity linking during the refinement stage to guide text processing, minimizing redundancy and computational load.
  • Achieves a significant speed improvement, demonstrating over 10x faster performance compared to existing graph-RAG tools.
  • Provides superior knowledge coverage and accuracy in extracting and representing information from input biomedical literature.
  • Enables rapid understanding, summarization, and accurate question-answering over large-scale biomedical literature collections.

Methodology

fastbmRAG employs a novel two-stage approach for knowledge graph construction specifically tailored for biomedical literature. Initially, a draft knowledge graph is built by processing paper abstracts. Subsequently, this draft is refined and expanded by analyzing the full main texts, with the refinement process guided by vector-based entity linking to ensure precision, minimize redundancy, and optimize computational efficiency. This structured approach leverages the inherent organization of biomedical papers.

Key Findings

The fastbmRAG framework significantly outperforms existing graph-RAG tools, achieving over a 10-fold increase in processing speed. Concurrently, it demonstrates superior knowledge coverage and accuracy when extracting and structuring information from large-scale biomedical literature.

Abstract

Large language models (LLMs) are rapidly transforming various domains, including biomedicine and healthcare, and demonstrate remarkable potential from scientific research to new drug discovery. Graph-based retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems, as a useful application of LLMs, can improve contextual reasoning through structured entity and relationship identification from long-context knowledge, e.g. biomedical literature. Even though many advantages over naive RAGs, most of graph-based RAGs are computationally intensive, which limits their application to large-scale dataset. To address this issue, we introduce fastbmRAG, an fast graph-based RAG optimized for biomedical literature. Utilizing well organized structure of biomedical papers, fastbmRAG divides the construction of knowledge graph into two stages, first drafting graphs using abstracts; and second, refining them using main texts guided by vector-based entity linking, which minimizes redundancy and computational load. Our evaluations demonstrate that fastbmRAG is over 10x faster than existing graph-RAG tools and achieve superior coverage and accuracy to input knowledge. FastbmRAG provides a fast solution for quickly understanding, summarizing, and answering questions about biomedical literature on a large scale. FastbmRAG is public available in https://github.com/menggf/fastbmRAG.
