Adaptive Identification and Modeling of Clinical Pathways with Process Mining

arXiv ID: 2512.03787v1
Published: 2025-12-03
Authors: Francesco Vitale, Nicola Mazzocca
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a two-phase process mining method designed to adaptively identify and model clinical pathways, addressing the limitations of manual, static modeling. By leveraging conformance checking, the approach dynamically expands the knowledge base of treatment protocols for various disease variants, demonstrating high precision and model simplicity on simulated SARS-CoV-2 infection data.

Medical Relevance

Improving clinical pathways through adaptive, data-driven modeling is crucial for standardizing patient care, optimizing resource utilization, and accelerating recovery by ensuring treatments reflect actual best practices for diverse patient conditions.

AI Health Application

The research leverages process mining, an AI/machine learning technique, to adaptively identify and model clinical pathways from historical patient treatment data. This AI application aims to improve the standardization and personalization of patient care, reduce resource use, and accelerate recovery by providing data-driven, evidence-based treatment protocols that evolve with new disease variants or combinations, moving beyond static, manually-defined guidelines.

Key Points

  • Manual modeling of clinical pathways from guidelines and expertise is often difficult and may not reflect actual best practices for diverse disease variations or combinations.
  • The proposed method utilizes a two-phase process mining approach to build and adapt a knowledge base of clinical pathways.
  • Phase one involves discovering a reference process model from historical patient treatment data for a given disease.
  • Phase two compares new treatment data against the reference model using conformance checking diagnostics to identify deviations.
  • Based on conformance checking results, the knowledge base can be expanded with more specific models tailored to new disease variants or combinations, making pathways adaptive.
  • The approach was demonstrated using the Synthea benchmark dataset, simulating SARS-CoV-2 infections with varying COVID-19 complications.
  • Results show the method enables knowledge base expansion with high precision (95.62% AUC) while maintaining reasonable model simplicity (67.11% arc-degree simplicity).

Methodology

The methodology is a two-phase process mining approach. In the first phase, historical patient treatment data for a specific disease is collected to discover and model a reference clinical pathway using process mining techniques. In the second phase, newly acquired patient data is compared against this established reference model through conformance checking diagnostics. The insights from conformance checking, particularly identifying deviations, are then used to expand the clinical pathway knowledge base by generating more refined and specific models for new disease variants or combinations.

Key Findings

The study's key findings indicate that the proposed two-phase process mining method effectively facilitates the adaptive expansion of clinical pathway knowledge bases. The generated models achieve high precision, peaking at 95.62% AUC (Area Under the Curve), while maintaining an arc-degree simplicity of 67.11%. This demonstrates the method's capability to accurately capture treatment flows and adapt to disease variations without excessive model complexity.

Clinical Impact

This method has the potential to significantly enhance the standardization and personalization of patient treatment by providing dynamic, data-driven clinical pathways that accurately reflect actual best practices. This could lead to improved quality of care, more efficient allocation of healthcare resources, and potentially faster patient recovery by optimizing treatment protocols for specific disease presentations and their complications.

Abstract

Clinical pathways are specialized healthcare plans that model patient treatment procedures. They are developed to provide criteria-based progression and standardize patient treatment, thereby improving care, reducing resource use, and accelerating patient recovery. However, manual modeling of these pathways based on clinical guidelines and domain expertise is difficult and may not reflect the actual best practices for different variations or combinations of diseases. We propose a two-phase modeling method using process mining, which extends the knowledge base of clinical pathways by leveraging conformance checking diagnostics. In the first phase, historical data of a given disease is collected to capture treatment in the form of a process model. In the second phase, new data is compared against the reference model to verify conformance. Based on the conformance checking results, the knowledge base can be expanded with more specific models tailored to new variants or disease combinations. We demonstrate our approach using Synthea, a benchmark dataset simulating patient treatments for SARS-CoV-2 infections with varying COVID-19 complications. The results show that our method enables expanding the knowledge base of clinical pathways with sufficient precision, peaking to 95.62% AUC while maintaining an arc-degree simplicity of 67.11%.
