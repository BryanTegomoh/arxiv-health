Summary

This paper introduces nnUNet-B, a Bayesian segmentation framework that leverages Multimodal Posterior Sampling (MPS) within nnUNet-v2 to directly infer PD-L1 expression from H&E-stained histology images. It achieves competitive segmentation performance while also providing pixel-wise epistemic uncertainty estimates that correlate with segmentation errors, aiming to deliver a more scalable and interpretable biomarker assessment for immunotherapy.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and efficient PD-L1 expression assessment is critical for guiding immunotherapy in cancer patients. This method offers a resource-friendly alternative to traditional immunohistochemistry (IHC), potentially accelerating and broadening access to vital biomarker information directly from routine H&E slides.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of a Bayesian deep learning model (nnUNet-B) for automated, uncertainty-aware segmentation and prediction of PD-L1 expression directly from H&E histopathology images. This AI system aims to serve as a diagnostic aid to streamline and enhance the assessment of a crucial biomarker for personalized cancer treatment decisions, particularly in guiding immunotherapy. By providing pixel-wise uncertainty maps, it enhances the interpretability and trustworthiness of AI predictions in a clinical setting, potentially making biomarker assessment more scalable and accessible.

Key Points

  • Addresses the resource-intensive nature of current IHC-based PD-L1 assessment by proposing a method using readily available H&E images.
  • Introduces nnUNet-B, a Bayesian segmentation framework built on nnUNet-v2, which employs Multimodal Posterior Sampling (MPS).
  • MPS approximates the model's posterior distribution by sampling diverse checkpoints saved during cyclic training.
  • The framework provides both accurate PD-L1 segmentation (mean Dice 0.805, mean IoU 0.709) and pixel-wise epistemic uncertainty maps, quantified by entropy and standard deviation.
  • Uncertainty estimates demonstrate a strong correlation with segmentation errors, offering valuable interpretability into model confidence.
  • Evaluated on lung squamous cell carcinoma, the method achieves competitive performance against established baselines.
  • While uncertainty calibration is noted as imperfect, the approach represents a promising step towards scalable and interpretable biomarker assessment in clinical workflows.

Methodology

The proposed method, nnUNet-B, is a Bayesian segmentation framework built upon the nnUNet-v2 architecture. It utilizes Multimodal Posterior Sampling (MPS), where the network undergoes cyclic training, and diverse model checkpoints are saved at various points. These sampled checkpoints are then used to approximate the posterior distribution over model parameters, allowing for the generation of both a segmentation mask and pixel-wise epistemic uncertainty estimates (calculated via entropy and standard deviation across predictions from the sampled models). The approach was evaluated on a dataset of lung squamous cell carcinoma.

Key Findings

The nnUNet-B framework achieved competitive PD-L1 segmentation performance, with a mean Dice Score of 0.805 and a mean IoU of 0.709. Crucially, it successfully generated pixel-wise uncertainty maps, and these uncertainty estimates showed a strong correlation with actual segmentation errors, indicating their potential utility in identifying unreliable predictions. However, the calibration of these uncertainty estimates was noted as imperfect.

Clinical Impact

This approach has the potential to significantly streamline PD-L1 assessment in clinical workflows by eliminating the need for separate, resource-intensive IHC staining, replacing it with analysis of readily available H&E slides. By providing both accurate predictions and interpretable uncertainty maps, it could enable more scalable, faster, and cost-effective immunotherapy guidance, improving access to personalized treatment decisions and enhancing confidence in AI-driven diagnostics.

Limitations

The primary limitation noted is that the calibration of the uncertainty estimates remains imperfect, meaning the model's expressed confidence may not precisely align with the true probability of correctness.

Future Directions

Future research should focus on improving the calibration of uncertainty estimates to enhance their reliability for clinical decision-making. Further validation across a broader range of cancer types and larger, diverse datasets would also be crucial to establish the generalizability and robustness of the method for widespread clinical adoption.

Abstract

Accurate assessment of PD-L1 expression is critical for guiding immunotherapy, yet current immunohistochemistry (IHC) based methods are resource-intensive. We present nnUNet-B: a Bayesian segmentation framework that infers PD-L1 expression directly from H&E-stained histology images using Multimodal Posterior Sampling (MPS). Built upon nnUNet-v2, our method samples diverse model checkpoints during cyclic training to approximate the posterior, enabling both accurate segmentation and epistemic uncertainty estimation via entropy and standard deviation. Evaluated on a dataset of lung squamous cell carcinoma, our approach achieves competitive performance against established baselines with mean Dice Score and mean IoU of 0.805 and 0.709, respectively, while providing pixel-wise uncertainty maps. Uncertainty estimates show strong correlation with segmentation error, though calibration remains imperfect. These results suggest that uncertainty-aware H&E-based PD-L1 prediction is a promising step toward scalable, interpretable biomarker assessment in clinical workflows.
