Summary

This paper introduces StrokeSeg, a modular and lightweight framework designed to transform high-performing research-grade deep learning models for stroke lesion segmentation into clinically deployable applications. It addresses the deployment challenges of existing frameworks by decoupling components, utilizing efficient runtimes and quantization, and demonstrating equivalent performance to original PyTorch pipelines while offering portability and user-friendly interfaces.

Medical Relevance

This tool is crucial for translating advanced AI research in medical imaging into practical clinical applications, enabling faster, more standardized, and accessible stroke lesion analysis directly within healthcare workflows, which can aid in diagnosis, treatment planning, and outcome assessment.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the automated segmentation of stroke lesions from medical images (e.g., MRI or CT scans) using deep learning. This tool facilitates the integration of advanced AI models into clinical workflows to assist in diagnosis, monitoring, treatment planning, or research for stroke patients by providing a portable and clinically usable method for lesion analysis.

Key Points

  • Addresses the challenge of deploying state-of-the-art deep learning models (like nnU-Net) for brain lesion segmentation in clinical settings due to their heavy dependencies and monolithic design.
  • Introduces StrokeSeg, a modular framework that decouples preprocessing, inference, and postprocessing steps for enhanced flexibility and deployment.
  • Utilizes the Anima toolbox for BIDS-compliant preprocessing and ONNX Runtime with Float16 quantization for inference, significantly reducing model size by approximately 50%.
  • Provides both graphical (GUI) and command-line interfaces (CLI) to cater to diverse user preferences and integration needs within clinical workflows.
  • Distributed as Python scripts and a standalone Windows executable, facilitating easy installation and use in various clinical environments.
  • Demonstrates segmentation performance equivalent to the original PyTorch pipeline on a held-out set of 300 sub-acute and chronic stroke subjects (Dice difference < 10^-3).
  • Validates the feasibility of transforming high-performing research models into portable, lightweight, and clinically usable tools without sacrificing accuracy.

Methodology

The methodology involves a modular design where preprocessing, inference, and postprocessing are decoupled. Preprocessing is handled by the Anima toolbox, producing BIDS-compliant outputs. Inference leverages ONNX Runtime with Float16 quantization to reduce model size by 50%. The tool provides both graphical and command-line interfaces and is distributed as Python scripts and a standalone Windows executable. Performance was validated by comparing against the original PyTorch pipeline on 300 stroke subjects.

Key Findings

The StrokeSeg framework achieved segmentation performance equivalent to the original PyTorch research pipeline, with a Dice difference of less than 10^-3, on a diverse dataset of 300 sub-acute and chronic stroke subjects. It also successfully reduced model size by approximately 50% through Float16 quantization while maintaining accuracy, demonstrating that high-performing research models can be made lightweight and clinically deployable.

Clinical Impact

StrokeSeg's clinical impact lies in providing a readily deployable, lightweight, and user-friendly tool for automated stroke lesion segmentation, directly integrating state-of-the-art AI into clinical workflows. This can standardize lesion quantification, reduce manual effort, improve the efficiency of stroke assessment, aid in therapeutic decision-making (e.g., rehabilitation planning), and facilitate longitudinal monitoring of stroke patients, even in resource-constrained environments.

Abstract

Deep learning frameworks such as nnU-Net achieve state-of-the-art performance in brain lesion segmentation but remain difficult to deploy clinically due to heavy dependencies and monolithic design. We introduce \textit{StrokeSeg}, a modular and lightweight framework that translates research-grade stroke lesion segmentation models into deployable applications. Preprocessing, inference, and postprocessing are decoupled: preprocessing relies on the Anima toolbox with BIDS-compliant outputs, and inference uses ONNX Runtime with \texttt{Float16} quantisation, reducing model size by about 50\%. \textit{StrokeSeg} provides both graphical and command-line interfaces and is distributed as Python scripts and as a standalone Windows executable. On a held-out set of 300 sub-acute and chronic stroke subjects, segmentation performance was equivalent to the original PyTorch pipeline (Dice difference $<10^{-3}$), demonstrating that high-performing research pipelines can be transformed into portable, clinically usable tools.
