DMAGT: Unveiling miRNA-Drug Associations by Integrating SMILES and RNA Sequence Structures through Graph Transformer Models

arXiv ID: 2512.05287v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Ziqi Zhang
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces DMAGT, a novel machine learning model based on a multi-layer transformer-based graph neural network, designed to predict associations between drugs and miRNAs. By integrating drug molecular structures (SMILES) and miRNA base structures via Word2Vec embeddings and graph transformers, DMAGT overcomes the limitations of traditional wet lab experiments. The model demonstrated superior performance with an AUC up to 95.24% and successfully validated 14 out of 20 top predictions for specific anticancer drugs, offering a significant shortcut for miRNA drug development.

Medical Relevance

MiRNAs are critical regulators of gene expression and are increasingly recognized as therapeutic targets. DMAGT's ability to accurately predict miRNA-drug associations can significantly accelerate the discovery and development of new miRNA-targeting drugs, potentially leading to more effective and personalized treatments for various diseases.

AI Health Application

The AI model (DMAGT) is applied to computationally predict novel associations between drugs and miRNAs. This serves as a 'shortcut' for miRNA drug development, significantly reducing the time, cost, and effort associated with traditional wet lab experiments. By accurately identifying potential drug-miRNA interactions, the model aids in identifying new therapeutic targets, repurposing existing drugs, and designing novel drugs for various diseases, ultimately accelerating the drug development pipeline and improving patient outcomes.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: Traditional wet lab experiments for identifying miRNA-drug associations are inefficient and cost-prohibitive, limiting extensive exploration.
  • **Proposed Solution**: DMAGT, a multi-layer transformer-based graph neural network, for computational prediction of drug-miRNA associations.
  • **Methodology**: Transforms drug-miRNA associations into graph structures. Employs Word2Vec for embedding features from drug molecular structures (SMILES) and miRNA base sequences. Utilizes a graph transformer model to learn from these embedded features and relational structures.
  • **Performance Evaluation**: Tested on three distinct drug-miRNA association datasets: ncDR, RNAInter, and SM2miR, achieving a peak AUC of $95.24\pm0.05$.
  • **Comparative Superiority**: DMAGT demonstrated superior performance compared to existing models designed for similar prediction tasks.
  • **Practical Validation**: For a targeted validation using 5-Fluorouracil and Oxaliplatin (known anticancer drugs), 14 out of the 20 most likely predicted drug-miRNA associations were successfully validated experimentally.
  • **Overall Implication**: The model offers a robust and stable computational shortcut for accelerating miRNA drug development and discovery.

Methodology

DMAGT employs a multi-layer transformer-based graph neural network architecture. It models drug-miRNA interactions as graphs. Drug molecular structures, typically represented by SMILES strings, and miRNA base sequences are first converted into numerical feature vectors using Word2Vec embeddings. These embeddings, along with the relational information from the graph structure, are then fed into a graph transformer model, which learns complex patterns to predict the likelihood of an association between a given drug and miRNA.

Key Findings

DMAGT achieved an impressive AUC of up to $95.24\pm0.05$ across three diverse datasets (ncDR, RNAInter, SM2miR), outperforming other state-of-the-art models. Crucially, in a focused practical validation for two specific drugs, 5-Fluorouracil and Oxaliplatin, 14 out of the 20 highest-confidence predicted drug-miRNA associations were experimentally confirmed, demonstrating strong predictive accuracy and real-world utility.

Clinical Impact

This computational tool has the potential to significantly reduce the time, cost, and resources required for identifying promising drug candidates that modulate miRNA activity. By rapidly screening vast numbers of potential drug-miRNA pairs, DMAGT can accelerate the pre-clinical phase of drug development, facilitate the repurposing of existing drugs, and contribute to the discovery of novel therapeutic strategies, particularly in oncology where drugs like 5-Fluorouracil and Oxaliplatin are relevant.

Abstract

MiRNAs, due to their role in gene regulation, have paved a new pathway for pharmacology, focusing on drug development that targets miRNAs. However, traditional wet lab experiments are limited by efficiency and cost constraints, making it difficult to extensively explore potential associations between developed drugs and target miRNAs. Therefore, we have designed a novel machine learning model based on a multi-layer transformer-based graph neural network, DMAGT, specifically for predicting associations between drugs and miRNAs. This model transforms drug-miRNA associations into graphs, employs Word2Vec for embedding features of drug molecular structures and miRNA base structures, and leverages a graph transformer model to learn from embedded features and relational structures, ultimately predicting associations between drugs and miRNAs. To evaluate DMAGT, we tested its performance on three datasets composed of drug-miRNA associations: ncDR, RNAInter, and SM2miR, achieving up to AUC of $95.24\pm0.05$. DMAGT demonstrated superior performance in comparative experiments tackling similar challenges. To validate its practical efficacy, we specifically focused on two drugs, namely 5-Fluorouracil and Oxaliplatin. Of the 20 potential drug-miRNA associations identified as the most likely, 14 were successfully validated. The above experiments demonstrate that DMAGT has an excellent performance and stability in predicting drug-miRNA associations, providing a new shortcut for miRNA drug development.
