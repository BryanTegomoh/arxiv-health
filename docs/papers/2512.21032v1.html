Multi-Attribute guided Thermal Face Image Translation based on Latent Diffusion Model

Summary

This paper introduces a novel latent diffusion model designed for Heterogeneous Face Recognition (HFR), specifically translating thermal face images into high-quality visible light images. The approach integrates a multi-attribute classifier to preserve critical identity features and proposes a Self-attn Mamba module to enhance global cross-modal feature modeling and improve inference speed. Experimental results demonstrate state-of-the-art performance in both generated image quality and identity preservation.

Medical Relevance

While focused on surveillance, this research is highly relevant to healthcare as thermal imaging is increasingly used for non-contact patient monitoring, fever screening, and identification. The ability to generate high-quality, identity-preserving visible face images from thermal inputs can significantly enhance the reliability and accuracy of medical facial recognition applications, especially in low-light or critical care environments.

Improved AI-driven identity verification and access control systems for biosecurity facilities, enabling more reliable personnel identification and monitoring in secure areas, especially under low-light or night conditions, to prevent unauthorized access and safeguard against biological threats.

Methodology

The core methodology involves a novel latent diffusion model for thermal-to-visible face image translation. Key components include a multi-attribute classifier designed to extract and preserve critical facial identity features from visible images, thereby mitigating feature loss. Additionally, a Self-attn Mamba module is integrated to improve global modeling of cross-modal features (thermal to visible) and to significantly accelerate inference speed. The model's performance was validated through experiments on two benchmark datasets, focusing on image quality and identity preservation metrics.

Key Findings

The proposed latent diffusion-based model, incorporating a multi-attribute classifier and the Self-attn Mamba module, achieved state-of-the-art performance. It demonstrated superior results in both the quality of the generated visible face images from thermal inputs and the preservation of critical identity features, alongside an improved inference speed, effectively overcoming limitations of previous generative HFR approaches.

Abstract

Modern surveillance systems increasingly rely on multi-wavelength sensors and deep neural networks to recognize faces in infrared images captured at night. However, most facial recognition models are trained on visible light datasets, leading to substantial performance degradation on infrared inputs due to significant domain shifts. Early feature-based methods for infrared face recognition proved ineffective, prompting researchers to adopt generative approaches that convert infrared images into visible light images for improved recognition. This paradigm, known as Heterogeneous Face Recognition (HFR), faces challenges such as model and modality discrepancies, leading to distortion and feature loss in generated images. To address these limitations, this paper introduces a novel latent diffusion-based model designed to generate high-quality visible face images from thermal inputs while preserving critical identity features. A multi-attribute classifier is incorporated to extract key facial attributes from visible images, mitigating feature loss during infrared-to-visible image restoration. Additionally, we propose the Self-attn Mamba module, which enhances global modeling of cross-modal features and significantly improves inference speed. Experimental results on two benchmark datasets demonstrate the superiority of our approach, achieving state-of-the-art performance in both image quality and identity preservation.
