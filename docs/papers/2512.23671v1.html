Summary

This paper introduces Multi-Level Quantile Tracker (MultiQT), an online method that guarantees simultaneous calibration of multi-level quantile forecasts from any existing forecaster. MultiQT corrects forecasts to ensure they are ordered and calibrated, even under adversarial distribution shifts, and comes with a no-regret guarantee. The method significantly improves forecast calibration in real-world applications, notably in epidemic and energy forecasting.

Medical Relevance

Accurate, calibrated, and ordered multi-level quantile forecasts are critical for robust public health decision-making, enabling better risk assessment, resource allocation (e.g., hospital beds, vaccines), and policy interventions during public health crises like epidemics.

AI Health Application

The research provides a method (MultiQT) to improve the calibration and reliability of AI/machine learning models used for forecasting, specifically in the context of epidemic forecasting. This enhances the trustworthiness and utility of AI systems deployed for public health decision-making and preparedness.

Key Points

  • MultiQT is an online, lightweight wrapper method designed to correct and calibrate multi-level quantile forecasts from any existing point or quantile forecaster.
  • It guarantees simultaneous calibration, meaning $Œ±$-level forecasts are larger than the true value an $Œ±$ fraction of the time, across multiple quantile levels.
  • The method ensures that corrected quantile forecasts are consistently ordered (e.g., the 0.5-level forecast is never larger than the 0.6-level forecast).
  • MultiQT is robust against adversarial distribution shifts, ensuring its calibration guarantees remain valid even in dynamic and unpredictable environments.
  • It offers a no-regret guarantee, implying that asymptotically, it will not worsen the performance of the underlying forecaster with respect to the quantile loss.
  • Empirical results demonstrate MultiQT's significant improvement in the calibration of real-world forecasters, specifically in epidemic and energy forecasting problems.

Methodology

MultiQT is an online, adaptive algorithm that functions as a wrapper around any pre-existing point or quantile forecaster. It continuously monitors the calibration of the input forecasts across multiple quantile levels and applies real-time corrections to ensure that the output forecasts are both calibrated and monotonically ordered (e.g., higher quantiles predict higher values). The method is designed to be robust against dynamic, even adversarial, changes in data distribution.

Abstract

We present an online method for guaranteeing calibration of quantile forecasts at multiple quantile levels simultaneously. A sequence of $Œ±$-level quantile forecasts is calibrated if the forecasts are larger than the target value at an $Œ±$-fraction of time steps. We introduce a lightweight method called Multi-Level Quantile Tracker (MultiQT) that wraps around any existing point or quantile forecaster to produce corrected forecasts guaranteed to achieve calibration, even against adversarial distribution shifts, while ensuring that the forecasts are ordered -- e.g., the 0.5-level quantile forecast is never larger than the 0.6-level forecast. Furthermore, the method comes with a no-regret guarantee that implies it will not worsen the performance of an existing forecaster, asymptotically, with respect to the quantile loss. In experiments, we find that MultiQT significantly improves the calibration of real forecasters in epidemic and energy forecasting problems.
