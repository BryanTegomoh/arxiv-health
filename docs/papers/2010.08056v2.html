IoT Platform for COVID-19 Prevention and Control: A Survey

arXiv ID: 2010.08056v2
Published: 2020-10-15
Authors: Yudi Dong, Yu-Dong Yao
Categories: cs.HC, cs.AI, eess.SY
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This survey paper proposes and demonstrates a potential fog-cloud combined Internet of Things (IoT) platform for systematic and intelligent COVID-19 prevention and control, addressing the need for long-term non-pharmaceutical interventions against the unprecedented pandemic. It investigates and reviews state-of-the-art literature across five critical interventions: Symptom Diagnosis, Quarantine Monitoring, Contact Tracing & Social Distancing, Outbreak Forecasting, and SARS-CoV-2 Mutation Tracking, highlighting IoT's capabilities.

Medical Relevance

This paper offers a critical framework for leveraging advanced technology to combat infectious disease pandemics, providing a systematic approach to public health surveillance, intervention, and control through non-pharmaceutical means. It addresses the urgent need for scalable and intelligent systems to manage health crises and reduce their impact on global public health.

AI Health Application

The paper describes an 'intelligent COVID-19 prevention and control' system leveraging IoT. This includes AI applications in: 1) COVID-19 Symptom Diagnosis (e.g., AI-powered analysis of sensor data for early detection); 2) Quarantine Monitoring (e.g., AI for anomaly detection in movement or physiological data); 3) Contact Tracing (e.g., AI algorithms to process proximity and movement data); 4) COVID-19 Outbreak Forecasting (e.g., machine learning models for predictive epidemiology); and 5) SARS-CoV-2 Mutation Tracking (e.g., AI for analyzing genomic or epidemiological patterns of viral variants).

Key Points

  • The COVID-19 pandemic necessitates the implementation of a long-term system leveraging non-pharmaceutical interventions (NPIs) for infectious disease control.
  • IoT technology is identified as a preferred solution for this purpose due to its ubiquitous sensing ability and seamless connectivity, enabling smart healthcare, home, and city applications.
  • The paper demonstrates a conceptual fog-cloud combined IoT platform designed for systematic and intelligent COVID-19 prevention and control.
  • The proposed platform integrates five key interventions: COVID-19 Symptom Diagnosis, Quarantine Monitoring, Contact Tracing & Social Distancing, COVID-19 Outbreak Forecasting, and SARS-CoV-2 Mutation Tracking.
  • A comprehensive review of state-of-the-art literature for each of these five interventions is conducted to showcase IoT's capabilities in combating current and future epidemics.
  • The overarching goal is to present IoT's potential to counteract the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic and prepare for future infectious disease outbreaks.

Methodology

The paper presents a comprehensive literature review (survey) to investigate and analyze state-of-the-art advancements in IoT technology relevant to COVID-19 prevention and control. It then synthesizes this information to propose and demonstrate a conceptual fog-cloud combined IoT platform architecture that integrates five specific intervention areas.

Key Findings

The survey reveals that IoT technology possesses significant capabilities to support a systematic and intelligent approach to infectious disease prevention and control. Specifically, it can effectively facilitate symptom diagnosis, monitor quarantines, enable robust contact tracing and social distancing enforcement, aid in outbreak forecasting, and assist in tracking SARS-CoV-2 mutations, thereby serving as a crucial tool for both current and future epidemics.

Clinical Impact

This proposed IoT platform could significantly enhance public health responses by enabling early and accurate symptom detection, ensuring effective quarantine compliance, streamlining contact tracing efforts, providing predictive insights for outbreak management, and supporting genomic surveillance. These capabilities could lead to reduced disease transmission rates, earlier interventions, decreased strain on healthcare facilities, and more informed public health policy decisions during pandemics.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state any limitations of the proposed platform or the survey itself. As a conceptual framework based on a literature review, practical implementation challenges, data privacy concerns, scalability hurdles, and the need for standardized protocols are not discussed within the abstract.

Future Directions

The paper implicitly suggests the future direction of developing and implementing such a long-term, systematic, and intelligent fog-cloud combined IoT platform. This includes further research into the practical deployment, interoperability, data security, and ethical considerations of these IoT-based interventions, especially for addressing future infectious disease epidemics.

Abstract

As a result of the worldwide transmission of severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2), coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) has evolved into an unprecedented pandemic. Currently, with unavailable pharmaceutical treatments and vaccines, this novel coronavirus results in a great impact on public health, human society, and global economy, which is likely to last for many years. One of the lessons learned from the COVID-19 pandemic is that a long-term system with non-pharmaceutical interventions for preventing and controlling new infectious diseases is desirable to be implemented. Internet of things (IoT) platform is preferred to be utilized to achieve this goal, due to its ubiquitous sensing ability and seamless connectivity. IoT technology is changing our lives through smart healthcare, smart home, and smart city, which aims to build a more convenient and intelligent community. This paper presents how the IoT could be incorporated into the epidemic prevention and control system. Specifically, we demonstrate a potential fog-cloud combined IoT platform that can be used in the systematic and intelligent COVID-19 prevention and control, which involves five interventions including COVID-19 Symptom Diagnosis, Quarantine Monitoring, Contact Tracing & Social Distancing, COVID-19 Outbreak Forecasting, and SARS-CoV-2 Mutation Tracking. We investigate and review the state-of-the-art literatures of these five interventions to present the capabilities of IoT in countering against the current COVID-19 pandemic or future infectious disease epidemics.
