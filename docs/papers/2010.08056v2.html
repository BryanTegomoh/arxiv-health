IoT Platform for COVID-19 Prevention and Control: A Survey

Published: 2020-10-15
Authors: Yudi Dong, Yu-Dong Yao
Categories: cs.HC, cs.AI, eess.SY
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00

Summary

This survey paper proposes a novel fog-cloud combined Internet of Things (IoT) platform designed for the systematic and intelligent prevention and control of COVID-19, as well as future infectious disease epidemics. It synthesizes state-of-the-art literature to demonstrate how IoT's ubiquitous sensing and connectivity can enable five critical non-pharmaceutical interventions, including symptom diagnosis, quarantine monitoring, contact tracing, outbreak forecasting, and SARS-CoV-2 mutation tracking.

Medical Relevance

This paper offers a critical technological framework for public health by proposing an integrated IoT system to implement crucial non-pharmaceutical interventions, vital for managing infectious disease pandemics in the absence of or as a complement to pharmaceutical treatments. It provides a structured approach for real-time monitoring, early intervention, and data-driven decision-making in epidemic control.

AI Health Application

The paper describes an 'intelligent' IoT platform for COVID-19 prevention and control. Given the 'cs.AI' category and mentions of 'COVID-19 Symptom Diagnosis' and 'COVID-19 Outbreak Forecasting', AI is directly applicable for tasks such as automated symptom analysis, predictive modeling for outbreak detection and spread, and potentially optimizing contact tracing algorithms or resource allocation within the IoT framework to enhance health interventions.

Key Points

  • The COVID-19 pandemic underscores the critical need for a long-term system leveraging non-pharmaceutical interventions for infectious disease prevention and control.
  • IoT technology is identified as an ideal enabler for such a system due to its ubiquitous sensing capabilities and seamless connectivity.
  • A conceptual fog-cloud combined IoT platform is proposed, integrating distributed processing at the edge (fog) with centralized cloud resources.
  • The platform is designed to support five core interventions: COVID-19 Symptom Diagnosis, Quarantine Monitoring, Contact Tracing & Social Distancing, COVID-19 Outbreak Forecasting, and SARS-CoV-2 Mutation Tracking.
  • The paper reviews existing literature to validate the feasibility and capabilities of IoT in implementing each of these five interventions.
  • The proposed IoT framework is presented as a valuable tool for managing the current COVID-19 pandemic and for preparedness against future infectious disease outbreaks.

Methodology

The paper employs a survey methodology, investigating and reviewing state-of-the-art literature related to IoT applications within the context of the five proposed COVID-19 prevention and control interventions. It synthesizes existing research to demonstrate the capabilities and a potential platform architecture.

Key Findings

The primary finding is the conceptualization and demonstration of a potential fog-cloud combined IoT platform that can systematically and intelligently manage infectious disease outbreaks like COVID-19. This platform is found capable of integrating and supporting five critical non-pharmaceutical interventions through leveraging existing and emerging IoT capabilities for symptom diagnosis, quarantine monitoring, contact tracing, outbreak forecasting, and viral mutation tracking.

Clinical Impact

The proposed IoT platform can significantly enhance public health and clinical responses by enabling automated, continuous monitoring for early symptom detection, ensuring adherence to quarantine protocols, facilitating rapid and efficient contact tracing, providing real-time data for epidemiological forecasting to optimize resource allocation, and tracking viral mutations crucial for vaccine and treatment efficacy. This integrated approach can lead to more effective and proactive pandemic management.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations of the proposed platform or the survey itself. It frames the 'unavailable pharmaceutical treatments and vaccines' as the contextual challenge motivating the *need* for such a system, rather than a limitation of the paper's approach.

Future Directions

The paper implicitly suggests the implementation and deployment of such a 'long-term system with non-pharmaceutical interventions' as a desirable future direction. It also explicitly highlights the platform's generalizability and applicability to 'future infectious disease epidemics,' indicating ongoing research for adaptability and broader impact.

Abstract

As a result of the worldwide transmission of severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2), coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) has evolved into an unprecedented pandemic. Currently, with unavailable pharmaceutical treatments and vaccines, this novel coronavirus results in a great impact on public health, human society, and global economy, which is likely to last for many years. One of the lessons learned from the COVID-19 pandemic is that a long-term system with non-pharmaceutical interventions for preventing and controlling new infectious diseases is desirable to be implemented. Internet of things (IoT) platform is preferred to be utilized to achieve this goal, due to its ubiquitous sensing ability and seamless connectivity. IoT technology is changing our lives through smart healthcare, smart home, and smart city, which aims to build a more convenient and intelligent community. This paper presents how the IoT could be incorporated into the epidemic prevention and control system. Specifically, we demonstrate a potential fog-cloud combined IoT platform that can be used in the systematic and intelligent COVID-19 prevention and control, which involves five interventions including COVID-19 Symptom Diagnosis, Quarantine Monitoring, Contact Tracing & Social Distancing, COVID-19 Outbreak Forecasting, and SARS-CoV-2 Mutation Tracking. We investigate and review the state-of-the-art literatures of these five interventions to present the capabilities of IoT in countering against the current COVID-19 pandemic or future infectious disease epidemics.
