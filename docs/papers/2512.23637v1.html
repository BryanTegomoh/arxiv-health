A Dataset and Benchmark for Consumer Healthcare Question Summarization

Published: 2025-12-29
Authors: Abhishek Basu, Deepak Gupta, Dina Demner-Fushman, Shweta Yadav

Summary

This paper introduces CHQ-Sum, a novel dataset comprising 1507 domain-expert annotated consumer health questions and their corresponding summaries, to address the challenge of verbose patient queries and the lack of specialized summarization datasets. The dataset, derived from community question answering forums, provides a critical resource for developing and benchmarking efficient Natural Language Processing (NLP) systems for healthcare question summarization, thereby propelling advancements in understanding consumer health information needs.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medical informatics and digital health, as it provides a foundational dataset crucial for developing AI tools that can accurately and efficiently process the often complex, free-text health queries posed by consumers. By enabling better summarization, it improves the capacity for automated systems to understand patient concerns, which is vital for patient engagement, information retrieval, and potentially clinical triage.

AI Health Application

The paper facilitates the development of AI models (specifically natural language summarization systems) designed to process and distill key information from complex consumer health questions. Such systems could be applied in medical chatbots, patient support forums, symptom checkers, or healthcare information retrieval to improve understanding of user needs and provide more accurate assistance.

Key Points

  • Consumers frequently use overly descriptive and peripheral language when asking health questions online, presenting a significant challenge for Natural Language Understanding (NLU) systems.
  • Summarizing these complex consumer health questions is essential to distill key information and overcome the inherent NLU difficulties.
  • A major impediment to developing effective summarization systems for this domain has been the absence of domain-expert annotated datasets.
  • The authors introduce CHQ-Sum, a new dataset containing 1507 consumer health questions, each paired with a summary meticulously annotated by domain experts.
  • The CHQ-Sum dataset is sourced from community question answering (CQA) forums, making it a valuable resource for analyzing real-world health-related posts on social media.
  • The dataset serves as a benchmark, having been tested against multiple state-of-the-art summarization models to demonstrate its effectiveness.
  • The primary goal is to facilitate the development of more efficient and accurate summarization systems specifically tailored for consumer healthcare questions.

Methodology

The study involved creating a novel dataset, CHQ-Sum, by collecting 1507 consumer health questions from community question answering forums. These raw questions were then professionally annotated by domain experts to produce concise, key-information-rich summaries. The efficacy and utility of this new dataset were subsequently demonstrated by using it as a benchmark to evaluate the performance of several state-of-the-art summarization models.

Key Findings

The main finding is the successful creation and introduction of CHQ-Sum, a pioneering domain-expert annotated dataset explicitly designed for the challenging task of consumer healthcare question summarization. The paper establishes CHQ-Sum as a vital benchmark resource, demonstrating its capacity to evaluate and drive the development of advanced summarization systems tailored for complex health-related queries.

Clinical Impact

Enhanced summarization systems, developed and refined using datasets like CHQ-Sum, hold significant clinical impact by enabling healthcare providers and AI-powered health applications to rapidly comprehend the core issues within lengthy patient inquiries. This can streamline telehealth consultations, improve the accuracy of symptom checkers, aid in patient triage, and ensure patients receive more targeted and relevant medical information, ultimately improving efficiency and patient outcomes in various digital health contexts.

Limitations

It also doesn't discuss potential biases inherent in sourcing data exclusively from community Q&A forums, nor does it elaborate on the specifics of the domain expertise involved in the annotation process or the spectrum of medical conditions covered within the dataset.

Future Directions

While not explicitly stated, the introduction of this benchmark dataset strongly implies future research will focus on developing and evaluating new, more sophisticated summarization models specifically optimized for consumer health questions using CHQ-Sum. Further work could also explore the generalizability of these models across diverse health information platforms, languages, and specific medical specialties.

Abstract

The quest for seeking health information has swamped the web with consumers health-related questions. Generally, consumers use overly descriptive and peripheral information to express their medical condition or other healthcare needs, contributing to the challenges of natural language understanding. One way to address this challenge is to summarize the questions and distill the key information of the original question. Recently, large-scale datasets have significantly propelled the development of several summarization tasks, such as multi-document summarization and dialogue summarization. However, a lack of a domain-expert annotated dataset for the consumer healthcare questions summarization task inhibits the development of an efficient summarization system. To address this issue, we introduce a new dataset, CHQ-Sum,m that contains 1507 domain-expert annotated consumer health questions and corresponding summaries. The dataset is derived from the community question answering forum and therefore provides a valuable resource for understanding consumer health-related posts on social media. We benchmark the dataset on multiple state-of-the-art summarization models to show the effectiveness of the dataset
