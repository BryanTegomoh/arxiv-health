A Dataset and Benchmark for Consumer Healthcare Question Summarization

Published: 2025-12-29
Authors: Abhishek Basu, Deepak Gupta, Dina Demner-Fushman, Shweta Yadav

Summary

This paper introduces CHQ-Sum, a novel dataset comprising 1507 domain-expert annotated consumer health questions and their corresponding summaries, to address the challenge of overly descriptive language in consumer health queries. The dataset aims to facilitate the development of efficient summarization systems for consumer healthcare questions, thereby improving natural language understanding in this domain. The authors benchmarked the CHQ-Sum dataset on multiple state-of-the-art summarization models to demonstrate its utility.

Medical Relevance

This work is crucial for improving how healthcare systems and providers understand and process patient-generated health information, especially from digital sources. By enabling better summarization, it can streamline communication, enhance information retrieval, and support more efficient triage or self-help resources.

AI Health Application

The paper facilitates the development of AI-powered Natural Language Processing (NLP) systems, specifically summarization models, to extract key information from unstructured consumer health questions. This can improve the efficiency and accuracy of understanding patient queries, online health forum posts, and potentially assist healthcare providers or automated health information systems in processing large volumes of consumer health data.

Key Points

  • Consumers often use overly descriptive and peripheral information in healthcare questions, posing a significant challenge for natural language understanding (NLU) systems.
  • Summarizing these questions can distill key information, making them more manageable for NLU processing.
  • A critical barrier to developing effective healthcare question summarization systems has been the lack of domain-expert annotated datasets.
  • The paper introduces CHQ-Sum, a new dataset containing 1507 consumer health questions and their expert-annotated summaries.
  • The dataset is derived from a community question answering (CQA) forum, making it a valuable resource for understanding social media health posts.
  • CHQ-Sum was benchmarked against several state-of-the-art summarization models to validate its effectiveness and utility.
  • This dataset provides a foundational resource for researchers to develop and evaluate advanced models for consumer healthcare question summarization.

Methodology

The authors created CHQ-Sum by collecting consumer health questions from community question answering forums. These questions were then subjected to domain-expert annotation to generate high-quality corresponding summaries. The resulting dataset, containing 1507 question-summary pairs, was subsequently used to benchmark the performance of multiple state-of-the-art summarization models, demonstrating the dataset's applicability and effectiveness.

Key Findings

The primary finding is the successful creation and validation of CHQ-Sum, a domain-expert annotated dataset of 1507 consumer health questions and summaries. The benchmarking process confirmed that this dataset is effective and valuable for developing and evaluating summarization models specifically tailored for the consumer healthcare domain, addressing a critical gap in available resources.

Clinical Impact

This research has significant potential to improve the efficiency and accuracy of understanding consumer health queries. Clinically, it could lead to better automated triage systems, more precise symptom checkers, enhanced search capabilities for patient information, and more effective communication tools for telehealth or patient portals, ultimately facilitating better patient care and resource allocation.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations regarding the dataset creation or benchmarking process itself. It primarily highlights the problem of lacking such datasets as a past limitation that this work addresses.

Future Directions

While not explicitly stated in the abstract, the creation of this benchmark dataset inherently paves the way for future research into developing more robust and accurate summarization algorithms for consumer healthcare questions. This includes exploring novel NLP techniques, fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) on CHQ-Sum, and integrating these summarization capabilities into real-world clinical and consumer-facing applications.

Abstract

The quest for seeking health information has swamped the web with consumers health-related questions. Generally, consumers use overly descriptive and peripheral information to express their medical condition or other healthcare needs, contributing to the challenges of natural language understanding. One way to address this challenge is to summarize the questions and distill the key information of the original question. Recently, large-scale datasets have significantly propelled the development of several summarization tasks, such as multi-document summarization and dialogue summarization. However, a lack of a domain-expert annotated dataset for the consumer healthcare questions summarization task inhibits the development of an efficient summarization system. To address this issue, we introduce a new dataset, CHQ-Sum,m that contains 1507 domain-expert annotated consumer health questions and corresponding summaries. The dataset is derived from the community question answering forum and therefore provides a valuable resource for understanding consumer health-related posts on social media. We benchmark the dataset on multiple state-of-the-art summarization models to show the effectiveness of the dataset
