Summary

This paper introduces RefTr, a novel 3D image-to-graph model that generates vascular tree centerlines by recurrently refining 'confluent trajectories'. RefTr achieves superior recall and comparable precision to existing state-of-the-art methods while significantly reducing model parameters and inference time, promising more accurate and efficient analysis of complex anatomical structures in medical imaging.

Medical Relevance

Accurate detection of centerlines with correct tree topology in tubular structures like blood vessels and airways is paramount for precise diagnosis, effective treatment planning, and safe surgical navigation, directly impacting patient outcomes and avoiding life-threatening errors.

AI Health Application

This research develops an AI-driven 3D image-to-graph model (RefTr) for the automated and accurate detection of centerlines and topology of tubular structures (like blood vessels and lung airways) from medical images. This AI application aims to improve the precision and recall of anatomical structure identification, thereby enhancing clinical tasks such as early disease diagnosis, more effective treatment planning, and safer surgical navigation by providing a comprehensive and accurate understanding of the vascular or airway tree.

  • It employs a Producer-Refiner architecture based on a Transformer decoder, where the Producer generates initial trajectories, and the Refiner iteratively refines them.
  • The model utilizes a 'confluent trajectory' representation, which allows for the refinement of entire trajectories while explicitly enforcing a valid tree topology.
  • A recurrent refinement scheme is implemented, improving precision and significantly reducing decoder parameters (2.4x) compared to previous SOTA by reusing the same Refiner block.
  • An efficient non-maximum suppression (NMS) algorithm specifically designed for spatial tree graphs is introduced to merge duplicate branches and enhance precision.
  • RefTr demonstrates superior recall and comparable precision against multiple public centerline datasets, while also offering faster inference and substantially fewer parameters.
  • Maintaining high recall is emphasized as crucial to avoid fatal clinical mistakes caused by missed small branches or undetected abnormalities.

Methodology

RefTr is a 3D image-to-graph model using a Producer-Refiner architecture with a Transformer decoder. The Producer proposes initial 'confluent trajectories', which are then recurrently refined by a shared Refiner block. This recurrent refinement improves precision and ensures a valid tree topology. The model also incorporates an efficient non-maximum suppression (NMS) algorithm tailored for spatial tree graphs to handle duplicate branches.

Abstract

Tubular trees, such as blood vessels and lung airways, are essential for material transport within the human body. Accurately detecting their centerlines with correct tree topology is critical for clinical tasks such as diagnosis, treatment planning, and surgical navigation. In these applications, maintaining high recall is crucial, as missing small branches can result in fatal mistakes caused by incomplete assessments or undetected abnormalities. We present RefTr, a 3D image-to-graph model for centerline generation of vascular trees via recurrent refinement of confluent trajectories. RefTr uses a Producer-Refiner architecture based on a Transformer decoder, where the Producer proposes a set of initial confluent trajectories that are recurrently refined by the Refiner to produce final trajectories, which forms the centerline graph. The confluent trajectory representation enables refinement of complete trajectories while explicitly enforcing a valid tree topology. The recurrent refinement scheme improves precision and reuses the same Refiner block across multiple steps, yielding a 2.4x reduction in decoder parameters compared to previous SOTA. We also introduce an efficient non-maximum suppression algorithm for spatial tree graphs to merge duplicate branches and boost precision. Across multiple public centerline datasets, RefTr achieves superior recall and comparable precision to previous SOTA, while offering faster inference and substantially fewer parameters, demonstrating its potential as a new state-of-the-art framework for vascular tree analysis in 3D medical imaging.
