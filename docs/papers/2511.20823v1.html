Summary

RefTr is a novel 3D image-to-graph model that generates vascular tree centerlines using recurrent refinement of confluent trajectories, a method explicitly enforcing valid tree topology. This Producer-Refiner architecture, built on a Transformer decoder, achieves superior recall and comparable precision with significantly fewer parameters and faster inference, demonstrating its potential as a new state-of-the-art for critical clinical applications.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and complete detection of vascular and airway centerlines, including small branches, is critical for precise diagnosis of diseases, meticulous planning of treatments, and safe navigation during surgical procedures, as missing small anomalies can lead to severe clinical errors.

AI Health Application

RefTr is an AI model (a 3D image-to-graph model based on a Transformer decoder) designed to automatically generate accurate centerline graphs of tubular structures like blood vessels and lung airways from 3D medical images. This directly supports critical healthcare functions by improving the precision and recall of anatomical structure detection, which is vital for clinical diagnosis, surgical planning and navigation, and treatment assessment, ultimately reducing the risk of medical errors.

Key Points

  • **RefTr Model:** A 3D image-to-graph model specifically designed for automatically generating accurate centerline graphs of tubular trees like blood vessels and lung airways from medical images.
  • **Producer-Refiner Architecture:** Employs a Transformer decoder-based architecture where a 'Producer' component proposes initial confluent trajectories, which are then iteratively refined by a 'Refiner' block.
  • **Confluent Trajectory Representation:** Utilizes a unique representation that allows for the refinement of complete trajectories while explicitly enforcing a valid tree topology, which is crucial for biological accuracy.
  • **Recurrent Refinement Scheme:** Improves precision over multiple steps and enhances computational efficiency by reusing the same Refiner block, leading to a 2.4x reduction in decoder parameters compared to previous SOTA.
  • **Efficient Non-Maximum Suppression (NMS):** Introduces a novel NMS algorithm tailored for spatial tree graphs, designed to merge duplicate branches and further boost overall precision.
  • **Superior Performance:** Achieves superior recall and comparable precision to previous state-of-the-art methods across multiple public centerline datasets.
  • **Resource Efficiency:** Offers substantially faster inference times and significantly fewer model parameters, making it more practical for real-world clinical deployment and reducing computational overhead.

Methodology

RefTr utilizes a Producer-Refiner architecture based on a Transformer decoder. The Producer initially proposes a set of 'confluent trajectories' from 3D medical images. These trajectories, which encode initial vessel paths and their topology, are then iteratively refined by the Refiner block. This recurrent refinement scheme enhances precision and allows for significant parameter sharing. An efficient non-maximum suppression (NMS) algorithm specifically designed for spatial tree graphs is then applied to merge any duplicate branches and further optimize precision, ultimately generating a comprehensive centerline graph.

Key Findings

RefTr significantly outperforms previous state-of-the-art methods by achieving superior recall while maintaining comparable precision across multiple public centerline datasets. Importantly, it also offers faster inference speeds and requires substantially fewer parameters (2.4x reduction in decoder parameters), indicating higher computational efficiency.

Clinical Impact

By providing highly accurate, complete, and topologically correct centerline graphs of vascular and airway trees, RefTr can significantly enhance the precision of clinical tasks. Its superior recall minimizes the risk of overlooking critical small branches or subtle abnormalities, thereby preventing potentially fatal diagnostic errors, improving the safety and efficacy of treatment planning, and enabling more accurate surgical navigation.

Abstract

Tubular trees, such as blood vessels and lung airways, are essential for material transport within the human body. Accurately detecting their centerlines with correct tree topology is critical for clinical tasks such as diagnosis, treatment planning, and surgical navigation. In these applications, maintaining high recall is crucial, as missing small branches can result in fatal mistakes caused by incomplete assessments or undetected abnormalities. We present RefTr, a 3D image-to-graph model for centerline generation of vascular trees via recurrent refinement of confluent trajectories. RefTr uses a Producer-Refiner architecture based on a Transformer decoder, where the Producer proposes a set of initial confluent trajectories that are recurrently refined by the Refiner to produce final trajectories, which forms the centerline graph. The confluent trajectory representation enables refinement of complete trajectories while explicitly enforcing a valid tree topology. The recurrent refinement scheme improves precision and reuses the same Refiner block across multiple steps, yielding a 2.4x reduction in decoder parameters compared to previous SOTA. We also introduce an efficient non-maximum suppression algorithm for spatial tree graphs to merge duplicate branches and boost precision. Across multiple public centerline datasets, RefTr achieves superior recall and comparable precision to previous SOTA, while offering faster inference and substantially fewer parameters, demonstrating its potential as a new state-of-the-art framework for vascular tree analysis in 3D medical imaging.
