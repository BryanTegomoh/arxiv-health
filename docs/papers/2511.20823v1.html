Summary

RefTr is a novel 3D image-to-graph model for generating centerlines of vascular and tubular trees by recurrently refining confluent trajectories using a Producer-Refiner Transformer architecture. It achieves superior recall and comparable precision to previous state-of-the-art methods, while significantly reducing model parameters and inference time. This approach is crucial for clinical applications where accurate detection of all branches, even small ones, is vital to prevent misdiagnosis or incomplete assessments.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and high-recall detection of vascular and airway centerlines is paramount for clinical tasks such as precise diagnosis of diseases (e.g., vascular abnormalities, lung pathology), planning complex surgical interventions, and guiding treatment delivery, where missing small branches can lead to critical errors or incomplete patient care.

AI Health Application

The AI model (RefTr) uses deep learning (Transformer decoder architecture) to automatically generate highly accurate 3D centerline graphs of vascular trees and lung airways from medical images. This application is crucial for aiding clinicians in automated diagnosis of pathologies, precise treatment planning (e.g., stent placement, tumor resection), and real-time surgical navigation, thereby improving patient outcomes and reducing errors in clinical workflows.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical need for accurate 3D centerline detection in tubular trees (e.g., blood vessels, lung airways) with correct topological structure, emphasizing high recall to avoid fatal clinical mistakes.
  • Introduces RefTr, a 3D image-to-graph model based on a Producer-Refiner architecture with a Transformer decoder, designed to generate centerline graphs from medical images.
  • Utilizes a novel "confluent trajectory" representation that enables the refinement of complete trajectories while explicitly enforcing a valid tree topology, crucial for anatomical accuracy.
  • Implements a recurrent refinement scheme that enhances precision and efficiently reuses the same Refiner block across multiple steps, leading to a significant 2.4x reduction in decoder parameters compared to prior state-of-the-art.
  • Incorporates an efficient non-maximum suppression (NMS) algorithm specifically adapted for spatial tree graphs to merge redundant branches and further boost precision.
  • Achieves superior recall performance and comparable precision against previous state-of-the-art models across multiple public centerline datasets.
  • Demonstrates practical advantages including faster inference times and substantially fewer parameters, positioning it as an efficient and high-performing framework for vascular tree analysis.

Methodology

RefTr employs a 3D image-to-graph model with a Producer-Refiner architecture built upon a Transformer decoder. The Producer generates an initial set of confluent trajectories, which are then recurrently refined by the Refiner block over multiple steps. This process leverages a confluent trajectory representation that allows for complete trajectory refinement while explicitly enforcing correct tree topology. Additionally, an efficient non-maximum suppression algorithm designed for spatial tree graphs is applied to merge duplicate branches and enhance precision.

Key Findings

RefTr achieved superior recall and comparable precision when evaluated against previous state-of-the-art methods across various public centerline datasets. It also demonstrated significant efficiency improvements, including faster inference and a 2.4x reduction in decoder parameters, showcasing its potential as a new benchmark for vascular tree analysis in medical imaging.

Clinical Impact

The enhanced recall offered by RefTr is critically important for clinical accuracy, ensuring that even small, pathologically significant branches are detected. This capability can lead to more thorough diagnostic assessments, improved surgical planning (e.g., pre-operative mapping for stent placement or tumor resection), and safer navigation during minimally invasive procedures, ultimately contributing to better patient outcomes and reduced medical errors. The efficiency gains also facilitate its integration into clinical imaging workflows.

Abstract

Tubular trees, such as blood vessels and lung airways, are essential for material transport within the human body. Accurately detecting their centerlines with correct tree topology is critical for clinical tasks such as diagnosis, treatment planning, and surgical navigation. In these applications, maintaining high recall is crucial, as missing small branches can result in fatal mistakes caused by incomplete assessments or undetected abnormalities. We present RefTr, a 3D image-to-graph model for centerline generation of vascular trees via recurrent refinement of confluent trajectories. RefTr uses a Producer-Refiner architecture based on a Transformer decoder, where the Producer proposes a set of initial confluent trajectories that are recurrently refined by the Refiner to produce final trajectories, which forms the centerline graph. The confluent trajectory representation enables refinement of complete trajectories while explicitly enforcing a valid tree topology. The recurrent refinement scheme improves precision and reuses the same Refiner block across multiple steps, yielding a 2.4x reduction in decoder parameters compared to previous SOTA. We also introduce an efficient non-maximum suppression algorithm for spatial tree graphs to merge duplicate branches and boost precision. Across multiple public centerline datasets, RefTr achieves superior recall and comparable precision to previous SOTA, while offering faster inference and substantially fewer parameters, demonstrating its potential as a new state-of-the-art framework for vascular tree analysis in 3D medical imaging.
