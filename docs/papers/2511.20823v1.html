Summary

This paper introduces RefTr, a 3D image-to-graph model designed for generating vascular tree centerlines through recurrent refinement of confluent trajectories. RefTr leverages a Transformer-based Producer-Refiner architecture and a novel non-maximum suppression, achieving superior recall, comparable precision, faster inference, and significantly reduced parameters compared to previous state-of-the-art methods.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and high-recall detection of vascular and airway centerlines is paramount in medicine for precise diagnosis, effective treatment planning (e.g., stent placement, tumor ablation), and safe surgical navigation, as missing even small branches can lead to incomplete assessments and severe clinical errors.

AI Health Application

The paper proposes RefTr, an AI-powered 3D image-to-graph model (based on a Transformer decoder) for the automated and accurate generation of centerline graphs of vascular trees from 3D medical images. This AI application aims to significantly improve clinical workflows for diagnosis, treatment planning, and surgical navigation by providing precise anatomical representations of blood vessels and airways, thus aiding clinicians in identifying abnormalities and guiding interventions.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical need for accurate 3D centerline detection and correct topological representation of tubular trees (e.g., blood vessels, lung airways) for clinical applications like diagnosis and surgical navigation, emphasizing high recall to prevent fatal mistakes.
  • Presents RefTr, a 3D image-to-graph model that employs a Producer-Refiner architecture built upon a Transformer decoder for centerline generation.
  • Utilizes a 'confluent trajectory' representation, enabling the refinement of complete trajectories while inherently enforcing a valid tree topology, which is crucial for biological structures.
  • Implements a recurrent refinement scheme where the Refiner block is reused across multiple steps, enhancing precision and yielding a 2.4x reduction in decoder parameters compared to prior state-of-the-art (SOTA) approaches.
  • Introduces an efficient non-maximum suppression (NMS) algorithm specifically tailored for spatial tree graphs to effectively merge duplicate branches and further boost precision.
  • Achieves superior recall and comparable precision across multiple public centerline datasets, outperforming previous SOTA in recall while maintaining high precision.

Methodology

RefTr is a 3D image-to-graph model that takes medical images as input and outputs a centerline graph. It features a Producer-Refiner architecture, where a Transformer decoder-based Producer generates initial 'confluent trajectories.' These initial trajectories are then iteratively refined by a recurrent Refiner block, which reuses its parameters across refinement steps to form the final centerline graph. The 'confluent trajectory' representation inherently enforces a valid tree topology. Additionally, the methodology includes a novel and efficient non-maximum suppression (NMS) algorithm specifically designed for spatial tree graphs to resolve duplicate branches and enhance output precision.

Key Findings

RefTr demonstrated superior recall and comparable precision on multiple public centerline datasets compared to previous state-of-the-art methods. It also achieved significant efficiency gains, including faster inference speeds and a 2.4x reduction in decoder parameters, indicating a more resource-efficient solution without sacrificing performance.

Clinical Impact

The enhanced recall of RefTr minimizes the risk of overlooking small yet clinically significant anatomical structures or abnormalities, leading to more comprehensive diagnoses and reduced potential for medical errors. Its accurate topological reconstruction, coupled with faster inference and reduced computational demands, can streamline clinical workflows for tasks such as surgical planning, pre-operative assessment, and intra-operative guidance, potentially improving patient outcomes and resource utilization.

Abstract

Tubular trees, such as blood vessels and lung airways, are essential for material transport within the human body. Accurately detecting their centerlines with correct tree topology is critical for clinical tasks such as diagnosis, treatment planning, and surgical navigation. In these applications, maintaining high recall is crucial, as missing small branches can result in fatal mistakes caused by incomplete assessments or undetected abnormalities. We present RefTr, a 3D image-to-graph model for centerline generation of vascular trees via recurrent refinement of confluent trajectories. RefTr uses a Producer-Refiner architecture based on a Transformer decoder, where the Producer proposes a set of initial confluent trajectories that are recurrently refined by the Refiner to produce final trajectories, which forms the centerline graph. The confluent trajectory representation enables refinement of complete trajectories while explicitly enforcing a valid tree topology. The recurrent refinement scheme improves precision and reuses the same Refiner block across multiple steps, yielding a 2.4x reduction in decoder parameters compared to previous SOTA. We also introduce an efficient non-maximum suppression algorithm for spatial tree graphs to merge duplicate branches and boost precision. Across multiple public centerline datasets, RefTr achieves superior recall and comparable precision to previous SOTA, while offering faster inference and substantially fewer parameters, demonstrating its potential as a new state-of-the-art framework for vascular tree analysis in 3D medical imaging.
