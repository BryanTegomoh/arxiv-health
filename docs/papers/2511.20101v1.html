Multi Head Attention Enhanced Inception v3 for Cardiomegaly Detection

arXiv ID: 2511.20101v1
Published: 2025-11-25
Authors: Abishek Karthik, Pandiyaraju V
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper presents an integrated deep learning approach for the automatic detection of cardiomegaly from X-ray images, leveraging a Multi-Head Attention enhanced Inception v3 model. The proposed system achieves high performance metrics, demonstrating its potential for sensitive and accurate diagnosis of this structural abnormality in the healthcare industry. By selectively focusing on critical regions of input images, the model enhances feature representation and diagnostic capability.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine as it offers an automated, sensitive, and accurate tool for detecting cardiomegaly from common chest X-ray images, a key indicator for various cardiovascular diseases. Such a tool can assist clinicians in early diagnosis and management, potentially improving patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of a deep learning model (Multi-Head Attention Enhanced Inception V3) for the automatic and sensitive detection and diagnosis of cardiomegaly from X-ray images, aiming to aid in the diagnosis of cardiovascular diseases.

Key Points

  • The project addresses the automated detection of cardiomegaly using X-ray images, a critical diagnostic task in cardiovascular health.
  • It proposes a deep learning system based on a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) architecture, specifically utilizing the Inception v3 model as a foundational block.
  • A key enhancement is the incorporation of a multi-head attention mechanism, which allows the model to automatically learn features and selectively focus on specific, relevant regions of the input X-ray images.
  • The methodology includes rigorous data collection of annotated X-ray images, a preprocessing module for quality fine-tuning, and a strict evaluation phase.
  • The model demonstrated high diagnostic performance with an accuracy of 95.6%, precision of 95.2%, recall of 96.2%, sensitivity of 95.7%, and specificity of 96.1%.
  • An Area Under Curve (AUC) of 96.0 further validates the model's strong discriminatory power.
  • The selective focusing capability of the multi-head attention mechanism is highlighted as crucial for sensitive identification of cardiomegaly by enhancing the representation of main data.

Methodology

The methodology involves a data collection phase of annotated X-ray images, followed by a preprocessing module to enhance image quality. The core of the proposed system is a CNN configured with Inception v3 as a primary block. This is further enhanced by a multilayer multi-head attention mechanism designed to automatically learn features and selectively focus on relevant regions of the input images. The model's performance is then strictly evaluated using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, sensitivity, specificity, and AUC.

Key Findings

The multi-head attention enhanced Inception v3 model achieved an accuracy of 95.6%, precision of 95.2%, recall of 96.2%, sensitivity of 95.7%, specificity of 96.1%, and an Area Under Curve (AUC) of 96.0 for cardiomegaly detection. The attention mechanism effectively enabled selective focusing on relevant input regions, contributing to the model's sensitive diagnostic capability.

Clinical Impact

This method has significant clinical impact by providing an automated, highly accurate, and sensitive system for cardiomegaly detection from X-ray images. It could serve as a valuable diagnostic aid for radiologists and cardiologists, potentially reducing inter-observer variability, speeding up diagnosis, and facilitating earlier intervention for patients with cardiovascular conditions.

Abstract

The healthcare industry has been revolutionized significantly by novel imaging technologies, not just in the diagnosis of cardiovascular diseases but also by the visualization of structural abnormalities like cardiomegaly. This article explains an integrated approach to the use of deep learning tools and attention mechanisms for automatic detection of cardiomegaly using X-ray images. The initiation of the project is grounded on a strong Data Collection phase and gathering the data of annotated X-ray images of various types. Then, while the Preprocessing module fine-tunes image quality, it is feasible to utilize the best out of the data quality in the proposed system. In our proposed system, the process is a CNN configuration leveraging the inception V3 model as one of the key blocks. Besides, we also employ a multilayer attention mechanism to enhance the strength. The most important feature of the method is the multi-head attention mechanism that can learn features automatically. By exact selective focusing on only some regions of input, the model can thus identify cardiomegaly in a sensitive manner. Attention rating is calculated, duplicated, and applied to enhance representation of main data, and therefore there is a successful diagnosis. The Evaluation stage will be extremely strict and it will thoroughly evaluate the model based on such measures as accuracy and precision. This will validate that the model can identify cardiomegaly and will also show the clinical significance of this method. The model has accuracy of 95.6, precision of 95.2, recall of 96.2, sensitivity of 95.7, specificity of 96.1 and an Area Under Curve(AUC) of 96.0 and their respective graphs are plotted for visualisation.
