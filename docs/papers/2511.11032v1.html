MPCGNet: A Multiscale Feature Extraction and Progressive Feature Aggregation Network Using Coupling Gates for Polyp Segmentation

Published: 2025-11-14
Authors: Wei Wang, Feng Jiang, Xin Wang
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00

Summary

MPCGNet addresses key challenges in automatic polyp segmentation, including missed small polyps, ambiguous boundaries, and image noise, through a novel deep learning architecture. It employs "coupling gates" within specialized modules for effective multiscale feature extraction, noise suppression, and progressive feature aggregation, achieving superior performance on benchmark datasets. This improved accuracy is crucial for enhancing colorectal cancer screening and diagnosis.

Medical Relevance

Improving the accuracy of automatic polyp segmentation is vital for assisting doctors in early and precise detection during colorectal cancer screening, which directly impacts patient outcomes through timely diagnosis and intervention.

AI Health Application

The AI application is an automated system (MPCGNet) designed for precise segmentation of polyps in colonoscopy images. This system aims to improve the accuracy and efficiency of colorectal polyp screening and early cancer diagnosis by identifying small polyps, clarifying boundaries, and filtering noise, thereby assisting doctors in making more informed decisions.

Key Points

  • Addresses three major challenges in automatic polyp segmentation: small-sized polyp detection, ambiguous boundaries with surrounding tissue, and noise from uneven lighting in colonoscopy images.
  • Introduces "coupling gates" as a core component for filtering noise and performing feature importance selection within the network.
  • Proposes the Coupling Gates Multiscale Feature Extraction (CGMFE) module to effectively extract local features and suppress noise.
  • Develops the Windows Cross Attention Decoder (WCAD) module for restoring fine details and precisely localizing polyps.
  • Implements the Decoder Feature Aggregation (DFA) module, which progressively aggregates features, further extracts them, and performs feature importance selection to mitigate the loss of small-sized polyps.
  • Outperformed recent state-of-the-art networks, achieving a 2.20% higher mDice score than the second-best network on the ETIS-LaribPolypDB dataset.
  • Demonstrated a 0.68% higher mDice score compared to the second-best network on the CVC-ColonDB dataset.

Methodology

The authors propose MPCGNet, a deep learning architecture incorporating novel modules designed for robust polyp segmentation. The method utilizes "coupling gates" to selectively filter noise and prioritize important features. Key architectural components include the Coupling Gates Multiscale Feature Extraction (CGMFE) module for local feature extraction across scales and noise suppression, the Windows Cross Attention Decoder (WCAD) module for detail restoration and precise localization via cross-attention mechanisms, and the Decoder Feature Aggregation (DFA) module for progressive feature aggregation and importance selection, specifically engineered to reduce small polyp omission. The network's performance was evaluated using mDice scores on the ETIS-LaribPolypDB and CVC-ColonDB datasets.

Key Findings

MPCGNet significantly surpasses current state-of-the-art methods in automatic polyp segmentation. It achieved mDice scores 2.20% higher than the second-best network on the ETIS-LaribPolypDB dataset and 0.68% higher on the CVC-ColonDB dataset, demonstrating its superior capability in handling challenging segmentation scenarios like small polyps, ambiguous boundaries, and noisy images.

Clinical Impact

The enhanced accuracy and robustness of MPCGNet in polyp segmentation, particularly its ability to identify small polyps and clarify ambiguous boundaries, holds significant clinical potential. This technology could lead to more reliable automated tools for assisting endoscopists in colorectal cancer screening, potentially improving detection rates, reducing inter-observer variability, and facilitating earlier diagnosis and treatment, ultimately benefiting patient care and outcomes.

Abstract

Automatic segmentation methods of polyps is crucial for assisting doctors in colorectal polyp screening and cancer diagnosis. Despite the progress made by existing methods, polyp segmentation faces several challenges: (1) small-sized polyps are prone to being missed during identification, (2) the boundaries between polyps and the surrounding environment are often ambiguous, (3) noise in colonoscopy images, caused by uneven lighting and other factors, affects segmentation results. To address these challenges, this paper introduces coupling gates as components in specific modules to filter noise and perform feature importance selection. Three modules are proposed: the coupling gates multiscale feature extraction (CGMFE) module, which effectively extracts local features and suppresses noise; the windows cross attention (WCAD) decoder module, which restores details after capturing the precise location of polyps; and the decoder feature aggregation (DFA) module, which progressively aggregates features, further extracts them, and performs feature importance selection to reduce the loss of small-sized polyps. Experimental results demonstrate that MPCGNet outperforms recent networks, with mDice scores 2.20% and 0.68% higher than the second-best network on the ETIS-LaribPolypDB and CVC-ColonDB datasets, respectively.
