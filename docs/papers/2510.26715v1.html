LSM-MS2: A Foundation Model Bridging Spectral Identification and Biological Interpretation

Summary

LSM-MS2 is a novel large-scale deep learning foundation model developed to enhance both spectral identification and biological interpretation of mass spectrometry data. Trained on millions of spectra, it achieves state-of-the-art performance in identifying challenging compounds and generating rich spectral embeddings that directly enable disease differentiation and clinical outcome prediction.

Medical Relevance

This technology significantly advances the utility of mass spectrometry in medical research and diagnostics by enabling more accurate, comprehensive, and interpretable characterization of biological molecules, crucial for biomarker discovery, disease diagnosis, and personalized medicine.

AI Health Application

LSM-MS2 is a deep learning foundation model that analyzes mass spectrometry data from biological samples. Its AI application to health includes enhanced spectral identification for uncovering biological and chemical information, enabling AI-driven differentiation of disease states (diagnostics) and prediction of clinical outcomes (prognostics), and supporting translational research in medicine.

Key Points

  • LSM-MS2 is a deep learning foundation model trained on millions of tandem mass spectrometry spectra to learn a semantic chemical space.
  • It achieves state-of-the-art performance in spectral identification, significantly outperforming existing methods.
  • The model demonstrates a 30% improvement in accuracy for identifying challenging isomeric compounds.
  • LSM-MS2 yields 42% more correct identifications in complex biological samples.
  • It maintains robust performance even under low-concentration conditions, crucial for clinical samples.
  • The model produces rich spectral embeddings that facilitate direct biological interpretation from minimal downstream data.

Abstract

A vast majority of mass spectrometry data remains uncharacterized, leaving much of its biological and chemical information untapped. Recent advances in machine learning have begun to address this gap, particularly for tasks such as spectral identification in tandem mass spectrometry data. Here, we present the latest generation of LSM-MS2, a large-scale deep learning foundation model trained on millions of spectra to learn a semantic chemical space. LSM-MS2 achieves state-of-the-art performance in spectral identification, improving on existing methods by 30% in accuracy of identifying challenging isomeric compounds, yielding 42% more correct identifications in complex biological samples, and maintaining robustness under low-concentration conditions. Furthermore, LSM-MS2 produces rich spectral embeddings that enable direct biological interpretation from minimal downstream data, successfully differentiating disease states and predicting clinical outcomes across diverse translational applications.
