Optimal vaccination program for two infectious diseases with cross immunity

arXiv ID: 2011.14255v1
Published: 2020-11-29
Authors: Yang Ye, Qingpeng Zhang, Zhidong Cao, Daniel Dajun Zeng
Categories: q-bio.PE, physics.soc-ph

Summary

The paper investigates optimal vaccination strategies for two infectious diseases exhibiting cross immunity, competing for resources within the same population. Utilizing a two-layer Susceptible-Infected-Removed (SIR) model, the authors identify three distinct scenarios for optimal vaccination programs aimed at preventing outbreaks of both diseases at minimum cost. A crucial analytical criterion, based on the costs of the respective vaccines, is derived to specify the most effective program.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a critical framework for public health policy makers to allocate scarce vaccination resources effectively when facing co-circulating infectious diseases with immunological cross-talk, ensuring maximum outbreak prevention at minimal economic burden. Abstract

There are often multiple diseases with cross immunity competing for vaccination resources. Here we investigate the optimal vaccination program in a two-layer Susceptible-Infected-Removed (SIR) model, where two diseases with cross immunity spread in the same population, and vaccines for both diseases are available. We identify three scenarios of the optimal vaccination program, which prevents the outbreaks of both diseases at the minimum cost. We analytically derive a criterion to specify the optimal program based on the costs for different vaccines.
