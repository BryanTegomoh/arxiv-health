Optimal vaccination program for two infectious diseases with cross immunity

arXiv ID: 2011.14255v1
Published: 2020-11-29
Authors: Yang Ye, Qingpeng Zhang, Zhidong Cao, Daniel Dajun Zeng
Categories: q-bio.PE, physics.soc-ph
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00

Summary

This paper investigates optimal vaccination programs for two infectious diseases exhibiting cross immunity, using a two-layer Susceptible-Infected-Removed (SIR) model. It identifies three distinct scenarios for an optimal program that effectively prevents outbreaks of both diseases while minimizing vaccination costs. The research analytically derives a specific criterion to determine the optimal program based on the relative costs of the available vaccines.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a framework for public health decision-makers to optimize vaccine resource allocation for co-circulating diseases with immunological interactions, enabling more efficient and cost-effective outbreak prevention strategies in a real-world setting.

Methodology

The study employs a two-layer Susceptible-Infected-Removed (SIR) mathematical model to simulate the spread of two infectious diseases with cross immunity within a population. Analytical methods are used to derive the conditions for optimal vaccination programs and to establish a criterion for selecting among these programs, based on cost parameters.

Key Findings

The research identified three specific scenarios for vaccination programs that optimally prevent outbreaks of both cross-immune diseases. A significant finding is the analytical derivation of a criterion that dictates the choice among these optimal programs, which is directly dependent on the relative costs of the vaccines available for the two diseases.

It offers a practical tool for health authorities to make evidence-based decisions on how to best allocate limited budgets and vaccine supplies to achieve maximal disease prevention, thereby potentially reducing morbidity, mortality, and healthcare burden during epidemics involving cross-immune pathogens (e.g., different influenza strains or related viral families).

Abstract

There are often multiple diseases with cross immunity competing for vaccination resources. Here we investigate the optimal vaccination program in a two-layer Susceptible-Infected-Removed (SIR) model, where two diseases with cross immunity spread in the same population, and vaccines for both diseases are available. We identify three scenarios of the optimal vaccination program, which prevents the outbreaks of both diseases at the minimum cost. We analytically derive a criterion to specify the optimal program based on the costs for different vaccines.
