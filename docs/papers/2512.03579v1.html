Optimal Transportation and Alignment Between Gaussian Measures

Summary

This paper significantly advances the computational tractability of Optimal Transport (OT) and Gromov-Wasserstein (GW) alignment by providing comprehensive closed-form solutions for Gaussian measures under quadratic cost. It addresses previously open problems, offering efficient methods for comparing, transforming, and aggregating heterogeneous datasets, particularly for uncentered Gaussians and their barycenters.

Medical Relevance

By providing computationally efficient frameworks for comparing, transforming, and aggregating heterogeneous datasets modeled as Gaussians, this work enables robust integration and analysis of diverse medical data types (e.g., imaging, genomics, clinical records), which is crucial for personalized medicine, disease phenotyping, and multi-modal biomedical research.

AI Health Application

This research improves foundational AI methods (Optimal Transport, Gromov-Wasserstein alignment) for handling complex, heterogeneous data and optimizing model efficiency. These advancements enable more robust and scalable medical AI applications, such as efficient deployment of diagnostic AI models via knowledge distillation, and sophisticated analysis of diverse patient data for personalized treatment strategies and disease subtyping through heterogeneous clustering.

Key Points

  • Provides novel closed-form expressions for Inner Product Gromov-Wasserstein (IGW) alignment between uncentered Gaussian measures on separable Hilbert spaces, reducing it to a quadratic optimization over unitary operators with tight analytic bounds.
  • Simplifies the IGW solution to a fully closed-form expression when at least one of the Gaussian measures is centered.
  • Extends the framework to an analytic solution for computing the IGW barycenter among multiple centered Gaussian measures, enabling the aggregation of several Gaussian distributions efficiently.
  • Develops a method to reduce Gaussian multimarginal Optimal Transport (OT) with pairwise quadratic costs to a tractable optimization problem, solvable with an efficient algorithm utilizing a rank-deficiency constraint.
  • Closes several theoretical gaps in the literature concerning Gaussian, quadratic cost OT and IGW, thereby enhancing their applicability in various data science and machine learning tasks.
  • Demonstrates the practical utility of these efficient computational methods through applications in knowledge distillation and heterogeneous clustering on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

Methodology

The paper employs advanced mathematical techniques, including the derivation of closed-form analytic expressions for optimal transport and Gromov-Wasserstein objectives between Gaussian measures. It utilizes quadratic optimization over unitary operators, leverages properties of separable Hilbert spaces for infinite-dimensional settings, and reduces complex multimarginal OT problems to tractable optimization via rank-deficiency constraints. The methodology is primarily theoretical and analytical, with computational validation on specific applications.

Key Findings

The central findings include a breakthrough closed-form solution for IGW between uncentered Gaussians (up to a unitary optimization with bounds), a fully closed-form solution for centered-uncentered IGW, and an analytic solution for IGW barycenters of centered Gaussians. Additionally, the paper provides a practical reduction of Gaussian multimarginal OT to an efficiently solvable optimization problem. These results significantly improve the computational feasibility of these complex data alignment methods.

Clinical Impact

This research can streamline the integration and analysis of multi-modal patient data (e.g., combining MRI scans with genetic profiles or clinical notes) by providing efficient tools for data alignment, comparison, and aggregation. This could facilitate more accurate patient stratification, enhance the discovery of disease subtypes through robust heterogeneous clustering, and enable more effective transfer learning (knowledge distillation) from large, general medical datasets to specific clinical applications, ultimately leading to improved diagnostic tools and more targeted therapeutic strategies.

Limitations

The primary limitation is the focus on Gaussian distributions and quadratic cost functions. While providing significant advancements within this specific scope, these methods may not directly apply to data with highly non-Gaussian distributions or scenarios requiring different, specialized cost functions without appropriate data transformations or extensions. Real-world medical data can often exhibit complex, non-Gaussian properties.

Future Directions

While not explicitly stated as future work, the paper's contributions pave the way for broader application and rigorous validation of these efficient OT/GW methods in diverse and large-scale real-world medical datasets. Further research could explore extensions to non-Gaussian distributions, different cost functions, or the development of robust data transformations to better approximate complex biomedical data within the computationally advantageous Gaussian framework.

Abstract

Optimal transport (OT) and Gromov-Wasserstein (GW) alignment provide interpretable geometric frameworks for comparing, transforming, and aggregating heterogeneous datasets -- tasks ubiquitous in data science and machine learning. Because these frameworks are computationally expensive, large-scale applications often rely on closed-form solutions for Gaussian distributions under quadratic cost. This work provides a comprehensive treatment of Gaussian, quadratic cost OT and inner product GW (IGW) alignment, closing several gaps in the literature to broaden applicability. First, we treat the open problem of IGW alignment between uncentered Gaussians on separable Hilbert spaces by giving a closed-form expression up to a quadratic optimization over unitary operators, for which we derive tight analytic upper and lower bounds. If at least one Gaussian measure is centered, the solution reduces to a fully closed-form expression, which we further extend to an analytic solution for the IGW barycenter between centered Gaussians. We also present a reduction of Gaussian multimarginal OT with pairwise quadratic costs to a tractable optimization problem and provide an efficient algorithm to solve it using a rank-deficiency constraint. To demonstrate utility, we apply our results to knowledge distillation and heterogeneous clustering on synthetic and real-world datasets.
