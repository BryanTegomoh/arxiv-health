Progressive self-supervised blind-spot denoising method for LDCT denoising

arXiv ID: 2601.14180v1
Published: 2026-01-20
Authors: Yichao Liu, Yueyang Teng, Junwen Guo
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a novel self-supervised learning strategy for denoising low-dose computed tomography (LDCT) images, addressing the challenge of acquiring paired normal-dose CT (NDCT) data. The method employs a progressive, step-wise blind-spot denoising mechanism and Gaussian noise regularization to achieve fine-grained denoising. Evaluated on the Mayo LDCT dataset, the proposed technique significantly outperforms existing self-supervised approaches and demonstrates performance comparable to, or better than, several supervised denoising methods.

Medical Relevance

Reducing radiation dose in CT scans is crucial for patient safety, especially for vulnerable populations or those requiring repeated scans. This method enables high-quality LDCT imaging without compromising diagnostic accuracy, which is vital for preventing misdiagnosis due to noise-induced artifacts.

AI Health Application

This research applies self-supervised learning, a subfield of Artificial Intelligence (AI), to improve the quality of low-dose computed tomography (LDCT) images. By denoising LDCT images effectively without requiring paired normal-dose CT data (which is difficult to acquire in clinical practice), the AI method enhances the diagnostic utility of CT scans, potentially leading to more accurate diagnoses, reduced radiation exposure for patients, and more efficient clinical workflows in radiology departments.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical problem of LDCT image denoising without relying on difficult-to-acquire paired NDCT data, using an exclusively self-supervised training strategy.
  • Proposes a novel 'step-wise blind-spot denoising mechanism' that enforces conditional independence progressively, enabling more fine-grained and effective denoising learning.
  • Incorporates the addition of Gaussian noise to LDCT images, serving as a regularization technique to mitigate overfitting during the self-supervised training process.
  • Evaluated on the widely recognized Mayo LDCT dataset, demonstrating the method's practical applicability and robustness in a clinically relevant context.
  • Achieves superior denoising performance compared to current state-of-the-art self-supervised learning approaches for LDCT.
  • Demonstrates performance that is comparable to, or even surpasses, several representative supervised denoising methods, despite not utilizing paired NDCT ground truth.
  • The self-supervised nature removes a major barrier to the adoption and development of advanced denoising techniques in clinical settings, making it more scalable and adaptable.

Methodology

The study proposes a novel self-supervised deep learning training strategy exclusively utilizing LDCT images. It introduces a 'step-wise blind-spot denoising mechanism' to progressively enforce conditional independence, allowing for more detailed denoising learning. Additionally, Gaussian noise is injected into the LDCT images during training, acting as a regularization technique to prevent overfitting. The method was evaluated by extensive experiments on the Mayo LDCT dataset.

Key Findings

The proposed self-supervised blind-spot denoising method consistently outperformed existing self-supervised approaches for LDCT denoising. Crucially, it achieved performance that was comparable to, or even better than, several representative supervised denoising methods, demonstrating its effectiveness without the need for paired normal-dose CT data.

Clinical Impact

This method has the potential to significantly improve the diagnostic utility of LDCT scans by effectively removing noise while maintaining crucial image details. This could lead to wider adoption of LDCT protocols, reducing patient exposure to ionizing radiation without sacrificing image quality, ultimately enhancing patient safety and potentially aiding earlier and more accurate diagnoses in clinical practice.

Abstract

Self-supervised learning is increasingly investigated for low-dose computed tomography (LDCT) image denoising, as it alleviates the dependence on paired normal-dose CT (NDCT) data, which are often difficult to acquire in clinical practice. In this paper, we propose a novel self-supervised training strategy that relies exclusively on LDCT images. We introduce a step-wise blind-spot denoising mechanism that enforces conditional independence in a progressive manner, enabling more fine-grained denoising learning. In addition, we add Gaussian noise to LDCT images, which acts as a regularization and mitigates overfitting. Extensive experiments on the Mayo LDCT dataset demonstrate that the proposed method consistently outperforms existing self-supervised approaches and achieves performance comparable to, or better than, several representative supervised denoising methods.
