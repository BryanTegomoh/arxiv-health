Multi-Stage Residual-Aware Unsupervised Deep Learning Framework for Consistent Ultrasound Strain Elastography

Summary

MUSSE-Net is a novel multi-stage, residual-aware unsupervised deep learning framework designed to improve Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE) by robustly and consistently estimating tissue strain. It addresses key limitations like decorrelation noise, lack of ground truth, and inconsistent strain, achieving state-of-the-art performance with enhanced lesion-to-background contrast and noise suppression across diverse datasets.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly advances Ultrasound Strain Elastography, a non-invasive diagnostic tool, by providing more accurate, consistent, and noise-suppressed assessments of tissue mechanical properties. This improvement is crucial for reliable diagnosis and characterization of various pathologies where tissue stiffness is an indicator, such as cancer or fibrosis.

AI Health Application

This research applies a multi-stage unsupervised deep learning framework (MUSSE-Net) to enhance Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE). The AI's purpose is to improve the robustness and consistency of strain estimation, overcoming issues like tissue decorrelation noise and inconsistent results. This leads to more accurate and clinically interpretable strain maps, thereby improving the diagnostic capabilities of USE for assessing tissue mechanical properties and identifying abnormalities with better lesion-to-background contrast and noise suppression in clinical settings.

Key Points

  • **Addresses Key USE Challenges**: Tackles critical issues in Ultrasound Strain Elastography, including tissue decorrelation noise, scarcity of ground truth data, and inconsistent strain estimation under varying deformation conditions.
  • **Proposed Framework MUSSE-Net**: Introduces a residual-aware, multi-stage unsupervised deep learning framework for robust and consistent strain estimation.
  • **Core Architecture USSE-Net**: Features an end-to-end multi-stream encoder-decoder network that processes pre- and post-deformation RF sequences in parallel to estimate displacement fields and axial strains.
  • **Advanced Architectural Components**: Incorporates a Context-Aware Complementary Feature Fusion (CACFF)-based encoder, a Tri-Cross Attention (TCA) bottleneck, and a Cross-Attentive Fusion (CAF)-based sequential decoder for enhanced feature processing.
  • **Temporal Coherence and Stability**: Leverages a tailored consistency loss to ensure temporal coherence and stable strain estimation across different deformation levels.
  • **Residual Refinement Stage**: The MUSSE-Net framework includes a secondary residual refinement stage to further enhance accuracy and suppress noise in the estimated strain maps.
  • **State-of-the-Art Performance**: Demonstrated superior performance over existing unsupervised approaches on simulation, in vivo, and private clinical datasets (BUET), achieving high metrics like target SNR of 24.54, CNR of 59.81, and elastographic SNR of 9.73 on simulation data, and producing clinically interpretable strain patterns with enhanced contrast on clinical data.

Methodology

The proposed MUSSE-Net is a multi-stage unsupervised sequential deep learning framework. Its core component, USSE-Net, is a multi-stream encoder-decoder architecture that takes pre- and post-deformation RF sequences as input to estimate displacement fields and axial strains. USSE-Net features a Context-Aware Complementary Feature Fusion (CACFF)-based encoder, a Tri-Cross Attention (TCA) bottleneck, and a Cross-Attentive Fusion (CAF)-based sequential decoder. The network is trained with a tailored consistency loss to ensure temporal coherence. A secondary residual refinement stage is then applied within the MUSSE-Net framework to further enhance accuracy and suppress noise in the strain estimations.

Key Findings

MUSSE-Net achieves state-of-the-art performance, outperforming existing unsupervised methods. On simulation data, it yielded a target SNR of 24.54, background SNR of 132.76, CNR of 59.81, and an elastographic SNR of 9.73. Furthermore, on the BUET clinical dataset, it produced strain maps with significantly enhanced lesion-to-background contrast and noise suppression, resulting in clinically interpretable strain patterns.

Clinical Impact

The improved accuracy, consistency, and noise reduction in strain maps offered by MUSSE-Net will enhance the diagnostic value of Ultrasound Strain Elastography. This can lead to more reliable and earlier detection and characterization of pathologies like tumors (distinguishing benign from malignant), liver fibrosis, or prostate cancer, potentially reducing the need for invasive biopsies and guiding more effective treatment strategies.

Abstract

Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE) is a powerful non-invasive imaging technique for assessing tissue mechanical properties, offering crucial diagnostic value across diverse clinical applications. However, its clinical application remains limited by tissue decorrelation noise, scarcity of ground truth, and inconsistent strain estimation under different deformation conditions. Overcoming these barriers, we propose MUSSE-Net, a residual-aware, multi-stage unsupervised sequential deep learning framework designed for robust and consistent strain estimation. At its backbone lies our proposed USSE-Net, an end-to-end multi-stream encoder-decoder architecture that parallelly processes pre- and post-deformation RF sequences to estimate displacement fields and axial strains. The novel architecture incorporates Context-Aware Complementary Feature Fusion (CACFF)-based encoder with Tri-Cross Attention (TCA) bottleneck with a Cross-Attentive Fusion (CAF)-based sequential decoder. To ensure temporal coherence and strain stability across varying deformation levels, this architecture leverages a tailored consistency loss. Finally, with the MUSSE-Net framework, a secondary residual refinement stage further enhances accuracy and suppresses noise. Extensive validation on simulation, in vivo, and private clinical datasets from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) medical center, demonstrates MUSSE-Net's outperformed existing unsupervised approaches. On MUSSE-Net achieves state-of-the-art performance with a target SNR of 24.54, background SNR of 132.76, CNR of 59.81, and elastographic SNR of 9.73 on simulation data. In particular, on the BUET dataset, MUSSE-Net produces strain maps with enhanced lesion-to-background contrast and significant noise suppression yielding clinically interpretable strain patterns.
