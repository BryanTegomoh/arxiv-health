Multi-Stage Residual-Aware Unsupervised Deep Learning Framework for Consistent Ultrasound Strain Elastography

Published: 2025-11-19
Authors: Shourov Joarder, Tushar Talukder Showrav, Md. Kamrul Hasan

Summary

MUSSE-Net is a novel multi-stage, residual-aware unsupervised deep learning framework designed for consistent and robust Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE). It addresses critical challenges such as tissue decorrelation noise and inconsistent strain estimation by employing a multi-stream encoder-decoder network (USSE-Net) with attention mechanisms and a tailored consistency loss, followed by a secondary residual refinement stage, demonstrating superior performance across diverse datasets. The framework significantly enhances lesion contrast and suppresses noise, yielding clinically interpretable strain patterns.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly enhances the robustness and consistency of Ultrasound Strain Elastography, making it a more reliable non-invasive tool for assessing tissue mechanical properties. Improved strain estimation is crucial for accurate diagnosis and staging of various diseases, particularly in differentiating benign from malignant lesions and characterizing fibrotic changes in organs.

AI Health Application

The AI (deep learning framework, MUSSE-Net) is applied to enhance the accuracy, consistency, and interpretability of Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE) for assessing tissue mechanical properties. This improves the diagnostic quality of ultrasound images, leading to better detection and characterization of pathologies (e.g., lesions) in various clinical settings.

  • **Core Architecture (USSE-Net)**: Proposes USSE-Net, an end-to-end multi-stream encoder-decoder architecture that parallelly processes pre- and post-deformation RF sequences to estimate displacement fields and axial strains.
  • **Novel Architectural Components**: Incorporates a Context-Aware Complementary Feature Fusion (CACFF)-based encoder, a Tri-Cross Attention (TCA) bottleneck, and a Cross-Attentive Fusion (CAF)-based sequential decoder to enhance feature processing and fusion.
  • **Consistency and Stability**: Leverages a tailored consistency loss within USSE-Net to ensure temporal coherence and strain stability across different deformation levels, crucial for clinical reliability.
  • **Multi-Stage Refinement (MUSSE-Net)**: The full MUSSE-Net framework integrates a secondary residual refinement stage to further enhance the accuracy of strain estimation and suppress residual noise.
  • **State-of-the-Art Performance**: Achieves state-of-the-art results on simulation data, with impressive quantitative metrics: target SNR of 24.54, background SNR of 132.76, CNR of 59.81, and elastographic SNR of 9.73.

Methodology

The proposed MUSSE-Net is a multi-stage residual-aware unsupervised deep learning framework. Its primary stage involves USSE-Net, an end-to-end multi-stream encoder-decoder that processes pre- and post-deformation RF sequences. USSE-Net features a Context-Aware Complementary Feature Fusion (CACFF) encoder, a Tri-Cross Attention (TCA) bottleneck, and a Cross-Attentive Fusion (CAF) sequential decoder. A tailored consistency loss is integrated to ensure temporal coherence and strain stability. The secondary stage of MUSSE-Net applies a residual refinement module to further improve accuracy and reduce noise. Validation was performed on simulation, in vivo, and a private clinical dataset from BUET.

Key Findings

MUSSE-Net significantly outperforms existing unsupervised approaches in Ultrasound Strain Elastography. It achieves state-of-the-art performance on simulation data, demonstrated by a target SNR of 24.54, background SNR of 132.76, CNR of 59.81, and an elastographic SNR of 9.73. On clinical data, the framework produces strain maps with enhanced lesion-to-background contrast and substantial noise suppression, resulting in highly consistent and clinically interpretable strain patterns.

Clinical Impact

By providing robust, consistent, and noise-suppressed strain maps, MUSSE-Net has the potential to significantly improve the diagnostic accuracy of Ultrasound Strain Elastography. This can lead to earlier and more reliable detection of pathological tissue stiffness, aiding in the differentiation of various diseases (e.g., tumors from healthy tissue, assessment of fibrosis), thereby supporting more informed clinical decision-making and patient management.

Abstract

Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE) is a powerful non-invasive imaging technique for assessing tissue mechanical properties, offering crucial diagnostic value across diverse clinical applications. However, its clinical application remains limited by tissue decorrelation noise, scarcity of ground truth, and inconsistent strain estimation under different deformation conditions. Overcoming these barriers, we propose MUSSE-Net, a residual-aware, multi-stage unsupervised sequential deep learning framework designed for robust and consistent strain estimation. At its backbone lies our proposed USSE-Net, an end-to-end multi-stream encoder-decoder architecture that parallelly processes pre- and post-deformation RF sequences to estimate displacement fields and axial strains. The novel architecture incorporates Context-Aware Complementary Feature Fusion (CACFF)-based encoder with Tri-Cross Attention (TCA) bottleneck with a Cross-Attentive Fusion (CAF)-based sequential decoder. To ensure temporal coherence and strain stability across varying deformation levels, this architecture leverages a tailored consistency loss. Finally, with the MUSSE-Net framework, a secondary residual refinement stage further enhances accuracy and suppresses noise. Extensive validation on simulation, in vivo, and private clinical datasets from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) medical center, demonstrates MUSSE-Net's outperformed existing unsupervised approaches. On MUSSE-Net achieves state-of-the-art performance with a target SNR of 24.54, background SNR of 132.76, CNR of 59.81, and elastographic SNR of 9.73 on simulation data. In particular, on the BUET dataset, MUSSE-Net produces strain maps with enhanced lesion-to-background contrast and significant noise suppression yielding clinically interpretable strain patterns.
