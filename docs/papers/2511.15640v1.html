Multi-Stage Residual-Aware Unsupervised Deep Learning Framework for Consistent Ultrasound Strain Elastography

Published: 2025-11-19
Authors: Shourov Joarder, Tushar Talukder Showrav, Md. Kamrul Hasan

Summary

This paper introduces MUSSE-Net, a multi-stage, residual-aware, unsupervised deep learning framework designed to improve the consistency and accuracy of Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE). It addresses limitations in USE such as tissue decorrelation noise, lack of ground truth, and inconsistent strain estimation by leveraging a novel multi-stream encoder-decoder architecture (USSE-Net) with attention mechanisms and a tailored consistency loss. The framework demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on simulation data and produces clinically interpretable strain maps with enhanced contrast and noise suppression on in vivo and clinical datasets.

Medical Relevance

Improving Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE) directly enhances its diagnostic utility by providing more reliable, consistent, and interpretable assessments of tissue mechanical properties. This can lead to earlier and more accurate diagnoses for conditions where tissue stiffness is a key indicator, such as cancer, liver fibrosis, or thyroid nodules, ultimately improving patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

The AI framework (MUSSE-Net) is applied to enhance Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE), a medical imaging technique. It aims to improve the accuracy, consistency, and clinical interpretability of strain maps derived from ultrasound data. This directly aids in the diagnosis and assessment of tissue mechanical properties, which is critical for identifying various pathologies (e.g., tumors, fibrosis), thereby improving diagnostic capabilities in healthcare settings.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: Tackles key limitations of Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE), including tissue decorrelation noise, scarcity of ground truth data, and inconsistent strain estimation under varying deformation conditions.
  • **MUSSE-Net Framework**: Proposes a residual-aware, multi-stage unsupervised sequential deep learning framework for robust and consistent strain estimation.
  • **USSE-Net Core Architecture**: Introduces USSE-Net, an end-to-end multi-stream encoder-decoder model that processes pre- and post-deformation RF sequences in parallel to estimate displacement fields and axial strains.
  • **Novel Architectural Components**: Incorporates a Context-Aware Complementary Feature Fusion (CACFF)-based encoder, a Tri-Cross Attention (TCA) bottleneck, and a Cross-Attentive Fusion (CAF)-based sequential decoder.
  • **Consistency Loss and Refinement**: Employs a tailored consistency loss to ensure temporal coherence and strain stability across different deformation levels, complemented by a secondary residual refinement stage within MUSSE-Net for enhanced accuracy and noise suppression.
  • **Validation and Performance**: Extensively validated on simulation, in vivo, and private clinical datasets, outperforming existing unsupervised approaches with state-of-the-art quantitative metrics (e.g., target SNR 24.54, CNR 59.81) on simulation data.
  • **Clinical Interpretability**: Demonstrates enhanced lesion-to-background contrast and significant noise suppression on clinical data (BUET dataset), yielding clinically interpretable strain patterns.

Methodology

MUSSE-Net is a residual-aware, multi-stage unsupervised sequential deep learning framework. Its core is USSE-Net, an end-to-end multi-stream encoder-decoder network. USSE-Net parallelly processes pre- and post-deformation RF sequences to estimate displacement fields and axial strains. The encoder utilizes Context-Aware Complementary Feature Fusion (CACFF), the bottleneck incorporates Tri-Cross Attention (TCA), and the decoder is a Cross-Attentive Fusion (CAF)-based sequential design. A tailored consistency loss is integrated to ensure temporal coherence and strain stability across varying deformation levels. The overall MUSSE-Net framework further enhances accuracy and suppresses noise via a secondary residual refinement stage.

Key Findings

MUSSE-Net achieves state-of-the-art performance, outperforming existing unsupervised approaches. On simulation data, it yielded a target SNR of 24.54, background SNR of 132.76, CNR of 59.81, and elastographic SNR of 9.73. On the BUET clinical dataset, the framework produced strain maps with significantly enhanced lesion-to-background contrast, substantial noise suppression, and clinically interpretable strain patterns.

Clinical Impact

The proposed MUSSE-Net framework has the potential to significantly enhance the clinical utility of Ultrasound Strain Elastography. By providing more consistent, accurate, and noise-suppressed strain maps, it can enable clinicians to make more confident and earlier diagnoses of diseases characterized by altered tissue stiffness, reducing diagnostic ambiguity and potentially improving treatment planning and patient prognosis. The improved lesion-to-background contrast can aid in clearer visualization and delineation of pathological tissues.

Abstract

Ultrasound Strain Elastography (USE) is a powerful non-invasive imaging technique for assessing tissue mechanical properties, offering crucial diagnostic value across diverse clinical applications. However, its clinical application remains limited by tissue decorrelation noise, scarcity of ground truth, and inconsistent strain estimation under different deformation conditions. Overcoming these barriers, we propose MUSSE-Net, a residual-aware, multi-stage unsupervised sequential deep learning framework designed for robust and consistent strain estimation. At its backbone lies our proposed USSE-Net, an end-to-end multi-stream encoder-decoder architecture that parallelly processes pre- and post-deformation RF sequences to estimate displacement fields and axial strains. The novel architecture incorporates Context-Aware Complementary Feature Fusion (CACFF)-based encoder with Tri-Cross Attention (TCA) bottleneck with a Cross-Attentive Fusion (CAF)-based sequential decoder. To ensure temporal coherence and strain stability across varying deformation levels, this architecture leverages a tailored consistency loss. Finally, with the MUSSE-Net framework, a secondary residual refinement stage further enhances accuracy and suppresses noise. Extensive validation on simulation, in vivo, and private clinical datasets from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) medical center, demonstrates MUSSE-Net's outperformed existing unsupervised approaches. On MUSSE-Net achieves state-of-the-art performance with a target SNR of 24.54, background SNR of 132.76, CNR of 59.81, and elastographic SNR of 9.73 on simulation data. In particular, on the BUET dataset, MUSSE-Net produces strain maps with enhanced lesion-to-background contrast and significant noise suppression yielding clinically interpretable strain patterns.
