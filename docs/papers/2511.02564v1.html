Seeing Across Time and Views: Multi-Temporal Cross-View Learning for Robust Video Person Re-Identification

arXiv ID: 2511.02564v1
Published: 2025-11-04
Authors: Md Rashidunnabi, Kailash A. Hambarde, Vasco Lopes, Joao C. Neves, Hugo Proenca
Categories: cs.CV

Summary

This paper introduces MTF-CVReID, a parameter-efficient deep learning framework designed to enhance robust video-based person re-identification in challenging cross-view environments with extreme viewpoint shifts and scale disparities. By integrating seven specialized adapter-based modules over a ViT backbone, the framework achieves state-of-the-art performance on relevant benchmarks while maintaining real-time efficiency.

Medical Relevance

This robust and real-time cross-view person re-identification technology has significant potential for enhancing patient safety and operational efficiency in complex healthcare environments. It can facilitate accurate tracking of individuals across large hospital campuses or care facilities, especially those at risk of wandering or requiring constant monitoring.

AI Health Application

This research provides a highly robust and efficient AI model for person re-identification, which can serve as a critical component for AI systems in health and biosecurity. For healthcare, it could enable AI applications for patient safety (e.g., tracking vulnerable patients like those with dementia to prevent wandering in hospitals or care facilities), hospital security (identifying unauthorized individuals, monitoring sensitive areas), and workflow optimization by analyzing staff movement patterns. In biosecurity, this technology can be leveraged for AI-driven access control and surveillance in high-containment laboratories, tracking personnel within sensitive biological zones, or monitoring individuals in restricted areas during public health emergencies.

Methodology

The MTF-CVReID framework utilizes a ViT-B/16 backbone enhanced with seven distinct adapter-based modules. These include Cross-Stream Feature Normalization (CSFN) to correct camera/view biases, Multi-Resolution Feature Harmonization (MRFH) for scale stabilization, an Identity-Aware Memory Module (IAMM) to reinforce persistent identity traits, Temporal Dynamics Modeling (TDM) for motion-aware encoding, Inter-View Feature Alignment (IVFA) for perspective-invariance, Hierarchical Temporal Pattern Learning (HTPL) for multi-scale temporal regularities, and Multi-View Identity Consistency Learning (MVICL) which employs a contrastive learning paradigm to enforce cross-view identity coherence.

Key Findings

The MTF-CVReID framework achieved state-of-the-art performance on the AG-VPReID benchmark across all altitude levels, exhibited strong cross-dataset generalization to G2A-VReID and MARS datasets, and maintained real-time efficiency (189 FPS) with a minimal increase in parameters (approximately 2 million) and GFLOPs (0.7) over the baseline. This demonstrates that strategically designed adapter-based modules can substantially improve cross-view robustness and temporal consistency in video person re-identification.

Abstract

Video-based person re-identification (ReID) in cross-view domains (for example, aerial-ground surveillance) remains an open problem because of extreme viewpoint shifts, scale disparities, and temporal inconsistencies. To address these challenges, we propose MTF-CVReID, a parameter-efficient framework that introduces seven complementary modules over a ViT-B/16 backbone. Specifically, we include: (1) Cross-Stream Feature Normalization (CSFN) to correct camera and view biases; (2) Multi-Resolution Feature Harmonization (MRFH) for scale stabilization across altitudes; (3) Identity-Aware Memory Module (IAMM) to reinforce persistent identity traits; (4) Temporal Dynamics Modeling (TDM) for motion-aware short-term temporal encoding; (5) Inter-View Feature Alignment (IVFA) for perspective-invariant representation alignment; (6) Hierarchical Temporal Pattern Learning (HTPL) to capture multi-scale temporal regularities; and (7) Multi-View Identity Consistency Learning (MVICL) that enforces cross-view identity coherence using a contrastive learning paradigm. Despite adding only about 2 million parameters and 0.7 GFLOPs over the baseline, MTF-CVReID maintains real-time efficiency (189 FPS) and achieves state-of-the-art performance on the AG-VPReID benchmark across all altitude levels, with strong cross-dataset generalization to G2A-VReID and MARS datasets. These results show that carefully designed adapter-based modules can substantially enhance cross-view robustness and temporal consistency without compromising computational efficiency. The source code is available at https://github.com/MdRashidunnabi/MTF-CVReID
