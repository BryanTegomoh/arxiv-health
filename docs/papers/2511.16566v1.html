NutriScreener: Retrieval-Augmented Multi-Pose Graph Attention Network for Malnourishment Screening

arXiv ID: 2511.16566v1
Published: 2025-11-20
Authors: Misaal Khan, Mayank Vatsa, Kuldeep Singh, Richa Singh
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

NutriScreener is a novel retrieval-augmented, multi-pose graph attention network designed for scalable and accurate detection of child malnutrition and anthropometric prediction from images. It integrates CLIP-based visual embeddings, class-boosted knowledge retrieval, and context awareness to address issues of generalizability and class imbalance in pediatric settings. Clinical studies and extensive testing confirm its high accuracy and efficiency, making it suitable for deployment in low-resource environments.

Medical Relevance

This technology provides a critical tool for early and widespread identification of child malnutrition, a leading cause of childhood morbidity and mortality. Its scalability and accuracy are particularly vital for low-resource settings where medical infrastructure and trained personnel are scarce.

AI Health Application

NutriScreener is an AI application designed to provide scalable and accurate early detection of child malnutrition and anthropometric measurements using images. It serves as an AI-powered diagnostic/screening tool to assist healthcare professionals, particularly in low-resource environments, by automating and improving the efficiency of existing labor-intensive screening methods. This facilitates timely intervention for a critical health issue.

Methodology

NutriScreener is a retrieval-augmented, multi-pose graph attention network. It utilizes CLIP-based visual embeddings for rich image feature extraction. To enhance accuracy and address class imbalance, it integrates a class-boosted knowledge retrieval system, which leverages demographically matched knowledge bases. Furthermore, it incorporates context awareness to improve the robustness of malnutrition detection and anthropometric prediction from children's images.

Key Findings

The system achieves 0.79 recall and 0.82 AUC for malnutrition detection, alongside significantly lower anthropometric RMSEs, indicating reliable measurement. Clinical validation by doctors rated its accuracy at 4.3/5 and efficiency at 4.6/5. Cross-dataset evaluations demonstrated substantial gains, including up to 25% recall increase and up to 3.5 cm reduction in RMSE through the use of demographically matched knowledge bases, confirming strong generalizability.

Clinical Impact

NutriScreener offers a highly scalable and accurate solution for early malnutrition detection, significantly reducing the labor and resource intensity of current screening methods. Its deployment readiness in low-resource settings could enable widespread, timely interventions, ultimately improving child health outcomes globally by facilitating rapid identification and referral for care.

Abstract

Child malnutrition remains a global crisis, yet existing screening methods are laborious and poorly scalable, hindering early intervention. In this work, we present NutriScreener, a retrieval-augmented, multi-pose graph attention network that combines CLIP-based visual embeddings, class-boosted knowledge retrieval, and context awareness to enable robust malnutrition detection and anthropometric prediction from children's images, simultaneously addressing generalizability and class imbalance. In a clinical study, doctors rated it 4.3/5 for accuracy and 4.6/5 for efficiency, confirming its deployment readiness in low-resource settings. Trained and tested on 2,141 children from AnthroVision and additionally evaluated on diverse cross-continent populations, including ARAN and an in-house collected CampusPose dataset, it achieves 0.79 recall, 0.82 AUC, and significantly lower anthropometric RMSEs, demonstrating reliable measurement in unconstrained pediatric settings. Cross-dataset results show up to 25% recall gain and up to 3.5 cm RMSE reduction using demographically matched knowledge bases. NutriScreener offers a scalable and accurate solution for early malnutrition detection in low-resource environments.
