Summary

LookAroundNet is a transformer-based EEG seizure detector designed to improve detection by incorporating an extended temporal context, mirroring how clinicians interpret surrounding EEG data. Evaluated across diverse datasets, the model demonstrates strong performance, robust generalization to varying recording conditions, and operates with computational efficiency suitable for clinical deployment, thereby advancing automated seizure detection towards clinical viability.

Medical Relevance

Automated seizure detection from EEG is critical for timely diagnosis and effective management of epilepsy, but its clinical utility is hampered by high variability in seizure presentation. This work offers a robust, generalizable, and computationally efficient solution that could significantly improve patient monitoring and clinical decision-making in diverse healthcare settings, including home care.

AI Health Application

The AI application is an automated, transformer-based seizure detection system (LookAroundNet) designed to analyze EEG data. Its purpose is to assist clinicians in identifying seizure activity, improve diagnostic accuracy, and enable efficient monitoring in both clinical and home-monitoring settings, moving towards real-world clinical deployment.

Methodology

LookAroundNet is a transformer-based neural network model for EEG seizure detection. Its core innovation is the use of an extended temporal context, where the model processes EEG data segments that precede and follow the immediate segment under analysis. This wider window allows the model to capture dynamic seizure evolution and contextual clues, mimicking expert human interpretation, and is applied across diverse datasets for training and evaluation.

Key Findings

The study found that LookAroundNet achieves strong performance metrics across a variety of EEG datasets, demonstrating excellent generalization capabilities to previously unseen recording conditions and diverse patient populations. Crucially, the model's computational requirements are low enough for practical clinical deployment. The research also highlighted that extending the temporal context, increasing the diversity of training data, and employing model ensembling are pivotal strategies for improving automatic seizure detection performance.

Abstract

Automated seizure detection from electroencephalography (EEG) remains difficult due to the large variability of seizure dynamics across patients, recording conditions, and clinical settings. We introduce LookAroundNet, a transformer-based seizure detector that uses a wider temporal window of EEG data to model seizure activity. The seizure detector incorporates EEG signals before and after the segment of interest, reflecting how clinicians use surrounding context when interpreting EEG recordings. We evaluate the proposed method on multiple EEG datasets spanning diverse clinical environments, patient populations, and recording modalities, including routine clinical EEG and long-term ambulatory recordings, in order to study performance across varying data distributions. The evaluation includes publicly available datasets as well as a large proprietary collection of home EEG recordings, providing complementary views of controlled clinical data and unconstrained home-monitoring conditions. Our results show that LookAroundNet achieves strong performance across datasets, generalizes well to previously unseen recording conditions, and operates with computational costs compatible with real-world clinical deployment. The results indicate that extended temporal context, increased training data diversity, and model ensembling are key factors for improving performance. This work contributes to moving automatic seizure detection models toward clinically viable solutions.
