MammoClean: Toward Reproducible and Bias-Aware AI in Mammography through Dataset Harmonization

Summary

MammoClean is a novel open-source framework designed to standardize and quantify bias in public mammography datasets, addressing the critical issue of data heterogeneity that compromises AI model generalizability. By harmonizing data quality, processing, and metadata, MammoClean facilitates the development of robust and reproducible AI systems, crucial for equitable performance across diverse patient populations. The framework demonstrably improves AI model performance by mitigating biases inherent in uncurated datasets.

Medical Relevance

This research directly addresses a fundamental barrier to deploying clinically reliable AI in mammography by ensuring models are trained on harmonized, bias-aware data. This improves diagnostic accuracy and fairness, reducing disparities in care across different patient groups and clinical environments by facilitating the development of robust and equitable AI tools.

AI Health Application

The research focuses on creating a framework (MammoClean) for standardizing and harmonizing heterogeneous mammography datasets. This aims to mitigate data-specific biases that compromise AI model generalizability, thereby facilitating the development of robust, fair, and clinically deployable AI systems for breast cancer detection and diagnosis, ensuring equitable performance across diverse patient populations.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** Profound heterogeneity in mammography data (quality, metadata, population distributions) across public datasets creates dataset-specific biases, severely limiting AI model generalizability and hindering clinical deployment.
  • **Solution Offered:** MammoClean, a public and open-source framework, systematically standardizes and quantifies bias in mammography datasets to enable reproducible and bias-aware AI development.
  • **Methodology:** The framework standardizes case selection, image processing (including laterality and intensity correction), and unifies metadata into a consistent multi-view structure, informed by a comprehensive review of breast anatomy and imaging characteristics.
  • **Application and Findings:** Applied to three heterogeneous datasets (CBIS-DDSM, TOMPEI-CMMD, VinDr-Mammo), MammoClean quantified substantial distributional shifts in breast density and abnormality prevalence, highlighting inherent biases.
  • **Impact on AI Performance:** The study critically demonstrated that AI models trained on corrupted (unharmonized) datasets exhibit significant performance degradation compared to those trained on MammoClean-curated, standardized counterparts.
  • **Enabling Robust AI:** MammoClean allows researchers to construct unified, multi-dataset training corpora, enabling the development of more robust AI models with superior cross-domain generalization.
  • **Reproducibility and Equity:** The framework provides an essential, reproducible pipeline for bias-aware AI development, fostering fairer comparisons and advancing the creation of safe, effective AI systems that perform equitably across diverse patient populations and clinical settings.

Methodology

MammoClean is a framework that standardizes mammography datasets through several key steps: standardized case selection, consistent image processing (including laterality and intensity correction), and unification of diverse metadata into a consistent multi-view structure. It incorporates a comprehensive review of breast anatomy and imaging characteristics to systematically identify bias sources. The framework's efficacy was demonstrated by applying it to three public datasets (CBIS-DDSM, TOMPEI-CMMD, VinDr-Mammo) to quantify distributional shifts in clinical parameters and evaluate the performance degradation of AI models trained on uncurated versus MammoClean-curated data.

Key Findings

The study quantified substantial distributional shifts in critical clinical parameters, specifically breast density and abnormality prevalence, across the analyzed heterogeneous datasets. A key finding was the direct impact of data corruption: AI models trained on these unharmonized datasets exhibited significant performance degradation compared to models trained on MammoClean-curated, standardized data. This highlights the framework's effectiveness in mitigating data-specific biases.

Clinical Impact

MammoClean provides a crucial, reproducible tool for developing robust and unbiased AI models in mammography, which can lead to more accurate and reliable diagnostic outcomes. By enabling superior cross-domain generalization, it helps ensure that AI systems perform equitably across diverse patient demographics and clinical settings, thereby accelerating the safe, ethical, and effective clinical deployment of AI, ultimately reducing diagnostic disparities and improving patient care.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations of the MammoClean framework itself. However, the study applied MammoClean to three specific public datasets, which, while diverse, may not capture every possible source of bias or data corruption present in the broader spectrum of real-world clinical data or proprietary datasets.

Future Directions

Future research can leverage MammoClean to construct larger, unified multi-dataset training corpora, enabling the development and rigorous testing of even more robust and generalizable AI models. This will further facilitate the creation of safer, more effective, and equitable AI systems that can perform consistently across a wider array of diverse patient populations and clinical environments, paving the way for broader clinical adoption and societal impact.

Abstract

The development of clinically reliable artificial intelligence (AI) systems for mammography is hindered by profound heterogeneity in data quality, metadata standards, and population distributions across public datasets. This heterogeneity introduces dataset-specific biases that severely compromise the generalizability of the model, a fundamental barrier to clinical deployment. We present MammoClean, a public framework for standardization and bias quantification in mammography datasets. MammoClean standardizes case selection, image processing (including laterality and intensity correction), and unifies metadata into a consistent multi-view structure. We provide a comprehensive review of breast anatomy, imaging characteristics, and public mammography datasets to systematically identify key sources of bias. Applying MammoClean to three heterogeneous datasets (CBIS-DDSM, TOMPEI-CMMD, VinDr-Mammo), we quantify substantial distributional shifts in breast density and abnormality prevalence. Critically, we demonstrate the direct impact of data corruption: AI models trained on corrupted datasets exhibit significant performance degradation compared to their curated counterparts. By using MammoClean to identify and mitigate bias sources, researchers can construct unified multi-dataset training corpora that enable development of robust models with superior cross-domain generalization. MammoClean provides an essential, reproducible pipeline for bias-aware AI development in mammography, facilitating fairer comparisons and advancing the creation of safe, effective systems that perform equitably across diverse patient populations and clinical settings. The open-source code is publicly available from: https://github.com/Minds-R-Lab/MammoClean.
