A Blockchain-Monitored Agentic AI Architecture for Trusted Perception-Reasoning-Action Pipelines

arXiv ID: 2512.20985v1
Published: 2025-12-24
Authors: Salman Jan, Hassan Ali Razzaqi, Ali Akarma, Mohammad Riyaz Belgaum
Categories: cs.AI, cs.MA
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper proposes a novel architecture integrating a LangChain-based multi-agent AI system with a permissioned blockchain (Hyperledger Fabric) to ensure trusted, auditable, and policy-compliant autonomous decision-making. The framework monitors, verifies, and records the AI's perception-reasoning-action pipeline, demonstrating its effectiveness in critical applications like healthcare monitoring while maintaining operational efficiency. It aims to address concerns regarding trust, oversight, and integrity in agentic AI systems used for autonomous decisions.

Medical Relevance

This framework is highly relevant to medicine by providing a robust mechanism for deploying trustworthy and accountable agentic AI in healthcare monitoring, clinical decision support, and other critical autonomous decision-making scenarios. It ensures data integrity, policy adherence, and transparent auditability of AI actions directly impacting patient care and safety.

The paper describes a framework for building trusted and auditable agentic AI systems using a combination of LangChain-based multi-agent systems and permissioned blockchain. In a healthcare context, this framework could be applied to AI agents performing tasks such as real-time patient monitoring (as explicitly mentioned 'healthcare monitoring' experiment), automated medical decision support, managing medical supply chains (linking to 'smart inventory management'), ensuring data integrity in electronic health records, or overseeing autonomous systems in remote care. The blockchain component ensures immutable audit trails, policy enforcement, and verifies inputs/outputs, which is critical for accountability and preventing unauthorized practices in high-stakes healthcare scenarios.

Methodology

The methodology involves the development of a unified architecture comprising a LangChain-based multi-agent system integrated with a Hyperledger Fabric-based permissioned blockchain. The blockchain layer is designed to monitor and govern the AI's perception-conceptualization-action cycle, specifically verifying inputs, evaluating recommended actions, and documenting execution outcomes. Action executors are integrated with MCP. The framework was experimentally validated across diverse use cases including smart inventory management, traffic-signal control, and a specific application in healthcare monitoring.

Key Findings

The research found that blockchain-security verification within the proposed architecture is efficient in preventing unauthorized practices by agentic AI systems. It provides comprehensive and immutable traceability throughout the entire decision-making process, from perception to action. Crucially, the system maintains operational latency within reasonable and acceptable ranges, demonstrating a practical balance between security, accountability, and performance for real-time applications.

Abstract

The application of agentic AI systems in autonomous decision-making is growing in the areas of healthcare, smart cities, digital forensics, and supply chain management. Even though these systems are flexible and offer real-time reasoning, they also raise concerns of trust and oversight, and integrity of the information and activities upon which they are founded. The paper suggests a single architecture model comprising of LangChain-based multi-agent system with a permissioned blockchain to guarantee constant monitoring, policy enforcement, and immutable auditability of agentic action. The framework relates the perception conceptualization-action cycle to a blockchain layer of governance that verifies the inputs, evaluates recommended actions, and documents the outcomes of the execution. A Hyperledger Fabric-based system, action executors MCP-integrated, and LangChain agent are introduced and experiments of smart inventory management, traffic-signal control, and healthcare monitoring are done. The results suggest that blockchain-security verification is efficient in preventing unauthorized practices, offers traceability throughout the whole decision-making process, and maintains operational latency within reasonable ranges. The suggested framework provides a universal system of implementing high-impact agentic AI applications that are autonomous yet responsible.
