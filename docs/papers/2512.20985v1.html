A Blockchain-Monitored Agentic AI Architecture for Trusted Perception-Reasoning-Action Pipelines

Summary

This paper proposes a novel architecture integrating a LangChain-based multi-agent system with a permissioned blockchain, specifically Hyperledger Fabric, to address trust and oversight concerns in autonomous agentic AI systems. The framework ensures constant monitoring, policy enforcement, and immutable auditability of AI actions across its perception-reasoning-action pipeline, with demonstrated efficiency in preventing unauthorized practices and maintaining traceability, particularly in applications like healthcare monitoring.

Medical Relevance

This architecture offers a critical solution for deploying trustworthy and accountable agentic AI systems in healthcare, enabling autonomous decision-making while ensuring patient safety, data integrity, and compliance through immutable audit trails and real-time monitoring.

AI Health Application

The paper presents an agentic AI architecture monitored by a blockchain, specifically designed to ensure trust, immutable auditability, and policy enforcement for autonomous decision-making systems. This is applied to healthcare monitoring, providing a robust framework for AI systems that require high levels of accountability, security, and traceability for patient care, diagnostics, or operational efficiency within healthcare settings. It aims to develop responsible, autonomous AI for high-impact applications in health.

  • Introduces a novel architecture combining a LangChain-based multi-agent system with a permissioned blockchain (Hyperledger Fabric) to provide robust governance and monitoring.
  • The blockchain layer functions as a verifiable governance mechanism, scrutinizing agent inputs, evaluating recommended actions, and immutably documenting execution outcomes within the AI's perception-reasoning-action pipeline.
  • The proposed system was experimentally validated across diverse applications, including smart inventory management, traffic-signal control, and crucial healthcare monitoring scenarios.

Methodology

The authors developed an architecture that integrates a LangChain-based multi-agent system with a permissioned blockchain, specifically Hyperledger Fabric. This blockchain layer serves as a governance mechanism, verifying agent inputs, evaluating proposed actions, and immutably recording execution outcomes within the perception-reasoning-action cycle. The system was implemented and evaluated through experiments in smart inventory management, traffic-signal control, and a healthcare monitoring scenario.

Key Findings

The experiments demonstrated that the blockchain-security verification is efficient in preventing unauthorized practices and ensuring comprehensive traceability throughout the AI's decision-making pipeline. Furthermore, the system maintained operational latency within reasonable ranges, confirming its practical viability for real-time applications.

This can significantly enhance patient monitoring, improve the reliability of AI-driven diagnostic or treatment recommendations, and support secure management of sensitive health data, ultimately improving care quality and patient outcomes while mitigating risks associated with AI autonomy.

Abstract

The application of agentic AI systems in autonomous decision-making is growing in the areas of healthcare, smart cities, digital forensics, and supply chain management. Even though these systems are flexible and offer real-time reasoning, they also raise concerns of trust and oversight, and integrity of the information and activities upon which they are founded. The paper suggests a single architecture model comprising of LangChain-based multi-agent system with a permissioned blockchain to guarantee constant monitoring, policy enforcement, and immutable auditability of agentic action. The framework relates the perception conceptualization-action cycle to a blockchain layer of governance that verifies the inputs, evaluates recommended actions, and documents the outcomes of the execution. A Hyperledger Fabric-based system, action executors MCP-integrated, and LangChain agent are introduced and experiments of smart inventory management, traffic-signal control, and healthcare monitoring are done. The results suggest that blockchain-security verification is efficient in preventing unauthorized practices, offers traceability throughout the whole decision-making process, and maintains operational latency within reasonable ranges. The suggested framework provides a universal system of implementing high-impact agentic AI applications that are autonomous yet responsible.
