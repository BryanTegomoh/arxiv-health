An Automatic Detection Method for Hematoma Features in Placental Abruption Ultrasound Images Based on Few-Shot Learning

arXiv ID: 2510.21495v1
Published: 2025-10-24
Authors: Xiaoqing Liu, Jitai Han, Hua Yan, Peng Li, Sida Tang, Ying Li, Kaiwen Zhang, Min Yu
Categories: cs.CV, cs.NE
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces EH-YOLOv11n, an improved deep learning model utilizing few-shot learning for the automatic detection of hematoma features in placental abruption ultrasound images. The model enhances detection accuracy and reliability by integrating advanced convolutional techniques and a cascaded group attention mechanism. Achieving 78% accuracy, it significantly outperforms existing YOLO variants and offers a robust, real-time solution for computer-aided diagnosis in a critical obstetric condition.

Medical Relevance

Placental abruption is a severe obstetric emergency where early and accurate detection of hematomas is crucial for maternal and fetal survival. This research offers an objective, consistent, and automated diagnostic tool that can improve diagnostic accuracy, reduce human error, and facilitate timely intervention.

AI Health Application

The AI application is a computer-aided diagnosis (CAD) system, EH-YOLOv11n, designed for the automatic detection of hematoma features in placental abruption ultrasound images. This system aims to reduce subjective bias and inconsistencies in traditional physician-reliant diagnostics, providing a more accurate and real-time tool for early diagnosis, ultimately enhancing maternal and fetal safety.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of early and accurate diagnosis for placental abruption, a severe pregnancy complication, aiming to reduce reliance on subjective physician experience.
  • Proposes EH-YOLOv11n (Enhanced Hemorrhage-YOLOv11n), a novel model based on few-shot learning for automatic detection of hematoma features in ultrasound images.
  • Enhances feature extraction by integrating wavelet convolution (for frequency features) and coordinate convolution (for spatial features) into the model architecture.
  • Incorporates a cascaded group attention mechanism to effectively suppress ultrasound artifacts and mitigate occlusion interference, thereby improving bounding box localization accuracy.
  • Achieves a detection accuracy of 78%, demonstrating a 2.5% improvement over the standard YOLOv11n and a substantial 13.7% increase compared to YOLOv8.
  • Exhibits superior performance in precision-recall curves, confidence scores, and particularly in challenging occlusion scenarios.
  • Combines high accuracy with real-time processing capabilities, providing a reliable computer-aided diagnostic solution with significant clinical application value.

Methodology

The paper proposes EH-YOLOv11n, an improved deep learning model based on few-shot learning for object detection. Its architecture incorporates specific enhancements: (1) **Wavelet convolution** and **coordinate convolution** are integrated to strengthen both frequency and spatial feature extraction from ultrasound images. (2) A **cascaded group attention mechanism** is introduced to suppress ultrasound artifacts and mitigate interference from occlusions, directly improving the accuracy of bounding box localization for hematoma features.

Key Findings

The EH-YOLOv11n model achieved a detection accuracy of 78%. This represents a 2.5% accuracy improvement over the baseline YOLOv11n model and a notable 13.7% increase compared to YOLOv8. The model also demonstrated significant superiority in precision-recall curves, confidence scores, and particularly in challenging occlusion scenarios, while maintaining real-time processing capabilities.

Clinical Impact

This model provides a robust and reliable computer-aided diagnosis (CAD) solution for placental abruption, reducing subjective bias and diagnostic inconsistencies inherent in traditional methods. Its high accuracy and real-time processing can lead to earlier, more precise identification of hematomas, enabling quicker clinical decisions and interventions, ultimately improving maternal and fetal safety outcomes.

Abstract

Placental abruption is a severe complication during pregnancy, and its early accurate diagnosis is crucial for ensuring maternal and fetal safety. Traditional ultrasound diagnostic methods heavily rely on physician experience, leading to issues such as subjective bias and diagnostic inconsistencies. This paper proposes an improved model, EH-YOLOv11n (Enhanced Hemorrhage-YOLOv11n), based on small-sample learning, aiming to achieve automatic detection of hematoma features in placental ultrasound images. The model enhances performance through multidimensional optimization: it integrates wavelet convolution and coordinate convolution to strengthen frequency and spatial feature extraction; incorporates a cascaded group attention mechanism to suppress ultrasound artifacts and occlusion interference, thereby improving bounding box localization accuracy. Experimental results demonstrate a detection accuracy of 78%, representing a 2.5% improvement over YOLOv11n and a 13.7% increase over YOLOv8. The model exhibits significant superiority in precision-recall curves, confidence scores, and occlusion scenarios. Combining high accuracy with real-time processing, this model provides a reliable solution for computer-aided diagnosis of placental abruption, holding significant clinical application value.
