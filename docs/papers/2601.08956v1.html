Variance-Penalized MC-Dropout as a Learned Smoothing Prior for Brain Tumour Segmentation

arXiv ID: 2601.08956v1
Published: 2026-01-13
Authors: Satyaki Roy Chowdhury, Golrokh Mirzaei
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces UAMSA-UNet, an Uncertainty-Aware Multi-Scale Attention-based Bayesian U-Net designed for robust brain tumor segmentation. It leverages Monte Carlo Dropout to learn a data-driven smoothing prior and employs a novel variance-penalized loss to produce spatially coherent masks, addressing the noisy boundaries common in existing U-Net approaches. UAMSA-UNet significantly improves segmentation accuracy and computational efficiency on BraTS datasets.

Medical Relevance

Accurate brain tumor segmentation is fundamental for precise diagnosis, surgical planning, radiation therapy planning, and monitoring treatment response. UAMSA-UNet's improvements in boundary coherence and accuracy directly translate to better clinical decision-making and potentially improved patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

The research develops an AI model, UAMSA-UNet (an Uncertainty-Aware Multi-Scale Attention-based Bayesian U-Net), which applies deep learning and Monte Carlo Dropout for automated and more accurate segmentation of brain tumors from medical scans. This application directly aids in the diagnosis and treatment planning of brain cancer, improving the quality and efficiency of medical imaging analysis.

Key Points

  • UAMSA-UNet is a Bayesian U-Net incorporating Monte Carlo Dropout to learn a data-driven smoothing prior for predictions.
  • It fuses multi-scale features and attention maps to capture both fine anatomical details and global contextual information, crucial for accurate tumor delineation.
  • A smoothing-regularized loss function, which augments binary cross-entropy with a variance penalty across stochastic forward passes, is introduced to discourage spurious fluctuations and ensure spatially coherent segmentation masks.
  • On the BraTS2023 dataset, UAMSA-UNet improved Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC) by up to 3.3% and mean IoU by up to 2.7% compared to the standard U-Net.
  • On the BraTS2024 dataset, it achieved even greater gains, with up to 4.5% improvement in DSC and 4.0% in IoU over the best baseline models.
  • Remarkably, UAMSA-UNet also demonstrated superior computational efficiency, reducing FLOPs by 42.5% relative to U-Net++ while maintaining higher accuracy.
  • The model's ability to combine multi-scale attention with a learned smoothing prior results in both enhanced segmentation quality and reduced computational overhead.

Methodology

UAMSA-UNet is an Uncertainty-Aware Multi-Scale Attention-based Bayesian U-Net. It uses Monte Carlo Dropout during inference to learn a data-driven smoothing prior, which introduces stochasticity and allows for uncertainty estimation. The architecture fuses multi-scale features with attention maps. The loss function is a smoothing-regularized binary cross-entropy, augmented with a variance penalty applied across multiple stochastic forward passes to enforce spatial coherence and reduce noisy fluctuations in the predicted segmentation masks.

Key Findings

UAMSA-UNet significantly outperforms existing U-Net and baseline models in brain tumor segmentation. It achieved up to 3.3% higher Dice and 2.7% higher IoU on BraTS2023, and up to 4.5% Dice and 4.0% IoU gains on BraTS2024. Critically, it also demonstrated a 42.5% reduction in FLOPs compared to U-Net++ while delivering superior accuracy, leading to spatially coherent and high-quality segmentation masks.

Clinical Impact

The enhanced accuracy and spatial coherence of brain tumor segmentations provided by UAMSA-UNet can directly improve clinical workflows by offering more reliable tumor volume and boundary estimations. This leads to more precise surgical planning, optimized radiation therapy dose distribution, and better assessment of tumor progression or regression, ultimately benefiting patient care and potentially reducing treatment-related morbidities. The improved computational efficiency also makes the model more practical for integration into clinical systems.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations of the UAMSA-UNet model itself, but rather highlights its ability to overcome the limitations (noisy boundaries) of existing CNN and U-Net based approaches.

Future Directions

The authors suggest that UAMSA-UNet provides a flexible foundation for future integration with transformer-based modules, aiming for further enhanced segmentation results.

Abstract

Brain tumor segmentation is essential for diagnosis and treatment planning, yet many CNN and U-Net based approaches produce noisy boundaries in regions of tumor infiltration. We introduce UAMSA-UNet, an Uncertainty-Aware Multi-Scale Attention-based Bayesian U-Net that in- stead leverages Monte Carlo Dropout to learn a data-driven smoothing prior over its predictions, while fusing multi-scale features and attention maps to capture both fine details and global context. Our smoothing-regularized loss augments binary cross-entropy with a variance penalty across stochas- tic forward passes, discouraging spurious fluctuations and yielding spatially coherent masks. On BraTS2023, UAMSA- UNet improves Dice Similarity Coefficient by up to 3.3% and mean IoU by up to 2.7% over U-Net; on BraTS2024, it delivers up to 4.5% Dice and 4.0% IoU gains over the best baseline. Remarkably, it also reduces FLOPs by 42.5% rel- ative to U-Net++ while maintaining higher accuracy. These results demonstrate that, by combining multi-scale attention with a learned smoothing prior, UAMSA-UNet achieves both better segmentation quality and computational efficiency, and provides a flexible foundation for future integration with transformer-based modules for further enhanced segmenta- tion results.
