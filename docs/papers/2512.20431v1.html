Skin Lesion Classification Using a Soft Voting Ensemble of Convolutional Neural Networks

Summary

This paper presents a novel method for early skin cancer classification using a soft voting ensemble of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). The approach incorporates comprehensive image preprocessing, transfer learning for accurate lesion segmentation, and an ensemble of MobileNetV2, VGG19, and InceptionV3 for classification, achieving high recognition accuracies across three benchmark dermoscopic datasets.

Medical Relevance

Early and accurate detection of skin cancer is paramount for increasing patient survival rates and guiding timely treatment. This AI-driven diagnostic tool has the potential to significantly enhance dermatologists' capabilities in screening and identifying malignant lesions, thereby improving patient care outcomes.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of an automated system, based on an ensemble of Convolutional Neural Networks, to classify skin lesions from dermoscopic images. Its purpose is to assist in the early and accurate detection of skin cancer, thereby improving diagnostic accuracy for clinicians and potentially increasing patient survival rates through timely intervention.

Key Points

  • The research addresses the critical need for early skin cancer detection to significantly improve patient survival chances.
  • A multi-stage methodology is proposed, involving data rebalancing, image augmentation, and filtering as initial preprocessing steps.
  • Accurate skin lesion segmentation is achieved using a hybrid dual encoder with transfer learning, ensuring classification models focus on clinically significant features and minimize background noise.
  • Classification is performed by a soft voting ensemble of three distinct CNN architectures: MobileNetV2, VGG19, and InceptionV3, chosen for their balance of accuracy and computational efficiency.
  • The model was evaluated on three benchmark datasets: HAM10000, ISIC 2016, and ISIC 2019.
  • Achieved impressive lesion recognition accuracies of 96.32% on HAM10000, 90.86% on ISIC 2016, and 93.92% on ISIC 2019.
  • The ensemble approach aims for robust performance suitable for real-world clinical deployment, validated using established skin lesion detection metrics.

Methodology

The methodology involves several stages: First, three benchmark datasets (HAM10000, ISIC 2016, ISIC 2019) undergo rebalancing, image augmentation, and filtering. Second, a hybrid dual encoder leveraging transfer learning performs accurate segmentation of skin lesions. This segmentation step is critical to focus classification on clinically significant features by reducing background artifacts. Third, an ensemble of three pre-trained CNNs (MobileNetV2, VGG19, and InceptionV3) is used with a soft voting mechanism for the final classification, aiming to balance accuracy with computational efficiency for real-world deployment.

Key Findings

The developed soft voting ensemble CNN model achieved impressive lesion recognition accuracies: 96.32% on the HAM10000 dataset, 90.86% on the ISIC 2016 dataset, and 93.92% on the ISIC 2019 dataset. These results were obtained after meticulous preprocessing and precise lesion segmentation, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed multi-stage ensemble approach.

Its balance of high accuracy and computational speed makes it practical for potential integration into clinical workflows, supporting dermatologists in high-volume screening environments and aiding diagnostic decision-making.

Abstract

Skin cancer can be identified by dermoscopic examination and ocular inspection, but early detection significantly increases survival chances. Artificial intelligence (AI), using annotated skin images and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), improves diagnostic accuracy. This paper presents an early skin cancer classification method using a soft voting ensemble of CNNs. In this investigation, three benchmark datasets, namely HAM10000, ISIC 2016, and ISIC 2019, were used. The process involved rebalancing, image augmentation, and filtering techniques, followed by a hybrid dual encoder for segmentation via transfer learning. Accurate segmentation focused classification models on clinically significant features, reducing background artifacts and improving accuracy. Classification was performed through an ensemble of MobileNetV2, VGG19, and InceptionV3, balancing accuracy and speed for real-world deployment. The method achieved lesion recognition accuracies of 96.32\%, 90.86\%, and 93.92\% for the three datasets. The system performance was evaluated using established skin lesion detection metrics, yielding impressive results.
