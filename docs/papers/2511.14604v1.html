XAttn-BMD: Multimodal Deep Learning with Cross-Attention for Femoral Neck Bone Mineral Density Estimation

arXiv ID: 2511.14604v1
Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Yilin Zhang, Leo D. Westbury, Elaine M. Dennison, Nicholas C. Harvey, Nicholas R. Fuggle, Rahman Attar

Summary

This paper introduces XAttn-BMD, a novel multimodal deep learning framework designed to accurately predict femoral neck Bone Mineral Density (BMD) using hip X-ray images and structured clinical metadata. It employs a bidirectional cross-attention mechanism for dynamic feature integration and a tailored Weighted Smooth L1 loss, significantly outperforming baseline models in BMD estimation robustness and generalization.

Medical Relevance

Poor bone health and low BMD are critical public health concerns, being primary drivers of increased fracture risk and osteoporosis. Accurate and accessible BMD estimation is crucial for early diagnosis, risk stratification, and timely intervention to prevent debilitating fractures.

AI Health Application

The paper describes XAttn-BMD, a multimodal deep learning framework utilizing cross-attention to predict femoral neck BMD from hip X-ray images and clinical metadata. This AI model aims to assist clinicians in the early screening and diagnosis of conditions like osteoporosis, quantify fracture risk, and enhance bone health assessment, thereby improving patient care in real-world clinical scenarios.

Key Points

  • XAttn-BMD is a multimodal deep learning framework predicting femoral neck BMD from hip X-ray images and structured clinical metadata.
  • It incorporates a novel bidirectional cross-attention mechanism for dynamic integration and mutual reinforcement of image and metadata features.
  • A Weighted Smooth L1 loss function is utilized, specifically tailored to address BMD imbalance and prioritize clinically significant cases.
  • Evaluated on data from the Hertfordshire Cohort Study, the model demonstrated superior performance over baseline models in regression generalization and robustness.
  • Ablation studies confirmed the individual effectiveness of both the cross-attention fusion mechanism and the customized loss function.
  • Cross-attention integration of multimodal data led to substantial performance improvements over naive feature concatenation: 16.7% reduction in MSE, 6.03% reduction in MAE, and a 16.4% increase in R2 score.
  • The model's screening performance was evaluated using binary classification at clinically relevant BMD thresholds, highlighting its potential for real-world application in osteoporosis risk assessment.

Methodology

XAttn-BMD is a deep learning framework combining convolutional neural networks for image feature extraction and other networks for processing structured clinical metadata. A novel bidirectional cross-attention mechanism dynamically integrates these disparate feature sets. The model is trained using a customized Weighted Smooth L1 loss function to handle data imbalance and clinical relevance, and evaluated via regression metrics (MSE, MAE, R2) and binary classification for screening.

Key Findings

The XAttn-BMD model significantly outperforms baseline methods in femoral neck BMD estimation, exhibiting improved regression generalization and robustness. The bidirectional cross-attention mechanism is highly effective, reducing MSE by 16.7%, MAE by 6.03%, and increasing the R2 score by 16.4% compared to naive feature concatenation. The tailored Weighted Smooth L1 loss and cross-attention fusion were confirmed as effective components through ablation studies, demonstrating the model's strong potential for clinical screening at relevant BMD thresholds.

Clinical Impact

This model has the potential to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of femoral neck BMD estimation from readily available hip X-ray images and clinical data. Its capability for robust prediction and screening at clinically relevant thresholds could facilitate earlier identification of individuals at high risk for osteoporosis and fractures, enabling timely preventative strategies and improved patient outcomes in real-world clinical settings.

Abstract

Poor bone health is a significant public health concern, and low bone mineral density (BMD) leads to an increased fracture risk, a key feature of osteoporosis. We present XAttn-BMD (Cross-Attention BMD), a multimodal deep learning framework that predicts femoral neck BMD from hip X-ray images and structured clinical metadata. It utilizes a novel bidirectional cross-attention mechanism to dynamically integrate image and metadata features for cross-modal mutual reinforcement. A Weighted Smooth L1 loss is tailored to address BMD imbalance and prioritize clinically significant cases. Extensive experiments on the data from the Hertfordshire Cohort Study show that our model outperforms the baseline models in regression generalization and robustness. Ablation studies confirm the effectiveness of both cross-attention fusion and the customized loss function. Experimental results show that the integration of multimodal data via cross-attention outperforms naive feature concatenation without cross-attention, reducing MSE by 16.7%, MAE by 6.03%, and increasing the R2 score by 16.4%, highlighting the effectiveness of the approach for femoral neck BMD estimation. Furthermore, screening performance was evaluated using binary classification at clinically relevant femoral neck BMD thresholds, demonstrating the model's potential in real-world scenarios.
