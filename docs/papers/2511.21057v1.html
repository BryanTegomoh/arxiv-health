Long-Term Alzheimers Disease Prediction: A Novel Image Generation Method Using Temporal Parameter Estimation with Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution on Uneven Time Series

arXiv ID: 2511.21057v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Xin Hong, Xinze Sun, Yinhao Li, Yen-Wei Chen
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces T-NIG, a novel image generation method for long-term Alzheimer's Disease (AD) prediction that addresses the challenge of irregular time intervals in sequential brain imaging data. T-NIG estimates a temporal parameter within a Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution to model disease characteristic changes over time, enabling the generation of intermediate and future brain images for disease forecasting. The model achieves state-of-the-art performance in both short-term and long-term AD prediction, maintaining crucial disease-related characteristics despite uneven temporal data distribution.

Medical Relevance

This research offers a significant advancement in the non-invasive prediction of Alzheimer's Disease progression, providing physicians with a more reliable and robust imaging-based tool for long-term prognosis and patient management, especially when sequential imaging data is sparse or inconsistently timed.

AI Health Application

This research applies a novel AI-driven image generation and temporal parameter estimation method (T-NIG) to predict the long-term progression of Alzheimer's Disease. By analyzing sequences of brain images, the AI model forecasts future brain states and generates intermediate images, thereby assisting physicians in early diagnosis, understanding disease progression, and potentially informing treatment strategies for AD patients. It addresses the challenge of irregular time intervals in medical sequential data for more accurate long-term health predictions.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical problem of long-term Alzheimer's Disease (AD) prediction using image generation, specifically overcoming difficulties in maintaining disease-related characteristics with irregular time intervals in sequential data.
  • Proposes the T-NIG (Temporal Parameter Estimation within the Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution) model, designed to estimate a temporal parameter to reflect disease changes in unevenly distributed imaging sequences.
  • Utilizes brain images from two distinct time points to generate intermediate and forecast future brain images, thereby assisting in disease prediction.
  • The T-NIG model incorporates a time parameter into the Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution to understand feature evolution, identifying features via coordinate neighborhoods.
  • Employs uncertainty estimation mechanisms to reduce both epistemic (model-inherent) and aleatoric (data-inherent) uncertainties, which commonly arise from insufficient temporal data.
  • Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in both short-term and long-term AD prediction tasks on the evaluated dataset.
  • Proven proficient in accurately forecasting disease progression while robustly maintaining disease-related characteristics, even when faced with irregular temporal data distributions.

Methodology

The T-NIG (Temporal Parameter Estimation within the Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution) model is introduced. It leverages brain images from two time points to generate intermediate and future images for AD prediction. The methodology involves identifying features using coordinate neighborhoods, integrating a time parameter into the Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution to capture feature changes across varying time intervals, and incorporating uncertainty estimation to mitigate epistemic and aleatoric uncertainties originating from insufficient temporal data. This framework allows for the synthetic generation of future brain states that accurately reflect disease progression.

Key Findings

The T-NIG model achieved state-of-the-art performance in both short-term and long-term AD prediction tasks. A key finding is its proficiency in accurately forecasting disease progression while consistently maintaining disease-related characteristics, even when processing irregularly distributed temporal imaging data.

Abstract

Image generation can provide physicians with an imaging diagnosis basis in the prediction of Alzheimer's Disease (AD). Recent research has shown that long-term AD predictions by image generation often face difficulties maintaining disease-related characteristics when dealing with irregular time intervals in sequential data. Considering that the time-related aspects of the distribution can reflect changes in disease-related characteristics when images are distributed unevenly, this research proposes a model to estimate the temporal parameter within the Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution (T-NIG) to assist in generating images over the long term. The T-NIG model employs brain images from two different time points to create intermediate brain images, forecast future images, and predict the disease. T-NIG is designed by identifying features using coordinate neighborhoods. It incorporates a time parameter into the normal inverse gamma distribution to understand how features change in brain imaging sequences that have varying time intervals. Additionally, T-NIG utilizes uncertainty estimation to reduce both epistemic and aleatoric uncertainties in the model, which arise from insufficient temporal data. In particular, the T-NIG model demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in both short-term and long-term prediction tasks within the dataset. Experimental results indicate that T-NIG is proficient in forecasting disease progression while maintaining disease-related characteristics, even when faced with an irregular temporal data distribution.
