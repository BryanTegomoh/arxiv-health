Long-Term Alzheimers Disease Prediction: A Novel Image Generation Method Using Temporal Parameter Estimation with Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution on Uneven Time Series

Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Xin Hong, Xinze Sun, Yinhao Li, Yen-Wei Chen
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00

Summary

This research introduces a novel Temporal Parameter Estimation within the Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution (T-NIG) model for long-term Alzheimer's Disease (AD) prediction. The model addresses the challenge of maintaining disease-related characteristics in image generation from unevenly spaced sequential data by incorporating a time parameter and uncertainty estimation. T-NIG demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in forecasting AD progression and generating future brain images while preserving critical disease features.

Medical Relevance

This method provides physicians with a crucial imaging diagnosis basis for long-term Alzheimer's Disease prediction, enabling earlier and more accurate forecasting of disease progression despite challenges posed by real-world irregular patient visit schedules.

AI Health Application

This research applies AI (specifically, image generation and temporal parameter estimation with Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution) to predict the long-term progression of Alzheimer's Disease from sequential brain imaging data. It aims to assist physicians by providing an imaging diagnosis basis and forecasting disease progression, particularly addressing challenges posed by irregular time intervals in patient data. This constitutes an AI application for medical diagnosis, prognosis, and disease monitoring.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of long-term AD prediction through image generation, specifically tackling the loss of disease characteristics due to irregular time intervals in sequential brain imaging data.
  • Proposes the T-NIG model, which incorporates a temporal parameter within a Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution to understand feature changes in brain imaging sequences with varying time intervals.
  • Utilizes brain images from two distinct time points to generate intermediate images, forecast future images, and ultimately predict AD progression.
  • Employs coordinate neighborhoods for feature identification and integrates uncertainty estimation to mitigate both epistemic and aleatoric uncertainties arising from insufficient temporal data.
  • Achieves state-of-the-art performance in both short-term and long-term AD prediction tasks within the evaluated dataset.
  • Demonstrates proficiency in forecasting disease progression and crucially maintaining disease-related characteristics, even when confronted with irregular temporal data distributions.

Methodology

The T-NIG model uses brain images from two time points to estimate a temporal parameter within a Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution. It identifies features using coordinate neighborhoods and integrates this time parameter to model changes in brain imaging sequences with uneven time intervals. The model also incorporates uncertainty estimation to reduce epistemic and aleatoric uncertainties caused by insufficient temporal data.

Key Findings

The T-NIG model achieved state-of-the-art performance in both short-term and long-term AD prediction tasks. It successfully forecasted disease progression while consistently maintaining disease-related characteristics, even when dealing with irregularly distributed temporal data.

Clinical Impact

This research could significantly enhance the clinical ability to predict AD progression over extended periods, providing a visual basis for diagnosis and prognosis. This improved long-term forecasting could aid in earlier intervention strategies, personalized treatment planning, and better management of patients with or at risk of AD, even with non-uniform clinical imaging schedules.

Abstract

Image generation can provide physicians with an imaging diagnosis basis in the prediction of Alzheimer's Disease (AD). Recent research has shown that long-term AD predictions by image generation often face difficulties maintaining disease-related characteristics when dealing with irregular time intervals in sequential data. Considering that the time-related aspects of the distribution can reflect changes in disease-related characteristics when images are distributed unevenly, this research proposes a model to estimate the temporal parameter within the Normal Inverse Gamma Distribution (T-NIG) to assist in generating images over the long term. The T-NIG model employs brain images from two different time points to create intermediate brain images, forecast future images, and predict the disease. T-NIG is designed by identifying features using coordinate neighborhoods. It incorporates a time parameter into the normal inverse gamma distribution to understand how features change in brain imaging sequences that have varying time intervals. Additionally, T-NIG utilizes uncertainty estimation to reduce both epistemic and aleatoric uncertainties in the model, which arise from insufficient temporal data. In particular, the T-NIG model demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in both short-term and long-term prediction tasks within the dataset. Experimental results indicate that T-NIG is proficient in forecasting disease progression while maintaining disease-related characteristics, even when faced with an irregular temporal data distribution.
