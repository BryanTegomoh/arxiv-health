Unrolled Networks are Conditional Probability Flows in MRI Reconstruction

Summary

This paper establishes a theoretical connection between unrolled networks and conditional probability flow ODEs for MRI reconstruction, proving that unrolled networks are discrete implementations of these flows. Leveraging this insight, the authors propose Flow-Aligned Training (FLAT), a novel method that significantly enhances reconstruction stability and convergence, yielding high-quality MRI images with substantially fewer iterations than diffusion models.

Medical Relevance

This research directly addresses the critical need for faster MRI scans in clinical settings without sacrificing image quality. By improving the stability and efficiency of deep learning reconstruction, it promises to reduce patient scan times, enhance patient comfort and throughput, and potentially broaden MRI accessibility.

This research applies advanced AI techniques (deep learning, unrolled networks, conditional probability flow ODEs) to accelerate and enhance the quality of MRI reconstruction. By improving the stability and convergence of reconstruction algorithms, it aims to enable faster generation of diagnostic-quality images from raw MRI data. This can lead to shorter scan times for patients (by allowing more aggressive data undersampling), improved diagnostic accuracy due to higher quality images, and increased efficiency in clinical workflows, ultimately benefiting patient care and healthcare delivery.

Methodology

The methodology involves a theoretical proof connecting unrolled networks to discrete implementations of conditional probability flow ODEs. Building on this theory, the authors developed Flow-Aligned Training (FLAT), which explicitly derives unrolled network parameters based on ODE discretization principles and aligns the intermediate reconstructions with the ideal trajectory predicted by the ODEs. The proposed method was empirically validated through experiments on three distinct MRI datasets.

Key Findings

The primary theoretical finding is that unrolled networks can be understood as discrete implementations of conditional probability flow ODEs, offering a clearer path for principled parameterization. Practically, the Flow-Aligned Training (FLAT) method, derived from this insight, achieves high-quality MRI reconstructions, demonstrates superior stability compared to existing unrolled networks, and significantly reduces computational cost by requiring up to 3x fewer iterations than diffusion-based generative models.

Clinical Impact

The enhanced efficiency and stability of MRI reconstruction offered by FLAT could lead to substantially shorter MRI scan times, improving patient experience and potentially increasing the number of patients that can be scanned daily. The improved reconstruction quality and reliability could also lead to more accurate and timely diagnoses, particularly crucial in time-sensitive medical conditions or for patients who have difficulty remaining still during long scans.

Abstract

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) offers excellent soft-tissue contrast without ionizing radiation, but its long acquisition time limits clinical utility. Recent methods accelerate MRI by under-sampling $k$-space and reconstructing the resulting images using deep learning. Unrolled networks have been widely used for the reconstruction task due to their efficiency, but suffer from unstable evolving caused by freely-learnable parameters in intermediate steps. In contrast, diffusion models based on stochastic differential equations offer theoretical stability in both medical and natural image tasks but are computationally expensive. In this work, we introduce flow ODEs to MRI reconstruction by theoretically proving that unrolled networks are discrete implementations of conditional probability flow ODEs. This connection provides explicit formulations for parameters and clarifies how intermediate states should evolve. Building on this insight, we propose Flow-Aligned Training (FLAT), which derives unrolled parameters from the ODE discretization and aligns intermediate reconstructions with the ideal ODE trajectory to improve stability and convergence. Experiments on three MRI datasets show that FLAT achieves high-quality reconstructions with up to $3\times$ fewer iterations than diffusion-based generative models and significantly greater stability than unrolled networks.
