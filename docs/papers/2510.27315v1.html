CASR-Net: An Image Processing-focused Deep Learning-based Coronary Artery Segmentation and Refinement Network for X-ray Coronary Angiogram

Summary

CASR-Net is a novel deep learning pipeline designed for automated coronary artery segmentation and refinement from X-ray coronary angiograms, specifically addressing challenges posed by poor image quality and narrow vessel continuity. This three-stage network integrates advanced preprocessing, a UNet-based segmentation model with a DenseNet121 encoder and Self-ONN decoder, and a contour refinement module. The system demonstrates superior performance compared to state-of-the-art models, making it a robust tool for supporting clinical diagnosis and treatment planning for Coronary Artery Disease (CAD).

Medical Relevance

Early and accurate detection of coronary artery disease (CAD), including the identification of stenotic arteries, is critical for reducing patient mortality and guiding effective treatment strategies. This automated segmentation tool can significantly enhance the precision and efficiency of analyzing angiographic images, directly improving clinical diagnostic capabilities.

The paper presents CASR-Net, an AI-powered image processing and deep learning model. This model automatically segments and refines coronary arteries from X-ray angiograms, aiming to assist clinicians in the early detection and diagnosis of Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) and to improve patient treatment planning by providing precise anatomical information.

Key Points

  • CASR-Net is a three-stage deep learning pipeline comprising image preprocessing, segmentation, and refinement, specifically targeting X-ray coronary angiograms.
  • A novel multichannel preprocessing strategy combines CLAHE (Contrast Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization) and an improved Ben Graham method, leading to incremental performance gains (0.31-0.89% increase in DSC, 0.40-1.16% in IoU) compared to individual techniques.
  • The core segmentation network is built on a UNet architecture, featuring a DenseNet121 encoder for robust feature extraction.
  • The decoder component of the UNet is based on a Self-organized Operational Neural Network (Self-ONN), which is specifically designed to preserve the continuity of narrow and stenotic vessel branches.
  • A final contour refinement module is incorporated to effectively suppress false positives in the segmented outputs.
  • The model was rigorously evaluated using 5-fold cross-validation on a combined dataset of two public resources, including both healthy and stenotic arteries.
  • CASR-Net achieved an IoU of 61.43%, a DSC of 76.10%, and a clDice of 79.36%, outperforming several state-of-the-art models in coronary artery segmentation.

Methodology

The CASR-Net employs a three-stage pipeline. The first stage involves a novel multichannel preprocessing technique combining CLAHE and an improved Ben Graham method for image enhancement. The second stage is the primary segmentation network, a UNet-based architecture where the encoder is a DenseNet121 and the decoder is built upon a Self-organized Operational Neural Network (Self-ONN), specifically designed to maintain vessel continuity. The final stage is a contour refinement module aimed at reducing false positives. The model was trained and validated using 5-fold cross-validation on two public datasets containing diverse artery conditions.

Key Findings

CASR-Net demonstrated superior performance in coronary artery segmentation, achieving an Intersection over Union (IoU) of 61.43%, a Dice Score Coefficient (DSC) of 76.10%, and a centerline Dice (clDice) of 79.36%, thereby outperforming several existing state-of-the-art models. The integrated multichannel preprocessing strategy yielded tangible improvements, increasing DSC by 0.31-0.89% and IoU by 0.40-1.16%. The network's design, particularly the Self-ONN decoder, proved effective in preserving the continuity of narrow and stenotic vessel branches.

Clinical Impact

This automated and robust coronary artery segmentation tool has the potential to significantly aid clinicians in the early and accurate diagnosis of Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) by providing high-quality segmentation of coronary arteries from X-ray angiograms. It can streamline the identification of stenotic regions, support more precise treatment planning, and ultimately contribute to improved patient outcomes and reduced mortality associated with CAD.

Abstract

Early detection of coronary artery disease (CAD) is critical for reducing mortality and improving patient treatment planning. While angiographic image analysis from X-rays is a common and cost-effective method for identifying cardiac abnormalities, including stenotic coronary arteries, poor image quality can significantly impede clinical diagnosis. We present the Coronary Artery Segmentation and Refinement Network (CASR-Net), a three-stage pipeline comprising image preprocessing, segmentation, and refinement. A novel multichannel preprocessing strategy combining CLAHE and an improved Ben Graham method provides incremental gains, increasing Dice Score Coefficient (DSC) by 0.31-0.89% and Intersection over Union (IoU) by 0.40-1.16% compared with using the techniques individually. The core innovation is a segmentation network built on a UNet with a DenseNet121 encoder and a Self-organized Operational Neural Network (Self-ONN) based decoder, which preserves the continuity of narrow and stenotic vessel branches. A final contour refinement module further suppresses false positives. Evaluated with 5-fold cross-validation on a combination of two public datasets that contain both healthy and stenotic arteries, CASR-Net outperformed several state-of-the-art models, achieving an IoU of 61.43%, a DSC of 76.10%, and clDice of 79.36%. These results highlight a robust approach to automated coronary artery segmentation, offering a valuable tool to support clinicians in diagnosis and treatment planning.
