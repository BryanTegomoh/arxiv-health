CASR-Net: An Image Processing-focused Deep Learning-based Coronary Artery Segmentation and Refinement Network for X-ray Coronary Angiogram

Summary

CASR-Net is a novel deep learning-based three-stage pipeline developed for accurate and robust coronary artery segmentation and refinement in X-ray angiograms, specifically designed to address challenges like poor image quality and stenotic vessels. It integrates a unique multichannel preprocessing strategy, a UNet with a DenseNet121 encoder and a Self-ONN based decoder, and a contour refinement module. The model achieved state-of-the-art performance, outperforming existing methods in segmenting both healthy and stenotic arteries, thus offering a valuable clinical tool.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated segmentation of coronary arteries from X-ray angiograms is paramount for the early detection and precise characterization of Coronary Artery Disease (CAD). This technology directly aids clinicians in identifying stenotic regions, improving diagnostic speed, and facilitating better-informed treatment planning, which can significantly reduce morbidity and mortality associated with cardiac abnormalities.

AI Health Application

The AI system, CASR-Net, is a deep learning-based model designed for automated segmentation and refinement of coronary arteries from X-ray coronary angiograms. This application provides a valuable tool to assist clinicians in the accurate and early identification of cardiac abnormalities, specifically stenotic coronary arteries, thereby improving clinical diagnosis of CAD and facilitating more effective patient treatment planning.

Key Points

  • **Three-stage Architecture:** CASR-Net operates through a pipeline comprising image preprocessing, deep learning-based segmentation, and a final contour refinement module.
  • **Novel Multichannel Preprocessing:** A unique strategy combining CLAHE (Contrast Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization) and an improved Ben Graham method provides significant incremental gains in segmentation metrics (DSC: 0.31-0.89%, IoU: 0.40-1.16%).
  • **Advanced Segmentation Network:** The core segmentation network is a UNet variant featuring a DenseNet121 encoder and a Self-organized Operational Neural Network (Self-ONN) based decoder, specifically engineered to preserve the continuity of narrow and stenotic vessel branches.
  • **False Positive Suppression:** A dedicated contour refinement module is implemented as the final stage to further enhance accuracy by suppressing false positive segmentations.
  • **Robust Evaluation:** The model was rigorously evaluated using 5-fold cross-validation on a combined dataset that includes both healthy and stenotic arteries, ensuring comprehensive assessment.
  • **Superior Performance:** CASR-Net achieved an Intersection over Union (IoU) of 61.43%, a Dice Score Coefficient (DSC) of 76.10%, and a centerline Dice (clDice) of 79.36%, demonstrating superior performance compared to several state-of-the-art models.
  • **Clinical Support Tool:** The robust and automated segmentation capabilities position CASR-Net as a valuable tool to support clinicians in the early diagnosis and treatment planning for Coronary Artery Disease (CAD).

Methodology

CASR-Net utilizes a three-stage deep learning pipeline. The first stage involves a novel multichannel preprocessing strategy combining CLAHE and an improved Ben Graham method to enhance image quality. The second stage is the primary segmentation network, a UNet architecture with a DenseNet121 encoder for robust feature extraction and a Self-ONN based decoder designed to maintain vessel continuity, especially for narrow and stenotic branches. The final stage incorporates a contour refinement module to suppress false positives. The model was trained and validated using 5-fold cross-validation on public datasets containing both healthy and stenotic coronary arteries.

Key Findings

The CASR-Net achieved an IoU of 61.43%, DSC of 76.10%, and clDice of 79.36%, outperforming existing state-of-the-art models. The novel multichannel preprocessing strategy alone provided significant performance gains, increasing DSC by 0.31-0.89% and IoU by 0.40-1.16%. These results confirm CASR-Net's robust ability to accurately segment coronary arteries, including challenging stenotic regions, from X-ray angiograms.

Clinical Impact

This research provides a powerful automated tool that can significantly enhance the clinical diagnosis and treatment planning for Coronary Artery Disease (CAD). By delivering highly accurate and robust segmentation of coronary arteries from X-ray angiograms, CASR-Net can assist clinicians in rapidly and reliably identifying stenotic regions, thereby enabling earlier interventions, optimizing treatment strategies, and potentially improving patient outcomes and reducing healthcare costs associated with late diagnoses.

Abstract

Early detection of coronary artery disease (CAD) is critical for reducing mortality and improving patient treatment planning. While angiographic image analysis from X-rays is a common and cost-effective method for identifying cardiac abnormalities, including stenotic coronary arteries, poor image quality can significantly impede clinical diagnosis. We present the Coronary Artery Segmentation and Refinement Network (CASR-Net), a three-stage pipeline comprising image preprocessing, segmentation, and refinement. A novel multichannel preprocessing strategy combining CLAHE and an improved Ben Graham method provides incremental gains, increasing Dice Score Coefficient (DSC) by 0.31-0.89% and Intersection over Union (IoU) by 0.40-1.16% compared with using the techniques individually. The core innovation is a segmentation network built on a UNet with a DenseNet121 encoder and a Self-organized Operational Neural Network (Self-ONN) based decoder, which preserves the continuity of narrow and stenotic vessel branches. A final contour refinement module further suppresses false positives. Evaluated with 5-fold cross-validation on a combination of two public datasets that contain both healthy and stenotic arteries, CASR-Net outperformed several state-of-the-art models, achieving an IoU of 61.43%, a DSC of 76.10%, and clDice of 79.36%. These results highlight a robust approach to automated coronary artery segmentation, offering a valuable tool to support clinicians in diagnosis and treatment planning.
