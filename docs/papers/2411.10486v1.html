Summary

This paper offers a comprehensive review of Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), and Deep Learning (DL) applications in predicting and preventing infectious diseases. It categorizes existing contributions based on their data sources (Public Health Data, Patients' Medical Data, or both) and corresponding research objectives, while critically assessing challenges related to data types, learning methods, and the inherent limitations of AI in this domain.

Medical Relevance

This review is highly relevant to medicine and public health by synthesizing how AI can enhance infectious disease surveillance, improve early diagnostic capabilities, and inform targeted preventative interventions, ultimately contributing to better outbreak control and patient management.

AI Health Application

The paper reviews the application of AI, specifically machine learning and deep learning, for predicting and preventing infectious diseases. This includes using public health data for regional spread prevention, patient medical data for individual infection detection, and a combination of both for population-level disease spread estimation, all contributing to infectious disease management and biosecurity.

Key Points

  • The review identifies significant advancements in AI, ML, and DL for infectious disease prediction and prevention, acknowledging their growing impact.
  • Contributions are categorized into three areas based on data usage and objective: (1) using Public Health Data to prevent regional disease spread, (2) using Patients' Medical Data to detect individual infections, and (3) combining Public and Patient data to estimate population-level disease spread.
  • The paper highlights ongoing conflicts and discussions within the field regarding the appropriate types of data to be used and the most effective learning methods to be applied for specific research objectives.
  • It provides an overview of current accomplishments while also critically outlining the potential and specific limitations of AI technologies in infectious disease management.
  • The analysis underscores the importance of resolving data and methodological inconsistencies to further advance AI applications in epidemiology and clinical diagnostics.
  • The review serves as a guide for researchers and practitioners on the current state, challenges, and future prospects of AI in combating infectious diseases.

Methodology

The paper employs a comprehensive literature review methodology, systematically analyzing and synthesizing existing research on AI, ML, and DL applications in infectious disease prediction and prevention. It categorizes these contributions based on the types of data utilized (public health, patient medical, or combined) and their specific objectives, followed by a critical assessment of the field's challenges and the inherent limitations of AI.

Key Findings

Key findings include the effective application of AI across three distinct data-driven approaches for infectious disease management: regional spread prevention (using public data), individual infection detection (using patient data), and population spread estimation (using both). Despite these successes, the review highlights persistent challenges and ongoing debates concerning the optimal selection of data types and appropriate learning methods, underscoring both the immense potential and notable limitations of AI in this critical domain.

Clinical Impact

The insights from this review can inform the development of more accurate and efficient AI-powered diagnostic tools for infectious diseases, enhance early warning systems for public health authorities to predict and contain outbreaks, and optimize resource allocation for prevention strategies, leading to improved patient outcomes and reduced public health burdens.

Limitations

The paper explicitly notes existing conflicts and ongoing discussions within the field regarding the suitability and exploitation of different data types (e.g., public vs. patient) and the applicability of various machine learning and deep learning methods for specific predictive tasks. It also critically assesses and outlines the inherent limitations of current AI technologies in the complex context of infectious disease management.

Future Directions

Implicitly, the review suggests future research should focus on resolving the identified conflicts related to the optimal selection and utilization of data types and the most effective application of learning methodologies for specific infectious disease prediction and prevention objectives. Further work is needed to address the outlined limitations of current AI approaches to fully leverage their potential in this field.

Abstract

Artificial Intelligence (AI) and infectious diseases prediction have recently experienced a common development and advancement. Machine learning (ML) apparition, along with deep learning (DL) emergence, extended many approaches against diseases apparition and their spread. And despite their outstanding results in predicting infectious diseases, conflicts appeared regarding the types of data used and how they can be studied, analyzed, and exploited using various emerging methods. This has led to some ongoing discussions in the field. This research aims not only to provide an overview of what has been accomplished, but also to highlight the difficulties related to the types of data used, and the learning methods applied for each research objective. It categorizes these contributions into three areas: predictions using Public Health Data to prevent the spread of a transmissible disease within a region; predictions using Patients' Medical Data to detect whether a person is infected by a transmissible disease; and predictions using both Public and patient medical data to estimate the extent of disease spread in a population. The paper also critically assesses the potential of AI and outlines its limitations in infectious disease management.
