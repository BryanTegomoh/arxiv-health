Summary

This comprehensive review analyzes the advancements of Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), and Deep Learning (DL) in infectious disease prediction and prevention. It highlights significant challenges concerning the types of data utilized and the appropriate learning methods applied, categorizing existing research into three distinct areas based on data source and prediction objective. The paper critically assesses AI's potential and outlines its limitations in the context of infectious disease management.

Medical Relevance

This review is crucial for guiding the development and deployment of AI solutions in public health and clinical settings, enabling more effective disease surveillance, early detection of infections in individuals, and comprehensive epidemiological modeling to prevent outbreaks and manage transmissible diseases.

AI Health Application

The paper focuses on using AI (ML, DL) for predicting infectious disease outbreaks, forecasting disease spread within regions and populations, detecting individual infections from patient medical data, and supporting the overall management and prevention of transmissible diseases.

Key Points

  • AI, ML, and DL have made significant advancements in predicting infectious diseases and their spread.
  • Despite promising results, there are ongoing conflicts and discussions regarding the types of data used and the optimal methods for their study, analysis, and exploitation.
  • The review provides an overview of existing accomplishments and highlights difficulties related to data types and the suitability of learning methods for specific research objectives.
  • Contributions are categorized into three main areas: predictions using Public Health Data for regional spread prevention, predictions using Patients' Medical Data for individual infection detection, and predictions using both Public and Patient Medical Data for estimating population-level disease spread.
  • The paper critically assesses the overall potential of AI in infectious disease management.
  • It outlines specific limitations of AI applications in the context of infectious disease prediction and prevention.

Methodology

The paper employs a comprehensive literature review methodology, surveying existing research on AI, ML, and DL applications in infectious disease prediction. It systematically categorizes these contributions based on the type of data utilized (public health, patient medical, or combined) and their specific prediction objectives. The review also includes a critical assessment of the state-of-the-art, identifying common challenges and limitations.

Key Findings

The review identifies that while AI, ML, and DL offer outstanding results in infectious disease prediction, significant challenges persist concerning the appropriate types of data and the selection of optimal learning methods. It systematically categorizes current research into three distinct applications based on data source and prediction target: regional spread prevention (public health data), individual infection detection (patient data), and population-level spread estimation (combined data).

Clinical Impact

This review informs clinicians, public health officials, and researchers about the current capabilities and critical gaps in AI-driven infectious disease management. It can guide the development of more targeted diagnostic tools, predictive models for outbreak forecasting, and resource allocation strategies, ultimately leading to earlier interventions, improved patient outcomes, and more efficient public health responses.

Limitations

The abstract explicitly notes ongoing "conflicts regarding the types of data used and how they can be studied, analyzed, and exploited" by various emerging AI methods. It also highlights "difficulties related to the types of data used, and the learning methods applied for each research objective," in addition to a critical assessment of AI's inherent limitations in infectious disease management.

Future Directions

While not explicitly detailed as "future directions" in the abstract, the identified challenges (conflicts over data types, appropriate learning methods, and inherent AI limitations) implicitly suggest future research should focus on developing standardized data utilization practices, tailoring AI algorithms to specific data characteristics and disease contexts, and addressing ethical and practical limitations in AI deployment for infectious disease management.

Abstract

Artificial Intelligence (AI) and infectious diseases prediction have recently experienced a common development and advancement. Machine learning (ML) apparition, along with deep learning (DL) emergence, extended many approaches against diseases apparition and their spread. And despite their outstanding results in predicting infectious diseases, conflicts appeared regarding the types of data used and how they can be studied, analyzed, and exploited using various emerging methods. This has led to some ongoing discussions in the field. This research aims not only to provide an overview of what has been accomplished, but also to highlight the difficulties related to the types of data used, and the learning methods applied for each research objective. It categorizes these contributions into three areas: predictions using Public Health Data to prevent the spread of a transmissible disease within a region; predictions using Patients' Medical Data to detect whether a person is infected by a transmissible disease; and predictions using both Public and patient medical data to estimate the extent of disease spread in a population. The paper also critically assesses the potential of AI and outlines its limitations in infectious disease management.
