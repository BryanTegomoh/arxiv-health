Human-like AI-based Auto-Field-in-Field Whole-Brain Radiotherapy Treatment Planning With Conversation Large Language Model Feedback

Published: 2026-01-02
Authors: Adnan Jafar, An Qin, Gavin Atkins, Xiaoyu Hu, Yin Gao, Xun Jia

Summary

This study introduces an automated whole-brain radiotherapy (WBRT) planning pipeline that integrates a deep learning (DL) model for patient-specific hyperparameter prediction and a large language model (LLM)-based conversational interface for interactive plan refinement. The system demonstrated high clinical acceptability, significantly reduced planning time compared to manual methods, and achieved dosimetric quality equivalent to clinical plans for critical structures, with the LLM successfully improving suboptimal plans.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to radiation oncology as it offers a solution to significantly automate and optimize WBRT planning, potentially leading to more efficient, consistent, and patient-specific treatments while reducing the workload on medical physicists and dosimetrists.

AI Health Application

This research applies AI (Deep Learning and Large Language Models) to automate and optimize whole-brain radiotherapy treatment planning. A DL model predicts patient-specific planning parameters, and an LLM-based conversational interface allows clinicians to provide feedback and refine treatment plans interactively, thereby improving plan quality, efficiency, and clinical workflow for cancer treatment.

Key Points

  • A novel automated WBRT planning pipeline combines a DL model for initial parameter prediction with an LLM for conversational plan refinement.
  • The DL Hyperparameter Prediction model was trained on 55 WBRT cases using geometric features of CTV and OARs to determine optimal Auto-FiF settings in the RayStation treatment planning system.
  • The LLM-based conversational module utilizes voice input (Whisper) interpreted by GPT-4o to adjust planning settings based on quality feedback for suboptimal plans.
  • 14 out of 15 DL-generated plans in an independent test set were clinically acceptable.
  • DL-generated plans, when normalized to identical CTV D95% as clinical plans, showed no statistically significant differences in doses to the eyes, lenses, or CTV dose metrics D1% and D99%.
  • The automated planning significantly reduced workflow time: under 1 minute for computation and approximately 7 minutes total execution, compared to 15 minutes for manual planning.
  • The conversational module effectively improved dose conformity and reduced hotspots in plans requiring adjustment, demonstrating successful interactive refinement.
  • Model explainability was supported through analysis of feature importance, contributing to transparency in AI-driven planning.

Methodology

The study developed a two-stage automated WBRT planning pipeline. The first stage involved training a deep learning (DL) model on geometric features of clinical target volume (CTV) and organs at risk (OARs) from 55 WBRT cases to predict optimal Auto-FiF hyperparameters for the RayStation treatment planning system. The second stage integrated an LLM-based conversational interface, where voice input feedback (transcribed by Whisper) was interpreted by GPT-4o to adjust planning settings for suboptimal plans. The system was evaluated on 15 independent cases using clinical dosimetric metrics, expert review, and analysis of workflow timing.

Key Findings

The DL-based hyperparameter prediction module successfully generated clinically acceptable plans in 14 of 15 independent cases. Normalized to identical CTV D95%, these plans showed no statistically significant differences in doses to critical OARs (eyes, lenses) and CTV dose metrics (D1%, D99%) compared to clinical plans. The automated workflow dramatically reduced planning time to approximately 7 minutes total execution (under 1 minute computation) from 15 minutes for manual planning. Furthermore, the LLM-driven conversational module effectively improved dose conformity and reduced hotspots in plans identified as suboptimal.

Clinical Impact

This automated WBRT planning system has the potential to significantly enhance clinical efficiency by reducing planning time and manual intervention, allowing medical physicists and dosimetrists to focus on more complex cases. It promises to deliver consistent, patient-specific, and high-quality radiotherapy plans, potentially improving treatment outcomes and access to care, while offering an interactive refinement mechanism for expert oversight.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations. However, potential limitations include the relatively small dataset size used for training the DL model (55 cases) and for independent evaluation (15 cases), which may impact the generalizability across diverse patient populations and institutions. The reliance on a specific treatment planning system (RayStation) and a commercial LLM (GPT-4o) also implies potential dependencies or integration challenges in other clinical environments.

Future Directions

While not explicitly stated in the abstract, potential future directions could include expanding the training and validation datasets to improve model robustness and generalizability, investigating the application of this AI-driven pipeline to other complex anatomical sites or treatment modalities, and conducting prospective clinical trials to fully assess its impact on patient outcomes and long-term clinical workflows.

Abstract

Whole-brain radiotherapy (WBRT) is a common treatment due to its simplicity and effectiveness. While automated Field-in-Field (Auto-FiF) functions assist WBRT planning in modern treatment planning systems, it still requires manual approaches for optimal plan generation including patient-specific hyperparameters definition and plan refinement based on quality feedback. This study introduces an automated WBRT planning pipeline that integrates a deep learning (DL) Hyperparameter Prediction model for patient-specific parameter generation and a large-language model (LLM)-based conversational interface for interactive plan refinement. The Hyperparameter Prediction module was trained on 55 WBRT cases using geometric features of clinical target volume (CTV) and organs at risk (OARs) to determine optimal Auto-FiF settings in RayStation treatment planning system. Plans were generated under predicted hyperparameters. For cases in which the generated plan was suboptimal, quality feedback via voice input was captured by a Conversation module, transcribed using Whisper, and interpreted by GPT-4o to adjust planning settings. Plan quality was evaluated in 15 independent cases using clinical metrics and expert review, and model explainability was supported through analysis of feature importance. Fourteen of 15 DL-generated plans were clinically acceptable. Normalized to identical CTV D95% as the clinical plans, the DL-generated and clinical plans showed no statistically significant differences in doses to the eyes, lenses, or CTV dose metrics D1% and D99%. The DL-based planning required under 1 minute of computation and achieved total workflow execution in approximately 7 minutes with a single mouse click, compared to 15 minutes for manual planning. In cases requiring adjustment, the Conversational module successfully improved dose conformity and hotspot reduction.
