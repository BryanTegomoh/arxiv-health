Summary

This paper evaluates a novel Œ≤-Ga$_2$O$_3$ metal-semiconductor-metal (MSM) detector combined with low-noise amplification for dosimetry in intensity-modulated proton therapy (IMPT). The study demonstrates the detector's linear response to proton dose and its accurate tracking of varying proton energies, matching established ionization chamber data and simulated distributions, positioning it as a promising advancement for validating complex IMPT treatment plans.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and comprehensive dosimetry is critical for patient safety and treatment efficacy in proton therapy, ensuring that cancerous tumors receive the prescribed radiation dose while minimizing damage to surrounding healthy tissues. This research offers a potential solution to current dosimetry limitations, leading to safer and more precise cancer treatments.

Methodology

The study involved an experimental evaluation of a Œ≤-Ga$_2$O$_3$ MSM detector with a low-noise amplifier. The detector was exposed to various proton radiation doses and energy levels delivered by a MEVION S250i proton accelerator. Its performance was then quantitatively compared against a standard ionization chamber and simulated dose distributions for linearity and energy response.

Key Findings

The Œ≤-Ga$_2$O$_3$ MSM detector demonstrated a linear response to proton dose for single-spot irradiations. Furthermore, its response to varying proton energies showed excellent agreement with both data obtained from a reference ionization chamber and established simulated dose distributions, indicating high accuracy and reliability for proton dosimetry.

Abstract

Intensity-modulated proton therapy (IMPT) employs proton radiation rather than conventional X-rays to treat cancerous tumors. This approach offers significant advantages by minimizing the radiation exposure of surrounding healthy tissue, leading to improved patient outcomes and reduced side effects compared to traditional X-ray therapy. To ensure patient safety, each treatment plan must be experimentally validated before clinical implementation. However, current dosimetry devices face limitations in performing angled beam measurements and obtaining multi-depth assessments, both of which are essential for verifying IMPT treatment plans. In this study, the performance of a Œ≤-Ga$_2$O$_3$-based metal-semiconductor-metal (MSM) detector with a low-noise amplifier is studied and evaluated under various proton radiation doses and energy levels delivered by a MEVION S250i proton accelerator. The detector performance is also compared with that of an ionization chamber. The Œ≤-Ga$_2$O$_3$ detector exhibits a linear response with proton dose for single-spot irradiations, and its response to varying proton energies closely matches both the ion chamber data and simulated dose distributions. These findings highlight the potential of Œ≤-Ga$_2$O$_3$-based detectors as robust dosimetry devices for IMPT applications.
