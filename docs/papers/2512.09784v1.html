Predicting Polymer Solubility in Solvents Using SMILES Strings

Published: 2025-12-10
Authors: Andrew Reinhard
Categories: cs.LG

Summary

This paper presents a deep learning framework designed to predict polymer solubility (expressed as weight percent) directly from the SMILES representations of both polymers and solvents. Utilizing a dataset of 8,049 polymer-solvent pairs and a fully connected neural network, the model achieved strong agreement with actual solubility values. The framework demonstrated robust generalizability on 25 unseen experimental combinations, validating its potential for scalable solubility prediction and high-throughput solvent screening, particularly relevant for pharmaceutical formulation.

Medical Relevance

Predicting polymer solubility is critically important for the design and optimization of various medical products, including drug delivery systems, pharmaceutical formulations, and implantable biomaterials. This enables precise control over drug release kinetics and material biocompatibility, directly impacting therapeutic efficacy and patient safety.

Methodology

A deep learning framework, featuring a six-hidden-layer fully connected neural network, was developed. It was trained on an 8,049-sample dataset of polymer-solvent pairs, where each pair was transformed into a 2,394-feature vector by combining molecular descriptors and fingerprints derived from SMILES strings. The model was optimized using the Adam optimizer and assessed with mean squared error loss, with generalizability evaluated against independent experimental data.

The deep learning model accurately predicted polymer solubility (wt%) with strong agreement to ground truth values. Crucially, it maintained high accuracy and demonstrated excellent generalizability when applied to 25 previously unseen experimental polymer-solvent combinations, confirming the viability of SMILES-based machine learning for this task.

Abstract

Understanding and predicting polymer solubility in various solvents is critical for applications ranging from recycling to pharmaceutical formulation. This work presents a deep learning framework that predicts polymer solubility, expressed as weight percent (wt%), directly from SMILES representations of both polymers and solvents. A dataset of 8,049 polymer solvent pairs at 25 deg C was constructed from calibrated molecular dynamics simulations (Zhou et al., 2023), and molecular descriptors and fingerprints were combined into a 2,394 feature representation per sample. A fully connected neural network with six hidden layers was trained using the Adam optimizer and evaluated using mean squared error loss, achieving strong agreement between predicted and actual solubility values. Generalizability was demonstrated using experimentally measured data from the Materials Genome Project, where the model maintained high accuracy on 25 unseen polymer solvent combinations. These findings highlight the viability of SMILES based machine learning models for scalable solubility prediction and high-throughput solvent screening, supporting applications in green chemistry, polymer processing, and materials design.
