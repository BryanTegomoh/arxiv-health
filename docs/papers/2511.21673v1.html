Revolutionizing Glioma Segmentation & Grading Using 3D MRI - Guided Hybrid Deep Learning Models

arXiv ID: 2511.21673v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Pandiyaraju V, Sreya Mynampati, Abishek Karthik, Poovarasan L, D. Saraswathi
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00

Summary

This paper introduces a novel hybrid deep learning framework for the accurate segmentation and grading of gliomas from 3D MRI scans. The model integrates a U-Net for precise tumor demarcation and a DenseNet-VGG classification network equipped with multihead and spatial-channel attention mechanisms. It achieved a 98% Dice coefficient for segmentation and 99% accuracy for classification, significantly outperforming traditional methods.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to neuro-oncology and diagnostic radiology by offering a robust, AI-powered tool that can significantly improve the speed and accuracy of glioma diagnosis and grading, thereby enabling more timely and effective patient treatment planning.

AI Health Application

The paper describes a medical AI application for automated and precise segmentation and grading of brain tumors (gliomas) from 3D MRI scans. This AI model aims to assist clinicians in faster, more accurate diagnosis and contribute to better treatment planning for patients, thereby improving health outcomes.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: High mortality rate of gliomas necessitates early and accurate diagnosis and grading for effective therapeutic intervention.
  • **Hybrid Architecture**: The proposed model combines a 3D U-Net for tumor segmentation and a hybrid DenseNet-VGG network for classification.
  • **Attention Mechanisms**: Multihead attention and spatial-channel attention are incorporated into the classification network to focus on clinically relevant features within the demarcated tumor regions.
  • **3D MRI Data Utilization**: The framework effectively processes high-dimensional 3D MRI data through crucial preprocessing steps including normalization, resampling, and data augmentation.
  • **Performance Metrics**: Evaluated using Dice coefficient and Mean IoU for segmentation, and accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score for classification.
  • **Superior Performance**: Achieved a Dice coefficient of 98% for tumor segmentation and 99% classification accuracy, surpassing traditional CNN models and attention-free approaches.
  • **Enhanced Interpretability**: The use of multi-head attention mechanisms not only improves accuracy but also enhances the interpretability by prioritizing clinically significant aspects of the tumor.

Methodology

The methodology involves a hybrid deep learning architecture. First, a U-Net based model is employed for precise 3D volumetric segmentation of glioma tumors from MRI data, guided by spatial and contextual information. Subsequently, a hybrid classification network, combining branches of DenseNet and VGG, processes the segmented tumor regions. This classification network is augmented with multihead attention and spatial-channel attention capabilities to identify and focus on clinically relevant features for grading. High-dimensional 3D MRI data undergo preprocessing steps including normalization, resampling, and data augmentation before model training and evaluation.

Key Findings

The hybrid deep learning framework demonstrated exceptional performance, achieving a Dice coefficient of 98% for glioma tumor segmentation and a 99% classification accuracy. This performance significantly surpasses that of traditional CNN models and methods lacking attention mechanisms. The integration of multi-head attention mechanisms was found to enhance both the accuracy and interpretability of the model, specifically by prioritizing clinically significant aspects of the tumor.

Clinical Impact

This framework holds promising potential for clinical adoption by enabling clinicians to achieve more timely and reliable diagnosis and grading of gliomas. This enhanced diagnostic capability could lead to improved patient stratification, more precise treatment planning, and ultimately, better patient outcomes in neuro-oncology. The interpretability offered by attention mechanisms could also foster greater trust and understanding of AI-driven diagnostics among medical professionals.

Abstract

Gliomas are brain tumor types that have a high mortality rate which means early and accurate diagnosis is important for therapeutic intervention for the tumors. To address this difficulty, the proposed research will develop a hybrid deep learning model which integrates U-Net based segmentation and a hybrid DenseNet-VGG classification network with multihead attention and spatial-channel attention capabilities. The segmentation model will precisely demarcate the tumors in a 3D volume of MRI data guided by spatial and contextual information. The classification network which combines a branch of both DenseNet and VGG, will incorporate the demarcated tumor on which features with attention mechanisms would be focused on clinically relevant features. High-dimensional 3D MRI data could successfully be utilized in the model through preprocessing steps which are normalization, resampling, and data augmentation. Through a variety of measures the framework is evaluated: measures of performance in segmentation are Dice coefficient and Mean Intersection over Union (IoU) and measures of performance in classification are accuracy precision, recall, and F1-score. The hybrid framework that has been proposed has demonstrated through physical testing that it has the capability of obtaining a Dice coefficient of 98% in tumor segmentation, and 99% on classification accuracy, outperforming traditional CNN models and attention-free methods. Utilizing multi-head attention mechanisms enhances notions of priority in aspects of the tumor that are clinically significant, and enhances interpretability and accuracy. The results suggest a great potential of the framework in facilitating the timely and reliable diagnosis and grading of glioma by clinicians is promising, allowing for better planning of patient treatment.
