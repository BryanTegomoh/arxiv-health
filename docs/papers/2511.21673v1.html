Revolutionizing Glioma Segmentation & Grading Using 3D MRI - Guided Hybrid Deep Learning Models

arXiv ID: 2511.21673v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Pandiyaraju V, Sreya Mynampati, Abishek Karthik, Poovarasan L, D. Saraswathi
Categories: cs.CV

Summary

This paper introduces a hybrid deep learning framework for accurate glioma segmentation and grading from 3D MRI data, integrating a U-Net for precise tumor demarcation and a DenseNet-VGG classifier with multihead and spatial-channel attention. The model achieved a 98% Dice coefficient for segmentation and 99% classification accuracy, significantly outperforming traditional methods and demonstrating potential for improved clinical diagnosis and treatment planning.

Medical Relevance

This research offers a significant advancement in automated glioma diagnosis and grading, which is critical for early therapeutic intervention and personalized treatment strategies for a highly aggressive brain tumor, potentially improving patient survival rates.

AI Health Application

This research applies deep learning (a subfield of AI) to medical imaging (3D MRI) for the purpose of automated or AI-assisted diagnosis and grading of brain tumors (gliomas). It specifically focuses on image segmentation to identify tumor boundaries and classification to determine tumor type/grade, which are critical steps in clinical decision-making and treatment planning for patients with brain cancer.

Key Points

  • Addresses the high mortality rate of gliomas by developing a hybrid deep learning model for early and accurate diagnosis and grading.
  • The framework integrates a U-Net based segmentation model for precise 3D tumor demarcation and a hybrid DenseNet-VGG network for classification.
  • Incorporates multihead attention and spatial-channel attention mechanisms to focus on clinically relevant features, enhancing interpretability and accuracy.
  • Utilizes 3D MRI data, undergoing preprocessing steps including normalization, resampling, and data augmentation to handle high-dimensional data.
  • Achieved a Dice coefficient of 98% for tumor segmentation and 99% classification accuracy for glioma grading.
  • Demonstrated superior performance compared to traditional CNN models and attention-free methods, highlighting the efficacy of the attention mechanisms.
  • Aims to facilitate timely and reliable diagnosis and grading of glioma by clinicians, enabling better patient treatment planning and potentially improving outcomes.

Methodology

The methodology involves a hybrid deep learning architecture. It begins with preprocessing high-dimensional 3D MRI data, including normalization, resampling, and data augmentation. A U-Net based model is employed for precise 3D tumor segmentation. Subsequently, a hybrid classification network, combining DenseNet and VGG architectures and augmented with multihead attention and spatial-channel attention mechanisms, processes the demarcated tumor regions to extract and classify clinically relevant features for glioma grading. Performance is evaluated using Dice coefficient and Mean Intersection over Union (IoU) for segmentation, and accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score for classification.

Key Findings

The proposed hybrid deep learning framework achieved a high Dice coefficient of 98% for 3D tumor segmentation and an impressive 99% accuracy for glioma classification. The integration of multi-head attention mechanisms was found to significantly enhance the model's ability to prioritize and focus on clinically significant tumor aspects, leading to improved interpretability and overall accuracy, demonstrably outperforming traditional CNN and attention-free approaches.

Clinical Impact

This framework has promising potential for facilitating faster, more accurate, and reliable diagnosis and grading of gliomas. By providing clinicians with precise segmentation and classification results, it can aid in earlier detection and enable more precise and timely planning of patient-specific therapeutic interventions, ultimately leading to improved clinical decision-making and better patient outcomes.

Abstract

Gliomas are brain tumor types that have a high mortality rate which means early and accurate diagnosis is important for therapeutic intervention for the tumors. To address this difficulty, the proposed research will develop a hybrid deep learning model which integrates U-Net based segmentation and a hybrid DenseNet-VGG classification network with multihead attention and spatial-channel attention capabilities. The segmentation model will precisely demarcate the tumors in a 3D volume of MRI data guided by spatial and contextual information. The classification network which combines a branch of both DenseNet and VGG, will incorporate the demarcated tumor on which features with attention mechanisms would be focused on clinically relevant features. High-dimensional 3D MRI data could successfully be utilized in the model through preprocessing steps which are normalization, resampling, and data augmentation. Through a variety of measures the framework is evaluated: measures of performance in segmentation are Dice coefficient and Mean Intersection over Union (IoU) and measures of performance in classification are accuracy precision, recall, and F1-score. The hybrid framework that has been proposed has demonstrated through physical testing that it has the capability of obtaining a Dice coefficient of 98% in tumor segmentation, and 99% on classification accuracy, outperforming traditional CNN models and attention-free methods. Utilizing multi-head attention mechanisms enhances notions of priority in aspects of the tumor that are clinically significant, and enhances interpretability and accuracy. The results suggest a great potential of the framework in facilitating the timely and reliable diagnosis and grading of glioma by clinicians is promising, allowing for better planning of patient treatment.
