Revolutionizing Glioma Segmentation & Grading Using 3D MRI - Guided Hybrid Deep Learning Models

arXiv ID: 2511.21673v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Pandiyaraju V, Sreya Mynampati, Abishek Karthik, Poovarasan L, D. Saraswathi
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This research introduces a novel hybrid deep learning model that integrates U-Net for 3D MRI-based glioma segmentation with a DenseNet-VGG classification network, enhanced by multihead and spatial-channel attention. The framework achieved high performance, with a 98% Dice coefficient for tumor segmentation and 99% classification accuracy, demonstrating its potential for accurate and timely glioma diagnosis and grading.

Medical Relevance

Gliomas are highly aggressive brain tumors with high mortality; therefore, early and accurate diagnosis and grading are paramount for effective therapeutic planning and improving patient survival rates. This research provides a powerful AI tool to aid clinicians in this critical task.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves developing a hybrid deep learning model (integrating U-Net for segmentation and a DenseNet-VGG network for classification with attention mechanisms) to automatically and accurately segment gliomas from 3D MRI scans and determine their grade. This system is designed to assist clinicians in the diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment planning for patients with brain tumors, thereby improving clinical efficiency and patient outcomes.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical need for early and accurate diagnosis and grading of gliomas, which are high-mortality brain tumors, to enable effective therapeutic intervention.
  • Proposes a hybrid deep learning architecture combining a U-Net based model for precise 3D volumetric tumor segmentation and a hybrid DenseNet-VGG network for classification.
  • Incorporates advanced attention mechanisms, specifically multihead attention and spatial-channel attention, to focus on clinically relevant features within the demarcated tumor regions.
  • Utilizes high-dimensional 3D MRI data, processed through essential preprocessing steps including normalization, resampling, and data augmentation.
  • Evaluates the framework using comprehensive metrics: Dice coefficient and Mean Intersection over Union (IoU) for segmentation, and accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score for classification.
  • Achieves state-of-the-art performance with a 98% Dice coefficient for tumor segmentation and 99% classification accuracy, significantly outperforming traditional CNN models and attention-free methods.
  • The multi-head attention mechanisms enhance interpretability and accuracy by prioritizing clinically significant aspects of the tumor, aiding clinician decision-making.

Methodology

The study employs a hybrid deep learning model consisting of a U-Net for 3D glioma tumor segmentation from MRI volumes and a hybrid DenseNet-VGG network for subsequent tumor classification (grading). The classification network integrates multihead attention and spatial-channel attention capabilities to emphasize clinically relevant features. High-dimensional 3D MRI data undergoes preprocessing including normalization, resampling, and data augmentation. Performance is quantitatively assessed using Dice coefficient and Mean IoU for segmentation, and accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score for classification.

Key Findings

The proposed hybrid deep learning framework demonstrated exceptional performance, achieving a Dice coefficient of 98% for 3D tumor segmentation and a classification accuracy of 99%. These results represent a significant improvement over traditional CNN models and attention-free methods, validating the effectiveness of the integrated attention mechanisms in enhancing both accuracy and interpretability.

Clinical Impact

This framework offers a promising solution for clinicians to facilitate timely and reliable diagnosis and grading of glioma, which is crucial for optimizing patient treatment plans. Its high accuracy and enhanced interpretability, particularly in focusing on clinically significant tumor features, can lead to more precise therapeutic interventions and potentially improve patient outcomes and survival rates.

Abstract

Gliomas are brain tumor types that have a high mortality rate which means early and accurate diagnosis is important for therapeutic intervention for the tumors. To address this difficulty, the proposed research will develop a hybrid deep learning model which integrates U-Net based segmentation and a hybrid DenseNet-VGG classification network with multihead attention and spatial-channel attention capabilities. The segmentation model will precisely demarcate the tumors in a 3D volume of MRI data guided by spatial and contextual information. The classification network which combines a branch of both DenseNet and VGG, will incorporate the demarcated tumor on which features with attention mechanisms would be focused on clinically relevant features. High-dimensional 3D MRI data could successfully be utilized in the model through preprocessing steps which are normalization, resampling, and data augmentation. Through a variety of measures the framework is evaluated: measures of performance in segmentation are Dice coefficient and Mean Intersection over Union (IoU) and measures of performance in classification are accuracy precision, recall, and F1-score. The hybrid framework that has been proposed has demonstrated through physical testing that it has the capability of obtaining a Dice coefficient of 98% in tumor segmentation, and 99% on classification accuracy, outperforming traditional CNN models and attention-free methods. Utilizing multi-head attention mechanisms enhances notions of priority in aspects of the tumor that are clinically significant, and enhances interpretability and accuracy. The results suggest a great potential of the framework in facilitating the timely and reliable diagnosis and grading of glioma by clinicians is promising, allowing for better planning of patient treatment.
