EvoMorph: Counterfactual Explanations for Continuous Time-Series Extrinsic Regression Applied to Photoplethysmography

arXiv ID: 2601.10356v1
Published: 2026-01-15
Authors: Mesut Ceylan, Alexis Tabin, Patrick Langer, Elgar Fleisch, Filipe Barata
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

EvoMorph introduces a multi-objective evolutionary framework for generating physiologically plausible and diverse counterfactual explanations (CFE) for Time-Series Extrinsic Regression (TSER) models applied to continuous biomedical signals like photoplethysmography (PPG). It addresses the limitations of existing CFE methods by optimizing morphology-aware objectives and preserving waveform structure, thereby enabling uncertainty-aware interpretability crucial for clinical trust and reasoning.

Medical Relevance

This research is vital for the safe and effective adoption of AI in clinical practice, as it provides clinicians with interpretable insights into how physiological changes influence model predictions from wearable data, fostering trust and enabling informed decision-making.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of an interpretable and trustworthy machine learning framework (EvoMorph) that generates physiologically plausible counterfactual explanations for continuous biomedical time-series data (specifically PPG). This enables clinicians to better understand, validate, and trust AI models used for predicting vital signs (heart rate, respiratory rate, oxygen saturation) and other clinically relevant outcomes, thereby supporting clinical assessment, reasoning, and potentially improving patient care through AI-assisted diagnosis and monitoring.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** Existing time-series CFE methods are largely restricted to classification, overlook waveform morphology, and often produce physiologically implausible signals, limiting their applicability to continuous biomedical time series.
  • **EvoMorph Framework:** A novel multi-objective evolutionary framework designed to generate diverse and physiologically plausible counterfactual explanations specifically for Time-Series Extrinsic Regression (TSER) applications.
  • **Morphology-Aware Optimization:** EvoMorph optimizes objectives defined on interpretable signal descriptors and applies transformations to explicitly preserve the underlying waveform structure, ensuring the generated counterfactuals are physiologically relevant and attainable.
  • **Application Domain:** Developed for continuous biomedical signals, demonstrated through photoplethysmography (PPG) data used to estimate clinically relevant outcomes such as heart rate, respiratory rate, and oxygen saturation.
  • **Evaluation and Baseline:** Evaluated on three distinct PPG datasets (HR, RR, SpO2) and compared against a nearest-unlike-neighbor baseline, showcasing its effectiveness in generating relevant counterfactuals.
  • **Uncertainty Quantification:** EvoMorph serves as a tool for uncertainty quantification, relating counterfactual sensitivity to bootstrap-ensemble uncertainty and data-density measures, enhancing model transparency.
  • **Clinical Trust and Reasoning:** The framework aims to improve clinical reasoning and trust by allowing clinicians to understand prediction stability under physiologically plausible variations and how realistic changes in signals would alter model predictions, moving beyond single point estimates.

Methodology

EvoMorph is a multi-objective evolutionary framework engineered to generate physiologically plausible and diverse counterfactual explanations (CFE) for Time-Series Extrinsic Regression (TSER) models. It achieves this by optimizing morphology-aware objectives defined on interpretable signal descriptors and applying specific transformations designed to preserve the waveform structure of continuous time-series data. The framework was evaluated on three distinct PPG datasets for estimating heart rate, respiratory rate, and oxygen saturation, with performance benchmarked against a nearest-unlike-neighbor baseline. A case study also explored its utility in uncertainty quantification by correlating counterfactual sensitivity with bootstrap-ensemble uncertainty and data-density measures.

Key Findings

EvoMorph successfully enables the generation of physiologically-aware, diverse, and plausible counterfactuals for continuous biomedical signals in TSER applications across different PPG datasets (HR, RR, SpO2). Furthermore, the study demonstrated EvoMorph's effectiveness as a tool for uncertainty quantification by establishing a relationship between counterfactual sensitivity and model uncertainty, as well as data density.

Clinical Impact

By providing physiologically plausible 'what-if' scenarios, EvoMorph significantly enhances clinical trust and reasoning for TSER models using PPG data. Clinicians can now understand the stability of predictions (e.g., HR, RR, SpO2) and how realistic physiological changes would alter model outputs, moving beyond opaque single-point estimates. This facilitates more informed diagnostic and therapeutic decision-making, personalized patient management, and improved monitoring in various clinical settings.

Limitations

The paper highlights the limitations of existing time series CFE generation methods, which EvoMorph aims to address: these methods are largely restricted to classification tasks, overlook crucial waveform morphology, and often produce physiologically implausible signals, thus limiting their applicability to continuous biomedical time series.

Future Directions

The paper suggests that EvoMorph advances 'trustworthy model analysis for clinical time-series applications.' Implicitly, this opens avenues for future research in expanding its application to a wider range of physiological signals and clinical scenarios, further refining morphology-aware objectives, integrating it into clinical decision support systems, and exploring its full potential for uncertainty-aware interpretability across diverse machine learning models and patient populations.

Abstract

Wearable devices enable continuous, population-scale monitoring of physiological signals, such as photoplethysmography (PPG), creating new opportunities for data-driven clinical assessment. Time-series extrinsic regression (TSER) models increasingly leverage PPG signals to estimate clinically relevant outcomes, including heart rate, respiratory rate, and oxygen saturation. For clinical reasoning and trust, however, single point estimates alone are insufficient: clinicians must also understand whether predictions are stable under physiologically plausible variations and to what extent realistic, attainable changes in physiological signals would meaningfully alter a model's prediction. Counterfactual explanations (CFE) address these "what-if" questions, yet existing time series CFE generation methods are largely restricted to classification, overlook waveform morphology, and often produce physiologically implausible signals, limiting their applicability to continuous biomedical time series. To address these limitations, we introduce EvoMorph, a multi-objective evolutionary framework for generating physiologically plausible and diverse CFE for TSER applications. EvoMorph optimizes morphology-aware objectives defined on interpretable signal descriptors and applies transformations to preserve the waveform structure. We evaluated EvoMorph on three PPG datasets (heart rate, respiratory rate, and oxygen saturation) against a nearest-unlike-neighbor baseline. In addition, in a case study, we evaluated EvoMorph as a tool for uncertainty quantification by relating counterfactual sensitivity to bootstrap-ensemble uncertainty and data-density measures. Overall, EvoMorph enables the generation of physiologically-aware counterfactuals for continuous biomedical signals and supports uncertainty-aware interpretability, advancing trustworthy model analysis for clinical time-series applications.
