Covariance Descriptors Meet General Vision Encoders: Riemannian Deep Learning for Medical Image Classification

Summary

This paper investigates the effectiveness of covariance descriptors, particularly when derived from features of pre-trained General Vision Encoders (GVEs) like DINOv2 and MedSAM, for medical image classification. It demonstrates that these GVE-based descriptors, especially in conjunction with the SPDNet architecture, consistently outperform handcrafted features and achieve superior performance compared to state-of-the-art methods on the MedMNSIT benchmark. The findings highlight a promising paradigm for enhancing medical image analysis.

Medical Relevance

This research offers a novel and effective approach to improve the accuracy and robustness of medical image classification, which is foundational for developing more reliable automated diagnostic tools, enhancing screening programs, and supporting precise treatment planning in various clinical settings.

This research aims to develop more accurate and efficient AI models for medical image classification. Such models can be applied in clinical settings to assist healthcare professionals in diagnosing diseases from various medical images (e.g., X-rays, MRIs, CT scans), screening for conditions, and potentially guiding treatment decisions, thereby improving patient outcomes and healthcare efficiency.

Methodology

The study constructed covariance descriptors from two sources of image features: traditional handcrafted methods and features extracted by pre-trained General Vision Encoders (DINOv2 and MedSAM). These descriptors, representing symmetric positive definite (SPD) matrices, were then classified using SPDNet, a network specifically designed for such matrices. Performance was rigorously evaluated and compared against state-of-the-art methods across eleven binary and multi-class datasets within the MedMNSIT benchmark.

Key Findings

Covariance descriptors derived from General Vision Encoder (GVE) features consistently demonstrated superior performance over those derived from handcrafted features for medical image classification. Notably, the integration of SPDNet with features extracted by DINOv2 achieved state-of-the-art performance, surpassing other methods across the evaluated MedMNSIT datasets.

Abstract

Covariance descriptors capture second-order statistics of image features. They have shown strong performance in general computer vision tasks, but remain underexplored in medical imaging. We investigate their effectiveness for both conventional and learning-based medical image classification, with a particular focus on SPDNet, a classification network specifically designed for symmetric positive definite (SPD) matrices. We propose constructing covariance descriptors from features extracted by pre-trained general vision encoders (GVEs) and comparing them with handcrafted descriptors. Two GVEs - DINOv2 and MedSAM - are evaluated across eleven binary and multi-class datasets from the MedMNSIT benchmark. Our results show that covariance descriptors derived from GVE features consistently outperform those derived from handcrafted features. Moreover, SPDNet yields superior performance to state-of-the-art methods when combined with DINOv2 features. Our findings highlight the potential of combining covariance descriptors with powerful pretrained vision encoders for medical image analysis.
