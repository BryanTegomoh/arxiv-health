Covariance Descriptors Meet General Vision Encoders: Riemannian Deep Learning for Medical Image Classification

Summary

This paper investigates the effectiveness of covariance descriptors for medical image classification, focusing on their combination with features extracted from pre-trained General Vision Encoders (GVEs) and processed by SPDNet. The study demonstrates that covariance descriptors derived from GVE features (DINOv2, MedSAM) consistently outperform handcrafted features. Notably, SPDNet combined with DINOv2 features achieves superior performance compared to state-of-the-art methods on the MedMNSIT benchmark.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a highly effective method for improving the accuracy of automated medical image classification, which is critical for precise and early disease diagnosis, screening programs, and enhanced clinical decision support, ultimately benefiting patient care.

The research aims to develop and improve AI models for automated classification of medical images, which can be used to assist clinicians in diagnosing diseases, detecting abnormalities, and streamlining medical image review processes.

Methodology

The study constructs covariance descriptors using features extracted by two pre-trained General Vision Encoders (GVEs): DINOv2 and MedSAM. These descriptors are then fed into SPDNet, a neural network optimized for Symmetric Positive Definite (SPD) matrices. Performance is compared against covariance descriptors derived from handcrafted features and against state-of-the-art methods. Evaluation is conducted on eleven binary and multi-class datasets from the MedMNSIT benchmark.

Key Findings

Covariance descriptors derived from General Vision Encoder (GVE) features consistently outperform those derived from handcrafted features. Furthermore, the combination of SPDNet with DINOv2 features yields classification performance superior to state-of-the-art methods across various medical imaging tasks in the MedMNSIT benchmark.

Abstract

Covariance descriptors capture second-order statistics of image features. They have shown strong performance in general computer vision tasks, but remain underexplored in medical imaging. We investigate their effectiveness for both conventional and learning-based medical image classification, with a particular focus on SPDNet, a classification network specifically designed for symmetric positive definite (SPD) matrices. We propose constructing covariance descriptors from features extracted by pre-trained general vision encoders (GVEs) and comparing them with handcrafted descriptors. Two GVEs - DINOv2 and MedSAM - are evaluated across eleven binary and multi-class datasets from the MedMNSIT benchmark. Our results show that covariance descriptors derived from GVE features consistently outperform those derived from handcrafted features. Moreover, SPDNet yields superior performance to state-of-the-art methods when combined with DINOv2 features. Our findings highlight the potential of combining covariance descriptors with powerful pretrained vision encoders for medical image analysis.
