Towards Multimodal Representation Learning in Paediatric Kidney Disease

arXiv ID: 2511.13637v1
Published: 2025-11-17
Authors: Ana Durica, John Booth, Ivana Drobnjak
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This pilot study explores a temporal modeling approach using a recurrent neural network on electronic health record (EHR) data from paediatric kidney disease patients at Great Ormond Street Hospital. It successfully demonstrated that integrating longitudinal laboratory sequences with demographic information can capture useful patterns to predict abnormal serum creatinine within 30 days, laying the groundwork for future multimodal extensions.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and timely prediction of renal function deterioration, as indicated by elevated serum creatinine, is critical for proactive clinical management, enabling early interventions and potentially preventing irreversible kidney damage in children with paediatric kidney disease.

AI Health Application

A recurrent neural model is applied to longitudinal laboratory sequences and demographic information from electronic health records to predict whether a child with paediatric kidney disease will record an abnormal serum creatinine value within 30 days. This is a predictive AI application aimed at early detection or risk stratification in a clinical setting.

Key Points

  • Addresses the challenge of varying presentation and progression in paediatric kidney disease, necessitating continuous renal function monitoring.
  • Utilizes electronic health records collected from 2019-2025 at Great Ormond Street Hospital, a leading UK paediatric facility.
  • Integrates two types of data: longitudinal laboratory sequences (e.g., serum creatinine) and demographic information.
  • Employs a recurrent neural model for temporal representation learning.
  • The primary prediction task is to determine whether a child will record an abnormal serum creatinine value within the subsequent thirty days.
  • Demonstrates that even 'simple temporal representations' can effectively capture useful patterns within routine paediatric clinical data.
  • Serves as a pilot study, setting the foundation for future work involving multimodal extensions with additional clinical signals and more detailed renal outcomes.

Methodology

The study adopted a temporal modelling approach by training a recurrent neural model. This model processed longitudinal sequences of laboratory data (specifically serum creatinine values) augmented with demographic information extracted from electronic health records (EHRs). The model's objective was to predict the binary outcome of whether a child would exhibit an abnormal serum creatinine value within a 30-day look-ahead period.

Key Findings

The pilot study successfully demonstrated that utilizing simple temporal representations, derived from routinely collected paediatric EHR data (longitudinal laboratory sequences and demographics), is effective in capturing clinically relevant patterns for predicting future abnormal serum creatinine values.

Clinical Impact

This work has the potential to enhance clinical decision support by enabling earlier identification of paediatric patients at risk of impending renal function abnormalities. This could lead to more timely clinical interventions, closer patient monitoring, or adjustments in treatment strategies, ultimately improving management and outcomes for children with kidney disease.

Limitations

Explicitly stated as a 'pilot study' and relying on 'simple temporal representations,' which suggests the current model's scope and complexity are introductory. It focuses solely on predicting one specific outcome (abnormal serum creatinine within 30 days) rather than broader or more granular renal outcomes.

Future Directions

The authors plan to extend this research towards multimodal representation learning. This will involve integrating additional diverse clinical signals (e.g., imaging data, genetic information, free-text clinical notes) and exploring predictions for more comprehensive and detailed renal outcomes beyond just a single abnormal lab value.

Abstract

Paediatric kidney disease varies widely in its presentation and progression, which calls for continuous monitoring of renal function. Using electronic health records collected between 2019 and 2025 at Great Ormond Street Hospital, a leading UK paediatric hospital, we explored a temporal modelling approach that integrates longitudinal laboratory sequences with demographic information. A recurrent neural model trained on these data was used to predict whether a child would record an abnormal serum creatinine value within the following thirty days. Framed as a pilot study, this work provides an initial demonstration that simple temporal representations can capture useful patterns in routine paediatric data and lays the groundwork for future multimodal extensions using additional clinical signals and more detailed renal outcomes.
