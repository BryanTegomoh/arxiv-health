Reproduction Numbers R_0, R_t for COVID-19 Infections with Gaussian Distribution of Generation Times, and of Serial Intervals including Presymptomatic Transmission

arXiv ID: 2511.04574v1
Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Derek Marsh
Categories: q-bio.PE
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00

Summary

This paper addresses inconsistencies in calculating COVID-19 reproduction numbers (R0, Rt) using the Lotka-Euler equation when considering presymptomatic transmission and Gaussian-distributed serial intervals. It formulates a new Lotka-Euler equation that explicitly incorporates a lower cut-off for these serial intervals, thereby resolving discrepancies with predictions from the discretized renewal equation and improving the accuracy of R number estimation.

Medical Relevance

Accurate calculation of reproduction numbers (R0, Rt) is fundamental for effective public health decision-making, guiding interventions like social distancing, assessing vaccine impact, and estimating population immunity thresholds required to control infectious disease outbreaks, particularly for novel pathogens like SARS-CoV-2.

Key Points

  • Reproduction numbers (R0, Rt) are critical metrics for assessing epidemic progression, intervention efficacy, and vaccination coverage needs.
  • Calculations of R numbers rely on either the renewal equation or the Lotka-Euler equation, both requiring the distribution of generation times, typically approximated by the serial interval (SI).
  • COVID-19's presymptomatic transmission results in serial intervals that can extend to negative values, which are appropriately modeled using a Gaussian distribution.
  • A common pitfall is allowing Gaussian-distributed serial intervals to extend to minus infinity in the Lotka-Euler equation, leading to underestimation of reproduction numbers compared to the discretized renewal equation.
  • The paper introduces a novel formulation of the Lotka-Euler equation for Gaussian distributions that explicitly includes a lower cut-off for serial intervals.
  • This new formulation is designed to explore and accurately quantify the impact of presymptomatic transmission on COVID-19 reproduction numbers.
  • Consistent application of these two approaches (renewal vs. Lotka-Euler) requires careful attention to the imposed lower limits on the serial interval distribution.

Methodology

The methodology involves a theoretical reformulation of the Lotka-Euler equation. Specifically, it develops a mathematical model that incorporates Gaussian distributions for serial intervals with an explicit lower cut-off, addressing the issue of negative serial intervals due to presymptomatic transmission and its impact on the calculation of reproduction numbers.

Key Findings

The primary finding is the successful formulation of a revised Lotka-Euler equation that explicitly accounts for Gaussian-distributed serial intervals with a defined lower cut-off. This formulation resolves the previously observed discrepancy where allowing serial intervals to extend to minus infinity resulted in lower reproduction number estimates compared to the renewal equation, thus enabling more accurate assessment of R values in the context of presymptomatic transmission.

Clinical Impact

This research provides a more precise and consistent mathematical framework for estimating R0 and Rt values for COVID-19. This improved accuracy directly impacts public health strategies by allowing health authorities to better monitor epidemic trajectories, more reliably evaluate the effectiveness of non-pharmaceutical interventions (NPIs), and set more informed targets for vaccination campaigns, ultimately leading to more effective disease control.

Abstract

Basic and instantaneous reproduction numbers, "R" _"0" and "R" _"t" , are important metrics to assess progress of an epidemic and effectiveness of preventative interventions undertaken, and also to estimate coverage needed for vaccination. Reproduction numbers are related to the daily number of positive cases recorded by the national public health authorities, via the renewal equation. During periods of exponential growth or decay they are linked also to the rate constants by the Lotka-Euler equation. For either application, we need the distribution of generation times between primary and secondary infections. In practice, we use instead the directly observable serial interval between symptoms onset of infector and infectee. Pre-symptomatic transmission that occurs in COVID infection causes serial intervals to extend to negative values, which can be described with a Gaussian distribution. Consistent application of the two approaches requires careful attention to lower limits imposed on the distribution. Allowing Gaussian-distributed serial intervals to extend to minus infinity with the Lotka-Euler equation, as commonly is done, results in lower reproduction numbers than predicted from the discretized renewal equation. Here, we formulate the Lotka-Euler equation for Gaussian distributions including an explicit lower cut-off, and use this to explore the consequences of presymptomatic transmission for COVID-19 infections.
