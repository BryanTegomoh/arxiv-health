Evolving Diagnostic Agents in a Virtual Clinical Environment

Summary

This paper introduces DiagAgent, a novel large language model trained with reinforcement learning in DiagGym, a virtual clinical environment that simulates multi-turn diagnostic processes using electronic health records. DiagAgent learns to adaptively select examinations and commit to final diagnoses, demonstrating significant performance improvements over state-of-the-art LLMs in diagnostic accuracy and examination recommendation efficiency across various diagnostic settings. The research highlights that interactive learning in clinical environments confers dynamic and clinically meaningful diagnostic management abilities unattainable through passive training alone.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine by proposing an AI framework that can learn to navigate complex diagnostic pathways dynamically, potentially improving the efficiency and accuracy of clinical decision-making, reducing unnecessary examinations, and providing advanced decision support to clinicians in challenging diagnostic scenarios.

AI Health Application

The paper describes the development of an AI-powered 'diagnostic agent' (DiagAgent) using reinforcement learning. This AI aims to assist in the medical diagnostic process by interpreting patient information, recommending examinations, and suggesting final diagnoses within a simulated clinical environment. It represents an advanced application of AI for clinical decision support and potentially for medical training.

Key Points

  • **Reinforcement Learning for Diagnostics**: DiagAgent is trained via end-to-end, multi-turn reinforcement learning (RL) to learn diagnostic policies, a novel approach compared to static instruction-tuned models.
  • **Virtual Clinical Environment (DiagGym)**: A diagnostics world model trained on electronic health records (EHRs) is presented, serving as an interactive simulator that emits realistic examination outcomes conditioned on patient history and recommended examinations.
  • **Comprehensive Diagnostic Benchmark (DiagBench)**: A new benchmark is introduced, comprising 750 cases with physician-validated examination recommendations and 99 cases annotated with 973 physician-written rubrics for process evaluation.
  • **Superior Diagnostic Accuracy**: DiagAgent significantly outperforms 10 state-of-the-art LLMs (including DeepSeek-v3 and GPT-4o) and prompt-engineered agents, achieving up to 15.12% higher diagnostic accuracy in end-to-end settings.
  • **Enhanced Examination Recommendation Efficiency**: The model demonstrates substantial improvements in examination recommendation hit ratio (44.03% increase in single-turn) and F1 score (23.09% boost in end-to-end settings), indicating better resource utilization.
  • **Clinically Meaningful Process Evaluation**: DiagAgent surpasses the next-best model, Claude-sonnet-4, by 7.1% in weighted rubric score, validating the quality and clinical appropriateness of its diagnostic process.
  • **Dynamic Diagnostic Management**: The findings suggest that interactive learning in simulated clinical environments enables LLMs to acquire dynamic and context-aware diagnostic management abilities crucial for real-world medical practice.

Methodology

The methodology centers on training large language models (LLMs), referred to as DiagAgent, using an end-to-end, multi-turn reinforcement learning (RL) approach. This training is conducted within a simulated virtual clinical environment called DiagGym, which is a "diagnostics world model" built upon electronic health records (EHRs) to generate realistic examination outcomes. DiagAgent's diagnostic policies are optimized for both information yield and diagnostic accuracy. Performance is rigorously evaluated using DiagBench, a multi-faceted benchmark that includes physician-validated examination recommendations and physician-written rubrics for assessing the diagnostic process.

Key Findings

DiagAgent achieved superior performance across diverse diagnostic settings. In single-turn scenarios, it demonstrated 9.34% higher diagnostic accuracy and a 44.03% improvement in examination recommendation hit ratio. For end-to-end diagnostic processes, it delivered a 15.12% increase in diagnostic accuracy and a 23.09% boost in examination recommendation F1 score. Notably, in rubric-based evaluations, DiagAgent outperformed the next-best model, Claude-sonnet-4, by 7.1% in weighted rubric score, indicating a more clinically sound diagnostic process. These results collectively highlight that learning through interactive clinical environments grants LLMs dynamic and clinically meaningful diagnostic management abilities beyond passively trained models.

Clinical Impact

This work has significant potential to impact clinical practice by developing highly capable AI agents that can assist clinicians in navigating complex diagnostic pathways. Such agents could enhance diagnostic accuracy, reduce diagnostic errors, and optimize healthcare resource utilization by recommending only truly necessary examinations. They could also serve as advanced, realistic training tools for medical students and residents, offering interactive diagnostic scenarios. Ultimately, this could lead to more efficient, patient-centered, and cost-effective healthcare delivery by augmenting human diagnostic capabilities.

Abstract

In this paper, we present a framework for training large language models (LLMs) as diagnostic agents with reinforcement learning, enabling them to manage multi-turn diagnostic processes, adaptively select examinations, and commit to final diagnoses. Unlike instruction-tuned models trained on static case summaries, our method acquires diagnostic strategies through interactive exploration and outcome-based feedback. Our contributions are fourfold: (i) We present DiagGym, a diagnostics world model trained with electronic health records that emits examination outcomes conditioned on patient history and recommended examination, serving as a virtual clinical environment for realistic diagnosis training and evaluation; (ii) We train DiagAgent via end-to-end, multi-turn reinforcement learning to learn diagnostic policies that optimize both information yield and diagnostic accuracy; (iii) We introduce DiagBench, a diagnostic benchmark comprising 750 cases with physician-validated examination recommendations and 99 cases annotated with 973 physician-written rubrics on diagnosis process; (iv) we demonstrate superior performance across diverse diagnostic settings. DiagAgent significantly outperforms 10 state-of-the-art LLMs, including DeepSeek-v3 and GPT-4o, as well as two prompt-engineered agents. In single-turn settings, DiagAgent achieves 9.34% higher diagnostic accuracy and 44.03% improvement in examination recommendation hit ratio. In end-to-end settings, it delivers 15.12% increase in diagnostic accuracy and 23.09% boost in examination recommendation F1 score. In rubric-based evaluation, it surpasses the next-best model, Claude-sonnet-4, by 7.1% in weighted rubric score. These findings indicate that learning policies in interactive clinical environments confers dynamic and clinically meaningful diagnostic management abilities unattainable through passive training alone.
