Summary

This paper introduces a generative modeling framework based on latent neural stochastic differential equations (SDEs) to address challenges in clinical time series data like irregular sampling, complex latent physiology, and inherent uncertainties. The approach models underlying dynamics probabilistically and demonstrates superior performance over baseline models (ODEs, LSTMs) in accuracy and uncertainty estimation, enabling precise, uncertainty-aware predictions for clinical decision-making.

Medical Relevance

Methodology

The proposed methodology is a generative modeling framework built upon latent neural Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs). It conceptualizes clinical time series as discrete-time partial observations originating from an underlying controlled stochastic dynamical system. The core involves modeling the latent dynamics using neural SDEs, complemented by modality-dependent emission models. State estimation and parameter learning within this probabilistic framework are performed through variational inference, allowing for robust handling of irregular sampling, learning of complex non-linear interactions, and capturing inherent stochasticity and measurement noise.

Key Findings

The framework successfully validated its capabilities across two distinct tasks: (i) individual treatment effect estimation utilizing a simulated pharmacokinetic-pharmacodynamic (PKPD) model for lung cancer, and (ii) probabilistic forecasting of physiological signals derived from real-world intensive care unit (ICU) data encompassing 12,000 patients. In both tasks, the framework demonstrably outperformed traditional ordinary differential equation (ODE) models and advanced long short-term memory (LSTM) baseline models, specifically excelling in both predictive accuracy and the critical estimation of uncertainty.

Clinical Impact

The ability to generate precise and uncertainty-aware predictions has significant clinical impact. It enables clinicians to make more informed decisions by providing reliable probabilistic forecasts of patient trajectories and individual treatment effects. This can lead to improved personalized medicine, optimized treatment strategies, better risk assessment, and ultimately, enhanced patient outcomes, particularly in complex scenarios like critical care or chronic disease management.

Abstract

Clinical time series data from electronic health records and medical registries offer unprecedented opportunities to understand patient trajectories and inform medical decision-making. However, leveraging such data presents significant challenges due to irregular sampling, complex latent physiology, and inherent uncertainties in both measurements and disease progression. To address these challenges, we propose a generative modeling framework based on latent neural stochastic differential equations (SDEs) that views clinical time series as discrete-time partial observations of an underlying controlled stochastic dynamical system. Our approach models latent dynamics via neural SDEs with modality-dependent emission models, while performing state estimation and parameter learning through variational inference. This formulation naturally handles irregularly sampled observations, learns complex non-linear interactions, and captures the stochasticity of disease progression and measurement noise within a unified scalable probabilistic framework. We validate the framework on two complementary tasks: (i) individual treatment effect estimation using a simulated pharmacokinetic-pharmacodynamic (PKPD) model of lung cancer, and (ii) probabilistic forecasting of physiological signals using real-world intensive care unit (ICU) data from 12,000 patients. Results show that our framework outperforms ordinary differential equation and long short-term memory baseline models in accuracy and uncertainty estimation. These results highlight its potential for enabling precise, uncertainty-aware predictions to support clinical decision-making.
