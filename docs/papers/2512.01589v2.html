Toward Content-based Indexing and Retrieval of Head and Neck CT with Abscess Segmentation

Summary

This paper introduces AbscessHeNe, a novel, comprehensively annotated dataset comprising 4,926 contrast-enhanced CT slices specifically for head and neck abscesses, aimed at advancing semantic segmentation. Baseline evaluations on this dataset, using CNN, Transformer, and Mamba architectures, revealed significant challenges in accurate delineation, with the best model achieving a Dice Similarity Coefficient of 0.39, underscoring the need for further research in this critical medical imaging task.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and prompt detection and delineation of head and neck abscesses are vital for preventing severe complications such as sepsis or mortality, directly impacting patient treatment planning, surgical guidance, and overall clinical outcomes.

AI Health Application

The paper focuses on developing and evaluating AI (CNN, Transformer, Mamba-based) models for semantic segmentation to automatically detect and delineate head and neck abscesses on CT scans. This AI application aims to improve the accuracy and efficiency of diagnosis, guide treatment planning, and assist in surgical interventions. Beyond segmentation, the dataset is designed to build 'intelligent retrieval systems' for content-based indexing and case-based retrieval of medical imaging data and clinical metadata, thereby supporting clinical workflows and knowledge sharing in healthcare.

Key Points

  • Creation and public release of AbscessHeNe, a curated dataset of 4,926 contrast-enhanced CT slices with pixel-level annotations of head and neck abscesses.
  • The dataset is specifically designed to facilitate the development of robust semantic segmentation models for accurate abscess delineation and evaluation of deep neck space involvement.
  • Performance baselines were established by evaluating several state-of-the-art deep learning segmentation architectures, including CNN, Transformer, and Mamba-based models.
  • The highest-performing model achieved a Dice Similarity Coefficient of 0.39, Intersection-over-Union (IoU) of 0.27, and Normalized Surface Distance (NSD) of 0.67.
  • These baseline results indicate the significant inherent challenges associated with accurate segmentation of head and neck abscesses on CT imaging.
  • AbscessHeNe is structured to support future applications in content-based multimedia indexing and case-based retrieval by linking pixel-level annotations with clinical metadata.
  • The dataset will be made publicly available at https://github.com/drthaodao3101/AbscessHeNe.git to foster collaborative research and development.

Methodology

The study involved creating and meticulously annotating a novel dataset, AbscessHeNe, consisting of 4,926 contrast-enhanced CT slices from patients with clinically confirmed head and neck abscesses. This dataset was then utilized to benchmark the performance of several state-of-the-art deep learning segmentation architectures, including CNN-based models, Transformer-based models, and Mamba-based models, evaluating their ability to delineate abscess boundaries using metrics such as Dice Similarity Coefficient, Intersection-over-Union, and Normalized Surface Distance.

Key Findings

The primary finding is the successful creation and public release of AbscessHeNe, a unique and comprehensively annotated dataset for head and neck abscess segmentation. Baseline evaluations using advanced deep learning models revealed that the task is highly challenging, with the top-performing model achieving only a Dice score of 0.39, an IoU of 0.27, and an NSD of 0.67, highlighting the significant room for improvement in automated segmentation of these lesions.

Clinical Impact

This work has the potential to significantly enhance clinical workflows by providing a foundation for developing AI tools that can more accurately and rapidly detect and delineate head and neck abscesses on CT scans. Such tools could improve diagnosis speed, facilitate precise treatment planning, optimize surgical approaches by evaluating deep neck space involvement, and ultimately contribute to better patient outcomes by reducing the risk of life-threatening complications.

Limitations

The observed relatively low performance metrics (Dice 0.39, IoU 0.27) from state-of-the-art models suggest that current deep learning architectures face considerable challenges in accurately segmenting head and neck abscesses, indicating the inherent complexity and variability of these lesions on CT images.

Future Directions

Future research should focus on developing more sophisticated and robust semantic segmentation models to improve the accuracy of abscess delineation. Additionally, the AbscessHeNe dataset is designed to support the development of content-based multimedia indexing and case-based retrieval systems, integrating pixel-level annotations with clinical metadata to build intelligent retrieval systems and facilitate knowledge-driven clinical workflows.

Abstract

Abscesses in the head and neck represent an acute infectious process that can potentially lead to sepsis or mortality if not diagnosed and managed promptly. Accurate detection and delineation of these lesions on imaging are essential for diagnosis, treatment planning, and surgical intervention. In this study, we introduce AbscessHeNe, a curated and comprehensively annotated dataset comprising 4,926 contrast-enhanced CT slices with clinically confirmed head and neck abscesses. The dataset is designed to facilitate the development of robust semantic segmentation models that can accurately delineate abscess boundaries and evaluate deep neck space involvement, thereby supporting informed clinical decision-making. To establish performance baselines, we evaluate several state-of-the-art segmentation architectures, including CNN, Transformer, and Mamba-based models. The highest-performing model achieved a Dice Similarity Coefficient of 0.39, Intersection-over-Union of 0.27, and Normalized Surface Distance of 0.67, indicating the challenges of this task and the need for further research. Beyond segmentation, AbscessHeNe is structured for future applications in content-based multimedia indexing and case-based retrieval. Each CT scan is linked with pixel-level annotations and clinical metadata, providing a foundation for building intelligent retrieval systems and supporting knowledge-driven clinical workflows. The dataset will be made publicly available at https://github.com/drthaodao3101/AbscessHeNe.git.
