Summary

CHMR is a novel framework designed to enhance molecular property prediction by integrating chemical structures with cellular responses like morphology and gene expression. It addresses limitations in current cell-aware methods by robustly modeling multi-modal local-global dependencies and capturing latent biological hierarchies through a tree-structured vector quantization module. This approach significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines across various tasks, yielding more reliable and biologically grounded molecular representations.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for advancing drug discovery and development by enabling more accurate and biologically relevant predictions of how chemical perturbations affect biological systems, thereby improving drug efficacy, safety, and targeting.

AI Health Application

This AI model (CHMR) is designed to improve the prediction of molecular properties and drug effects by integrating chemical structures with cellular responses (morphology, gene expression). This application directly aids in accelerating and enhancing the accuracy of drug discovery pipelines, identifying potential drug candidates, understanding drug mechanisms of action, and potentially predicting adverse effects more robustly. It serves as a generalizable AI framework for complex biomedical modeling.

Methodology

CHMR (Cell-aware Hierarchical Multi-modal Representations) is a deep learning framework designed to integrate diverse biological data. It jointly models multi-modal inputs, specifically chemical structures and cellular responses (e.g., morphology, gene expression), to capture local-global dependencies. A core component is a novel tree-structured vector quantization module, which is employed to explicitly capture and represent latent biological hierarchies spanning molecular, cellular, and genomic levels, thereby providing structured, context-rich representations.

Key Findings

The CHMR framework significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, demonstrating an average improvement of 3.6% on classification tasks and a notable 17.2% improvement on regression tasks across 728 diverse benchmarks. These results validate the efficacy of hierarchy-aware, multi-modal learning for producing more robust and biologically grounded molecular representations.

Abstract

Understanding how chemical perturbations propagate through biological systems is essential for robust molecular property prediction. While most existing methods focus on chemical structures alone, recent advances highlight the crucial role of cellular responses such as morphology and gene expression in shaping drug effects. However, current cell-aware approaches face two key limitations: (1) modality incompleteness in external biological data, and (2) insufficient modeling of hierarchical dependencies across molecular, cellular, and genomic levels. We propose CHMR (Cell-aware Hierarchical Multi-modal Representations), a robust framework that jointly models local-global dependencies between molecules and cellular responses and captures latent biological hierarchies via a novel tree-structured vector quantization module. Evaluated on nine public benchmarks spanning 728 tasks, CHMR outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, yielding average improvements of 3.6% on classification and 17.2% on regression tasks. These results demonstrate the advantage of hierarchy-aware, multimodal learning for reliable and biologically grounded molecular representations, offering a generalizable framework for integrative biomedical modeling. The code is in https://github.com/limengran98/CHMR.
