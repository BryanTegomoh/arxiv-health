HiFusion: Hierarchical Intra-Spot Alignment and Regional Context Fusion for Spatial Gene Expression Prediction from Histopathology

arXiv ID: 2511.12969v1
Published: 2025-11-17
Authors: Ziqiao Weng, Yaoyu Fang, Jiahe Qian, Xinkun Wang, Lee AD Cooper, Weidong Cai, Bo Zhou
Categories: cs.CV

Summary

HiFusion is a novel deep learning framework designed to accurately predict spatial gene expression from H&E-stained whole-slide images, addressing the limitations of existing computational methods in capturing intra-spot heterogeneity and integrating contextual information. It achieves this by combining hierarchical intra-spot modeling with context-aware cross-scale fusion, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on benchmark spatial transcriptomics datasets.

Medical Relevance

This technology enables the generation of high-resolution spatial gene expression data from standard H&E slides, making spatial omics significantly more accessible and cost-effective for clinical research and diagnostics. This facilitates deeper understanding of disease mechanisms, identification of novel biomarkers, and progression towards precision medicine without specialized ST platforms.

AI Health Application

HiFusion is a deep learning model designed to predict spatial gene expression from standard histopathology images (H&E-stained WSIs). This AI application aims to provide detailed molecular insights from routine pathology slides, circumventing the high cost and technical complexity of direct spatial transcriptomics assays. This can enhance diagnostic accuracy, inform prognosis, facilitate personalized treatment strategies, and deepen our understanding of disease mechanisms by inferring comprehensive gene expression maps from readily available clinical data.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical problem of high technical complexity and prohibitive costs of direct spatial transcriptomics (ST), proposing a computational alternative from routine H&E images.
  • Introduces a Hierarchical Intra-Spot Modeling (HIM) module that extracts fine-grained, multi-resolution morphological representations within ST spots, guided by a feature alignment loss for semantic consistency across scales.
  • Proposes a Context-aware Cross-scale Fusion (CCF) module that employs cross-attention to selectively incorporate biologically relevant regional context, mitigating morphological noise and enhancing representational capacity.
  • HiFusion's architecture comprehensively models both cellular-level features (intra-spot) and tissue microenvironmental cues (contextual), which are crucial for accurate gene expression prediction.
  • Achieves state-of-the-art performance on two benchmark ST datasets, validating its accuracy and robustness against existing computational methods.
  • Demonstrated superior performance in both standard 2D slide-wise cross-validation and more challenging 3D sample-specific prediction scenarios, indicating high generalizability.
  • Positions itself as a robust, accurate, and scalable solution for inferring spatial transcriptomics information from widely available and cost-effective histopathology slides.

Methodology

HiFusion is a deep learning framework integrating two main modules. The Hierarchical Intra-Spot Modeling (HIM) module processes multi-resolution sub-patches within ST spots, extracting fine-grained morphological features, and employs a feature alignment loss to ensure semantic consistency across scales. Concurrently, the Context-aware Cross-scale Fusion (CCF) module uses a cross-attention mechanism to selectively integrate biologically relevant regional context from the surrounding tissue, enhancing the model's ability to capture microenvironmental cues for gene expression prediction.

Key Findings

HiFusion consistently achieved state-of-the-art performance in predicting spatial gene expression from H&E images across two distinct benchmark spatial transcriptomics datasets. Its superior accuracy was demonstrated in both 2D slide-wise cross-validation and more stringent 3D sample-specific prediction scenarios, confirming its robustness and generalizability in capturing intricate cellular and microenvironmental details.

Clinical Impact

By providing accurate spatial gene expression insights from routine H&E histopathology, HiFusion can significantly accelerate biomarker discovery and validate therapeutic targets in oncology and other complex diseases. It could enable more precise patient stratification, guide personalized treatment strategies, and enhance diagnostic capabilities by integrating genomic and morphological information at an unprecedented scale and accessibility in clinical settings.

Abstract

Spatial transcriptomics (ST) bridges gene expression and tissue morphology but faces clinical adoption barriers due to technical complexity and prohibitive costs. While computational methods predict gene expression from H&E-stained whole-slide images (WSIs), existing approaches often fail to capture the intricate biological heterogeneity within spots and are susceptible to morphological noise when integrating contextual information from surrounding tissue. To overcome these limitations, we propose HiFusion, a novel deep learning framework that integrates two complementary components. First, we introduce the Hierarchical Intra-Spot Modeling module that extracts fine-grained morphological representations through multi-resolution sub-patch decomposition, guided by a feature alignment loss to ensure semantic consistency across scales. Concurrently, we present the Context-aware Cross-scale Fusion module, which employs cross-attention to selectively incorporate biologically relevant regional context, thereby enhancing representational capacity. This architecture enables comprehensive modeling of both cellular-level features and tissue microenvironmental cues, which are essential for accurate gene expression prediction. Extensive experiments on two benchmark ST datasets demonstrate that HiFusion achieves state-of-the-art performance across both 2D slide-wise cross-validation and more challenging 3D sample-specific scenarios. These results underscore HiFusion's potential as a robust, accurate, and scalable solution for ST inference from routine histopathology.
