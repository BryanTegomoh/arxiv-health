CardioEmbed: Domain-Specialized Text Embeddings for Clinical Cardiology

arXiv ID: 2511.10930v1
Published: 2025-11-14
Authors: Richard J. Young, Alice M. Matthews
Categories: cs.CL, cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This study introduces CardioEmbed, a novel domain-specialized text embedding model for clinical cardiology, designed to overcome the limitations of existing models primarily trained on general biomedical research literature. By utilizing contrastive learning on a curated corpus of comprehensive cardiology textbooks, CardioEmbed achieved near-perfect (99.60%) retrieval accuracy on cardiac-specific semantic tasks. This represents a significant 15.94 percentage point improvement over MedTE, the current state-of-the-art medical embedding model.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for advancing precision in clinical information retrieval within cardiology, directly impacting the development of more effective clinical decision support systems and educational tools. By accurately understanding clinical texts, it can significantly enhance the efficiency and quality of care for cardiac patients.

AI Health Application

CardioEmbed is a domain-specialized AI text embedding model designed to improve the understanding and retrieval of information specific to clinical cardiology. This could be applied to enhance clinical decision support systems, semantic search for medical professionals accessing textbooks or patient records, intelligent summarization of cardiology literature, or advanced natural language processing tasks relevant to cardiology care, ultimately aiming to improve the effectiveness of AI tools in a clinical setting.

Key Points

  • Existing biomedical text embeddings, predominantly trained on PubMed research abstracts, are inadequate for clinical cardiology due to their lack of specialized procedural knowledge and terminology found in textbooks.
  • CardioEmbed is a domain-specialized embedding model, built upon Qwen3-Embedding-8B, developed to address the specific needs of clinical cardiology.
  • The model was trained using contrastive learning with InfoNCE loss and in-batch negatives on a unique corpus of seven comprehensive cardiology textbooks, comprising approximately 150,000 deduplicated sentences.
  • CardioEmbed demonstrated exceptional performance on cardiac-specific semantic retrieval tasks, achieving an accuracy of 99.60%.
  • This specialized training yielded a substantial +15.94 percentage point improvement in retrieval accuracy compared to MedTE, which is currently the state-of-the-art general medical embedding model.
  • On broader MTEB medical benchmarks, CardioEmbed showed competitive performance, achieving BIOSSES 0.77 Spearman and SciFact 0.61 NDCG@10.
  • The research underscores the critical importance of domain-specialized training using comprehensive clinical textbooks for achieving high accuracy in clinical information retrieval tasks within specific medical specialties.

Methodology

The study involved training CardioEmbed, a domain-specialized text embedding model, by fine-tuning the Qwen3-Embedding-8B base model. The training employed contrastive learning, utilizing InfoNCE loss with in-batch negatives, on a meticulously curated corpus of seven comprehensive cardiology textbooks, which totaled roughly 150,000 deduplicated sentences. Performance was primarily evaluated using cardiac-specific semantic retrieval tasks and further assessed on general MTEB medical benchmarks (BIOSSES, SciFact) for comparative analysis.

Key Findings

CardioEmbed achieved a remarkable 99.60% retrieval accuracy on cardiac-specific semantic retrieval tasks. This performance represented a significant +15.94 percentage point improvement over MedTE, the state-of-the-art general medical embedding model. The model also demonstrated competitive performance on broader MTEB medical benchmarks with BIOSSES 0.77 Spearman and SciFact 0.61 NDCG@10, affirming the effectiveness of domain-specialized training on comprehensive clinical textbooks for specialized clinical applications.

Clinical Impact

CardioEmbed has the potential to revolutionize information retrieval within clinical cardiology, leading to more accurate and contextually relevant search results for clinicians. This can greatly improve the efficacy of clinical decision support tools, enhance medical education and training materials, and streamline research efforts, ultimately contributing to better patient outcomes and increased operational efficiency in cardiology practices.

Abstract

Biomedical text embeddings have primarily been developed using research literature from PubMed, yet clinical cardiology practice relies heavily on procedural knowledge and specialized terminology found in comprehensive textbooks rather than research abstracts. This research practice gap limits the effectiveness of existing embedding models for clinical applications incardiology. This study trained CardioEmbed, a domain-specialized embedding model based on Qwen3-Embedding-8B, using contrastive learning on a curated corpus of seven comprehensive cardiology textbooks totaling approximately 150,000 sentences after deduplication. The model employs InfoNCE loss with in-batch negatives and achieves 99.60% retrieval accuracy on cardiac-specific semantic retrieval tasks, a +15.94 percentage point improvement over MedTE, the current state-of-the-art medical embedding model. On MTEB medical benchmarks, the model obtained BIOSSES 0.77 Spearman and SciFact 0.61 NDCG@10, indicating competitive performance on related biomedical domains. Domain-specialized training on comprehensive clinical textbooks yields near-perfect cardiology retrieval (99.60% Acc@1), improving over MedTE by +15.94 percentage points.
