WaveRNet: Wavelet-Guided Frequency Learning for Multi-Source Domain-Generalized Retinal Vessel Segmentation

Authors: Chanchan Wang, Yuanfang Wang, Qing Xu, Guanxin Chen

Summary

This paper introduces WaveRNet, a wavelet-guided frequency learning framework designed for robust multi-source domain-generalized retinal vessel segmentation. By integrating frequency-domain insights and a hierarchical refinement strategy into a SAM-based architecture, WaveRNet effectively addresses challenges such as domain shift from varying illumination and contrast, and the loss of fine vessel structures, achieving state-of-the-art generalization performance.

Medical Relevance

This research is critically important for advancing automated ophthalmic diagnosis by providing more robust and accurate retinal vessel segmentation, which is a foundational step for detecting and monitoring numerous eye conditions like diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma, and hypertension.

AI Health Application

This research develops an AI model (WaveRNet) to perform robust and accurate segmentation of blood vessels in retinal images. This AI application assists healthcare professionals by automating a key step in ophthalmic diagnosis, potentially leading to earlier detection, more consistent analysis, and improved management of eye diseases and systemic conditions with ocular manifestations.

Key Points

  • Addresses significant challenges in domain-generalized retinal vessel segmentation, including domain shift caused by non-uniform illumination/contrast and the difficulty of preserving fine vessel structures.
  • Proposes WaveRNet, a novel framework that enhances Segment Anything Model (SAM) capabilities by incorporating wavelet-guided frequency learning for domain-invariant feature extraction.
  • Introduces a Spectral-guided Domain Modulator (SDM) which uses wavelet decomposition and learnable domain tokens to separate illumination-robust low-frequency structures from high-frequency vessel boundaries, facilitating domain-specific feature generation.
  • Develops a Frequency-Adaptive Domain Fusion (FADF) module that performs intelligent test-time domain selection and soft-weighted fusion based on wavelet-based frequency similarity.
  • Presents a Hierarchical Mask-Prompt Refiner (HMPR) to overcome SAM's direct upsampling limitations, enabling coarse-to-fine refinement with long-range dependency modeling to preserve fine vessel details.
  • Achieves state-of-the-art generalization performance across four public retinal datasets under the rigorous Leave-One-Domain-Out experimental protocol.
  • The source code is made publicly available, promoting reproducibility and further research.

Methodology

The WaveRNet framework integrates wavelet decomposition with a modified Segment Anything Model (SAM). Its core components include a Spectral-guided Domain Modulator (SDM) for frequency-based feature separation and domain-specific feature generation, a Frequency-Adaptive Domain Fusion (FADF) module for intelligent test-time domain selection and soft-weighted fusion, and a Hierarchical Mask-Prompt Refiner (HMPR) for coarse-to-fine mask refinement. The method was validated using the Leave-One-Domain-Out protocol across four public retinal datasets.

Key Findings

WaveRNet demonstrates state-of-the-art generalization performance in retinal vessel segmentation, effectively addressing domain shift caused by variations in illumination and contrast while successfully preserving fine vessel structures, a significant improvement over existing SAM-based approaches.

Clinical Impact

This method has the potential to significantly enhance the reliability and universality of automated ophthalmic diagnostic tools. By providing robust segmentation across diverse imaging conditions, it can aid clinicians in earlier and more consistent detection, diagnosis, and monitoring of retinal diseases, ultimately improving patient outcomes and reducing diagnostic variability.

Abstract

Domain-generalized retinal vessel segmentation is critical for automated ophthalmic diagnosis, yet faces significant challenges from domain shift induced by non-uniform illumination and varying contrast, compounded by the difficulty of preserving fine vessel structures. While the Segment Anything Model (SAM) exhibits remarkable zero-shot capabilities, existing SAM-based methods rely on simple adapter fine-tuning while overlooking frequency-domain information that encodes domain-invariant features, resulting in degraded generalization under illumination and contrast variations. Furthermore, SAM's direct upsampling inevitably loses fine vessel details. To address these limitations, we propose WaveRNet, a wavelet-guided frequency learning framework for robust multi-source domain-generalized retinal vessel segmentation. Specifically, we devise a Spectral-guided Domain Modulator (SDM) that integrates wavelet decomposition with learnable domain tokens, enabling the separation of illumination-robust low-frequency structures from high-frequency vessel boundaries while facilitating domain-specific feature generation. Furthermore, we introduce a Frequency-Adaptive Domain Fusion (FADF) module that performs intelligent test-time domain selection through wavelet-based frequency similarity and soft-weighted fusion. Finally, we present a Hierarchical Mask-Prompt Refiner (HMPR) that overcomes SAM's upsampling limitation through coarse-to-fine refinement with long-range dependency modeling. Extensive experiments under the Leave-One-Domain-Out protocol on four public retinal datasets demonstrate that WaveRNet achieves state-of-the-art generalization performance. The source code is available at https://github.com/Chanchan-Wang/WaveRNet.
