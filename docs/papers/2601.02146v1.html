Summary

This paper presents a novel non-enzymatic electrochemical sensor for rapid and accurate creatinine detection using a carbon electrode modified with a copper-PEDOT:PSS matrix. The sensor leverages the formation of specific Cu(I) and Cu(II) creatinine coordination complexes as the detection mechanism, demonstrating high selectivity and long-term stability in artificial urine. This technology promises enhanced point-of-care kidney-function monitoring.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for advancing point-of-care diagnostics in nephrology by providing a rapid, reagent-free, and stable method for monitoring creatinine, a critical biomarker for kidney function. Early and accessible detection of renal failure can significantly improve patient outcomes and management.

Methodology

The methodology involves modifying carbon electrodes with a composite matrix of PEDOT:PSS and copper nanoparticles (CuNPs). CuNP embedding is achieved electrochemically by applying redox potential sweeps using a copper sulfate (CuSO4) solution. Creatinine detection is then performed using electrochemical techniques, with the underlying mechanism of Cu-creatinine coordination complex formation (Cu(I) and Cu(II) states) validated through supporting analytical methods.

Key Findings

The study successfully demonstrated a highly selective and stable non-enzymatic electrochemical creatinine sensor. It was found that creatinine detection is facilitated by the formation of specific Cu(I) and Cu(II) coordination complexes with creatinine. The sensor maintained high performance for a month with only 0.53% degradation in peak current and exhibited strong selectivity against common artificial urine interferents.

Abstract

Electrochemical creatinine sensors offer great promise towards rapid, reagent-free and point-of-care (POC) kidney-function monitoring. However, challenges related to analyte binding, data reproducibility, sensitivity, fouling and device degradation deter its widespread implementation. Here, we show how a carbon electrode modified with a combination of poly(3,4-ethylene dioxythiophene): poly(styrene sulfonate) (PEDOT:PSS) and copper nanoparticles (CuNPs) can rapidly and accurately detect creatinine (CT) in artificial urine media employing electrochemical techniques. Applying redox potential sweeps (vs Ag/AgCl) using copper sulfate (CuSO4) solution on such sensor facilitates the CuNP embedding process inside the conjugated polymer matrix which has been further validated by supporting techniques. We predicted and validated the formation and contribution of two Cu-CT coordination complexes corresponding to Cu(I) and Cu(II) states, which are responsible for CT detection. The fabricated CT sensor exhibits high selectivity against major artificial urine interferents and is stable for a month showing minimal degradation (0.53%) in peak current value. Such sensors can be utilized to detect and monitor different stages of renal failure in real-time patient samples.
