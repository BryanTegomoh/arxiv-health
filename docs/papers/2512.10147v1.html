Murmur2Vec: A Hashing Based Solution For Embedding Generation Of COVID-19 Spike Sequences

arXiv ID: 2512.10147v1
Published: 2025-12-10
Authors: Sarwan Ali, Taslim Murad
Categories: cs.LG, q-bio.GN
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces Murmur2Vec, a novel hashing-based method for generating highly compact, low-dimensional embeddings of SARS-CoV-2 spike protein sequences. These embeddings enable scalable and efficient supervised machine learning for lineage classification, addressing the computational limitations of existing phylogenetic and alignment-dependent approaches for large-scale viral sequence analysis. The method achieves high classification accuracy while drastically reducing the time required for embedding generation.

Medical Relevance

This method significantly accelerates the analysis of SARS-CoV-2 genomic data, which is crucial for early detection, rapid characterization of emerging variants, and informing effective clinical responses and public health strategies, including vaccine development and epidemiological surveillance.

AI Health Application

The AI method (Murmur2Vec, a hashing-based embedding generation for machine learning) is applied to rapidly and efficiently classify SARS-CoV-2 lineages from spike protein sequences. This enables faster characterization of viral variants, which is crucial for real-time epidemiological surveillance, informing clinical and public health responses (e.g., vaccine updates, treatment strategies), and understanding disease spread and evolution during pandemics.

Key Points

  • Traditional phylogenetic methods and current embedding techniques for viral sequence analysis are computationally intensive, do not scale well to multi-million sequence datasets, or require sequence alignment.
  • The study focuses on SARS-CoV-2 spike protein sequences, which are critical for viral entry and vaccine development, to characterize prevalent lineages.
  • Murmur2Vec leverages hashing to create compact, low-dimensional embeddings (vector representations) directly from unaligned spike sequences.
  • These generated embeddings are then utilized to train various machine learning models for supervised classification of SARS-CoV-2 lineages.
  • Extensive evaluation against multiple baseline and state-of-the-art biological sequence embedding methods was conducted across diverse metrics.
  • Murmur2Vec achieved classification accuracy of up to 86.4% for lineage identification.
  • A significant advantage of Murmur2Vec is its efficiency, reducing embedding generation time by as much as 99.81% compared to other methods, highlighting its scalability for large datasets.

Methodology

The core methodology involves applying a hashing-based technique (Murmur2Vec) to SARS-CoV-2 spike protein sequences to generate compact, low-dimensional numerical embeddings. These embeddings are designed to capture the essential features of the sequences without requiring prior alignment. Subsequently, these numerical representations are used as input to train and evaluate a variety of supervised machine learning models for the task of viral lineage classification. The performance of this approach is then rigorously compared against established baseline and state-of-the-art biological sequence embedding methods.

Key Findings

The proposed Murmur2Vec method offers substantial improvements in efficiency for viral sequence analysis. It achieved up to 86.4% classification accuracy for SARS-CoV-2 lineage identification. Crucially, it demonstrated a dramatic reduction in embedding generation time, achieving up to 99.81% faster processing compared to existing methods, making it highly scalable for large-scale datasets.

Clinical Impact

This research provides a tool that can drastically speed up the identification and characterization of new SARS-CoV-2 variants and lineages. This rapid analysis capability is vital for informing public health interventions, adapting diagnostic tests, optimizing vaccine strategies, and guiding clinical management decisions in real-time, especially during outbreaks or the emergence of novel strains. It facilitates more agile genomic surveillance, enabling quicker responses to epidemiological shifts.

Limitations

The abstract primarily highlights the limitations of existing methods rather than explicitly stating limitations of Murmur2Vec itself. Potential unstated limitations could include the interpretability of hashing-based embeddings compared to alignment-based methods, or the generalizability to highly divergent viral sequences beyond SARS-CoV-2 spike protein.

Future Directions

The abstract does not explicitly mention future research directions for Murmur2Vec. However, potential directions could involve applying this scalable embedding approach to other viral genomic regions or pathogens, exploring its utility for tasks like mutation prediction or drug resistance identification, or integrating it into real-time surveillance dashboards.

Abstract

Early detection and characterization of coronavirus disease (COVID-19), caused by SARS-CoV-2, remain critical for effective clinical response and public-health planning. The global availability of large-scale viral sequence data presents significant opportunities for computational analysis; however, existing approaches face notable limitations. Phylogenetic tree-based methods are computationally intensive and do not scale efficiently to today's multi-million-sequence datasets. Similarly, current embedding-based techniques often rely on aligned sequences or exhibit suboptimal predictive performance and high runtime costs, creating barriers to practical large-scale analysis. In this study, we focus on the most prevalent SARS-CoV-2 lineages associated with the spike protein region and introduce a scalable embedding method that leverages hashing to generate compact, low-dimensional representations of spike sequences. These embeddings are subsequently used to train a variety of machine learning models for supervised lineage classification. We conduct an extensive evaluation comparing our approach with multiple baseline and state-of-the-art biological sequence embedding methods across diverse metrics. Our results demonstrate that the proposed embeddings offer substantial improvements in efficiency, achieving up to 86.4\% classification accuracy while reducing embedding generation time by as much as 99.81\%. This highlights the method's potential as a fast, effective, and scalable solution for large-scale viral sequence analysis.
