MedRule-KG: A Knowledge-Graph--Steered Scaffold for Reliable Mathematical and Biomedical Reasoning

Summary

This paper introduces MedRule-KG, a novel system designed to impose domain-consistent structure on large language models (LLMs) for scientific reasoning and early-stage drug discovery. By combining a knowledge-graph scaffold with a lightweight verifier, MedRule-KG steers LLM generation towards mathematically and biomedically valid outputs. The system significantly reduces violation counts by 83.2% while improving exact match across tasks like reaction feasibility, metabolic compatibility, and toxicity screening, making LLM-based drug discovery more reliable and practical.

Medical Relevance

MedRule-KG directly enhances the reliability and trustworthiness of AI models in crucial early-stage drug discovery, ensuring that proposed chemical reactions, metabolic interactions, and toxicity predictions are scientifically sound. This mitigates risks associated with invalid AI outputs, accelerating the development of safer and more effective pharmaceutical compounds.

AI Health Application

The AI application described is the use of knowledge-graph-steered large language models (LLMs) to enhance reliability in scientific (specifically biomedical) reasoning for early-stage drug discovery. This includes predicting reaction feasibility, metabolic compatibility, and toxicity screening of potential compounds, thereby accelerating and improving the drug development process.

Methodology

MedRule-KG consists of a compact knowledge-graph scaffold and a lightweight verifier. The system injects curated symbolic facts directly into LLM prompts. A deterministic checker then enforces the satisfaction of these rules on the LLM's generated output. Generation is formalized as constrained inference, utilizing a soft guidance surrogate suitable for decoding. Performance was thoroughly analyzed statistically with uncertainty quantification across 90 tasks, comparing against a chain-of-thought baseline.

Key Findings

The MedRule-KG system reduced violation counts by 83.2% relative to a strong chain-of-thought baseline across diverse tasks (reaction feasibility, metabolic compatibility, toxicity screening). It also improved exact match performance. Results demonstrated stability under data stratification and scaled effectively with dataset size. Crucially, the verifier introduced negligible latency, making the approach practical for interactive design and deployment.

Clinical Impact

This research holds significant potential for transforming early-stage drug discovery and development. By ensuring the mathematical and biomedical validity of LLM-generated insights, it can accelerate the identification of promising drug candidates, reduce the rate of false positives or biologically unsound hypotheses, and enhance the safety profile of drugs by improving the accuracy of toxicity and metabolic predictions. This could lead to more efficient R&D processes, lower development costs, and ultimately, a faster pipeline for bringing new, reliable medications to patients.

Abstract

We study how to impose domain-consistent structure on large language models (LLMs) used for scientific reasoning and early-stage drug discovery. We present MedRule-KG, a compact knowledge-graph scaffold paired with a lightweight verifier that steers generation toward mathematically and biomedically valid outputs. The system injects curated symbolic facts into prompts and then enforces rule satisfaction with a deterministic checker. We formalize generation as constrained inference, introduce a soft guidance surrogate suitable for decoding, and perform a thorough statistical analysis with uncertainty quantification. Across 90 tasks spanning reaction feasibility, metabolic compatibility, and toxicity screening, MedRule-KG reduces violation counts by 83.2\% relative to a strong chain-of-thought baseline while improving exact match. Results remain stable under stratification and scale with dataset size, and the verifier adds negligible latency, making the approach practical for interactive design.
