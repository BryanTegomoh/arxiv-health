MICCAI STS 2024 Challenge: Semi-Supervised Instance-Level Tooth Segmentation in Panoramic X-ray and CBCT Images

Summary

The MICCAI STS 2024 Challenge successfully benchmarked and advanced semi-supervised learning (SSL) for instance-level tooth segmentation in OPG and CBCT images, addressing the critical issue of limited labeled data. Winning SSL models demonstrated impressive performance gains (over 44 pp IA for OPG, 61 pp Dice for CBCT) over fully-supervised baselines, proving the substantial benefit of SSL for complex medical image segmentation. The most effective approaches leveraged hybrid SSL frameworks, foundational models like SAM, and multi-stage refinement pipelines.

Medical Relevance

Automated, accurate instance-level tooth segmentation is vital for precise dental diagnostics, treatment planning, and surgical guidance. This research offers a robust solution for achieving high segmentation accuracy even with scarce labeled data, accelerating the adoption of AI in clinical dentistry and making advanced tools more practical and accessible.

AI Health Application

The AI application described is the automated, instance-level segmentation of teeth from panoramic X-ray (OPG) and Cone-Beam Computed Tomography (CBCT) images. This leverages semi-supervised deep learning to overcome the challenge of limited labeled data in medical imaging. This technology can significantly aid dental practitioners in diagnostics, treatment planning (e.g., orthodontics, prosthodontics, implantology), and patient monitoring by providing accurate and efficient tooth identification and localization, reducing the burden of manual annotation and improving workflow.

Key Points

  • The STS 2024 Challenge aimed to advance and benchmark semi-supervised learning (SSL) for instance-level tooth segmentation in OPG and CBCT images due to labor-intensive manual annotation.
  • A large-scale dataset was provided, comprising over 90,000 2D images and 3D axial slices (2,380 OPGs, 330 CBCTs), with detailed instance-level FDI annotations on only part of the data, suitable for SSL.

  • For 3D CBCTs, the winning approach boosted the Instance Dice score by 61 percentage points.

Methodology

The study organized a competitive challenge (STS 2024) providing a large, partially instance-level annotated dataset of 2,380 OPG images and 330 CBCT scans. Participants developed and submitted deep learning-based semi-supervised learning (SSL) models for instance-level tooth segmentation. Submissions were rigorously evaluated against a fully-supervised nnU-Net baseline trained exclusively on the labeled subset of the data, with performance measured using metrics like Instance Affinity (IA) for OPG and Instance Dice for CBCT. Successful approaches commonly integrated hybrid SSL frameworks, leveraged foundational models (e.g., SAM), and employed multi-stage, coarse-to-fine refinement strategies.

Key Findings

The challenge firmly established the substantial utility of semi-supervised learning for complex, instance-level medical image segmentation tasks where labeled data is scarce. The top-performing SSL models demonstrated significant performance improvements: over 44 percentage points increase in Instance Affinity for 2D OPGs and a 61 percentage point increase in Instance Dice for 3D CBCTs, relative to fully-supervised methods utilizing only the labeled data. The most effective SSL strategies were characterized by hybrid frameworks, integration of foundational models like SAM, and multi-stage, coarse-to-fine refinement pipelines.

Clinical Impact

This research provides a validated pathway for developing highly accurate automated tooth segmentation tools, which can significantly reduce the labor burden on dental professionals and radiologists. This automation promises to enhance efficiency in dental diagnostics, streamline treatment planning for orthodontics and implantology, and improve precision in surgical guidance, thereby supporting more informed and timely clinical decision-making even when extensive manual annotations are impractical.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations of the study or the winning methodologies presented. However, as is common with challenge results, generalization of these models to diverse clinical populations or datasets beyond those used in the challenge would require further validation.

Future Directions

The success of the challenge and the identified effective methodologies implicitly suggest future research should focus on further optimizing hybrid SSL frameworks, exploring more advanced foundational models beyond SAM, and refining multi-stage, coarse-to-fine segmentation pipelines. Continued work on validating these methods across broader clinical scenarios and integrating them into clinical workflows would also be beneficial.

Abstract

Orthopantomogram (OPGs) and Cone-Beam Computed Tomography (CBCT) are vital for dentistry, but creating large datasets for automated tooth segmentation is hindered by the labor-intensive process of manual instance-level annotation. This research aimed to benchmark and advance semi-supervised learning (SSL) as a solution for this data scarcity problem. We organized the 2nd Semi-supervised Teeth Segmentation (STS 2024) Challenge at MICCAI 2024. We provided a large-scale dataset comprising over 90,000 2D images and 3D axial slices, which includes 2,380 OPG images and 330 CBCT scans, all featuring detailed instance-level FDI annotations on part of the data. The challenge attracted 114 (OPG) and 106 (CBCT) registered teams. To ensure algorithmic excellence and full transparency, we rigorously evaluated the valid, open-source submissions from the top 10 (OPG) and top 5 (CBCT) teams, respectively. All successful submissions were deep learning-based SSL methods. The winning semi-supervised models demonstrated impressive performance gains over a fully-supervised nnU-Net baseline trained only on the labeled data. For the 2D OPG track, the top method improved the Instance Affinity (IA) score by over 44 percentage points. For the 3D CBCT track, the winning approach boosted the Instance Dice score by 61 percentage points. This challenge confirms the substantial benefit of SSL for complex, instance-level medical image segmentation tasks where labeled data is scarce. The most effective approaches consistently leveraged hybrid semi-supervised frameworks that combined knowledge from foundational models like SAM with multi-stage, coarse-to-fine refinement pipelines. Both the challenge dataset and the participants' submitted code have been made publicly available on GitHub (https://github.com/ricoleehduu/STS-Challenge-2024), ensuring transparency and reproducibility.
