Agentic AI Framework for Individuals with Disabilities and Neurodivergence: A Multi-Agent System for Healthy Eating, Daily Routines, and Inclusive Well-Being

Published: 2025-11-27
Authors: Salman Jan, Toqeer Ali Syed, Gohar Ali, Ali Akarma, Mohammad Riyaz Belgaum, Ahmad Ali

Summary

This paper introduces a detailed Agentic AI framework designed to empower individuals with disabilities and neurodivergence to achieve healthier lives and maintain structured daily routines. The multi-agent system utilizes a three-layer architecture to deliver adaptive, personalized support for nutrition, scheduling, and health monitoring, leveraging explainable AI and secure multi-modal data. It aims to transcend traditional assistive technologies by fostering autonomy, health, and digital equity through human-centered design.

Medical Relevance

This framework offers a significant step towards enabling greater independence and improved health outcomes for individuals with disabilities and neurodivergence by providing intelligent, personalized, and continuous support for critical aspects of daily living, thereby addressing key health disparities and enhancing quality of life.

AI Health Application

The paper describes a multi-agent AI system designed to provide adaptive, transparent, and inclusive support for individuals with disabilities and neurodivergence. This includes a Meal Planner Agent for personalized nutrition, a Reminder Agent for daily routines, a Food Guidance Agent for shopping/cooking assistance, and a Monitoring Agent for physiological tracking. The system integrates with EHRs, wearable sensors, and clinician dashboards, aiming to enhance autonomy, health, and digital equity through AI.

Key Points

  • Proposes a multi-layered Agentic AI architecture comprising an Application/Interface Layer, an Agents Layer, and a Data Source Layer.
  • Features a hybrid reasoning engine synchronizing four specialized agents: a Meal Planner Agent (personalized nutrition), a Reminder Agent (adaptive scheduling), a Food Guidance Agent (interactive grocery/cooking assistance), and a Monitoring Agent (continuous physiological tracking).
  • Agent interactions are facilitated via a central Blackboard/Event Bus for autonomous communication and real-time feedback loops.
  • Integrates diverse, privacy-sensitive data sources including Electronic Health Records (EHRs), nutritional databases, wearable sensors, and smart kitchen IoT, managed within a policy-controlled layer for data safety and consent compliance.
  • Includes clinician dashboards for collaborative care supervision and Discussable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) modules to provide transparent explanations for AI decisions, enhancing user trust and responsibility.
  • Emphasizes personalization, inclusiveness, and accessibility as core design principles, positioning the framework as an advancement beyond traditional assistive systems.
  • Aims to foster autonomy, health, and digital equity among people with disabilities and neurodivergence through the intersection of multi-agent reasoning, multi-modal interfaces, and human-centered design.

Methodology

The paper outlines a conceptual framework for a multi-agent AI system. It details a multi-layer architecture (Application/Interface, Agents, Data Source) with a hybrid reasoning engine coordinating four specialized agents (Meal Planner, Reminder, Food Guidance, Monitoring) via a Blackboard/Event Bus. It specifies the integration of multi-modal data (EHRs, wearables, IoT) secured by a policy-controlled layer, alongside collaborative care features (clinician dashboards) and Explainable AI (XAI) modules.

Key Findings

As a proposed framework, the key findings represent its designed capabilities and principles: the ability to provide adaptive, transparent, and inclusive support for healthy eating and daily routines; seamless synchronization of specialized agents for personalized health management; secure integration of diverse multi-modal health data; facilitation of collaborative care and user empowerment through XAI; and the demonstration of a novel, human-centered approach extending beyond conventional assistive technologies by prioritizing personalization and accessibility.

Clinical Impact

This framework has the potential to profoundly impact clinical practice by providing a robust, intelligent tool for continuous patient support and self-management. It could improve adherence to dietary recommendations, medication schedules, and daily routines, leading to better chronic disease management and overall well-being. Clinicians could leverage dashboards for remote monitoring and more informed collaborative care, potentially reducing caregiver burnout and enhancing the independence of individuals with disabilities and neurodivergence.

Limitations

The abstract presents a conceptual framework, implying that the system is currently theoretical and has not yet undergone empirical implementation or validation. Specific technical challenges related to real-time data integration, system interoperability, security robustness in practice, and widespread user adoption and acceptance are not detailed.

Future Directions

While not explicitly stated, the proposed nature of the framework suggests future work would involve developing and implementing the described multi-agent system, conducting pilot studies and clinical trials to validate its efficacy and usability, refining the AI components based on real-world data, and exploring its scalability and integration within existing healthcare infrastructures, along with continued research into ethical considerations and long-term user impact.

Abstract

The paper presents a detailed Agentic Artificial Intelligence (AI) model that would enable people with disabilities and neurodivergence to lead healthier lives and have more regular days. The system will use a multi-layer structure; it will include an Application and Interface Layer, an Agents Layer, and a Data Source Layer to provide adaptive, transparent, and inclusive support. Fundamentally, a hybrid reasoning engine will synchronize four special-purpose agents, which include: a personalized-nutrition-based, called a Meal Planner Agent; an adaptive-scheduling-based, called a Reminder Agent; interactive assistance during grocery shopping and cooking, called a Food Guidance Agent; and a continuous-intake-and-physiological-tracking, called a Monitoring Agent. All the agents interact through a central communicative system called the Blackboard/Event Bus, which allows autonomous interaction and real-time feedback loops with multimedia user interfaces. Privacy-sensitive data sources, including electronic health records (EHRs), nutritional databases, wearable sensors, and smart kitchen Internet of Things, are also included in the framework and placed into a policy-controlled layer, which ensures data safety and compliance with consent. Collaborative care and clinician dashboards allow common supervision, and discussable artificial intelligence (XAI) modules give brief explanations of why a decision was made, making users responsible and reliant. The proposed agentic AI framework is an extension beyond traditional assistive systems since it incorporates inclusiveness, personalization, and accessibility at all levels. It displays the intersection of multi-agent reasoning, multi-modal interfaces, and human-centered design that will enable the development of autonomy, health, and digital equity among people with disabilities and neurodivergence.
