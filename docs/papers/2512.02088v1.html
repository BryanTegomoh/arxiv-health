Summary

This study demonstrates that 24-hour (J1) diffusion MRI, combined with clinical and lesion-volume features, provides superior prognostic value for predicting three-month functional outcomes in acute ischemic stroke patients compared to baseline (J0) imaging. Utilizing multimodal deep embeddings from 3D ResNet-50, fused with clinical variables and classified by SVM, the J1-based models achieved significantly higher predictive performance (AUC = 0.923) than J0 models (AUC <= 0.86). The robust multimodal framework offers a promising tool for personalized stroke prognosis.

Medical Relevance

This research highlights the critical importance of early post-treatment imaging (24-hour dMRI) over baseline scans for accurate prognosis in acute ischemic stroke, potentially enabling more timely and tailored rehabilitation strategies and resource allocation for patients.

AI Health Application

The paper develops an AI model that integrates deep embeddings extracted from diffusion MRI scans (using a ResNet-50 architecture) with structured clinical variables. This multimodal AI system is designed to predict three-month functional outcomes in acute ischemic stroke patients, providing a robust and interpretable framework for medical prognostication and potentially guiding clinical decisions.

Key Points

  • The study aimed to compare the prognostic value of baseline (J0) versus 24-hour (J1) diffusion MRI (specifically ADC scans) for predicting 3-month functional outcomes in acute ischemic stroke (AIS) patients.
  • Analysis was conducted on 74 AIS patients with paired J0 and J1 apparent diffusion coefficient (ADC) scans and comprehensive clinical data.
  • A multimodal deep learning approach was employed, involving 3D ResNet-50 for MRI embeddings, fusion with structured clinical variables, and integration of lesion-volume features.
  • Dimensionality reduction was performed using Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to <=12 components, followed by classification using linear Support Vector Machines (SVMs) with eight-fold stratified group cross-validation.
  • J1 multimodal models demonstrated significantly superior predictive performance (AUC = 0.923 +/- 0.085) compared to J0-based configurations (AUC <= 0.86) for 3-month functional outcomes.
  • The incorporation of lesion-volume features into the multimodal models further improved their stability and interpretability, underscoring the benefit of comprehensive data integration.

Methodology

The study utilized a multimodal deep learning framework. Three-dimensional ResNet-50 was employed to generate deep embeddings from apparent diffusion coefficient (ADC) MRI scans obtained at baseline (J0) and 24 hours (J1) post-stroke. These image embeddings were then fused with structured clinical variables and specific lesion-volume features. Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was applied to reduce the dimensionality of the combined features to 12 or fewer components. Finally, linear Support Vector Machines (SVMs) were used for classification to predict three-month functional outcomes, with model evaluation performed via eight-fold stratified group cross-validation.

Key Findings

The primary finding is that multimodal models incorporating 24-hour (J1) diffusion MRI data achieved significantly higher predictive performance for three-month functional outcomes (AUC = 0.923 +/- 0.085) compared to models based on baseline (J0) imaging (AUC <= 0.86). This demonstrates the superior prognostic value of early post-treatment imaging. Furthermore, the inclusion of lesion-volume features notably improved both the stability and interpretability of the predictive models.

Clinical Impact

This study provides compelling evidence that imaging performed 24 hours post-stroke treatment is more valuable for predicting long-term functional recovery than pre-treatment imaging. Clinically, this could lead to a paradigm shift in prognostic imaging for Acute Ischemic Stroke (AIS), allowing clinicians to make earlier, more informed decisions regarding patient management, resource allocation for rehabilitation, and realistic outcome expectations. The robust and interpretable multimodal framework offers a promising tool for personalized prognosis, potentially optimizing patient care pathways.

Abstract

This study compares baseline (J0) and 24-hour (J1) diffusion magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) for predicting three-month functional outcomes after acute ischemic stroke (AIS). Seventy-four AIS patients with paired apparent diffusion coefficient (ADC) scans and clinical data were analyzed. Three-dimensional ResNet-50 embeddings were fused with structured clinical variables, reduced via principal component analysis (<=12 components), and classified using linear support vector machines with eight-fold stratified group cross-validation. J1 multimodal models achieved the highest predictive performance (AUC = 0.923 +/- 0.085), outperforming J0-based configurations (AUC <= 0.86). Incorporating lesion-volume features further improved model stability and interpretability. These findings demonstrate that early post-treatment diffusion MRI provides superior prognostic value to pre-treatment imaging and that combining MRI, clinical, and lesion-volume features produces a robust and interpretable framework for predicting three-month functional outcomes in AIS patients.
