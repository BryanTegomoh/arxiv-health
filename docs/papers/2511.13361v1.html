MedDCR: Learning to Design Agentic Workflows for Medical Coding

Published: 2025-11-17

Summary

MedDCR introduces a novel closed-loop framework for learning to design adaptive agentic workflows for medical coding, overcoming the limitations of rigid, manually crafted approaches. It leverages a Designer, Coder, and Reflector with a memory archive to systematically refine coding workflows, achieving state-of-the-art performance and generating interpretable, adaptable solutions that mirror real-world practice. This approach enhances the reliability and trustworthiness of automated medical coding systems.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine as it automates the complex and crucial task of medical coding, which is essential for accurate billing, efficient hospital operations, and robust medical research data collection. By improving automation, it can reduce administrative burden and errors in healthcare.

AI Health Application

MedDCR is an AI application designed to automate and improve the accuracy of medical coding. It uses agentic LLMs within a closed-loop learning framework to design, execute, and refine workflows for converting clinical notes into standardized medical codes. This aims to enhance the reliability and trustworthiness of automated systems in a critical healthcare process.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: Current agentic LLM-based medical coding systems rely on rigid, manually crafted workflows that fail to capture the multi-step reasoning, nuance, and variability required in real-world clinical documentation.
  • **Novel Framework (MedDCR)**: MedDCR is a closed-loop, learning-based framework that treats workflow design itself as a solvable problem, moving beyond static workflow definitions.
  • **Agentic Components**: The system comprises a 'Designer' (proposes workflows), a 'Coder' (executes workflows), and a 'Reflector' (evaluates predictions and provides feedback), fostering an iterative learning process.
  • **Memory and Iterative Refinement**: A crucial 'memory archive' component stores prior successful workflow designs, enabling reuse and continuous iterative refinement for improved adaptability.
  • **Superior Performance**: MedDCR demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on benchmark datasets, significantly outperforming existing baselines in medical coding accuracy.
  • **Interpretable & Adaptable Workflows**: The framework produces workflows that are not only interpretable but also adaptable, better reflecting the complexities and specific constraints of real medical coding practices.
  • **Enhanced System Trustworthiness**: By generating more realistic and adaptive workflows, MedDCR improves the reliability and trustworthiness of automated medical coding systems, crucial for sensitive healthcare applications.

Methodology

MedDCR employs a closed-loop, agent-based learning framework. A 'Designer' agent proposes multi-step coding workflows. A 'Coder' agent executes these workflows on free-text clinical notes to convert them into standardized diagnostic and procedural codes. A 'Reflector' agent evaluates the Coder's predictions against ground truth and provides constructive feedback for improvement. This feedback loop, combined with a 'memory archive' that stores and reuses successful workflow designs, enables systematic learning and iterative refinement of optimal coding workflows.

Key Findings

MedDCR significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines on medical coding benchmark datasets. It successfully learns to generate interpretable and adaptable workflows that are more representative of actual human coding practices, capturing the nuance and variability of real-world documentation. This leads to improved reliability and trustworthiness of automated medical coding systems.

It can streamline hospital operations by automating a labor-intensive process, reducing administrative overhead and human error. Furthermore, by ensuring consistent and accurate coding, it enhances the quality and reliability of data used for medical research, potentially leading to better public health insights and treatment advancements.

Abstract

Medical coding converts free-text clinical notes into standardized diagnostic and procedural codes, which are essential for billing, hospital operations, and medical research. Unlike ordinary text classification, it requires multi-step reasoning: extracting diagnostic concepts, applying guideline constraints, mapping to hierarchical codebooks, and ensuring cross-document consistency. Recent advances leverage agentic LLMs, but most rely on rigid, manually crafted workflows that fail to capture the nuance and variability of real-world documentation, leaving open the question of how to systematically learn effective workflows. We present MedDCR, a closed-loop framework that treats workflow design as a learning problem. A Designer proposes workflows, a Coder executes them, and a Reflector evaluates predictions and provides constructive feedback, while a memory archive preserves prior designs for reuse and iterative refinement. On benchmark datasets, MedDCR outperforms state-of-the-art baselines and produces interpretable, adaptable workflows that better reflect real coding practice, improving both the reliability and trustworthiness of automated systems.
