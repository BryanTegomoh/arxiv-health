Graph Distance Based on Cause-Effect Estimands with Latents

arXiv ID: 2510.25037v1
Published: 2025-10-28
Authors: Zhufeng Li, Niki Kilbertus
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.85 / 1.00

Summary

This paper introduces a novel graph distance measure for Acyclic Directed Mixed Graphs (ADMGs) designed to evaluate causal discovery methods, especially in the presence of latent confounding. The measure quantifies how differences between causal graphs distort cause-effect estimands using identification via fixing and a symbolic verifier, offering a more robust evaluation tool than existing metrics.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine as accurate causal inference is foundational for understanding disease mechanisms, developing effective treatments, and designing public health interventions. A robust method for evaluating causal graphs, especially when unobserved factors (latent confounding) influence health outcomes, ensures that medical research derives more reliable insights from observational data.

AI Health Application

This research provides a critical methodological improvement for Causal AI applications in health. AI systems in medicine often aim to discover causal relationships from complex data (e.g., electronic health records, omics data) to inform decisions such as personalized treatment recommendations, predicting disease progression, identifying drug targets, or evaluating the impact of health policies. The proposed graph distance measure helps to rigorously evaluate the quality and reliability of causal graphs discovered by AI algorithms, particularly when dealing with latent confounders. This, in turn, leads to more robust, interpretable, and trustworthy causal conclusions from medical AI systems, enhancing their utility in clinical practice and health research.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of properly evaluating discovered causal graphs, particularly under conditions of latent confounding, which is a significant hurdle in causal discovery.
  • Proposes a novel graph distance measure specifically tailored for Acyclic Directed Mixed Graphs (ADMGs), which are commonly used to represent causal structures with unobserved variables.
  • The proposed distance metric is grounded in the downstream task of cause-effect estimation, directly quantifying how structural differences between graphs distort the cause-effect estimands for various treatment-outcome pairs.
  • Methodologically, it employs 'identification via fixing' to establish identifiable causal effects in the presence of unobserved confounding, and a 'symbolic verifier' to precisely quantify the impact of graph differences on these estimands.
  • The behavior of the new measure is rigorously analyzed under different graph perturbations to assess its sensitivity, stability, and utility in practical scenarios.
  • The proposed metric is comparatively evaluated against existing graph distance metrics, demonstrating its advantages and performance characteristics in reflecting the true impact on causal inference.
  • Aims to provide a more accurate and robust evaluation framework for causal discovery algorithms, which is crucial for building reliable causal models in complex data environments.

Methodology

The methodology centers on defining a graph distance based on the distortion of cause-effect estimands in Acyclic Directed Mixed Graphs (ADMGs). It leverages 'identification via fixing' to establish identifiable causal effects under unobserved confounding and employs a 'symbolic verifier' to precisely quantify how structural differences between graphs alter these estimands. The resulting measure is then analyzed for its behavior under various graph perturbations and compared with existing metrics.

Key Findings

The paper introduces a novel graph distance measure that effectively quantifies how differences between ADMGs distort cause-effect estimands, particularly under unobserved confounding. Analysis demonstrates its behavior under various graph perturbations and suggests it offers a more robust and relevant evaluation metric for causal discovery compared to existing methods, as it directly assesses the impact on downstream causal inference tasks.

This can lead to more accurate disease etiology understanding, optimized treatment strategies in personalized medicine, and evidence-based public health policies that effectively account for unobserved confounders, ultimately improving patient outcomes and public health.

Abstract

Causal discovery aims to recover graphs that represent causal relations among given variables from observations, and new methods are constantly being proposed. Increasingly, the community raises questions about how much progress is made, because properly evaluating discovered graphs remains notoriously difficult, particularly under latent confounding. We propose a graph distance measure for acyclic directed mixed graphs (ADMGs) based on the downstream task of cause-effect estimation under unobserved confounding. Our approach uses identification via fixing and a symbolic verifier to quantify how graph differences distort cause-effect estimands for different treatment-outcome pairs. We analyze the behavior of the measure under different graph perturbations and compare it against existing distance metrics.
