Summary

This paper introduces POEMS (Product Of Experts for Interpretable Multiomics Integration using Sparse Decoding), an unsupervised probabilistic framework designed to integrate diverse molecular layers for disease understanding. POEMS overcomes the common tradeoff between predictive performance and interpretability in deep generative models by providing biomarker-based insights without sacrificing nonlinear expressiveness, demonstrating its efficacy in cancer subtyping.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant for unraveling the complexity of diseases by providing interpretable multiomics integration, enabling the discovery of disease biomarkers and improving disease subtyping, which is critical for personalized medicine and targeted therapies.

AI Health Application

The paper describes an AI/ML framework (POEMS) designed for interpretable integration of multi-omic data. This AI application is directly relevant to health for purposes such as identifying disease subtypes (e.g., cancer subtyping), discovering new biomarkers for diagnosis and prognosis, and understanding the complex molecular underpinnings of diseases to potentially guide treatment strategies. The emphasis on interpretability is crucial for clinical adoption of AI in healthcare.

Key Points

  • Addresses the key challenge in multiomics integration: the tradeoff between achieving high predictive performance and maintaining model interpretability in deep generative models.
  • Introduces POEMS, an unsupervised probabilistic framework that simultaneously preserves predictive performance and delivers interpretability without linearizing the network.
  • Achieves interpretability through three core mechanisms: 1) sparse connections mapping features to latent factors for direct biomarker discovery, 2) a Product of Experts (PoE) model for cross-omic associations in a shared latent space, and 3) a gating network that quantifies the adaptive contribution of each omic to representation learning.
  • Incorporates an efficient sparse decoder as part of its architectural innovations.
  • Evaluated in a cancer subtyping case study, POEMS demonstrates competitive clustering and classification performance.
  • The model's key finding is the coexistence of biomarker-based insights and predictive accuracy in multiomics representation learning, offering a novel set of interpretations.
  • The framework's ability to identify sparse feature-to-latent factor mappings directly facilitates biomarker discovery.

Methodology

POEMS is an unsupervised probabilistic framework built on a Product of Experts (PoE) model for multi-omic integration into a shared latent space. Interpretability is achieved via sparse connections that map features to latent factors, directly enabling biomarker identification. A gating network adaptively computes and reports the contributions of each omic, and the architecture includes an efficient sparse decoder.

Key Findings

POEMS achieved competitive clustering and classification performance in a cancer subtyping case study. Crucially, it demonstrated that high predictive accuracy can coexist with biomarker-based interpretability in multiomics representation learning, providing novel insights into disease mechanisms and omic contributions.

Clinical Impact

By enabling interpretable biomarker discovery and improved disease subtyping from multiomics data, POEMS has the potential to enhance diagnostic accuracy, facilitate personalized treatment strategies, and accelerate the development of targeted therapies, particularly in complex diseases like cancer. Its interpretability could increase clinician trust and adoption of AI-driven insights.

Abstract

Integrating different molecular layers, i.e., multiomics data, is crucial for unraveling the complexity of diseases; yet, most deep generative models either prioritize predictive performance at the expense of interpretability or enforce interpretability by linearizing the decoder, thereby weakening the network's nonlinear expressiveness. To overcome this tradeoff, we introduce POEMS: Product Of Experts for Interpretable Multiomics Integration using Sparse Decoding, an unsupervised probabilistic framework that preserves predictive performance while providing interpretability. POEMS provides interpretability without linearizing any part of the network by 1) mapping features to latent factors using sparse connections, which directly translates to biomarker discovery, 2) allowing for cross-omic associations through a shared latent space using product of experts model, and 3) reporting contributions of each omic by a gating network that adaptively computes their influence in the representation learning. Additionally, we present an efficient sparse decoder. In a cancer subtyping case study, POEMS achieves competitive clustering and classification performance while offering our novel set of interpretations, demonstrating that biomarker based insight and predictive accuracy can coexist in multiomics representation learning.
