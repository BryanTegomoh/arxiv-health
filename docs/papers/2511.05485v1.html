MIMIC-SR-ICD11: A Dataset for Narrative-Based Diagnosis

Summary

This paper introduces MIMIC-SR-ICD11, a novel large English diagnostic dataset derived from EHR discharge notes and aligned with WHO ICD-11 terminology, aiming to capture rich narrative details for improved diagnosis. It also proposes LL-Rank, a likelihood-based re-ranking framework that consistently outperforms existing baselines by leveraging PMI-based scoring to enhance semantic compatibility and mitigate label frequency bias in narrative-based diagnosis.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for advancing accurate disease diagnosis by utilizing the nuanced details often present in patient narratives but lost in structured EHRs, thereby enabling earlier detection and more precise interventions. Its alignment with ICD-11 facilitates global standardization in medical coding and epidemiological research.

AI Health Application

The AI application aims to improve disease diagnosis by developing models that can accurately extract and classify diagnostic information from unstructured, free-text clinical narratives (self-reports and discharge notes) and map them to standardized medical codes (ICD-11). This can assist clinicians in diagnosis, enhance the efficiency and accuracy of medical coding, and improve data quality for healthcare management, research, and public health.

Key Points

  • **Dataset Creation:** Introduction of MIMIC-SR-ICD11, a large English diagnostic dataset constructed from Electronic Health Record (EHR) discharge notes.
  • **ICD-11 Alignment:** The dataset is natively aligned to the World Health Organization's (WHO) ICD-11 terminology, promoting standardization and global applicability.
  • **Addressing EHR Limitations:** The dataset is designed to preserve clinically salient signals from narrative self-reports that are often attenuated or omitted in templated EHR documentation.
  • **Novel Framework:** Presentation of LL-Rank, a likelihood-based re-ranking framework specifically for narrative-based disease diagnosis.
  • **LL-Rank Mechanism:** LL-Rank computes a length-normalized joint likelihood of each diagnostic label given the clinical report context and subtracts the corresponding report-free prior likelihood for that label.
  • **Performance Superiority:** LL-Rank consistently outperforms a strong generation-plus-mapping (GenMap) baseline across seven different model backbones.
  • **Core Innovation:** Ablation experiments reveal that LL-Rank's performance gains primarily stem from its PMI (Pointwise Mutual Information)-based scoring, which effectively isolates semantic compatibility from label frequency bias.

Methodology

The study involved creating MIMIC-SR-ICD11, a large English diagnostic dataset derived from EHR discharge notes, meticulously aligned with WHO ICD-11. They then developed LL-Rank, a likelihood-based re-ranking framework that calculates the length-normalized joint likelihood of a label given the clinical report and subtracts its prior likelihood. This framework was evaluated against a strong GenMap baseline across seven distinct model backbones, with ablation studies performed to dissect the sources of LL-Rank's performance gains.

Key Findings

LL-Rank consistently demonstrated superior performance over a robust generation-plus-mapping baseline (GenMap) across all seven tested model backbones for narrative-based diagnosis. The primary factor contributing to LL-Rank's effectiveness was identified through ablation experiments as its PMI-based scoring, which successfully separates semantic compatibility from inherent label frequency bias.

Clinical Impact

This work holds significant clinical potential by enabling more accurate and comprehensive disease diagnosis, leveraging the rich, often subtle, information in clinical narratives that structured EHRs might miss. This could lead to improved patient outcomes through earlier and more precise interventions for both acute and chronic conditions, while also streamlining medical coding, health record management, and epidemiological research through standardized ICD-11 alignment.

Abstract

Disease diagnosis is a central pillar of modern healthcare, enabling early detection and timely intervention for acute conditions while guiding lifestyle adjustments and medication regimens to prevent or slow chronic disease. Self-reports preserve clinically salient signals that templated electronic health record (EHR) documentation often attenuates or omits, especially subtle but consequential details. To operationalize this shift, we introduce MIMIC-SR-ICD11, a large English diagnostic dataset built from EHR discharge notes and natively aligned to WHO ICD-11 terminology. We further present LL-Rank, a likelihood-based re-ranking framework that computes a length-normalized joint likelihood of each label given the clinical report context and subtracts the corresponding report-free prior likelihood for that label. Across seven model backbones, LL-Rank consistently outperforms a strong generation-plus-mapping baseline (GenMap). Ablation experiments show that LL-Rank's gains primarily stem from its PMI-based scoring, which isolates semantic compatibility from label frequency bias.
