CureAgent: A Training-Free Executor-Analyst Framework for Clinical Reasoning

arXiv ID: 2512.05576v1
Published: 2025-12-05
Authors: Ting-Ting Xie, Yixin Zhang
Categories: cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00

Summary

This paper introduces CureAgent, an Executor-Analyst Framework designed to overcome "Context Utilization Failure" in small LLM-based clinical agents by decoupling tool execution from clinical reasoning. By orchestrating specialized TxAgents (Executors) with long-context foundation models (Analysts) and employing a Stratified Ensemble strategy, it achieves state-of-the-art performance on CURE-Bench without costly finetuning. The work provides insights into context scaling and toolset complexity, laying a foundation for trustworthy AI-driven therapeutics.

This research is crucial for advancing AI in healthcare by addressing core challenges in clinical reasoning, ensuring AI models can reliably integrate and act upon biomedical evidence to make accurate diagnoses and inform treatment strategies, thereby improving patient care.

The research aims to develop a more effective and trustworthy AI framework (CureAgent) that can assist with or automate complex clinical reasoning and diagnosis tasks. This has direct applications in clinical decision support systems, AI-powered diagnostic tools, and potentially in guiding the development and selection of therapeutic interventions.

Key Points

  • Identifies "Context Utilization Failure" in existing small LLM clinical agents (e.g., TxAgent), where retrieved biomedical evidence is not effectively grounded in diagnosis.
  • Proposes the Executor-Analyst Framework, a modular architecture that separates the syntactic precision of tool execution (Executors, specialized TxAgents) from the semantic robustness of clinical reasoning (Analysts, long-context foundation models).
  • Demonstrates that a Stratified Ensemble strategy significantly outperforms global pooling, improving performance by preserving evidentiary diversity and mitigating information bottlenecks.
  • Reveals a "Context-Performance Paradox": extending reasoning contexts beyond 12k tokens can degrade accuracy due to the introduction of noise.
  • Highlights the "Curse of Dimensionality" in action spaces, indicating that expanding toolsets necessitates hierarchical retrieval strategies for effective reasoning.
  • Achieves state-of-the-art performance on CURE-Bench through training-free architectural engineering, circumventing the need for expensive end-to-end finetuning.
  • Aims to establish a scalable, agile, and trustworthy foundation for the development of next-generation AI-driven therapeutics.

Methodology

The methodology centers on a modular Executor-Analyst Framework. It leverages specialized TxAgents as 'Executors' for tool execution and long-context foundation models as 'Analysts' for semantic clinical reasoning. This architecture is enhanced with a 'Stratified Ensemble' strategy to optimize evidentiary diversity and mitigate information bottlenecks. The entire approach is designed as training-free architectural engineering, avoiding extensive model finetuning.

Abstract

Current clinical agent built on small LLMs, such as TxAgent suffer from a \textit{Context Utilization Failure}, where models successfully retrieve biomedical evidence due to supervised finetuning but fail to ground their diagnosis in that information. In this work, we propose the Executor-Analyst Framework, a modular architecture that decouples the syntactic precision of tool execution from the semantic robustness of clinical reasoning. By orchestrating specialized TxAgents (Executors) with long-context foundation models (Analysts), we mitigate the reasoning deficits observed in monolithic models. Beyond simple modularity, we demonstrate that a Stratified Ensemble strategy significantly outperforms global pooling by preserving evidentiary diversity, effectively addressing the information bottleneck. Furthermore, our stress tests reveal critical scaling insights: (1) a \textit{Context-Performance Paradox}, where extending reasoning contexts beyond 12k tokens introduces noise that degrades accuracy; and (2) the \textit{Curse of Dimensionality} in action spaces, where expanding toolsets necessitates hierarchical retrieval strategies. Crucially, our approach underscores the potential of training-free architectural engineering, achieving state-of-the-art performance on CURE-Bench without the need for expensive end-to-end finetuning. This provides a scalable, agile foundation for the next generation of trustworthy AI-driven therapeutics. Code has been released on https://github.com/June01/CureAgent.
