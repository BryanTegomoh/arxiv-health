TAB-DRW: A DFT-based Robust Watermark for Generative Tabular Data

arXiv ID: 2511.21600v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Yizhou Zhao, Xiang Li, Peter Song, Qi Long, Weijie Su
Categories: cs.CR, cs.LG

Summary

TAB-DRW addresses the critical need for traceability and misuse prevention in high-fidelity synthetic tabular data generated by AI, particularly relevant in sensitive sectors like healthcare. It proposes an efficient and robust post-editing watermarking scheme that embeds signals in the frequency domain using Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) and a novel rank-based pseudorandom bit generation. Experiments confirm its strong detectability and robustness against common post-processing attacks, while preserving high data fidelity and fully supporting mixed-type features.

Methodology

TAB-DRW operates as a post-editing watermarking scheme. It normalizes heterogeneous features (both discrete and continuous) using the Yeo-Johnson transformation followed by standardization. The Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) is then applied to the processed data. Watermark signals are embedded by adaptively adjusting the imaginary parts of selected DFT entries, guided by precomputed pseudorandom bits. A novel rank-based pseudorandom bit generation method is employed to enhance robustness and efficiency, facilitating row-wise retrieval without requiring additional storage.

Key Findings

TAB-DRW achieves strong detectability for embedded watermarks and demonstrates high robustness against common post-processing attacks. The method successfully preserves high data fidelity, ensuring the utility and statistical properties of the synthetic data are maintained. Furthermore, it fully supports and effectively handles mixed-type features (discrete and continuous), a critical requirement for real-world tabular datasets.

Abstract

The rise of generative AI has enabled the production of high-fidelity synthetic tabular data across fields such as healthcare, finance, and public policy, raising growing concerns about data provenance and misuse. Watermarking offers a promising solution to address these concerns by ensuring the traceability of synthetic data, but existing methods face many limitations: they are computationally expensive due to reliance on large diffusion models, struggle with mixed discrete-continuous data, or lack robustness to post-modifications. To address them, we propose TAB-DRW, an efficient and robust post-editing watermarking scheme for generative tabular data. TAB-DRW embeds watermark signals in the frequency domain: it normalizes heterogeneous features via the Yeo-Johnson transformation and standardization, applies the discrete Fourier transform (DFT), and adjusts the imaginary parts of adaptively selected entries according to precomputed pseudorandom bits. To further enhance robustness and efficiency, we introduce a novel rank-based pseudorandom bit generation method that enables row-wise retrieval without incurring storage overhead. Experiments on five benchmark tabular datasets show that TAB-DRW achieves strong detectability and robustness against common post-processing attacks, while preserving high data fidelity and fully supporting mixed-type features.
