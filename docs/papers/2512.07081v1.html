ClinNoteAgents: An LLM Multi-Agent System for Predicting and Interpreting Heart Failure 30-Day Readmission from Clinical Notes

arXiv ID: 2512.07081v1
Published: 2025-12-08
Authors: Rongjia Zhou, Chengzhuo Li, Carl Yang, Jiaying Lu
Categories: cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

ClinNoteAgents is an LLM-based multi-agent system designed to predict and interpret 30-day heart failure (HF) readmission risk from free-text clinical notes. It transforms unstructured notes into structured clinical/social risk factors and clinician-style abstractions, demonstrating strong performance in risk factor identification and readmission prediction. This approach offers a scalable and interpretable solution, reducing reliance on structured data and manual efforts in healthcare systems.

Medical Relevance

Heart failure readmission imposes a significant clinical and financial burden. This system leverages rich, often underutilized, unstructured clinical data to proactively identify patients at high risk, potentially enabling timely interventions to improve patient outcomes and reduce healthcare costs.

AI Health Application

It transforms free-text notes into structured risk factors and clinician-style abstractions to enhance readmission prediction and provide interpretable insights for healthcare providers, thereby serving as a clinical decision support tool.

Key Points

  • Addresses the problem of high 30-day heart failure readmission rates and the underutilization of rich, free-text clinical notes for risk analysis due to their unstructured nature.
  • Introduces ClinNoteAgents, a novel LLM-based multi-agent framework specifically engineered to process complex, free-text clinical notes.
  • Performs a dual transformation: (1) extracting structured representations of clinical and social risk factors for association analysis, and (2) generating clinician-style abstractions for HF 30-day readmission prediction.
  • Evaluated on a dataset comprising 3,544 clinical notes from 2,065 patients, where the 30-day HF readmission rate was 35.16%.
  • Achieved strong performance in extracting relevant risk factors directly from free-text, identifying key contributing factors, and accurately predicting 30-day HF readmission risk.
  • Significantly reduces the reliance on structured EHR fields and minimizes the need for extensive manual annotation and traditional model training.
  • Provides a scalable and interpretable approach to note-based HF readmission risk modeling, especially beneficial for data-limited healthcare systems.

Methodology

ClinNoteAgents is an LLM-based multi-agent system designed to process free-text clinical notes. It employs two primary mechanisms: 1) converting free-text into structured representations of clinical and social risk factors suitable for association analysis, and 2) generating clinician-style abstractions from the notes to directly predict 30-day heart failure readmission. This framework aims to overcome the challenges of jargon, abbreviations, and misspellings in clinical text without relying on traditional expert-crafted rules or extensive ontologies.

Key Findings

The evaluation demonstrated that ClinNoteAgents achieved strong performance in three critical areas: effectively extracting clinical and social risk factors from unstructured free-text notes, accurately identifying the key factors contributing to 30-day HF readmission risk, and robustly predicting 30-day HF readmission risk.

Clinical Impact

This system offers a scalable and interpretable solution for HF readmission risk modeling, particularly valuable for healthcare systems with limited structured data. By efficiently converting complex clinical narratives into actionable insights, it can facilitate proactive identification of high-risk patients, guide targeted interventions, and potentially reduce rehospitalization rates, while simultaneously reducing the burden of manual data annotation and model development.

Abstract

Heart failure (HF) is one of the leading causes of rehospitalization among older adults in the United States. Although clinical notes contain rich, detailed patient information and make up a large portion of electronic health records (EHRs), they remain underutilized for HF readmission risk analysis. Traditional computational models for HF readmission often rely on expert-crafted rules, medical thesauri, and ontologies to interpret clinical notes, which are typically written under time pressure and may contain misspellings, abbreviations, and domain-specific jargon. We present ClinNoteAgents, an LLM-based multi-agent framework that transforms free-text clinical notes into (1) structured representations of clinical and social risk factors for association analysis and (2) clinician-style abstractions for HF 30-day readmission prediction. We evaluate ClinNoteAgents on 3,544 notes from 2,065 patients (readmission rate=35.16%), demonstrating strong performance in extracting risk factors from free-text, identifying key contributing factors, and predicting readmission risk. By reducing reliance on structured fields and minimizing manual annotation and model training, ClinNoteAgents provides a scalable and interpretable approach to note-based HF readmission risk modeling in data-limited healthcare systems.
