Summary

This paper introduces a novel two-stage methodology combining voxel sparsification and Submanifold Sparse Convolutional Networks (SSCNs) for automated, high-resolution 3D segmentation of kidneys and kidney tumors in Computed Tomography (CT) images. This approach achieves state-of-the-art competitive accuracy on the KiTS23 challenge while significantly reducing computational resources, offering substantial improvements in inference time and GPU memory usage.

Medical Relevance

Automating the accurate and efficient 3D segmentation of kidneys and kidney tumors from CT images is critical for clinical oncology and urology, enabling faster diagnosis, precise staging, personalized treatment planning, and consistent monitoring of renal cancer, thereby improving patient care and clinical workflow efficiency.

AI Health Application

The paper develops a novel AI methodology using voxel sparsification and submanifold sparse convolutional networks for automated, high-resolution 3D segmentation of kidneys and kidney tumors in Computed Tomography (CT) images. This application aims to reduce the manual effort and time required by clinicians, improve the accuracy of tumor delineation for diagnosis and treatment planning in renal cancer, and facilitate routine quantitative analyses in the clinical setting by making the process computationally efficient.

Key Points

  • Manual tumor delineation in radiological images is a specialized, time-consuming task, creating a bottleneck for routine quantitative clinical analyses.
  • Traditional 3D Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) struggle with the high voxel counts in 3D medical scans, often requiring downsampling or patch-based processing, which can lose information or context.
  • The proposed methodology addresses this by employing a two-stage process: initial voxel sparsification followed by the application of Submanifold Sparse Convolutional Networks (SSCNs).
  • This approach allows for segmentation with high-resolution native 3D inputs, circumventing the need for downsampling or patches.
  • The method achieved competitive Dice similarity coefficients on the KiTS23 challenge: 95.8% for kidneys + masses, 85.7% for tumors + cysts, and 80.3% for tumors alone.
  • Crucially, it demonstrates significant computational efficiency improvements, including up to a 60% reduction in inference time and up to a 75% reduction in VRAM usage compared to equivalent dense architectures.
  • The technique is specifically demonstrated for automated 3D segmentation of kidneys and kidney tumors in CT images, offering a robust and resource-efficient solution.

Methodology

The paper introduces a two-stage deep learning methodology. The first stage involves voxel sparsification, which intelligently prunes non-essential voxels to reduce the input data size. The second stage applies Submanifold Sparse Convolutional Networks (SSCNs) to the sparsified, high-resolution 3D input. This allows for native 3D model architecture processing without resorting to traditional methods like downsampling or using image patches, while significantly reducing computational demands.

Key Findings

The proposed method achieved Dice similarity coefficients competitive with KiTS23 challenge winners, specifically 95.8% for kidneys + masses, 85.7% for tumors + cysts, and 80.3% for tumors alone. A major finding is the significant computational improvement: up to a 60% reduction in inference time and up to a 75% reduction in VRAM usage compared to an equivalent dense convolutional network architecture.

Clinical Impact

This automated segmentation method can significantly reduce the laborious and time-consuming manual efforts of radiologists and clinicians, freeing up valuable specialist time. It facilitates routine quantitative analyses of tumor characteristics (e.g., volume, growth), which are currently difficult to perform. This can lead to more consistent diagnostics, optimized treatment planning (e.g., surgical resection margins, radiation therapy), and improved monitoring of treatment response for renal cancer patients, ultimately enhancing precision medicine and patient outcomes.

Abstract

The accurate delineation of tumours in radiological images like Computed Tomography is a very specialised and time-consuming task, and currently a bottleneck preventing quantitative analyses to be performed routinely in the clinical setting. For this reason, developing methods for the automated segmentation of tumours in medical imaging is of the utmost importance and has driven significant efforts in recent years. However, challenges regarding the impracticality of 3D scans, given the large amount of voxels to be analysed, usually requires the downsampling of such images or using patches thereof when applying traditional convolutional neural networks. To overcome this problem, in this paper we propose a new methodology that uses, divided into two stages, voxel sparsification and submanifold sparse convolutional networks. This method allows segmentations to be performed with high-resolution inputs and a native 3D model architecture, obtaining state-of-the-art accuracies while significantly reducing the computational resources needed in terms of GPU memory and time. We studied the deployment of this methodology in the context of Computed Tomography images of renal cancer patients from the KiTS23 challenge, and our method achieved results competitive with the challenge winners, with Dice similarity coefficients of 95.8% for kidneys + masses, 85.7% for tumours + cysts, and 80.3% for tumours alone. Crucially, our method also offers significant computational improvements, achieving up to a 60% reduction in inference time and up to a 75\% reduction in VRAM usage compared to an equivalent dense architecture, across both CPU and various GPU cards tested.
