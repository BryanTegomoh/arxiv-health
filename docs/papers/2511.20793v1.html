Adversarial Multi-Task Learning for Liver Tumor Segmentation, Dynamic Enhancement Regression, and Classification

Summary

This paper introduces MTI-Net, a novel end-to-end adversarial multi-task learning framework designed to simultaneously perform liver tumor segmentation, dynamic enhancement regression, and classification. By integrating multi-domain information fusion and task interaction modules, MTI-Net effectively leverages dynamic MRI data and inter-task dependencies, demonstrating high performance on a dataset of 238 subjects for clinical liver tumor assessment.

Medical Relevance

Simultaneously automating liver tumor segmentation, dynamic enhancement analysis, and classification is crucial for accurate and efficient clinical assessment, diagnosis, and treatment planning of liver cancers, reducing inter-observer variability and improving diagnostic precision.

AI Health Application

The AI application is an end-to-end multi-task learning framework (MTI-Net) that utilizes deep learning (Transformer network, adversarial learning) and computer vision techniques to automate and improve the accuracy of liver tumor segmentation, dynamic enhancement regression, and classification from dynamic MRI scans. Its purpose is to assist clinicians in the assessment and diagnosis of liver tumors, functioning as a medical AI diagnostic aid.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical clinical tasks of liver tumor segmentation, dynamic enhancement regression, and classification simultaneously within a single end-to-end framework, a first in prior work.
  • Proposes the Multi-Task Interaction adversarial learning Network (MTI-Net), which is an integrated framework designed to tackle these tasks simultaneously.
  • Incorporates Multi-domain Information Entropy Fusion (MdIEF) utilizing entropy-aware, high-frequency spectral information to effectively integrate features from both frequency and spectral domains, enhancing dynamic MRI data extraction and utilization.
  • Introduces a task interaction module that establishes higher-order consistency between the segmentation and regression tasks, fostering inter-task synergy for improved overall performance.
  • Employs a novel task-driven discriminator (TDD) specifically designed to capture internal high-order relationships among the multiple integrated tasks.
  • Utilizes a shallow Transformer network for positional encoding to effectively capture relationships within dynamic MRI sequences, crucial for dynamic enhancement analysis.
  • Demonstrates high performance across all three tasks on a dataset of 238 subjects, indicating strong potential for assisting in the clinical assessment and diagnosis of liver tumors.

Methodology

The proposed method is the Multi-Task Interaction adversarial learning Network (MTI-Net), an end-to-end deep learning framework. It features Multi-domain Information Entropy Fusion (MdIEF) for integrating frequency and spectral domain features from dynamic MRI, a task interaction module for establishing higher-order consistency between segmentation and regression, and a novel task-driven discriminator (TDD) to capture high-order inter-task relationships. Additionally, a shallow Transformer network is employed for positional encoding to extract relationships within dynamic MRI sequences. The overall architecture is built on an adversarial learning paradigm.

This can lead to more consistent diagnoses, improved treatment planning, and potentially earlier detection of disease progression, thereby enhancing patient care and operational efficiency.

Abstract

Liver tumor segmentation, dynamic enhancement regression, and classification are critical for clinical assessment and diagnosis. However, no prior work has attempted to achieve these tasks simultaneously in an end-to-end framework, primarily due to the lack of an effective framework that captures inter-task relevance for mutual improvement and the absence of a mechanism to extract dynamic MRI information effectively. To address these challenges, we propose the Multi-Task Interaction adversarial learning Network (MTI-Net), a novel integrated framework designed to tackle these tasks simultaneously. MTI-Net incorporates Multi-domain Information Entropy Fusion (MdIEF), which utilizes entropy-aware, high-frequency spectral information to effectively integrate features from both frequency and spectral domains, enhancing the extraction and utilization of dynamic MRI data. The network also introduces a task interaction module that establishes higher-order consistency between segmentation and regression, thus fostering inter-task synergy and improving overall performance. Additionally, we designed a novel task-driven discriminator (TDD) to capture internal high-order relationships between tasks. For dynamic MRI information extraction, we employ a shallow Transformer network to perform positional encoding, which captures the relationships within dynamic MRI sequences. In experiments on a dataset of 238 subjects, MTI-Net demonstrates high performance across multiple tasks, indicating its strong potential for assisting in the clinical assessment of liver tumors.
