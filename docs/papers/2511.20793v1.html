Adversarial Multi-Task Learning for Liver Tumor Segmentation, Dynamic Enhancement Regression, and Classification

Summary

This paper introduces MTI-Net, an end-to-end adversarial multi-task learning framework for simultaneous liver tumor segmentation, dynamic enhancement regression, and classification from MRI. MTI-Net utilizes Multi-domain Information Entropy Fusion (MdIEF) for dynamic MRI data, a task interaction module for inter-task synergy, and a novel task-driven discriminator (TDD) to capture internal task relationships. Experiments on 238 subjects demonstrate high performance across all tasks, indicating significant potential for clinical assessment of liver tumors.

Medical Relevance

Accurate liver tumor segmentation, precise dynamic enhancement characterization, and reliable classification are indispensable for timely diagnosis, staging, treatment planning, and prognosis monitoring in patients with liver diseases and malignancies.

AI Health Application

The research proposes an AI-driven multi-task learning network (MTI-Net) that performs liver tumor segmentation, dynamic enhancement regression, and classification from MRI data. This system is designed to assist clinicians in the diagnostic assessment of liver tumors, potentially improving accuracy and efficiency in the detection and characterization of liver cancer.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical gap in an end-to-end framework for simultaneously performing liver tumor segmentation, dynamic enhancement regression, and classification.
  • Proposes MTI-Net, a novel integrated adversarial multi-task learning network designed to tackle these three tasks concurrently.
  • Incorporates Multi-domain Information Entropy Fusion (MdIEF) to effectively integrate features from both frequency and spectral domains, enhancing dynamic MRI data extraction through entropy-aware high-frequency spectral information.
  • Introduces a task interaction module that establishes higher-order consistency between segmentation and regression tasks, fostering inter-task synergy for improved overall performance.
  • Designs a novel task-driven discriminator (TDD) specifically to capture and leverage internal high-order relationships between the segmentation, regression, and classification tasks.
  • Employs a shallow Transformer network for positional encoding to effectively capture intricate relationships within dynamic MRI sequences.
  • Demonstrates high performance across all tasks on a dataset of 238 subjects, showcasing strong potential for assisting in the clinical assessment and diagnosis of liver tumors.

Methodology

The proposed MTI-Net is an end-to-end adversarial multi-task learning framework. It incorporates a Multi-domain Information Entropy Fusion (MdIEF) module that uses entropy-aware, high-frequency spectral information to integrate features from both frequency and spectral domains, optimizing dynamic MRI data utilization. A task interaction module establishes higher-order consistency between segmentation and regression tasks to enhance synergy. A novel task-driven discriminator (TDD) is employed to capture and leverage internal high-order relationships among all tasks. Additionally, a shallow Transformer network performs positional encoding to extract temporal relationships within dynamic MRI sequences. The entire framework is trained adversarially to jointly optimize performance across all three tasks.

Key Findings

MTI-Net achieved high performance across all three critical tasks: liver tumor segmentation, dynamic enhancement regression, and classification, as demonstrated on a dataset of 238 subjects. This indicates the framework's effectiveness in capturing inter-task relevance and improving overall diagnostic capabilities for liver tumors.

Clinical Impact

This research has strong potential to significantly assist clinicians in the automated, precise assessment and diagnosis of liver tumors from dynamic MRI. It could lead to more accurate tumor segmentation for surgical planning or targeted therapies, better quantitative characterization of tumor enhancement patterns for differential diagnosis, and improved overall classification, thereby enhancing patient management and outcomes.

Abstract

Liver tumor segmentation, dynamic enhancement regression, and classification are critical for clinical assessment and diagnosis. However, no prior work has attempted to achieve these tasks simultaneously in an end-to-end framework, primarily due to the lack of an effective framework that captures inter-task relevance for mutual improvement and the absence of a mechanism to extract dynamic MRI information effectively. To address these challenges, we propose the Multi-Task Interaction adversarial learning Network (MTI-Net), a novel integrated framework designed to tackle these tasks simultaneously. MTI-Net incorporates Multi-domain Information Entropy Fusion (MdIEF), which utilizes entropy-aware, high-frequency spectral information to effectively integrate features from both frequency and spectral domains, enhancing the extraction and utilization of dynamic MRI data. The network also introduces a task interaction module that establishes higher-order consistency between segmentation and regression, thus fostering inter-task synergy and improving overall performance. Additionally, we designed a novel task-driven discriminator (TDD) to capture internal high-order relationships between tasks. For dynamic MRI information extraction, we employ a shallow Transformer network to perform positional encoding, which captures the relationships within dynamic MRI sequences. In experiments on a dataset of 238 subjects, MTI-Net demonstrates high performance across multiple tasks, indicating its strong potential for assisting in the clinical assessment of liver tumors.
