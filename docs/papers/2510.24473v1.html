Summary

This study presents a comparative methodological analysis of six machine learning models for survival analysis (MLSA) using a large dataset of nearly 45,000 colorectal cancer patients. It identified XGBoost-AFT as the best-performing model for predicting patient survival while accounting for censored data. The research highlights the significant potential of MLSA to enhance prognostic accuracy and support clinical decision-making in oncology.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medical practice by providing a robust methodology for identifying superior machine learning models that can accurately predict survival outcomes for cancer patients, thereby aiding personalized treatment strategies and improving prognostic counseling.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development and comparative analysis of various machine learning algorithms to predict survival outcomes for colorectal cancer patients. This aims to enhance the accuracy of prognostic assessments and provide data-driven insights to support medical professionals in making more informed treatment and care decisions.

Methodology

A comparative methodological analysis was performed on six machine learning models for survival analysis (Random Survival Forest, Gradient Boosting for Survival Analysis, Survival SVM, XGBoost-Cox, XGBoost-AFT, LightGBM). The models were trained and evaluated using a dataset of nearly 45,000 colorectal cancer patients from Hospital-Based Cancer Registries. Hyperparameter optimization was conducted with various samplers. Model performance was assessed using the Concordance Index (C-Index), C-Index IPCW (Inverse Probability of Censoring Weights), time-dependent Area Under the Curve (AUC), and Integrated Brier Score (IBS). Predictor importance and model interpretability were analyzed using SHAP values and permutation importance.

Key Findings

XGBoost-AFT emerged as the top-performing model, achieving a C-Index of 0.7618 and C-Index IPCW of 0.7532. It consistently outperformed other advanced MLSA models, including GBSA and RSF, in predicting survival outcomes for colorectal cancer patients. The study also highlighted that MLSA models provide significantly improved survival predictions compared to traditional classification algorithms.

Clinical Impact

The enhanced accuracy in survival prediction offered by MLSA models, particularly XGBoost-AFT, has direct clinical implications. It can inform more precise patient risk stratification, guide treatment selection, optimize resource allocation, and provide more reliable prognoses to patients and their families, ultimately improving patient care and shared decision-making in colorectal cancer management.

Abstract

This study presents a comparative methodological analysis of six machine learning models for survival analysis (MLSA). Using data from nearly 45,000 colorectal cancer patients in the Hospital-Based Cancer Registries of S\~ao Paulo, we evaluated Random Survival Forest (RSF), Gradient Boosting for Survival Analysis (GBSA), Survival SVM (SSVM), XGBoost-Cox (XGB-Cox), XGBoost-AFT (XGB-AFT), and LightGBM (LGBM), capable of predicting survival considering censored data. Hyperparameter optimization was performed with different samplers, and model performance was assessed using the Concordance Index (C-Index), C-Index IPCW, time-dependent AUC, and Integrated Brier Score (IBS). Survival curves produced by the models were compared with predictions from classification algorithms, and predictor interpretation was conducted using SHAP and permutation importance. XGB-AFT achieved the best performance (C-Index = 0.7618; IPCW = 0.7532), followed by GBSA and RSF. The results highlight the potential and applicability of MLSA to improve survival prediction and support decision making.
