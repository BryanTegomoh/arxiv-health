Equivariant Multiscale Learned Invertible Reconstruction for Cone Beam CT: From Simulated to Real Data

Summary

This paper introduces LIRE++, a novel deep learning framework designed to significantly improve Cone Beam CT (CBCT) image quality and computational efficiency. By employing a rotationally-equivariant, multiscale, and invertible primal-dual scheme, LIRE++ addresses key challenges like data scarcity, memory constraints, and the need for fast inference in CBCT reconstruction. The method demonstrates superior performance over existing baselines on both simulated and real clinical data, offering a promising solution for enhancing CBCT applications.

Medical Relevance

Improving CBCT image quality is vital for enhancing diagnostic accuracy, treatment planning, and image guidance in various medical procedures, particularly in radiation oncology and interventional radiology, where precise anatomical visualization is paramount.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development and evaluation of a deep learning model (LIRE++) to enhance the image quality and reconstruction speed of Cone Beam CT (CBCT) scans. This aims to overcome current clinical limitations of CBCT, providing more accurate and reliable imaging for diagnosis, treatment planning (such as for radiation therapy), and intraoperative guidance, thereby improving patient outcomes.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical problem of lower image quality in Cone Beam CT (CBCT) compared to conventional CT, which limits its clinical utility.
  • Proposes LIRE++, an end-to-end rotationally-equivariant multiscale learned invertible primal-dual scheme for CBCT reconstruction.
  • LIRE++ incorporates memory optimizations and multiscale reconstruction to achieve fast training and inference at clinically relevant resolutions.
  • Utilizes rotational equivariance to improve the parameter efficiency of the deep learning model, aiding in robust learning from data.
  • Training data was generated using a newly developed fast quasi-Monte Carlo CBCT projection simulator, overcoming the common challenge of lacking ground truth data for deep learning in medical imaging.
  • Achieved an average improvement of 1 dB in Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR) over alternative deep learning baselines when evaluated on synthetic data.
  • Demonstrated significant clinical relevance by improving the average Mean Absolute Error (MAE) by 10 Hounsfield Units (HU) on real clinical data compared to a proprietary state-of-the-art hybrid deep-learning/iterative method.

Methodology

The methodology centers on LIRE++, an end-to-end deep learning model that leverages a rotationally-equivariant multiscale learned invertible primal-dual scheme. This architecture is engineered for memory efficiency and fast inference. To address the scarcity of ground truth data, the model was trained on simulated projection data generated by a custom-developed fast quasi-Monte Carlo CBCT projection simulator.

Key Findings

LIRE++ demonstrated superior performance in CBCT reconstruction, yielding an average 1 dB PSNR improvement on synthetic data over deep learning baselines. Crucially, on real clinical data, it reduced the average Mean Absolute Error (MAE) by 10 Hounsfield Units (HU) between the reconstruction and planning CT, significantly outperforming a state-of-the-art hybrid deep-learning/iterative method. The method's design also enables fast and memory-efficient operation.

Clinical Impact

This work has the potential to significantly enhance the clinical utility of CBCT by providing higher quality, faster reconstructed images. This can lead to more accurate diagnoses, more precise treatment planning in areas like radiation therapy where CBCT is used for patient positioning and dose calculation, and safer image-guided interventions by offering better soft tissue contrast and reduced artifacts.

Abstract

Cone Beam CT (CBCT) is an important imaging modality nowadays, however lower image quality of CBCT compared to more conventional Computed Tomography (CT) remains a limiting factor in CBCT applications. Deep learning reconstruction methods are a promising alternative to classical analytical and iterative reconstruction methods, but applying such methods to CBCT is often difficult due to the lack of ground truth data, memory limitations and the need for fast inference at clinically-relevant resolutions. In this work we propose LIRE++, an end-to-end rotationally-equivariant multiscale learned invertible primal-dual scheme for fast and memory-efficient CBCT reconstruction. Memory optimizations and multiscale reconstruction allow for fast training and inference, while rotational equivariance improves parameter efficiency. LIRE++ was trained on simulated projection data from a fast quasi-Monte Carlo CBCT projection simulator that we developed as well. Evaluated on synthetic data, LIRE++ gave an average improvement of 1 dB in Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio over alternative deep learning baselines. On real clinical data, LIRE++ improved the average Mean Absolute Error between the reconstruction and the corresponding planning CT by 10 Hounsfield Units with respect to current proprietary state-of-the-art hybrid deep-learning/iterative method.
