DARTS: A Drone-Based AI-Powered Real-Time Traffic Incident Detection System

arXiv ID: 2510.26004v1
Published: 2025-10-29
Authors: Bai Li, Achilleas Kourtellis, Rong Cao, Joseph Post, Brian Porter, Yu Zhang
Categories: cs.RO, cs.AI, cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

DARTS is a novel drone-based, AI-powered system for real-time traffic incident detection, addressing the limitations of conventional methods like slow response times and infrastructure dependency. It integrates drone mobility, thermal imaging, and a lightweight deep learning framework to achieve high detection accuracy and provide rapid incident verification, severity assessment, and congestion monitoring. A field test demonstrated DARTS detected a collision 12 minutes earlier than existing systems, offering significant potential for faster emergency response and reducing crash-related injuries and fatalities.

Medical Relevance

This system directly impacts public health by significantly reducing the time to detect and respond to traffic incidents, which are major causes of trauma, fatalities, and severe injuries. Faster emergency response facilitated by DARTS can lead to earlier medical intervention, improved patient outcomes, and a reduction in both morbidity and mortality associated with vehicular accidents.

AI Health Application

The AI (deep learning and computer vision) is applied to real-time drone-based surveillance footage to detect traffic incidents, assess severity, and monitor congestion. This AI-driven capability directly impacts health by enabling significantly faster identification of accidents, which in turn facilitates quicker dispatch of medical emergency services, thereby improving response times for trauma victims, potentially reducing fatalities and severe injuries, and preventing secondary crashes. It acts as an AI-powered early warning system for incidents requiring immediate medical intervention.

Key Points

  • **Addresses Conventional Method Shortcomings**: Overcomes issues with traditional incident detection, such as slow response, limited flexibility, infrastructure dependence, and separation of detection from verification, which contribute to fatalities, injuries, and congestion.
  • **Integrated System Architecture**: Combines high-mobility drones for adaptive aerial surveillance, thermal imaging for improved low-visibility performance and privacy protection, and a lightweight deep learning framework for real-time vehicle trajectory extraction and incident detection.
  • **Comprehensive Real-Time Monitoring**: Supports simultaneous online visual verification, severity assessment, and monitoring of incident-induced congestion propagation through a web-based interface.
  • **High Detection Accuracy**: Achieved 99% detection accuracy on a self-collected dataset, validating its core incident identification capabilities.
  • **Superior Field Performance**: In a real-world field test on Interstate 75 in Florida, DARTS detected and verified a rear-end collision 12 minutes earlier than the local transportation management center.
  • **Promotes Faster Emergency Response**: Earlier incident detection enables faster emergency medical services dispatch, proactive traffic control, and reduced secondary crash risk, directly impacting patient outcomes and public safety.
  • **Scalable and Cost-Effective Deployment**: Its flexible architecture reduces reliance on frequent physical patrols and dense infrastructure, suggesting potential for scalable and cost-effective use in remote or resource-constrained environments.

Methodology

DARTS employs drones equipped with thermal imaging cameras for adaptive aerial surveillance. A lightweight deep learning framework is utilized to process the real-time thermal imagery, enabling precise vehicle trajectory extraction and automated incident detection. The system integrates a web-based interface for concurrent online visual verification, severity assessment, and monitoring of incident-induced congestion propagation.

Key Findings

The system demonstrated 99% detection accuracy on a self-collected dataset. A critical field test on Interstate 75 revealed DARTS detected and verified a rear-end collision 12 minutes earlier than the local transportation management center. It also successfully monitored incident-induced congestion propagation and provided simultaneous online visual verification and severity assessment.

Clinical Impact

The primary clinical impact is the potential for drastically reducing prehospital times for trauma patients involved in traffic incidents. By providing incident detection up to 12 minutes faster, DARTS can enable earlier dispatch of emergency medical services (EMS), leading to quicker arrival at the scene, faster patient stabilization, and more rapid transport to definitive care. This accelerated response time is crucial for patients with life-threatening injuries, potentially improving survival rates and reducing long-term disability, while also mitigating the risk of secondary crashes and subsequent additional injuries.

Abstract

Rapid and reliable incident detection is critical for reducing crash-related fatalities, injuries, and congestion. However, conventional methods, such as closed-circuit television, dashcam footage, and sensor-based detection, separate detection from verification, suffer from limited flexibility, and require dense infrastructure or high penetration rates, restricting adaptability and scalability to shifting incident hotspots. To overcome these challenges, we developed DARTS, a drone-based, AI-powered real-time traffic incident detection system. DARTS integrates drones' high mobility and aerial perspective for adaptive surveillance, thermal imaging for better low-visibility performance and privacy protection, and a lightweight deep learning framework for real-time vehicle trajectory extraction and incident detection. The system achieved 99% detection accuracy on a self-collected dataset and supports simultaneous online visual verification, severity assessment, and incident-induced congestion propagation monitoring via a web-based interface. In a field test on Interstate 75 in Florida, DARTS detected and verified a rear-end collision 12 minutes earlier than the local transportation management center and monitored incident-induced congestion propagation, suggesting potential to support faster emergency response and enable proactive traffic control to reduce congestion and secondary crash risk. Crucially, DARTS's flexible deployment architecture reduces dependence on frequent physical patrols, indicating potential scalability and cost-effectiveness for use in remote areas and resource-constrained settings. This study presents a promising step toward a more flexible and integrated real-time traffic incident detection system, with significant implications for the operational efficiency and responsiveness of modern transportation management.
