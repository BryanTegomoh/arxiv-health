DARTS: A Drone-Based AI-Powered Real-Time Traffic Incident Detection System

arXiv ID: 2510.26004v1
Published: 2025-10-29
Authors: Bai Li, Achilleas Kourtellis, Rong Cao, Joseph Post, Brian Porter, Yu Zhang
Categories: cs.RO, cs.AI, cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.80 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

DARTS is a novel drone-based, AI-powered system designed for real-time traffic incident detection, addressing limitations of conventional methods such as slow response and infrastructure dependence. By integrating adaptive drone surveillance, thermal imaging, and a lightweight deep learning framework, DARTS achieved 99% detection accuracy and demonstrated a 12-minute earlier incident verification in a field test compared to traditional systems. This facilitates significantly faster emergency response, proactive traffic control, and improved management of incident-induced congestion.

Medical Relevance

Faster and more reliable incident detection directly and critically impacts emergency medical services by significantly reducing response times for crash victims. This allows for earlier on-scene medical intervention, quicker transport to trauma centers, and improved chances of survival and reduced morbidity for patients suffering from crash-related injuries.

AI Health Application

The AI-powered drone system (DARTS) serves as a critical enabler for healthcare applications by providing real-time, accurate traffic incident detection. This AI application indirectly yet significantly impacts health by accelerating the dispatch and arrival of emergency medical services to accident sites, thereby reducing mortality and morbidity rates associated with traffic collisions. It acts as an intelligent surveillance tool that enhances the operational efficiency and responsiveness of the entire emergency response chain, including medical personnel.

Key Points

  • Addresses critical issues in conventional incident detection, including slow response, limited flexibility, and high infrastructure dependence, which contribute to fatalities, injuries, and congestion.
  • Utilizes a novel integration of highly mobile drones for adaptive aerial surveillance and thermal imaging for robust low-visibility performance and privacy protection.
  • Employs a lightweight deep learning framework specifically for real-time vehicle trajectory extraction and automated incident detection.
  • Achieved a high detection accuracy of 99% on a self-collected dataset, demonstrating reliability in identifying traffic incidents.
  • In a field test on Interstate 75 in Florida, DARTS detected and verified a rear-end collision 12 minutes earlier than the local transportation management center.
  • Supports simultaneous online visual verification, severity assessment, and incident-induced congestion propagation monitoring via a user-friendly web-based interface.
  • Offers a flexible deployment architecture that reduces dependence on frequent physical patrols, indicating potential for scalability and cost-effectiveness in remote or resource-constrained settings.

Methodology

The DARTS system integrates three key technologies: (1) **Drones** providing high mobility and an aerial perspective for adaptive and dynamic surveillance; (2) **Thermal Imaging** for enhanced incident detection in low-visibility conditions (e.g., night, fog) while offering improved privacy protection by not capturing identifiable facial features; and (3) a **Lightweight Deep Learning Framework** specifically trained for real-time vehicle trajectory extraction and automated incident detection. The system includes a web-based interface for online visual verification, severity assessment, and congestion propagation monitoring.

Key Findings

DARTS achieved 99% detection accuracy on a self-collected dataset. In a real-world field test, it detected and verified a rear-end collision 12 minutes earlier than the local transportation management center. The system successfully monitored incident-induced congestion propagation and enabled simultaneous online visual verification and severity assessment via its web-based interface.

Clinical Impact

The ability of DARTS to provide significantly earlier and verified incident detection has a profound clinical impact by drastically cutting down Emergency Medical Services (EMS) response times to traffic accidents. This allows for the expedited delivery of life-saving medical interventions, optimization of pre-hospital care, and more rapid transportation of critically injured patients to appropriate trauma facilities, thereby enhancing patient survival rates and mitigating the long-term impact of severe injuries. Furthermore, its capacity for proactive traffic control can help prevent secondary crashes, reducing the overall burden of trauma.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations. However, it implies that DARTS is currently a 'promising step' and focuses on its 'potential' for scalability and cost-effectiveness, suggesting that broad, widespread deployment and validation across diverse operational environments or large-scale urban/rural settings are yet to be fully achieved. The system's reliance on a 'self-collected dataset' may also suggest a need for further validation on more diverse and larger datasets to ensure generalizability.

Future Directions

The study positions DARTS as a "promising step toward a more flexible and integrated real-time traffic incident detection system." Implicit future directions include expanding field testing to diverse geographical and environmental conditions, further refining the deep learning models for a wider array of incident types and complex scenarios, and exploring full integration with existing emergency response and transportation management infrastructures to ensure seamless operation and rapid data dissemination. Realizing the full potential for scalability and cost-effectiveness, especially in remote and resource-constrained settings, also represents a key future endeavor.

Abstract

Rapid and reliable incident detection is critical for reducing crash-related fatalities, injuries, and congestion. However, conventional methods, such as closed-circuit television, dashcam footage, and sensor-based detection, separate detection from verification, suffer from limited flexibility, and require dense infrastructure or high penetration rates, restricting adaptability and scalability to shifting incident hotspots. To overcome these challenges, we developed DARTS, a drone-based, AI-powered real-time traffic incident detection system. DARTS integrates drones' high mobility and aerial perspective for adaptive surveillance, thermal imaging for better low-visibility performance and privacy protection, and a lightweight deep learning framework for real-time vehicle trajectory extraction and incident detection. The system achieved 99% detection accuracy on a self-collected dataset and supports simultaneous online visual verification, severity assessment, and incident-induced congestion propagation monitoring via a web-based interface. In a field test on Interstate 75 in Florida, DARTS detected and verified a rear-end collision 12 minutes earlier than the local transportation management center and monitored incident-induced congestion propagation, suggesting potential to support faster emergency response and enable proactive traffic control to reduce congestion and secondary crash risk. Crucially, DARTS's flexible deployment architecture reduces dependence on frequent physical patrols, indicating potential scalability and cost-effectiveness for use in remote areas and resource-constrained settings. This study presents a promising step toward a more flexible and integrated real-time traffic incident detection system, with significant implications for the operational efficiency and responsiveness of modern transportation management.
