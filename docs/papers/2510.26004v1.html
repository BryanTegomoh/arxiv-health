DARTS: A Drone-Based AI-Powered Real-Time Traffic Incident Detection System

arXiv ID: 2510.26004v1
Published: 2025-10-29
Authors: Bai Li, Achilleas Kourtellis, Rong Cao, Joseph Post, Brian Porter, Yu Zhang
Categories: cs.RO, cs.AI, cs.CV

Summary

This paper introduces DARTS, a drone-based, AI-powered real-time traffic incident detection system designed to overcome the limitations of conventional methods. DARTS integrates drones for adaptive aerial surveillance, thermal imaging for robust low-visibility performance and privacy, and a lightweight deep learning framework for accurate incident detection and vehicle trajectory extraction. It achieved 99% detection accuracy and demonstrated the ability to detect and verify incidents significantly faster than existing systems, supporting quicker emergency response and proactive traffic control.

Medical Relevance

Faster and more reliable traffic incident detection directly translates to quicker dispatch of emergency medical services (EMS), potentially reducing the time to first medical contact for trauma patients, minimizing injury severity, and improving survival rates from crash-related fatalities and injuries.

AI Health Application

The AI (deep learning framework) is applied to real-time vehicle trajectory extraction and incident detection. This AI-powered detection directly contributes to health by enabling faster notification of emergency services, which can lead to more rapid medical intervention for accident victims, thereby reducing fatalities and the severity of injuries. It's an indirect but significant application of AI to improve health outcomes and emergency response efficiency.

  • The system achieves 99% detection accuracy on a self-collected dataset.

Methodology

DARTS integrates a drone platform for adaptive aerial surveillance with thermal imaging capabilities. It employs a lightweight deep learning framework for real-time vehicle trajectory extraction and incident detection. The system's performance was evaluated using a self-collected dataset for accuracy and validated through a field test on a major interstate, comparing its detection time against a local transportation management center.

Key Findings

DARTS achieved 99% detection accuracy for traffic incidents and, in a real-world field test, detected and verified a rear-end collision 12 minutes earlier than conventional methods. The system also demonstrated the capability for online visual verification, severity assessment, and congestion monitoring, indicating significant potential for accelerating emergency response and enhancing traffic management.

Clinical Impact

By enabling 12-minute faster incident detection and verification, DARTS has the potential to dramatically reduce EMS response times to critical traffic incidents. This earlier notification can lead to a 'golden hour' advantage, allowing for more rapid triage, stabilization, and transport of trauma victims, ultimately improving clinical outcomes and reducing mortality and morbidity associated with road traffic injuries.

Abstract

Rapid and reliable incident detection is critical for reducing crash-related fatalities, injuries, and congestion. However, conventional methods, such as closed-circuit television, dashcam footage, and sensor-based detection, separate detection from verification, suffer from limited flexibility, and require dense infrastructure or high penetration rates, restricting adaptability and scalability to shifting incident hotspots. To overcome these challenges, we developed DARTS, a drone-based, AI-powered real-time traffic incident detection system. DARTS integrates drones' high mobility and aerial perspective for adaptive surveillance, thermal imaging for better low-visibility performance and privacy protection, and a lightweight deep learning framework for real-time vehicle trajectory extraction and incident detection. The system achieved 99% detection accuracy on a self-collected dataset and supports simultaneous online visual verification, severity assessment, and incident-induced congestion propagation monitoring via a web-based interface. In a field test on Interstate 75 in Florida, DARTS detected and verified a rear-end collision 12 minutes earlier than the local transportation management center and monitored incident-induced congestion propagation, suggesting potential to support faster emergency response and enable proactive traffic control to reduce congestion and secondary crash risk. Crucially, DARTS's flexible deployment architecture reduces dependence on frequent physical patrols, indicating potential scalability and cost-effectiveness for use in remote areas and resource-constrained settings. This study presents a promising step toward a more flexible and integrated real-time traffic incident detection system, with significant implications for the operational efficiency and responsiveness of modern transportation management.
