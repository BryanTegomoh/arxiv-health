Lost in Time? A Meta-Learning Framework for Time-Shift-Tolerant Physiological Signal Transformation

arXiv ID: 2511.21500v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Qian Hong, Cheng Bian, Xiao Zhou, Xiaoyu Li, Yelei Li, Zijing Zeng
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces ShiftSyncNet, a meta-learning framework designed to overcome performance degradation caused by temporal misalignment in multimodal physiological signal transformation. By integrating a transformation network with a time-shift correction network that utilizes Fourier phase shifts, ShiftSyncNet automatically aligns supervision signals, significantly improving accuracy in translating non-invasive signals like PPG/BCG into clinical measurements such as ABP. It offers a robust solution for enhancing continuous, low-cost healthcare monitoring.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for advancing continuous, low-cost healthcare monitoring by enabling more accurate and reliable translation of readily available non-invasive physiological signals into vital clinical parameters like arterial blood pressure, which is essential for early disease detection, chronic disease management, and remote patient care.

AI Health Application

The AI application is an intelligent system (ShiftSyncNet) that leverages meta-learning and neural networks to process multimodal non-invasive physiological signals (like PPG and BCG). Its primary function is to accurately transform these signals into clinically meaningful outputs (such as arterial blood pressure, ABP), specifically addressing and correcting temporal misalignments, thereby enabling more reliable and continuous low-cost health monitoring.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical problem of temporal misalignment in transforming non-invasive physiological signals (e.g., PPG, BCG) into clinically meaningful ones (e.g., ABP), which severely impairs transformation accuracy and feature capturing.
  • Proposes ShiftSyncNet, a novel meta-learning-based bi-level optimization framework for automatically mitigating performance degradation due to time misalignment.
  • ShiftSyncNet comprises two core components: a Transformation Network (TransNet) for the primary signal conversion, and a Time-Shift Correction Network (SyncNet).
  • SyncNet learns time offsets between training pairs and applies Fourier phase shifts to the supervision signals, effectively aligning them before they are used to train TransNet.
  • The meta-learning approach enables SyncNet to automatically learn and correct shifts, improving label quality without relying on strong similarity assumptions or manual tuning, unlike conventional methods or existing Learning with Noisy Labels (LNL) approaches.
  • Experimental results on one real-world industrial dataset and two public datasets demonstrate that ShiftSyncNet significantly outperforms strong baselines by 9.4%, 6.0%, and 12.8% respectively.
  • The framework's effectiveness in correcting time shifts, enhancing label quality, and improving transformation accuracy highlights its potential as a unified solution for temporal inconsistencies in multimodal physiological transformation.

Methodology

The proposed ShiftSyncNet framework employs a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization strategy. It features a Transformation Network (TransNet) responsible for converting non-invasive input signals (e.g., PPG, BCG) into target clinical signals (e.g., ABP). Complementing this is a Time-Shift Correction Network (SyncNet), which acts as a meta-learner. SyncNet is trained to learn the optimal time offsets between input signals and their corresponding misaligned supervision labels. It then applies Fourier phase shifts to precisely align these supervision signals, effectively correcting temporal misalignments before they are fed to TransNet for training. This iterative bi-level optimization allows SyncNet to dynamically improve label quality, thereby enhancing TransNet's performance.

Key Findings

ShiftSyncNet achieved significant performance improvements over strong baseline methods, demonstrating 9.4%, 6.0%, and 12.8% better accuracy on one industrial and two public datasets, respectively. The framework effectively learned to correct temporal misalignments inherent in the data, leading to a substantial improvement in the quality of supervision signals (labels). This correction ultimately enhanced the accuracy of the physiological signal transformation across diverse misalignment scenarios, proving the method's robustness and efficacy.

Clinical Impact

The enhanced accuracy and robustness of ShiftSyncNet in handling time-shifted physiological signals have profound clinical implications. It promises more reliable and precise continuous monitoring of vital signs like blood pressure using non-invasive, low-cost technologies. This capability can lead to improved early detection and diagnosis of cardiovascular conditions, more effective management of chronic diseases, and better remote patient monitoring, ultimately improving patient outcomes, reducing the need for invasive procedures, and potentially lowering healthcare costs by enabling widespread, unobtrusive health tracking.

Abstract

Translating non-invasive signals such as photoplethysmography (PPG) and ballistocardiography (BCG) into clinically meaningful signals like arterial blood pressure (ABP) is vital for continuous, low-cost healthcare monitoring. However, temporal misalignment in multimodal signal transformation impairs transformation accuracy, especially in capturing critical features like ABP peaks. Conventional synchronization methods often rely on strong similarity assumptions or manual tuning, while existing Learning with Noisy Labels (LNL) approaches are ineffective under time-shifted supervision, either discarding excessive data or failing to correct label shifts. To address this challenge, we propose ShiftSyncNet, a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization framework that automatically mitigates performance degradation due to time misalignment. It comprises a transformation network (TransNet) and a time-shift correction network (SyncNet), where SyncNet learns time offsets between training pairs and applies Fourier phase shifts to align supervision signals. Experiments on one real-world industrial dataset and two public datasets show that ShiftSyncNet outperforms strong baselines by 9.4%, 6.0%, and 12.8%, respectively. The results highlight its effectiveness in correcting time shifts, improving label quality, and enhancing transformation accuracy across diverse misalignment scenarios, pointing toward a unified direction for addressing temporal inconsistencies in multimodal physiological transformation.
