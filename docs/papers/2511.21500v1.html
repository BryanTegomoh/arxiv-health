Lost in Time? A Meta-Learning Framework for Time-Shift-Tolerant Physiological Signal Transformation

arXiv ID: 2511.21500v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Qian Hong, Cheng Bian, Xiao Zhou, Xiaoyu Li, Yelei Li, Zijing Zeng
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

ShiftSyncNet is a novel meta-learning framework designed to overcome temporal misalignment in transforming non-invasive physiological signals like PPG and BCG into clinically meaningful signals such as arterial blood pressure (ABP). This framework automatically learns and corrects time shifts using a bi-level optimization approach with Fourier phase shifts, significantly improving transformation accuracy and label quality compared to existing methods. Its effectiveness was demonstrated with substantial performance gains across multiple datasets.

Medical Relevance

Enabling accurate, continuous, and low-cost blood pressure monitoring from non-invasive signals like PPG and BCG is crucial for preventative healthcare, chronic disease management, and remote patient monitoring, reducing the need for cumbersome or invasive measurements.

AI Health Application

The paper presents an AI (meta-learning) framework, ShiftSyncNet, designed to improve the accuracy of continuous, non-invasive physiological signal transformation. Specifically, it enables more accurate derivation of clinically vital signs like arterial blood pressure (ABP) from non-invasive signals like PPG and BCG, by automatically correcting temporal misalignments. This application facilitates advanced health monitoring systems, potentially in wearables or telemedicine, by providing reliable physiological data through AI-driven signal processing.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** The core challenge is temporal misalignment between multimodal physiological signals (e.g., non-invasive inputs like PPG/BCG and target clinical signals like ABP), which severely degrades transformation accuracy, particularly for critical features like ABP peaks.
  • **Proposed Solution:** ShiftSyncNet, a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization framework, is introduced to automatically mitigate performance degradation caused by time misalignment.
  • **Framework Architecture:** ShiftSyncNet comprises two main networks: a Transformation Network (TransNet) responsible for the actual signal conversion, and a Time-Shift Correction Network (SyncNet) dedicated to identifying and correcting temporal offsets.
  • **Correction Mechanism:** SyncNet learns precise time offsets between training input-label pairs and applies these corrections as Fourier phase shifts directly to the supervision signals (labels) to achieve accurate temporal alignment.
  • **Performance Improvement:** Experiments on one real-world industrial dataset and two public datasets showed ShiftSyncNet outperforming strong baselines by significant margins: 9.4%, 6.0%, and 12.8% respectively.
  • **Key Benefits:** The framework effectively corrects time shifts, enhances the quality of noisy labels (time-shifted supervision), and improves overall transformation accuracy across diverse misalignment scenarios.
  • **Future Direction:** The approach offers a unified direction for addressing temporal inconsistencies, suggesting broader applicability in multimodal physiological signal transformation.

Methodology

The ShiftSyncNet framework utilizes a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization approach. It consists of a TransNet that performs the primary signal transformation and a SyncNet. SyncNet is designed to learn the optimal time offsets between misaligned physiological input signals and their corresponding clinical supervision signals (labels). It then applies these learned offsets as Fourier phase shifts to the supervision signals, effectively correcting the temporal misalignment and improving the quality of the training labels used by TransNet.

Key Findings

ShiftSyncNet consistently demonstrated superior performance, achieving accuracy improvements of 9.4%, 6.0%, and 12.8% over strong baseline methods across one industrial and two public physiological datasets. These results confirm its significant effectiveness in automatically correcting temporal misalignments, enhancing label quality, and substantially improving the overall accuracy of transformations from non-invasive physiological signals to clinically meaningful outputs like ABP.

Clinical Impact

This technology has the potential to significantly enhance the reliability and accuracy of continuous non-invasive blood pressure monitoring, even in challenging real-world conditions where signal synchronization issues are common. This can lead to more precise real-time patient monitoring in hospitals and homes, facilitate earlier detection of cardiovascular diseases, and broaden the accessibility of low-cost, continuous physiological measurements, ultimately improving patient outcomes and potentially reducing healthcare costs associated with invasive monitoring.

Future Directions

The paper suggests that its meta-learning approach for time-shift correction points toward a "unified direction for addressing temporal inconsistencies in multimodal physiological transformation." This implies that the methodology could be generalized and applied to a wider range of multimodal physiological signal analysis problems beyond PPG/BCG to ABP, potentially including other vital signs or sensor fusions.

Abstract

Translating non-invasive signals such as photoplethysmography (PPG) and ballistocardiography (BCG) into clinically meaningful signals like arterial blood pressure (ABP) is vital for continuous, low-cost healthcare monitoring. However, temporal misalignment in multimodal signal transformation impairs transformation accuracy, especially in capturing critical features like ABP peaks. Conventional synchronization methods often rely on strong similarity assumptions or manual tuning, while existing Learning with Noisy Labels (LNL) approaches are ineffective under time-shifted supervision, either discarding excessive data or failing to correct label shifts. To address this challenge, we propose ShiftSyncNet, a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization framework that automatically mitigates performance degradation due to time misalignment. It comprises a transformation network (TransNet) and a time-shift correction network (SyncNet), where SyncNet learns time offsets between training pairs and applies Fourier phase shifts to align supervision signals. Experiments on one real-world industrial dataset and two public datasets show that ShiftSyncNet outperforms strong baselines by 9.4%, 6.0%, and 12.8%, respectively. The results highlight its effectiveness in correcting time shifts, improving label quality, and enhancing transformation accuracy across diverse misalignment scenarios, pointing toward a unified direction for addressing temporal inconsistencies in multimodal physiological transformation.
