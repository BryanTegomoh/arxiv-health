Lost in Time? A Meta-Learning Framework for Time-Shift-Tolerant Physiological Signal Transformation

arXiv ID: 2511.21500v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Qian Hong, Cheng Bian, Xiao Zhou, Xiaoyu Li, Yelei Li, Zijing Zeng
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

ShiftSyncNet is a novel meta-learning framework designed to mitigate performance degradation caused by temporal misalignment in multimodal physiological signal transformation, crucial for continuous healthcare monitoring. It employs a bi-level optimization approach with a transformation network and a time-shift correction network that learns and applies Fourier phase shifts for signal alignment. The framework significantly outperforms strong baselines across multiple datasets, demonstrating enhanced accuracy and improved label quality in converting non-invasive signals to clinically meaningful ones.

Medical Relevance

This research is vital for advancing continuous, low-cost healthcare monitoring by enabling highly accurate translation of non-invasive physiological signals like PPG and BCG into critical clinical parameters such as arterial blood pressure (ABP), which is fundamental for diagnosing and managing cardiovascular conditions.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using a meta-learning framework (ShiftSyncNet) to automatically correct temporal misalignments in multimodal physiological signals (e.g., PPG, BCG) when transforming them into clinically meaningful metrics like arterial blood pressure (ABP). This enhances the accuracy and reliability of AI models used for continuous, non-invasive health monitoring, enabling better diagnostic insights and supporting low-cost healthcare solutions.

Key Points

  • Temporal misalignment is a critical problem in transforming non-invasive physiological signals (e.g., PPG, BCG) into clinical signals (e.g., ABP), impairing accuracy, especially for crucial features like ABP peaks.
  • Existing synchronization methods suffer from strong similarity assumptions or manual tuning, while current Learning with Noisy Labels (LNL) approaches are ineffective for time-shifted supervision.
  • ShiftSyncNet is proposed as a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization framework designed to automatically correct performance degradation due to time misalignment.
  • The framework consists of two core components: a Transformation Network (TransNet) responsible for the signal translation task, and a Time-Shift Correction Network (SyncNet).
  • SyncNet learns specific time offsets between training pairs and applies Fourier phase shifts to align the supervision signals, effectively correcting temporal discrepancies.
  • Experiments on one real-world industrial dataset and two public datasets show that ShiftSyncNet significantly outperforms strong baselines by 9.4%, 6.0%, and 12.8%, respectively.
  • The results highlight the framework's effectiveness in correcting time shifts, improving the quality of supervisory labels, and enhancing overall transformation accuracy across diverse misalignment scenarios.

Methodology

ShiftSyncNet utilizes a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization framework. It integrates a Transformation Network (TransNet) for the primary signal conversion and a Time-Shift Correction Network (SyncNet). SyncNet is trained to learn and predict precise time offsets between misaligned input and supervision signal pairs. These learned offsets are then used to apply Fourier phase shifts to the supervision signals, effectively realigning them to the input before being used for TransNet training, thereby correcting temporal discrepancies without manual intervention.

Key Findings

ShiftSyncNet effectively mitigates temporal misalignment in multimodal physiological signal transformation, leading to significant performance improvements. It outperformed strong baselines by 9.4%, 6.0%, and 12.8% on various datasets. The framework proves adept at correcting time shifts, enhancing the quality of training labels, and improving the overall accuracy of translating non-invasive signals into clinically meaningful data like ABP.

Clinical Impact

This framework holds substantial clinical impact by improving the reliability and accuracy of non-invasive continuous physiological monitoring. It can enable more dependable early detection of health issues, facilitate better personalized healthcare management, and reduce the need for invasive or expensive monitoring, making advanced health insights more accessible and cost-effective in both clinical and home-based settings.

Abstract

Translating non-invasive signals such as photoplethysmography (PPG) and ballistocardiography (BCG) into clinically meaningful signals like arterial blood pressure (ABP) is vital for continuous, low-cost healthcare monitoring. However, temporal misalignment in multimodal signal transformation impairs transformation accuracy, especially in capturing critical features like ABP peaks. Conventional synchronization methods often rely on strong similarity assumptions or manual tuning, while existing Learning with Noisy Labels (LNL) approaches are ineffective under time-shifted supervision, either discarding excessive data or failing to correct label shifts. To address this challenge, we propose ShiftSyncNet, a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization framework that automatically mitigates performance degradation due to time misalignment. It comprises a transformation network (TransNet) and a time-shift correction network (SyncNet), where SyncNet learns time offsets between training pairs and applies Fourier phase shifts to align supervision signals. Experiments on one real-world industrial dataset and two public datasets show that ShiftSyncNet outperforms strong baselines by 9.4%, 6.0%, and 12.8%, respectively. The results highlight its effectiveness in correcting time shifts, improving label quality, and enhancing transformation accuracy across diverse misalignment scenarios, pointing toward a unified direction for addressing temporal inconsistencies in multimodal physiological transformation.
