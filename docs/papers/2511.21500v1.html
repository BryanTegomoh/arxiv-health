Lost in Time? A Meta-Learning Framework for Time-Shift-Tolerant Physiological Signal Transformation

arXiv ID: 2511.21500v1
Published: 2025-11-26
Authors: Qian Hong, Cheng Bian, Xiao Zhou, Xiaoyu Li, Yelei Li, Zijing Zeng
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces ShiftSyncNet, a novel meta-learning framework that addresses the critical problem of temporal misalignment in multimodal physiological signal transformation (e.g., PPG to ABP). By employing a bi-level optimization approach with a transformation network and a time-shift correction network, ShiftSyncNet automatically learns and applies Fourier phase shifts to align supervision signals, significantly improving the accuracy of deriving clinically meaningful information from non-invasive data. Experiments show it outperforms strong baselines by substantial margins across various datasets.

Medical Relevance

Accurate transformation of non-invasive physiological signals into clinically meaningful ones (like ABP from PPG) is vital for continuous, low-cost healthcare monitoring. This method directly enhances the reliability and precision of such monitoring, enabling better diagnosis and management of conditions without the need for invasive or manual synchronization efforts.

AI Health Application

The ShiftSyncNet framework is a medical AI application that uses meta-learning to enhance the accuracy of transforming non-invasive physiological signals (like PPG and BCG) into clinically meaningful data such as arterial blood pressure (ABP). By automatically correcting temporal misalignments, it improves the reliability of continuous, low-cost healthcare monitoring, enabling better patient diagnostics and management outside of traditional clinical settings.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** Temporal misalignment in multimodal physiological signal transformation (e.g., PPG/BCG to ABP) impairs transformation accuracy, especially for capturing critical features, and is not effectively handled by conventional or existing Learning with Noisy Labels (LNL) methods under time-shifted supervision.
  • **Proposed Solution:** ShiftSyncNet, a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization framework, is introduced to automatically mitigate performance degradation due to time misalignment.
  • **Framework Components:** ShiftSyncNet comprises a Transformation Network (TransNet) for the primary signal transformation and a dedicated Time-Shift Correction Network (SyncNet).
  • **Shift Correction Mechanism:** SyncNet learns time offsets between training pairs and applies Fourier phase shifts to align the supervision signals, thereby correcting the temporal misalignment in a data-driven manner.
  • **Performance Improvement:** ShiftSyncNet demonstrated significant performance enhancements, outperforming strong baselines by 9.4% on one real-world industrial dataset, 6.0% on one public dataset, and 12.8% on another public dataset.
  • **Impact on Label Quality:** The framework effectively corrects time shifts, leading to improved label quality for the supervision signals, which directly enhances the accuracy of the physiological signal transformation.
  • **Generality:** The effectiveness of ShiftSyncNet across diverse misalignment scenarios suggests a unified and robust approach for addressing temporal inconsistencies in multimodal physiological signal transformation.

Methodology

The proposed ShiftSyncNet is a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization framework. It integrates a Transformation Network (TransNet) responsible for the primary signal conversion (e.g., PPG to ABP), and a Time-Shift Correction Network (SyncNet). SyncNet is designed to learn optimal time offsets between input physiological signals and their corresponding supervision labels. It then applies these learned offsets via Fourier phase shifts to align the supervision signals, effectively correcting temporal misalignment within the training data. This process is optimized using a meta-learning approach, allowing the framework to automatically adapt and improve alignment without manual tuning.

Key Findings

ShiftSyncNet achieved significant performance improvements over strong baseline methods across diverse datasets. Specifically, it outperformed baselines by 9.4% on a real-world industrial dataset, 6.0% on one public dataset, and 12.8% on another public dataset. These findings highlight its robust effectiveness in correcting time shifts, improving label quality, and enhancing overall transformation accuracy.

Clinical Impact

This research holds substantial clinical impact by enabling more accurate and reliable continuous physiological monitoring using non-invasive, low-cost methods, such as deriving ABP from PPG. By mitigating temporal misalignment, it improves the fidelity of derived clinical parameters, potentially leading to earlier and more precise detection of health issues, refined patient management strategies, and expanded access to advanced monitoring technologies in various healthcare settings, from hospitals to remote home care.

Abstract

Translating non-invasive signals such as photoplethysmography (PPG) and ballistocardiography (BCG) into clinically meaningful signals like arterial blood pressure (ABP) is vital for continuous, low-cost healthcare monitoring. However, temporal misalignment in multimodal signal transformation impairs transformation accuracy, especially in capturing critical features like ABP peaks. Conventional synchronization methods often rely on strong similarity assumptions or manual tuning, while existing Learning with Noisy Labels (LNL) approaches are ineffective under time-shifted supervision, either discarding excessive data or failing to correct label shifts. To address this challenge, we propose ShiftSyncNet, a meta-learning-based bi-level optimization framework that automatically mitigates performance degradation due to time misalignment. It comprises a transformation network (TransNet) and a time-shift correction network (SyncNet), where SyncNet learns time offsets between training pairs and applies Fourier phase shifts to align supervision signals. Experiments on one real-world industrial dataset and two public datasets show that ShiftSyncNet outperforms strong baselines by 9.4%, 6.0%, and 12.8%, respectively. The results highlight its effectiveness in correcting time shifts, improving label quality, and enhancing transformation accuracy across diverse misalignment scenarios, pointing toward a unified direction for addressing temporal inconsistencies in multimodal physiological transformation.
