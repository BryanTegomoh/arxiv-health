Radiology Workflow-Guided Hierarchical Reinforcement Fine-Tuning for Medical Report Generation

arXiv ID: 2511.10065v1
Published: 2025-11-13
Authors: Bodong Du, Honglong Yang, Xiaomeng Li
Categories: cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces RadFlow, a novel hierarchical reinforcement fine-tuning framework that mimics radiologists' structured workflow for medical report generation. By incorporating a clinically grounded reward hierarchy and a critical-aware policy optimization, RadFlow addresses inconsistencies in existing flat-sequence models, generating reports that are both linguistically consistent and clinically aligned. Experiments on chest X-ray and carotid ultrasound datasets demonstrate RadFlow's superior performance in improving diagnostic coherence and overall report quality.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly enhances the reliability and clinical utility of automated medical report generation by ensuring diagnostic reports are consistent with professional radiological workflow and standards, thereby improving patient care, diagnostic accuracy, and clinical efficiency.

AI Health Application

The AI application is 'Medical Report Generation' for diagnostic imaging (e.g., chest X-ray, carotid ultrasound). It aims to assist radiologists by generating structured, clinically accurate, and consistent reports, thereby potentially improving efficiency and supporting diagnostic processes in healthcare settings. The 'critical-aware policy optimization' also suggests an application in ensuring patient safety in high-risk cases.

Methodology

RadFlow employs a hierarchical reinforcement fine-tuning framework. It integrates a clinically grounded reward hierarchy comprising global rewards (for linguistic fluency, medical correctness, and cross-sectional consistency between Finding and Impression sections) and local, section-specific rewards (emphasizing Impression quality). Furthermore, a critical-aware policy optimization mechanism adaptively regularizes learning based on the clinical criticality of cases, mimicking radiologists' cautious refinement process during high-risk reporting.

Key Findings

Experiments on chest X-ray and carotid ultrasound datasets demonstrated that RadFlow consistently achieved improvements in diagnostic coherence and overall report quality. The hierarchical reinforcement learning and critical-aware policy components collectively enabled the model to generate reports that are both linguistically consistent and clinically aligned, outperforming state-of-the-art baselines.

Clinical Impact

This framework could lead to more accurate and reliable AI-generated medical reports, reducing the workload on radiologists while improving consistency and reducing errors, especially in critical cases. Enhanced report quality and diagnostic coherence could accelerate diagnosis, facilitate better treatment planning, and ultimately improve patient outcomes in diagnostic imaging.

Abstract

Radiologists compose diagnostic reports through a structured workflow: they describe visual findings, summarize them into impressions, and carefully refine statements in clinically critical cases. However, most existing medical report generation (MRG) systems treat reports as flat sequences, overlooking this hierarchical organization and leading to inconsistencies between descriptive and diagnostic content. To align model behavior with real-world reporting practices, we propose RadFlow, a hierarchical workflow-guided reinforcement optimization framework that explicitly models the structured nature of clinical reporting. RadFlow introduces a clinically grounded reward hierarchy that mirrors the organization of radiological reports. At the global level, the reward integrates linguistic fluency, medical-domain correctness, and cross-sectional consistency between Finding and Impression, promoting coherent and clinically faithful narratives. At the local level, a section-specific reward emphasizes Impression quality, reflecting its central role in diagnostic accuracy. Furthermore, a critical-aware policy optimization mechanism adaptively regularizes learning for high-risk or clinically sensitive cases, emulating the cautious refinement behavior of radiologists when documenting critical findings. Together, these components translate the structured reporting paradigm into the reinforcement fine-tuning process, enabling the model to generate reports that are both linguistically consistent and clinically aligned. Experiments on chest X-ray and carotid ultrasound datasets demonstrate that RadFlow consistently improves diagnostic coherence and overall report quality compared with state-of-the-art baselines.
