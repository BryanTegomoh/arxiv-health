Dual-LoRA and Quality-Enhanced Pseudo Replay for Multimodal Continual Food Learning

Authors: Xinlan Wu, Bin Zhu, Feng Han, Pengkun Jiao, Jingjing Chen

Summary

This paper introduces a novel continual learning framework, integrating a Dual-LoRA architecture with Quality-Enhanced Pseudo Replay, to address catastrophic forgetting in large multimodal models (LMMs) used for food analysis. The approach enables LMMs to learn new food-related tasks without forgetting previously acquired knowledge, crucial for health applications like personalized nutrition and chronic disease prevention. Experiments on the Uni-Food dataset demonstrate superior performance in mitigating forgetting, establishing the first effective continual learning method for complex food tasks.

  • Proposes a Dual-LoRA architecture featuring two complementary low-rank adapters: a 'specialized LoRA' for task-specific knowledge (with orthogonal constraints) and a 'cooperative LoRA' for consolidating shared knowledge.
  • Introduces orthogonal constraints for the specialized LoRA to prevent interference with knowledge from previous tasks' subspaces, enhancing task isolation.
  • Implements a Quality-Enhanced Pseudo Replay strategy that improves the reliability of generated replay data by leveraging self-consistency and semantic similarity to reduce model hallucinations.

  • The overall goal is to enable LMMs to continuously adapt and learn new food data for dynamic health applications without costly retraining from scratch.

Methodology

The methodology combines a Dual-LoRA (Low-Rank Adapter) architecture with a Quality-Enhanced Pseudo Replay strategy. The Dual-LoRA uses specialized adapters with orthogonal constraints for new task-specific knowledge and cooperative adapters to consolidate shared knowledge via pseudo replay. The pseudo replay mechanism actively reduces hallucinations in generated samples by enforcing self-consistency and semantic similarity checks, ensuring high-quality replay data for knowledge retention.

Key Findings

The proposed Dual-LoRA and Quality-Enhanced Pseudo Replay framework successfully mitigates catastrophic forgetting in multimodal food learning, achieving superior performance on the Uni-Food dataset. This establishes it as the first effective continual learning approach for complex food analysis tasks, enabling LMMs to continuously learn without significant knowledge degradation.

Abstract

Food analysis has become increasingly critical for health-related tasks such as personalized nutrition and chronic disease prevention. However, existing large multimodal models (LMMs) in food analysis suffer from catastrophic forgetting when learning new tasks, requiring costly retraining from scratch. To address this, we propose a novel continual learning framework for multimodal food learning, integrating a Dual-LoRA architecture with Quality-Enhanced Pseudo Replay. We introduce two complementary low-rank adapters for each task: a specialized LoRA that learns task-specific knowledge with orthogonal constraints to previous tasks' subspaces, and a cooperative LoRA that consolidates shared knowledge across tasks via pseudo replay. To improve the reliability of replay data, our Quality-Enhanced Pseudo Replay strategy leverages self-consistency and semantic similarity to reduce hallucinations in generated samples. Experiments on the comprehensive Uni-Food dataset show superior performance in mitigating forgetting, representing the first effective continual learning approach for complex food tasks.
