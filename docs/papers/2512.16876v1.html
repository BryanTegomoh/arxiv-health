Summary

This paper presents a groundbreaking application of Federated Learning (FL) to address the diagnostic challenges of rare diseases, specifically collagen VI-related dystrophies (COL6-RD), where data scarcity and privacy hinder traditional ML approaches. By enabling collaborative model training across decentralized datasets from two international organizations using the Sherpa.ai platform, the researchers developed an ML model that accurately classifies COL6-RD pathogenic mechanisms from immunofluorescence images. This FL-driven model achieved a superior F1-score of 0.82, significantly outperforming isolated institutional models and demonstrating enhanced diagnostic utility and generalizability.

This research is critically important for rare disease diagnosis, offering a privacy-preserving and collaborative method to overcome the fundamental data limitations. It enables the development of more accurate and generalized diagnostic tools for conditions like COL6-RD, directly benefiting patients through improved, faster, and more reliable diagnoses.

Methodology

The study employed a global Federated Learning (FL) framework using the Sherpa.ai FL platform. This involved training a Machine Learning model collaboratively across two distinct international organizations, where patient data remained local and private. The model's input comprised collagen VI immunofluorescence microscopy images obtained from patient-derived fibroblast cultures. The objective of the ML model was to classify these images into three specific pathogenic mechanisms associated with COL6-RD: exon skipping, glycine substitution, and pseudoexon insertion.

Key Findings

The primary finding is that the Federated Learning model achieved a robust F1-score of 0.82 in classifying the pathogenic mechanisms of COL6-RD from immunofluorescence images. This performance markedly surpassed that of models trained in isolation within single organizations, which yielded F1-scores between 0.57 and 0.75. The FL approach successfully demonstrated a substantial improvement in diagnostic utility and generalizability.

Abstract

The application of Machine Learning (ML) to the diagnosis of rare diseases, such as collagen VI-related dystrophies (COL6-RD), is fundamentally limited by the scarcity and fragmentation of available data. Attempts to expand sampling across hospitals, institutions, or countries with differing regulations face severe privacy, regulatory, and logistical obstacles that are often difficult to overcome. The Federated Learning (FL) provides a promising solution by enabling collaborative model training across decentralized datasets while keeping patient data local and private. Here, we report a novel global FL initiative using the Sherpa.ai FL platform, which leverages FL across distributed datasets in two international organizations for the diagnosis of COL6-RD, using collagen VI immunofluorescence microscopy images from patient-derived fibroblast cultures. Our solution resulted in an ML model capable of classifying collagen VI patient images into the three primary pathogenic mechanism groups associated with COL6-RD: exon skipping, glycine substitution, and pseudoexon insertion. This new approach achieved an F1-score of 0.82, outperforming single-organization models (0.57-0.75). These results demonstrate that FL substantially improves diagnostic utility and generalizability compared to isolated institutional models. Beyond enabling more accurate diagnosis, we anticipate that this approach will support the interpretation of variants of uncertain significance and guide the prioritization of sequencing strategies to identify novel pathogenic variants.
