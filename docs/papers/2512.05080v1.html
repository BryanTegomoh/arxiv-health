OMTRA: A Multi-Task Generative Model for Structure-Based Drug Design

arXiv ID: 2512.05080v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Ian Dunn, Liv Toft, Tyler Katz, Juhi Gupta, Riya Shah, Ramith Hettiarachchi, David R. Koes
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

OMTRA is a novel multi-task generative model leveraging multi-modal flow matching for structure-based drug design (SBDD). It unifies various SBDD tasks, including de novo ligand design and docking, achieving state-of-the-art performance. This is accomplished through a consistent generative framework trained on a newly curated, large-scale dataset of 500M 3D molecular conformers.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly advances computational drug discovery by enabling more efficient and precise design of novel small-molecule ligands that bind to specific protein targets. This accelerates the early stages of drug development, potentially leading to faster discovery of new therapeutics for various diseases.

AI Health Application

OMTRA is a generative AI model (specifically, a multi-modal flow matching model) designed to improve computational methods in Structure-Based Drug Design. It performs tasks like pocket-conditioned de novo ligand design and molecular docking, which are critical steps in identifying and optimizing potential drug candidates for medical treatments.

Key Points

  • OMTRA is a unified multi-modal flow matching generative model designed to flexibly perform various Structure-Based Drug Design (SBDD) tasks.
  • It addresses the common underlying structure of tasks like de novo ligand design and virtual screening by representing them within a consistent generative modeling framework.
  • A substantial new dataset comprising 500 million 3D molecular conformers was curated to significantly expand the chemical diversity available for model training.
  • OMTRA achieves state-of-the-art performance in critical SBDD areas, specifically pocket-conditioned de novo ligand design and docking.
  • The study found that the performance benefits derived from large-scale pretraining and the multi-task training paradigm were surprisingly modest.
  • All code, trained models, and the curated dataset are publicly available, fostering reproducibility and further research.

Methodology

OMTRA employs a multi-modal flow matching generative modeling approach, designed as a unified framework to encompass various SBDD tasks by recognizing their shared underlying structure. The model was trained on both existing protein-ligand data and a newly curated, large-scale dataset of 500 million 3D molecular conformers to enhance chemical diversity.

Key Findings

OMTRA demonstrated state-of-the-art performance on key SBDD tasks, including pocket-conditioned de novo design and docking. However, a notable finding was that the positive effects of large-scale pretraining and the multi-task training approach itself on performance were observed to be modest.

Clinical Impact

By streamlining and improving the accuracy of identifying and designing novel small-molecule drugs, OMTRA can accelerate the preclinical phase of drug development. This can lead to a more efficient pipeline for new therapeutic agents, potentially reducing research and development costs and bringing more effective, targeted treatments to patients more quickly.

Limitations

The primary limitation noted is that the impact of large-scale pretraining and the multi-task training strategy on overall model performance was found to be modest, suggesting that these aspects might not have contributed as significantly as expected to the state-of-the-art results.

Future Directions

While not explicitly stated as 'future directions,' the observation of modest effects from large-scale pretraining and multi-task training implies future research could focus on optimizing these aspects. This might involve exploring alternative multi-task learning architectures, refining pretraining objectives, or investigating how to better leverage large diverse datasets for more substantial performance gains in SBDD.

Abstract

Structure-based drug design (SBDD) focuses on designing small-molecule ligands that bind to specific protein pockets. Computational methods are integral in modern SBDD workflows and often make use of virtual screening methods via docking or pharmacophore search. Modern generative modeling approaches have focused on improving novel ligand discovery by enabling de novo design. In this work, we recognize that these tasks share a common structure and can therefore be represented as different instantiations of a consistent generative modeling framework. We propose a unified approach in OMTRA, a multi-modal flow matching model that flexibly performs many tasks relevant to SBDD, including some with no analogue in conventional workflows. Additionally, we curate a dataset of 500M 3D molecular conformers, complementing protein-ligand data and expanding the chemical diversity available for training. OMTRA obtains state of the art performance on pocket-conditioned de novo design and docking; however, the effects of large-scale pretraining and multi-task training are modest. All code, trained models, and dataset for reproducing this work are available at https://github.com/gnina/OMTRA
