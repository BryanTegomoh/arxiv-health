Tell Me: An LLM-powered Mental Well-being Assistant with RAG, Synthetic Dialogue Generation, and Agentic Planning

Summary

Tell Me is an LLM-powered mental well-being system designed to provide accessible, context-aware support through three integrated components: a RAG-based conversational assistant, a synthetic client-therapist dialogue generator, and an agentic AI crew for personalized self-care. It aims to lower barriers to support, complement existing care, and broaden access to mental health resources, explicitly not as a substitute for professional therapy.

Medical Relevance

This system offers an innovative pathway to enhance mental well-being support accessibility, provide a crucial tool for mental health research by generating synthetic therapeutic data, and act as a valuable complement to traditional care models, particularly in underserved populations.

AI Health Application

An LLM-powered virtual assistant for mental well-being, offering personalized, knowledge-grounded dialogue (RAG), generating synthetic client-therapist dialogues for research into therapeutic language, and creating dynamic self-care plans with guided meditation audio.

Key Points

  • **Multi-component Architecture**: The system integrates a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) assistant for knowledge-grounded dialogue, a synthetic client-therapist dialogue generator, and an agentic AI crew for personalized self-care.
  • **Personalized RAG Assistant**: Provides context-aware, knowledge-grounded conversational support for emotional processing, enhancing accessibility to mental well-being resources.
  • **Novel Synthetic Dialogue Generation**: Addresses the critical shortage of confidential therapeutic data by generating client-therapist dialogues conditioned on client profiles, facilitating research on therapeutic language and data augmentation.
  • **Agentic Planning with CrewAI**: Implements a 'Well-being AI crew' using CrewAI to dynamically generate personalized weekly self-care plans and guided meditation audio, moving beyond static well-being tools.
  • **Evaluation Methodology**: The RAG assistant's performance was evaluated using a dual approach: automatic LLM-based judgments and a human-user study within curated well-being scenarios.
  • **Complementary Role**: Explicitly positioned as a reflective space for emotional processing to complement existing professional care and broaden access, rather than to replace human therapists.
  • **Call for Interdisciplinary Collaboration**: Highlights the need for NLP researchers and mental health professionals to collaborate to ensure responsible innovation in AI for well-being.

Methodology

The methodology centers on leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) within three distinct modules: a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) framework for conversational assistance, a synthetic dialogue generator conditioned on client profiles for data augmentation, and an 'Agentic AI crew' built with CrewAI for dynamic self-care planning. The RAG assistant's efficacy was assessed through both automatic LLM-based judgments and a human-user study in specific well-being scenarios.

Key Findings

The evaluation of the RAG assistant in curated well-being scenarios, utilizing both LLM-based judgments and a human-user study, demonstrates its capacity to provide personalized, knowledge-grounded dialogue. The system successfully illustrates how an integrated LLM approach can lower barriers to mental health support, complement existing care, and facilitate research through synthetic data generation and adaptive self-care planning.

Clinical Impact

Tell Me has significant potential to enhance the accessibility of mental well-being support, acting as a valuable adjunct to professional therapy and broadening the reach of mental health resources to individuals who might otherwise face barriers. The synthetic dialogue generation offers a novel, ethical method for generating research data to advance our understanding of therapeutic language and improve AI-driven interventions without compromising patient privacy.

Limitations

The system is explicitly designed as a reflective space for emotional processing and is not intended to be a substitute for professional therapy, underscoring its supportive rather than curative role.

Future Directions

Future work is encouraged to involve interdisciplinary collaboration between NLP researchers and mental health professionals to further advance responsible innovation in human-AI interaction for well-being, suggesting a path towards rigorous validation and expansion of the system's capabilities in real-world contexts.

Abstract

We present Tell Me, a mental well-being system that leverages advances in large language models to provide accessible, context-aware support for users and researchers. The system integrates three components: (i) a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) assistant for personalized, knowledge-grounded dialogue; (ii) a synthetic client-therapist dialogue generator conditioned on client profiles to facilitate research on therapeutic language and data augmentation; and (iii) a Well-being AI crew, implemented with CrewAI, that produces weekly self-care plans and guided meditation audio. The system is designed as a reflective space for emotional processing rather than a substitute for professional therapy. It illustrates how conversational assistants can lower barriers to support, complement existing care, and broaden access to mental health resources. To address the shortage of confidential therapeutic data, we introduce synthetic client-therapist dialogue generation conditioned on client profiles. Finally, the planner demonstrates an innovative agentic workflow for dynamically adaptive, personalized self-care, bridging the limitations of static well-being tools. We describe the architecture, demonstrate its functionalities, and report evaluation of the RAG assistant in curated well-being scenarios using both automatic LLM-based judgments and a human-user study. This work highlights opportunities for interdisciplinary collaboration between NLP researchers and mental health professionals to advance responsible innovation in human-AI interaction for well-being.
