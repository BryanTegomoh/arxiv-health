LA-MARRVEL: A Knowledge-Grounded and Language-Aware LLM Reranker for AI-MARRVEL in Rare Disease Diagnosis

arXiv ID: 2511.02263v1
Published: 2025-11-04
Authors: Jaeyeon Lee, Hyun-Hwan Jeong, Zhandong Liu
Categories: q-bio.GN, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

LA-MARRVEL introduces a knowledge-grounded and language-aware Large Language Model (LLM) reranking layer for AI-MARRVEL, designed to improve rare disease diagnosis by enhancing gene prioritization. It leverages expert-engineered context and multiple LLM queries to produce stable, explainable gene rankings that consistently outperform existing tools. This system significantly improves recall, particularly for challenging cases, and provides interpretable reasoning to aid clinical review.

Medical Relevance

This research directly tackles a major challenge in rare disease diagnostics by automating and significantly improving the accuracy and interpretability of gene prioritization. By reducing the manual burden and enhancing diagnostic certainty, LA-MARRVEL has the potential to shorten the diagnostic odyssey for rare disease patients and enable earlier, more effective interventions.

AI Health Application

LA-MARRVEL is an AI-powered diagnostic support tool for rare genetic diseases. It leverages Large Language Models (LLMs) to create a 'knowledge-grounded and language-aware reranking layer' that enhances the accuracy and interpretability of gene rankings. This application aims to assist clinicians in identifying causal genes by integrating various forms of clinical and genetic evidence, ultimately improving the efficiency and reliability of rare disease diagnosis.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical bottleneck in rare disease diagnosis where variant-bearing genes must be manually linked to unstructured clinical evidence.
  • Introduces LA-MARRVEL as an LLM-powered reranking layer that operates on top of the established AI-MARRVEL pipeline.
  • Methodology involves supplying expert-engineered context to the LLM, querying it multiple times, and aggregating the resulting partial rankings using a ranked voting method to ensure stability.
  • Evaluated on three real-world cohorts (BG, DDD, UDN), LA-MARRVEL consistently demonstrated improved Recall@K compared to both the base AI-MARRVEL system and other phenotype-driven tools like Exomiser and LIRICAL.
  • Showed particularly significant gains in cases where the initial AI-MARRVEL ranker had placed the causal gene lower, indicating its effectiveness in refining challenging diagnoses.
  • Generates LLM-based reasoning for each ranked gene, integrating multi-modal evidence including phenotypic, inheritance patterns, and variant-level information.
  • The enhanced interpretability and comprehensive reasoning facilitate and expedite clinical review, making the diagnostic process more efficient and reliable.

Methodology

LA-MARRVEL functions as a post-processing reranking layer for AI-MARRVEL. It incorporates expert-engineered contextual information and iteratively queries a large language model (LLM). The partial rankings generated by the LLM from these multiple queries are then consolidated using a ranked voting method to produce a final, stable, and explainable gene ranking.

Key Findings

LA-MARRVEL consistently improved Recall@K across three diverse real-world cohorts (BG, DDD, UDN) when compared to AI-MARRVEL and established phenotype-driven tools such as Exomiser and LIRICAL. The system showed particularly strong performance gains in cases where the initial AI-MARRVEL ranking of the causal gene was low. Additionally, LA-MARRVEL provides LLM-generated reasoning that integrates phenotypic, inheritance, and variant-level evidence for each ranked gene, enhancing interpretability.

Clinical Impact

The increased accuracy and improved Recall@K, especially for difficult diagnostic cases, will lead to faster and more confident identification of causal genes in rare diseases. The provision of LLM-generated, integrated reasoning will empower clinicians with transparent and interpretable diagnostic support, streamlining the clinical review process and potentially reducing misdiagnosis rates and diagnostic delays for rare disease patients.

Abstract

Diagnosing rare diseases often requires connecting variant-bearing genes to evidence that is written as unstructured clinical prose, which the current established pipelines still leave for clinicians to reconcile manually. To this end, we introduce LA-MARRVEL, a knowledge-grounded and language-aware reranking layer that operates on top of AI-MARRVEL: it supplies expert-engineered context, queries a large language model multiple times, and aggregates the resulting partial rankings with a ranked voting method to produce a stable, explainable gene ranking. Evaluated on three real-world cohorts (BG, DDD, UDN), LA-MARRVEL consistently improves Recall@K over AI-MARRVEL and established phenotype-driven tools such as Exomiser and LIRICAL, with especially large gains on cases where the first-stage ranker placed the causal gene lower. Each ranked gene is accompanied by LLM-generated reasoning that integrates phenotypic, inheritance, and variant-level evidence, thereby making the output more interpretable and facilitating clinical review.
