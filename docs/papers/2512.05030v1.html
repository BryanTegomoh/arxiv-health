Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network for Ground Reaction Force and Moment Regression

arXiv ID: 2512.05030v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Xuan Li, Samuel Bello
Categories: cs.LG, eess.SP
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a novel Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network for accurate, insole-based estimation of three-dimensional ground reaction forces and moments (GRFs/GRMs). The network integrates anatomy-inspired spatial and temporal priors with a region-level attention mechanism and a complementary full sensor field context path. The model demonstrates superior performance over strong deep learning baselines on two datasets, achieving robust GRF/GRM estimation crucial for biomechanics and clinical rehabilitation.

Medical Relevance

Accurate estimation of GRFs/GRMs is paramount in clinical rehabilitation for objectively evaluating patient progress, assessing intervention efficacy, and understanding movement pathologies. This insole-based approach offers a portable and non-invasive method for obtaining these critical biomechanical insights outside of traditional laboratory settings.

AI Health Application

An AI-powered system (Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network) that non-invasively estimates 3D ground reaction forces and moments from insole sensors. This application can provide objective metrics for evaluating patient progress in clinical rehabilitation, analyzing gait abnormalities, assessing fall risk in elderly or neurologically impaired individuals, and informing interventions in orthopedics and sports medicine.

Key Points

  • A novel Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network (DP-RGAN) is proposed for precise regression of GRFs/GRMs using insole sensor data.
  • The network incorporates anatomy-inspired spatial and temporal priors through a region-level attention mechanism, focusing on specific foot regions.
  • A complementary path within the DP-RGAN captures broader contextual information from the entire sensor field to enhance prediction accuracy.
  • The model was specifically developed for insole-based GRF/GRM estimation and validated on both a proprietary insole dataset and a public walking dataset.
  • It significantly outperformed strong baseline deep learning architectures, including CNN and CNN-LSTM models, in comparative evaluations.
  • Achieved a low six-component average NRMSE of 5.78% on the insole dataset and an even lower NRMSE of 1.42% for the vertical ground reaction force (vGRF) on the public dataset.
  • The robust and highly accurate performance demonstrates its strong potential for applications requiring precise biomechanical assessment.

Methodology

The study proposes a Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network. This deep learning architecture is designed with two parallel processing paths: one path integrates anatomy-inspired spatial and temporal priors through a region-level attention mechanism, allowing it to focus on relevant foot regions and their temporal dynamics. The other complementary path concurrently captures context from the full sensor field, ensuring a comprehensive understanding of pressure distribution. Both paths are jointly trained, and their outputs are combined to generate the final three-dimensional GRF/GRM predictions. The method was evaluated using data collected from pressure-sensing insoles and validated on a publicly available walking dataset.

Key Findings

The proposed Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network demonstrated superior performance for GRF/GRM regression, significantly outperforming strong baseline models like CNN and CNN-LSTM architectures. It achieved a six-component average Normalized Root Mean Square Error (NRMSE) of 5.78% on the proprietary insole dataset. For the vertical ground reaction force (vGRF) specifically, it attained an NRMSE of 1.42% on a public walking dataset. These results indicate highly accurate and robust estimation capabilities.

Clinical Impact

This technology holds significant clinical potential for revolutionizing gait analysis and rehabilitation evaluation. By providing accurate, insole-based GRF/GRM estimation, it enables objective quantification of patient movement patterns, aids in the assessment of treatment effectiveness, prosthetic/orthotic device fitting, and injury risk evaluation. Its portability allows for continuous monitoring in real-world environments, moving beyond the constraints of lab-based force plates and offering more relevant data for clinical decision-making and personalized intervention strategies.

Abstract

Accurate estimation of three-dimensional ground reaction forces and moments (GRFs/GRMs) is crucial for both biomechanics research and clinical rehabilitation evaluation. In this study, we focus on insole-based GRF/GRM estimation and further validate our approach on a public walking dataset. We propose a Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network that integrates anatomy-inspired spatial priors and temporal priors into a region-level attention mechanism, while a complementary path captures context from the full sensor field. The two paths are trained jointly and their outputs are combined to produce the final GRF/GRM predictions. Conclusions: Our model outperforms strong baseline models, including CNN and CNN-LSTM architectures on two datasets, achieving the lowest six-component average NRMSE of 5.78% on the insole dataset and 1.42% for the vertical ground reaction force on the public dataset. This demonstrates robust performance for ground reaction force and moment estimation.
