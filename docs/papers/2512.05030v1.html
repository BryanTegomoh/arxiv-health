Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network for Ground Reaction Force and Moment Regression

arXiv ID: 2512.05030v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Xuan Li, Samuel Bello
Categories: cs.LG, eess.SP
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network for accurate, insole-based estimation of three-dimensional ground reaction forces (GRFs) and moments (GRMs), vital for biomechanics and clinical rehabilitation. The network leverages anatomy-inspired spatial and temporal priors through a region-level attention mechanism alongside a complementary path capturing full sensor field context. The model demonstrates robust performance, outperforming strong CNN and CNN-LSTM baselines on two datasets with impressive normalized root mean square error (NRMSE) metrics.

Medical Relevance

Accurate GRF/GRM estimation is fundamental for understanding human movement mechanics, diagnosing gait abnormalities, monitoring rehabilitation progress, and evaluating the effectiveness of interventions in various clinical settings.

AI Health Application

The paper describes an AI (deep learning) model designed to accurately estimate ground reaction forces and moments from insole-based sensors. This AI application can be used in healthcare for objective and continuous monitoring of patient gait, balance, and movement patterns. It could aid clinicians in evaluating rehabilitation progress, diagnosing gait abnormalities, assessing the risk of falls, optimizing prosthetic or orthotic fit, and personalizing treatment plans for individuals recovering from injuries, surgeries, or managing neurological conditions affecting mobility.

Key Points

  • The study addresses the critical need for accurate 3D GRF/GRM estimation in biomechanics research and clinical rehabilitation evaluation, specifically focusing on insole-based measurements.
  • A novel Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network (DP-RGAN) is proposed, featuring two distinct processing paths for enhanced estimation.
  • One path integrates anatomy-inspired spatial and temporal priors into a region-level attention mechanism, allowing the model to focus on functionally relevant areas of the foot.
  • A complementary second path captures broader contextual information from the entire sensor field, ensuring comprehensive data utilization.
  • The network architecture is trained jointly, combining the outputs from both paths for final GRF/GRM predictions.
  • The DP-RGAN significantly outperforms established baseline models, including CNN and CNN-LSTM architectures, on both an insole dataset and a public walking dataset.
  • Achieved a low average six-component NRMSE of 5.78% on the insole dataset and an NRMSE of 1.42% for the vertical GRF on the public dataset, demonstrating high accuracy and robustness.

Methodology

The study employs a Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network, a deep learning architecture. One path incorporates anatomy-inspired spatial and temporal priors into a region-level attention mechanism, focusing on specific foot regions. A second, complementary path concurrently processes the full sensor field to capture global context. Both paths are trained jointly, and their respective outputs are combined to yield the final GRF/GRM regression predictions. The model's performance was validated against CNN and CNN-LSTM baselines on an insole dataset and a public walking dataset, using Normalized Root Mean Square Error (NRMSE) as the primary evaluation metric.

Key Findings

The Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network achieved superior performance compared to strong CNN and CNN-LSTM baseline models. It recorded an average six-component NRMSE of 5.78% on the insole dataset and an NRMSE of 1.42% for the vertical ground reaction force on a public walking dataset, indicating robust and highly accurate estimation of GRFs and GRMs.

Clinical Impact

This technology holds significant clinical impact by enabling more accurate and potentially portable GRF/GRM measurements. It could facilitate precise gait analysis for diagnosing neurological or musculoskeletal conditions, objectively monitoring patient recovery during rehabilitation, guiding personalized intervention strategies, and evaluating prosthetic or orthotic device effectiveness, moving beyond lab-based force plates towards more ecologically valid assessments.

Abstract

Accurate estimation of three-dimensional ground reaction forces and moments (GRFs/GRMs) is crucial for both biomechanics research and clinical rehabilitation evaluation. In this study, we focus on insole-based GRF/GRM estimation and further validate our approach on a public walking dataset. We propose a Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network that integrates anatomy-inspired spatial priors and temporal priors into a region-level attention mechanism, while a complementary path captures context from the full sensor field. The two paths are trained jointly and their outputs are combined to produce the final GRF/GRM predictions. Conclusions: Our model outperforms strong baseline models, including CNN and CNN-LSTM architectures on two datasets, achieving the lowest six-component average NRMSE of 5.78% on the insole dataset and 1.42% for the vertical ground reaction force on the public dataset. This demonstrates robust performance for ground reaction force and moment estimation.
