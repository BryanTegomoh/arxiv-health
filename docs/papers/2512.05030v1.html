Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network for Ground Reaction Force and Moment Regression

arXiv ID: 2512.05030v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Xuan Li, Samuel Bello
Categories: cs.LG, eess.SP
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network for accurate, insole-based estimation of three-dimensional ground reaction forces and moments (GRFs/GRMs). The network integrates anatomy-inspired spatial and temporal priors with a region-level attention mechanism and a complementary path for full sensor context. It demonstrates superior performance over strong baselines, achieving significantly lower normalized root mean square errors on both an insole dataset and a public walking dataset, proving robust performance for GRF/GRM estimation.

Medical Relevance

Accurate estimation of GRFs/GRMs is fundamental for assessing human movement, diagnosing gait abnormalities, evaluating rehabilitation progress, and designing assistive devices, making this work highly relevant for improving clinical diagnostic tools and patient care by providing objective, quantitative metrics.

AI Health Application

The AI model (Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network) is applied to objectively quantify patient movement and gait parameters (ground reaction forces and moments) using insole sensors. This can be used in healthcare for: 1) diagnosing gait abnormalities, 2) monitoring patient progress during rehabilitation, 3) evaluating the effectiveness of interventions (e.g., new prosthetics, surgical procedures, physical therapy), and 4) providing data for personalized rehabilitation plans.

Key Points

  • Addresses the crucial need for accurate estimation of three-dimensional ground reaction forces and moments (GRFs/GRMs) in biomechanics research and clinical rehabilitation evaluation.
  • Focuses on insole-based GRF/GRM estimation, offering a potentially portable and less intrusive measurement approach.
  • Proposes a novel 'Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network' architecture to enhance estimation accuracy by integrating specialized processing streams.
  • Incorporates anatomy-inspired spatial priors and temporal priors directly into a region-level attention mechanism, leveraging physiological knowledge to guide the network's focus.
  • Features a complementary path that captures broader contextual information from the full sensor field, working in conjunction with the region-guided attention path, with combined outputs for final predictions.
  • Demonstrates superior performance against strong baseline models, including traditional CNN and CNN-LSTM architectures, on two distinct datasets.
  • Achieved a low six-component average Normalized Root Mean Square Error (NRMSE) of 5.78% on the insole dataset and an NRMSE of 1.42% for the vertical ground reaction force on a public walking dataset, indicating high accuracy and robustness.

Methodology

The study proposes a Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network. One path integrates anatomy-inspired spatial priors and temporal priors into a region-level attention mechanism, enabling the network to focus on biologically relevant areas. A complementary path concurrently captures context from the full sensor field, ensuring comprehensive data utilization. Both paths are trained jointly, and their outputs are combined to produce the final GRF/GRM predictions. The model's efficacy was validated by comparing its performance against strong CNN and CNN-LSTM baselines on an insole dataset and a public walking dataset.

Key Findings

The Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network consistently outperformed strong CNN and CNN-LSTM baseline models on both validation datasets. It achieved a six-component average Normalized Root Mean Square Error (NRMSE) of 5.78% on the insole dataset, and a significantly low NRMSE of 1.42% for the vertical ground reaction force on a public walking dataset, thereby demonstrating robust and superior accuracy for estimating ground reaction forces and moments.

Clinical Impact

This technology holds significant potential to revolutionize clinical gait analysis by enabling more accurate, portable, and potentially real-time assessment of movement in diverse patient populations. It can aid in precise diagnosis of musculoskeletal and neurological movement disorders, objectively monitor rehabilitation progress, inform the design and optimization of prosthetics and orthotics, and provide quantitative feedback for physical therapy interventions, ultimately leading to personalized and more effective patient care strategies.

Abstract

Accurate estimation of three-dimensional ground reaction forces and moments (GRFs/GRMs) is crucial for both biomechanics research and clinical rehabilitation evaluation. In this study, we focus on insole-based GRF/GRM estimation and further validate our approach on a public walking dataset. We propose a Dual-Path Region-Guided Attention Network that integrates anatomy-inspired spatial priors and temporal priors into a region-level attention mechanism, while a complementary path captures context from the full sensor field. The two paths are trained jointly and their outputs are combined to produce the final GRF/GRM predictions. Conclusions: Our model outperforms strong baseline models, including CNN and CNN-LSTM architectures on two datasets, achieving the lowest six-component average NRMSE of 5.78% on the insole dataset and 1.42% for the vertical ground reaction force on the public dataset. This demonstrates robust performance for ground reaction force and moment estimation.
