DeepPNI: Language- and graph-based model for mutation-driven protein-nucleic acid energetics

arXiv ID: 2511.22239v1
Published: 2025-11-27
Authors: Somnath Mondal, Tinkal Mondal, Soumajit Pramanik, Rukmankesh Mehra
Categories: q-bio.BM, cs.AI

Summary

DeepPNI is a novel deep learning model designed to accurately predict mutation-induced binding free energy changes in protein-nucleic acid complexes. It integrates structural features encoded by an edge-aware RGCN and sequential features from the ESM-2 protein language model, achieving robust and generalizable performance with a 0.76 Pearson correlation coefficient across a diverse dataset of 1951 mutations. This tool addresses limitations of experimental techniques in understanding disease-relevant mutational effects.

Medical Relevance

Understanding the energetic impact of mutations on protein-nucleic acid interactions is fundamental, as these processes underpin DNA maintenance, gene expression, and other vital cellular functions, and their disruption often leads to serious diseases. DeepPNI provides a computational tool to rapidly and accurately predict these effects, aiding in the elucidation of disease mechanisms and the identification of potential therapeutic targets.

AI Health Application

DeepPNI is a deep learning model that serves as an AI application to predict the biophysical impact of mutations on protein-nucleic acid interactions. This application has direct relevance to health by enabling researchers and clinicians to better understand how genetic mutations lead to disease, identify potential drug targets, and possibly aid in the interpretation of genetic variants in clinical genomics.

Methodology

DeepPNI employs a deep learning-based regression architecture that integrates two distinct feature types. Structural features from protein-nucleic acid complexes are processed via an edge-aware Relational Graph Convolutional Network (RGCN), while sequential features are extracted using the ESM-2 protein language model. These combined features are then fed into the model to predict mutation-induced binding free energy changes. The model was rigorously validated using five-fold cross-validation on a dataset of 1951 mutations, including both protein-DNA and protein-RNA complexes, and also through external dataset validation and comparison with existing tools.

Key Findings

The DeepPNI model achieved a high average Pearson correlation coefficient (PCC) of 0.76 during five-fold cross-validation, indicating its strong predictive capability for mutation-induced binding free energy changes. It demonstrated remarkable consistency and generalizability across diverse data subsets, specifically individual protein-DNA and protein-RNA complexes, and also across variations in experimental temperature. Furthermore, the model exhibited robust performance in complex-based cross-validation and significantly outperformed established computational tools in external validation datasets.

Clinical Impact

DeepPNI has the potential to significantly impact clinical research and drug discovery by providing a fast and reliable method to predict the energetic consequences of genetic mutations on protein-nucleic acid interactions. This can accelerate the identification of pathogenic mutations in genetic disorders, cancers, and infectious diseases, help elucidate disease mechanisms at a molecular level, and inform the design of targeted therapeutics by predicting how small molecules might modulate these critical interactions, thereby reducing the need for costly and time-consuming experimental validation.

Abstract

The interaction between proteins and nucleic acids is crucial for processes that sustain cellular function, including DNA maintenance and the regulation of gene expression and translation. Amino acid mutations in protein-nucleic acid complexes often lead to vital diseases. Experimental techniques have their own specific limitations in predicting mutational effects in protein-nucleic acid complexes. In this study, we compiled a large dataset of 1951 mutations including both protein-DNA and protein-RNA complexes and integrated structural and sequential features to build a deep learning-based regression model named DeepPNI. This model estimates mutation-induced binding free energy changes in protein-nucleic acid complexes. The structural features are encoded via edge-aware RGCN and the sequential features are extracted using protein language model ESM-2. Consistent performance across individual dataset of protein-DNA, protein-RNA complexes, and different experimental temperature split dataset make the model generalizable.
