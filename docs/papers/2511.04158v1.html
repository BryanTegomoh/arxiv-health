Deep Learning Approach for Clinical Risk Identification Using Transformer Modeling of Heterogeneous EHR Data

Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Anzhuo Xie, Wei-Chen Chang

Summary

This study proposes a novel Transformer-based deep learning model designed to address the complexities of clinical risk classification using heterogeneous Electronic Health Record (EHR) data. The method effectively handles irregular temporal patterns and diverse data modalities through specialized embedding, temporal encoding, multi-head self-attention, and a semantic-weighted pooling module. Experimental results demonstrate its superior performance over traditional machine learning and temporal deep learning models, offering a stable and precise framework for clinical risk identification.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to healthcare as it provides a more accurate and robust method for predicting patient risks from their comprehensive medical histories, enabling clinicians to make proactive and data-driven decisions for improved patient outcomes and personalized care.

AI Health Application

The paper proposes a Transformer-based deep learning model to analyze multi-source, heterogeneous Electronic Health Record (EHR) data. This AI application aims to identify clinical risks in patients by processing structured and unstructured medical features, capturing temporal patterns and semantic structures, and ultimately generating individual-level risk scores to aid clinical intelligent decision-making.

Key Points

  • Addresses critical challenges in clinical risk classification from heterogeneous EHR data, including irregular temporal patterns, significant modality differences (structured/unstructured), and complex semantic structures.
  • Introduces a Transformer-based longitudinal modeling method that processes multi-source medical features as input.
  • Utilizes a feature embedding layer to create a unified representation of both structured and unstructured EHR data.
  • Incorporates a learnable temporal encoding mechanism specifically designed to capture dynamic evolution in data with uneven sampling intervals.
  • Employs a multi-head self-attention core to perform global dependency modeling on longitudinal sequences, aggregating long-term trends and short-term fluctuations.
  • Features a semantic-weighted pooling module that assigns adaptive importance to key medical events, thereby enhancing the discriminative ability of risk-related features.
  • Outperforms traditional machine learning and temporal deep learning models across key metrics (accuracy, recall, precision, F1-Score), achieving stable and precise risk identification.

Methodology

The proposed method is a Transformer-based deep learning architecture. It begins with a feature embedding layer to unify structured and unstructured EHR data into a common representation. A learnable temporal encoding mechanism is then applied to model dynamic changes over irregular time intervals. The core of the model is a multi-head self-attention structure, which captures global dependencies across longitudinal sequences. Finally, a semantic-weighted pooling module adaptively prioritizes key medical events before a linear mapping layer generates individual-level risk scores.

Key Findings

The study's key finding is that the proposed Transformer-based model significantly outperforms both traditional machine learning models and other temporal deep learning models. It achieved superior accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score, demonstrating its capacity for stable and precise clinical risk identification within multi-source heterogeneous EHR environments.

Clinical Impact

This framework offers an efficient and reliable tool for clinical intelligent decision-making. By providing accurate individual-level risk scores, it can assist healthcare providers in early identification of patients at risk, facilitate timely interventions, optimize resource allocation, and support the development of more personalized and effective treatment strategies.

Abstract

This study proposes a Transformer-based longitudinal modeling method to address challenges in clinical risk classification with heterogeneous Electronic Health Record (EHR) data, including irregular temporal patterns, large modality differences, and complex semantic structures. The method takes multi-source medical features as input and employs a feature embedding layer to achieve a unified representation of structured and unstructured data. A learnable temporal encoding mechanism is introduced to capture dynamic evolution under uneven sampling intervals. The core model adopts a multi-head self-attention structure to perform global dependency modeling on longitudinal sequences, enabling the aggregation of long-term trends and short-term fluctuations across different temporal scales. To enhance semantic representation, a semantic-weighted pooling module is designed to assign adaptive importance to key medical events, improving the discriminative ability of risk-related features. Finally, a linear mapping layer generates individual-level risk scores. Experimental results show that the proposed model outperforms traditional machine learning and temporal deep learning models in accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score, achieving stable and precise risk identification in multi-source heterogeneous EHR environments and providing an efficient and reliable framework for clinical intelligent decision-making.
