Deep Learning Approach for Clinical Risk Identification Using Transformer Modeling of Heterogeneous EHR Data

arXiv ID: 2511.04158v1
Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Anzhuo Xie, Wei-Chen Chang
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00

Summary

This study introduces a novel Transformer-based deep learning approach to model heterogeneous Electronic Health Record (EHR) data for clinical risk identification, effectively addressing challenges like irregular temporal patterns and diverse data modalities. The method integrates a learnable temporal encoding, multi-head self-attention, and a semantic-weighted pooling module to capture complex dependencies and semantic nuances. Experimental results demonstrate superior performance over existing models in key metrics, offering an efficient and reliable framework for clinical intelligent decision-making.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a more robust and accurate method for predicting clinical risks from complex, real-world patient data, which can lead to earlier intervention, personalized treatment strategies, and improved patient outcomes through enhanced intelligent decision support in healthcare settings.

AI Health Application

The AI application is a Transformer-based deep learning model designed to analyze heterogeneous Electronic Health Record (EHR) data (structured and unstructured) to identify and classify individual-level clinical risks. This provides an intelligent decision-making framework for healthcare professionals by predicting potential adverse health events or conditions.

Key Points

  • Addresses critical challenges in clinical risk classification using heterogeneous EHR data, including irregular temporal patterns, large modality differences (structured and unstructured), and complex semantic structures.
  • Proposes a Transformer-based longitudinal modeling method that takes multi-source medical features as input for unified representation.
  • Incorporates a feature embedding layer to transform both structured and unstructured EHR data into a cohesive, unified representation.
  • Introduces a learnable temporal encoding mechanism specifically designed to capture dynamic patient evolution under uneven and irregular data sampling intervals.
  • Utilizes a multi-head self-attention core model to perform global dependency modeling on longitudinal sequences, enabling the aggregation of both long-term trends and short-term fluctuations across varying temporal scales.
  • Designs a semantic-weighted pooling module that assigns adaptive importance to key medical events, thereby enhancing the semantic representation and discriminative ability of risk-related features.
  • Experimentally shown to outperform traditional machine learning and other temporal deep learning models across key metrics: accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score, achieving stable and precise risk identification.

Methodology

The proposed method employs a Transformer architecture for longitudinal EHR data. It starts with a feature embedding layer to create a unified representation of multi-source structured and unstructured medical data. A learnable temporal encoding mechanism is then introduced to capture dynamic evolution under uneven sampling intervals. The core model uses a multi-head self-attention structure to perform global dependency modeling on longitudinal sequences. To enhance semantic representation, a semantic-weighted pooling module assigns adaptive importance to key medical events. Finally, a linear mapping layer generates individual-level risk scores.

Key Findings

The Transformer-based model significantly outperforms both traditional machine learning and existing temporal deep learning models in accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score. It demonstrates stable and precise clinical risk identification in real-world scenarios involving multi-source, heterogeneous EHR data.

Clinical Impact

This model provides an efficient and reliable framework for intelligent clinical decision-making by accurately identifying individual patient risks from complex EHR data. Its ability to process diverse and temporally irregular information can facilitate proactive interventions, optimize resource allocation, and support personalized medicine initiatives, ultimately enhancing patient safety and the quality of care.

Abstract

This study proposes a Transformer-based longitudinal modeling method to address challenges in clinical risk classification with heterogeneous Electronic Health Record (EHR) data, including irregular temporal patterns, large modality differences, and complex semantic structures. The method takes multi-source medical features as input and employs a feature embedding layer to achieve a unified representation of structured and unstructured data. A learnable temporal encoding mechanism is introduced to capture dynamic evolution under uneven sampling intervals. The core model adopts a multi-head self-attention structure to perform global dependency modeling on longitudinal sequences, enabling the aggregation of long-term trends and short-term fluctuations across different temporal scales. To enhance semantic representation, a semantic-weighted pooling module is designed to assign adaptive importance to key medical events, improving the discriminative ability of risk-related features. Finally, a linear mapping layer generates individual-level risk scores. Experimental results show that the proposed model outperforms traditional machine learning and temporal deep learning models in accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score, achieving stable and precise risk identification in multi-source heterogeneous EHR environments and providing an efficient and reliable framework for clinical intelligent decision-making.
