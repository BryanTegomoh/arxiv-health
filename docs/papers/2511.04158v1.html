Deep Learning Approach for Clinical Risk Identification Using Transformer Modeling of Heterogeneous EHR Data

arXiv ID: 2511.04158v1
Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Anzhuo Xie, Wei-Chen Chang
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00

Summary

This study introduces a novel Transformer-based deep learning model designed for clinical risk identification from heterogeneous Electronic Health Record (EHR) data. The model effectively addresses challenges like irregular temporal patterns, large modality differences, and complex semantic structures by employing specialized embedding, temporal encoding, and attention mechanisms. Experimental results demonstrate its superior performance over existing models, offering a robust framework for intelligent clinical decision-making.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for advancing precision medicine and predictive analytics in healthcare by enabling more accurate and timely identification of patient risks directly from complex, real-world EHR data. It offers a powerful tool for clinicians to proactively intervene, personalize care, and ultimately improve patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

This research applies deep learning (specifically Transformer models) to analyze complex, heterogeneous Electronic Health Record (EHR) data to predict individual patient risk for various clinical outcomes. The AI application is to develop intelligent systems that can assist clinicians in proactive risk identification and decision-making, ultimately improving patient care.

Key Points

  • Addresses critical challenges in clinical risk classification from heterogeneous EHR data, including irregular temporal patterns, diverse data modalities (structured/unstructured), and complex semantic structures.
  • Proposes a Transformer-based longitudinal modeling method, leveraging a multi-head self-attention structure to capture global dependencies across different temporal scales.
  • Incorporates a feature embedding layer for unified representation of multi-source structured and unstructured medical data.
  • Introduces a learnable temporal encoding mechanism to effectively capture dynamic evolution within unevenly sampled longitudinal patient data.
  • Designs a semantic-weighted pooling module that assigns adaptive importance to key medical events, thereby enhancing the discriminative ability of risk-related features.
  • Outperforms traditional machine learning and temporal deep learning models across key metrics (accuracy, recall, precision, F1-Score), achieving stable and precise risk identification.

Methodology

The study proposes a Transformer-based longitudinal deep learning model. It integrates a feature embedding layer for unified representation of multi-source structured and unstructured EHR data, followed by a learnable temporal encoding mechanism to handle irregular sampling intervals. The core architecture utilizes a multi-head self-attention structure for global dependency modeling on longitudinal sequences, capturing both long-term trends and short-term fluctuations. A semantic-weighted pooling module enhances semantic representation by adaptively weighting key medical events. Finally, a linear mapping layer generates individual-level risk scores. The model's performance was experimentally validated against traditional machine learning and temporal deep learning models.

Key Findings

The proposed Transformer-based deep learning model consistently and significantly outperforms traditional machine learning models and other temporal deep learning models across crucial evaluation metrics, including accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score. It demonstrates superior stability and precision in clinical risk identification within complex multi-source heterogeneous EHR environments.

Clinical Impact

This framework offers a highly efficient and reliable tool for clinicians, providing accurate, individualized risk scores that can facilitate earlier interventions, optimize personalized treatment plans, and support more informed clinical decision-making. Its adoption has the potential to enhance patient safety, improve the allocation of healthcare resources, and move healthcare towards a more proactive and preventative paradigm.

Abstract

This study proposes a Transformer-based longitudinal modeling method to address challenges in clinical risk classification with heterogeneous Electronic Health Record (EHR) data, including irregular temporal patterns, large modality differences, and complex semantic structures. The method takes multi-source medical features as input and employs a feature embedding layer to achieve a unified representation of structured and unstructured data. A learnable temporal encoding mechanism is introduced to capture dynamic evolution under uneven sampling intervals. The core model adopts a multi-head self-attention structure to perform global dependency modeling on longitudinal sequences, enabling the aggregation of long-term trends and short-term fluctuations across different temporal scales. To enhance semantic representation, a semantic-weighted pooling module is designed to assign adaptive importance to key medical events, improving the discriminative ability of risk-related features. Finally, a linear mapping layer generates individual-level risk scores. Experimental results show that the proposed model outperforms traditional machine learning and temporal deep learning models in accuracy, recall, precision, and F1-Score, achieving stable and precise risk identification in multi-source heterogeneous EHR environments and providing an efficient and reliable framework for clinical intelligent decision-making.
