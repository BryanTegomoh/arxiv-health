GCA-ResUNet:Image segmentation in medical images using grouped coordinate attention

arXiv ID: 2511.14087v1
Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Jun Ding, Shang Gao
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces GCA-ResUNet, a novel and efficient convolutional neural network for medical image segmentation. It integrates Grouped Coordinate Attention (GCA) into ResNet-50 residual blocks to effectively capture long-range dependencies across channels and spatial locations, addressing limitations of traditional U-Net architectures and computational burdens of Transformers. GCA-ResUNet achieves high accuracy, evidenced by Dice scores of 86.11% on Synapse and 92.64% on ACDC datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art baselines with minimal computational overhead.

Medical Relevance

Medical image segmentation is foundational for computer-aided diagnosis and therapy, directly supporting clinical tasks like disease diagnosis, precise preoperative planning, and accurate monitoring of disease progression over time. Enhancing its accuracy and efficiency directly translates to improved patient care and clinical workflows.

AI Health Application

The AI application is to develop high-accuracy and resource-efficient medical image segmentation models. These models assist healthcare professionals by providing precise automated segmentation of anatomical structures or pathologies from medical scans (e.g., CT, MRI), thereby supporting computer-aided diagnosis, guiding surgical planning, and enabling quantitative assessment for disease monitoring.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: Traditional U-Net architectures struggle with capturing long-range dependencies, while Transformer-based methods, though effective for global context, are computationally expensive and data-hungry.
  • **Proposed Solution**: GCA-ResUNet integrates Grouped Coordinate Attention (GCA) into ResNet-50 residual blocks within a U-Net style encoder-decoder framework.
  • **GCA Mechanism**: GCA employs grouped coordinate modeling to jointly encode global dependencies across both channel and spatial dimensions, enhancing feature representation and boundary delineation.
  • **Computational Efficiency**: GCA is designed to add minimal parameter and FLOP overhead compared to self-attention mechanisms, leading to fast inference and favorable computational efficiency.
  • **Performance on Synapse**: Achieved a Dice score of 86.11% on the Synapse multi-organ segmentation dataset, demonstrating robust performance for abdominal CT imaging.
  • **Performance on ACDC**: Achieved a Dice score of 92.64% on the ACDC (Automated Cardiac Diagnosis Challenge) dataset, indicating high accuracy for cardiac MRI segmentation.
  • **State-of-the-Art Comparison**: GCA-ResUNet surpasses several existing state-of-the-art baselines across both evaluated datasets.

Methodology

The proposed GCA-ResUNet is an encoder-decoder network architecturally inspired by U-Net. It incorporates ResNet-50 as its backbone for robust feature extraction within the encoder. The core innovation is the integration of Grouped Coordinate Attention (GCA) modules within these ResNet-50 residual blocks. GCA facilitates global dependency modeling by grouping channels and coordinates, jointly encoding spatial and channel-wise relationships, thereby strengthening feature representation and improving boundary delineation with minimal computational overhead compared to traditional self-attention.

Key Findings

GCA-ResUNet achieved a Dice score of 86.11% on the Synapse multi-organ CT segmentation dataset and 92.64% on the ACDC cardiac MRI segmentation dataset. These results not only indicate high segmentation accuracy but also surpass several state-of-the-art baselines while maintaining computational efficiency and fast inference times.

Abstract

Medical image segmentation underpins computer-aided diagnosis and therapy by supporting clinical diagnosis, preoperative planning, and disease monitoring. While U-Net style convolutional neural networks perform well due to their encoder-decoder structures with skip connections, they struggle to capture long-range dependencies. Transformer-based variants address global context but often require heavy computation and large training datasets. This paper proposes GCA-ResUNet, an efficient segmentation network that integrates Grouped Coordinate Attention (GCA) into ResNet-50 residual blocks. GCA uses grouped coordinate modeling to jointly encode global dependencies across channels and spatial locations, strengthening feature representation and boundary delineation while adding minimal parameter and FLOP overhead compared with self-attention. On the Synapse dataset, GCA-ResUNet achieves a Dice score of 86.11%, and on the ACDC dataset, it reaches 92.64%, surpassing several state-of-the-art baselines while maintaining fast inference and favorable computational efficiency. These results indicate that GCA offers a practical way to enhance convolutional architectures with global modeling capability, enabling high-accuracy and resource-efficient medical image segmentation.
