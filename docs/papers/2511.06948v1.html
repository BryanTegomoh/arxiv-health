Summary

The paper introduces PADM, a novel CT-free Physics-aware Diffusion Model, to effectively correct attenuation artifacts in cardiac SPECT Myocardial Perfusion Imaging (MPI). Leveraging explicit physics priors via a teacher-student distillation mechanism, PADM accurately generates Attenuation-Corrected (AC) images solely from Non-Attenuation-Corrected (NAC) inputs. Supported by the new CardiAC dataset, extensive experiments demonstrate PADM's superior reconstruction fidelity compared to state-of-the-art generative models.

Medical Relevance

Attenuation artifacts severely hinder the reliable diagnosis of myocardial ischemia and infarct in cardiac SPECT, potentially leading to misdiagnosis or inappropriate patient management. This CT-free solution offers a means to achieve highly accurate attenuation correction, directly improving the diagnostic utility and trustworthiness of cardiac SPECT imaging.

AI Health Application

The paper develops PADM, a Physics-aware Attenuation Correction Diffusion Model, which is a diffusion-based generative AI model. This AI model is designed to perform CT-free attenuation correction on Non-Attenuation-Corrected (NAC) cardiac SPECT images. Its application is to enhance the quality and diagnostic utility of SPECT scans, leading to improved diagnostic accuracy for cardiac diseases, reduced patient radiation exposure, and increased accessibility to critical medical imaging, directly impacting patient health outcomes.

Methodology

The study proposes PADM, a diffusion-based generative model for attenuation correction. It incorporates explicit physics priors during training through a teacher-student distillation mechanism. The model is trained on the newly introduced CardiAC dataset, which provides paired Non-Attenuation-Corrected (NAC) and Attenuation-Corrected (AC) SPECT images, along with CT-based attenuation maps, to learn the mapping required for artifact correction. This allows PADM to generate AC images from NAC input without requiring a separate CT scan during inference.

Key Findings

PADM demonstrates superior reconstruction fidelity for attenuation-corrected cardiac SPECT images compared to current state-of-the-art generative models. This superiority is confirmed through both objective quantitative metrics and visual assessment, indicating a significant improvement in the quality and accuracy of the corrected images.

Clinical Impact

This technology has the potential to significantly improve the diagnostic accuracy and clinical interpretability of cardiac SPECT MPI by effectively removing attenuation artifacts. By offering a CT-free solution, PADM can enhance accessibility to high-quality attenuation correction, reduce patient radiation exposure, and lower healthcare costs, thereby broadening the clinical utility and impact of SPECT in cardiology, especially in settings with limited access to hybrid SPECT/CT systems.

Abstract

Attenuation artifacts remain a significant challenge in cardiac Myocardial Perfusion Imaging (MPI) using Single-Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT), often compromising diagnostic accuracy and reducing clinical interpretability. While hybrid SPECT/CT systems mitigate these artifacts through CT-derived attenuation maps, their high cost, limited accessibility, and added radiation exposure hinder widespread clinical adoption. In this study, we propose a novel CT-free solution to attenuation correction in cardiac SPECT. Specifically, we introduce Physics-aware Attenuation Correction Diffusion Model (PADM), a diffusion-based generative method that incorporates explicit physics priors via a teacher--student distillation mechanism. This approach enables attenuation artifact correction using only Non-Attenuation-Corrected (NAC) input, while still benefiting from physics-informed supervision during training. To support this work, we also introduce CardiAC, a comprehensive dataset comprising 424 patient studies with paired NAC and Attenuation-Corrected (AC) reconstructions, alongside high-resolution CT-based attenuation maps. Extensive experiments demonstrate that PADM outperforms state-of-the-art generative models, delivering superior reconstruction fidelity across both quantitative metrics and visual assessment.
