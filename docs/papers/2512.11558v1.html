DentalGPT: Incentivizing Multimodal Complex Reasoning in Dentistry

Summary

DentalGPT introduces a specialized Multimodal Large Language Model (MLLM) designed to overcome current MLLM limitations in capturing fine-grained dental visual details and performing complex reasoning for precise diagnoses. Leveraging the largest annotated multimodal dental dataset to date (over 120k images with detailed descriptions) and a staged training approach including domain knowledge injection and reinforcement learning, DentalGPT achieves superior performance in dental disease classification and VQA tasks. This 7B-parameter model outperforms many state-of-the-art MLLMs on key dental benchmarks, showcasing an effective pathway for domain-specialized MLLM development.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for advancing automated oral healthcare by providing an AI model capable of reliable, fine-grained interpretation of multimodal dental data, which can significantly aid dentists in precise diagnosis, treatment planning, and overall patient care.

AI Health Application

DentalGPT is an AI application designed to enhance automated oral healthcare by accurately interpreting multimodal dental data. It aims to assist dental professionals with precise diagnosis of dental conditions, disease classification, and answering complex visual questions related to dental images, thereby potentially improving the efficiency and accuracy of dental care.

Key Points

  • Current MLLMs struggle with fine-grained dental visual details and lack sufficient reasoning for precise dental diagnosis.
  • DentalGPT is introduced as a specialized dental MLLM developed to address these limitations through domain knowledge injection and reinforcement learning.
  • A novel, largest-to-date multimodal dental dataset was constructed, comprising over 120,000 dental images paired with detailed, diagnostically relevant textual descriptions.
  • The model's development involved two stages: initial training on the new dataset to enhance visual understanding, followed by reinforcement learning to strengthen multimodal complex reasoning capabilities.
  • DentalGPT achieved superior performance in dental disease classification and dental Visual Question Answering (VQA) tasks compared to many state-of-the-art MLLMs.
  • The model demonstrated this superior performance across various benchmarks, including intraoral, panoramic images, and dental subsets of medical VQA, despite having only 7 billion parameters.

Methodology

The authors developed DentalGPT, a specialized Multimodal Large Language Model (MLLM) with 7 billion parameters. The methodology involved two primary stages: 1) **Dataset Construction**: Creation of the largest annotated multimodal dataset for dentistry, aggregating over 120,000 dental images with detailed descriptions highlighting diagnostically relevant visual features. 2) **Staged Model Adaptation**: An initial training phase on this high-quality dental dataset to inject domain knowledge and enhance visual understanding of dental conditions, followed by a reinforcement learning stage to further strengthen the model's capability for multimodal complex reasoning.

Key Findings

DentalGPT achieved superior performance in dental disease classification and dental Visual Question Answering (VQA) tasks. It significantly outperformed many existing state-of-the-art MLLMs on comprehensive evaluations across intraoral and panoramic benchmarks, as well as dental subsets of medical VQA benchmarks. Notably, DentalGPT demonstrated this enhanced capability with a relatively compact architecture of only 7 billion parameters, validating that high-quality dental data and a staged adaptation approach effectively build capable and domain-specialized dental MLLMs.

Abstract

Reliable interpretation of multimodal data in dentistry is essential for automated oral healthcare, yet current multimodal large language models (MLLMs) struggle to capture fine-grained dental visual details and lack sufficient reasoning ability for precise diagnosis. To address these limitations, we present DentalGPT, a specialized dental MLLM developed through high-quality domain knowledge injection and reinforcement learning. Specifically, the largest annotated multimodal dataset for dentistry to date was constructed by aggregating over 120k dental images paired with detailed descriptions that highlight diagnostically relevant visual features, making it the multimodal dataset with the most extensive collection of dental images to date. Training on this dataset significantly enhances the MLLM's visual understanding of dental conditions, while the subsequent reinforcement learning stage further strengthens its capability for multimodal complex reasoning. Comprehensive evaluations on intraoral and panoramic benchmarks, along with dental subsets of medical VQA benchmarks, show that DentalGPT achieves superior performance in disease classification and dental VQA tasks, outperforming many state-of-the-art MLLMs despite having only 7B parameters. These results demonstrate that high-quality dental data combined with staged adaptation provides an effective pathway for building capable and domain-specialized dental MLLMs.
