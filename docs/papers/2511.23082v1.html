Implementation of a Skin Lesion Detection System for Managing Children with Atopic Dermatitis Based on Ensemble Learning

arXiv ID: 2511.23082v1
Published: 2025-11-28
Authors: Soobin Jeon, Sujong Kim, Dongmahn Seo
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces ENSEL, an ensemble learning-based skin lesion detection system designed to provide objective diagnostic support for managing children with atopic dermatitis. By integrating various deep learning models, ENSEL enhances diagnostic accuracy and achieves high recall with a sub-second processing speed, validated using real-world user-taken images.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant as it provides an objective, AI-driven diagnostic aid for atopic dermatitis, addressing the current subjective evaluation methods that lead to misdiagnosis and improving the management of this chronic condition in children.

AI Health Application

The paper proposes an ensemble learning-based skin lesion detection system (ENSEL) that integrates various deep learning models to enhance the diagnostic accuracy of atopic dermatitis and other skin lesions, particularly for managing children. This system aims to provide objective diagnostic methods, reduce misdiagnosis, and improve real-world clinical utility by using images from actual users and ensuring fast response times.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical need for objective diagnostic methods for atopic dermatitis (AD), a chronic inflammatory skin disease prone to subjective misdiagnosis and often confused with psoriasis.
  • Proposes ENSEL (Ensemble Learning-based Skin Lesion Detection system), which leverages an ensemble approach to integrate various deep learning models, thereby enhancing diagnostic accuracy.
  • The system is specifically tailored for the management of children with atopic dermatitis, contributing to improved care in a vulnerable population.
  • Unlike existing studies that rely on high-quality dermoscopic images, ENSEL's performance was validated using images of skin lesions taken by actual users, reflecting practical clinical settings.
  • Experimental results demonstrate ENSEL's effectiveness, achieving high recall across most skin disease images tested.
  • The system exhibits a rapid processing speed of less than 1 second, making it suitable for real-time applications in digital healthcare environments.
  • The research aims to contribute to the objective diagnosis of skin lesions and promote the advancement of digital healthcare, particularly in South Korea where the market is growing.

Methodology

The study proposes ENSEL, an ensemble learning framework that integrates various deep learning models to enhance diagnostic accuracy. Its performance was verified through skin lesion detection experiments using images of skin lesions taken by actual users, simulating real-world data. Accuracy (specifically recall) and response time were measured using randomly sampled skin disease images.

Key Findings

The ENSEL system achieved high recall for most skin disease images and demonstrated an efficient processing speed of less than 1 second, indicating its potential for accurate and rapid real-time diagnostic support in clinical settings.

Clinical Impact

ENSEL has the potential to significantly improve the accuracy and objectivity of atopic dermatitis diagnosis, reducing misdiagnosis rates and facilitating more effective management strategies, especially for children. Its rapid response time supports integration into digital healthcare platforms for accessible and efficient dermatological screening and monitoring.

Abstract

The amendments made to the Data 3 Act and impact of COVID-19 have fostered the growth of digital healthcare market and promoted the use of medical data in artificial intelligence in South Korea. Atopic dermatitis, a chronic inflammatory skin disease, is diagnosed via subjective evaluations without using objective diagnostic methods, thereby increasing the risk of misdiagnosis. It is also similar to psoriasis in appearance, further complicating its accurate diagnosis. Existing studies on skin diseases have used high-quality dermoscopic image datasets, but such high-quality images cannot be obtained in actual clinical settings. Moreover, existing systems must ensure accuracy and fast response times. To this end, an ensemble learning-based skin lesion detection system (ENSEL) was proposed herein. ENSEL enhanced diagnostic accuracy by integrating various deep learning models via an ensemble approach. Its performance was verified by conducting skin lesion detection experiments using images of skin lesions taken by actual users. Its accuracy and response time were measured using randomly sampled skin disease images. Results revealed that ENSEL achieved high recall in most images and less than 1s s processing speed. This study contributes to the objective diagnosis of skin lesions and promotes the advancement of digital healthcare.
