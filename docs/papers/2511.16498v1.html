Acquisition Time-Informed Breast Tumor Segmentation from Dynamic Contrast-Enhanced MRI

Summary

This paper introduces a novel deep learning method for breast tumor segmentation from Dynamic Contrast-Enhanced MRI (DCE-MRI) that addresses image variability by incorporating acquisition time. The proposed approach uses feature-wise linear modulation (FiLM) layers to modulate model features based on the specific acquisition sequence, leading to improved segmentation performance. Evaluation demonstrated enhanced tumor segmentation accuracy and better model generalization across diverse datasets.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated breast tumor segmentation from DCE-MRI is critical for precise cancer screening, thorough tumor assessment, and effective monitoring of treatment response, directly impacting patient diagnosis and care pathways.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the automated segmentation of breast tumors from Dynamic Contrast-Enhanced MRI scans. This aims to improve the accuracy and reliability of breast cancer screening, tumor assessment, and assist in treatment planning and monitoring, thereby enhancing diagnostic capabilities in oncology.

Key Points

  • Automated breast tumor segmentation from DCE-MRI is crucial for cancer management but challenged by significant variability in image appearance due to diverse acquisition protocols and individual factors.
  • The proposed method integrates knowledge of the image acquisition time directly into the deep learning model to modulate features, allowing it to adapt to specific acquisition sequences.
  • Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM) layers are utilized for incorporating temporal information, chosen for their lightweight nature and ability to handle a variable number of images per imaging study.
  • The models, including baseline and time-modulated configurations with varying backbone architectures, were trained on a large public multisite breast DCE-MRI dataset.
  • Evaluation results showed that explicitly incorporating phase acquisition time significantly improved breast tumor segmentation performance.
  • The time-modulated approach also demonstrated enhanced model generalization capabilities, performing better on both in-domain and a public out-of-domain dataset.

Methodology

The researchers developed a deep learning-based tumor segmentation method for DCE-MRI. They incorporated image acquisition time into the model by using Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM) layers, which dynamically adjust model features based on the specific acquisition sequence. This technique is designed to be lightweight and accommodate the variable number of images acquired in DCE-MRI studies. Models were trained and evaluated, comparing baseline architectures with various time-modulated configurations, on a large public multisite breast DCE-MRI dataset, with additional generalization testing on an independent out-of-domain dataset.

Key Findings

The study's main finding is that incorporating knowledge of phase acquisition time into a deep learning model significantly improves breast tumor segmentation performance. Crucially, this time-informed approach also led to superior model generalization, yielding better results on both the images from the training domain and an external, out-of-domain dataset.

Clinical Impact

This method has the potential to enhance the reliability and consistency of automated breast tumor segmentation in clinical practice. By accounting for variations in DCE-MRI protocols, it could improve diagnostic accuracy for breast cancer, facilitate more precise tumor characterization, and enable more robust monitoring of treatment effectiveness, ultimately aiding radiologists and oncologists in making better-informed clinical decisions.

Abstract

Dynamic contrast-enhanced magnetic resonance imaging (DCE-MRI) plays an important role in breast cancer screening, tumor assessment, and treatment planning and monitoring. The dynamic changes in contrast in different tissues help to highlight the tumor in post-contrast images. However, varying acquisition protocols and individual factors result in large variation in the appearance of tissues, even for images acquired in the same phase (e.g., first post-contrast phase), making automated tumor segmentation challenging. Here, we propose a tumor segmentation method that leverages knowledge of the image acquisition time to modulate model features according to the specific acquisition sequence. We incorporate the acquisition times using feature-wise linear modulation (FiLM) layers, a lightweight method for incorporating temporal information that also allows for capitalizing on the full, variables number of images acquired per imaging study. We trained baseline and different configurations for the time-modulated models with varying backbone architectures on a large public multisite breast DCE-MRI dataset. Evaluation on in-domain images and a public out-of-domain dataset showed that incorporating knowledge of phase acquisition time improved tumor segmentation performance and model generalization.
