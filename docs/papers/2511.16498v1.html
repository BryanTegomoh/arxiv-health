Acquisition Time-Informed Breast Tumor Segmentation from Dynamic Contrast-Enhanced MRI

Summary

This paper addresses the challenge of automated breast tumor segmentation from Dynamic Contrast-Enhanced MRI (DCE-MRI) caused by variations in acquisition protocols and patient factors. It proposes a novel deep learning method that leverages image acquisition time, incorporated via Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM) layers, to modulate model features. The study demonstrates that this time-informed approach significantly improves tumor segmentation performance and enhances model generalization across diverse datasets.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated breast tumor segmentation from DCE-MRI is critical for precise cancer screening, diagnosis, treatment planning, and monitoring. This method offers a more robust and generalizable solution, directly addressing a major hurdle in clinical deployment of AI for breast cancer, potentially leading to earlier detection and improved patient care.

AI Health Application

The AI application is an automated tumor segmentation method that leverages deep learning (using FiLM layers and various backbone architectures) to accurately identify and delineate breast tumors in Dynamic Contrast-Enhanced MRI scans. This aids in improving the efficiency and consistency of breast cancer screening, diagnosis, and treatment planning by overcoming variations in image acquisition protocols.

Key Points

  • Automated breast tumor segmentation from DCE-MRI is difficult due to large variations in tissue appearance, even within the same contrast phase, stemming from diverse acquisition protocols and individual patient factors.
  • The proposed solution integrates knowledge of image acquisition time directly into the segmentation model to modulate features according to the specific acquisition sequence.
  • Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM) layers are employed as a lightweight and efficient mechanism to incorporate temporal information, capable of handling a variable number of images per study.
  • The method was trained on a large public multisite breast DCE-MRI dataset, testing baseline models and various configurations of time-modulated models with different backbone architectures.
  • Evaluation encompassed both in-domain images and a public out-of-domain dataset to rigorously assess performance and generalization capabilities.
  • A key finding is that explicitly incorporating acquisition time knowledge using FiLM layers substantially improved breast tumor segmentation accuracy.
  • The time-modulated models also demonstrated enhanced generalization performance, indicating their robustness across different imaging environments and protocols.

Methodology

The methodology centers on a deep learning segmentation framework where image acquisition time is introduced as a modulating factor for model features. This is achieved using Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM) layers, which are lightweight conditional normalization layers that apply an affine transformation to features based on an external condition (in this case, acquisition time). Different backbone architectures were explored for the segmentation network, and both baseline and time-modulated models were trained. Training was conducted on a large, publicly available multisite breast DCE-MRI dataset, and evaluation involved both in-domain and out-of-domain public datasets to assess segmentation performance and model generalization.

Key Findings

The study's primary findings indicate that integrating acquisition time information into deep learning models via FiLM layers significantly enhances breast tumor segmentation performance. Furthermore, this time-informed approach substantially improves the model's generalization capabilities, allowing it to perform more effectively on images acquired under varying protocols and across different institutions (out-of-domain data).

Clinical Impact

This research holds significant clinical impact by enabling more accurate and reliable automated breast tumor segmentation from DCE-MRI, especially in diverse clinical settings. By improving model generalization, it paves the way for wider adoption of AI tools in breast cancer diagnosis and management, potentially leading to more consistent tumor assessment, streamlined workflows, better treatment planning, and improved patient outcomes by reducing inter-protocol variability challenges.

Abstract

Dynamic contrast-enhanced magnetic resonance imaging (DCE-MRI) plays an important role in breast cancer screening, tumor assessment, and treatment planning and monitoring. The dynamic changes in contrast in different tissues help to highlight the tumor in post-contrast images. However, varying acquisition protocols and individual factors result in large variation in the appearance of tissues, even for images acquired in the same phase (e.g., first post-contrast phase), making automated tumor segmentation challenging. Here, we propose a tumor segmentation method that leverages knowledge of the image acquisition time to modulate model features according to the specific acquisition sequence. We incorporate the acquisition times using feature-wise linear modulation (FiLM) layers, a lightweight method for incorporating temporal information that also allows for capitalizing on the full, variables number of images acquired per imaging study. We trained baseline and different configurations for the time-modulated models with varying backbone architectures on a large public multisite breast DCE-MRI dataset. Evaluation on in-domain images and a public out-of-domain dataset showed that incorporating knowledge of phase acquisition time improved tumor segmentation performance and model generalization.
