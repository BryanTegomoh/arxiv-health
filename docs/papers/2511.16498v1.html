Acquisition Time-Informed Breast Tumor Segmentation from Dynamic Contrast-Enhanced MRI

Summary

This paper proposes a novel breast tumor segmentation method for Dynamic Contrast-Enhanced MRI (DCE-MRI) that addresses the challenge of image variability by incorporating image acquisition time. By leveraging Feature-wise linear modulation (FiLM) layers, the model's features are dynamically adjusted based on the specific acquisition sequence, leading to improved tumor segmentation performance and enhanced model generalization across diverse datasets.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and reliable breast tumor segmentation from DCE-MRI is critical for early cancer detection, precise staging, and effective monitoring of treatment response. This research directly addresses a major hurdle in clinical deployment of AI tools by improving the robustness and generalizability of segmentation models across varied imaging protocols and patient populations.

AI Health Application

The paper details an AI-powered system designed for automated and more accurate segmentation of breast tumors from DCE-MRI scans. This application aims to enhance the efficiency and reliability of breast cancer screening, aid in precise tumor assessment, and support informed treatment planning and monitoring, thereby improving clinical workflows and patient outcomes in oncology.

Key Points

  • Automated breast tumor segmentation from DCE-MRI is challenged by significant variations in tissue appearance due to diverse acquisition protocols and individual patient factors.
  • The proposed method integrates knowledge of image acquisition time to modulate model features, allowing for context-aware segmentation.
  • Feature-wise linear modulation (FiLM) layers are employed as a lightweight mechanism to incorporate temporal information, enabling the model to handle a variable number of images per study.
  • The approach was implemented with various backbone architectures and trained on a large, public, multisite breast DCE-MRI dataset.
  • Evaluation demonstrated that incorporating phase acquisition time significantly improved tumor segmentation performance.
  • The time-modulated models exhibited enhanced generalization capabilities when tested on both in-domain images and a public out-of-domain dataset.
  • The method capitalizes on the full temporal sequence available in DCE-MRI, making it robust to variations in imaging protocols.

Methodology

The study proposes a deep learning approach for breast tumor segmentation. It incorporates image acquisition time using Feature-wise linear modulation (FiLM) layers within various backbone neural network architectures. FiLM layers modulate feature maps based on temporal information, allowing the model to adapt to specific acquisition sequences. Models were trained on a large, public, multisite breast DCE-MRI dataset and evaluated for performance and generalization on both in-domain and public out-of-domain datasets.

Key Findings

The primary finding is that explicitly incorporating phase acquisition time into deep learning models via FiLM layers significantly enhances breast tumor segmentation accuracy. This approach also demonstrably improves the generalization ability of these models, making them more robust to variations in DCE-MRI data from different acquisition protocols and sites.

Clinical Impact

This research could lead to the development of more robust and reliable automated breast tumor segmentation tools for clinical use. By improving generalization, these tools could be more readily deployed across various hospitals and scanner types without extensive re-training, aiding in more consistent breast cancer screening, diagnosis, and treatment monitoring, thereby potentially reducing radiologist workload and improving patient outcomes.

Abstract

Dynamic contrast-enhanced magnetic resonance imaging (DCE-MRI) plays an important role in breast cancer screening, tumor assessment, and treatment planning and monitoring. The dynamic changes in contrast in different tissues help to highlight the tumor in post-contrast images. However, varying acquisition protocols and individual factors result in large variation in the appearance of tissues, even for images acquired in the same phase (e.g., first post-contrast phase), making automated tumor segmentation challenging. Here, we propose a tumor segmentation method that leverages knowledge of the image acquisition time to modulate model features according to the specific acquisition sequence. We incorporate the acquisition times using feature-wise linear modulation (FiLM) layers, a lightweight method for incorporating temporal information that also allows for capitalizing on the full, variables number of images acquired per imaging study. We trained baseline and different configurations for the time-modulated models with varying backbone architectures on a large public multisite breast DCE-MRI dataset. Evaluation on in-domain images and a public out-of-domain dataset showed that incorporating knowledge of phase acquisition time improved tumor segmentation performance and model generalization.
