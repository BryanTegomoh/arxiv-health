Detection of Intoxicated Individuals from Facial Video Sequences via a Recurrent Fusion Model

arXiv ID: 2512.04536v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Bita Baroutian, Atefe Aghaei, Mohsen Ebrahimi Moghaddam
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This study introduces a novel video-based facial sequence analysis approach for detecting alcohol intoxication using a recurrent fusion model. The method integrates facial landmark analysis via a Graph Attention Network (GAT) with spatiotemporal visual features from a 3D ResNet, dynamically fusing them with adaptive prioritization. It achieves 95.82% accuracy, outperforming existing baselines and demonstrating strong potential for practical deployment in public safety.

Medical Relevance

Alcohol intoxication is a significant public health concern. This non-invasive, objective detection method could greatly aid in preventing alcohol-related accidents, injuries, and fatalities, contributing to safer communities and more effective public health interventions.

The AI model utilizes facial video sequence analysis (integrating Graph Attention Network and 3D ResNet with dynamic fusion) to non-invasively detect alcohol intoxication. This AI application functions as a diagnostic or assessment tool, enabling real-time monitoring relevant for public safety, accident prevention, and potentially informing interventions related to alcohol consumption and abuse.

Methodology

The study employs a recurrent fusion model that integrates two primary streams: a Graph Attention Network (GAT) for analyzing facial landmarks to capture subtle structural changes, and a 3D ResNet for extracting rich spatiotemporal visual features from the facial video sequences. These diverse features are then combined using a dynamic fusion approach with adaptive prioritization, allowing the model to weigh different feature types based on their relevance. The model was trained and evaluated on a custom-curated dataset comprising 3,542 video segments derived from 202 individuals.

Key Findings

The proposed recurrent fusion model achieved significant performance in detecting alcohol intoxication, reaching 95.82% accuracy, 0.977 precision, and 0.97 recall. These results demonstrate that the model substantially outperforms established baseline methods, specifically a custom 3D-CNN and a VGGFace+LSTM architecture, showcasing its effectiveness and robustness.

It could be deployed in public safety systems to prevent impaired driving, reduce workplace accidents, and facilitate prompt intervention in various settings without the need for physical contact or breathalyzer tests. This could lead to a reduction in alcohol-related morbidity and mortality, enhancing overall public health and safety.

Abstract

Alcohol consumption is a significant public health concern and a major cause of accidents and fatalities worldwide. This study introduces a novel video-based facial sequence analysis approach dedicated to the detection of alcohol intoxication. The method integrates facial landmark analysis via a Graph Attention Network (GAT) with spatiotemporal visual features extracted using a 3D ResNet. These features are dynamically fused with adaptive prioritization to enhance classification performance. Additionally, we introduce a curated dataset comprising 3,542 video segments derived from 202 individuals to support training and evaluation. Our model is compared against two baselines: a custom 3D-CNN and a VGGFace+LSTM architecture. Experimental results show that our approach achieves 95.82% accuracy, 0.977 precision, and 0.97 recall, outperforming prior methods. The findings demonstrate the model's potential for practical deployment in public safety systems for non-invasive, reliable alcohol intoxication detection.
