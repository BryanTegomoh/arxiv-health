Detection of Intoxicated Individuals from Facial Video Sequences via a Recurrent Fusion Model

arXiv ID: 2512.04536v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Bita Baroutian, Atefe Aghaei, Mohsen Ebrahimi Moghaddam
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This study presents a novel recurrent fusion model for detecting alcohol intoxication from facial video sequences. The model combines facial landmark analysis via a Graph Attention Network (GAT) with spatiotemporal visual features extracted by a 3D ResNet, employing dynamic adaptive prioritization for feature fusion. Achieving 95.82% accuracy, 0.977 precision, and 0.97 recall on a new curated dataset, the approach significantly outperforms baseline methods and shows promise for practical deployment in public safety.

Medical Relevance

Alcohol intoxication is a significant contributor to accidents, injuries, and fatalities worldwide, posing a substantial public health burden. This research provides a non-invasive, objective, and potentially real-time method for identifying intoxicated individuals, which could be critical for preventing harm and informing timely interventions.

Methodology

The study's methodology involves a recurrent fusion model that processes facial video sequences. It leverages a Graph Attention Network (GAT) for analyzing facial landmarks and a 3D ResNet for extracting spatiotemporal visual features. These two feature sets are then dynamically fused using an adaptive prioritization mechanism to optimize the classification of intoxicated versus non-intoxicated states. A proprietary dataset of 3,542 video segments from 202 individuals was compiled for model training and evaluation against custom 3D-CNN and VGGFace+LSTM baselines.

Key Findings

The proposed recurrent fusion model demonstrated superior performance in detecting alcohol intoxication, achieving an impressive 95.82% accuracy, 0.977 precision, and 0.97 recall. These metrics significantly surpassed those of the custom 3D-CNN and VGGFace+LSTM baseline architectures, highlighting the effectiveness of integrating GAT-based landmark analysis with 3D ResNet spatiotemporal features and dynamic fusion.

Abstract

Alcohol consumption is a significant public health concern and a major cause of accidents and fatalities worldwide. This study introduces a novel video-based facial sequence analysis approach dedicated to the detection of alcohol intoxication. The method integrates facial landmark analysis via a Graph Attention Network (GAT) with spatiotemporal visual features extracted using a 3D ResNet. These features are dynamically fused with adaptive prioritization to enhance classification performance. Additionally, we introduce a curated dataset comprising 3,542 video segments derived from 202 individuals to support training and evaluation. Our model is compared against two baselines: a custom 3D-CNN and a VGGFace+LSTM architecture. Experimental results show that our approach achieves 95.82% accuracy, 0.977 precision, and 0.97 recall, outperforming prior methods. The findings demonstrate the model's potential for practical deployment in public safety systems for non-invasive, reliable alcohol intoxication detection.
