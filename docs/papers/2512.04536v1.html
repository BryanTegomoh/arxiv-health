Detection of Intoxicated Individuals from Facial Video Sequences via a Recurrent Fusion Model

arXiv ID: 2512.04536v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Bita Baroutian, Atefe Aghaei, Mohsen Ebrahimi Moghaddam
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This study introduces a novel video-based recurrent fusion model for detecting alcohol intoxication from facial video sequences, integrating facial landmark analysis via a Graph Attention Network (GAT) with spatiotemporal visual features from a 3D ResNet. The model dynamically fuses these features with adaptive prioritization, achieving 95.82% accuracy, 0.977 precision, and 0.97 recall. It outperforms prior methods, demonstrating significant potential for non-invasive public safety applications.

Medical Relevance

Alcohol consumption is a major public health issue leading to accidents and fatalities. This research offers a non-invasive, objective tool for detecting intoxication, which can be crucial for prevention strategies, public safety, and potentially informing healthcare interventions.

AI Health Application

This research develops an AI model (recurrent fusion model integrating Graph Attention Networks and 3D ResNet) for the automated, non-invasive detection of alcohol intoxication from facial video sequences. This AI application could be used in healthcare settings for rapid screening, triage in emergency departments, monitoring in substance abuse treatment programs, or as a component in public health systems aimed at preventing alcohol-related accidents and fatalities.

Methodology

The study employs a video-based facial sequence analysis approach. It extracts facial landmark features using a Graph Attention Network (GAT) and spatiotemporal visual features from the video using a 3D ResNet. These two distinct feature sets are then combined through a dynamic fusion mechanism with adaptive prioritization within a recurrent model architecture. A newly curated dataset comprising 3,542 video segments from 202 individuals was used for training and evaluation. Performance was compared against a custom 3D-CNN and a VGGFace+LSTM baseline.

Key Findings

The proposed recurrent fusion model achieved a high accuracy of 95.82% for detecting alcohol intoxication. It demonstrated strong classification performance with 0.977 precision and 0.97 recall. These results significantly surpassed the performance of the established baseline models, namely a custom 3D-CNN and a VGGFace+LSTM architecture.

Abstract

Alcohol consumption is a significant public health concern and a major cause of accidents and fatalities worldwide. This study introduces a novel video-based facial sequence analysis approach dedicated to the detection of alcohol intoxication. The method integrates facial landmark analysis via a Graph Attention Network (GAT) with spatiotemporal visual features extracted using a 3D ResNet. These features are dynamically fused with adaptive prioritization to enhance classification performance. Additionally, we introduce a curated dataset comprising 3,542 video segments derived from 202 individuals to support training and evaluation. Our model is compared against two baselines: a custom 3D-CNN and a VGGFace+LSTM architecture. Experimental results show that our approach achieves 95.82% accuracy, 0.977 precision, and 0.97 recall, outperforming prior methods. The findings demonstrate the model's potential for practical deployment in public safety systems for non-invasive, reliable alcohol intoxication detection.
