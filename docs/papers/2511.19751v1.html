Leveraging Foundation Models for Histological Grading in Cutaneous Squamous Cell Carcinoma using PathFMTools

Authors: Abdul Rahman Diab, Emily E. Karn, Renchin Wu, Emily S. Ruiz, William Lotter

Summary

This paper introduces PathFMTools, a lightweight Python package designed to streamline the execution, analysis, and visualization of pathology foundation models (FMs). The authors demonstrate its utility by benchmarking two state-of-the-art vision-language FMs, CONCH and MUSK, for histological grading in cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (cSCC), revealing effective adaptation strategies and the potential of FM embeddings for specialized downstream models.

Medical Relevance

Accurate histological grading of cSCC is paramount for appropriate disease staging and guiding patient management decisions. This research provides a computational tool and framework to enhance the efficiency and reliability of this process, potentially leading to more precise diagnoses and optimized patient care.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using vision-language foundation models and developing a specialized toolkit (PathFMTools) to perform automated or AI-assisted histological grading of cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma from whole-slide pathology images. This aims to improve the accuracy and efficiency of cancer diagnosis, staging, and ultimately inform patient management decisions in a clinical setting.

  • Introduces PathFMTools, a lightweight, extensible Python package that facilitates efficient execution, analysis, and visualization of pathology foundation models.
  • Evaluated two state-of-the-art vision-language foundation models, CONCH and MUSK, on the critical task of histological grading in cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (cSCC).
  • Utilized a cohort of 440 cSCC H&E whole-slide images to benchmark multiple adaptation strategies for the foundation models.

Methodology

The study involved developing PathFMTools, a Python package, which was then used to interface with and evaluate two state-of-the-art vision-language foundation models, CONCH and MUSK. These models were applied to the task of histological grading in cSCC using a dataset of 440 H&E WSIs. The methodology included benchmarking various adaptation strategies to assess model performance and trade-offs, particularly focusing on the utility of foundation model embeddings for training specialist models.

Key Findings

The research revealed distinct trade-offs across different prediction approaches for integrating foundation models into clinical tasks. A primary finding was the successful validation that embeddings derived from these pathology foundation models can serve as a robust basis for training smaller, specialized models, offering an efficient strategy for practical clinical deployment.

PathFMTools can empower pathologists and researchers to more efficiently develop, validate, and deploy advanced AI-driven diagnostic tools, thereby enhancing clinical decision-making and patient outcomes.

Abstract

Despite the promise of computational pathology foundation models, adapting them to specific clinical tasks remains challenging due to the complexity of whole-slide image (WSI) processing, the opacity of learned features, and the wide range of potential adaptation strategies. To address these challenges, we introduce PathFMTools, a lightweight, extensible Python package that enables efficient execution, analysis, and visualization of pathology foundation models. We use this tool to interface with and evaluate two state-of-the-art vision-language foundation models, CONCH and MUSK, on the task of histological grading in cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (cSCC), a critical criterion that informs cSCC staging and patient management. Using a cohort of 440 cSCC H&E WSIs, we benchmark multiple adaptation strategies, demonstrating trade-offs across prediction approaches and validating the potential of using foundation model embeddings to train small specialist models. These findings underscore the promise of pathology foundation models for real-world clinical applications, with PathFMTools enabling efficient analysis and validation.
