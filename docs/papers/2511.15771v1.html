UniUltra: Interactive Parameter-Efficient SAM2 for Universal Ultrasound Segmentation

Summary

This paper introduces UniUltra, a framework addressing the performance degradation of SAM2 on ultrasound images and its deployment challenges in resource-constrained clinical settings. UniUltra leverages a novel context-edge hybrid adapter for parameter-efficient fine-tuning and a deep-supervised knowledge distillation technique to create a lightweight, high-performing model for universal ultrasound segmentation. The final model significantly reduces parameter count while maintaining superior generalization capabilities, making it suitable for clinical deployment.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated ultrasound segmentation is crucial for various medical tasks, from diagnosis and prognosis to surgical planning and monitoring. This research makes advanced, universal segmentation models accessible and deployable in resource-constrained clinical environments, potentially improving diagnostic efficiency, reducing physician workload, and enhancing patient care across diverse healthcare settings.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of a highly efficient and accurate universal segmentation model for ultrasound images. This model, UniUltra, leverages AI to automate or assist in the segmentation of anatomical structures or pathologies within ultrasound scans, which can aid clinicians in diagnosis, measurement, treatment planning, and monitoring. Its focus on parameter efficiency and suitability for 'clinical deployment' highlights its aim to integrate AI into routine medical practice, especially in settings with limited computational resources.

Key Points

  • Identifies and addresses SAM2's significant performance degradation on ultrasound images due to domain disparities.
  • Proposes UniUltra, a framework for universal ultrasound segmentation, designed for both performance and computational efficiency.
  • Introduces a novel Context-Edge Hybrid Adapter (CH-Adapter) for parameter-efficient fine-tuning, enhancing fine-grained perception across diverse ultrasound modalities.
  • Develops a Deep-Supervised Knowledge Distillation (DSKD) technique to transfer knowledge from the large, fine-tuned SAM2 encoder to a super lightweight encoder, substantially reducing computational requirements.
  • Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance with superior generalization capabilities compared to existing methods.
  • Achieves remarkable parameter efficiency: fine-tuning uses only 8.91% of SAM2's parameters, and the final compressed model reduces parameters by 94.08% compared to the original SAM2.
  • The resulting lightweight and accurate model is highly suitable for practical clinical deployment, particularly in resource-constrained environments.

Methodology

The UniUltra framework employs a two-stage approach. First, it introduces a Context-Edge Hybrid Adapter (CH-Adapter) for parameter-efficient fine-tuning of the Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM2), specifically designed to enhance fine-grained perception on diverse ultrasound imaging modalities. Second, a Deep-Supervised Knowledge Distillation (DSKD) technique is implemented to transfer the acquired knowledge from the large image encoder of the fine-tuned SAM2 (teacher model) to a significantly smaller, super lightweight encoder (student model), thereby minimizing computational overhead while preserving performance.

Key Findings

UniUltra significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods in universal ultrasound segmentation, exhibiting superior generalization. The framework achieves competitive segmentation performance while leveraging only 8.91% of SAM2's parameters during the fine-tuning phase. Critically, the final compressed model substantially reduces the parameter count by 94.08% compared to the original SAM2, making it exceptionally efficient for real-world application.

Clinical Impact

This work directly enables the practical deployment of highly accurate, AI-powered ultrasound segmentation tools in clinical settings, including those with limited computational resources. The significant reduction in model size and computational demands facilitates integration into portable ultrasound devices, remote clinics, and general hospital systems, improving accessibility to advanced diagnostics, streamlining clinical workflows, and potentially leading to faster and more accurate diagnoses for a broader patient population globally.

Abstract

The Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM2) demonstrates remarkable universal segmentation capabilities on natural images. However, its performance on ultrasound images is significantly degraded due to domain disparities. This limitation raises two critical challenges: how to efficiently adapt SAM2 to ultrasound imaging while maintaining parameter efficiency, and how to deploy the adapted model effectively in resource-constrained clinical environments. To address these issues, we propose UniUltra for universal ultrasound segmentation. Specifically, we first introduce a novel context-edge hybrid adapter (CH-Adapter) that enhances fine-grained perception across diverse ultrasound imaging modalities while achieving parameter-efficient fine-tuning. To further improve clinical applicability, we develop a deep-supervised knowledge distillation (DSKD) technique that transfers knowledge from the large image encoder of the fine-tuned SAM2 to a super lightweight encoder, substantially reducing computational requirements without compromising performance. Extensive experiments demonstrate that UniUltra outperforms state-of-the-arts with superior generalization capabilities. Notably, our framework achieves competitive performance using only 8.91% of SAM2's parameters during fine-tuning, and the final compressed model reduces the parameter count by 94.08% compared to the original SAM2, making it highly suitable for practical clinical deployment. The source code is available at https://github.com/xq141839/UniUltra.
