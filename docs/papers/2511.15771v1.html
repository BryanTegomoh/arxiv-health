UniUltra: Interactive Parameter-Efficient SAM2 for Universal Ultrasound Segmentation

Summary

UniUltra addresses the degradation of SAM2's performance on ultrasound images and the challenges of deploying large models in clinical settings. It introduces a parameter-efficient Context-Edge Hybrid Adapter (CH-Adapter) for domain adaptation and a Deep-Supervised Knowledge Distillation (DSKD) technique for model compression, resulting in a lightweight, high-performing, and universal ultrasound segmentation model.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for advancing diagnostic capabilities in medical imaging, particularly for ultrasound, by enabling accurate and automated segmentation. The focus on parameter efficiency and deployability addresses real-world clinical needs, allowing advanced AI tools to be used in resource-constrained healthcare settings.

AI Health Application

This paper develops an AI model (UniUltra) designed to perform accurate and efficient segmentation of anatomical structures or pathologies within medical ultrasound images. This application can significantly aid healthcare professionals by automating or semi-automating the analysis of ultrasound scans, leading to faster diagnoses, more precise measurements, and improved workflows in clinical settings, especially where computational resources are limited.

Key Points

  • SAM2 demonstrates significant performance degradation on ultrasound images due to domain disparities, posing challenges for clinical applicability.
  • UniUltra proposes a novel Context-Edge Hybrid Adapter (CH-Adapter) to enhance fine-grained perception across diverse ultrasound modalities while ensuring parameter-efficient fine-tuning.
  • A Deep-Supervised Knowledge Distillation (DSKD) technique is developed to transfer knowledge from the large, fine-tuned SAM2 encoder to a super lightweight encoder, substantially reducing computational requirements.
  • Extensive experiments show that UniUltra outperforms state-of-the-art methods with superior generalization capabilities for universal ultrasound segmentation.
  • During fine-tuning, UniUltra utilizes only 8.91% of SAM2's original parameters, highlighting its parameter efficiency.
  • The final compressed model achieves a remarkable 94.08% reduction in parameter count compared to the original SAM2 without compromising performance.
  • The framework is specifically designed to be highly suitable for practical deployment in resource-constrained clinical environments due to its efficiency and performance.

Methodology

UniUltra employs a two-pronged approach: first, a Context-Edge Hybrid Adapter (CH-Adapter) is introduced for parameter-efficient fine-tuning of SAM2, optimizing its perception for ultrasound features. Second, a Deep-Supervised Knowledge Distillation (DSKD) technique is applied, transferring knowledge from the fine-tuned, larger SAM2 encoder (teacher) to a much smaller, super lightweight encoder (student) to drastically reduce the model's computational footprint while preserving performance.

Key Findings

UniUltra achieves superior performance and generalization over state-of-the-art methods in universal ultrasound segmentation. It demonstrates high parameter efficiency during fine-tuning, utilizing only 8.91% of SAM2's parameters. The final compressed model significantly reduces the parameter count by 94.08% compared to the original SAM2, making it exceptionally lightweight and suitable for clinical deployment without compromising segmentation accuracy.

Clinical Impact

The ability of UniUltra to provide accurate, universal ultrasound segmentation with significantly reduced computational requirements will enable the practical deployment of advanced AI diagnostics in diverse clinical settings, including those with limited resources. This can lead to more consistent, faster, and precise diagnoses, improved treatment planning, and enhanced patient care across various medical conditions reliant on ultrasound imaging.

Abstract

The Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM2) demonstrates remarkable universal segmentation capabilities on natural images. However, its performance on ultrasound images is significantly degraded due to domain disparities. This limitation raises two critical challenges: how to efficiently adapt SAM2 to ultrasound imaging while maintaining parameter efficiency, and how to deploy the adapted model effectively in resource-constrained clinical environments. To address these issues, we propose UniUltra for universal ultrasound segmentation. Specifically, we first introduce a novel context-edge hybrid adapter (CH-Adapter) that enhances fine-grained perception across diverse ultrasound imaging modalities while achieving parameter-efficient fine-tuning. To further improve clinical applicability, we develop a deep-supervised knowledge distillation (DSKD) technique that transfers knowledge from the large image encoder of the fine-tuned SAM2 to a super lightweight encoder, substantially reducing computational requirements without compromising performance. Extensive experiments demonstrate that UniUltra outperforms state-of-the-arts with superior generalization capabilities. Notably, our framework achieves competitive performance using only 8.91% of SAM2's parameters during fine-tuning, and the final compressed model reduces the parameter count by 94.08% compared to the original SAM2, making it highly suitable for practical clinical deployment. The source code is available at https://github.com/xq141839/UniUltra.
