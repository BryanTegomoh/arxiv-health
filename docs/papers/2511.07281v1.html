Segmentation of Ischemic Stroke Lesions using Transfer Learning on Multi-sequence MRI

arXiv ID: 2511.07281v1
Published: 2025-11-10
Authors: R. P. Chowdhury, T. Rahman
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00

Summary

This paper presents a novel deep learning framework using a Res-Unet architecture to quickly and automatically segment ischemic stroke lesions on multi-sequence MRI. By leveraging transfer learning and integrating a Majority Voting Classifier, the method achieved a Dice score of 80.5% and an accuracy of 74.03% on the ISLES 2015 dataset, demonstrating effective automation for crucial diagnostic tasks.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and consistent segmentation of ischemic stroke lesions is paramount for precise diagnosis, guiding patient-specific therapy selection, predicting prognosis, and monitoring treatment efficacy, directly impacting patient care and outcomes.

AI Health Application

This research develops an AI application for automating the segmentation of ischemic stroke lesions from multi-sequence MRI scans. This application aims to assist clinicians in the diagnosis, treatment planning, and prognosis of stroke patients by providing a faster, more consistent, and potentially more accurate method for identifying and quantifying stroke lesions compared to manual segmentation.

Key Points

  • Addresses the challenge of manual, time-consuming, and inconsistent ischemic stroke lesion segmentation performed by experts.
  • Proposes a novel deep learning framework for automatic segmentation using the Res-Unet architecture.
  • Utilizes multi-sequence MRI data (T1-weighted, T2-weighted, DWI, FLAIR) to capture comprehensive lesion characteristics.
  • Explores the benefits of transfer learning by training the Res-Unet with and without pre-existing weights.
  • Validates the methodology on the publicly available ISLES 2015 Brain Stroke sequence dataset.
  • Integrates a Majority Voting Classifier to amalgamate segmentation outcomes from each axis, enhancing the robustness of 3D segmentation.
  • Achieves a high segmentation performance with a Dice score of 80.5% and an accuracy of 74.03%.

Methodology

The study employs a deep learning approach based on the Res-Unet architecture for segmenting ischemic stroke lesions. The model was trained on multi-sequence MRI data (T1, T2, DWI, FLAIR) from the ISLES 2015 Brain Stroke sequence dataset. To assess the impact of transfer learning, the model was trained twice: once with pre-existing weights and once without. A Majority Voting Classifier was finally integrated to combine segmentation results from different anatomical axes, yielding a comprehensive 3D segmentation. Performance was evaluated using the Dice score and sensitivity.

Key Findings

The developed segmentation framework achieved a Dice score of 80.5% and an accuracy of 74.03% for ischemic stroke lesion segmentation on the ISLES 2015 dataset, demonstrating strong efficacy for automatic detection and delineation.

Clinical Impact

This automated segmentation method can significantly reduce the manual workload, time, and inter-observer variability currently associated with expert-driven lesion delineation. This translates to faster diagnosis, more consistent assessment of stroke severity and location, improved precision in treatment planning (e.g., thrombolysis, thrombectomy), and more reliable prognostic assessments for stroke patients.

Abstract

The accurate understanding of ischemic stroke lesions is critical for efficient therapy and prognosis of stroke patients. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is sensitive to acute ischemic stroke and is a common diagnostic method for stroke. However, manual lesion segmentation performed by experts is tedious, time-consuming, and prone to observer inconsistency. Automatic medical image analysis methods have been proposed to overcome this challenge. However, previous approaches have relied on hand-crafted features that may not capture the irregular and physiologically complex shapes of ischemic stroke lesions. In this study, we present a novel framework for quickly and automatically segmenting ischemic stroke lesions on various MRI sequences, including T1-weighted, T2-weighted, DWI, and FLAIR. The proposed methodology is validated on the ISLES 2015 Brain Stroke sequence dataset, where we trained our model using the Res-Unet architecture twice: first, with pre-existing weights, and then without, to explore the benefits of transfer learning. Evaluation metrics, including the Dice score and sensitivity, were computed across 3D volumes. Finally, a Majority Voting Classifier was integrated to amalgamate the outcomes from each axis, resulting in a comprehensive segmentation method. Our efforts culminated in achieving a Dice score of 80.5\% and an accuracy of 74.03\%, showcasing the efficacy of our segmentation approach.
