Privacy-Aware Continual Self-Supervised Learning on Multi-Window Chest Computed Tomography for Domain-Shift Robustness

Summary

This paper introduces a novel privacy-aware continual self-supervised learning (CSSL) framework designed to build robust and generalizable AI models for multi-window chest computed tomography (CT). It addresses challenges like data scarcity, domain shifts (particularly due to varying CT window settings), and privacy concerns by integrating a latent replay-based mechanism to mitigate catastrophic forgetting and a feature distillation technique for learning domain-shift-robust representations. The framework demonstrates superior performance in learning diverse features across different CT window settings.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant as it enables the development of more robust, generalizable, and privacy-preserving AI models for medical image diagnosis, particularly in critical areas like chest CT where varying acquisition parameters (e.g., window settings) lead to significant domain shifts and hinder model reliability.

AI Health Application

This paper aims to develop more robust, generalizable, and privacy-aware AI models for assisting in the diagnosis of conditions visible on chest CT scans. By mitigating issues like domain shift (e.g., varying CT window settings) and catastrophic forgetting, these models can become more reliable and adaptable for use across different clinical settings, ultimately enhancing diagnostic accuracy and efficiency while respecting patient data privacy.

Key Points

  • Proposes a novel privacy-aware Continual Self-Supervised Learning (CSSL) framework for multi-window chest CT images.
  • Aims to overcome domain shifts arising from differences in CT window settings, data scarcity, and privacy constraints in medical imaging.
  • Incorporates a latent replay-based mechanism into CSSL to mitigate catastrophic forgetting during continual pretraining, ensuring data privacy by not directly reusing past data.
  • Introduces a feature distillation technique combining Wasserstein distance-based Knowledge Distillation (WKD) and Batch-Knowledge Ensemble (BKE) to enhance robust representation learning.
  • Leverages continual pretraining on unlabeled images to capture relationships between past and new knowledge across training stages.
  • Validated using chest CT images obtained across two different window settings, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing approaches.

Methodology

The proposed approach is a Continual Self-Supervised Learning (CSSL) framework that performs continual pretraining on unlabeled multi-window chest CT images. It integrates a latent replay-based mechanism to prevent catastrophic forgetting due to domain shifts while preserving data privacy. Additionally, a sophisticated feature distillation technique is employed, combining Wasserstein distance-based Knowledge Distillation (WKD) with a Batch-Knowledge Ensemble (BKE) strategy to learn robust and diverse representations. The method was validated on chest CT images across two distinct window settings.

Key Findings

The study found that the proposed privacy-aware CSSL framework achieves superior performance compared to other approaches in learning diverse and domain-shift-robust features from multi-window chest CT images. It effectively mitigates catastrophic forgetting in continual learning settings, allowing for robust model generalization across different CT window settings while adhering to privacy principles.

By developing models robust to variations in CT window settings and respecting patient data privacy, it facilitates more consistent and accurate diagnoses in real-world scenarios, potentially reducing misdiagnosis risks and improving patient outcomes in chest CT interpretations. It also paves the way for AI models that can continually learn and adapt to new data without compromising past knowledge or privacy.

Abstract

We propose a novel continual self-supervised learning (CSSL) framework for simultaneously learning diverse features from multi-window-obtained chest computed tomography (CT) images and ensuring data privacy. Achieving a robust and highly generalizable model in medical image diagnosis is challenging, mainly because of issues, such as the scarcity of large-scale, accurately annotated datasets and domain shifts inherent to dynamic healthcare environments. Specifically, in chest CT, these domain shifts often arise from differences in window settings, which are optimized for distinct clinical purposes. Previous CSSL frameworks often mitigated domain shift by reusing past data, a typically impractical approach owing to privacy constraints. Our approach addresses these challenges by effectively capturing the relationship between previously learned knowledge and new information across different training stages through continual pretraining on unlabeled images. Specifically, by incorporating a latent replay-based mechanism into CSSL, our method mitigates catastrophic forgetting due to domain shifts during continual pretraining while ensuring data privacy. Additionally, we introduce a feature distillation technique that integrates Wasserstein distance-based knowledge distillation (WKD) and batch-knowledge ensemble (BKE), enhancing the ability of the model to learn meaningful, domain-shift-robust representations. Finally, we validate our approach using chest CT images obtained across two different window settings, demonstrating superior performance compared with other approaches.
