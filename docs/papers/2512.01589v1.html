Toward Content-based Indexing and Retrieval of Head and Neck CT with Abscess Segmentation

Summary

This study introduces AbscessHeNe, a new publicly available dataset of 4,926 contrast-enhanced CT slices with comprehensively annotated head and neck abscesses, designed to advance semantic segmentation and content-based image retrieval in this critical medical domain. Baseline evaluations of state-of-the-art deep learning models (CNN, Transformer, Mamba-based) reveal significant challenges in accurately segmenting these lesions, with the best model achieving a Dice Similarity Coefficient of only 0.39.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and prompt detection and delineation of head and neck abscesses on CT imaging are crucial for preventing severe complications like sepsis or mortality, guiding treatment planning, and facilitating surgical intervention. This research directly addresses this need by providing a dedicated resource for developing advanced AI tools.

AI Health Application

The paper applies AI through semantic segmentation models to automatically and accurately delineate abscess boundaries on head and neck CT scans. This AI application aims to assist clinicians in the diagnosis, assessment of deep neck space involvement, and planning of treatment or surgical interventions for acute infections. Additionally, the dataset is structured for future AI applications in content-based medical image indexing and case-based retrieval systems, enhancing knowledge management and clinical workflow efficiency.

Key Points

  • Head and neck abscesses are acute, potentially life-threatening infections requiring prompt and accurate imaging diagnosis.
  • The AbscessHeNe dataset comprises 4,926 contrast-enhanced CT slices of clinically confirmed head and neck abscesses, featuring pixel-level annotations and linked clinical metadata.
  • The dataset is curated for developing robust semantic segmentation models to delineate abscess boundaries and assess deep neck space involvement.
  • Several state-of-the-art segmentation architectures (CNN, Transformer, Mamba-based) were evaluated to establish performance baselines.
  • The highest-performing model achieved a Dice Similarity Coefficient of 0.39, Intersection-over-Union of 0.27, and Normalized Surface Distance of 0.67, highlighting the complexity and difficulty of the segmentation task.
  • AbscessHeNe is also structured to support future applications in content-based multimedia indexing and case-based retrieval, fostering intelligent clinical workflows.
  • The AbscessHeNe dataset is made publicly available to encourage further research and development.

Methodology

The study involved creating AbscessHeNe, a curated dataset of 4,926 contrast-enhanced CT slices with pixel-level annotations of head and neck abscesses and associated clinical metadata. To establish performance baselines, several state-of-the-art deep learning segmentation architectures, including models based on Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Transformers, and Mamba, were evaluated on the dataset.

Key Findings

The evaluation demonstrated that even the highest-performing state-of-the-art segmentation model achieved relatively low performance metrics: a Dice Similarity Coefficient of 0.39, an Intersection-over-Union of 0.27, and a Normalized Surface Distance of 0.67. These results indicate that accurate segmentation of head and neck abscesses on CT scans remains a significant challenge for current deep learning models.

Clinical Impact

This work has the potential to significantly impact clinical practice by providing a foundational dataset for developing AI models that can precisely detect and delineate head and neck abscesses. Such models could aid in earlier diagnosis, more accurate assessment of deep neck space involvement, optimized treatment planning, and improved surgical outcomes, ultimately reducing morbidity and mortality rates. Furthermore, the dataset's structure supports the development of intelligent retrieval systems for knowledge-driven clinical decision-making.

Limitations

The current performance of state-of-the-art segmentation models (Dice 0.39) on the AbscessHeNe dataset indicates significant challenges in accurately segmenting head and neck abscesses, suggesting that existing methodologies may not yet be robust enough for clinical deployment without further advancements.

Future Directions

Future research should focus on developing more advanced and robust semantic segmentation models to improve the accuracy of abscess delineation. Additionally, the AbscessHeNe dataset is designed to facilitate the development of content-based multimedia indexing and case-based retrieval systems, leveraging its pixel-level annotations and clinical metadata to build intelligent retrieval tools and support knowledge-driven clinical workflows.

Abstract

Abscesses in the head and neck represent an acute infectious process that can potentially lead to sepsis or mortality if not diagnosed and managed promptly. Accurate detection and delineation of these lesions on imaging are essential for diagnosis, treatment planning, and surgical intervention. In this study, we introduce AbscessHeNe, a curated and comprehensively annotated dataset comprising 4,926 contrast-enhanced CT slices with clinically confirmed head and neck abscesses. The dataset is designed to facilitate the development of robust semantic segmentation models that can accurately delineate abscess boundaries and evaluate deep neck space involvement, thereby supporting informed clinical decision-making. To establish performance baselines, we evaluate several state-of-the-art segmentation architectures, including CNN, Transformer, and Mamba-based models. The highest-performing model achieved a Dice Similarity Coefficient of 0.39, Intersection-over-Union of 0.27, and Normalized Surface Distance of 0.67, indicating the challenges of this task and the need for further research. Beyond segmentation, AbscessHeNe is structured for future applications in content-based multimedia indexing and case-based retrieval. Each CT scan is linked with pixel-level annotations and clinical metadata, providing a foundation for building intelligent retrieval systems and supporting knowledge-driven clinical workflows. The dataset will be made publicly available at https://github.com/drthaodao3101/AbscessHeNe.git.
