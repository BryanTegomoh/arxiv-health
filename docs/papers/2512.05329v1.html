CATNUS: Coordinate-Aware Thalamic Nuclei Segmentation Using T1-Weighted MRI

Authors: Anqi Feng, Zhangxing Bian, Samuel W. Remedios, Savannah P. Hays, Blake E. Dewey, Alexa Colinco, Jiachen Zhuo, Dan Benjamini, Jerry L. Prince

Summary

CATNUS addresses the challenge of accurate thalamic nuclei segmentation from T1-weighted MRI using a 3D U-Net enhanced with coordinate convolution layers. It demonstrates improved segmentation accuracy, robust test-retest reliability, and strong out-of-distribution generalization across diverse MRI acquisitions, facilitating large-scale neuroimaging studies and clinical assessments.

Medical Relevance

Accurate segmentation of thalamic nuclei is critical because these structures have distinct roles in overall brain function and are differentially implicated in numerous neurological and psychiatric disorders. This method provides a more precise tool for understanding disease pathophysiology, diagnosis, and monitoring treatment effects.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the automated, accurate segmentation of thalamic nuclei from MRI scans using a deep learning model (3D U-Net). This AI tool can assist clinicians and researchers in precisely identifying and quantifying these brain structures, which is crucial for studying neurological and psychiatric disorders, potentially aiding in diagnosis, prognosis, treatment planning (e.g., deep brain stimulation targeting), and large-scale neuroimaging studies.

Key Points

  • Introduces CATNUS (Coordinate-Aware Thalamic Nuclei Segmentation), a 3D U-Net architecture enhanced with coordinate convolution layers for precise segmentation of 13 thalamic nuclei or nuclear groups.
  • Designed for broad clinical applicability, supporting input from quantitative T1 maps as well as widely used MPRAGE and FGATIR T1-weighted MRI sequences.
  • Benchmarked against established methods (FreeSurfer, THOMAS, HIPS-THOMAS), demonstrating improved segmentation accuracy and robust test-retest reliability across multiple nuclei.
  • Showcased strong out-of-distribution generalization on traveling-subject datasets, spanning multiple scanners, field strengths, and vendors.
  • Produces reliable and anatomically coherent segmentations even under diverse acquisition conditions, which is crucial for multi-site studies.
  • Provides an accurate and generalizable solution with strong potential to facilitate large-scale neuroimaging studies, enabling deeper insights into brain function and disease.
  • Offers a valuable tool to support real-world clinical assessment due to its robustness and compatibility with common clinical MRI protocols.

Methodology

CATNUS utilizes a 3D U-Net deep learning architecture, specifically augmented with coordinate convolution layers to provide more precise localization during segmentation. The model was developed to be compatible with various T1-weighted MRI sequences, including quantitative T1 maps, MPRAGE, and FGATIR. Its performance was rigorously benchmarked against established methods like FreeSurfer, THOMAS, and HIPS-THOMAS, assessing segmentation accuracy and test-retest reliability. Out-of-distribution generalization was evaluated using traveling-subject datasets acquired from multiple scanners, field strengths, and vendors.

Key Findings

CATNUS achieved improved segmentation accuracy and robust test-retest reliability for 13 thalamic nuclei compared to established methods. Critically, it demonstrated strong out-of-distribution generalization on diverse traveling-subject datasets, consistently producing reliable and anatomically coherent segmentations across various acquisition conditions, scanner types, and field strengths.

Clinical Impact

This accurate, robust, and generalizable thalamic nuclei segmentation solution has the potential to significantly facilitate large-scale neuroimaging studies, enabling more powerful research into brain disorders. Furthermore, its compatibility with standard clinical MRI sequences makes it a strong candidate for supporting real-world clinical assessment, aiding in the diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment planning for neurological and psychiatric conditions.

Abstract

Accurate segmentation of thalamic nuclei from magnetic resonance images is important due to the distinct roles of these nuclei in overall brain function and to their differential involvement in neurological and psychiatric disorders. However, segmentation remains challenging given the small size of many nuclei, limited intrathalamic contrast and image resolution, and inter-subject anatomical variability. In this work, we present CATNUS (Coordinate-Aware Thalamic Nuclei Segmentation), segmenting 13 thalamic nuclei (or nuclear groups) using a 3D U-Net architecture enhanced with coordinate convolution layers, which provide more precise localization of both large and small nuclei. To support broad clinical applicability, we provide pre-trained model variants that can operate on quantitative T1 maps as well as on widely used magnetization-prepared rapid gradient echo (MPRAGE) and fast gray matter acquisition T1 inversion recovery (FGATIR) sequences. We benchmarked CATNUS against established methods, including FreeSurfer, THOMAS and HIPS-THOMAS, demonstrating improved segmentation accuracy and robust test-retest reliability across multiple nuclei. Furthermore, CATNUS demonstrated strong out-of-distribution generalization on traveling-subject datasets spanning multiple scanners, field strengths, and vendors, producing reliable and anatomically coherent segmentations across diverse acquisition conditions. Overall, CATNUS provides an accurate and generalizable solution for thalamic nuclei segmentation, with strong potential to facilitate large-scale neuroimaging studies and support real-world clinical assessment.
