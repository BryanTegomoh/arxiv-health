Balanced Few-Shot Episodic Learning for Accurate Retinal Disease Diagnosis

arXiv ID: 2512.04967v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Jasmaine Khale, Ravi Prakash Srivastava
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a balanced few-shot episodic learning framework designed for accurate retinal disease diagnosis, specifically addressing challenges posed by limited and imbalanced annotated datasets. By integrating balanced episodic sampling, targeted augmentation (including CLAHE), and a ResNet-50 encoder, the method significantly improves diagnostic accuracy and reduces bias towards majority classes, particularly benefiting underrepresented retinal conditions.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated retinal disease diagnosis is crucial for timely intervention, preventing vision loss from prevalent conditions like diabetic retinopathy and macular degeneration. This research provides a pathway to reliable AI-driven diagnosis even when large, perfectly balanced datasets are unavailable, a common scenario in clinical medicine.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the automated diagnosis of various retinal diseases (e.g., diabetic retinopathy, macular degeneration, optic disc edema) from retinal fundus images. It specifically leverages few-shot learning to overcome limitations of large datasets, aiming for robust and clinically fair diagnostic capabilities, particularly for underrepresented diseases, thereby assisting ophthalmologists and improving healthcare access/efficiency.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical need for automated retinal disease diagnosis despite the scarcity and imbalance of annotated medical image datasets.
  • Proposes a novel balanced few-shot episodic learning framework tailored for the Retinal Fundus Multi-Disease Image Dataset (RFMiD).
  • Incorporates three key methodological components: (i) balanced episodic sampling ensuring equal class participation (5-way 5-shot), (ii) targeted augmentation (CLAHE, color/geometry transformations) to enhance minority class diversity, and (iii) a pre-trained ResNet-50 encoder for feature extraction.
  • Utilizes prototype computation in the embedding space and cosine similarity for robust classification.
  • Evaluated on the RFMiD's ten most represented but still imbalanced classes (e.g., Diabetic Retinopathy, Macular Hole vs. Optic Disc Edema, Branch Retinal Vein Occlusion).
  • Achieves substantial accuracy gains and effectively reduces diagnostic bias towards majority classes, showing notable improvements for underrepresented diseases.
  • Demonstrates that dataset-aware few-shot pipelines, combined with balanced sampling and CLAHE-enhanced preprocessing, can deliver more robust and clinically fair diagnosis under data-constrained conditions.

Methodology

The proposed framework is a balanced few-shot episodic learning approach. It integrates (i) balanced episodic sampling to ensure that all classes participate equally in each 5-way 5-shot episode, preventing bias from class imbalance during training. (ii) Targeted augmentation techniques, including Contrast Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization (CLAHE) and various color/geometry transformations, are applied specifically to improve the diversity and representation of minority classes. (iii) A ResNet-50 encoder, pre-trained on ImageNet, is used to extract fine-grained features from retinal images. Prototypes for each class are computed in the embedding space generated by the encoder, and classification is performed using cosine similarity for enhanced stability. The model was trained on 100 episodes and evaluated on 1,000 test episodes using the RFMiD dataset.

Key Findings

The framework achieved substantial accuracy gains compared to conventional methods, effectively reducing bias toward majority classes. Crucially, it demonstrated notable improvements in diagnostic performance for underrepresented diseases such as Optic Disc Edema and Branch Retinal Vein Occlusion. These results confirm that dataset-aware few-shot pipelines, incorporating balanced sampling and CLAHE-enhanced preprocessing, can lead to more robust and clinically fair retinal disease diagnosis even in data-constrained environments.

Clinical Impact

This research has significant clinical impact by enabling the development of more reliable and equitable AI diagnostic tools for retinal diseases. It addresses the practical challenge of limited and imbalanced medical data, which often hinders the deployment of deep learning models in real-world clinical settings. Improved accuracy for rare or minority conditions ensures that all patients, regardless of the prevalence of their disease, receive fair and accurate automated diagnosis, potentially leading to earlier detection and better patient outcomes.

Abstract

Automated retinal disease diagnosis is vital given the rising prevalence of conditions such as diabetic retinopathy and macular degeneration. Conventional deep learning approaches require large annotated datasets, which are costly and often imbalanced across disease categories, limiting their reliability in practice. Few-shot learning (FSL) addresses this challenge by enabling models to generalize from only a few labeled samples per class. In this study,we propose a balanced few-shot episodic learning framework tailored to the Retinal Fundus Multi-Disease Image Dataset (RFMiD). Focusing on the ten most represented classes, which still show substantial imbalance between majority diseases (e.g., Diabetic Retinopathy, Macular Hole) and minority ones (e.g., Optic Disc Edema, Branch Retinal Vein Occlusion), our method integrates three key components: (i) balanced episodic sampling, ensuring equal participation of all classes in each 5-way 5-shot episode; (ii) targeted augmentation, including Contrast Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization (CLAHE) and color/geometry transformations, to improve minority-class di- versity; and (iii) a ResNet-50 encoder pretrained on ImageNet, selected for its superior ability to capture fine-grained retinal features. Prototypes are computed in the embedding space and classification is performed with cosine similarity for improved stability. Trained on 100 episodes and evaluated on 1,000 test episodes, our framework achieves substantial accuracy gains and reduces bias toward majority classes, with notable improvements for underrepresented diseases. These results demonstrate that dataset-aware few-shot pipelines, combined with balanced sampling and CLAHE-enhanced preprocessing, can deliver more robust and clinically fair retinal disease diagnosis under data-constrained conditions.
