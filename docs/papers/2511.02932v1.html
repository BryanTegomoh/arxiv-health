Phospho-Proteomics Method Optimization and Application to Stimulated Jurkat Cells

Summary

This study optimized phospho-proteomics methods for limited input and formalin-fixed cells, demonstrating that phospho-bulk titration improves phospho-peptide identification from low input. It established an efficient decrosslinking protocol for fixed samples and successfully applied these optimized methods to identify key phospho-sites in stimulated Jurkat cells relevant to T-cell activation.

Medical Relevance

Optimized phospho-proteomics enables sensitive analysis of protein phosphorylation, crucial for identifying disease biomarkers, understanding pathology, and developing therapeutic strategies, particularly in cancer and immunology, even with scarce or historically fixed clinical samples.

Key Points

  • Phospho-proteomics is crucial for understanding post-translational modification (PTM) dysregulation in diseases like cancer, but often challenged by limited sample input in clinical settings.
  • Phospho-bulk titration was found to be a more effective strategy for low-input phospho-proteomics, identifying more phospho-peptides due to reduced sample loss compared to preparing low-input starting material directly.
  • An optimal decrosslinking protocol for formalin-fixed cells (FFC) was identified: boiling in 0.05M Tris pH 7.6 with 5% SDS for 60 minutes, which yielded the highest number of phospho-peptides.
  • The optimized decrosslinking protocol addresses a significant challenge in utilizing valuable formalin-fixed archival clinical samples for phospho-proteomic analysis.
  • The study successfully applied the optimized methods, including Evotips Pure and phospho-bulk titration, to treated Jurkat cells.
  • Application to stimulated Jurkat cells led to the identification of 7 specific phospho-sites directly involved in T-cell stimulation pathways.
  • The research provides robust methodological advancements for sensitive and comprehensive phospho-proteomic analysis of challenging clinical and research samples.

Methodology

The study involved evaluating phospho-bulk titration against direct low-input material preparation, optimizing various lysis buffers and boiling times for efficient decrosslinking of formalin-fixed cells, and applying Evotips Pure in conjunction with phospho-bulk titration for mass spectrometry-based phospho-peptide identification in stimulated Jurkat cells.

Key Findings

Phospho-bulk titration significantly increased phospho-peptide identification compared to direct low-input starting material due to reduced sample loss. Optimal decrosslinking of formalin-fixed cells for phospho-proteomics was achieved by boiling in 0.05M Tris pH 7.6 with 5% SDS for 60 minutes. The optimized methods successfully identified 7 specific phospho-sites involved in T-cell stimulation in Jurkat cells.

These methodological advancements enable more comprehensive and sensitive phospho-proteomic analysis of limited and formalin-fixed clinical samples, facilitating the discovery of phosphorylation-based biomarkers in diseases like cancer, improving understanding of immune responses, and accelerating the development of targeted therapies.

Abstract

In clinical proteomics, available input is often limited. In addition, phospho-proteomics is of particular interest since the dysregulation of these post-translational modifications (PTMs) has been implicated in various diseases such as cancer. We therefore assessed the feasibility of low input phospho-proteomics via phospho-bulk titration and low-input starting material. We found that there was identification of more phospho-peptides through phospho-bulk titration because of sample loss during preparation of low input starting material. Additionally, we explored various lysis buffers and boiling times for efficiency of decrosslinking formalin-fixed cells since cells and tissues are often fixed for preservation and sorting via FACS. We found that boiling in 0.05M Tris pH 7.6 with 5% SDS for 60 min yielded the highest number of phospho-peptides. Lastly, we applied Evotips Pure and phospho-bulk titration to treated Jurkat cells and identified 7 phospho-sites involved in T-cell stimulation.
