Sepsis Prediction Using Graph Convolutional Networks over Patient-Feature-Value Triplets

arXiv ID: 2512.05416v1
Published: 2025-12-05
Authors: Bozhi Dan, Di Wu, Ji Xu, Xiang Liu, Yiziting Zhu, Xin Shu, Yujie Li, Bin Yi
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces Triplet-GCN, a novel single-branch graph convolutional network model designed for early sepsis prediction from complex, sparse, and heterogeneous Electronic Health Record (EHR) data. By representing EHR as patient-feature-value triplets and constructing a bipartite patient-feature graph, the model learns enriched patient embeddings, significantly outperforming traditional tabular machine learning baselines in discrimination and balanced error metrics for timely sepsis detection.

Medical Relevance

Timely sepsis prediction is critical for reducing patient illness and mortality in intensive care settings. This model offers a new, effective approach to overcome the data complexities of EHRs, potentially leading to earlier intervention and improved patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of an early warning system and risk stratification tool for sepsis in intensive care units. It leverages Graph Convolutional Networks (GCN) to process complex Electronic Health Record (EHR) data, representing patient information as feature-value triplets, to provide more informative patient representations for predicting sepsis onset.

Key Points

  • Sepsis is a leading cause of mortality in intensive care, but its timely detection is hampered by the challenging nature of EHR data (complex, sparse, heterogeneous).
  • Triplet-GCN is proposed as a single-branch Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) coupled with a lightweight Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) for sepsis prediction.
  • EHR data is transformed into 'patient-feature-value' triplets, which are then used to construct a bipartite patient-feature graph.
  • The model incorporates type-specific preprocessing, including median imputation and standardization for numeric variables, effect coding for binary features, and mode imputation with low-dimensional embeddings for rare categorical attributes.
  • Patient nodes are initialized with summary statistics, while measurement values are retained on graph edges to preserve detailed 'who measured what and by how much' information.
  • Evaluated on a retrospective, multi-center Chinese cohort (N=648), Triplet-GCN consistently outperformed strong tabular baselines (KNN, SVM, XGBoost, Random Forest) across discrimination and balanced error metrics.
  • The model demonstrated a more favorable sensitivity-specificity trade-off and improved overall utility for early warning, indicating more informative patient representations are learned through graph propagation.

Methodology

The Triplet-GCN model processes EHR data by first converting patient encounters into 'patient-feature-value' triplets. These triplets are then used to construct a bipartite graph connecting patient nodes and feature nodes. Numeric features undergo median imputation and standardization, binary features are effect coded, and rare categorical features receive mode imputation with low-dimensional embeddings. Patient nodes are initialized using summary statistics derived from their features, while the specific measurement values are retained on the edges connecting patient and feature nodes. A Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) then propagates information across this graph to learn patient embeddings, which are subsequently fed into a lightweight Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) for final sepsis prediction. The model was trained and tested on a 70/30 split of a retrospective, multi-center Chinese cohort.

Key Findings

Triplet-GCN consistently outperformed strong traditional tabular machine learning models (KNN, SVM, XGBoost, Random Forest) in predicting sepsis. It achieved superior discrimination and balanced error metrics, demonstrating a better balance between sensitivity and specificity, thereby enhancing its overall utility as an early warning system. The findings suggest that representing EHR as triplets and leveraging graph-based information propagation yields more informative and robust patient representations than feature-independent models.

Clinical Impact

This model provides a simple, end-to-end blueprint for deployable sepsis risk stratification in intensive care units. Its ability to provide early warnings with improved accuracy and a better sensitivity-specificity trade-off can enable earlier clinical interventions, potentially leading to reduced morbidity, mortality, and healthcare costs associated with sepsis. The approach's robustness to sparse and heterogeneous EHR data makes it highly practical for real-world clinical deployment.

Limitations

The study was conducted on a retrospective cohort, which inherently limits causal inferences and generalizability compared to prospective studies. The cohort size (N=648) from a specific demographic (Chinese population across three tertiary hospitals) may limit generalizability to broader, diverse patient populations or different healthcare systems. The abstract does not explicitly discuss model interpretability, which is often a crucial aspect for clinical adoption.

Future Directions

The abstract frames the model as an 'end-to-end blueprint for deployable sepsis risk stratification,' implying future work on clinical deployment and real-world validation. Further research could involve prospective studies, validation on larger and more diverse international cohorts, exploration of model interpretability, and integration into existing clinical workflows for real-time risk assessment.

Abstract

In the intensive care setting, sepsis continues to be a major contributor to patient illness and death; however, its timely detection is hindered by the complex, sparse, and heterogeneous nature of electronic health record (EHR) data. We propose Triplet-GCN, a single-branch graph convolutional model that represents each encounter as patient-feature-value triplets, constructs a bipartite EHR graph, and learns patient embeddings via a Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) followed by a lightweight multilayer perceptron (MLP). The pipeline applies type-specific preprocessing -- median imputation and standardization for numeric variables, effect coding for binary features, and mode imputation with low-dimensional embeddings for rare categorical attributes -- and initializes patient nodes with summary statistics, while retaining measurement values on edges to preserve "who measured what and by how much". In a retrospective, multi-center Chinese cohort (N = 648; 70/30 train-test split) drawn from three tertiary hospitals, Triplet-GCN consistently outperforms strong tabular baselines (KNN, SVM, XGBoost, Random Forest) across discrimination and balanced error metrics, yielding a more favorable sensitivity-specificity trade-off and improved overall utility for early warning. These findings indicate that encoding EHR as triplets and propagating information over a patient-feature graph produce more informative patient representations than feature-independent models, offering a simple, end-to-end blueprint for deployable sepsis risk stratification.
