QuanvNeXt: An end-to-end quanvolutional neural network for EEG-based detection of major depressive disorder

arXiv ID: 2512.09517v1
Published: 2025-12-10
Authors: Nabil Anan Orka, Ehtashamul Haque, Maftahul Jannat, Md Abdul Awal, Mohammad Ali Moni
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

QuanvNeXt is an end-to-end fully quanvolutional neural network designed for EEG-based detection of major depressive disorder (MDD), incorporating a novel Cross Residual block for enhanced feature learning and parameter efficiency. The model achieved high diagnostic performance with 93.1% accuracy and 97.2% AUC-ROC on two datasets, surpassing state-of-the-art models, while also demonstrating well-calibrated predictions and interpretable identification of disease-specific spectrotemporal patterns. This establishes QuanvNeXt as an efficient and reliable approach for objective MDD diagnosis.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a significant advancement in developing objective, automated, and interpretable diagnostic tools for Major Depressive Disorder using readily available EEG data. Such a tool could enable earlier, more consistent, and less subjective diagnosis, critical for prompt and effective treatment initiation and improved patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

An AI model (QuanvNeXt, a quanvolutional neural network) designed to automate or assist in the diagnosis of Major Depressive Disorder by analyzing EEG data. It aims to provide an efficient, reliable, and interpretable tool for clinical depression diagnosis.

Key Points

  • **Novel Model Architecture**: Introduces QuanvNeXt, an end-to-end fully quanvolutional neural network specifically for EEG-based Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) diagnosis.
  • **Innovative Cross Residual Block**: Integrates a novel Cross Residual block designed to reduce feature homogeneity, strengthen cross-feature relationships, and maintain parameter efficiency.
  • **Superior Diagnostic Performance**: Achieved an average accuracy of 93.1% and an average AUC-ROC of 97.2% on two distinct open-source EEG datasets.
  • **Outperforms Baselines**: Demonstrated superior performance compared to state-of-the-art models, specifically outperforming InceptionTime (91.7% accuracy, 95.9% AUC-ROC).
  • **Robustness and Calibration**: Uncertainty analysis across Gaussian noise levels showed well-calibrated predictions, with low to moderate ECE scores (0.0436 for Dataset 1, 0.1159 for Dataset 2) even at high perturbation levels (Œµ = 0.1).
  • **Interpretability via Explainable AI (XAI)**: Post-hoc XAI analysis confirmed that QuanvNeXt effectively identifies and learns relevant spectrotemporal EEG patterns that distinguish MDD patients from healthy controls.
  • **Efficient and Reliable Approach**: Overall, the study establishes QuanvNeXt as an efficient, reliable, and interpretable method, offering a robust tool for objective EEG-based depression diagnosis.

Methodology

The study developed QuanvNeXt, an end-to-end fully quanvolutional neural network, incorporating a novel Cross Residual block for enhanced feature learning and parameter efficiency. The model was trained and evaluated on two open-source EEG datasets for binary classification (Major Depressive Disorder vs. healthy controls). Performance was measured using average accuracy and AUC-ROC, comparing QuanvNeXt against state-of-the-art baselines like InceptionTime. Additional analyses included uncertainty quantification through ECE scores under varying Gaussian noise perturbations and post-hoc Explainable AI (XAI) to confirm the model's ability to identify relevant spectrotemporal patterns.

Key Findings

QuanvNeXt achieved an average accuracy of 93.1% and an average AUC-ROC of 97.2%, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art baselines, including InceptionTime. It demonstrated robust and well-calibrated predictions, with ECE scores ranging from low (0.0436) to moderate (0.1159) even under substantial data perturbation. Furthermore, an Explainable AI analysis confirmed that the model effectively learns and leverages spectrotemporal patterns within EEG signals that are discriminative for Major Depressive Disorder.

Clinical Impact

The development of QuanvNeXt offers a promising objective and interpretable diagnostic aid for Major Depressive Disorder. Its high accuracy, robustness, and ability to highlight specific neurophysiological markers could lead to more consistent and earlier diagnoses, reducing diagnostic delays and variability associated with subjective assessments. This could facilitate more timely therapeutic interventions and monitoring, potentially improving treatment efficacy and patient quality of life.

Abstract

This study presents QuanvNeXt, an end-to-end fully quanvolutional model for EEG-based depression diagnosis. QuanvNeXt incorporates a novel Cross Residual block, which reduces feature homogeneity and strengthens cross-feature relationships while retaining parameter efficiency. We evaluated QuanvNeXt on two open-source datasets, where it achieved an average accuracy of 93.1% and an average AUC-ROC of 97.2%, outperforming state-of-the-art baselines such as InceptionTime (91.7% accuracy, 95.9% AUC-ROC). An uncertainty analysis across Gaussian noise levels demonstrated well-calibrated predictions, with ECE scores remaining low (0.0436, Dataset 1) to moderate (0.1159, Dataset 2) even at the highest perturbation (Œµ = 0.1). Additionally, a post-hoc explainable AI analysis confirmed that QuanvNeXt effectively identifies and learns spectrotemporal patterns that distinguish between healthy controls and major depressive disorder. Overall, QuanvNeXt establishes an efficient and reliable approach for EEG-based depression diagnosis.
