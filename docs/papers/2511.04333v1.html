LUME-DBN: Full Bayesian Learning of DBNs from Incomplete data in Intensive Care

Summary

This paper introduces LUME-DBN, a novel Gibbs sampling-based full Bayesian method designed for learning Dynamic Bayesian Networks (DBNs) directly from incomplete longitudinal clinical data. It addresses the critical limitation of existing missing data imputation techniques that fail to properly account for the temporal nature of medical datasets, especially in intensive care. By treating missing values as unknown Gaussian parameters sampled from their full conditional distributions, the approach achieves superior reconstruction accuracy and convergence compared to standard model-agnostic methods like MICE, leading to more reliable imputations and supporting safer clinical decisions.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine as it directly addresses a pervasive challenge of missing data in longitudinal patient records, particularly in critical care. By enabling more reliable and interpretable DBNs, it supports more accurate temporal modeling of patient states, which is fundamental for advanced clinical decision-making and patient management.

Key Points

  • **Problem Statement:** Existing missing data handling in DBNs, often derived from static Bayesian network literature, inadequately addresses the temporal nature of longitudinal clinical data, limiting uncertainty quantification over time, which is critical in intensive care.
  • **Proposed Solution (LUME-DBN):** A novel full Bayesian framework utilizing Gibbs sampling for learning DBNs from incomplete data, specifically designed to integrate missing data handling within the temporal model.
  • **Methodology for Missing Data:** Each missing value is treated as an unknown parameter assumed to follow a Gaussian distribution, and these unobserved values are sampled from their full conditional distributions at each iteration of the Gibbs sampler.
  • **Benefits of Methodology:** This principled approach allows for robust imputation and effective estimation of uncertainty over time, enhancing model trustworthiness and applicability.
  • **Evaluation Datasets:** The method's performance was rigorously evaluated on both simulated datasets and real-world intensive care data obtained from critically ill patients.
  • **Performance Superiority:** LUME-DBN demonstrated superior reconstruction accuracy and improved convergence properties when compared against standard model-agnostic imputation techniques, such as Multiple Imputation by Chained Equations (MICE).
  • **Clinical Relevance Highlighted:** The results underscore the significant clinical relevance of integrating full Bayesian inference into temporal models for healthcare, providing more reliable data imputations and deeper insights into complex patient dynamics.

Methodology

The core methodology is a novel Gibbs sampling-based full Bayesian approach for learning DBNs. It integrates missing data handling by treating each unobserved value as an unknown parameter, modeled with a Gaussian distribution. At each iteration of the Gibbs sampler, these missing values are sampled from their respective full conditional distributions, allowing for a principled, probabilistic imputation that inherently accounts for temporal dependencies and quantifies uncertainty.

Key Findings

The full Bayesian LUME-DBN method exhibited superior reconstruction accuracy and enhanced convergence properties compared to standard model-agnostic imputation techniques, such as MICE, across both simulated and real-world intensive care datasets. This demonstrates its ability to provide more reliable imputations and a deeper understanding of the underlying temporal model behavior by properly incorporating uncertainty.

Abstract

Dynamic Bayesian networks (DBNs) are increasingly used in healthcare due to their ability to model complex temporal relationships in patient data while maintaining interpretability, an essential feature for clinical decision-making. However, existing approaches to handling missing data in longitudinal clinical datasets are largely derived from static Bayesian networks literature, failing to properly account for the temporal nature of the data. This gap limits the ability to quantify uncertainty over time, which is particularly critical in settings such as intensive care, where understanding the temporal dynamics is fundamental for model trustworthiness and applicability across diverse patient groups. Despite the potential of DBNs, a full Bayesian framework that integrates missing data handling remains underdeveloped. In this work, we propose a novel Gibbs sampling-based method for learning DBNs from incomplete data. Our method treats each missing value as an unknown parameter following a Gaussian distribution. At each iteration, the unobserved values are sampled from their full conditional distributions, allowing for principled imputation and uncertainty estimation. We evaluate our method on both simulated datasets and real-world intensive care data from critically ill patients. Compared to standard model-agnostic techniques such as MICE, our Bayesian approach demonstrates superior reconstruction accuracy and convergence properties. These results highlight the clinical relevance of incorporating full Bayesian inference in temporal models, providing more reliable imputations and offering deeper insight into model behavior. Our approach supports safer and more informed clinical decision-making, particularly in settings where missing data are frequent and potentially impactful.
