Summary

The `causalfe` package provides a Python implementation of Causal Forests with Fixed Effects (CFFE), designed to estimate heterogeneous treatment effects robustly in panel data settings. It addresses the common challenge of spurious heterogeneity caused by unit and time fixed effects in standard causal forest methods by employing node-level residualization during tree construction. The paper details the methodology, software interface, and validates its performance through simulation studies across various data generating processes.

Medical Relevance

This methodology is highly relevant for medicine as it enables robust estimation of heterogeneous treatment effects in longitudinal patient data, such as electronic health records or clinical trials, controlling for unobserved patient-level and time-specific confounding factors. This capability is crucial for developing personalized treatment strategies and understanding differential treatment responses in real-world clinical settings, improving the precision of medical interventions.

Methodology

The paper introduces the Causal Forests with Fixed Effects (CFFE) approach, implemented in the `causalfe` Python package. The core methodology involves performing node-level residualization during the construction of the causal forest trees. This means that within each candidate split for tree growth, unit-specific and time-specific fixed effects are estimated and removed from the outcome and treatment variables. This localized residualization prevents these fixed effects from spuriously driving heterogeneity in treatment effect estimates, a problem common in standard causal forests when applied to panel data. The performance is validated via extensive simulation studies.
