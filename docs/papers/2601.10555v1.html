causalfe: Causal Forests with Fixed Effects in Python

Summary

The causalfe package introduces a Python implementation of Causal Forests with Fixed Effects (CFFE) to accurately estimate heterogeneous treatment effects in panel data settings. This method addresses the challenge of unit and time fixed effects, which typically induce spurious heterogeneity in standard causal forest estimates, by performing node-level residualization during tree construction. Through simulation studies, the package demonstrates validated performance, offering a robust tool for causal inference in longitudinal settings relevant to precision medicine and health research.

Medical Relevance

This methodology is crucial for precision medicine and real-world evidence generation in healthcare, allowing researchers to accurately identify patient subgroups who respond differently to treatments in longitudinal studies (e.g., EHR data, observational cohorts). By correctly accounting for stable patient characteristics and time-varying confounders, it enables more precise and reliable estimations of treatment efficacy and safety for diverse patient populations.

Key Points

  • The paper addresses the critical problem of accurately estimating heterogeneous treatment effects in panel data, where individual patients or units are observed over multiple time periods.
  • Standard causal forest methods struggle in panel data due to unit and time fixed effects that can induce spurious (non-causal) heterogeneity in treatment effect estimates.
  • The proposed Causal Forests with Fixed Effects (CFFE) methodology resolves this by performing 'node-level residualization,' which removes fixed effects within each candidate split during the tree construction process, rather than globally.
  • The `causalfe` package provides a practical Python implementation of the CFFE method, making this advanced causal inference technique accessible to researchers.
  • The estimator's performance and validity are thoroughly demonstrated and confirmed through rigorous simulation studies under various data generating processes.
  • This approach prevents misattribution of treatment effect heterogeneity to patient characteristics when it is, in fact, due to unobserved stable factors or time trends, leading to more reliable personalized treatment insights.
  • The method offers a robust tool for researchers in medical and health fields to identify specific patient subgroups who benefit most or least from an intervention over time.

Methodology

The paper describes Causal Forests with Fixed Effects (CFFE), an ensemble machine learning method that builds multiple causal trees specifically adapted for panel data. The core innovation lies in 'node-level residualization,' where unit-specific and time-specific fixed effects are removed within each candidate split of a decision tree. This ensures that splits identifying treatment effect heterogeneity are driven by true patient-level or time-level variation rather than confounding fixed effects. The methodology is implemented in the Python package `causalfe` and validated through extensive simulation studies that test its performance under various data generating processes.

Key Findings

The `causalfe` package successfully implements Causal Forests with Fixed Effects (CFFE), which is demonstrated to accurately estimate heterogeneous treatment effects in panel data. The key to its success is the novel node-level residualization technique, which effectively addresses the confounding influence of unit and time fixed effects. Simulation studies confirm the estimator's robust performance and validity across different data generating processes, showcasing its ability to prevent spurious heterogeneity.
