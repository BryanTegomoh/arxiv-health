An Allele-Centric Pan-Graph-Matrix Representation for Scalable Pangenome Analysis

Summary

This paper introduces the H1 pan-graph-matrix, an allele-centric representation that encodes exact haplotype membership using adaptive per-allele compression, designed to unify single-nucleotide and structural variation for scalable pangenome analysis. By treating alleles as first-class objects, H1 achieves near-optimal storage and significant compression gains, particularly for structural variants, as validated with real human genome data. It provides a unified, population-aware foundation that also yields H2, a path-centric dual representation, both exactly equivalent in information content to pangenome graphs.

Medical Relevance

By enabling more scalable and efficient representation of genomic variation, this framework significantly enhances our capacity to analyze large population cohorts for disease-causing variants and complex genetic architecture. Its superior handling of structural variants is critical for understanding many genetic disorders and cancer, providing a powerful tool for precision medicine.

Methodology

The paper introduces a computational representation called the H1 pan-graph-matrix, which is allele-centric and encodes exact haplotype membership using adaptive per-allele compression based on carrier distribution. It also describes H2, a path-centric dual representation derived from H1's allele-haplotype incidence data. The approach was evaluated using real human genome data to demonstrate its compression efficiency, particularly for structural variants, and its information equivalence to pangenome graphs.

Abstract

Population-scale pangenome analysis increasingly requires representations that unify single-nucleotide and structural variation while remaining scalable across large cohorts. Existing formats are typically sequence-centric, path-centric, or sample-centric, and often obscure population structure or fail to exploit carrier sparsity. We introduce the H1 pan-graph-matrix, an allele-centric representation that encodes exact haplotype membership using adaptive per-allele compression. By treating alleles as first-class objects and selecting optimal encodings based on carrier distribution, H1 achieves near-optimal storage across both common and rare variants. We further introduce H2, a path-centric dual representation derived from the same underlying allele-haplotype incidence information that restores explicit haplotype ordering while remaining exactly equivalent in information content. Using real human genome data, we show that this representation yields substantial compression gains, particularly for structural variants, while remaining equivalent in information content to pangenome graphs. H1 provides a unified, population-aware foundation for scalable pangenome analysis and downstream applications such as rare-variant interpretation and drug discovery.
