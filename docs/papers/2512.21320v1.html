An Allele-Centric Pan-Graph-Matrix Representation for Scalable Pangenome Analysis

Summary

This paper introduces H1, an allele-centric pan-graph-matrix, and its dual H2, a path-centric representation, designed to enhance scalability and efficiency in population-scale pangenome analysis. By utilizing adaptive per-allele compression and treating alleles as first-class objects, these representations achieve significant storage compression, especially for structural variants, while retaining full information equivalence to pangenome graphs. This novel approach provides a unified, population-aware foundation critical for advanced genomic applications.

Medical Relevance

This innovation significantly improves the ability to efficiently store and analyze vast human genomic datasets, directly impacting the identification of disease-causing variants, understanding genetic risk factors, and accelerating drug target discovery. By better handling complex genetic variations across large populations, it enhances the foundation for precision medicine.

This enhanced data representation is critical for enabling AI applications in health, as it allows large-scale genomic datasets to be processed and fed into machine learning models for tasks such as identifying disease-causing rare variants, predicting drug targets, understanding drug response variability, and developing personalized treatment strategies. While not an AI application itself, it is an essential enabling technology for future AI applications in genomic medicine and drug discovery.

Methodology

The authors developed two novel data structures: the H1 pan-graph-matrix (allele-centric) and its H2 dual representation (path-centric). H1 employs an adaptive per-allele compression algorithm that optimizes encoding based on the carrier distribution of alleles to store exact haplotype membership. The efficacy of these representations was evaluated using real human genome data, demonstrating compression gains (especially for structural variants) and confirming information equivalence to existing pangenome graph formats.

Key Findings

The H1 and H2 representations yield substantial compression gains, particularly for structural variants, achieving near-optimal storage efficiency for both common and rare variants. They effectively encode population structure and exploit carrier sparsity, maintaining full information equivalence to pangenome graphs while providing a more scalable and population-aware foundation for pangenome analysis.

Abstract

Population-scale pangenome analysis increasingly requires representations that unify single-nucleotide and structural variation while remaining scalable across large cohorts. Existing formats are typically sequence-centric, path-centric, or sample-centric, and often obscure population structure or fail to exploit carrier sparsity. We introduce the H1 pan-graph-matrix, an allele-centric representation that encodes exact haplotype membership using adaptive per-allele compression. By treating alleles as first-class objects and selecting optimal encodings based on carrier distribution, H1 achieves near-optimal storage across both common and rare variants. We further introduce H2, a path-centric dual representation derived from the same underlying allele-haplotype incidence information that restores explicit haplotype ordering while remaining exactly equivalent in information content. Using real human genome data, we show that this representation yields substantial compression gains, particularly for structural variants, while remaining equivalent in information content to pangenome graphs. H1 provides a unified, population-aware foundation for scalable pangenome analysis and downstream applications such as rare-variant interpretation and drug discovery.
