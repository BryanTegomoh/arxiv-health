Pattern-Aware Diffusion Synthesis of fMRI/dMRI with Tissue and Microstructural Refinement

arXiv ID: 2511.04963v1
Published: 2025-11-07
Authors: Xiongri Shen, Jiaqi Wang, Yi Zhong, Zhenxi Song, Leilei Zhao, Yichen Wei, Lingyan Liang, Shuqiang Wang, Baiying Lei, Demao Deng, Zhiguo Zhang
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI

View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces PDS, a novel pattern-aware dual-modal 3D diffusion framework with tissue and microstructure refinement for synthesizing fMRI and dMRI data. PDS addresses limitations of previous generative models by effectively handling signal differences and integrating disease-related neuroanatomical patterns. The method achieves state-of-the-art synthesis quality and demonstrates strong diagnostic performance in classifying neurodegenerative diseases using the generated data.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine and health by providing a robust solution for generating missing fMRI/dMRI data, which are crucial for studying and diagnosing neurodegenerative diseases. It facilitates more comprehensive patient evaluations and enables data augmentation for machine learning models, ultimately improving diagnostic accuracy and research into conditions like Alzheimer's Disease.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using pattern-aware dual-modal 3D diffusion models and neural networks to synthesize fMRI and dMRI data. This aims to complete missing medical imaging modalities, thereby enhancing the diagnostic capabilities and clinical utility of these imaging techniques for neurodegenerative diseases, specifically improving diagnostic accuracy for conditions like Alzheimer's disease and MCI.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical problem of missing fMRI/dMRI modalities in neurodegenerative disease research and clinical diagnosis, overcoming limitations of existing GAN and diffusion models.
  • Proposes PDS, a pattern-aware dual-modal 3D diffusion framework specifically designed for robust cross-modality learning between fMRI and dMRI.
  • Integrates a tissue refinement network and an efficient microstructure refinement network to maintain high structural fidelity and preserve fine anatomical details in the synthesized images.
  • Achieves state-of-the-art quantitative synthesis results, with fMRI scoring PSNR 29.83 dB / SSIM 90.84% and dMRI scoring PSNR 30.00 dB / SSIM 77.55% on OASIS-3, ADNI, and in-house datasets.
  • Demonstrates significant improvements over baselines, with gains of +1.54 dB/+4.12% for fMRI and +1.02 dB/+2.2% for dMRI synthesis quality.
  • Shows strong clinical diagnostic performance, achieving 67.92%/66.02%/64.15% accuracy in hybrid real-synthetic experiments for differentiating Normal Control (NC), Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI), and Alzheimer's Disease (AD).

Methodology

The PDS method employs a pattern-aware dual-modal 3D diffusion framework engineered for cross-modality learning, designed to handle the significant BOLD vs. diffusion-weighted signal differences between fMRI and dMRI. This framework is further enhanced by integrating a tissue refinement network to preserve overall structural fidelity and an efficient microstructure refinement network to maintain fine anatomical details. The model explicitly incorporates disease-related neuroanatomical patterns during the image generation process.

Key Findings

The PDS method demonstrated state-of-the-art performance in synthesizing fMRI and dMRI, achieving PSNR of 29.83 dB and SSIM of 90.84% for fMRI (outperforming baselines by +1.54 dB and +4.12%) and PSNR of 30.00 dB and SSIM of 77.55% for dMRI (outperforming baselines by +1.02 dB and +2.2%). Critically, in clinical validation, the synthesized data showed strong diagnostic utility, achieving 67.92%/66.02%/64.15% accuracy in hybrid real-synthetic experiments for classifying Normal Controls (NC), Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI), and Alzheimer's Disease (AD).

Clinical Impact

The ability to reliably synthesize fMRI and dMRI data has a profound clinical impact by addressing the prevalent issue of missing modalities in patient cohorts and research studies. This can lead to more complete diagnostic workups for neurodegenerative diseases, enable the creation of larger and more diverse datasets for training diagnostic AI models, and potentially facilitate earlier and more accurate detection and monitoring of conditions like Alzheimer's Disease and Mild Cognitive Impairment.

Abstract

Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), especially functional MRI (fMRI) and diffusion MRI (dMRI), is essential for studying neurodegenerative diseases. However, missing modalities pose a major barrier to their clinical use. Although GAN- and diffusion model-based approaches have shown some promise in modality completion, they remain limited in fMRI-dMRI synthesis due to (1) significant BOLD vs. diffusion-weighted signal differences between fMRI and dMRI in time/gradient axis, and (2) inadequate integration of disease-related neuroanatomical patterns during generation. To address these challenges, we propose PDS, introducing two key innovations: (1) a pattern-aware dual-modal 3D diffusion framework for cross-modality learning, and (2) a tissue refinement network integrated with a efficient microstructure refinement to maintain structural fidelity and fine details. Evaluated on OASIS-3, ADNI, and in-house datasets, our method achieves state-of-the-art results, with PSNR/SSIM scores of 29.83 dB/90.84\% for fMRI synthesis (+1.54 dB/+4.12\% over baselines) and 30.00 dB/77.55\% for dMRI synthesis (+1.02 dB/+2.2\%). In clinical validation, the synthesized data show strong diagnostic performance, achieving 67.92\%/66.02\%/64.15\% accuracy (NC vs. MCI vs. AD) in hybrid real-synthetic experiments.
