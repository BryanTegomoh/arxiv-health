Summary

This paper introduces the Multi-Organ Medical Image REconstruction (MORE) dataset, designed to overcome the limited generalization capabilities of current deep learning CT reconstruction methods. Comprising CT scans across 9 diverse anatomies and 15 lesion types, MORE enables robust model training and rigorous evaluation of generalization for deep learning-based CT reconstruction. The authors also establish a strong baseline solution that demonstrates improved generalization and highlights the robustness of optimization-based methods for unseen anatomies.

Medical Relevance

This dataset addresses a critical bottleneck in medical imaging AI: the lack of generalizable deep learning models for CT reconstruction. By providing diverse, multi-organ data, it aims to create more robust and reliable AI tools that can assist radiologists in diagnosing various conditions across different body parts, ultimately improving diagnostic accuracy and efficiency.

AI Health Application

The paper describes the creation of a multi-organ medical image dataset (MORE) specifically designed to train and evaluate deep learning models for CT reconstruction. The goal is to enhance the robustness and generalization capabilities of these AI models, thereby improving the quality and reliability of CT images provided to radiologists for accurate diagnosis and treatment planning across diverse patient anatomies and disease conditions.

Key Points

  • Current deep learning CT reconstruction methods are limited by specific anatomies and datasets, hindering generalization.
  • The MORE dataset comprises CT scans from 9 diverse anatomies and 15 distinct lesion types to promote heterogeneity.
  • Its primary purposes are to facilitate robust training of deep learning models on extensive data and enable rigorous evaluation of model generalization.
  • A strong baseline solution is established using the MORE dataset, outperforming prior approaches under challenging multi-organ conditions.
  • Findings indicate that comprehensive, heterogeneous datasets significantly improve the generalization capability of deep learning models in CT reconstruction.
  • Optimization-based methods are demonstrated to offer enhanced robustness when dealing with previously unseen anatomical structures.
  • The MORE dataset is freely accessible under a CC-BY-NC 4.0 license, promoting open research and development.

Methodology

The methodology involves the creation and curation of the MORE dataset, which includes CT scans from 9 distinct anatomical regions and features 15 different lesion types to ensure high diversity. Subsequently, the authors establish a strong baseline deep learning solution using this dataset. This baseline is evaluated under challenging conditions to assess its generalization capabilities compared to existing approaches, specifically noting the performance of optimization-based methods.

Key Findings

The study's key findings are twofold: (1) The availability of a comprehensive, heterogeneous dataset significantly enhances the generalization capability of deep learning models for CT reconstruction. (2) Optimization-based methods exhibit superior robustness when confronted with previously unseen anatomical structures, making them particularly suitable for broad clinical application.

Clinical Impact

The MORE dataset and its accompanying findings could lead to the development of more versatile and reliable deep learning models for CT image reconstruction. This could improve the quality and consistency of diagnostic images across a wider range of patients and pathologies, reduce the need for organ-specific model training, and potentially enable faster, more accurate diagnoses by radiologists, especially for rare or complex conditions.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state specific limitations of the MORE dataset itself or the proposed baseline solution. However, it implicitly highlights the limitations of prior deep learning CT reconstruction methods, which were characterized by poor generalization due to their training on specific anatomies and datasets.

Future Directions

The MORE dataset is positioned as a foundational resource to enable future research in robust deep learning for CT reconstruction. It encourages the development and rigorous evaluation of new deep learning models capable of generalizing across diverse anatomies and lesion types. Future work will likely focus on leveraging this dataset to train highly generalizable models and further explore the robustness of different reconstruction methodologies.

Abstract

CT reconstruction provides radiologists with images for diagnosis and treatment, yet current deep learning methods are typically limited to specific anatomies and datasets, hindering generalization ability to unseen anatomies and lesions. To address this, we introduce the Multi-Organ medical image REconstruction (MORE) dataset, comprising CT scans across 9 diverse anatomies with 15 lesion types. This dataset serves two key purposes: (1) enabling robust training of deep learning models on extensive, heterogeneous data, and (2) facilitating rigorous evaluation of model generalization for CT reconstruction. We further establish a strong baseline solution that outperforms prior approaches under these challenging conditions. Our results demonstrate that: (1) a comprehensive dataset helps improve the generalization capability of models, and (2) optimization-based methods offer enhanced robustness for unseen anatomies. The MORE dataset is freely accessible under CC-BY-NC 4.0 at our project page https://more-med.github.io/
