Beyond Pixel Simulation: Pathology Image Generation via Diagnostic Semantic Tokens and Prototype Control

Summary

UniPath is a novel semantics-driven framework for pathology image generation that overcomes the limitations of pixel-based simulation by integrating mature diagnostic understanding. It introduces Multi-Stream Control, leveraging Raw-Text, High-Level Semantics with Diagnostic Semantic Tokens, and Prototype control, achieving state-of-the-art performance in image quality and fine-grained semantic control while addressing data scarcity with new curated datasets.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine as it provides a powerful tool for generating realistic and precisely controllable pathology images. This capability is critical for augmenting scarce diagnostic datasets, training and validating advanced AI models for pathology, enhancing medical education, and facilitating research into specific morphological features of diseases.

AI Health Application

This research develops UniPath, an AI framework for generating synthetic pathology images with fine-grained control over diagnostic semantic features and morphological prototypes. This has critical applications in: 1) **Data augmentation** for training more robust and accurate AI models for diagnostic tasks in pathology (e.g., cancer detection, disease classification), especially for rare conditions where real data is scarce. 2) **Medical education and training** by providing diverse and controllable examples of various diseases, stages, or subtle morphological variations for pathologists and medical students. 3) **Medical research** to investigate specific disease characteristics by generating images that emphasize or combine particular diagnostic attributes. 4) Potentially aiding in the development of more interpretable AI diagnostic tools by allowing the generation of images corresponding to specific diagnostic concepts.

Methodology

UniPath employs a Multi-Stream Control architecture. It integrates a Raw-Text stream for basic conditioning; a High-Level Semantics stream that uses learnable queries on a frozen pathology Multimodal Large Language Model (MLLM) to distill paraphrase-robust Diagnostic Semantic Tokens and expand prompts into diagnosis-aware attribute bundles; and a Prototype stream for component-level morphological control via a prototype bank. The framework was trained on a newly curated dataset comprising a 2.65 million image-text corpus and a 68,000 finely annotated subset. Evaluation was conducted using a custom four-tier hierarchy tailored for pathology.

Key Findings

UniPath achieved SOTA performance in pathology image generation, with a Patho-FID of 80.9, representing a 51% improvement over the second-best method. It demonstrated exceptional fine-grained semantic control, reaching performance levels equivalent to 98.7% of real-image quality. This indicates a significant leap in the realism and diagnostically relevant controllability of generated pathology images.

Furthermore, it facilitates research into the intricate relationships between morphological features and diagnostic outcomes, potentially leading to more robust and accurate computational pathology solutions that can aid clinicians in diagnosis and prognosis.

Abstract

In computational pathology, understanding and generation have evolved along disparate paths: advanced understanding models already exhibit diagnostic-level competence, whereas generative models largely simulate pixels. Progress remains hindered by three coupled factors: the scarcity of large, high-quality image-text corpora; the lack of precise, fine-grained semantic control, which forces reliance on non-semantic cues; and terminological heterogeneity, where diverse phrasings for the same diagnostic concept impede reliable text conditioning. We introduce UniPath, a semantics-driven pathology image generation framework that leverages mature diagnostic understanding to enable controllable generation. UniPath implements Multi-Stream Control: a Raw-Text stream; a High-Level Semantics stream that uses learnable queries to a frozen pathology MLLM to distill paraphrase-robust Diagnostic Semantic Tokens and to expand prompts into diagnosis-aware attribute bundles; and a Prototype stream that affords component-level morphological control via a prototype bank. On the data front, we curate a 2.65M image-text corpus and a finely annotated, high-quality 68K subset to alleviate data scarcity. For a comprehensive assessment, we establish a four-tier evaluation hierarchy tailored to pathology. Extensive experiments demonstrate UniPath's SOTA performance, including a Patho-FID of 80.9 (51% better than the second-best) and fine-grained semantic control achieving 98.7% of the real-image. The meticulously curated datasets, complete source code, and pre-trained model weights developed in this study will be made openly accessible to the public.
