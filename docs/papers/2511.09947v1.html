EEGAgent: A Unified Framework for Automated EEG Analysis Using Large Language Models

Summary

EEGAgent introduces a unified framework leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) to automate multi-task EEG analysis, addressing the limitations of existing task-specific models. By scheduling specialized EEG tools, it performs functions like basic information perception, spatiotemporal exploration, event detection, user interaction, and report generation, demonstrating flexible and interpretable analysis with significant potential for clinical applications.

Medical Relevance

This framework offers a significant advancement for clinical diagnostics and cognitive research by enabling scalable, generalizable, and automated multi-task analysis of brain activity from EEG. It has the potential to streamline complex neurological interpretations, improving efficiency and accuracy in healthcare settings.

AI Health Application

The research proposes 'EEGAgent,' an AI framework leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) to automate and streamline the analysis of EEG data. This involves performing tasks like EEG data perception, spatiotemporal exploration, event detection (e.g., detecting seizures or sleep events), and generating clinical reports. Its application aims to provide flexible, interpretable, and scalable EEG analysis to assist clinicians in diagnosis and monitoring of neurological conditions.

Methodology

EEGAgent employs a Large Language Model (LLM) as a central orchestrator responsible for scheduling and planning the execution of various specialized EEG analysis tools. These tools comprise functions for essential steps such as EEG preprocessing, feature extraction, and event detection. The framework's capabilities and performance were validated through evaluation on public EEG datasets.

Key Findings

EEGAgent successfully unifies diverse EEG analysis tasks within a single framework, demonstrating proficiency in perceiving basic EEG information, exploring spatiotemporal patterns, detecting relevant events, interacting with users, and generating comprehensive reports. It provides flexible and interpretable analysis, confirming its strong potential for practical clinical integration and applications.

Its capability for interpretable analysis and user interaction could empower clinicians with more robust decision-making tools for a wide range of neurological conditions, improving patient care and diagnostic throughput.

Abstract

Scalable and generalizable analysis of brain activity is essential for advancing both clinical diagnostics and cognitive research. Electroencephalography (EEG), a non-invasive modality with high temporal resolution, has been widely used for brain states analysis. However, most existing EEG models are usually tailored for individual specific tasks, limiting their utility in realistic scenarios where EEG analysis often involves multi-task and continuous reasoning. In this work, we introduce EEGAgent, a general-purpose framework that leverages large language models (LLMs) to schedule and plan multiple tools to automatically complete EEG-related tasks. EEGAgent is capable of performing the key functions: EEG basic information perception, spatiotemporal EEG exploration, EEG event detection, interaction with users, and EEG report generation. To realize these capabilities, we design a toolbox composed of different tools for EEG preprocessing, feature extraction, event detection, etc. These capabilities were evaluated on public datasets, and our EEGAgent can support flexible and interpretable EEG analysis, highlighting its potential for real-world clinical applications.
