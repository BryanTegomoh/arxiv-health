Mask-Robust Face Verification for Online Learning via YOLOv5 and Residual Networks

Published: 2025-10-29
Authors: Zhifeng Wang, Minghui Wang, Chunyan Zeng, Jialong Yao, Yang Yang, Hongmin Xu

Summary

This paper proposes a mask-robust face verification system for online learning environments, leveraging YOLOv5 for real-time face detection and a residual network for deep feature extraction. The system aims to enhance the security and stability of online education by accurately authenticating student identities even when masks are present, using Euclidean distance comparison against a student face database.

Medical Relevance

While focused on education, this technology offers a robust solution for identity verification crucial in healthcare, such as authenticating patients or providers in telemedicine, securing access to electronic health records (EHRs), or ensuring compliance in remote health monitoring platforms. Its mask-robust nature is particularly pertinent in clinical settings where masks are common.

AI Health Application

This AI system for mask-robust identity verification could be applied in healthcare settings to enhance security and operational efficiency. For instance, it could be used for secure access control in hospitals, clinics, or biosecurity laboratories, allowing authorized masked medical personnel or researchers to verify their identity without removing their masks, thereby maintaining sterility and security protocols.

Key Points

  • **Context:** Addresses the critical need for robust identity authentication in online education, particularly in the post-pandemic era where e-learning and mask-wearing are prevalent.
  • **Methodology - Face Detection:** Employs the YOLOv5 network, trained on a proprietary dataset, to detect faces from live camera feeds of students, designed to be robust to mask-wearing.
  • **Methodology - Feature Extraction:** Utilizes an enhanced convolutional neural network, specifically a residual network model, to extract intricate, deeper-level facial features from the detected faces.
  • **Methodology - Verification:** Performs identity authentication by comparing the extracted facial features (via Euclidean distance) against a pre-existing database of student face embeddings.
  • **Objective:** The primary goal is to bolster the security and stability of online education by ensuring accurate and reliable student identity verification.
  • **Technological Foundation:** Leverages advanced deep learning techniques (YOLOv5, Residual Networks) to address the challenges of real-time, mask-robust face verification.
  • **Implication for Online Systems:** Provides a framework for secure authentication that can be adapted for various online platforms requiring identity verification.

Methodology

The system first uses the YOLOv5 network, custom-trained on a proprietary dataset, to detect faces from images captured by online cameras. The detected facial information is then fed into a residual network for deep feature extraction. Finally, the extracted features are compared using Euclidean distance against a database of registered student face embeddings to verify identity.

Abstract

In the contemporary landscape, the fusion of information technology and the rapid advancement of artificial intelligence have ushered school education into a transformative phase characterized by digitization and heightened intelligence. Concurrently, the global paradigm shift caused by the Covid-19 pandemic has catalyzed the evolution of e-learning, accentuating its significance. Amidst these developments, one pivotal facet of the online education paradigm that warrants attention is the authentication of identities within the digital learning sphere. Within this context, our study delves into a solution for online learning authentication, utilizing an enhanced convolutional neural network architecture, specifically the residual network model. By harnessing the power of deep learning, this technological approach aims to galvanize the ongoing progress of online education, while concurrently bolstering its security and stability. Such fortification is imperative in enabling online education to seamlessly align with the swift evolution of the educational landscape. This paper's focal proposition involves the deployment of the YOLOv5 network, meticulously trained on our proprietary dataset. This network is tasked with identifying individuals' faces culled from images captured by students' open online cameras. The resultant facial information is then channeled into the residual network to extract intricate features at a deeper level. Subsequently, a comparative analysis of Euclidean distances against students' face databases is performed, effectively ascertaining the identity of each student.
