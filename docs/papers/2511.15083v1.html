Fourier-KAN-Mamba: A Novel State-Space Equation Approach for Time-Series Anomaly Detection

arXiv ID: 2511.15083v1
Published: 2025-11-19
Authors: Xiancheng Wang, Lin Wang, Rui Wang, Zhibo Zhang, Minghang Zhao
Categories: cs.LG, eess.SP
Relevance Score: 0.85 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces Fourier-KAN-Mamba, a novel hybrid state-space model designed for enhanced time-series anomaly detection, addressing the limitations of direct Mamba application in capturing complex temporal and nonlinear dynamics. The architecture integrates a Fourier layer for multi-scale frequency feature extraction, Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KAN) for superior nonlinear representation, and the Mamba selective state-space model for efficient long-sequence processing, augmented by a temporal gating mechanism. Extensive experiments on MSL, SMAP, and SWaT datasets demonstrate that Fourier-KAN-Mamba significantly outperforms current state-of-the-art approaches in anomaly detection.

This novel approach for real-time anomaly detection in complex time-series data has profound implications for medicine, enabling early and precise identification of deviations in physiological signals, patient monitoring data, and medical device outputs, which can be critical for preventing adverse events and improving patient outcomes.

It can also be used for predictive maintenance of complex medical devices by identifying abnormal operational patterns, contribute to public health by flagging unusual disease incidence patterns (biosecurity), and analyze data from wearable health devices to identify subtle deviations indicative of health issues.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** Mamba-based state-space models face challenges in capturing complex temporal patterns and nonlinear dynamics when directly applied to time-series anomaly detection tasks.
  • **Novel Hybrid Architecture:** Proposes Fourier-KAN-Mamba, a unique integration of a Fourier layer, Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KAN), and the Mamba selective state-space model.
  • **Multi-scale Frequency Feature Extraction:** The Fourier layer is specifically designed to extract multi-scale frequency features, which are crucial for identifying subtle anomalies in time-series data.
  • **Enhanced Nonlinear Representation:** Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KAN) are incorporated to significantly improve the model's capability to learn and represent complex nonlinear relationships within the data.
  • **Temporal Gating Control Mechanism:** A novel temporal gating mechanism is introduced to further refine the model's ability to precisely distinguish between normal and anomalous temporal patterns.
  • **Superior Performance:** Experimental results on benchmark datasets (MSL, SMAP, SWaT) demonstrate that Fourier-KAN-Mamba achieves significantly better performance compared to existing state-of-the-art anomaly detection methods.
  • **Robust Solution:** The proposed model offers a more robust and efficient approach to anomaly detection in long time-series sequences, with broad applicability across various domains.

Methodology

The Fourier-KAN-Mamba model is a hybrid deep learning architecture. It utilizes a Fourier layer to transform time-series data into the frequency domain, extracting multi-scale frequency features. These features are then processed by Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KAN) for their ability to learn complex nonlinear mappings. The core temporal modeling is performed by a Mamba selective state-space model, known for its efficiency in handling long sequences. A temporal gating control mechanism is integrated throughout the architecture to refine the distinction between normal and anomalous temporal patterns, enhancing the model's overall anomaly detection capability.

Key Findings

The primary finding is that the Fourier-KAN-Mamba model achieves significant improvements in time-series anomaly detection performance, outperforming existing state-of-the-art methods. This superior efficacy was rigorously demonstrated through extensive experiments conducted on well-known benchmark datasets: MSL (Mars Science Laboratory), SMAP (Soil Moisture Active Passive satellite), and SWaT (Secure Water Treatment testbed).

Abstract

Time-series anomaly detection plays a critical role in numerous real-world applications, including industrial monitoring and fault diagnosis. Recently, Mamba-based state-space models have shown remarkable efficiency in long-sequence modeling. However, directly applying Mamba to anomaly detection tasks still faces challenges in capturing complex temporal patterns and nonlinear dynamics. In this paper, we propose Fourier-KAN-Mamba, a novel hybrid architecture that integrates Fourier layer, Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KAN), and Mamba selective state-space model. The Fourier layer extracts multi-scale frequency features, KAN enhances nonlinear representation capability, and a temporal gating control mechanism further improves the model's ability to distinguish normal and anomalous patterns. Extensive experiments on MSL, SMAP, and SWaT datasets demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-art approaches.
