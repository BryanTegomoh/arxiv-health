DeepBlip: Estimating Conditional Average Treatment Effects Over Time

Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Haorui Ma, Dennis Frauen, Stefan Feuerriegel

Summary

DeepBlip introduces the first neural framework for Structural Nested Mean Models (SNMMs), enabling the estimation of conditional average treatment effects over time. It overcomes the prior limitation of sequential g-estimation preventing end-to-end training by utilizing a novel double optimization trick for simultaneous, gradient-based learning of all blip functions. This framework integrates sequential neural networks, provides unbiased estimates by correctly adjusting for time-varying confounding, and demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on clinical datasets.

Medical Relevance

This method is crucial for developing personalized, dynamic treatment strategies in clinical settings, allowing clinicians and researchers to precisely understand the incremental benefit of interventions over time while rigorously accounting for evolving patient states and confounding factors.

AI Health Application

DeepBlip is a medical AI application that uses advanced neural networks (LSTMs, transformers) to estimate the causal effects of medical treatments or interventions over time. It can help identify optimal treatment policies for patients by providing unbiased estimates even in complex clinical settings with time-varying confounding, thus improving clinical decision-making and potentially leading to more effective personalized medicine strategies.

Methodology

DeepBlip is a neural framework built upon Structural Nested Mean Models (SNMMs). Its core methodological innovation lies in a novel 'double optimization trick' that transforms the traditional sequential g-estimation into an end-to-end, gradient-based learning process, allowing for simultaneous estimation of blip functions. It integrates state-of-the-art sequential neural networks (e.g., LSTMs, transformers) to model complex temporal dependencies. The framework is designed to provide unbiased estimates by explicitly adjusting for time-varying confounding and incorporates a Neyman-orthogonal loss function to ensure robustness against nuisance model misspecification.

The authors successfully developed the first neural framework for SNMMs, overcoming the long-standing challenge of sequential g-estimation in deep learning contexts. DeepBlip enables simultaneous, end-to-end training of blip functions, produces unbiased causal effect estimates by correctly accounting for time-varying confounding, and exhibits robustness to nuisance model misspecification. Empirically, it achieved state-of-the-art performance across diverse clinical datasets.

Abstract

Structural nested mean models (SNMMs) are a principled approach to estimate the treatment effects over time. A particular strength of SNMMs is to break the joint effect of treatment sequences over time into localized, time-specific ``blip effects''. This decomposition promotes interpretability through the incremental effects and enables the efficient offline evaluation of optimal treatment policies without re-computation. However, neural frameworks for SNMMs are lacking, as their inherently sequential g-estimation scheme prevents end-to-end, gradient-based training. Here, we propose DeepBlip, the first neural framework for SNMMs, which overcomes this limitation with a novel double optimization trick to enable simultaneous learning of all blip functions. Our DeepBlip seamlessly integrates sequential neural networks like LSTMs or transformers to capture complex temporal dependencies. By design, our method correctly adjusts for time-varying confounding to produce unbiased estimates, and its Neyman-orthogonal loss function ensures robustness to nuisance model misspecification. Finally, we evaluate our DeepBlip across various clinical datasets, where it achieves state-of-the-art performance.
