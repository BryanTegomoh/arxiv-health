Hybrid Modeling of Photoplethysmography for Non-invasive Monitoring of Cardiovascular Parameters

Published: 2025-11-18

Summary

This paper addresses the challenge of non-invasively monitoring crucial cardiovascular biomarkers like stroke volume (SV) and cardiac output (CO), which traditionally require invasive arterial pressure waveform (APW) measurements. It proposes a novel hybrid model that leverages photoplethysmography (PPG) signals by combining a conditional variational autoencoder trained on real paired PPG-APW data with a conditional density estimator trained on labeled simulated APW data. The study demonstrates that this approach can effectively detect fluctuations and monitor temporal changes in SV and CO, outperforming supervised baselines.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a pathway for continuous, non-invasive monitoring of vital cardiovascular parameters, which currently necessitate invasive procedures, thereby enhancing patient safety, comfort, and the accessibility of advanced hemodynamic assessment in clinical settings.

AI Health Application

The research proposes a hybrid AI model combining a conditional variational autoencoder and a conditional density estimator to non-invasively predict fundamental cardiac biomarkers (stroke volume, cardiac output) from photoplethysmography (PPG) signals. This AI application aims to provide continuous, non-invasive cardiovascular monitoring, contributing to precision health by overcoming the limitations of invasive measurements and scarce annotated data.

Methodology

The study employs a hybrid modeling approach. It combines a Conditional Variational Autoencoder (CVAE) which is trained on *paired clinical PPG-APW data* to learn representations of cardiovascular dynamics from real signals. This is coupled with a Conditional Density Estimator (CDE) that is trained on *labeled simulated APW segments* to accurately estimate specific cardiac biomarkers (Stroke Volume and Cardiac Output). This methodology effectively leverages the strengths of limited real-world annotated data with abundant simulated data to tackle the data scarcity problem.

Key Findings

The proposed hybrid model successfully demonstrated its capability to detect fluctuations in fundamental cardiovascular parameters, specifically Stroke Volume (SV) and Cardiac Output (CO). A crucial finding is that the model *outperformed a supervised baseline* in monitoring temporal changes in these biomarkers, indicating superior performance for dynamic, continuous assessment.

Clinical Impact

This research has the potential to significantly transform cardiovascular monitoring by enabling non-invasive, continuous assessment of Stroke Volume and Cardiac Output. It could reduce the reliance on invasive arterial catheters, leading to fewer patient complications, lower healthcare costs, and broader application of advanced hemodynamic monitoring in critical care, perioperative settings, and remote patient management, fostering more personalized and proactive precision health strategies.

Limitations

The primary limitation addressed by this work is the scarcity of *annotated* PPG measurements in real-world clinical data, which hinders the development of purely data-driven supervised models for biomarker prediction. The reliance on hemodynamic simulations for biomarker labeling introduces assumptions about the accuracy and physiological realism of the simulated data.

Future Directions

While not explicitly stated in the abstract, implied future directions include validating the model on larger, more diverse patient cohorts, exploring its real-time implementation capabilities, and extending its application to predict additional cardiovascular biomarkers or integrate with other physiological signals.

Abstract

Continuous cardiovascular monitoring can play a key role in precision health. However, some fundamental cardiac biomarkers of interest, including stroke volume and cardiac output, require invasive measurements, e.g., arterial pressure waveforms (APW). As a non-invasive alternative, photoplethysmography (PPG) measurements are routinely collected in hospital settings. Unfortunately, the prediction of key cardiac biomarkers from PPG instead of APW remains an open challenge, further complicated by the scarcity of annotated PPG measurements. As a solution, we propose a hybrid approach that uses hemodynamic simulations and unlabeled clinical data to estimate cardiovascular biomarkers directly from PPG signals. Our hybrid model combines a conditional variational autoencoder trained on paired PPG-APW data with a conditional density estimator of cardiac biomarkers trained on labeled simulated APW segments. As a key result, our experiments demonstrate that the proposed approach can detect fluctuations of cardiac output and stroke volume and outperform a supervised baseline in monitoring temporal changes in these biomarkers.
