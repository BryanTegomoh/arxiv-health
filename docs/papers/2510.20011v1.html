Summary

This paper introduces Online Label Smoothing (OLS), a dynamic approach to enhance predictive confidence and accuracy in deep learning models for medical image classification. By adjusting soft labels based on the model's real-time prediction patterns, OLS consistently improves classification accuracy and calibration compared to traditional methods, leading to more trustworthy AI systems in healthcare.

Medical Relevance

Overconfident AI predictions in medical imaging can lead to misdiagnoses or inappropriate clinical decisions, posing significant risks to patient safety. This research directly addresses this critical issue by improving model reliability and calibration, which is essential for integrating AI safely and effectively into diagnostic workflows.

AI Health Application

Improving the accuracy, calibration, and trustworthiness of AI models, specifically convolutional neural networks, used for classification tasks within medical imaging. This aims to make AI systems more reliable for clinical decision support, diagnosis, and screening in healthcare settings.

Key Points

  • Deep learning models often produce overconfident predictions in medical imaging, undermining reliability in critical healthcare settings.
  • Traditional label smoothing (LS) is limited as it treats all non-target classes equally, failing to consider inter-class relationships.
  • Online Label Smoothing (OLS) is proposed as a dynamic method that continuously adjusts soft labels during training based on the model's evolving prediction patterns.
  • The effectiveness of OLS was evaluated on the large-scale RadImageNet dataset using three widely-used architectures: ResNet-50, MobileNetV2, and VGG-19.
  • OLS consistently outperformed standard training methods, including hard labels, conventional label smoothing, and teacher-free knowledge distillation, in both Top-1 and Top-5 classification accuracy.
  • Beyond accuracy gains, OLS led to more compact and well-separated feature embeddings, indicating improved representation learning and better data organization.
  • The findings suggest OLS significantly enhances predictive performance and calibration, making AI systems more reliable and trustworthy for medical imaging applications.

Methodology

The study employed Online Label Smoothing (OLS), a dynamic technique for adjusting soft labels during the deep learning model's training. Unlike static label smoothing, OLS adapts label values based on the model's real-time prediction outputs. This method was evaluated using three prominent Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) architectures (ResNet-50, MobileNetV2, VGG-19) on the large-scale RadImageNet dataset. Performance was assessed by comparing OLS against hard labels, conventional label smoothing, and teacher-free knowledge distillation, focusing on Top-1/Top-5 classification accuracy and the quality of learned feature embeddings.

Key Findings

Online Label Smoothing (OLS) consistently improved both Top-1 and Top-5 classification accuracy across all tested architectures compared to hard labels, conventional label smoothing, and teacher-free knowledge distillation. Furthermore, OLS resulted in the generation of more compact and well-separated feature embeddings, indicating enhanced representation learning. These findings collectively demonstrate that OLS not only strengthens predictive performance but also significantly improves model calibration, leading to more reliable and less overconfident predictions.

Clinical Impact

By delivering more accurate and calibrated predictions, OLS can enhance the reliability of AI tools used in medical imaging, leading to more confident and precise diagnostic support for clinicians. This improved trustworthiness can accelerate the adoption of AI in critical healthcare settings, potentially reducing diagnostic errors, optimizing patient management, and ultimately improving patient outcomes.

Abstract

Deep learning models, especially convolutional neural networks, have achieved impressive results in medical image classification. However, these models often produce overconfident predictions, which can undermine their reliability in critical healthcare settings. While traditional label smoothing offers a simple way to reduce such overconfidence, it fails to consider relationships between classes by treating all non-target classes equally. In this study, we explore the use of Online Label Smoothing (OLS), a dynamic approach that adjusts soft labels throughout training based on the model's own prediction patterns. We evaluate OLS on the large-scale RadImageNet dataset using three widely used architectures: ResNet-50, MobileNetV2, and VGG-19. Our results show that OLS consistently improves both Top-1 and Top-5 classification accuracy compared to standard training methods, including hard labels, conventional label smoothing, and teacher-free knowledge distillation. In addition to accuracy gains, OLS leads to more compact and well-separated feature embeddings, indicating improved representation learning. These findings suggest that OLS not only strengthens predictive performance but also enhances calibration, making it a practical and effective solution for developing trustworthy AI systems in the medical imaging domain.
