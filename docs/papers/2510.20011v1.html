Summary

This paper introduces Online Label Smoothing (OLS), a dynamic method to improve predictive confidence and accuracy in medical image classification by adjusting soft labels based on the model's own prediction patterns during training. OLS consistently enhances Top-1 and Top-5 accuracy, fosters better feature representation, and improves calibration across various architectures on the RadImageNet dataset, making AI systems more trustworthy in healthcare.

This research directly addresses this by enhancing predictive accuracy and calibration, making AI tools safer, more reliable, and ultimately more valuable for patient care in medical diagnostics.

Methodology

The study explores Online Label Smoothing (OLS), a dynamic approach that adjusts soft labels throughout training based on the deep learning model's own prediction patterns, contrasting with static conventional label smoothing. This method was evaluated on the large-scale RadImageNet dataset, utilizing three prominent Convolutional Neural Network architectures: ResNet-50, MobileNetV2, and VGG-19. Performance was measured using Top-1 and Top-5 classification accuracy and qualitative analysis of feature embeddings, with comparisons against hard labels, conventional label smoothing, and teacher-free knowledge distillation.

Key Findings

OLS consistently improved both Top-1 and Top-5 classification accuracy across all evaluated architectures (ResNet-50, MobileNetV2, VGG-19) on the RadImageNet dataset. It outperformed hard labels, conventional label smoothing, and teacher-free knowledge distillation. Furthermore, OLS resulted in more compact and well-separated feature embeddings, indicating superior representation learning. These findings collectively demonstrate enhanced predictive performance and improved model calibration.

Clinical Impact

By significantly improving predictive accuracy and confidence (calibration) in medical image classification, OLS can lead to more robust and reliable AI-powered diagnostic tools. This enhanced trustworthiness can reduce diagnostic errors, support clinicians in making more precise and confident decisions, and accelerate the adoption of AI in critical healthcare workflows, ultimately leading to better patient outcomes and more efficient healthcare delivery.

Abstract

Deep learning models, especially convolutional neural networks, have achieved impressive results in medical image classification. However, these models often produce overconfident predictions, which can undermine their reliability in critical healthcare settings. While traditional label smoothing offers a simple way to reduce such overconfidence, it fails to consider relationships between classes by treating all non-target classes equally. In this study, we explore the use of Online Label Smoothing (OLS), a dynamic approach that adjusts soft labels throughout training based on the model's own prediction patterns. We evaluate OLS on the large-scale RadImageNet dataset using three widely used architectures: ResNet-50, MobileNetV2, and VGG-19. Our results show that OLS consistently improves both Top-1 and Top-5 classification accuracy compared to standard training methods, including hard labels, conventional label smoothing, and teacher-free knowledge distillation. In addition to accuracy gains, OLS leads to more compact and well-separated feature embeddings, indicating improved representation learning. These findings suggest that OLS not only strengthens predictive performance but also enhances calibration, making it a practical and effective solution for developing trustworthy AI systems in the medical imaging domain.
