Summary

This paper introduces Online Label Smoothing (OLS) to mitigate overconfident predictions in deep learning models for medical image classification. OLS dynamically adjusts soft labels throughout training based on the model's own prediction patterns, leading to improved classification accuracy, enhanced calibration, and more robust feature representations.

Medical Relevance

Improving predictive confidence and calibration in medical imaging AI is paramount for clinical safety and accurate diagnosis, as overconfident or erroneous model outputs can directly impact patient care and lead to misinformed clinical decisions.

AI Health Application

The AI application is to improve the accuracy, calibration, and trustworthiness of deep learning models used for analyzing medical images. This would lead to more reliable AI systems for tasks such as automated diagnosis, abnormality detection, or disease progression monitoring in clinical settings, thereby enhancing patient care and operational efficiency in healthcare.

Key Points

  • Deep learning models, while highly accurate in medical imaging, often suffer from overconfidence, undermining their reliability in critical healthcare settings.
  • Traditional label smoothing (LS) is limited as it treats all non-target classes equally, failing to capture inter-class relationships.
  • Online Label Smoothing (OLS) is proposed as a dynamic approach that adjusts soft labels throughout training, adapting based on the model's evolving prediction patterns.
  • The effectiveness of OLS was evaluated on the large-scale RadImageNet dataset using three widely used CNN architectures: ResNet-50, MobileNetV2, and VGG-19.
  • OLS consistently improved both Top-1 and Top-5 classification accuracy compared to standard hard labels, conventional label smoothing, and teacher-free knowledge distillation.
  • Beyond accuracy, OLS also led to the generation of more compact and well-separated feature embeddings, indicating superior representation learning capabilities.
  • These findings collectively demonstrate that OLS not only strengthens predictive performance but also significantly enhances model calibration, fostering more trustworthy AI systems in medical imaging.

Methodology

The study implemented Online Label Smoothing (OLS), a dynamic method for adjusting soft labels during the training of deep learning models. This approach was evaluated on the RadImageNet dataset, utilizing three prominent convolutional neural network architectures: ResNet-50, MobileNetV2, and VGG-19. Performance was benchmarked against hard labels, conventional label smoothing, and teacher-free knowledge distillation, with evaluation focusing on Top-1 and Top-5 classification accuracy, as well as the quality of feature embeddings.

Key Findings

OLS consistently achieved superior Top-1 and Top-5 classification accuracy across all tested architectures compared to baseline methods. It also resulted in more compact and well-separated feature embeddings, signifying improved representation learning, and demonstrably enhanced model calibration by reducing overconfidence in predictions.

Clinical Impact

By enhancing both the accuracy and reliability (calibration) of AI models in medical imaging, OLS has the potential to foster more trustworthy diagnostic tools. This could lead to more confident and precise clinical decisions by radiologists and other healthcare professionals, ultimately improving patient outcomes and reducing the risks associated with uncertain or overconfident AI-driven diagnoses.

Abstract

Deep learning models, especially convolutional neural networks, have achieved impressive results in medical image classification. However, these models often produce overconfident predictions, which can undermine their reliability in critical healthcare settings. While traditional label smoothing offers a simple way to reduce such overconfidence, it fails to consider relationships between classes by treating all non-target classes equally. In this study, we explore the use of Online Label Smoothing (OLS), a dynamic approach that adjusts soft labels throughout training based on the model's own prediction patterns. We evaluate OLS on the large-scale RadImageNet dataset using three widely used architectures: ResNet-50, MobileNetV2, and VGG-19. Our results show that OLS consistently improves both Top-1 and Top-5 classification accuracy compared to standard training methods, including hard labels, conventional label smoothing, and teacher-free knowledge distillation. In addition to accuracy gains, OLS leads to more compact and well-separated feature embeddings, indicating improved representation learning. These findings suggest that OLS not only strengthens predictive performance but also enhances calibration, making it a practical and effective solution for developing trustworthy AI systems in the medical imaging domain.
