Summary

SCORE (Segmentation COrrection from Regional Evaluations) introduces a novel weakly supervised framework for refining anatomical segmentations, addressing the limitations of existing methods that require extensive user interaction or dense annotations. It leverages 'light feedback' in the form of region-wise quality scores and over/under-segmentation error labels to significantly improve initial automated predictions. Demonstrated on humerus CT scans, SCORE achieves performance comparable to fully supervised methods while drastically reducing supervision requirements and annotation time.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and consistent anatomical segmentation is fundamental for medical diagnosis, treatment planning (e.g., surgery, radiation therapy), and disease monitoring. SCORE's ability to refine segmentations with minimal expert input can significantly improve the reliability of automated tools, reduce clinician workload, and accelerate clinical workflows, ultimately leading to better patient outcomes and more efficient healthcare delivery.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of a resource-efficient, weakly supervised machine learning framework (SCORE) for the automated refinement of medical image segmentations. This aims to improve the accuracy of initial AI-driven segmentation models for anatomical regions in medical scans (e.g., CT scans of the humerus), thereby enhancing diagnostic capabilities, treatment planning, and overall efficiency in clinical settings by reducing the need for extensive manual correction or supervision.

Key Points

  • Automated segmentations, even from advanced foundation models, often fall short of clinical accuracy standards, necessitating refinement.
  • Current segmentation refinement strategies are resource-intensive, requiring either significant manual interaction or fully supervised, pixel-wise ground truth segmentations for training.
  • SCORE proposes a weakly supervised framework that learns to refine mask predictions using only 'light feedback' during training, making it highly resource-efficient.
  • The core innovation is a novel loss function that utilizes region-wise quality scores and specific over/under-segmentation error labels instead of dense annotations.
  • The framework was successfully applied to humerus CT scans, where it considerably improved initial segmentation predictions generated by TotalSegmentator.
  • SCORE achieves a performance level on par with existing, more heavily supervised or interactive, refinement methods.
  • A key benefit of SCORE is the substantial reduction in supervision requirements and expert annotation time, making it practical for real-world clinical deployment.

Methodology

SCORE is a weakly supervised deep learning framework. Its central methodological innovation lies in its novel loss function, designed to learn from 'light feedback' rather than dense pixel-level annotations. This feedback comprises two types of regional evaluations: region-wise quality scores (e.g., indicating the overall quality of a segmented region) and specific labels for over-segmentation or under-segmentation errors within a region. By optimizing this loss, SCORE trains a model to correct and refine initial mask predictions generated by existing automated segmentation tools (e.g., foundation models like TotalSegmentator). The training process effectively guides the model to minimize these regional errors, leading to improved segmentation accuracy.

Key Findings

The primary finding is that SCORE considerably improves the accuracy of initial automated segmentations, specifically demonstrated on humerus CT scans, where it refined predictions from TotalSegmentator. Crucially, SCORE achieved this enhanced performance at a level comparable to existing segmentation refinement methods that typically demand significantly more supervision (e.g., dense ground truth masks or extensive user interactions). The study highlights the framework's capability to deliver high-quality refinement while drastically reducing the resource burden in terms of training data annotation time and expert involvement.

Clinical Impact

SCORE holds significant clinical impact by making high-quality anatomical segmentation more accessible and less labor-intensive. It can accelerate the integration of AI-powered segmentation tools into routine clinical practice by mitigating the need for extensive manual correction or costly dense annotations for training. This could lead to faster and more consistent reporting, improved pre-surgical planning, and more reliable quantitative analysis in various diagnostic and interventional procedures, particularly benefiting departments with limited resources or expertise for manual annotation.

Abstract

Delineating anatomical regions is a key task in medical image analysis. Manual segmentation achieves high accuracy but is labor-intensive and prone to variability, thus prompting the development of automated approaches. Recently, a breadth of foundation models has enabled automated segmentations across diverse anatomies and imaging modalities, but these may not always meet the clinical accuracy standards. While segmentation refinement strategies can improve performance, current methods depend on heavy user interactions or require fully supervised segmentations for training. Here, we present SCORE (Segmentation COrrection from Regional Evaluations), a weakly supervised framework that learns to refine mask predictions only using light feedback during training. Specifically, instead of relying on dense training image annotations, SCORE introduces a novel loss that leverages region-wise quality scores and over/under-segmentation error labels. We demonstrate SCORE on humerus CT scans, where it considerably improves initial predictions from TotalSegmentator, and achieves performance on par with existing refinement methods, while greatly reducing their supervision requirements and annotation time. Our code is available at: https://gitlab.inria.fr/adelangl/SCORE.
