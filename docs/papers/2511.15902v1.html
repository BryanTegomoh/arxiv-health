EEG Emotion Recognition Through Deep Learning

arXiv ID: 2511.15902v1
Published: 2025-11-19
Authors: Roman Dolgopolyi, Antonis Chatzipanagiotou
Categories: eess.SP, cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper presents an advanced CNN-Transformer deep learning model for EEG-based emotion recognition, classifying positive, neutral, and negative states with 91% testing accuracy. The model significantly reduces hardware requirements by effectively using only 5 EEG electrodes, enabling accessible and affordable at-home use for continuous emotional monitoring. This innovation holds promise for transforming mental health diagnostics and interventions, particularly in settings where traditional behavioral cues are difficult to interpret.

Medical Relevance

This research offers an accessible, affordable, and highly accurate method for continuous emotional monitoring via EEG, which is critical for objective mental health diagnostics and interventions, especially in scenarios where traditional behavioral cues are unreliable or unavailable.

AI Health Application

The AI model (CNN-Transformer) is applied to recognize emotional states from EEG signals. Its application is to enable continuous, passive emotional monitoring, particularly beneficial in clinical or caregiving settings where traditional behavioral cues are diminished or difficult to interpret. This AI application aims to transform mental health diagnostics and interventions, offering a more accessible and computationally efficient method for emotional state assessment, including for at-home health monitoring.

Key Points

  • A CNN-Transformer deep learning architecture was developed for emotion recognition from EEG brain wave signals, distinguishing among positive, neutral, and negative emotional states.
  • The model achieved a high testing accuracy of 91%, outperforming traditional models such as SVM, DNN, and Logistic Regression.
  • Training was conducted on a custom dataset of 1,455 EEG samples, merged from SEED, SEED-FRA, and SEED-GER repositories, forming one of the largest and most culturally diverse collections.
  • The model allows for a significant reduction in EEG apparatus requirements, leveraging only 5 electrodes out of 62.
  • This reduction enables the deployment of affordable, consumer-grade EEG headsets for accessible, at-home use, while also requiring less computational power.
  • The advancement sets the groundwork for future exploration into mood changes induced by media content consumption.
  • Integration into medical, wellness, and home-health platforms can facilitate continuous, passive emotional monitoring, particularly beneficial in clinical or caregiving settings where traditional behavioral cues are diminished or difficult to interpret.

Methodology

The study involved developing an advanced emotion classification model based on a CNN-Transformer deep learning architecture. This model was trained on a custom dataset comprising 1,455 EEG samples, created by merging data from SEED, SEED-FRA, and SEED-GER repositories, with recordings labeled for positive, neutral, and negative emotional states. A key part of the methodology also focused on optimizing the model's performance while significantly reducing the number of required EEG electrodes from 62 to just 5.

Key Findings

The developed CNN-Transformer model achieved a high testing accuracy of 91% in classifying positive, neutral, and negative emotional states from EEG signals, significantly outperforming traditional machine learning and deep neural network models. A pivotal finding is the model's capability to operate effectively with only 5 EEG electrodes, demonstrating the feasibility of using more affordable, consumer-grade EEG headsets for accessible, at-home emotional monitoring with reduced computational requirements.

Clinical Impact

This technology has the potential to transform mental health diagnostics and interventions by enabling continuous, passive emotional monitoring through affordable and accessible consumer-grade EEG devices. It is particularly impactful in clinical or caregiving settings where traditional observational cues like facial expressions or vocal tone are diminished or difficult to interpret, providing an objective and non-invasive means for emotional assessment and early detection of mood changes.

Limitations

The abstract primarily highlights the groundwork laid by the research, implicitly suggesting that broader applications, such as a full exploration of mood changes induced by media content consumption, remain areas for future research rather than fully addressed in this paper. No explicit current limitations of the model itself are stated beyond this.

Future Directions

Future research will specifically explore mood changes induced by media content consumption, an area identified as currently underresearched. The advancements also set the stage for broader integration into medical, wellness, and home-health platforms to enable continuous, passive emotional monitoring.

Abstract

An advanced emotion classification model was developed using a CNN-Transformer architecture for emotion recognition from EEG brain wave signals, effectively distinguishing among three emotional states, positive, neutral and negative. The model achieved a testing accuracy of 91%, outperforming traditional models such as SVM, DNN, and Logistic Regression. Training was conducted on a custom dataset created by merging data from SEED, SEED-FRA, and SEED-GER repositories, comprising 1,455 samples with EEG recordings labeled according to emotional states. The combined dataset represents one of the largest and most culturally diverse collections available. Additionally, the model allows for the reduction of the requirements of the EEG apparatus, by leveraging only 5 electrodes of the 62. This reduction demonstrates the feasibility of deploying a more affordable consumer-grade EEG headset, thereby enabling accessible, at-home use, while also requiring less computational power. This advancement sets the groundwork for future exploration into mood changes induced by media content consumption, an area that remains underresearched. Integration into medical, wellness, and home-health platforms could enable continuous, passive emotional monitoring, particularly beneficial in clinical or caregiving settings where traditional behavioral cues, such as facial expressions or vocal tone, are diminished, restricted, or difficult to interpret, thus potentially transforming mental health diagnostics and interventions...
