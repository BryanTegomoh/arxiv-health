EEG Emotion Recognition Through Deep Learning

arXiv ID: 2511.15902v1
Published: 2025-11-19
Authors: Roman Dolgopolyi, Antonis Chatzipanagiotou
Categories: eess.SP, cs.LG
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a novel CNN-Transformer deep learning model for EEG-based emotion recognition, capable of classifying positive, neutral, and negative emotional states with 91% accuracy. A key innovation is its ability to operate effectively with only 5 out of 62 EEG electrodes, significantly reducing hardware requirements and enabling accessible, at-home emotional monitoring.

Medical Relevance

This research offers a highly accurate, non-invasive, and cost-effective method for continuous emotional monitoring via EEG. It addresses a crucial need in mental health by providing objective emotional data, particularly valuable in clinical and caregiving settings where traditional behavioral cues are often diminished or difficult to interpret, thus enabling more timely and effective diagnostics and interventions.

AI Health Application

The AI (CNN-Transformer) is applied to process EEG signals for continuous, passive emotional monitoring. This enables the detection of emotional states (positive, neutral, negative) for mental health diagnostics and interventions in medical, wellness, and home-health platforms. It specifically targets situations where traditional behavioral cues are insufficient, making it valuable for clinical, caregiving, and accessible at-home use.

Key Points

  • Developed an advanced emotion classification model using a CNN-Transformer architecture for EEG signal analysis.
  • Achieved a high testing accuracy of 91% in distinguishing between positive, neutral, and negative emotional states.
  • Outperformed traditional machine learning models including SVM, DNN, and Logistic Regression.
  • Trained on a custom, large, and culturally diverse dataset created by merging SEED, SEED-FRA, and SEED-GER repositories, comprising 1,455 EEG samples.
  • Significantly reduced EEG apparatus requirements, leveraging only 5 electrodes out of 62, enabling consumer-grade, affordable headsets.
  • Demonstrates feasibility for accessible, at-home use and requires less computational power.
  • Paves the way for continuous, passive emotional monitoring in medical, wellness, and home-health platforms, especially beneficial where traditional behavioral cues are compromised.

Methodology

The study involved developing a CNN-Transformer deep learning architecture for classifying emotional states (positive, neutral, negative) from EEG brain wave signals. Training was conducted on a custom dataset of 1,455 samples, created by merging data from SEED, SEED-FRA, and SEED-GER repositories, ensuring a large and culturally diverse collection. The model was designed and optimized to function effectively even with a significantly reduced number of EEG electrodes (5 out of 62).

Key Findings

The CNN-Transformer model achieved a high testing accuracy of 91% in classifying positive, neutral, and negative emotional states, outperforming traditional models (SVM, DNN, Logistic Regression). A significant finding is the model's ability to maintain high performance while utilizing only 5 of the 62 available EEG electrodes, demonstrating a major reduction in hardware requirements and computational power needed.

Clinical Impact

This advancement enables the deployment of more affordable, consumer-grade EEG headsets for accessible, at-home, continuous, and passive emotional monitoring. This is particularly impactful in clinical or caregiving environments where traditional behavioral cues (e.g., facial expressions, vocal tone) are difficult to interpret or restricted. It holds the potential to transform mental health diagnostics and interventions by providing objective, real-time emotional data, facilitating earlier detection of mood changes and personalized care.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state specific limitations of the developed model. It highlights that 'mood changes induced by media content consumption' is an area that 'remains underresearched,' indicating a broader research gap rather than a direct limitation of the current work.

Future Directions

Future research directions include exploring mood changes induced by media content consumption, an area identified as underresearched. The advancement also lays the groundwork for integration into medical, wellness, and home-health platforms to enable continuous, passive emotional monitoring.

Abstract

An advanced emotion classification model was developed using a CNN-Transformer architecture for emotion recognition from EEG brain wave signals, effectively distinguishing among three emotional states, positive, neutral and negative. The model achieved a testing accuracy of 91%, outperforming traditional models such as SVM, DNN, and Logistic Regression. Training was conducted on a custom dataset created by merging data from SEED, SEED-FRA, and SEED-GER repositories, comprising 1,455 samples with EEG recordings labeled according to emotional states. The combined dataset represents one of the largest and most culturally diverse collections available. Additionally, the model allows for the reduction of the requirements of the EEG apparatus, by leveraging only 5 electrodes of the 62. This reduction demonstrates the feasibility of deploying a more affordable consumer-grade EEG headset, thereby enabling accessible, at-home use, while also requiring less computational power. This advancement sets the groundwork for future exploration into mood changes induced by media content consumption, an area that remains underresearched. Integration into medical, wellness, and home-health platforms could enable continuous, passive emotional monitoring, particularly beneficial in clinical or caregiving settings where traditional behavioral cues, such as facial expressions or vocal tone, are diminished, restricted, or difficult to interpret, thus potentially transforming mental health diagnostics and interventions...
