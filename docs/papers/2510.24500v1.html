MIMIC-Sepsis: A Curated Benchmark for Modeling and Learning from Sepsis Trajectories in the ICU

Summary

MIMIC-Sepsis introduces a carefully curated benchmark dataset and framework derived from MIMIC-IV, specifically designed for reproducible modeling of sepsis trajectories in Intensive Care Units (ICUs). It addresses limitations of existing research by providing time-aligned clinical and comprehensive treatment data for 35,239 patients, demonstrating that incorporating treatment variables significantly enhances predictive model performance, particularly for Transformer architectures.

Medical Relevance

Sepsis remains a leading cause of ICU mortality, and this resource provides a critical, reproducible platform for developing and evaluating advanced predictive models. By including comprehensive treatment data, it enables the creation of more clinically relevant and accurate models that can potentially improve early diagnosis, prognosis, and personalized treatment strategies in critical care.

AI Health Application

The research introduces a curated dataset and benchmark tasks (MIMIC-Sepsis) specifically designed to train and evaluate AI/machine learning models for predicting key clinical outcomes in sepsis patients. These applications include early mortality prediction, length-of-stay estimation, and shock onset classification, with the aim of improving diagnostic accuracy, prognostic capabilities, and guiding treatment strategies in critical care settings using computational methods.

Methodology

The study derived MIMIC-Sepsis from the MIMIC-IV database, applying a transparent preprocessing pipeline based on Sepsis-3 criteria for cohort identification and diagnosis. It included structured imputation strategies and standardized the collection of key time-aligned treatment variables (vasopressors, fluids, mechanical ventilation, antibiotics). This curated data forms a benchmark framework for evaluating predictive models on tasks like early mortality prediction, length-of-stay estimation, and shock onset classification, with empirical testing involving Transformer-based architectures among others.

Key Findings

The primary finding is that the incorporation of comprehensive treatment variables (e.g., vasopressors, fluids, mechanical ventilation, antibiotics) into predictive models substantially improves their performance across benchmark tasks such as early mortality prediction, length-of-stay estimation, and shock onset classification. This improvement was particularly notable for Transformer-based architectures, highlighting the value of detailed, time-aligned clinical intervention data.

Clinical Impact

This curated benchmark dataset and framework can accelerate the development of more accurate and clinically relevant machine learning models for sepsis. Such advanced models could empower intensivists with better tools for earlier identification of sepsis and septic shock, more precise prognostication, and optimization of personalized treatment decisions, potentially leading to reduced patient mortality and improved outcomes in the ICU.

Abstract

Sepsis is a leading cause of mortality in intensive care units (ICUs), yet existing research often relies on outdated datasets, non-reproducible preprocessing pipelines, and limited coverage of clinical interventions. We introduce MIMIC-Sepsis, a curated cohort and benchmark framework derived from the MIMIC-IV database, designed to support reproducible modeling of sepsis trajectories. Our cohort includes 35,239 ICU patients with time-aligned clinical variables and standardized treatment data, including vasopressors, fluids, mechanical ventilation and antibiotics. We describe a transparent preprocessing pipeline-based on Sepsis-3 criteria, structured imputation strategies, and treatment inclusion-and release it alongside benchmark tasks focused on early mortality prediction, length-of-stay estimation, and shock onset classification. Empirical results demonstrate that incorporating treatment variables substantially improves model performance, particularly for Transformer-based architectures. MIMIC-Sepsis serves as a robust platform for evaluating predictive and sequential models in critical care research.
