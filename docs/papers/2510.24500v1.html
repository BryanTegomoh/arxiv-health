MIMIC-Sepsis: A Curated Benchmark for Modeling and Learning from Sepsis Trajectories in the ICU

arXiv ID: 2510.24500v1
Published: 2025-10-28
Authors: Yong Huang, Zhongqi Yang, Amir Rahmani
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

MIMIC-Sepsis introduces a new, curated benchmark dataset and framework derived from MIMIC-IV, designed for reproducible modeling of sepsis trajectories in the ICU. It addresses limitations of outdated datasets and limited clinical intervention coverage in prior research. A key finding is that incorporating standardized treatment variables significantly improves the performance of predictive models, particularly Transformer-based architectures, across various sepsis-related tasks.

Medical Relevance

Sepsis is a leading cause of mortality in ICUs; this high-quality, reproducible dataset with detailed treatment data is crucial for developing advanced, clinically relevant machine learning models. Such models can facilitate earlier diagnosis, more precise interventions, and improved patient outcomes by leveraging real-world treatment responses.

AI Health Application

This research applies AI (specifically predictive and sequential models, including Transformer-based architectures) to real-world clinical data (MIMIC-IV database) to create a benchmark for improving prognostic and diagnostic capabilities in sepsis. The AI applications aim to predict early mortality, estimate length-of-stay, and classify shock onset in ICU patients with sepsis, thereby potentially informing clinical decision-making and improving patient outcomes in critical care.

  • Addresses critical gaps in existing sepsis research, including reliance on outdated datasets, non-reproducible preprocessing, and insufficient coverage of clinical interventions.
  • Introduces MIMIC-Sepsis, a curated cohort and benchmark framework based on the MIMIC-IV database, facilitating reproducible sepsis trajectory modeling.
  • The cohort comprises 35,239 ICU patients with meticulously time-aligned clinical variables and standardized treatment data (e.g., vasopressors, fluids, mechanical ventilation, antibiotics).
  • Features a transparent preprocessing pipeline grounded in Sepsis-3 criteria, structured imputation strategies, and comprehensive treatment inclusion.

Methodology

The authors developed MIMIC-Sepsis by curating a cohort of 35,239 ICU patients from the MIMIC-IV database. Their methodology involved a transparent preprocessing pipeline, applying Sepsis-3 criteria for patient identification, implementing structured imputation strategies for missing data, and standardizing the inclusion of critical time-aligned treatment variables (e.g., vasopressors, fluids, mechanical ventilation, antibiotics). This curated data was then used to establish benchmark tasks for early mortality prediction, length-of-stay estimation, and shock onset classification, on which various predictive and sequential models, including Transformer-based architectures, were empirically evaluated.

Key Findings

The primary key finding is that incorporating standardized treatment variables (such as vasopressors, fluids, mechanical ventilation, and antibiotics) into predictive models leads to a substantial improvement in performance. This enhancement was particularly pronounced for Transformer-based architectures across benchmark tasks like early mortality prediction, length-of-stay estimation, and shock onset classification.

Clinical Impact

MIMIC-Sepsis has the potential to significantly advance the development of more accurate and clinically actionable machine learning models for sepsis management. By providing a rich, reproducible dataset that includes treatment variables, it can enable the creation of tools for earlier and more precise identification of sepsis, better prediction of disease progression, and more personalized treatment strategies in the ICU, ultimately aiming to reduce sepsis-related mortality and optimize resource utilization.

Abstract

Sepsis is a leading cause of mortality in intensive care units (ICUs), yet existing research often relies on outdated datasets, non-reproducible preprocessing pipelines, and limited coverage of clinical interventions. We introduce MIMIC-Sepsis, a curated cohort and benchmark framework derived from the MIMIC-IV database, designed to support reproducible modeling of sepsis trajectories. Our cohort includes 35,239 ICU patients with time-aligned clinical variables and standardized treatment data, including vasopressors, fluids, mechanical ventilation and antibiotics. We describe a transparent preprocessing pipeline-based on Sepsis-3 criteria, structured imputation strategies, and treatment inclusion-and release it alongside benchmark tasks focused on early mortality prediction, length-of-stay estimation, and shock onset classification. Empirical results demonstrate that incorporating treatment variables substantially improves model performance, particularly for Transformer-based architectures. MIMIC-Sepsis serves as a robust platform for evaluating predictive and sequential models in critical care research.
