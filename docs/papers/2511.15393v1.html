EVA-Net: Interpretable Brain Age Prediction via Continuous Aging Prototypes from EEG

arXiv ID: 2511.15393v1
Published: 2025-11-19
Authors: Kunyu Zhang, Mingxuan Wang, Xiangjie Shi, Haoxing Xu, Chao Zhang
Categories: cs.LG
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

EVA-Net is a novel framework designed for interpretable brain age prediction from EEG, addressing the challenges of imperfect medical data and the black-box nature of existing models. It recasts brain age as an anomaly detection problem, leveraging a Transformer for long EEG sequences, a Variational Information Bottleneck for robust representations, and a continuous prototype network to explicitly learn the normative healthy aging manifold. The model achieves state-of-the-art accuracy in healthy subjects and accurately identifies deviations in MCI and AD patients through elevated brain-age gaps and a novel Prototype Alignment Error.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and interpretable brain age prediction from readily available EEG is a key indicator of brain health. This framework provides a practical tool for early identification of deviations from normal aging, which is critical for the timely diagnosis and intervention in neurodegenerative diseases like Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) and Alzheimer's Disease (AD).

AI Health Application

EVA-Net is an AI application designed to provide an interpretable framework for predicting brain age from EEG data. Its primary health application is as an anomaly detection tool to identify individuals who deviate from healthy aging patterns, potentially indicating neurological conditions like Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) and Alzheimer's Disease (AD). This serves as a diagnostic aid or early detection system within healthcare intelligence.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of learning a 'normal' baseline from weakly supervised, healthy-only cohorts in brain age prediction from EEG, which is crucial for anomaly detection and disease identification.
  • Proposes EVA-Net, an interpretable framework that reframes brain age prediction as an anomaly detection task, overcoming the limitations of black-box models.
  • Utilizes an efficient, sparsified-attention Transformer architecture to effectively model long sequences of EEG data, capturing complex temporal patterns.
  • Incorporates a Variational Information Bottleneck (VIB) to learn a robust and compressed representation, enhancing the model's resilience to noise and variability inherent in imperfect medical data.
  • Employs a continuous prototype network to align the learned representation, explicitly defining and visualizing the normative healthy aging manifold for enhanced interpretability.
  • Achieved state-of-the-art accuracy in brain age prediction when trained on a cohort of 1297 healthy subjects.
  • Validated its anomaly detection capabilities on an unseen pathological cohort of 27 MCI and AD patients, where they exhibited significantly higher brain-age gaps and a novel Prototype Alignment Error, confirming their deviation from the healthy aging manifold.

Methodology

EVA-Net is a deep learning framework comprising an efficient, sparsified-attention Transformer for processing long EEG sequences. It integrates a Variational Information Bottleneck (VIB) to extract a robust, compressed feature representation from noisy input data. For interpretability and anomaly detection, this learned representation is explicitly aligned to a continuous prototype network, which is trained on 1297 healthy subjects to model the normative healthy aging manifold.

Key Findings

EVA-Net achieved state-of-the-art accuracy in brain age prediction on healthy subjects. Critically, in an unseen validation cohort of 27 MCI and AD patients, the model identified significantly higher brain-age gaps and introduced a novel 'Prototype Alignment Error'. These metrics successfully confirmed the pathological deviation of these patients from the healthy aging manifold learned by the model, demonstrating its efficacy in anomaly detection.

Clinical Impact

This interpretable framework offers a transparent diagnostic aid for clinicians, potentially enabling earlier and more reliable identification of individuals at risk of or suffering from neurodegenerative conditions like MCI and AD by detecting significant deviations from normative brain aging using EEG. It could improve patient stratification, guide personalized therapeutic strategies, and facilitate objective monitoring of disease progression, particularly given the accessibility of EEG.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations. However, the validation cohort for pathological conditions (MCI and AD patients) was relatively small (27 subjects). Further validation on larger, more diverse patient populations and across a broader spectrum of neurological disorders would be necessary to establish widespread clinical utility and generalizability.

Abstract

The brain age is a key indicator of brain health. While electroencephalography (EEG) is a practical tool for this task, existing models struggle with the common challenge of imperfect medical data, such as learning a ``normal'' baseline from weakly supervised, healthy-only cohorts. This is a critical anomaly detection task for identifying disease, but standard models are often black boxes lacking an interpretable structure. We propose EVA-Net, a novel framework that recasts brain age as an interpretable anomaly detection problem. EVA-Net uses an efficient, sparsified-attention Transformer to model long EEG sequences. To handle noise and variability in imperfect data, it employs a Variational Information Bottleneck to learn a robust, compressed representation. For interpretability, this representation is aligned to a continuous prototype network that explicitly learns the normative healthy aging manifold. Trained on 1297 healthy subjects, EVA-Net achieves state-of-the-art accuracy. We validated its anomaly detection capabilities on an unseen cohort of 27 MCI and AD patients. This pathological group showed significantly higher brain-age gaps and a novel Prototype Alignment Error, confirming their deviation from the healthy manifold. EVA-Net provides an interpretable framework for healthcare intelligence using imperfect medical data.
