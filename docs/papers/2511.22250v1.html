ColonAdapter: Geometry Estimation Through Foundation Model Adaptation for Colonoscopy

Published: 2025-11-27

Summary

This paper introduces ColonAdapter, a self-supervised fine-tuning framework designed to adapt general geometric foundation models for accurate 3D geometry estimation from monocular colonoscopy images. It addresses the poor performance of existing models in clinical scenes due to challenges like specularity and homogeneous textures, achieving state-of-the-art results in camera pose estimation, monocular depth prediction, and dense 3D point map reconstruction on both synthetic and real datasets.

Medical Relevance

Accurate 3D geometry reconstruction from colonoscopy images is critical for improving diagnostic precision, enhancing navigation within the colon, and facilitating precise localization of polyps or lesions. This technology can lead to earlier and more accurate detection of colorectal diseases, improving patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

This research develops an AI application for enhanced 3D geometric understanding during colonoscopy procedures. It could lead to improved navigation for endoscopists, more accurate localization and measurement of polyps or lesions, and potentially assist in robot-assisted endoscopy by providing precise spatial information of the colon's anatomy.

Key Points

  • Existing 3D geometric foundation models, primarily trained on natural scenes, perform inadequately in clinical colonoscopy environments due to specularity and homogeneous textures.
  • ColonAdapter is proposed as a self-supervised fine-tuning framework that leverages pretrained geometric priors and adapts them specifically for colonoscopy data.
  • A Detail Restoration Module (DRM) is introduced to enhance performance in low-texture regions and ensure scale consistency, complemented by a geometry consistency loss.
  • A confidence-weighted photometric loss is incorporated to improve training stability, which is crucial in challenging clinical imaging conditions.

  • The method operates effectively on both synthetic and real datasets without the requirement for ground-truth intrinsic camera parameters, simplifying its application.

Methodology

ColonAdapter is a self-supervised fine-tuning framework that adapts pre-trained geometric foundation models to colonoscopy data. It integrates a Detail Restoration Module (DRM) to improve detail in low-texture areas, employs a geometry consistency loss for scale coherence, and utilizes a confidence-weighted photometric loss to enhance training stability in clinical scenes. The framework builds upon existing geometric priors, leveraging them for specialized medical applications without requiring ground-truth intrinsic camera parameters.

Key Findings

The proposed ColonAdapter framework achieves state-of-the-art performance in multiple critical tasks related to 3D geometry estimation from monocular colonoscopy images. It significantly improves camera pose estimation, provides more accurate monocular depth predictions, and enables superior dense 3D point map reconstruction on both synthetic and real datasets, all while operating without the need for ground-truth intrinsic camera parameters.

Clinical Impact

This technology can profoundly impact clinical colonoscopy by providing clinicians with more reliable and accurate 3D spatial awareness during procedures. This enhanced geometric understanding can aid in precise navigation, better localization and characterization of lesions (e.g., polyps, tumors), more effective guidance for biopsies, and potentially improved planning for surgical interventions, ultimately enhancing the safety and efficacy of diagnostic and therapeutic colonoscopies.

Abstract

Estimating 3D geometry from monocular colonoscopy images is challenging due to non-Lambertian surfaces, moving light sources, and large textureless regions. While recent 3D geometric foundation models eliminate the need for multi-stage pipelines, their performance deteriorates in clinical scenes. These models are primarily trained on natural scene datasets and struggle with specularity and homogeneous textures typical in colonoscopy, leading to inaccurate geometry estimation. In this paper, we present ColonAdapter, a self-supervised fine-tuning framework that adapts geometric foundation models for colonoscopy geometry estimation. Our method leverages pretrained geometric priors while tailoring them to clinical data. To improve performance in low-texture regions and ensure scale consistency, we introduce a Detail Restoration Module (DRM) and a geometry consistency loss. Furthermore, a confidence-weighted photometric loss enhances training stability in clinical environments. Experiments on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrate that our approach achieves state-of-the-art performance in camera pose estimation, monocular depth prediction, and dense 3D point map reconstruction, without requiring ground-truth intrinsic parameters.
