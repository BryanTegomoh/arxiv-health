Silhouette-to-Contour Registration: Aligning Intraoral Scan Models with Cephalometric Radiographs

arXiv ID: 2511.14343v1
Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Yiyi Miao, Taoyu Wu, Ji Jiang, Tong Chen, Zhe Tang, Zhengyong Jiang, Angelos Stefanidis, Limin Yu, Jionglong Su
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00

Summary

This paper introduces DentalSCR, a novel contour-guided framework for robust and accurate 3D-2D alignment of intraoral scan models with lateral cephalometric radiographs. By establishing a unified anatomical coordinate system and employing a surface-based DRR generation with subsequent Chamfer distance optimization, DentalSCR overcomes challenges of conventional methods, achieving high-fidelity alignment with reduced landmark errors, particularly for posterior teeth, and superior performance compared to baselines.

Medical Relevance

Accurate 3D-2D alignment is critical for precise orthodontic diagnosis and treatment planning, enabling clinicians to integrate detailed 3D dental models with 2D radiographic skeletal and soft tissue information, leading to more personalized and effective patient care.

AI Health Application

This research contributes to medical AI by developing an advanced computer vision framework (DentalSCR) for robust 3D-2D image registration in a clinical setting. This application aims to improve the accuracy and reliability of integrating different medical imaging modalities (intraoral scans and cephalometric radiographs) for better orthodontic diagnosis and treatment planning, potentially leading to more automated and precise analytical tools for dental professionals.

Key Points

  • Addresses limitations of conventional intensity-driven registration methods for 3D-2D alignment in orthodontics due to cephalogram artifacts like projective magnification, geometric distortion, and low-contrast dental crowns.
  • Proposes DentalSCR, a pose-stable, contour-guided framework that utilizes a U-Midline Dental Axis (UMDA) to establish a unified cross-arch anatomical coordinate system, stabilizing initialization and standardizing projection geometry.
  • Generates radiograph-like projections (DRRs) from 3D intraoral scans using a surface-based formulation with coronal-axis perspective and Gaussian splatting, designed to preserve clinical magnification and emphasize external silhouettes.
  • Formulates registration as a 2D similarity transform optimized via a symmetric bidirectional Chamfer distance under a hierarchical coarse-to-fine schedule, enabling both large capture range and subpixel-level contour agreement.
  • Evaluated on 34 expert-annotated clinical cases, demonstrating substantial reductions in landmark error, particularly at posterior teeth, and tighter dispersion on the lower jaw compared to baselines.
  • Achieves low Chamfer and controlled Hausdorff distances at the curve level, indicating high-fidelity contour agreement between the projected 3D model and the 2D cephalogram.
  • Outperforms conventional appearance-based baselines, providing robust, clinically inspectable 3D-2D alignment suitable for real-world cephalograms.

Methodology

DentalSCR employs a multi-stage, contour-guided registration approach. It first constructs a U-Midline Dental Axis (UMDA) to establish a consistent anatomical coordinate system for initialization stabilization and projection standardization. Next, it generates radiograph-like projections from the 3D intraoral scan models using a surface-based Digital Reconstructed Radiograph (DRR) formulation, incorporating a coronal-axis perspective and Gaussian splatting to emulate clinical magnification and highlight external silhouettes. Finally, registration is performed as a 2D similarity transform, optimized using a symmetric bidirectional Chamfer distance under a hierarchical coarse-to-fine scheme to achieve both large capture range and subpixel contour agreement.

Key Findings

The study found that DentalSCR significantly reduces landmark errors, particularly in posterior teeth, and exhibits tighter dispersion on the lower jaw compared to conventional methods. It achieved low Chamfer and controlled Hausdorff distances at the curve level, indicating precise contour matching. The system demonstrated robust performance on real-world cephalograms, delivering high-fidelity and clinically inspectable 3D-2D alignments, effectively performing better than established baselines.

Clinical Impact

This technology has the potential to significantly enhance orthodontic diagnosis and treatment planning by providing clinicians with a more reliable and accurate method for integrating 3D dental models with 2D cephalometric data. This improved alignment can lead to better understanding of craniofacial relationships, more precise treatment simulations, and ultimately, more predictable and effective patient outcomes in orthodontics.

Abstract

Reliable 3D-2D alignment between intraoral scan (IOS) models and lateral cephalometric radiographs is critical for orthodontic diagnosis, yet conventional intensity-driven registration methods struggle under real clinical conditions, where cephalograms exhibit projective magnification, geometric distortion, low-contrast dental crowns, and acquisition-dependent variation. These factors hinder the stability of appearance-based similarity metrics and often lead to convergence failures or anatomically implausible alignments. To address these limitations, we propose DentalSCR, a pose-stable, contour-guided framework for accurate and interpretable silhouette-to-contour registration. Our method first constructs a U-Midline Dental Axis (UMDA) to establish a unified cross-arch anatomical coordinate system, thereby stabilizing initialization and standardizing projection geometry across cases. Using this reference frame, we generate radiograph-like projections via a surface-based DRR formulation with coronal-axis perspective and Gaussian splatting, which preserves clinical source-object-detector magnification and emphasizes external silhouettes. Registration is then formulated as a 2D similarity transform optimized with a symmetric bidirectional Chamfer distance under a hierarchical coarse-to-fine schedule, enabling both large capture range and subpixel-level contour agreement. We evaluate DentalSCR on 34 expert-annotated clinical cases. Experimental results demonstrate substantial reductions in landmark error-particularly at posterior teeth-tighter dispersion on the lower jaw, and low Chamfer and controlled Hausdorff distances at the curve level. These findings indicate that DentalSCR robustly handles real-world cephalograms and delivers high-fidelity, clinically inspectable 3D--2D alignment, outperforming conventional baselines.
