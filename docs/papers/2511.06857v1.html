Ambiguity-aware Truncated Flow Matching for Ambiguous Medical Image Segmentation

Summary

This paper introduces Ambiguity-aware Truncated Flow Matching (ATFM), a novel approach designed to overcome the inherent trade-offs between prediction accuracy and diversity in ambiguous medical image segmentation (AMIS). ATFM achieves this by proposing a new inference paradigm called Data-Hierarchical Inference and incorporating dedicated components like Gaussian Truncation Representation (GTR) and Segmentation Flow Matching (SFM). Comprehensive evaluations demonstrate that ATFM significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods, yielding superior segmentation performance and more efficient inference.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and diverse segmentation of ambiguous regions in medical images is crucial for reliable diagnosis, staging, and treatment planning, especially in conditions like cancer or skin lesions where boundaries can be ill-defined. This work directly improves the capability of AI systems to handle such ambiguities, leading to more robust clinical decision support.

AI Health Application

This research applies advanced AI (specifically generative models and flow matching) to medical image segmentation. Accurate and diverse segmentation of medical images is critical for assisting clinicians in tasks such as early disease detection (e.g., identifying lung nodules, skin lesions), precise diagnosis, treatment planning (e.g., radiation therapy targeting), disease progression monitoring, and quantitative analysis in various medical fields.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge in Ambiguous Medical Image Segmentation (AMIS) where existing Truncated Diffusion Probabilistic Models (TDPMs) struggle with entangled accuracy and diversity, leading to insufficient fidelity and plausibility.
  • Proposes Ambiguity-aware Truncated Flow Matching (ATFM) which introduces a novel inference paradigm and dedicated model components to effectively disentangle and enhance both accuracy and diversity.
  • Introduces Data-Hierarchical Inference, a redefined AMIS-specific inference paradigm, which improves accuracy at the data-distribution level and diversity at the data-sample level.
  • Develops Gaussian Truncation Representation (GTR) to enhance the fidelity of predictions and reliability of the truncation distribution by explicitly modeling it as a Gaussian distribution at `T_trunc`.
  • Proposes Segmentation Flow Matching (SFM) to improve the plausibility of diverse predictions by extending the semantic-aware flow transformation capabilities of standard Flow Matching (FM).
  • Evaluated on LIDC and ISIC3 datasets, ATFM demonstrates superior performance compared to current SOTA methods.
  • Achieves significant improvements, enhancing Generalized Energy Distance (GED) by up to 12% and Hausdorff Mean Intersection over Union (HM-IoU) by up to 7.3% against advanced segmentation techniques, alongside more efficient inference.

Methodology

ATFM integrates Data-Hierarchical Inference to disentangle accuracy and diversity, Gaussian Truncation Representation (GTR) to model reliable truncation distributions, and Segmentation Flow Matching (SFM) to ensure plausible diverse predictions. This framework extends the principles of Flow Matching (FM) with semantic-aware transformations to address the unique challenges of ambiguous medical image segmentation.

Key Findings

ATFM significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods in ambiguous medical image segmentation. It achieves improvements of up to 12% in Generalized Energy Distance (GED) and 7.3% in Hausdorff Mean Intersection over Union (HM-IoU) while also offering a more efficient inference process, demonstrating both enhanced performance and practical utility.

Clinical Impact

By providing more accurate and plausibly diverse segmentation outputs for ambiguous medical images, ATFM can enhance the reliability of automated diagnostic and prognostic tools. This can lead to improved detection of subtle lesions, better delineation for surgical planning or radiation therapy, and more confident clinical decisions in areas with inherent imaging uncertainty, ultimately benefiting patient outcomes.

Abstract

A simultaneous enhancement of accuracy and diversity of predictions remains a challenge in ambiguous medical image segmentation (AMIS) due to the inherent trade-offs. While truncated diffusion probabilistic models (TDPMs) hold strong potential with a paradigm optimization, existing TDPMs suffer from entangled accuracy and diversity of predictions with insufficient fidelity and plausibility. To address the aforementioned challenges, we propose Ambiguity-aware Truncated Flow Matching (ATFM), which introduces a novel inference paradigm and dedicated model components. Firstly, we propose Data-Hierarchical Inference, a redefinition of AMIS-specific inference paradigm, which enhances accuracy and diversity at data-distribution and data-sample level, respectively, for an effective disentanglement. Secondly, Gaussian Truncation Representation (GTR) is introduced to enhance both fidelity of predictions and reliability of truncation distribution, by explicitly modeling it as a Gaussian distribution at $T_{\text{trunc}}$ instead of using sampling-based approximations.Thirdly, Segmentation Flow Matching (SFM) is proposed to enhance the plausibility of diverse predictions by extending semantic-aware flow transformation in Flow Matching (FM). Comprehensive evaluations on LIDC and ISIC3 datasets demonstrate that ATFM outperforms SOTA methods and simultaneously achieves a more efficient inference. ATFM improves GED and HM-IoU by up to $12\%$ and $7.3\%$ compared to advanced methods.
