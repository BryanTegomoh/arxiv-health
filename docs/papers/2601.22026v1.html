Summary

This paper introduces a novel hybrid rendering approach for high-quality, interactive, and immersive visualization of volumetric medical data, particularly for virtual reality (VR) environments. It combines streamed foveated path tracing for the high-detail central view with a lightweight, dynamically updated Gaussian Splatting approximation for the periphery. This method eliminates extensive preprocessing and computational bottlenecks, allowing for real-time anatomical exploration and improved spatial understanding compared to traditional methods.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine as it offers a solution for overcoming the limitations of 2D medical imaging in interpreting complex spatial anatomy, enabling clinicians and educators to interactively explore and understand volumetric data in a high-fidelity 3D environment, crucial for diagnosis, surgical planning, and medical training.

AI Health Application

While the core technique described is a graphics rendering method and not an AI model itself, it is highly relevant as an enabling technology for medical AI applications. Effective visualization of complex 3D medical data, often generated or analyzed by AI systems (e.g., AI-driven segmentation, reconstruction, diagnosis predictions, surgical guidance), is crucial for human interpretation and interaction. This interactive, high-quality immersive visualization method can enhance the user interface of medical AI platforms, facilitate the understanding of AI outputs in diagnostics, improve AI-assisted surgical planning and simulation, and provide better training environments for AI models dealing with 3D anatomy.

Key Points

  • Addresses the computational expense of direct volumetric path tracing and the precomputation requirements of static representations (like Gaussian Splatting) for immersive medical visualization.
  • Proposes a hybrid rendering architecture utilizing streamed foveated path tracing for the high-fidelity foveal region and a lightweight Gaussian Splatting model for the peripheral view.
  • The peripheral Gaussian Splatting model is dynamically generated and continuously refined using information from the foveal renderings and optimized with volume data.
  • Enables interactive updates with peripheral model regeneration in under a second, eliminating the need for extensive preprocessing steps common in static representations.
  • Incorporates depth-guided reprojection to improve robustness against rendering latency and allows users to dynamically balance visual fidelity with refresh rate.
  • Demonstrates that the hybrid approach preserves the visual quality strengths of direct path tracing while offering significantly enhanced interactivity.
  • Aims to improve the perception and understanding of complex spatial relationships within volumetric medical imaging, supporting better interpretation of pathologies.

Methodology

The authors developed a hybrid rendering system that streams foveated path tracing for the central field of view, providing high-fidelity detail. Concurrently, a lightweight Gaussian Splatting approximation renders the peripheral vision. This peripheral model is dynamically generated from the volumetric data, optimized, and continuously refined using real-time input from the foveal path tracing. The system also employs depth-guided reprojection to enhance latency robustness and allow user control over the balance between visual fidelity and refresh rate.

Key Findings

The hybrid rendering method successfully combines the high visual quality of direct path tracing with the efficiency required for interactive VR applications, overcoming the limitations of extensive preprocessing found in static representations. It demonstrates the ability to regenerate the peripheral model in under a second, providing real-time interactive updates and maintaining visual quality while significantly reducing computational overhead, thus enabling new options for interactive medical visualization.

It can also enhance diagnostic accuracy for radiologists by facilitating better spatial understanding of complex pathologies and revolutionize medical education by offering immersive, real-time anatomical exploration for students and trainees.

Abstract

Volumetric medical imaging offers great potential for understanding complex pathologies. Yet, traditional 2D slices provide little support for interpreting spatial relationships, forcing users to mentally reconstruct anatomy into three dimensions. Direct volumetric path tracing and VR rendering can improve perception but are computationally expensive, while precomputed representations, like Gaussian Splatting, require planning ahead. Both approaches limit interactive use. We propose a hybrid rendering approach for high-quality, interactive, and immersive anatomical visualization. Our method combines streamed foveated path tracing with a lightweight Gaussian Splatting approximation of the periphery. The peripheral model generation is optimized with volume data and continuously refined using foveal renderings, enabling interactive updates. Depth-guided reprojection further improves robustness to latency and allows users to balance fidelity with refresh rate. We compare our method against direct path tracing and Gaussian Splatting. Our results highlight how their combination can preserve strengths in visual quality while re-generating the peripheral model in under a second, eliminating extensive preprocessing and approximations. This opens new options for interactive medical visualization.
