Summary

This paper introduces Scale Adaptive Curriculum Learning (SACL), a novel dynamic training strategy for data-efficient lung nodule detection using deep learning, specifically with YOLOv11. SACL addresses the challenge of limited annotated medical data by dynamically adjusting curriculum design based on the available data scale. It achieves significant performance improvements over traditional methods in data-scarce clinical scenarios, providing a practical solution for early lung cancer diagnosis.

Medical Relevance

Early and accurate detection of lung nodules in chest CT scans is paramount for the timely diagnosis and treatment of lung cancer, which directly improves patient survival rates. This research makes deep learning tools more viable and effective for real-world clinical deployment, particularly in environments with limited resources, by overcoming the annotation bottleneck.

AI Health Application

The paper develops an AI-driven method (Scale Adaptive Curriculum Learning with YOLOv11) for automated and data-efficient detection of lung nodules in CT scans. This application directly assists medical professionals in the early diagnosis of lung cancer, thereby improving patient outcomes and streamlining clinical workflows in healthcare institutions, even with limited annotated medical data.

Methodology

The authors developed Scale Adaptive Curriculum Learning (SACL), a dynamic training strategy for deep learning models. SACL incorporates adaptive epoch scheduling, hard sample injection, and scale-aware optimization. This strategy was implemented with YOLOv11 as the base detector and evaluated on the LUNA25 dataset. Performance was assessed, specifically measuring mAP50, under various simulated data scarcity conditions (10%, 20%, and 50% of the full training data) compared to a baseline and static curriculum learning.

Key Findings

SACL achieved performance on par with static curriculum learning on the full LUNA25 dataset (in mAP50). However, its primary advantage was demonstrated in data-limited scenarios, where it yielded significant mAP50 improvements over the baseline: 4.6% at 10% data, 3.5% at 20% data, and 2.0% at 50% data. This indicates SACL's superior robustness and efficiency when annotation resources are scarce.

Clinical Impact

SACL provides a crucial practical solution for healthcare institutions facing limitations in annotated data, allowing them to develop and deploy effective lung nodule detection systems using deep learning. By enabling robust model training without demanding extensive manual annotations or architectural changes, it can accelerate the integration of AI for early lung cancer diagnosis, potentially leading to earlier intervention and improved patient outcomes in resource-constrained clinical settings.

Abstract

Lung nodule detection in chest CT is crucial for early lung cancer diagnosis, yet existing deep learning approaches face challenges when deployed in clinical settings with limited annotated data. While curriculum learning has shown promise in improving model training, traditional static curriculum strategies fail in data-scarce scenarios. We propose Scale Adaptive Curriculum Learning (SACL), a novel training strategy that dynamically adjusts curriculum design based on available data scale. SACL introduces three key mechanisms:(1) adaptive epoch scheduling, (2) hard sample injection, and (3) scale-aware optimization. We evaluate SACL on the LUNA25 dataset using YOLOv11 as the base detector. Experimental results demonstrate that while SACL achieves comparable performance to static curriculum learning on the full dataset in mAP50, it shows significant advantages under data-limited conditions with 4.6%, 3.5%, and 2.0% improvements over baseline at 10%, 20%, and 50% of training data respectively. By enabling robust training across varying data scales without architectural modifications, SACL provides a practical solution for healthcare institutions to develop effective lung nodule detection systems despite limited annotation resources.
