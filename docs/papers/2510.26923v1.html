Scale-Aware Curriculum Learning for Ddata-Efficient Lung Nodule Detection with YOLOv11

Summary

This paper introduces Scale Adaptive Curriculum Learning (SACL), a novel training strategy designed to overcome challenges in deep learning-based lung nodule detection when annotated data is scarce. SACL dynamically adjusts its curriculum based on available data scale, demonstrating significant performance improvements (up to 4.6% mAP50) over baseline methods in data-limited scenarios, while maintaining comparable performance on full datasets without architectural modifications.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and early lung nodule detection in chest CT scans is critical for timely lung cancer diagnosis. This research provides a vital tool for healthcare systems to deploy effective AI-driven diagnostic assistance, particularly in environments where extensive, expertly annotated medical imaging datasets are scarce.

The AI application is the development of a data-efficient deep learning model (YOLOv11 with Scale Adaptive Curriculum Learning) for automated detection of lung nodules in chest CT scans. This aids radiologists in early lung cancer diagnosis, a critical step for improving patient prognosis and health outcomes, especially in clinical settings with limited annotated medical data.

Methodology

The authors propose Scale Adaptive Curriculum Learning (SACL), a training strategy that dynamically adjusts curriculum design. It integrates adaptive epoch scheduling, hard sample injection, and scale-aware optimization. This strategy was implemented with YOLOv11 as the base detector and evaluated on the LUNA25 dataset. Performance was quantified using mean Average Precision at an Intersection over Union (IoU) threshold of 0.5 (mAP50), comparing SACL against a baseline and static curriculum learning across various proportions of the training data (10%, 20%, 50%, and full dataset).

Key Findings

SACL demonstrated comparable mAP50 performance to static curriculum learning on the full LUNA25 dataset. Crucially, under data-limited conditions, SACL significantly outperformed the baseline, achieving 4.6% mAP50 improvement with only 10% of the training data, 3.5% improvement with 20% data, and 2.0% improvement with 50% of the training data. The method enables robust model training across varying data scales without requiring architectural modifications.

Abstract

Lung nodule detection in chest CT is crucial for early lung cancer diagnosis, yet existing deep learning approaches face challenges when deployed in clinical settings with limited annotated data. While curriculum learning has shown promise in improving model training, traditional static curriculum strategies fail in data-scarce scenarios. We propose Scale Adaptive Curriculum Learning (SACL), a novel training strategy that dynamically adjusts curriculum design based on available data scale. SACL introduces three key mechanisms:(1) adaptive epoch scheduling, (2) hard sample injection, and (3) scale-aware optimization. We evaluate SACL on the LUNA25 dataset using YOLOv11 as the base detector. Experimental results demonstrate that while SACL achieves comparable performance to static curriculum learning on the full dataset in mAP50, it shows significant advantages under data-limited conditions with 4.6%, 3.5%, and 2.0% improvements over baseline at 10%, 20%, and 50% of training data respectively. By enabling robust training across varying data scales without architectural modifications, SACL provides a practical solution for healthcare institutions to develop effective lung nodule detection systems despite limited annotation resources.
