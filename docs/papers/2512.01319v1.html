Rethinking Intracranial Aneurysm Vessel Segmentation: A Perspective from Computational Fluid Dynamics Applications

Published: 2025-12-01
Authors: Feiyang Xiao, Yichi Zhang, Xigui Li, Yuanye Zhou, Chen Jiang, Xin Guo, Limei Han, Yuxin Li, Fengping Zhu, Yuan Cheng

Summary

This paper introduces the Intracranial Aneurysm Vessel Segmentation (IAVS) dataset, a comprehensive multi-center collection of 3D MRA images with IA-Vessel annotations and detailed hemodynamic analysis outcomes. It addresses the critical gap where current segmentation methods often neglect their practical effectiveness in subsequent Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) applications, by establishing new benchmarks and a standardized CFD applicability evaluation system.

Medical Relevance

Precise segmentation of intracranial aneurysms and their parent vessels is fundamental for accurate hemodynamic analyses via CFD, which are crucial for assessing rupture risk, guiding treatment decisions, and understanding aneurysm progression. This work ensures segmentation results are directly useful for clinical CFD, leading to more reliable diagnostic and prognostic tools.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using computer vision and machine learning techniques for the segmentation of intracranial aneurysms and associated vessels from 3D MRA medical images. This segmentation is a prerequisite for subsequent computational fluid dynamics (CFD) analysis, which provides critical hemodynamic insights for clinical assessment, diagnosis, risk stratification, and treatment planning of intracranial aneurysms. The research aims to improve the applicability and clinical utility of these AI-driven segmentation methods in a healthcare setting.

Key Points

  • Addresses the deficiency of current intracranial aneurysm (IA) and parent vessel (IA-Vessel) segmentation methods that prioritize image-based metrics over practical effectiveness in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) applications.
  • Introduces the Intracranial Aneurysm Vessel Segmentation (IAVS) dataset, the first multi-center collection of 641 3D MRA images with 587 IA and IA-Vessel annotations, uniquely including detailed hemodynamic analysis outcomes.
  • The IAVS dataset specifically tackles limitations of existing datasets by ensuring topological integrity and direct applicability for CFD simulations.
  • Establishes two evaluation benchmarks: Stage I for global aneurysm localization and Stage II for fine-grained IA-Vessel segmentation, to facilitate development of clinically relevant techniques.
  • Develops a simple yet effective two-stage framework that serves as both an out-of-the-box method and a strong baseline for IA-Vessel segmentation.
  • Presents a standardized CFD applicability evaluation system for automated and consistent conversion of segmentation masks into CFD models, enabling an applicability-focused assessment.
  • The dataset, code, and model are made publicly available to foster community research and development.

Methodology

The methodology involves constructing the IAVS dataset from multi-center 3D MRA images, meticulously annotating 587 aneurysms and IA-Vessels, and integrating detailed hemodynamic analysis outcomes. Two distinct evaluation benchmarks (Stage I: global localization, Stage II: fine-grained segmentation) were established. A two-stage deep learning framework was developed as a baseline. Crucially, a standardized system was devised for automated conversion of segmentation masks into CFD models, allowing for an 'applicability-focused' evaluation beyond traditional image metrics.

Key Findings

The primary contributions are the creation and public release of the IAVS dataset, which is the first to explicitly link aneurysm vessel segmentation with detailed hemodynamic analysis outcomes and CFD applicability. The paper also establishes novel two-stage evaluation benchmarks, provides a robust baseline segmentation framework, and introduces a standardized CFD applicability evaluation system, thereby defining a new paradigm for assessing segmentation performance based on its utility in downstream CFD applications.

Clinical Impact

This research has significant clinical impact by enabling more accurate and clinically relevant hemodynamic simulations for intracranial aneurysms. This will lead to improved aneurysm rupture risk assessment, more informed treatment planning (e.g., surgical vs. endovascular), and enhanced understanding of disease progression. The public resources will accelerate the development of AI-driven tools that are directly translatable to clinical practice and research.

Limitations

The paper primarily highlights the limitations of *existing* segmentation methods and datasets: current methods predominantly rely on image-based evaluation metrics, neglecting practical effectiveness in CFD, and existing datasets often lack topological integrity and CFD applicability. The introduced IAVS dataset and evaluation system aim to overcome these specific limitations inherent in the field.

Future Directions

The public availability of the IAVS dataset, code, and models, along with the established benchmarks and CFD applicability evaluation system, is intended to facilitate and accelerate the development of future, more clinically relevant techniques for IA-Vessel segmentation. This implicitly encourages research into advanced AI models that optimize for direct CFD applicability, leading to more robust and trustworthy hemodynamic analyses.

Abstract

The precise segmentation of intracranial aneurysms and their parent vessels (IA-Vessel) is a critical step for hemodynamic analyses, which mainly depends on computational fluid dynamics (CFD). However, current segmentation methods predominantly focus on image-based evaluation metrics, often neglecting their practical effectiveness in subsequent CFD applications. To address this deficiency, we present the Intracranial Aneurysm Vessel Segmentation (IAVS) dataset, the first comprehensive, multi-center collection comprising 641 3D MRA images with 587 annotations of aneurysms and IA-Vessels. In addition to image-mask pairs, IAVS dataset includes detailed hemodynamic analysis outcomes, addressing the limitations of existing datasets that neglect topological integrity and CFD applicability. To facilitate the development and evaluation of clinically relevant techniques, we construct two evaluation benchmarks including global localization of aneurysms (Stage I) and fine-grained segmentation of IA-Vessel (Stage II) and develop a simple and effective two-stage framework, which can be used as a out-of-the-box method and strong baseline. For comprehensive evaluation of applicability of segmentation results, we establish a standardized CFD applicability evaluation system that enables the automated and consistent conversion of segmentation masks into CFD models, offering an applicability-focused assessment of segmentation outcomes. The dataset, code, and model will be public available at https://github.com/AbsoluteResonance/IAVS.
