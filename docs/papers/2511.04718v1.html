Ada-FCN: Adaptive Frequency-Coupled Network for fMRI-Based Brain Disorder Classification

arXiv ID: 2511.04718v1
Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Yue Xun, Jiaxing Xu, Wenbo Gao, Chen Yang, Shujun Wang

Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces Ada-FCN, an Adaptive Frequency-Coupled Network, designed to improve fMRI-based brain disorder classification by explicitly addressing the multi-frequency nature of neuronal oscillations. It adaptively learns task-relevant frequency sub-bands for each brain region and constructs a unified functional connectivity network that captures both intra- and nuanced cross-band interactions, leading to superior diagnostic performance on ADNI and ABIDE datasets.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly enhances the diagnostic capabilities for neurological and psychiatric disorders by leveraging the nuanced multi-frequency information from fMRI BOLD signals. By adaptively identifying disorder-specific frequency disruptions, it offers a more precise and sensitive approach to brain disorder classification, potentially leading to earlier and more accurate diagnoses.

AI Health Application

This research develops a novel AI framework (Ada-FCN, based on graph convolutional networks and adaptive frequency decomposition) to analyze fMRI data. The application is to enhance the classification and diagnostic prediction of brain disorders, such as Alzheimer's disease and autism spectrum disorder, by better capturing disease-specific alterations in brain functional connectivity.

Key Points

  • Addresses the limitation of existing fMRI models that treat BOLD signals as monolithic time series, overlooking critical multi-frequency manifestations of neurological disorders.
  • Proposes Adaptive Cascade Decomposition (ACD) to learn task-relevant and individualized frequency sub-bands for each brain region, rather than relying on predefined frequency bands.
  • Introduces Frequency-Coupled Connectivity Learning (FCCL) to construct a unified functional network that captures both within-sub-band (intra-band) and subtle cross-sub-band (cross-band) interactions.
  • Develops a novel message-passing mechanism within a Unified-GCN to leverage this multi-frequency unified network, generating refined node representations for diagnostic prediction.
  • Demonstrates superior diagnostic performance over existing methods on two widely used brain disorder datasets: ADNI (for Alzheimer's Disease and related dementias) and ABIDE (for Autism Spectrum Disorder).
  • Aims to enhance diagnostic sensitivity and specificity by precisely identifying disruptions within specific, adaptively learned frequency bands that are indicative of particular neurological disorders.

Methodology

The Ada-FCN framework integrates three core components: (1) Adaptive Cascade Decomposition (ACD) to learn task-relevant, region-specific frequency sub-bands from BOLD signals; (2) Frequency-Coupled Connectivity Learning (FCCL) to construct a unified functional connectivity network that models both intra-band and cross-band interactions; and (3) a Unified-GCN, which utilizes a novel message-passing mechanism on this unified, multi-frequency network to generate enriched brain region representations for subsequent classification of brain disorders.

Key Findings

The Ada-FCN framework achieved superior performance in classifying brain disorders compared to existing methods. This was empirically validated on the ADNI dataset for neurodegenerative diseases and the ABIDE dataset for Autism Spectrum Disorder, demonstrating the effectiveness of adaptively learning and integrating multi-frequency brain signal information for improved diagnostic accuracy.

Clinical Impact

This methodology holds significant potential for clinical applications, offering a pathway to more accurate and earlier diagnosis of complex brain disorders like Alzheimer's disease and Autism Spectrum Disorder. By precisely pinpointing frequency-specific disruptions, it could facilitate more targeted interventions, personalize treatment strategies, and ultimately improve patient management and outcomes.

Abstract

Resting-state fMRI has become a valuable tool for classifying brain disorders and constructing brain functional connectivity networks by tracking BOLD signals across brain regions. However, existing mod els largely neglect the multi-frequency nature of neuronal oscillations, treating BOLD signals as monolithic time series. This overlooks the cru cial fact that neurological disorders often manifest as disruptions within specific frequency bands, limiting diagnostic sensitivity and specificity. While some methods have attempted to incorporate frequency informa tion, they often rely on predefined frequency bands, which may not be optimal for capturing individual variability or disease-specific alterations. To address this, we propose a novel framework featuring Adaptive Cas cade Decomposition to learn task-relevant frequency sub-bands for each brain region and Frequency-Coupled Connectivity Learning to capture both intra- and nuanced cross-band interactions in a unified functional network. This unified network informs a novel message-passing mecha nism within our Unified-GCN, generating refined node representations for diagnostic prediction. Experimental results on the ADNI and ABIDE datasets demonstrate superior performance over existing methods. The code is available at https://github.com/XXYY20221234/Ada-FCN.
