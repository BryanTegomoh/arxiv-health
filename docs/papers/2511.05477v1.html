GroupKAN: Rethinking Nonlinearity with Grouped Spline-based KAN Modeling for Efficient Medical Image Segmentation

arXiv ID: 2511.05477v1
Published: 2025-11-07
Authors: Guojie Li, Anwar P. P. Abdul Majeed, Muhammad Ateeq, Anh Nguyen, Fan Zhang
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

GroupKAN introduces a novel, lightweight medical image segmentation network that enhances the scalability and efficiency of Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) by overcoming the O(C^2) complexity of U-KAN. It achieves this through Grouped KAN Transform and Grouped KAN Activation modules, which enable multivariate spline mappings within channel groups. GroupKAN demonstrates superior accuracy and interpretability while significantly reducing model parameters on various medical benchmarks.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a more accurate, lightweight, and interpretable deep learning solution for medical image segmentation, which is critical for enhancing diagnostic precision, optimizing treatment planning, and supporting clinical decision-making across various medical specialties.

AI Health Application

This research develops an AI model (GroupKAN) specifically designed for automated or semi-automated segmentation of medical images. This application aims to assist clinicians in tasks such as identifying and delineating tumors (breast, colon polyps), segmenting glands in histopathology for cancer diagnosis and prognosis, thereby improving diagnostic accuracy, efficiency, and potentially patient outcomes.

Key Points

  • Addresses the limitations of conventional CNNs (lack of adaptive nonlinearity, opaque decision-making) and Transformers (quadratic complexity, opaque attention) in medical image segmentation.
  • Solves the O(C^2) complexity issue of U-KAN (a KAN-based segmentation model) due to full-channel transformations, which previously limited its scalability.
  • Introduces two novel functional modules: (1) Grouped KAN Transform, partitioning channels into 'G' groups for multivariate spline mappings, reducing complexity to O(C^2/G).
  • (2) Grouped KAN Activation, applying shared spline-based mappings within each channel group for efficient, token-wise nonlinearity.
  • Achieved an average IoU of 79.80% across three medical benchmarks (BUSI, GlaS, CVC), surpassing U-KAN by +1.11%.
  • Significantly reduces parameter count, requiring only 3.02M parameters (47.6% of U-KAN's 6.35M) while improving performance.
  • Maintains and improves upon the interpretability benefits inherent to Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks compared to conventional approaches.

Methodology

The authors developed GroupKAN by integrating two novel modules: Grouped KAN Transform, which partitions input channels into 'G' groups for multivariate spline transformations, thereby reducing computational complexity; and Grouped KAN Activation, which applies shared spline-based mappings within these channel groups for efficient, token-wise nonlinearity. The model's performance was evaluated against U-KAN and other methods on three distinct medical image segmentation benchmarks: Breast Ultrasound Images (BUSI), GlaS (colonoscopy gland segmentation), and CVC (endoscopic polyp segmentation).

Key Findings

GroupKAN achieved an average Intersection over Union (IoU) of 79.80% across the evaluated medical benchmarks (BUSI, GlaS, CVC), outperforming U-KAN by 1.11 percentage points. Critically, it accomplished this with significantly fewer parameters, using only 3.02 million parameters (47.6% of U-KAN's 6.35 million), while also demonstrating improved interpretability and reducing computational complexity from O(C^2) to O(C^2/G).

Clinical Impact

GroupKAN offers a robust and practical deep learning model for clinical use, providing superior segmentation accuracy for critical medical tasks while being significantly more resource-efficient. Its reduced parameter count facilitates deployment on edge devices or in resource-constrained clinical environments, and its enhanced interpretability can foster greater clinician trust and understanding of AI-driven diagnostic aids.

Abstract

Medical image segmentation requires models that are accurate, lightweight, and interpretable. Convolutional architectures lack adaptive nonlinearity and transparent decision-making, whereas Transformer architectures are hindered by quadratic complexity and opaque attention mechanisms. U-KAN addresses these challenges using Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks, achieving higher accuracy than both convolutional and attention-based methods, fewer parameters than Transformer variants, and improved interpretability compared to conventional approaches. However, its O(C^2) complexity due to full-channel transformations limits its scalability as the number of channels increases. To overcome this, we introduce GroupKAN, a lightweight segmentation network that incorporates two novel, structured functional modules: (1) Grouped KAN Transform, which partitions channels into G groups for multivariate spline mappings, reducing complexity to O(C^2/G), and (2) Grouped KAN Activation, which applies shared spline-based mappings within each channel group for efficient, token-wise nonlinearity. Evaluated on three medical benchmarks (BUSI, GlaS, and CVC), GroupKAN achieves an average IoU of 79.80 percent, surpassing U-KAN by +1.11 percent while requiring only 47.6 percent of the parameters (3.02M vs 6.35M), and shows improved interpretability.
