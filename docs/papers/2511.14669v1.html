Hyperbolic Graph Embeddings Reveal the Host-Pathogen Interactome

Published: 2025-11-18
Authors: Xiaoqiong Xia, Cesar de la Fuente-Nunez

Summary

This paper introduces ApexPPI, a deep learning framework utilizing hyperbolic graph embeddings to accurately map host-pathogen protein networks, effectively capturing their hierarchical and scale-free characteristics. By integrating diverse biological data and employing multi-task hyperbolic graph neural networks, the model predicted thousands of high-confidence interactions, including many involving human G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs), with dozens validated by AlphaFold 3 structural modeling.

Medical Relevance

Accurately mapping host-pathogen protein interactions is fundamental for understanding disease mechanisms, identifying critical vulnerability points in infectious pathways, and discovering novel therapeutic targets for drug development against pathogens.

AI Health Application

The research applies deep learning (hyperbolic graph neural networks) to integrate multimodal biological data and predict host-pathogen protein interactions. This AI application aims to provide a comprehensive map for identifying potential drug targets and informing the discovery of new treatments for infections.

Key Points

  • ApexPPI is a novel deep learning framework that represents protein networks in hyperbolic Riemannian space, optimized for capturing hierarchical and scale-free biological structures.
  • The model integrates multimodal biological data, including protein sequences, gene perturbation experiments, and complementary interaction networks, for robust interaction prediction.
  • It employs multi-task hyperbolic graph neural networks to predict likely interactions between pathogen and host proteins.
  • Mapping protein features into hyperbolic space resulted in significantly higher accuracy compared to previous methods for host-pathogen interaction prediction.
  • From millions of potential pairs, ApexPPI identified thousands of high-confidence interactions, notably uncovering many involving human G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs).
  • Dozens of the predicted protein complexes were validated using AlphaFold 3 structural modeling, providing strong computational support for the predictions' accuracy.
  • The generated comprehensive map serves as a critical resource for discovering new treatments and advancing the understanding of complex biological systems via AI.

Methodology

ApexPPI is a deep learning framework designed to embed protein networks in hyperbolic Riemannian space to naturally represent their hierarchical and scale-free properties. It integrates multimodal biological data, comprising protein sequences, gene perturbation experiments, and existing complementary interaction networks. The model then uses multi-task hyperbolic graph neural networks to predict likely protein-protein interactions between host and pathogen, leveraging the unique geometry of hyperbolic space for enhanced accuracy.

Key Findings

The study found that mapping protein features into hyperbolic space dramatically increased the accuracy of host-pathogen interaction prediction compared to previous methods. ApexPPI identified thousands of high-confidence protein interactions from tens of millions of possibilities, with a significant number involving human G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs). The accuracy of these predicted complexes was further substantiated by validating dozens of them through AlphaFold 3 structural modeling.

Clinical Impact

This comprehensive map of host-pathogen interactomes provides a crucial resource for the clinical translation of biological discoveries. It can directly accelerate the identification of novel drug targets, particularly within the GPCR family which are highly druggable, and inform the development of new treatments and intervention strategies for infectious diseases by elucidating critical interaction pathways.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state any limitations of the ApexPPI model or the scope of its predictions. While AlphaFold 3 provides strong computational validation, the ultimate confirmation of all predicted interactions would require extensive experimental *in vitro* and *in vivo* validation.

Future Directions

The primary future direction highlighted is the utilization of the generated comprehensive map of host-pathogen interactions as a resource for discovering new treatments. The work also broadly illustrates the potential of advanced AI frameworks to unravel and understand complex biological systems, suggesting future applications in other intricate biological networks.

Abstract

Infections depend on interactions between pathogen and host proteins, but comprehensively mapping these interactions is challenging and labor intensive. Many biological networks have hierarchical, scale-free structure, so we developed a deep learning framework, ApexPPI, that represents protein networks in hyperbolic Riemannian space to capture these features. Our model integrates multimodal biological data (protein sequences, gene perturbation experiments, and complementary interaction networks) to predict likely interactions between pathogen and host proteins through multi-task hyperbolic graph neural networks. Mapping protein features into hyperbolic space led to much higher accuracy than previous methods in predicting host-pathogen interactions. From tens of millions of possible protein pairs, our model identified thousands of high-confidence interactions, including many involving human G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs). We validated dozens of these predicted complexes using AlphaFold 3 structural modeling, supporting the accuracy of our predictions. This comprehensive map of host-pathogen protein interactions provides a resource for discovering new treatments and illustrates how advanced AI can unravel complex biological systems.
