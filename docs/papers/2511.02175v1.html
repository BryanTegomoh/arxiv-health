Tackling Incomplete Data in Air Quality Prediction: A Bayesian Deep Learning Framework for Uncertainty Quantification

arXiv ID: 2511.02175v1
Published: 2025-11-04
Authors: Yuzhuang Pian, Taiyu Wang, Shiqi Zhang, Rui Xu, Yonghong Liu
Categories: cs.LG, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces CGLUBNF (Channel Gated Learning Unit based Spatiotemporal Bayesian Neural Field), an end-to-end Bayesian deep learning framework designed to tackle the critical challenge of incomplete spatiotemporal data in air quality prediction. The framework delivers superior prediction accuracy and sharper, calibrated confidence intervals across various missing data patterns and prediction horizons on real-world datasets. This advancement provides more reliable air quality forecasts crucial for public health decision-making.

Medical Relevance

Accurate air quality forecasts are paramount for issuing timely public health alerts, conducting precise exposure assessments, and informing effective emissions control strategies, all directly impacting respiratory and cardiovascular health outcomes. The CGLUBNF framework's ability to provide reliable predictions and quantified uncertainty despite incomplete data ensures that health-related decisions and risk assessments are based on more robust information, preventing overconfident and potentially harmful extrapolations.

Key Points

  • Addresses the significant challenge of incomplete spatiotemporal observational data in air quality monitoring, which impedes reliable inference, risk assessment, and leads to overconfident extrapolation.
  • Proposes CGLUBNF, a novel end-to-end framework that leverages Fourier features with a graph attention encoder to capture multiscale spatial dependencies and seasonal temporal dynamics.
  • Incorporates a Channel Gated Learning Unit (CGLU) equipped with learnable activations and gated residual connections, enabling adaptive filtering and amplification of informative features.
  • Utilizes Bayesian inference to jointly optimize predictive distributions and quantify parameter uncertainty, thereby producing robust point estimates alongside calibrated prediction intervals.
  • Systematically evaluated on two real-world datasets, demonstrating superior prediction accuracy and sharper (better calibrated) confidence intervals compared to five state-of-the-art baselines.
  • Validated across four typical missing data patterns and multiple prediction horizons, confirming its robustness and reliability in diverse real-world scenarios.
  • Lays a foundational framework for reliable deep learning-based spatio-temporal forecasting with incomplete observations, particularly relevant for emerging sensing paradigms such as vehicle-borne mobile monitoring.

Methodology

The CGLUBNF framework integrates Fourier features and a graph attention encoder to model multiscale spatial dependencies and seasonal temporal dynamics in air quality data. A novel Channel Gated Learning Unit (CGLU), featuring learnable activations and gated residual connections, adaptively filters and amplifies informative features. Bayesian inference is applied to jointly optimize predictive distributions and quantify parameter uncertainty, yielding both point estimates and calibrated prediction intervals. The framework was systematically evaluated on two real-world datasets, compared against five state-of-the-art baselines, and tested across four typical missing data patterns and multiple prediction horizons.

Key Findings

The CGLUBNF framework achieved superior prediction accuracy and produced significantly sharper (more precise and calibrated) confidence intervals compared to existing state-of-the-art baselines. It demonstrated strong robustness across various missing data patterns and multiple prediction horizons, validating its reliability for real-world air quality forecasting with incomplete observations.

Abstract

Accurate air quality forecasts are vital for public health alerts, exposure assessment, and emissions control. In practice, observational data are often missing in varying proportions and patterns due to collection and transmission issues. These incomplete spatiotemporal records impede reliable inference and risk assessment and can lead to overconfident extrapolation. To address these challenges, we propose an end to end framework, the channel gated learning unit based spatiotemporal bayesian neural field (CGLUBNF). It uses Fourier features with a graph attention encoder to capture multiscale spatial dependencies and seasonal temporal dynamics. A channel gated learning unit, equipped with learnable activations and gated residual connections, adaptively filters and amplifies informative features. Bayesian inference jointly optimizes predictive distributions and parameter uncertainty, producing point estimates and calibrated prediction intervals. We conduct a systematic evaluation on two real world datasets, covering four typical missing data patterns and comparing against five state of the art baselines. CGLUBNF achieves superior prediction accuracy and sharper confidence intervals. In addition, we further validate robustness across multiple prediction horizons and analysis the contribution of extraneous variables. This research lays a foundation for reliable deep learning based spatio-temporal forecasting with incomplete observations in emerging sensing paradigms, such as real world vehicle borne mobile monitoring.
