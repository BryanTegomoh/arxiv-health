Classical Feature Embeddings Help in BERT-Based Human Mobility Prediction

Summary

The paper introduces STaBERT (Semantic-Temporal aware BERT), a novel BERT-based model designed to significantly improve human mobility prediction by deeply integrating rich semantic context from Points of Interest (POIs) and derived temporal descriptors. This approach constructs a unified, semantically enriched representation for each location, addressing the limitations of prior models that underutilized such contextual information. Experimental results demonstrate substantial improvements in prediction accuracy for both single-city and multi-city scenarios.

Methodology

The authors developed STaBERT, an enhanced BERT-based mobility model. This model integrates 'classical feature embeddings' by incorporating both derived temporal descriptors (e.g., time of day, day of week) and POI embeddings (representing the semantic nature of a location) directly into the representation for each point in a human mobility sequence. This creates a unified, semantically enriched representation, allowing the BERT architecture to capture deeper contextual information that previous models, which often only used location sequences or minimal temporal input, failed to leverage.

Key Findings

STaBERT significantly improves human mobility prediction accuracy. The GEO-BLEU score, a common metric for mobility sequence prediction, increased from 0.34 to 0.75 for single-city predictions. For multi-city prediction tasks, the GEO-BLEU score improved from 0.34 to 0.56, demonstrating the model's robustness and superior performance across different geographic scales.

The enhanced accuracy in human mobility prediction offered by STaBERT has profound clinical and practical implications. It can enable public health authorities to more precisely model and forecast the spread of infectious diseases, allocate medical personnel and resources (e.g., vaccines, medications) more effectively during outbreaks, and optimize logistical planning for emergency medical services and disaster relief efforts, potentially saving lives and mitigating health impacts during crises.

Abstract

Human mobility forecasting is crucial for disaster relief, city planning, and public health. However, existing models either only model location sequences or include time information merely as auxiliary input, thereby failing to leverage the rich semantic context provided by points of interest (POIs). To address this, we enrich a BERT-based mobility model with derived temporal descriptors and POI embeddings to better capture the semantics underlying human movement. We propose STaBERT (Semantic-Temporal aware BERT), which integrates both POI and temporal information at each location to construct a unified, semantically enriched representation of mobility. Experimental results show that STaBERT significantly improves prediction accuracy: for single-city prediction, the GEO-BLEU score improved from 0.34 to 0.75; for multi-city prediction, from 0.34 to 0.56.
