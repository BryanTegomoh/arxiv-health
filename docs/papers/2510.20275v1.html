Classical Feature Embeddings Help in BERT-Based Human Mobility Prediction

Published: 2025-10-23
Authors: Yunzhi Liu, Haokai Tan, Rushi Kanjaria, Lihuan Li, Flora D. Salim

Summary

This paper introduces STaBERT (Semantic-Temporal aware BERT), a novel BERT-based model designed to significantly enhance human mobility forecasting by integrating both derived temporal descriptors and Points of Interest (POI) embeddings. It addresses the shortcomings of existing models that either neglect POI semantics or treat time as mere auxiliary input, thereby failing to capture rich contextual information. Experimental results demonstrate STaBERT's superior performance, boosting GEO-BLEU scores dramatically for both single-city (0.34 to 0.75) and multi-city (0.34 to 0.56) predictions.

Methodology

The authors developed STaBERT, a BERT-based deep learning model, which is augmented with 'classical feature embeddings'. The core methodology involves incorporating derived temporal descriptors (e.g., hour of day, day of week, specific event times) and POI embeddings (semantic features of locations like 'hospital', 'park', 'residential area') directly into the input sequence at each location. This integration creates a unified, semantically enriched representation of human mobility, which is then processed by the BERT architecture to improve the accuracy of movement sequence prediction.

Key Findings

STaBERT significantly improves human mobility prediction accuracy across different scales. For single-city predictions, the GEO-BLEU score surged from a baseline of 0.34 to 0.75. In multi-city prediction scenarios, the GEO-BLEU score also saw a substantial increase from 0.34 to 0.56, demonstrating the model's robustness and the effectiveness of integrating semantic and temporal features.

Abstract

Human mobility forecasting is crucial for disaster relief, city planning, and public health. However, existing models either only model location sequences or include time information merely as auxiliary input, thereby failing to leverage the rich semantic context provided by points of interest (POIs). To address this, we enrich a BERT-based mobility model with derived temporal descriptors and POI embeddings to better capture the semantics underlying human movement. We propose STaBERT (Semantic-Temporal aware BERT), which integrates both POI and temporal information at each location to construct a unified, semantically enriched representation of mobility. Experimental results show that STaBERT significantly improves prediction accuracy: for single-city prediction, the GEO-BLEU score improved from 0.34 to 0.75; for multi-city prediction, from 0.34 to 0.56.
