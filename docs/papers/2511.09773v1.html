NeuroLingua: A Language-Inspired Hierarchical Framework for Multimodal Sleep Stage Classification Using EEG and EOG

arXiv ID: 2511.09773v1
Published: 2025-11-12
Authors: Mahdi Samaee, Mehran Yazdi, Daniel Massicotte
Categories: cs.LG, eess.SP
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

NeuroLingua introduces a novel language-inspired hierarchical framework for automated sleep stage classification, addressing current limitations in expressive temporal hierarchies, multimodal EEG and EOG fusion, and deep learning model interpretability. By conceptualizing sleep as a structured physiological language and employing dual-level Transformers with Graph Convolutional Network-based fusion, it achieves state-of-the-art results on benchmark datasets. This framework not only improves classification accuracy but also enhances the detection of clinically relevant sleep microevents, paving the way for more transparent and clinically meaningful applications.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly enhances the accuracy and interpretability of automated sleep stage classification, which is crucial for the diagnosis and management of sleep disorders. Improved detection of sleep microevents and overall staging can lead to more precise and timely clinical interventions for patients.

AI Health Application

The AI framework, NeuroLingua, is designed for automated sleep stage classification using EEG and EOG signals. This application aims to improve the accuracy, efficiency, and interpretability of diagnosing and monitoring sleep disorders (e.g., insomnia, sleep apnea, narcolepsy), thereby assisting clinicians and enhancing patient care in sleep health.

Key Points

  • **Language-Inspired Framework**: Proposes NeuroLingua, which conceptualizes sleep as a structured physiological language, enabling hierarchical sequence modeling.
  • **Hierarchical Temporal Modeling**: Decomposes 30-second epochs into overlapping 3-second subwindows ("tokens") using a CNN-based tokenizer, processed by dual-level Transformers (intra-segment for local dependencies and inter-segment for 3.5-minute extended context).
  • **Robust Multimodal Fusion**: Employs a Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) to fuse modality-specific embeddings from EEG and EOG channels, facilitating robust multimodal integration.
  • **State-of-the-Art Performance on Sleep-EDF**: Achieves state-of-the-art results on the Sleep-EDF Expanded dataset (85.3% accuracy, 0.800 macro F1, and 0.796 Cohen's kappa).
  • **Competitive Performance on ISRUC**: Demonstrates competitive performance on the ISRUC-Sleep dataset (81.9% accuracy, 0.802 macro F1, and 0.755 kappa), matching or exceeding published baselines.
  • **Enhanced Interpretability**: The architecture's attention mechanisms improve the detection of clinically relevant sleep microevents, providing a foundation for future interpretability and explainability.
  • **Unified Approach**: Unifies hierarchical sequence modeling and multimodal fusion, advancing automated sleep staging towards more transparent and clinically meaningful applications.

Methodology

NeuroLingua uses a CNN-based tokenizer to convert 30-second sleep epochs into 3-second overlapping subwindow "tokens." These tokens are then processed by dual-level Transformers: an intra-segment Transformer to capture local dependencies within an epoch, and an inter-segment Transformer to integrate context across seven consecutive epochs (3.5 minutes). Modality-specific embeddings from EEG and EOG channels are fused using a Graph Convolutional Network (GCN). The framework's performance was evaluated on the Sleep-EDF Expanded and ISRUC-Sleep datasets.

Key Findings

The NeuroLingua framework achieved state-of-the-art results on the Sleep-EDF Expanded dataset with an accuracy of 85.3%, macro F1 of 0.800, and Cohen's kappa of 0.796. On the ISRUC-Sleep dataset, it demonstrated competitive performance, yielding 81.9% accuracy, 0.802 macro F1, and 0.755 kappa, consistently matching or exceeding published baselines. A significant finding is the enhanced detection of clinically relevant sleep microevents through the architecture's attention mechanisms.

Clinical Impact

The enhanced accuracy and interpretability offered by NeuroLingua have significant clinical implications, providing a more reliable and transparent tool for the automated diagnosis and monitoring of sleep disorders. This could lead to earlier and more precise identification of conditions like sleep apnea, insomnia, and narcolepsy, enabling clinicians to tailor treatment plans more effectively and ultimately improve patient outcomes and quality of life.

Abstract

Automated sleep stage classification from polysomnography remains limited by the lack of expressive temporal hierarchies, challenges in multimodal EEG and EOG fusion, and the limited interpretability of deep learning models. We propose NeuroLingua, a language-inspired framework that conceptualizes sleep as a structured physiological language. Each 30-second epoch is decomposed into overlapping 3-second subwindows ("tokens") using a CNN-based tokenizer, enabling hierarchical temporal modeling through dual-level Transformers: intra-segment encoding of local dependencies and inter-segment integration across seven consecutive epochs (3.5 minutes) for extended context. Modality-specific embeddings from EEG and EOG channels are fused via a Graph Convolutional Network, facilitating robust multimodal integration. NeuroLingua is evaluated on the Sleep-EDF Expanded and ISRUC-Sleep datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results on Sleep-EDF (85.3% accuracy, 0.800 macro F1, and 0.796 Cohen's kappa) and competitive performance on ISRUC (81.9% accuracy, 0.802 macro F1, and 0.755 kappa), matching or exceeding published baselines in overall and per-class metrics. The architecture's attention mechanisms enhance the detection of clinically relevant sleep microevents, providing a principled foundation for future interpretability, explainability, and causal inference in sleep research. By framing sleep as a compositional language, NeuroLingua unifies hierarchical sequence modeling and multimodal fusion, advancing automated sleep staging toward more transparent and clinically meaningful applications.
