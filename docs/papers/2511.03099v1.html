DentalSplat: Dental Occlusion Novel View Synthesis from Sparse Intra-Oral Photographs

arXiv ID: 2511.03099v1
Published: 2025-11-05
Authors: Yiyi Miao, Taoyu Wu, Tong Chen, Sihao Li, Ji Jiang, Youpeng Yang, Angelos Stefanidis, Limin Yu, Jionglong Su
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

DentalSplat addresses the critical need for multi-view dental occlusion visualization in orthodontics, particularly for telemedicine, by overcoming limitations of 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) with sparse intra-oral photographs. The framework leverages a prior-guided dense stereo reconstruction, scale-adaptive pruning, and incorporates optical flow with gradient regularization for extremely sparse views, demonstrating superior novel view synthesis quality on extensive clinical datasets.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to orthodontics and telemedicine, enabling clinicians to obtain comprehensive multi-view visualizations of dental occlusion from limited patient-provided photographs, which is crucial for remote diagnosis, treatment planning, and progress monitoring.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using advanced computer vision and 3D reconstruction techniques (e.g., 3D Gaussian Splatting, prior-guided dense stereo reconstruction, optical flow, gradient regularization) to process sparse intra-oral photographs of dental occlusion. The goal is to synthesize high-quality novel views, which enables better visualization for orthodontists, particularly in remote or telemedicine settings, facilitating timely and informed clinical decision-making for patient treatment.

Key Points

  • The paper tackles the challenge of 3D reconstruction and novel view synthesis of dental occlusion from extremely sparse input, typically three intra-oral photographs (anterior and bilateral buccal views), which current 3DGS methods struggle with.
  • DentalSplat proposes a novel framework that initializes the 3D point cloud using a prior-guided dense stereo reconstruction model, addressing the lack of dense input and precise camera poses.
  • A scale-adaptive pruning strategy is integrated into the 3DGS pipeline to enhance training efficiency and improve the quality of the final reconstruction, optimizing the representation for sparse data.
  • For scenarios with particularly extreme sparsity, the method further incorporates optical flow as a geometric constraint, combined with gradient regularization, to ensure high fidelity in rendering novel views.
  • The approach was rigorously validated on a large-scale dataset comprising 950 clinical orthodontic cases and an additional 195 video-based test cases simulating remote imaging conditions.
  • Experimental results confirm that DentalSplat effectively handles sparse input, achieving superior novel view synthesis quality for dental occlusion visualization compared to state-of-the-art techniques.
  • This advancement significantly improves the ability to monitor and assess orthodontic progress remotely, facilitating timely clinical decision-making in telemedicine contexts.

Methodology

The DentalSplat framework employs a multi-stage approach: 1) point cloud initialization via a prior-guided dense stereo reconstruction model, 2) integration of this initialization into a 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) pipeline, 3) application of a scale-adaptive pruning strategy to optimize 3DGS training, and 4) for extreme sparsity, incorporation of optical flow as a geometric constraint along with gradient regularization to enhance rendering fidelity. The method was validated on clinical datasets of still images and simulated video sequences.

Key Findings

DentalSplat successfully overcomes the limitations of sparse input and absent camera pose information in orthodontic imaging for 3D reconstruction. It demonstrates superior novel view synthesis quality for dental occlusion visualization compared to existing state-of-the-art methods, effectively handling both standard and extremely sparse intra-oral photographs.

Clinical Impact

The ability to generate accurate multi-view 3D models of dental occlusion from sparse intra-oral photographs will significantly enhance remote orthodontic consultation and treatment monitoring. This enables more timely and informed clinical decision-making in telemedicine, potentially reducing the need for in-person visits and improving patient access to specialized care, especially in remote areas.

Limitations

While the method effectively handles sparse inputs, the abstract implicitly notes that 'extreme sparsity' still requires additional geometric constraints (optical flow), indicating a threshold where the core technique alone may require augmentation. The method is specifically tailored for a few intra-oral photographs, suggesting its current applicability might be constrained by the input format and number.

Future Directions

Not explicitly mentioned in the abstract, but potential future directions could include exploring real-time applications, reducing the number of input images even further, integrating patient-specific anatomical priors, or extending its use to other craniofacial imaging tasks beyond occlusion.

Abstract

In orthodontic treatment, particularly within telemedicine contexts, observing patients' dental occlusion from multiple viewpoints facilitates timely clinical decision-making. Recent advances in 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) have shown strong potential in 3D reconstruction and novel view synthesis. However, conventional 3DGS pipelines typically rely on densely captured multi-view inputs and precisely initialized camera poses, limiting their practicality. Orthodontic cases, in contrast, often comprise only three sparse images, specifically, the anterior view and bilateral buccal views, rendering the reconstruction task especially challenging. The extreme sparsity of input views severely degrades reconstruction quality, while the absence of camera pose information further complicates the process. To overcome these limitations, we propose DentalSplat, an effective framework for 3D reconstruction from sparse orthodontic imagery. Our method leverages a prior-guided dense stereo reconstruction model to initialize the point cloud, followed by a scale-adaptive pruning strategy to improve the training efficiency and reconstruction quality of 3DGS. In scenarios with extremely sparse viewpoints, we further incorporate optical flow as a geometric constraint, coupled with gradient regularization, to enhance rendering fidelity. We validate our approach on a large-scale dataset comprising 950 clinical cases and an additional video-based test set of 195 cases designed to simulate real-world remote orthodontic imaging conditions. Experimental results demonstrate that our method effectively handles sparse input scenarios and achieves superior novel view synthesis quality for dental occlusion visualization, outperforming state-of-the-art techniques.
