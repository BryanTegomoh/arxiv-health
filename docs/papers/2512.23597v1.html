Scalable Residual Feature Aggregation Framework with Hybrid Metaheuristic Optimization for Robust Early Pancreatic Neoplasm Detection in Multimodal CT Imaging

arXiv ID: 2512.23597v1
Published: 2025-12-29
Authors: Janani Annur Thiruvengadam, Kiran Mayee Nabigaru, Anusha Kovi
Categories: cs.CV, cs.IR
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces the Scalable Residual Feature Aggregation (SRFA) framework, a novel AI-driven system designed for robust early pancreatic neoplasm detection in multimodal CT imaging. The framework integrates advanced deep learning techniques, metaheuristic optimization, and a hybrid classification model to overcome challenges posed by subtle tumor cues and anatomical variations. Experimental results demonstrate a significant performance improvement, achieving 96.23% accuracy, 95.58% F1-score, and 94.83% specificity, substantially outperforming existing CNN and transformer-based methods.

Medical Relevance

Early and accurate detection of pancreatic neoplasm is critically important for improving patient prognosis, as it is often diagnosed at advanced stages. This framework offers a powerful, AI-driven tool that can assist clinicians in identifying subtle tumor indicators earlier, potentially leading to more timely and effective interventions.

AI Health Application

The paper proposes an AI-powered system (Scalable Residual Feature Aggregation framework) for robust early detection of pancreatic neoplasms using multimodal CT imaging. This AI application aims to serve as a diagnostic aid for clinicians, improving the accuracy and efficiency of identifying tumors, which can lead to earlier intervention and better patient outcomes for a challenging disease.

Key Points

  • Addresses the significant clinical challenge of early pancreatic neoplasm detection, which is complicated by minimal contrast margins and high anatomical variability in CT scans.
  • Proposes the Scalable Residual Feature Aggregation (SRFA) framework, a multi-stage pipeline designed for enhancing subtle visual cues and ensuring high generalization on multimodal CT data.
  • Utilizes MAGRes-UNet for effective preprocessing and segmentation, which enhances the visibility of pancreatic structures and isolates specific regions of interest (ROIs).
  • Employs DenseNet-121 with residual feature storage for extracting and aggregating deep hierarchical features, preserving feature properties throughout the process.
  • Integrates a hybrid HHO-BA (Humpback Whale Optimization - Bat Algorithm) metaheuristic strategy for optimal feature subset selection, thereby refining the extracted features.
  • Develops a novel hybrid classification model combining Vision Transformer (ViT) for its global attention capabilities with EfficientNet-B3 for its high representational efficiency.
  • Enhances model robustness and reduces overfitting through a dual hyperparameter optimization mechanism, incorporating Salp Swarm Algorithm (SSA) and Grey Wolf Optimizer (GWO).
  • Achieves superior performance metrics including 96.23% accuracy, 95.58% F1-score, and 94.83% specificity, demonstrating significant improvement over traditional CNNs and other contemporary transformer-based models.

Methodology

The SRFA framework begins with preprocessing and segmentation of multimodal CT images using MAGRes-UNet to delineate pancreatic structures and identify regions of interest. Feature extraction is then performed by DenseNet-121, leveraging residual feature storage to capture deep hierarchical features. A hybrid HHO-BA metaheuristic algorithm refines these extracted features into an optimal subset. For classification, a novel hybrid model integrates the global attention capabilities of Vision Transformer (ViT) with the representational efficiency of EfficientNet-B3. Finally, a dual metaheuristic optimization mechanism, combining SSA and GWO, is applied for hyperparameter tuning to ensure robustness and mitigate overfitting.

Key Findings

The proposed SRFA framework achieved exceptional performance in early pancreatic neoplasm detection, reporting 96.23% accuracy, 95.58% F1-score, and 94.83% specificity. These results represent a significant advancement, with the model notably outperforming both traditional Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and contemporary transformer-based models in comparative evaluations.

Clinical Impact

This framework holds substantial promise for clinical application, potentially serving as a crucial assistive tool for radiologists and oncologists. By enabling earlier and more precise identification of pancreatic neoplasms, even those with subtle radiological signs, it could facilitate earlier staging, optimize treatment planning, and ultimately contribute to improved patient outcomes and survival rates for this challenging cancer.

Abstract

The early detection of pancreatic neoplasm is a major clinical dilemma, and it is predominantly so because tumors are likely to occur with minimal contrast margins and a large spread anatomy-wide variation amongst patients on a CT scan. These complexities require to be addressed with an effective and scalable system that can assist in enhancing the salience of the subtle visual cues and provide a high level of the generalization on the multimodal imaging data. A Scalable Residual Feature Aggregation (SRFA) framework is proposed to be used to meet these conditions in this study. The framework integrates a pipeline of preprocessing followed by the segmentation using the MAGRes-UNet that is effective in making the pancreatic structures and isolating regions of interest more visible. DenseNet-121 performed with residual feature storage is used to extract features to allow deep hierarchical features to be aggregated without properties loss. To go further, hybrid HHO-BA metaheuristic feature selection strategy is used, which guarantees the best feature subset refinement. To be classified, the system is trained based on a new hybrid model that integrates the ability to pay attention on the world, which is the Vision Transformer (ViT) with the high representational efficiency of EfficientNet-B3. A dual optimization mechanism incorporating SSA and GWO is used to fine-tune hyperparameters to enhance greater robustness and less overfitting. Experimental results support the significant improvement in performance, with the suggested model reaching 96.23% accuracy, 95.58% F1-score and 94.83% specificity, the model is significantly better than the traditional CNNs and contemporary transformer-based models. Such results highlight the possibility of the SRFA framework as a useful instrument in the early detection of pancreatic tumors.
