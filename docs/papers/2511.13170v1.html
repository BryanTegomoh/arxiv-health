THIR: Topological Histopathological Image Retrieval

Summary

THIR (Topological Histopathological Image Retrieval) is a novel unsupervised Content-Based Medical Image Retrieval (CBMIR) framework that leverages Betti numbers from persistent homology, a topological data analysis method, to characterize histopathological images based on their intrinsic structural patterns. It demonstrates superior performance compared to state-of-the-art supervised and unsupervised methods on the BreaKHis dataset, offering a fast, scalable, and training-free solution for clinical image retrieval without requiring extensive data annotation or powerful GPU resources.

Medical Relevance

This technology is critical for improving early diagnosis and accurate clinical decision-making in breast cancer by providing a rapid, reliable, and interpretable method for retrieving structurally similar pathological cases, thereby contributing to the reduction of the global breast cancer burden.

AI Health Application

This research presents THIR, a computational framework for Content-Based Medical Image Retrieval (CBMIR) of histopathological images. While it explicitly contrasts itself with conventional deep learning by operating unsupervised and without extensive training, it functions as an AI application in health by assisting in the retrieval of similar medical images. This capability is critical for supporting pathologists in diagnosing breast cancer, improving diagnostic accuracy, and aiding clinical decision-making by providing relevant comparative cases from image databases.

Key Points

  • THIR is an unsupervised Content-Based Medical Image Retrieval (CBMIR) framework specifically designed for histopathological images.
  • It employs Topological Data Analysis (TDA), utilizing Betti numbers derived from cubical persistent homology, to extract intrinsic structural patterns and characterize images.
  • The method generates compact, interpretable "topological fingerprints" as feature vectors, encoding the evolution of topological features like loops in RGB histopathological images.
  • Unlike conventional deep learning, THIR operates entirely without supervision, eliminating the need for extensive training, annotated datasets, or powerful GPU resources.
  • Image similarity retrieval is performed by computing the distances between these topological descriptors, efficiently returning top-K relevant matches.
  • Extensive experiments on the BreaKHis dataset demonstrate that THIR outperforms both state-of-the-art supervised and unsupervised methods.
  • The framework processes the entire BreaKHis dataset in under 20 minutes on a standard CPU, highlighting its speed, scalability, and computational efficiency.

Methodology

The THIR framework applies cubical persistence, a method within Topological Data Analysis (TDA), directly to RGB histopathological images. It extracts Betti numbers, which quantify topological features (e.g., connected components and loops) at varying scales, to generate compact, interpretable "topological fingerprints" as feature vectors. Image retrieval is subsequently achieved by computing the distances between these unsupervisedly derived topological descriptors.

Key Findings

THIR significantly outperforms state-of-the-art supervised and unsupervised methods on the BreaKHis dataset. It demonstrates exceptional computational efficiency, processing the entire dataset in less than 20 minutes on a standard CPU, proving its speed, scalability, and independence from extensive training or powerful GPUs.

Its unsupervised nature and low computational requirements make it highly accessible and adoptable for broader use in digital pathology settings, especially in resource-constrained environments.

Abstract

According to the World Health Organization, breast cancer claimed the lives of approximately 685,000 women in 2020. Early diagnosis and accurate clinical decision making are critical in reducing this global burden. In this study, we propose THIR, a novel Content-Based Medical Image Retrieval (CBMIR) framework that leverages topological data analysis specifically, Betti numbers derived from persistent homology to characterize and retrieve histopathological images based on their intrinsic structural patterns. Unlike conventional deep learning approaches that rely on extensive training, annotated datasets, and powerful GPU resources, THIR operates entirely without supervision. It extracts topological fingerprints directly from RGB histopathological images using cubical persistence, encoding the evolution of loops as compact, interpretable feature vectors. The similarity retrieval is then performed by computing the distances between these topological descriptors, efficiently returning the top-K most relevant matches. Extensive experiments on the BreaKHis dataset demonstrate that THIR outperforms state of the art supervised and unsupervised methods. It processes the entire dataset in under 20 minutes on a standard CPU, offering a fast, scalable, and training free solution for clinical image retrieval.
