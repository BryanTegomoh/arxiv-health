Bayesian model selection and misspecification testing in imaging inverse problems only from noisy and partial measurements

arXiv ID: 2510.27663v1
Published: 2025-10-31
Authors: Tom Sprunck, Marcelo Pereyra, Tobias Liaudat
Categories: eess.IV, cs.LG, stat.ME, stat.ML

Summary

This paper introduces a novel methodology for unsupervised model selection and misspecification testing in Bayesian imaging inverse problems, particularly when ground truth data is unavailable. By combining Bayesian cross-validation with a randomized measurement splitting technique called data fission, the approach effectively evaluates imaging models, including those with modern machine learning-defined priors. The methodology demonstrates excellent selection and detection accuracy with low computational cost, making it highly suitable for complex computational imaging tasks.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine as modern medical imaging heavily relies on advanced Bayesian models for image reconstruction and enhancement from noisy, partial data. The ability to objectively evaluate, select, and diagnose misspecification in these models without ground truth ensures the reliability and accuracy of diagnostic images, directly impacting patient care and treatment decisions.

AI Health Application

This research provides a critical methodology for evaluating the underlying Bayesian and machine learning models (including diffusion and plug-and-play samplers) used in medical AI applications for image reconstruction and restoration. By enabling better model selection and misspecification detection, it contributes to the development of more reliable, accurate, and trustworthy AI-powered medical imaging systems, thereby enhancing diagnostic capabilities and ensuring patient safety in healthcare settings.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of objectively evaluating Bayesian imaging statistical models (model selection and misspecification diagnosis) without access to ground truth, a common scenario in many applications.
  • Identifies significant limitations of existing unsupervised model evaluation methods, including high computational cost and incompatibility with modern implicit image priors derived from machine learning (e.g., diffusion models, plug-and-play samplers).
  • Proposes a novel, general methodology that integrates Bayesian cross-validation with 'data fission,' a technique for random splitting of measurement data, to enable robust model assessment.
  • The developed approach is compatible with a wide range of Bayesian imaging samplers, including advanced, implicitly defined priors like diffusion samplers and plug-and-play methods.
  • Experimental results demonstrate excellent accuracy in both selecting the best model among candidates and detecting instances of model misspecification.
  • A key benefit of the proposed methodology is its low computational cost, making it practical for real-world imaging inverse problems.
  • The work focuses on providing unsupervised evaluation criteria, allowing for model assessment directly from noisy and partial measurements without the need for pristine reference images.

Methodology

The proposed methodology combines Bayesian cross-validation with data fission, a randomized measurement splitting technique. This allows for the unsupervised evaluation of Bayesian imaging models by splitting noisy and partial measurements into training and validation sets. It utilizes various scoring rules to assess model performance and is designed to be compatible with any Bayesian imaging sampler, including those employing implicit priors from modern machine learning models like diffusion and plug-and-play samplers.

Key Findings

The study successfully demonstrated that its novel combination of Bayesian cross-validation and data fission achieves excellent accuracy in both model selection and misspecification detection. Crucially, this is accomplished with a low computational cost and is compatible with advanced, implicitly defined machine learning priors, overcoming major limitations of prior evaluation methods.

This research has significant clinical impact by providing a robust, unsupervised framework for evaluating and selecting the most appropriate Bayesian models for medical image reconstruction and analysis. This capability ensures higher confidence in images used for diagnosis, treatment planning, and monitoring, potentially leading to more accurate diagnoses, reduced false positives/negatives, and improved patient outcomes. It facilitates the safe and effective adoption of cutting-edge AI-powered imaging techniques in clinical practice.

Abstract

Modern imaging techniques heavily rely on Bayesian statistical models to address difficult image reconstruction and restoration tasks. This paper addresses the objective evaluation of such models in settings where ground truth is unavailable, with a focus on model selection and misspecification diagnosis. Existing unsupervised model evaluation methods are often unsuitable for computational imaging due to their high computational cost and incompatibility with modern image priors defined implicitly via machine learning models. We herein propose a general methodology for unsupervised model selection and misspecification detection in Bayesian imaging sciences, based on a novel combination of Bayesian cross-validation and data fission, a randomized measurement splitting technique. The approach is compatible with any Bayesian imaging sampler, including diffusion and plug-and-play samplers. We demonstrate the methodology through experiments involving various scoring rules and types of model misspecification, where we achieve excellent selection and detection accuracy with a low computational cost.
