Bayesian model selection and misspecification testing in imaging inverse problems only from noisy and partial measurements

Summary

This paper introduces a novel, general methodology for the unsupervised evaluation of Bayesian statistical models in computational imaging, focusing on model selection and misspecification testing when ground truth is unavailable. By combining Bayesian cross-validation with data fission, the approach efficiently and accurately assesses imaging models, including those with implicitly defined machine learning priors. This advancement is crucial for ensuring the reliability and robustness of image reconstruction and restoration techniques across various modern imaging samplers.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and reliable medical imaging is paramount for diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment planning. This research provides a robust, unsupervised framework to objectively evaluate and select optimal Bayesian models for medical image reconstruction and restoration, directly improving the quality and trustworthiness of diagnostic images despite inherent noise and data incompleteness.

AI Health Application

The proposed methodology would be used by researchers and developers of AI models in medical imaging to objectively evaluate, select, and diagnose issues in Bayesian models used for tasks like image reconstruction (e.g., from raw MRI k-space data), denoising, super-resolution, and synthesis. This is crucial for ensuring the accuracy, reliability, and clinical applicability of AI-powered diagnostic and therapeutic imaging tools, particularly when ground truth data is scarce, aiding in the development of safer and more effective medical AI systems.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of objectively evaluating Bayesian imaging models in settings where ground truth data is inaccessible.
  • Highlights the limitations of existing unsupervised evaluation methods, specifically their high computational cost and incompatibility with modern machine learning-based image priors (e.g., implicit priors via diffusion or plug-and-play models).
  • Proposes a general methodology for unsupervised model selection and misspecification detection by combining Bayesian cross-validation with data fission, a randomized measurement splitting technique.
  • The approach is highly versatile and compatible with any Bayesian imaging sampler, including advanced techniques like diffusion samplers and plug-and-play samplers.
  • Demonstrates excellent accuracy in both model selection (choosing the best model from a set) and the detection of model misspecification (identifying when a chosen model is inadequate).
  • Achieves these high-accuracy results across various scoring rules and types of model misspecification, while maintaining a notably low computational cost.
  • Focuses on solving imaging inverse problems, which are fundamental to reconstructing images from noisy and partial measurements in real-world scenarios.

Methodology

The proposed methodology combines Bayesian cross-validation with data fission, a technique that involves randomly splitting the raw measurement data into independent subsets. This unsupervised approach does not require access to ground truth images. It is designed for broad compatibility, supporting any Bayesian imaging sampler, including modern methods like diffusion models and plug-and-play (PnP) samplers, allowing for its integration into diverse imaging pipelines.

Key Findings

The study demonstrates that the proposed methodology achieves excellent accuracy in both model selection and the detection of model misspecification. These highly accurate results were consistent across experiments involving various scoring rules and different types of model errors, all while maintaining a remarkably low computational cost, making the approach practical for real-world applications.

Clinical Impact

By enabling robust and unsupervised evaluation of imaging models, this methodology can significantly enhance the reliability and diagnostic utility of medical image interpretation. Clinicians can benefit from more accurately reconstructed images, potentially leading to earlier and more precise disease detection, improved treatment planning, and better monitoring of patient responses. It also facilitates the faster and more confident adoption of advanced, AI-driven imaging reconstruction algorithms in clinical practice by providing a reliable validation framework.

Abstract

Modern imaging techniques heavily rely on Bayesian statistical models to address difficult image reconstruction and restoration tasks. This paper addresses the objective evaluation of such models in settings where ground truth is unavailable, with a focus on model selection and misspecification diagnosis. Existing unsupervised model evaluation methods are often unsuitable for computational imaging due to their high computational cost and incompatibility with modern image priors defined implicitly via machine learning models. We herein propose a general methodology for unsupervised model selection and misspecification detection in Bayesian imaging sciences, based on a novel combination of Bayesian cross-validation and data fission, a randomized measurement splitting technique. The approach is compatible with any Bayesian imaging sampler, including diffusion and plug-and-play samplers. We demonstrate the methodology through experiments involving various scoring rules and types of model misspecification, where we achieve excellent selection and detection accuracy with a low computational cost.
