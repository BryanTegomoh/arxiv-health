Summary

This paper introduces GRACE, a novel Graph Neural Network (GNN) framework, designed to automate the critical clinical decision of determining the appropriate locus of care for addiction patients. By formalizing this as a structured learning problem and employing extensive feature engineering with an unbiased meta-graph approach, GRACE effectively mitigates severe class imbalance in addiction datasets. The framework demonstrates significant improvements, achieving an 11-35% increase in the F1 score for the minority class over competitive baselines on real-world data.

Medical Relevance

Accurate prediction of the locus of care for addiction patients is paramount for optimizing individual treatment trajectories and ensuring the judicious allocation of limited specialized healthcare resources, such as inpatient beds and qualified staff. This directly contributes to better patient outcomes and addresses a significant unmet need in addiction medicine.

AI Health Application

An AI-driven clinical decision support system that leverages graph neural networks to predict the optimal locus of care for addiction patients, thereby assisting clinicians in making better resource allocation decisions and improving patient treatment outcomes.

Methodology

The GRACE framework utilizes a Graph Neural Network (GNN) to formalize the locus of care prediction as a structured learning problem. This involves extensive feature engineering to create comprehensive patient representations. A key methodological innovation is the development of a novel approach to obtain an unbiased meta-graph, which is then used to train the GNN. This meta-graph construction is specifically designed to counteract the severe class imbalance typically found in addiction patient datasets, thereby improving the model's ability to learn from underrepresented classes.

Key Findings

The GRACE framework demonstrated a significant improvement in predictive performance, specifically for the underrepresented patient groups. Experimental results on real-world data showed an improvement of 11-35% in terms of the F1 score of the minority class over competitive baseline models.

Abstract

Determining the appropriate locus of care for addiction patients is one of the most critical clinical decisions that affects patient treatment outcomes and effective use of resources. With a lack of sufficient specialized treatment resources, such as inpatient beds or staff, there is an unmet need to develop an automated framework for the same. Current decision-making approaches suffer from severe class imbalances in addiction datasets. To address this limitation, we propose a novel graph neural network (GRACE) framework that formalizes locus of care prediction as a structured learning problem. Further, we perform extensive feature engineering and propose a new approach of obtaining an unbiased meta-graph to train a GNN to overcome the class imbalance problem. Experimental results in real-world data show an improvement of 11-35% in terms of the F1 score of the minority class over competitive baselines.
