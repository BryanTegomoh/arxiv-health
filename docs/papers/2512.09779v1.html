PathCo-LatticE: Pathology-Constrained Lattice-Of Experts Framework for Fully-supervised Few-Shot Cardiac MRI Segmentation

arXiv ID: 2512.09779v1
Published: 2025-12-10
Authors: Mohamed Elbayumi, Mohammed S. M. Elbaz
Categories: eess.IV, cs.AI, cs.CV, cs.LG

Summary

This paper introduces PathCo-LatticE, a novel fully supervised Few-Shot Learning (FSL) framework for cardiac MRI segmentation that overcomes data scarcity and domain shift issues by leveraging pathology-guided synthetic supervision. It employs a Virtual Patient Engine to generate physiologically plausible 3D cohorts, a Self-Reinforcing Interleaved Validation protocol for leakage-free evaluation, and a dynamic Lattice-of-Experts for robust zero-shot generalization, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art methods with minimal labeled real data.

Medical Relevance

This research addresses the critical challenge of data scarcity in medical image analysis, particularly for cardiac MRI segmentation, enabling the development of robust AI models with significantly less manual annotation effort, which is vital for clinical translation and broader applicability across diverse patient populations and imaging protocols.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of a robust Few-Shot Learning (FSL) framework for segmenting cardiac MRI images. This involves using generative AI to create synthetic, pathology-guided medical data ('Virtual Patient Engine') to train models more effectively with limited real data. The aim is to enhance the diagnostic capabilities in cardiology by providing highly accurate and generalizable AI models for medical image analysis, enabling better detection and assessment of heart conditions from MRI scans, even for rare diseases or challenging out-of-distribution data.

Key Points

  • PathCo-LatticE is a fully supervised FSL framework designed for cardiac MRI segmentation, addressing limitations of semi-supervised FSL methods like sensitivity to domain shifts and validation bias.
  • It replaces unlabeled data with pathology-guided synthetic supervision generated by a 'Virtual Patient Engine' that models continuous latent disease trajectories from sparse clinical anchors using generative modeling.
  • A 'Self-Reinforcing Interleaved Validation (SIV)' protocol provides leakage-free online model evaluation using progressively challenging synthetic samples, removing the need for real validation data.
  • A 'dynamic Lattice-of-Experts (LoE)' organizes specialized networks in a pathology-aware topology, activating relevant experts per input for robust zero-shot generalization to unseen data without fine-tuning.
  • Evaluated in a strict out-of-distribution (OOD) setting (ACDC for anchors, M&Ms for zero-shot testing), PathCo-LatticE significantly outperforms four SOTA FSL methods.
  • Achieves 4.2-11% higher Dice scores starting from only 7 labeled anchors and approaches fully supervised performance (within 1% Dice) with just 19 labeled anchors.
  • Demonstrates superior harmonization across four different vendors and effective generalization to unseen cardiac pathologies.

Methodology

The methodology comprises three core components: 1) A Virtual Patient Engine that uses generative modeling to synthesize fully labeled 3D cardiac MRI cohorts by modeling continuous latent disease trajectories from sparse clinical anchors. 2) Self-Reinforcing Interleaved Validation (SIV), a leakage-free protocol for online model evaluation using progressively challenging synthetic samples, obviating the need for real validation data. 3) A dynamic Lattice-of-Experts (LoE) that organizes specialized segmentation networks based on a pathology-aware topology, dynamically activating the most relevant expert(s) for a given input to achieve robust zero-shot generalization.

Key Findings

PathCo-LatticE significantly outperforms state-of-the-art FSL methods by 4.2-11% Dice scores with as few as 7 labeled real data anchors. It achieves near fully supervised performance (within 1% Dice) using only 19 labeled anchors. The framework demonstrates superior harmonization across four distinct MRI vendors and robust generalization capabilities to previously unseen pathologies in a strict out-of-distribution evaluation setting.

Clinical Impact

The ability to achieve high-performance cardiac MRI segmentation with minimal labeled data will accelerate the development and deployment of AI tools in clinical settings. This approach can reduce the significant burden of manual annotation, enable more generalized models that work across different scanner vendors and patient populations, and facilitate research into rare cardiac conditions where data is inherently scarce, ultimately improving diagnostic accuracy and efficiency.

Abstract

Few-shot learning (FSL) mitigates data scarcity in cardiac MRI segmentation but typically relies on semi-supervised techniques sensitive to domain shifts and validation bias, restricting zero-shot generalizability. We propose PathCo-LatticE, a fully supervised FSL framework that replaces unlabeled data with pathology-guided synthetic supervision. First, our Virtual Patient Engine models continuous latent disease trajectories from sparse clinical anchors, using generative modeling to synthesize physiologically plausible, fully labeled 3D cohorts. Second, Self-Reinforcing Interleaved Validation (SIV) provides a leakage-free protocol that evaluates models online with progressively challenging synthetic samples, eliminating the need for real validation data. Finally, a dynamic Lattice-of-Experts (LoE) organizes specialized networks within a pathology-aware topology and activates the most relevant experts per input, enabling robust zero-shot generalization to unseen data without target-domain fine-tuning. We evaluated PathCo-LatticE in a strict out-of-distribution (OOD) setting, deriving all anchors and severity statistics from a single-source domain (ACDC) and performing zero-shot testing on the multi-center, multi-vendor M&Ms dataset. PathCo-LatticE outperforms four state-of-the-art FSL methods by 4.2-11% Dice starting from only 7 labeled anchors, and approaches fully supervised performance (within 1% Dice) with only 19 labeled anchors. The method shows superior harmonization across four vendors and generalization to unseen pathologies. [Code will be made publicly available].
