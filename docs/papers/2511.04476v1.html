Summary

This paper introduces PTTSD (Probabilistic Textual Time Series Depression Detection), a novel framework for predicting PHQ-8 depression severity scores from utterance-level clinical interview text. PTTSD innovatively models uncertainty over time, offering well-calibrated prediction intervals in addition to point estimates. It achieves state-of-the-art performance among text-only systems on E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ datasets, demonstrating high interpretability and clinical relevance of its uncertainty-aware forecasting.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to mental health as it provides a more nuanced and reliable approach to assessing depression severity. By quantifying uncertainty and integrating temporal dynamics, it offers clinicians richer, actionable insights for diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient monitoring, moving beyond simple point estimates.

AI Health Application

The AI application is an NLP and time series framework (PTTSD) designed to predict depression severity (PHQ-8 scores) from textual data derived from clinical interviews. It aims to provide uncertainty-aware forecasts to support clinical decision-making for mental health professionals.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: Addresses the critical need for uncertainty estimates and temporal modeling in depression severity prediction to enhance clinical decision support, which is often lacking in existing models.
  • **Proposed Framework (PTTSD)**: Introduces a Probabilistic Textual Time Series Depression Detection framework that predicts PHQ-8 scores from utterance-level clinical interview transcripts, explicitly modeling uncertainty over time.
  • **Methodology**: PTTSD incorporates sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one deep learning variants, combining bidirectional LSTMs, self-attention mechanisms, and residual connections. It utilizes probabilistic Gaussian or Student-t output heads, trained via negative log-likelihood, to quantify prediction uncertainty.
  • **State-of-the-Art Performance**: Achieves leading performance among text-only systems on standard datasets (E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ), exemplified by competitive Mean Absolute Error (MAE) values (e.g., 3.85 on E-DAIC, 3.55 on DAIC).
  • **Uncertainty Quantification**: A core contribution is the generation of well-calibrated prediction intervals, providing robust uncertainty estimates alongside severity predictions, which is vital for clinical interpretation.
  • **Validation and Generality**: Ablation studies confirm the significant contributions of self-attention and the probabilistic modeling approach. Comparisons with MentalBERT further establish the framework's generality and robustness.
  • **Clinical Interpretability**: A detailed three-part calibration analysis and qualitative case studies highlight the practical interpretability and clinical relevance of integrating uncertainty into depression severity forecasting.

Methodology

PTTSD is a deep learning framework built on bidirectional LSTMs, self-attention mechanisms, and residual connections, available in sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one variants. It employs probabilistic Gaussian or Student-t output heads to model uncertainty and is trained using negative log-likelihood. Evaluation was conducted on E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ datasets, supported by ablation studies and comparisons with MentalBERT for robustness and generality.

Key Findings

PTTSD achieved state-of-the-art performance in PHQ-8 score prediction for text-only systems (MAE of 3.85 on E-DAIC, 3.55 on DAIC). It successfully produced well-calibrated prediction intervals, effectively quantifying uncertainty over time. Ablation studies confirmed the value of attention and probabilistic modeling, while comparisons with MentalBERT established the model's generality. The interpretability and clinical relevance of uncertainty-aware forecasting were further demonstrated through calibration analysis and qualitative case studies.

Clinical Impact

PTTSD has the potential to significantly enhance clinical decision-making in mental health by providing clinicians with more informative and reliable depression severity assessments. The inclusion of uncertainty estimates can guide more precise interventions, improve risk stratification, and facilitate personalized care pathways, potentially improving patient outcomes and allowing for earlier, more targeted support in settings like tele-mental health.

Abstract

Accurate and interpretable predictions of depression severity are essential for clinical decision support, yet existing models often lack uncertainty estimates and temporal modeling. We propose PTTSD, a Probabilistic Textual Time Series Depression Detection framework that predicts PHQ-8 scores from utterance-level clinical interviews while modeling uncertainty over time. PTTSD includes sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one variants, both combining bidirectional LSTMs, self-attention, and residual connections with Gaussian or Student-t output heads trained via negative log-likelihood. Evaluated on E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ, PTTSD achieves state-of-the-art performance among text-only systems (e.g., MAE = 3.85 on E-DAIC, 3.55 on DAIC) and produces well-calibrated prediction intervals. Ablations confirm the value of attention and probabilistic modeling, while comparisons with MentalBERT establish generality. A three-part calibration analysis and qualitative case studies further highlight the interpretability and clinical relevance of uncertainty-aware forecasting.
