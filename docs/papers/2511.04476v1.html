Summary

This paper introduces PTTSD, a novel Probabilistic Textual Time Series Depression Detection framework that predicts PHQ-8 scores from clinical interview utterances while explicitly modeling temporal uncertainty. Leveraging advanced deep learning architectures with probabilistic output heads, PTTSD achieves state-of-the-art performance among text-only systems and provides well-calibrated prediction intervals, enhancing clinical decision support.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a more robust and interpretable tool for predicting depression severity, offering clinicians crucial uncertainty estimates and temporal insights from patient interviews. This can significantly improve the accuracy of diagnosis, monitoring, and personalized treatment planning in mental health care.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of a probabilistic textual time series model (PTTSD) to detect and predict the severity of depression from spoken or written clinical interviews. This AI system could serve as a clinical decision support tool to assist healthcare professionals in assessing mental health conditions, monitoring patient progress, and aiding in early intervention for depression.

Key Points

  • PTTSD is designed to predict PHQ-8 depression severity scores directly from utterance-level clinical interview transcripts.
  • The framework explicitly models uncertainty over time, addressing a critical gap in existing depression detection models.
  • It incorporates advanced deep learning components, including bidirectional LSTMs, self-attention, and residual connections, with probabilistic Gaussian or Student-t output heads.
  • PTTSD is trained using negative log-likelihood, allowing it to generate not just point estimates but also prediction intervals for depression severity.
  • Evaluated on E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ datasets, PTTSD achieved state-of-the-art performance for text-only systems (e.g., MAE = 3.85 on E-DAIC, 3.55 on DAIC).
  • The model produces well-calibrated prediction intervals, which are crucial for reliable clinical interpretation and decision-making.
  • Ablation studies confirmed the significant contributions of both the self-attention mechanism and the probabilistic modeling approach to the framework's effectiveness.

Methodology

PTTSD is a deep learning framework offered in sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one variants. It processes utterance-level clinical interview text using bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks to capture temporal context, augmented with self-attention mechanisms for weighting important linguistic features and residual connections for stable training. The core innovation lies in its probabilistic output heads (Gaussian or Student-t distributions), which predict both the mean and variance of PHQ-8 scores, trained via negative log-likelihood to enable robust uncertainty quantification over time.

Key Findings

The PTTSD framework achieved state-of-the-art performance among text-only depression detection systems, demonstrating MAE scores of 3.85 on the E-DAIC dataset and 3.55 on DAIC-WOZ. It successfully produces well-calibrated prediction intervals, directly addressing the need for uncertainty quantification in clinical predictions. Ablation studies confirmed the essential contributions of both self-attention and the probabilistic modeling approach, validating these design choices for improved accuracy and reliability.

Clinical Impact

This research has substantial clinical impact by offering a robust, interpretable, and uncertainty-aware method for predicting depression severity. By providing well-calibrated prediction intervals alongside severity scores, PTTSD can aid clinicians in making more informed decisions regarding patient assessment, treatment monitoring, and risk stratification. The ability to track changes and quantify confidence over time from interview text supports more personalized and evidence-based mental healthcare interventions.

Abstract

Accurate and interpretable predictions of depression severity are essential for clinical decision support, yet existing models often lack uncertainty estimates and temporal modeling. We propose PTTSD, a Probabilistic Textual Time Series Depression Detection framework that predicts PHQ-8 scores from utterance-level clinical interviews while modeling uncertainty over time. PTTSD includes sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one variants, both combining bidirectional LSTMs, self-attention, and residual connections with Gaussian or Student-t output heads trained via negative log-likelihood. Evaluated on E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ, PTTSD achieves state-of-the-art performance among text-only systems (e.g., MAE = 3.85 on E-DAIC, 3.55 on DAIC) and produces well-calibrated prediction intervals. Ablations confirm the value of attention and probabilistic modeling, while comparisons with MentalBERT establish generality. A three-part calibration analysis and qualitative case studies further highlight the interpretability and clinical relevance of uncertainty-aware forecasting.
