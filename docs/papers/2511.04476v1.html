Summary

PTTSD is a novel probabilistic framework designed for detecting depression severity from utterance-level clinical interviews by predicting PHQ-8 scores. It significantly improves upon existing models by explicitly modeling uncertainty over time, offering state-of-the-art performance among text-only systems and providing well-calibrated, interpretable, and clinically relevant uncertainty-aware forecasts for decision support.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to mental healthcare as it provides a more nuanced and robust tool for assessing depression severity. By incorporating uncertainty estimates, clinicians can make more informed and cautious decisions, particularly in cases where a precise point estimate might be insufficient or misleading, thereby improving patient care and risk management.

AI Health Application

This research proposes PTTSD, an AI framework utilizing deep learning (LSTMs, self-attention) and probabilistic modeling to predict depression severity (PHQ-8 scores) from textual clinical interviews. It aims to provide AI-driven clinical decision support by offering accurate, interpretable, and uncertainty-aware forecasts of a patient's depression state over time.

Key Points

  • PTTSD predicts PHQ-8 depression scores directly from textual, utterance-level clinical interview data.
  • It is a probabilistic time series model that explicitly quantifies uncertainty over time, utilizing Gaussian or Student-t output heads trained via negative log-likelihood.
  • The framework incorporates a deep learning architecture combining bidirectional LSTMs, self-attention mechanisms, and residual connections, with both sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one variants.
  • PTTSD achieves state-of-the-art performance among text-only depression detection systems on E-DAIC (MAE=3.85) and DAIC-WOZ (MAE=3.55) datasets.
  • A critical feature is its ability to produce well-calibrated prediction intervals, which enhances the reliability and interpretability of its forecasts.
  • Ablation studies confirmed the significant value and necessity of both self-attention and probabilistic modeling components within the PTTSD architecture.
  • The research emphasizes the interpretability and clinical relevance of uncertainty-aware forecasting, making predictions more useful for clinicians.

Methodology

PTTSD is a deep learning framework implemented in sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one variants. It processes utterance-level textual data using bidirectional LSTMs, incorporates self-attention for capturing contextual dependencies, and employs residual connections for enhanced training stability. The model's output layer utilizes Gaussian or Student-t distributions, trained via negative log-likelihood, to predict PHQ-8 scores along with associated uncertainty. Evaluation involved standard metrics (e.g., MAE) on E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ datasets, complemented by ablation studies and a three-part calibration analysis.

Key Findings

PTTSD demonstrated state-of-the-art performance for text-only depression detection, achieving MAEs of 3.85 on E-DAIC and 3.55 on DAIC-WOZ. A key finding is its ability to generate well-calibrated prediction intervals, signifying reliable uncertainty estimates. Ablation studies rigorously confirmed the critical contributions of both attention mechanisms and the probabilistic modeling approach to the overall effectiveness and utility of the framework.

This allows clinicians to assess the certainty of a diagnosis, identify ambiguous cases that require closer scrutiny, or prioritize patients based on the model's confidence. The interpretable uncertainty estimates facilitate more transparent clinical communication, support personalized treatment adjustments, and empower more confident clinical decision-making, ultimately leading to improved patient management and outcomes in mental health.

Abstract

Accurate and interpretable predictions of depression severity are essential for clinical decision support, yet existing models often lack uncertainty estimates and temporal modeling. We propose PTTSD, a Probabilistic Textual Time Series Depression Detection framework that predicts PHQ-8 scores from utterance-level clinical interviews while modeling uncertainty over time. PTTSD includes sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one variants, both combining bidirectional LSTMs, self-attention, and residual connections with Gaussian or Student-t output heads trained via negative log-likelihood. Evaluated on E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ, PTTSD achieves state-of-the-art performance among text-only systems (e.g., MAE = 3.85 on E-DAIC, 3.55 on DAIC) and produces well-calibrated prediction intervals. Ablations confirm the value of attention and probabilistic modeling, while comparisons with MentalBERT establish generality. A three-part calibration analysis and qualitative case studies further highlight the interpretability and clinical relevance of uncertainty-aware forecasting.
