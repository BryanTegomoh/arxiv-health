Probabilistic Textual Time Series Depression Detection

Summary

This paper introduces PTTSD (Probabilistic Textual Time Series Depression Detection), a novel framework for predicting PHQ-8 depression severity scores from utterance-level clinical interviews. PTTSD uniquely models uncertainty over time, achieving state-of-the-art performance among text-only systems on E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ datasets while providing well-calibrated prediction intervals essential for clinical decision support.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and interpretable prediction of depression severity, particularly when accompanied by quantifiable uncertainty, is vital for clinical decision-making. This framework offers clinicians more reliable insights into patient mental states, enabling better-informed diagnostic and treatment strategies.

AI Health Application

The AI application is a 'Probabilistic Textual Time Series Depression Detection' (PTTSD) framework. It utilizes advanced AI techniques (bidirectional LSTMs, self-attention, residual connections) to analyze textual data from patient-clinician interviews. The goal is to probabilistically predict depression severity, measured by PHQ-8 scores, over time, providing uncertainty estimates that are valuable for clinical decision-making and supporting mental health professionals in diagnosis and monitoring.

Key Points

  • PTTSD predicts PHQ-8 depression severity scores from textual, utterance-level clinical interviews, addressing the lack of uncertainty estimates and temporal modeling in existing systems.
  • The framework incorporates deep learning architectures including bidirectional LSTMs, self-attention, and residual connections, available in both sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one variants.
  • Probabilistic output heads (Gaussian or Student-t distributions) are employed, trained via negative log-likelihood, to explicitly model prediction uncertainty over time.
  • PTTSD achieves state-of-the-art performance among text-only depression detection systems, with MAE values of 3.85 on E-DAIC and 3.55 on DAIC-WOZ datasets.
  • The model produces well-calibrated prediction intervals, significantly enhancing the reliability and trustworthiness of its severity estimates for clinical application.
  • Ablation studies confirm the critical value of both self-attention mechanisms and the probabilistic modeling approach, while comparisons with MentalBERT establish the framework's generality.
  • A three-part calibration analysis and qualitative case studies highlight the interpretability and direct clinical relevance of the uncertainty-aware forecasts.

Methodology

PTTSD is a deep learning framework that processes utterance-level clinical interview text. It comprises sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one variants, built upon bidirectional LSTMs for sequential context, self-attention mechanisms to capture long-range dependencies, and residual connections. The framework distinguishes itself by using probabilistic output heads (Gaussian or Student-t distributions) trained via negative log-likelihood, allowing it to predict not just a score but also its uncertainty as prediction intervals. Evaluation was conducted on the E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ datasets, employing ablation studies to validate architectural components and comparisons with MentalBERT to demonstrate generalizability.

Key Findings

The PTTSD framework achieved state-of-the-art performance among text-only depression detection systems, evidenced by MAE scores of 3.85 on E-DAIC and 3.55 on DAIC-WOZ. Crucially, it demonstrated the ability to produce well-calibrated prediction intervals, effectively quantifying uncertainty in its predictions. Ablation studies confirmed the significant contributions of attention mechanisms and the probabilistic modeling aspect. The model's generality was established through comparisons with MentalBERT, and its clinical relevance and interpretability were further highlighted by a three-part calibration analysis and qualitative case studies.

Clinical Impact

This framework offers a significant advancement for clinical practice by providing objective and interpretable assessments of depression severity from patient interviews, coupled with crucial uncertainty estimates. Clinicians can leverage these well-calibrated prediction intervals to gauge the confidence of a severity score, potentially guiding more personalized and timely interventions, facilitating early detection of worsening symptoms, and improving the overall robustness of clinical decision-making in mental healthcare settings.

Abstract

Accurate and interpretable predictions of depression severity are essential for clinical decision support, yet existing models often lack uncertainty estimates and temporal modeling. We propose PTTSD, a Probabilistic Textual Time Series Depression Detection framework that predicts PHQ-8 scores from utterance-level clinical interviews while modeling uncertainty over time. PTTSD includes sequence-to-sequence and sequence-to-one variants, both combining bidirectional LSTMs, self-attention, and residual connections with Gaussian or Student-t output heads trained via negative log-likelihood. Evaluated on E-DAIC and DAIC-WOZ, PTTSD achieves state-of-the-art performance among text-only systems (e.g., MAE = 3.85 on E-DAIC, 3.55 on DAIC) and produces well-calibrated prediction intervals. Ablations confirm the value of attention and probabilistic modeling, while comparisons with MentalBERT establish generality. A three-part calibration analysis and qualitative case studies further highlight the interpretability and clinical relevance of uncertainty-aware forecasting.
