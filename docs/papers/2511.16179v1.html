Age-structured model of dengue transmission dynamics with time-varying parameters, and its application to Brazil

Summary

This paper develops and analyzes an age-structured mathematical model with time-dependent parameters to investigate dengue transmission dynamics. It applies this model to Brazil using weekly time series data, evaluating medical and environmental parameters to estimate time-varying effective reproduction numbers and predict future trends, ultimately highlighting the critical roles of age distribution, vector dynamics, and climate.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a more sophisticated understanding of dengue transmission by integrating age structure, time-varying parameters, and environmental factors, which is critical for developing precise public health interventions, targeted vaccination strategies, and more accurate outbreak predictions.

Methodology

The study employs an age-structured mathematical model with time-dependent parameters. It involves theoretical analysis of model properties (e.g., disease-free steady state, basic and effective reproduction numbers via Euler, Lotka, and Next Generation Matrix methods). The model is then applied through numerical simulations using weekly epidemiological and environmental time series data from Brazil. Parameter estimation for transmission rates is derived from both epidemiological and climate (temperature, humidity) data. Sensitivity analysis is conducted to identify influential parameters.

Key Findings

The study found that population age distribution, vector population dynamics, and climatic conditions (temperature, humidity) are significant factors contributing to dengue transmission dynamics in Brazil. The model successfully estimated time-varying effective reproduction numbers and identified sensitive parameters, providing a deeper, more nuanced understanding of dengue spread.

It can inform public health strategies by identifying vulnerable age groups, predicting seasonal variations based on climate, and guiding resource allocation for vector control and vaccination campaigns more precisely.

Abstract

An age structured mathematical model with time dependent parameters is developed to investigate the dynamics of dengue transmission. Its properties are thoroughly analyzed in the first part of this work, as for example its disease free steady state, the corresponding effective reproduction numbers, its basic reproduction number (obtained via the Euler and Lotka equation and the next generation matrix approach). We also provide formulas for the time-varying effective reproduction number, and draw relations with the instantaneous growth rate. In the second part, we apply this model to Brazil and use weekly time series data from this country. Various medical parameters are firstly evaluated from these data, and an extensive numerical simulations for the period 2021 to 2024 is then carried out. Estimation of the transmission rates are derived both from epidemiological data and from environmental data such as temperature and humidity. The time-varying effective reproduction numbers are then estimated on these data, following the theoretical investigations performed in the first part. The sensitive parameters that significantly affect the model dynamics are presented graphically. Model predictions for following year by using different transmission rates are finally presented. Our findings show the importance of population age distribution, vector population dynamics, and climate, contributing to a deeper understanding of dengue transmission dynamics in Brazil.
