Age-structured model of dengue transmission dynamics with time-varying parameters, and its application to Brazil

Summary

This paper develops and analyzes a novel age-structured mathematical model with time-varying parameters to investigate the complex dynamics of dengue transmission. It provides comprehensive theoretical characterizations, including various reproduction numbers, and applies the model to Brazil using real-world epidemiological and climate data. The research highlights the critical influence of population age distribution, vector dynamics, and climate on dengue spread, offering valuable predictions and a deeper understanding of the disease in the region.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a robust epidemiological tool for understanding, predicting, and ultimately controlling dengue epidemics. By integrating age structure and time-varying environmental factors, it offers a more nuanced view critical for designing effective public health interventions and resource allocation strategies against this significant vector-borne disease.

Key Points

  • Development of an age-structured mathematical model with time-dependent parameters specifically designed for dengue transmission dynamics.

Methodology

The methodology involves developing an age-structured mathematical model with time-dependent parameters for dengue transmission. This includes theoretical analysis to identify the disease-free steady state, derive the basic reproduction number (R0) using three different approaches (Euler, Lotka, next generation matrix), and formulate the time-varying effective reproduction number (Rt) along with its relation to the instantaneous growth rate. The model is then applied to weekly epidemiological and environmental (temperature, humidity) time series data from Brazil. Medical parameters and transmission rates are estimated from these data, followed by extensive numerical simulations, sensitivity analysis, and model predictions.

Key Findings

The study successfully developed and theoretically validated an age-structured model incorporating time-varying parameters, providing specific formulas for R0 and Rt. Its application to Brazil demonstrated that population age distribution, vector population dynamics, and climate (temperature, humidity) are crucial and sensitive factors significantly influencing dengue transmission. The model effectively estimated time-varying Rt from real-world data and provided actionable predictions for future dengue spread.

Clinical Impact

The ability of this model to estimate real-time effective reproduction numbers and provide future predictions offers a powerful tool for public health decision-makers. It can facilitate the proactive allocation of resources for vector control, timely implementation of public awareness campaigns, and preparedness for potential outbreaks, thus improving the efficiency and effectiveness of dengue prevention and control strategies in endemic regions like Brazil.

Abstract

An age structured mathematical model with time dependent parameters is developed to investigate the dynamics of dengue transmission. Its properties are thoroughly analyzed in the first part of this work, as for example its disease free steady state, the corresponding effective reproduction numbers, its basic reproduction number (obtained via the Euler and Lotka equation and the next generation matrix approach). We also provide formulas for the time-varying effective reproduction number, and draw relations with the instantaneous growth rate. In the second part, we apply this model to Brazil and use weekly time series data from this country. Various medical parameters are firstly evaluated from these data, and an extensive numerical simulations for the period 2021 to 2024 is then carried out. Estimation of the transmission rates are derived both from epidemiological data and from environmental data such as temperature and humidity. The time-varying effective reproduction numbers are then estimated on these data, following the theoretical investigations performed in the first part. The sensitive parameters that significantly affect the model dynamics are presented graphically. Model predictions for following year by using different transmission rates are finally presented. Our findings show the importance of population age distribution, vector population dynamics, and climate, contributing to a deeper understanding of dengue transmission dynamics in Brazil.
