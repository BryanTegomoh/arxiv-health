Automated Interpretable 2D Video Extraction from 3D Echocardiography

Summary

This paper introduces an automated deep learning method to extract standard 2D echocardiography views from 3D cardiac ultrasound volumes. The system enables physicians to interpret complex 3D data in their conventional 2D format, benefiting from the speed and usability of 3D scanning while preserving diagnostic accuracy and spatial calibration. Validated by cardiologists and AI models, this approach aims to streamline cardiac imaging workflows.

Medical Relevance

This innovation significantly streamlines cardiac ultrasound interpretation by automating the conversion of advanced 3D echocardiography data into familiar 2D views. This can reduce variability, improve the efficiency of cardiac assessment, and facilitate the widespread adoption of 3D imaging in cardiology by making it more accessible for interpretation.

AI Health Application

The AI application automates the selection and reconstruction of standard 2D diagnostic echocardiography views from complex 3D ultrasound volumes. This allows cardiologists to interpret 3D data in a familiar 2D format, benefiting from the speed and usability of 3D scanning, while preserving critical diagnostic features for detecting cardiac abnormalities and generating clinical-grade measurements. It streamlines the diagnostic process and enhances the utility of advanced cardiac imaging in clinical practice.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: Bridges the gap between complex 3D cardiac anatomy captured by 3D echocardiography and the conventional 2D video interpretation relied upon by clinicians.
  • **Automated Extraction Methodology**: Utilizes a deep learning view classifier combined with downstream heuristics based on anatomical landmarks and cardiologist input to reconstruct standard 2D echocardiography views from 3D volumes.
  • **High Accuracy Validation**: Achieved 96% accuracy in blinded evaluation by three cardiologists across 1,600 extracted videos sourced from two different hospitals.
  • **Preservation of Diagnostic Utility**: The extracted 2D videos were validated to preserve essential spatial calibration and diagnostic features, crucial for detecting cardiac abnormalities using established AI models (EchoPrime, PanEcho).
  • **Clinical Measurement Capability**: Demonstrated the ability to generate clinical-grade measurements of cardiac anatomy from the extracted 2D videos using a specialized AI measurement tool (EchoNet-Measurement).
  • **Workflow Efficiency**: Facilitates a streamlined workflow by allowing physicians to leverage the faster acquisition times and usability of 3D echocardiography while interpreting data in their accustomed 2D format.
  • **Resource Availability**: The authors have publicly released the code and a dataset of 29 3D echocardiography videos to encourage further research and development in the field.

Methodology

The proposed method employs a multi-stage approach. First, a deep learning view classifier processes the 3D cardiac ultrasound volumes. Subsequently, anatomical landmark-based heuristics, refined by insights from expert cardiologists, are applied to precisely select and reconstruct standard 2D echocardiography views. The performance was validated through blinded human expert evaluation and by assessing the ability of extracted 2D videos to drive existing AI models for abnormality detection and anatomical measurements.

Key Findings

The automated system achieved a high accuracy of 96% in extracting standard 2D echocardiography views when evaluated by cardiologists. Critically, the extracted 2D videos were confirmed to preserve both spatial calibration and diagnostic features, enabling accurate detection of cardiac abnormalities by AI models and supporting the generation of reliable, clinical-grade anatomical measurements.

Clinical Impact

This technology has the potential to transform cardiac ultrasound by simplifying the interpretation of 3D echocardiography, thereby potentially reducing scan times and improving diagnostic consistency. By automating the extraction of standard views, it could lower the barrier to entry for 3D imaging, enhance the integration of AI-powered diagnostic and measurement tools, and make cardiovascular assessments more efficient and standardized in clinical practice.

Abstract

Although the heart has complex three-dimensional (3D) anatomy, conventional medical imaging with cardiac ultrasound relies on a series of 2D videos showing individual cardiac structures. 3D echocardiography is a developing modality that now offers adequate image quality for clinical use, with potential to streamline acquisition and improve assessment of off-axis features. We propose an automated method to select standard 2D views from 3D cardiac ultrasound volumes, allowing physicians to interpret the data in their usual format while benefiting from the speed and usability of 3D scanning. Applying a deep learning view classifier and downstream heuristics based on anatomical landmarks together with heuristics provided by cardiologists, we reconstruct standard echocardiography views. This approach was validated by three cardiologists in blinded evaluation (96\% accuracy in 1,600 videos from 2 hospitals). The downstream 2D videos were also validated in their ability to detect cardiac abnormalities using AI echocardiography models (EchoPrime and PanEcho) as well as ability to generate clinical-grade measurements of cardiac anatomy (EchoNet-Measurement). We demonstrated that the extracted 2D videos preserve spatial calibration and diagnostic features, allowing clinicians to obtain accurate real-world interpretations from 3D volumes. We release the code and a dataset of 29 3D echocardiography videos https://github.com/echonet/3d-echo .
