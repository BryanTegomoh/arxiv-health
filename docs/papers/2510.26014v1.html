Dual Mixture-of-Experts Framework for Discrete-Time Survival Analysis

Summary

This paper introduces a novel dual Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) framework designed for discrete-time survival analysis, aiming to precisely model patient heterogeneity and temporal risk dynamics. The framework integrates a feature-encoder MoE for subgroup-aware representation learning with a hazard MoE that leverages time embeddings to capture temporal patterns. Evaluated on breast cancer datasets, the method significantly boosts the time-dependent C-index by up to 0.04, demonstrating consistent performance improvements and further gains when integrated into the Consurv framework.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a more sophisticated and accurate method for predicting event times in clinical settings, which is crucial for personalized risk stratification, prognostic assessment, and guiding treatment decisions in diverse patient populations, particularly in oncology.

AI Health Application

This paper presents an AI/machine learning framework (Dual Mixture-of-Experts) designed to enhance discrete-time survival analysis. This allows for more accurate and personalized patient risk prediction and prognosis in clinical settings, particularly demonstrated in breast cancer. By modeling patient heterogeneity and temporal dynamics, it aims to improve precision medicine and treatment planning.

Key Points

  • Addresses critical challenges in survival analysis: modeling patient heterogeneity and adapting risk predictions to individual characteristics and temporal dynamics.
  • Proposes a novel dual Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) framework specifically for discrete-time survival analysis.
  • The framework's first component is a feature-encoder MoE, responsible for learning subgroup-aware representations from patient characteristics.
  • The second component is a hazard MoE, which utilizes these learned patient features and incorporates time embeddings to effectively capture and model temporal dynamics of risk.
  • Designed for flexible integration with existing deep learning based survival pipelines, offering a modular enhancement to current methodologies.
  • Demonstrates consistent and significant performance improvements on the METABRIC and GBSG breast cancer datasets, boosting the time-dependent C-index by up to 0.04 on test sets.
  • Achieves further performance gains and robustness when incorporated into the Consurv deep learning survival analysis framework.

Methodology

The methodology centers on a dual Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture. A feature-encoder MoE first processes patient features to learn subgroup-aware representations. These representations, combined with time embeddings, are then fed into a second MoE, a hazard MoE, which models time-varying hazard rates within a discrete-time framework. This modular design allows for flexible integration with existing deep learning survival pipelines.

Key Findings

The proposed dual MoE framework consistently improved survival prediction performance on METABRIC and GBSG breast cancer datasets. Quantitatively, it boosted the time-dependent C-index by up to 0.04 on the test sets. Qualitatively, the approach effectively models patient heterogeneity and temporal risk dynamics, showing additional performance benefits when integrated into the Consurv framework.

For instance, in breast cancer, it could better predict recurrence or survival probability, thereby optimizing surveillance protocols or adjuvant therapy choices based on individual patient profiles and their evolving risk over time.

Abstract

Survival analysis is a task to model the time until an event of interest occurs, widely used in clinical and biomedical research. A key challenge is to model patient heterogeneity while also adapting risk predictions to both individual characteristics and temporal dynamics. We propose a dual mixture-of-experts (MoE) framework for discrete-time survival analysis. Our approach combines a feature-encoder MoE for subgroup-aware representation learning with a hazard MoE that leverages patient features and time embeddings to capture temporal dynamics. This dual-MoE design flexibly integrates with existing deep learning based survival pipelines. On METABRIC and GBSG breast cancer datasets, our method consistently improves performance, boosting the time-dependent C-index up to 0.04 on the test sets, and yields further gains when incorporated into the Consurv framework.
