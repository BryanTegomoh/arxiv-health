Summary

This paper introduces BabySeg, a deep learning framework designed for robust and accurate brain segmentation in infants and young children using multi-contrast MRI. It addresses the significant challenges of pediatric brain MRI acquisition and the fragmentation of existing segmentation methods by employing domain randomization and flexible feature pooling from multiple input scans. BabySeg achieves state-of-the-art performance, matching or exceeding existing tools across diverse age cohorts and imaging protocols, all while significantly reducing computational runtime.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and reliable segmentation of infant brains is fundamental for studying normal neurodevelopment and diagnosing neurodevelopmental disorders early. This method provides a robust tool for quantitative analysis of brain structures, crucial for understanding disease progression, evaluating interventions, and facilitating pediatric neuroimaging research.

AI Health Application

The AI application is a deep learning model named 'BabySeg' designed to perform accurate brain segmentation from multi-contrast MRI scans of infants and young children. This application aims to overcome existing challenges in pediatric neuroimaging analysis, enabling more reliable and efficient assessment of brain development, potentially aiding in early diagnosis of neurological conditions, tracking developmental progress, and advancing research in infant brain science.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of accurate infant brain segmentation, complicated by developmental changes, difficult MRI acquisition (motion artifacts, inconsistent modalities), and non-head anatomy.
  • Introduces BabySeg, a novel deep learning framework specifically tailored for brain segmentation in infants and young children.
  • Employs domain randomization techniques during training, synthesizing images far beyond realistic bounds to enhance invariance to dataset shifts and support diverse MRI protocols (including untrained types and repeat scans).
  • Integrates a mechanism allowing flexible pooling and interaction of features from any number of input MRI scans, improving robustness to variable imaging configurations.
  • Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance, achieving accuracy that matches or surpasses several existing segmentation methods across various age groups and input scan types.
  • Operates with a single, unified model, eliminating the need for specialized models for different image types or age cohorts, thereby simplifying deployment and use.
  • Significantly reduces the runtime required for segmentation compared to many established tools, making it more efficient for clinical and research applications.

Methodology

BabySeg is a deep learning-based segmentation framework. Its core methodology includes extensive use of domain randomization during training to synthesize a vast range of diverse and even 'unrealistic' MRI images. This strategy aims to improve the model's robustness and generalization to unseen or variable clinical data. Additionally, the framework incorporates a novel mechanism that allows the deep learning model to flexibly combine and interact features derived from an arbitrary number of input multi-contrast MRI scans, enhancing its adaptability to different input configurations.

Key Findings

The primary findings indicate that BabySeg achieves state-of-the-art performance in deep infant brain segmentation. It delivers accuracy comparable to or superior to several existing methods across a wide range of age cohorts and diverse input MRI configurations. Crucially, it accomplishes this with a single, unified model and significantly faster runtime compared to many current tools, demonstrating strong generalization capabilities to various MRI protocols, including those not encountered during initial training.

Its robustness to diverse clinical MRI protocols and reduced runtime could streamline workflow, facilitate large-scale studies, and improve the clinical utility of pediatric brain MRI in understanding normal development and pathology.

Abstract

Segmentation of magnetic resonance images (MRI) facilitates analysis of human brain development by delineating anatomical structures. However, in infants and young children, accurate segmentation is challenging due to development and imaging constraints. Pediatric brain MRI is notoriously difficult to acquire, with inconsistent availability of imaging modalities, substantial non-head anatomy in the field of view, and frequent motion artifacts. This has led to specialized segmentation models that are often limited to specific image types or narrow age groups, or that are fragile for more variable images such as those acquired clinically. We address this method fragmentation with BabySeg, a deep learning brain segmentation framework for infants and young children that supports diverse MRI protocols, including repeat scans and image types unavailable during training. Our approach builds on recent domain randomization techniques, which synthesize training images far beyond realistic bounds to promote dataset shift invariance. We also describe a mechanism that enables models to flexibly pool and interact features from any number of input scans. We demonstrate state-of-the-art performance that matches or exceeds the accuracy of several existing methods for various age cohorts and input configurations using a single model, in a fraction of the runtime required by many existing tools.
