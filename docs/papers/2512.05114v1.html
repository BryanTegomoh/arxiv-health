Summary

This paper introduces BabySeg, a deep learning framework for robust and accurate brain segmentation in infants and young children from multi-contrast MRI. It addresses the challenges of diverse pediatric neuroimaging protocols by employing domain randomization and flexible feature pooling, achieving state-of-the-art performance with a single model and significantly reduced runtime.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and robust segmentation of infant brain MRI is critical for precisely analyzing normal and pathological brain development, facilitating early diagnosis of neurological disorders, and objectively monitoring treatment efficacy in this vulnerable patient population.

AI Health Application

The paper introduces BabySeg, a deep learning framework specifically designed for automated and accurate segmentation of infant brain MRI scans. This AI application aims to improve the efficiency and reliability of analyzing brain development in infants and young children, overcoming challenges faced with traditional methods and existing specialized tools. This can aid in earlier diagnosis of neurological conditions, monitoring treatment effectiveness, and advancing research into pediatric brain health.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** Accurate infant brain segmentation is challenging due to rapid developmental changes, frequent motion artifacts, inconsistent availability of imaging modalities, and significant non-head anatomy in pediatric MRI.
  • **Limitations of Existing Methods:** Current specialized segmentation models are often confined to specific image types or narrow age groups, or prove fragile when applied to more variable images, such as those acquired clinically.
  • **Proposed Solution (BabySeg):** A deep learning framework designed to support diverse MRI protocols, including repeat scans and image types not available during initial training.
  • **Methodological Innovation 1:** Leverages domain randomization techniques, synthesizing training images far beyond realistic bounds to promote strong invariance to dataset shifts across different scanners, protocols, and acquisition parameters.
  • **Methodological Innovation 2:** Incorporates a novel mechanism that enables the deep learning model to flexibly pool and interact with features from any number of available input scans (e.g., T1w, T2w, etc.).
  • **Performance:** Demonstrates state-of-the-art accuracy, matching or exceeding that of several existing methods across various age cohorts and input configurations, utilizing a single, unified model.
  • **Efficiency:** Achieves these results in a fraction of the runtime typically required by many existing pediatric brain segmentation tools.

Methodology

BabySeg is a deep learning-based framework that addresses the variability in infant MRI by two main mechanisms: 1) **Domain randomization:** Training images are synthesized with extreme variations (beyond typical real-world ranges) to build a model robust to unseen imaging protocols, scanner types, and acquisition artifacts (dataset shift invariance). 2) **Flexible feature pooling:** The model includes a novel architecture component that can dynamically aggregate and combine features from an arbitrary number of input MRI sequences (e.g., T1-weighted, T2-weighted, etc.), allowing it to adapt to varying availability of multi-contrast data.

Key Findings

BabySeg achieves state-of-the-art performance in infant and young child brain segmentation, successfully matching or surpassing the accuracy of several existing specialized methods across diverse age groups and various input configurations. Importantly, it accomplishes this using a single, unified model, and delivers these results with significantly enhanced computational efficiency, operating in a fraction of the time required by many current tools.

Clinical Impact

This method has the potential to significantly improve the clinical workflow and research capabilities in pediatric neuroimaging. Its robustness to diverse MRI protocols and imaging constraints, coupled with high accuracy and efficiency, will enable more consistent, reliable, and standardized quantitative analysis of infant brain development across different clinical settings and research sites. This can lead to earlier and more precise diagnosis of neurodevelopmental disorders, better objective assessment of treatment responses, and more robust longitudinal studies.

Abstract

Segmentation of magnetic resonance images (MRI) facilitates analysis of human brain development by delineating anatomical structures. However, in infants and young children, accurate segmentation is challenging due to development and imaging constraints. Pediatric brain MRI is notoriously difficult to acquire, with inconsistent availability of imaging modalities, substantial non-head anatomy in the field of view, and frequent motion artifacts. This has led to specialized segmentation models that are often limited to specific image types or narrow age groups, or that are fragile for more variable images such as those acquired clinically. We address this method fragmentation with BabySeg, a deep learning brain segmentation framework for infants and young children that supports diverse MRI protocols, including repeat scans and image types unavailable during training. Our approach builds on recent domain randomization techniques, which synthesize training images far beyond realistic bounds to promote dataset shift invariance. We also describe a mechanism that enables models to flexibly pool and interact features from any number of input scans. We demonstrate state-of-the-art performance that matches or exceeds the accuracy of several existing methods for various age cohorts and input configurations using a single model, in a fraction of the runtime required by many existing tools.
