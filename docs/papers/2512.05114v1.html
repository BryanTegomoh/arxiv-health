Summary

This paper introduces BabySeg, a novel deep learning framework designed for accurate and robust brain segmentation from multi-contrast MRI in infants and young children. It addresses the existing challenges of pediatric brain MRI analysis, offering a single model solution that achieves state-of-the-art performance across diverse imaging protocols and age groups, significantly outperforming and outrunning current fragmented methods.

Medical Relevance

Accurate segmentation of infant brain MRI is crucial for studying normal brain development and diagnosing neurological disorders early. This tool significantly improves the reliability and efficiency of analyzing these challenging images, providing clinicians and researchers with better data for patient care and developmental insights.

AI Health Application

The paper describes BabySeg, a deep learning framework designed for automated and robust segmentation of infant and young child brain MRI scans. This AI application aims to provide more accurate and efficient tools for clinicians and researchers to analyze brain development, potentially aiding in the diagnosis and management of neurodevelopmental disorders, and supporting large-scale research studies on brain growth and health in early life.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** Existing infant brain MRI segmentation is challenging due to difficult image acquisition (motion artifacts, non-head anatomy, inconsistent modalities) leading to specialized, fragmented, and often fragile segmentation models.
  • **Proposed Solution:** BabySeg, a deep learning framework, designed to be robust across diverse MRI protocols, including repeat scans and image types not seen during training.
  • **Methodological Innovation 1 (Domain Randomization):** The framework leverages advanced domain randomization techniques, synthesizing a wide range of training images (beyond realistic bounds) to enhance invariance to dataset shifts and promote generalizability.
  • **Methodological Innovation 2 (Multi-Input Feature Pooling):** BabySeg incorporates a mechanism allowing models to flexibly combine and interact features from any number of input scans, improving its versatility with multi-contrast data.
  • **Performance:** Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy, matching or exceeding several existing methods across various age cohorts and input configurations.
  • **Efficiency:** Operates in a fraction of the runtime typically required by many existing segmentation tools, making it more practical for clinical use.
  • **Versatility:** A single BabySeg model can handle a wide range of input MRI configurations and pediatric age groups, reducing the need for multiple specialized tools.

Methodology

BabySeg is a deep learning-based framework. Its core methodological advancements include the use of domain randomization to synthesize highly varied training images, promoting robustness to diverse real-world MRI acquisitions. Additionally, it features a novel mechanism for flexibly pooling and interacting features from multiple input scans (e.g., different MRI contrasts), enabling it to leverage all available data effectively for segmentation.

Key Findings

The primary findings are that BabySeg achieves state-of-the-art performance in infant brain segmentation, outperforming or matching existing methods in accuracy. It demonstrates superior versatility by handling a wide array of imaging protocols, age groups, and input configurations with a single model. Furthermore, it significantly reduces computational runtime compared to many current tools.

This could lead to more precise diagnosis of developmental abnormalities, better monitoring of neurological conditions, and more efficient research into early brain development, ultimately benefiting pediatric patient care and understanding of early neurodevelopmental trajectories.

Abstract

Segmentation of magnetic resonance images (MRI) facilitates analysis of human brain development by delineating anatomical structures. However, in infants and young children, accurate segmentation is challenging due to development and imaging constraints. Pediatric brain MRI is notoriously difficult to acquire, with inconsistent availability of imaging modalities, substantial non-head anatomy in the field of view, and frequent motion artifacts. This has led to specialized segmentation models that are often limited to specific image types or narrow age groups, or that are fragile for more variable images such as those acquired clinically. We address this method fragmentation with BabySeg, a deep learning brain segmentation framework for infants and young children that supports diverse MRI protocols, including repeat scans and image types unavailable during training. Our approach builds on recent domain randomization techniques, which synthesize training images far beyond realistic bounds to promote dataset shift invariance. We also describe a mechanism that enables models to flexibly pool and interact features from any number of input scans. We demonstrate state-of-the-art performance that matches or exceeds the accuracy of several existing methods for various age cohorts and input configurations using a single model, in a fraction of the runtime required by many existing tools.
