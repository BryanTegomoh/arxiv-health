Summary

This paper introduces BabySeg, a deep learning framework designed for accurate and robust brain segmentation from multi-contrast MRI in infants and young children. It addresses the challenges of pediatric neuroimaging, such as diverse protocols and artifacts, by employing domain randomization and a novel feature pooling mechanism, achieving state-of-the-art performance with a single model and significantly faster runtime.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and robust segmentation of infant brains is critical for understanding early brain development, diagnosing neurological disorders like autism or epilepsy, and monitoring treatment effects, providing objective measures that are difficult to obtain with current fragmented and often unreliable methods.

AI Health Application

BabySeg is a deep learning framework designed to automatically and accurately segment various anatomical structures within the brains of infants and young children from multi-contrast MRI scans. This AI application directly supports healthcare by providing a robust tool for clinicians and researchers to analyze brain development, identify developmental abnormalities, track neurological conditions, and potentially aid in diagnosis and treatment planning in a vulnerable patient population where traditional methods are challenging.

Key Points

  • The paper tackles the significant challenges in infant and young child brain MRI segmentation, including difficult acquisition, inconsistent imaging modalities, and susceptibility to motion artifacts.
  • Existing segmentation models are often fragmented, limited to specific image types or narrow age groups, and prove fragile when applied to variable clinical MRI data.
  • BabySeg is proposed as a unified deep learning framework for infants and young children, capable of supporting diverse MRI protocols, including repeat scans and previously unseen image types, by leveraging domain randomization techniques.
  • Domain randomization synthesizes training images far beyond realistic bounds, promoting dataset shift invariance and enhancing the model's generalization capabilities to variable clinical data.
  • A novel mechanism is described that allows the BabySeg model to flexibly pool and interact features from any number of input scans, enhancing its ability to utilize multi-contrast MRI effectively.
  • The framework demonstrates state-of-the-art performance, matching or exceeding the accuracy of several existing methods across various age cohorts and input configurations.
  • BabySeg operates as a single model and significantly reduces runtime compared to many traditional or existing segmentation tools, making it highly efficient.

Methodology

The study utilizes a deep learning framework, dubbed BabySeg, for infant brain MRI segmentation. Its core technical innovations include the application of domain randomization during training to synthesize a vast and varied set of training images, promoting robustness to diverse and unseen MRI acquisition protocols. Additionally, it incorporates a specialized mechanism for flexibly pooling and interacting features from an arbitrary number of input multi-contrast MRI scans.

Key Findings

BabySeg achieved state-of-the-art brain segmentation accuracy for infants and young children. It demonstrated exceptional robustness, performing consistently across various age cohorts and diverse MRI input configurations. Notably, it accomplished this using a single model and significantly reduced processing time compared to many existing methods.

Clinical Impact

This framework has the potential to significantly streamline and improve clinical and research workflows in pediatric neuroimaging. It can enable more consistent and reliable quantitative analysis of infant brain development, facilitate earlier and more accurate diagnosis of developmental neurological conditions, and support better monitoring of treatment outcomes, especially when dealing with the heterogeneous data often encountered in clinical practice.

Abstract

Segmentation of magnetic resonance images (MRI) facilitates analysis of human brain development by delineating anatomical structures. However, in infants and young children, accurate segmentation is challenging due to development and imaging constraints. Pediatric brain MRI is notoriously difficult to acquire, with inconsistent availability of imaging modalities, substantial non-head anatomy in the field of view, and frequent motion artifacts. This has led to specialized segmentation models that are often limited to specific image types or narrow age groups, or that are fragile for more variable images such as those acquired clinically. We address this method fragmentation with BabySeg, a deep learning brain segmentation framework for infants and young children that supports diverse MRI protocols, including repeat scans and image types unavailable during training. Our approach builds on recent domain randomization techniques, which synthesize training images far beyond realistic bounds to promote dataset shift invariance. We also describe a mechanism that enables models to flexibly pool and interact features from any number of input scans. We demonstrate state-of-the-art performance that matches or exceeds the accuracy of several existing methods for various age cohorts and input configurations using a single model, in a fraction of the runtime required by many existing tools.
