Decentralized Trust for Space AI: Blockchain-Based Federated Learning Across Multi-Vendor LEO Satellite Networks

arXiv ID: 2512.08882v1
Published: 2025-12-09
Authors: Mohamed Elmahallawy, Asma Jodeiri Akbarfam
Categories: cs.CR, cs.LG

Summary

This paper introduces OrbitChain, a blockchain-backed framework designed to address critical trust challenges and slow convergence in Federated Satellite Learning (FSL) across multi-vendor Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite networks. By offloading consensus to High-Altitude Platforms (HAPs) and ensuring auditable provenance of model updates, OrbitChain significantly improves privacy, security, and global model accuracy while reducing computational overhead and convergence time.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** FSL in LEO networks suffers from slow convergence due to intermittent connectivity and severe trust issues arising from biased/falsified updates and cyberattacks across satellite-ground links.
  • **Proposed Solution (OrbitChain):** A blockchain-backed framework enabling trustworthy multi-vendor collaboration for Space AI, addressing security, privacy, and efficiency.
  • **HAP-based Consensus:** OrbitChain offloads the computationally intensive blockchain consensus mechanism to High-Altitude Platforms (HAPs) to enhance overall system efficiency and scalability.
  • **Transparent & Auditable Provenance:** The framework ensures transparent, auditable tracking of model updates from various orbits and vendors, preventing manipulated or incomplete contributions from corrupting the global FSL model.
  • **Performance & Security Improvements:** Simulations demonstrate OrbitChain's ability to reduce computational/communication overhead and improve privacy, security, and the accuracy of the global AI model.
  • **Accelerated Convergence:** Its permissioned proof-of-authority ledger finalizes over 1000 blocks with sub-second latency (0.16s to 0.35s) and reduces FSL global model convergence time by up to 30 hours on real satellite datasets compared to single-vendor approaches.
  • **Multi-Vendor Real-time Learning:** The framework proves effective for enabling real-time, collaborative learning across diverse and heterogeneous multi-vendor satellite constellations.

Methodology

The OrbitChain framework implements a permissioned proof-of-authority (PoA) blockchain ledger, with consensus offloaded to High-Altitude Platforms (HAPs). Its efficacy was evaluated through extensive simulations, measuring computational and communication overhead, block finalization latency (for varying quorum sizes), and the global model convergence time and accuracy on real satellite datasets.

Key Findings

OrbitChain effectively mitigates trust challenges and significantly enhances efficiency in Federated Satellite Learning. It achieves sub-second block finalization latency (e.g., 0.16s for a 1-of-5 quorum) and drastically reduces global model convergence time by up to 30 hours on real satellite datasets. The system ensures improved privacy, security, and global model accuracy by guaranteeing transparent, auditable provenance of contributions and preventing manipulated or incomplete updates, while also reducing overhead.

Abstract

The rise of space AI is reshaping government and industry through applications such as disaster detection, border surveillance, and climate monitoring, powered by massive data from commercial and governmental low Earth orbit (LEO) satellites. Federated satellite learning (FSL) enables joint model training without sharing raw data, but suffers from slow convergence due to intermittent connectivity and introduces critical trust challenges--where biased or falsified updates can arise across satellite constellations, including those injected through cyberattacks on inter-satellite or satellite-ground communication links. We propose OrbitChain, a blockchain-backed framework that empowers trustworthy multi-vendor collaboration in LEO networks. OrbitChain (i) offloads consensus to high-altitude platforms (HAPs) with greater computational capacity, (ii) ensures transparent, auditable provenance of model updates from different orbits owned by different vendors, and (iii) prevents manipulated or incomplete contributions from affecting global FSL model aggregation. Extensive simulations show that OrbitChain reduces computational and communication overhead while improving privacy, security, and global model accuracy. Its permissioned proof-of-authority ledger finalizes over 1000 blocks with sub-second latency (0.16,s, 0.26,s, 0.35,s for 1-of-5, 3-of-5, and 5-of-5 quorums). Moreover, OrbitChain reduces convergence time by up to 30 hours on real satellite datasets compared to single-vendor, demonstrating its effectiveness for real-time, multi-vendor learning. Our code is available at https://github.com/wsu-cyber-security-lab-ai/OrbitChain.git
