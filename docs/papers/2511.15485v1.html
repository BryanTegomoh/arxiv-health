A Novel CustNetGC Boosted Model with Spectral Features for Parkinson's Disease Prediction

arXiv ID: 2511.15485v1
Published: 2025-11-19
Authors: Abishek Karthik, Pandiyaraju V, Dominic Savio M, Rohit Swaminathan S
Categories: cs.SD, cs.CV

Summary

This paper introduces CustNetGC, a novel classification and visualization model combining a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) with Custom Network Grad-CAM and CatBoost, for early Parkinson's Disease (PD) prediction. Utilizing spectral features (L-mHP and Spectral Slopes) extracted from voice recordings, the model achieved high diagnostic accuracy (99.06%) and provided interpretability for its predictions. The findings suggest a potential improvement in diagnostic accuracy and interpretability for PD detection.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine as it offers a non-invasive, highly accurate, and interpretable method for early Parkinson's disease diagnosis using voice analysis. Early detection is paramount for initiating timely interventions, managing disease progression, and ultimately improving patient outcomes and quality of life for individuals with this neurodegenerative disorder.

Methodology

The study utilized voice recordings from a publicly available Figshare dataset, involving 81 participants (40 Parkinson's Disease patients and 41 healthy controls). From these recordings, spectral features, specifically L-mHP (a combination of Log-Mel spectrogram, harmonic spectrogram, and percussive spectrogram derived using Harmonic-Percussive Source Separation) and Spectral Slopes, were extracted. The proposed CustNetGC model integrates a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) for feature learning and classification, Custom Network Grad-CAM for visualizing and interpreting important data regions, and CatBoost, a gradient boosting algorithm, to enhance robustness and classification performance.

Key Findings

The CustNetGC model demonstrated exceptional performance in predicting Parkinson's Disease, achieving an accuracy of 99.06% and a precision of 95.83%. The Area Under the ROC Curve (AUC) was recorded at 0.90 for the PD class and 0.89 for the healthy control (HC) class. Grad-CAM successfully highlighted significant regions within the voice data, providing interpretability to the predictions, while CatBoost further enhanced the model's robustness and overall classification capabilities.

By enabling non-invasive and efficient detection through voice analysis, it could facilitate earlier clinical interventions, allow for more personalized treatment plans, and ultimately improve the long-term management and prognosis for patients afflicted with PD.

Abstract

Parkinson's disease is a neurodegenerative disorder that can be very tricky to diagnose and treat. Such early symptoms can include tremors, wheezy breathing, and changes in voice quality as critical indicators of neural damage. Notably, there has been growing interest in utilizing changes in vocal attributes as markers for the detection of PD early on. Based on this understanding, the present paper was designed to focus on the acoustic feature analysis based on voice recordings of patients diagnosed with PD and healthy controls (HC). In this paper, we introduce a novel classification and visualization model known as CustNetGC, combining a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) with Custom Network Grad-CAM and CatBoost to enhance the efficiency of PD diagnosis. We use a publicly available dataset from Figshare, including voice recordings of 81 participants: 40 patients with PD and 41 healthy controls. From these recordings, we extracted the key spectral features: L-mHP and Spectral Slopes. The L-mHP feature combines three spectrogram representations: Log-Mel spectrogram, harmonic spectrogram, and percussive spectrogram, which are derived using Harmonic-Percussive Source Separation (HPSS). Grad-CAM was used to highlight the important regions in the data, thus making the PD predictions interpretable and effective. Our proposed CustNetGC model achieved an accuracy of 99.06% and precision of 95.83%, with the area under the ROC curve (AUC) recorded at 0.90 for the PD class and 0.89 for the HC class. Additionally, the combination of CatBoost, a gradient boosting algorithm, enhanced the robustness and the prediction performance by properly classifying PD and non-PD samples. Therefore, the results provide the potential improvement in the CustNetGC system in enhancing diagnostic accuracy and the interpretability of the Parkinson's Disease prediction model.
