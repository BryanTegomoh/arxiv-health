Learning Causality for Longitudinal Data

arXiv ID: 2512.04980v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Mouad EL Bouchattaoui
Categories: stat.ML, cs.IT, cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.90 / 1.00

Summary

This thesis presents advanced methods for causal inference and causal representation learning (CRL) specifically designed for high-dimensional, time-varying longitudinal data. It introduces the Causal Dynamic Variational Autoencoder (CDVAE) for robust Individual Treatment Effect (ITE) estimation under unobserved confounding, an efficient RNN-based framework for long-term counterfactual prediction, and a novel model-agnostic interpretability layer to elucidate how latent causes manifest in observed variables.

Medical Relevance

These methods are profoundly relevant for personalized medicine and public health, enabling more accurate prediction of individual patient responses to treatments, understanding complex disease trajectories over time, and deciphering the latent biological mechanisms underlying observable health outcomes from high-dimensional patient data, even in the presence of unobserved confounding.

AI Health Application

This research provides foundational AI methods for developing applications such as: predicting individual patient responses to therapies (personalized treatment recommendations), simulating long-term disease progression under various interventions, optimizing sequential treatment strategies for chronic conditions, identifying latent disease subtypes or risk factors from complex medical data, and creating more interpretable AI models for clinical decision support systems.

Key Points

  • **Causal Dynamic Variational Autoencoder (CDVAE):** A novel VAE-based model for estimating Individual Treatment Effects (ITEs) by effectively capturing unobserved heterogeneity through latent risk factors influencing outcomes, backed by theoretical guarantees for valid latent adjustment and generalization.
  • **Enhanced ITE Estimation:** CDVAE significantly outperforms baselines and demonstrably improves state-of-the-art models by augmenting them with its learned latent substitutes, achieving near-oracle performance even without access to true adjustment variables.
  • **Efficient Long-Term Counterfactual Regression:** An RNN-based framework, enhanced with Contrastive Predictive Coding (CPC) and InfoMax, is proposed for capturing long-range dependencies under time-varying confounding, achieving state-of-the-art results.
  • **Computational Efficiency:** The RNN-CPC/InfoMax approach provides a computationally efficient alternative to transformer-based models for long-term counterfactual prediction, crucial for large-scale longitudinal datasets.
  • **Advanced Causal Representation Learning (CRL):** A model-agnostic interpretability layer is introduced to understand how latent causes manifest in observed variables, leveraging the geometry of the decoder Jacobian.
  • **Meaningful Latent-to-Observed Structure Recovery:** This interpretability layer, using a sparse self-expression prior, recovers modular (potentially overlapping) groups of observed features aligned with shared latent influences, with theoretical recovery guarantees and without requiring strong assumptions like anchor features or single-parent dependencies.
  • **Scalable Regularization:** Development of scalable Jacobian-based regularization techniques further supports the interpretability and recovery of latent-to-observed causal structures.

Methodology

The paper introduces three distinct methodological contributions: 1. **Causal Dynamic Variational Autoencoder (CDVAE):** A VAE-based architecture designed to learn a disentangled representation of latent risk factors influencing outcomes, thereby enabling robust Individual Treatment Effect (ITE) estimation with theoretical guarantees for adjustment and generalization. 2. **RNN-based Counterfactual Regression Framework:** An efficient approach leveraging Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) augmented with Contrastive Predictive Coding (CPC) and InfoMax to model long-range temporal dependencies for predicting long-term counterfactual outcomes under time-varying confounding. 3. **Model-Agnostic Interpretability Layer for CRL:** A technique based on analyzing the geometry of the decoder Jacobian and applying a sparse self-expression prior. This allows for identifying modular, potentially overlapping, groups of observed features that are aligned with specific shared latent influences, supported by scalable Jacobian-based regularization.

Key Findings

1. CDVAE significantly improves Individual Treatment Effect (ITE) estimation, outperforming existing baselines and allowing state-of-the-art models to achieve near-oracle performance by incorporating its learned latent substitutes, even without access to true confounders. 2. The RNNs enhanced with Contrastive Predictive Coding (CPC) and InfoMax provide a computationally efficient framework that achieves state-of-the-art results in long-term counterfactual regression, effectively capturing intricate temporal dependencies. 3. The proposed model-agnostic interpretability layer successfully recovers meaningful latent-to-observed causal structures, identifying modular and potentially overlapping groups of observed features influenced by shared latent causes, with theoretical guarantees for recovery in various settings and without strong prior assumptions.

Clinical Impact

This research offers significant potential to improve clinical decision-making by providing more accurate predictions of individual patient responses to various treatments and interventions. It can guide the design of more effective long-term therapeutic strategies for chronic diseases, enable better patient stratification for clinical trials, and offer valuable, interpretable insights into the underlying mechanisms of disease and treatment efficacy, thereby fostering greater trust in AI-driven healthcare solutions.

Abstract

This thesis develops methods for causal inference and causal representation learning (CRL) in high-dimensional, time-varying data. The first contribution introduces the Causal Dynamic Variational Autoencoder (CDVAE), a model for estimating Individual Treatment Effects (ITEs) by capturing unobserved heterogeneity in treatment response driven by latent risk factors that affect only outcomes. CDVAE comes with theoretical guarantees on valid latent adjustment and generalization bounds for ITE error. Experiments on synthetic and real datasets show that CDVAE outperforms baselines, and that state-of-the-art models greatly improve when augmented with its latent substitutes, approaching oracle performance without access to true adjustment variables. The second contribution proposes an efficient framework for long-term counterfactual regression based on RNNs enhanced with Contrastive Predictive Coding (CPC) and InfoMax. It captures long-range dependencies under time-varying confounding while avoiding the computational cost of transformers, achieving state-of-the-art results and introducing CPC into causal inference. The third contribution advances CRL by addressing how latent causes manifest in observed variables. We introduce a model-agnostic interpretability layer based on the geometry of the decoder Jacobian. A sparse self-expression prior induces modular, possibly overlapping groups of observed features aligned with shared latent influences. We provide recovery guarantees in both disjoint and overlapping settings and show that meaningful latent-to-observed structure can be recovered without anchor features or single-parent assumptions. Scalable Jacobian-based regularization techniques are also developed.
