Privacy-Preserving Federated Vision Transformer Learning Leveraging Lightweight Homomorphic Encryption in Medical AI

Summary

This paper addresses the critical challenge of privacy in collaborative medical AI by proposing a novel federated learning framework that integrates Vision Transformers (ViT) with lightweight homomorphic encryption (HE). It secures multi-institutional histopathology classification by encrypting compact ViT CLS tokens, effectively preventing sensitive data reconstruction attacks while achieving significant communication efficiency and high diagnostic accuracy.

Medical Relevance

This framework is highly relevant for medicine as it enables healthcare institutions to collaboratively train powerful AI models using diverse patient datasets, overcoming stringent privacy regulations like HIPAA, without directly sharing sensitive patient information. This fosters the development of more accurate diagnostic tools and accelerates medical research.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of privacy-preserving federated learning models using Vision Transformers for automated diagnosis and classification of medical images, specifically histopathology slides, to identify conditions like lung cancer. This enables healthcare institutions to collaboratively train robust AI models without directly sharing sensitive patient data, improving diagnostic accuracy across diverse datasets.

Key Points

  • Addresses the vulnerability of federated learning (FL) gradients to model inversion attacks, which can reconstruct sensitive patient data, particularly crucial in medical AI.
  • Proposes a privacy-preserving FL framework combining Vision Transformers (ViT) with homomorphic encryption (CKKS HE) for secure multi-institutional training.
  • Utilizes the ViT CLS token as a compact (768-dimensional) feature representation, encrypting these tokens before transmission for secure aggregation, rather than encrypting full gradients.
  • Achieves a 30-fold communication reduction (326 KB per aggregation round) compared to encrypting entire model gradients, enhancing scalability.
  • Demonstrates that unencrypted gradients are highly susceptible to reconstruction attacks (PSNR: 52.26 dB, SSIM: 0.999, NMI: 0.741), whereas the proposed CLS-protected HE approach effectively prevents such attacks.
  • Enables secure encrypted inference directly on ciphertexts, maintaining robust privacy guarantees throughout the entire machine learning pipeline.
  • Evaluated on a three-client federated setup for lung cancer histopathology, achieving 96.12% global classification accuracy in the unencrypted domain and 90.02% in the encrypted domain.

Methodology

The methodology involves a federated learning setup where local clients train Vision Transformer (ViT) models. Instead of sending raw gradients, clients extract the 768-dimensional CLS (Classification) token from their ViT model, which encapsulates high-level features. These CLS tokens are then encrypted using CKKS homomorphic encryption before being transmitted to a central server. The server performs secure aggregation on these encrypted tokens, and inference can subsequently be performed directly on the ciphertexts.

Key Findings

Unencrypted gradients were found to be highly vulnerable to model inversion attacks, allowing near-perfect image reconstruction (PSNR: 52.26 dB, SSIM: 0.999, NMI: 0.741). In contrast, the proposed CLS-protected homomorphic encryption successfully prevented such attacks. The framework achieved a 30-fold communication reduction (only 326 KB per round) compared to gradient encryption. Global classification accuracy for lung cancer histopathology reached 96.12% unencrypted and 90.02% encrypted.

Clinical Impact

This research provides a crucial pathway for healthcare systems to collaboratively build more accurate and robust AI diagnostic models, particularly for complex tasks like histopathology analysis for lung cancer, without violating patient privacy laws. It could lead to improved diagnostic precision, faster identification of diseases, and more personalized treatment strategies by leveraging larger, more diverse datasets that were previously inaccessible due to privacy concerns.

Abstract

Collaborative machine learning across healthcare institutions promises improved diagnostic accuracy by leveraging diverse datasets, yet privacy regulations such as HIPAA prohibit direct patient data sharing. While federated learning (FL) enables decentralized training without raw data exchange, recent studies show that model gradients in conventional FL remain vulnerable to reconstruction attacks, potentially exposing sensitive medical information. This paper presents a privacy-preserving federated learning framework combining Vision Transformers (ViT) with homomorphic encryption (HE) for secure multi-institutional histopathology classification. The approach leverages the ViT CLS token as a compact 768-dimensional feature representation for secure aggregation, encrypting these tokens using CKKS homomorphic encryption before transmission to the server. We demonstrate that encrypting CLS tokens achieves a 30-fold communication reduction compared to gradient encryption while maintaining strong privacy guarantees. Through evaluation on a three-client federated setup for lung cancer histopathology classification, we show that gradients are highly susceptible to model inversion attacks (PSNR: 52.26 dB, SSIM: 0.999, NMI: 0.741), enabling near-perfect image reconstruction. In contrast, the proposed CLS-protected HE approach prevents such attacks while enabling encrypted inference directly on ciphertexts, requiring only 326 KB of encrypted data transmission per aggregation round. The framework achieves 96.12 percent global classification accuracy in the unencrypted domain and 90.02 percent in the encrypted domain.
