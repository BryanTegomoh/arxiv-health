COMPARE: Clinical Optimization with Modular Planning and Assessment via RAG-Enhanced AI-OCT: Superior Decision Support for Percutaneous Coronary Intervention Compared to ChatGPT-5 and Junior Operators

Published: 2025-12-11
Authors: Wei Fang, Chiyao Wang, Wenshuai Ma, Hui Liu, Jianqiang Hu, Xiaona Niu, Yi Chu, Mingming Zhang, Jingxiao Yang, Dongwei Zhang, Zelin Li, Pengyun Liu, Jiawei Zheng, Pengke Zhang, Chaoshi Qin, Wangang Guo, Bin Wang, Yugang Xue, Wei Zhang, Zikuan Wang, Rui Zhu, Yihui Cao, Quanmao Lu, Rui Meng, Yan Li

Summary

This study introduces CA-GPT, a specialized RAG-enhanced AI model integrated into an AI-OCT system, demonstrating superior performance in OCT-guided percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) planning and assessment. It significantly outperformed both the general-purpose ChatGPT-5 and junior physicians in decision-making agreement when compared against expert-derived procedural records. The system offers a standardized and reliable method for intravascular imaging interpretation, promising to augment operator expertise and optimize PCI outcomes.

Medical Relevance

Intravascular imaging, particularly OCT, is crucial for optimizing PCI outcomes, but its interpretation is highly operator-dependent, leading to variability. This research presents a domain-specific AI solution that can standardize complex OCT interpretation, potentially reducing human error, improving procedural precision, and democratizing access to expert-level guidance in interventional cardiology.

AI Health Application

The paper describes 'CA-GPT', a novel large model deployed on an AI-OCT system, which functions as an AI-powered clinical decision support tool. It provides superior recommendations for Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI) planning (e.g., stent diameter and length selection) and post-PCI assessment by interpreting intravascular imaging (OCT). This application aims to standardize medical decision-making, augment operator expertise, and optimize outcomes in interventional cardiology procedures.

Key Points

  • The research addresses the operator-dependency in interpreting optical coherence tomography (OCT) images for percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI), highlighting the need for reliable, domain-specific AI.
  • CA-GPT is a novel, RAG-enhanced large AI model specifically developed and deployed on an AI-OCT system for enhanced decision support in PCI.
  • The study compared CA-GPT's performance against general-purpose ChatGPT-5 and junior physicians in a single-center analysis involving 96 patients undergoing OCT-guided PCI.
  • For pre-PCI planning, CA-GPT achieved significantly higher median agreement scores (5 [IQR 3.75-5]) compared to ChatGPT-5 (3 [2-4], P<0.001) and junior physicians (4 [3-4], P<0.001).
  • CA-GPT demonstrated superior performance over junior physicians in specific pre-PCI metrics, including stent diameter (90.3% vs. 72.2%, P<0.05) and length selection (80.6% vs. 52.8%, P<0.01).
  • In post-PCI assessment, CA-GPT maintained excellent overall agreement (5 [4.75-5]), significantly outperforming both ChatGPT-5 (4 [4-5], P<0.001) and junior physicians (5 [4-5], P<0.05).
  • Subgroup analysis confirmed CA-GPT's robust performance advantage, especially in complex PCI scenarios, indicating its potential to standardize and optimize decision-making in interventional cardiology.

Methodology

This was a single-center, comparative analysis involving 96 patients who underwent OCT-guided PCI. The study compared procedural decisions generated by three distinct entities: the novel CA-GPT AI-OCT system, the general-purpose ChatGPT-5 model, and junior physicians. These decisions were then assessed for agreement against an expert-derived procedural record. Agreement was quantified using ten pre-specified metrics, categorized into pre-PCI planning and post-PCI assessment phases, with statistical significance determined using appropriate tests (e.g., P-values for comparisons).

Key Findings

CA-GPT demonstrated significantly higher median agreement scores for pre-PCI planning (5 [IQR 3.75-5]) compared to ChatGPT-5 (3 [2-4], P<0.001) and junior physicians (4 [3-4], P<0.001). CA-GPT consistently outperformed ChatGPT-5 across all individual pre-PCI metrics. Specifically, CA-GPT showed superior agreement over junior physicians in stent diameter (90.3% vs. 72.2%, P<0.05) and length (80.6% vs. 52.8%, P<0.01) selection. In post-PCI assessment, CA-GPT maintained excellent overall agreement (5 [4.75-5]), significantly surpassing ChatGPT-5 (4 [4-5], P<0.001) and showing significantly higher agreement than junior physicians (5 [4-5], P<0.05). Subgroup analysis further confirmed CA-GPT's robust performance, particularly in complex PCI scenarios.

Clinical Impact

The CA-GPT-based AI-OCT system holds significant potential to revolutionize decision-making in OCT-guided PCI. By providing highly standardized and reliable interpretation of complex intravascular images, it can mitigate operator-dependent variability, enhance the precision of stent selection and deployment, and optimize post-PCI assessment. This advancement could lead to improved procedural efficiency, enhanced patient safety, superior long-term clinical outcomes, and serve as an invaluable educational and decision-support tool for interventional cardiologists, particularly junior operators.

Limitations

The abstract explicitly notes this as a 'single-center analysis,' which may limit the generalizability of the findings to other institutions, diverse patient populations, or different OCT platforms. The use of an 'expert-derived procedural record' as the gold standard, while practical, could introduce subtle biases. Furthermore, the study focused on decision *agreement* rather than direct clinical outcomes, necessitating future trials to assess actual patient benefit.

Future Directions

While not explicitly detailed in the abstract, logical future directions include conducting multi-center validation studies to confirm generalizability, performing prospective randomized controlled trials to evaluate the direct impact of CA-GPT-guided PCI on hard clinical outcomes (e.g., MACE, revascularization rates). Further research could also focus on refining the CA-GPT model, integrating it with other patient data, and investigating its cost-effectiveness and seamless integration into routine clinical workflows.

Abstract

Background: While intravascular imaging, particularly optical coherence tomography (OCT), improves percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) outcomes, its interpretation is operator-dependent. General-purpose artificial intelligence (AI) shows promise but lacks domain-specific reliability. We evaluated the performance of CA-GPT, a novel large model deployed on an AI-OCT system, against that of the general-purpose ChatGPT-5 and junior physicians for OCT-guided PCI planning and assessment. Methods: In this single-center analysis of 96 patients who underwent OCT-guided PCI, the procedural decisions generated by the CA-GPT, ChatGPT-5, and junior physicians were compared with an expert-derived procedural record. Agreement was assessed using ten pre-specified metrics across pre-PCI and post-PCI phases. Results: For pre-PCI planning, CA-GPT demonstrated significantly higher median agreement scores (5[IQR 3.75-5]) compared to both ChatGPT-5 (3[2-4], P<0.001) and junior physicians (4[3-4], P<0.001). CA-GPT significantly outperformed ChatGPT-5 across all individual pre-PCI metrics and showed superior performance to junior physicians in stent diameter (90.3% vs. 72.2%, P<0.05) and length selection (80.6% vs. 52.8%, P<0.01). In post-PCI assessment, CA-GPT maintained excellent overall agreement (5[4.75-5]), significantly higher than both ChatGPT-5 (4[4-5], P<0.001) and junior physicians (5[4-5], P<0.05). Subgroup analysis confirmed CA-GPT's robust performance advantage in complex scenarios. Conclusion: The CA-GPT-based AI-OCT system achieved superior decision-making agreement versus a general-purpose large language model and junior physicians across both PCI planning and assessment phases. This approach provides a standardized and reliable method for intravascular imaging interpretation, demonstrating significant potential to augment operator expertise and optimize OCT-guided PCI.
