FENCE: Flexible Electric Noise Cancellation Endo-shield for the Suppression of Electromagnetic Interference in Low-Field MRI

arXiv ID: 2512.04889v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Julia Pfitzer, Martin Uecker, Hermann Scharfetter
Categories: physics.med-ph
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces FENCE (Flexible Electric Noise Cancellation Endo-shield), a novel, retrofittable solution designed to suppress electromagnetic interference (EMI) in low-field MRI systems operating without conventional Faraday shields. By blocking capacitive EMI coupling from the body to the RF coil, FENCE, especially when combined with segmented coils, significantly improves signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) and reduces EMI, thereby enhancing image quality while preserving system portability and accessibility.

Medical Relevance

Electromagnetic interference severely degrades image quality in low-field MRI, hindering accurate diagnosis. FENCE directly addresses this by substantially increasing SNR and reducing noise, enabling clearer diagnostic images crucial for expanding the utility and accessibility of MRI in diverse, often unshielded, clinical environments such as emergency rooms or remote healthcare settings.

Key Points

  • Low-field MRI systems without Faraday-shielded rooms face significant challenges from electromagnetic interference (EMI), which traditional solutions often compromise portability or patient comfort.
  • The core hypothesis posits that EMI primarily couples capacitively from the patient's body to the RF coil.
  • Two methods were investigated: capacitive segmentation of the RF coil and FENCE (Flexible Electric Noise Cancellation Endo-shield), which uses flexible PCB shields placed inside the RF coil to block capacitive coupling.
  • FENCE is designed to be retrofittable to existing RF coils, and its expected performance and impact on RF coil losses were initially estimated using finite element (FE) simulations.
  • Practical testing in various realistic scenarios demonstrated that the combination of FENCE with segmented coils effectively mitigates both environmental and controlled EMI.
  • Phantom experiments showed FENCE increased SNR by up to a factor of 9 and reduced EMI levels to near-baseline, with a 9% reduction in coil quality factor (Q factor), consistent with FE simulation predictions.
  • In-vivo head imaging confirmed these results, achieving up to a 2x increase in SNR across diverse electromagnetic environments, alongside an 18% decrease in Q factor.
  • The simple design of FENCE offers a low-cost solution for EMI suppression, significantly enhancing image quality in low-field MRI while maintaining the system's portability and accessibility.

Methodology

The methodology involved an initial phase of finite element (FE) simulations to predict the shielding performance of FENCE and its impact on RF coil losses. This was followed by extensive practical validation through phantom experiments and in-vivo head imaging in various realistic electromagnetic environments, testing the effectiveness of FENCE (and segmented coils) against both environmental and controlled EMI.

Key Findings

The study found that the combination of FENCE (a flexible PCB shield) and capacitively segmented RF coils effectively blocks EMI primarily coupled from the body, leading to a significant increase in SNR (up to 9x in phantoms, 2x in-vivo) and reduction of EMI to near-baseline levels. This was achieved with a manageable reduction in coil quality factor (Q factor) (9% for phantoms, 18% for in-vivo), showing strong agreement with initial FE simulation predictions.

Clinical Impact

FENCE offers a practical, low-cost, and retrofittable solution to a major impediment for low-field MRI systems: EMI in unshielded environments. By dramatically improving image quality and SNR, it enhances the diagnostic capabilities of portable MRI, making it a more viable and accessible tool for bedside imaging, emergency diagnostics, and deployment in resource-limited settings, ultimately improving patient care through wider MRI availability.

Limitations

While effective, the implementation of FENCE resulted in a measurable reduction in the RF coil's quality factor (Q factor) (9% in phantom experiments, 18% in-vivo), indicating a trade-off in coil efficiency. The focus of this research is specifically on low-field MRI systems operating without conventional Faraday shields, and its direct applicability or performance in high-field or fully shielded MRI environments is not addressed.

Abstract

Electromagnetic interference (EMI) is a significant challenge for low-field MRI systems operating without conventional Faraday-shielded rooms. Traditional EMI mitigation approaches include external shields, subject grounding via electrodes, or active noise cancellation requiring synchronized receive channels. These methods either limit portability, introduce patient discomfort, or demand expensive hardware. In this work, we start from the hypothesis that EMI primarily couples capacitively from the body to the RF coil. We investigated two methods of blocking capacitive coupling while preserving inductive MRI signal detection: First, we employed capacitive segmentation of the RF coil and studied its effect on EMI coupling. Second, we present FENCE (Flexible Electromagnetic Noise Cancellation Endo-shield), a novel approach blocking capacitive coupling using flexible PCB shields placed inside the RF coil. FENCE can be retrofitted to existing RF coils. Finite element (FE) simulations were used to estimate the expected shielding performance and the impact on RF coil losses prior to practical implementation. Testing in various realistic scenarios then demonstrated that the combination of FENCE with segmented coils is effective against both environmental noise sources and controlled EMI. In phantom experiments, FENCE increased SNR by up to a factor of 9 and reduced EMI levels to near-baseline levels with 9% reduction in coil quality factor (Q factor), showing good agreement with the predictions from the FE simulations. In-vivo head imaging confirmed these results across diverse electromagnetic environments where SNR increased by up to a factor 2 while showing an 18% decrease in Q factor. FENCE's simple design provides a low-cost solution to EMI in low-field MRI, enhancing image quality while maintaining system portability and accessibility.
