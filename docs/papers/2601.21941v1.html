Robust Multimodal Representation Learning in Healthcare

Summary

This paper introduces a Dual-Stream Feature Decorrelation Framework to address systematic biases in real-world medical multimodal datasets, which typically hinder the generalization ability of patient representations. By employing structural causal analysis and dual-stream neural networks, the framework disentangles causal features from spurious correlations, leading to more robust patient representations for clinical outcome prediction. Experiments on MIMIC-IV, eICU, and ADNI datasets demonstrate consistent performance improvements.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for developing more reliable and generalizable AI models in healthcare, ensuring that clinical predictions are based on genuine patient characteristics rather than systematic biases inherent in data collection, thereby improving diagnostic and prognostic accuracy.

AI Health Application

The AI application is to create robust and bias-mitigated AI models for analyzing diverse patient data (multimodal representation learning) to generate unified patient representations. These representations are then used to improve the accuracy and reliability of clinical outcome prediction and other downstream medical AI tasks, thereby enhancing the generalization ability and trustworthiness of AI in healthcare settings.

Key Points

  • Identifies and tackles the critical problem of systematic biases in real-world medical multimodal datasets, which impact the generalization ability of learned representations.
  • Proposes a Dual-Stream Feature Decorrelation Framework utilizing structural causal analysis to identify and manage biases introduced by latent confounders.
  • Employs a causal-biased decorrelation mechanism with dual-stream neural networks to effectively disentangle true causal features from spurious correlations.
  • Leverages generalized cross-entropy loss and mutual information minimization as core techniques for achieving effective feature decorrelation.
  • The framework is designed to be model-agnostic, allowing for integration into existing medical multimodal learning methodologies.
  • Demonstrates consistent performance improvements on diverse and widely used medical datasets: MIMIC-IV (critical care), eICU (critical care), and ADNI (neuroimaging/neurodegenerative diseases).
  • Aims to create unified, robust patient representations that can better support downstream clinical outcome prediction tasks.

Methodology

The proposed Dual-Stream Feature Decorrelation Framework employs structural causal analysis to identify latent confounders causing systematic biases. It utilizes a causal-biased decorrelation framework with dual-stream neural networks designed to disentangle causal features from spurious correlations. This disentanglement is achieved through the application of generalized cross-entropy loss and mutual information minimization.

Key Findings

The framework consistently achieves improved performance in clinical outcome prediction tasks across challenging real-world medical datasets, including MIMIC-IV, eICU, and ADNI. This improvement is attributed to its ability to effectively identify and handle systematic biases, leading to more robust and generalized multimodal patient representations.

Clinical Impact

By providing more robust and less biased patient representations, this method can lead to more accurate and reliable clinical outcome predictions. This could enhance clinical decision-making, support personalized medicine, improve risk stratification, and potentially lead to earlier and more effective interventions in various medical domains.

Abstract

Medical multimodal representation learning aims to integrate heterogeneous data into unified patient representations to support clinical outcome prediction. However, real-world medical datasets commonly contain systematic biases from multiple sources, which poses significant challenges for medical multimodal representation learning. Existing approaches typically focus on effective multimodal fusion, neglecting inherent biased features that affect the generalization ability. To address these challenges, we propose a Dual-Stream Feature Decorrelation Framework that identifies and handles the biases through structural causal analysis introduced by latent confounders. Our method employs a causal-biased decorrelation framework with dual-stream neural networks to disentangle causal features from spurious correlations, utilizing generalized cross-entropy loss and mutual information minimization for effective decorrelation. The framework is model-agnostic and can be integrated into existing medical multimodal learning methods. Comprehensive experiments on MIMIC-IV, eICU, and ADNI datasets demonstrate consistent performance improvements.
