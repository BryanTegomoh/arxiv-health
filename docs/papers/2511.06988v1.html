HCFSLN: Adaptive Hyperbolic Few-Shot Learning for Multimodal Anxiety Detection

arXiv ID: 2511.06988v1
Published: 2025-11-10
Authors: Aditya Sneh, Nilesh Kumar Sahu, Anushka Sanjay Shelke, Arya Adyasha, Haroon R. Lone

Summary

This paper introduces the Hyperbolic Curvature Few-Shot Learning Network (HCFSLN), a novel Few-Shot Learning (FSL) framework for multimodal anxiety detection using speech, physiological signals, and video data. HCFSLN employs hyperbolic embeddings, cross-modal attention, and an adaptive gating network to achieve robust classification with minimal data. Evaluated on a newly collected dataset from 108 participants, HCFSLN attained 88% accuracy, outperforming existing FSL baselines by 14%, demonstrating the effectiveness of hyperbolic space and FSL for anxiety detection.

Methodology

The study involved developing the Hyperbolic Curvature Few-Shot Learning Network (HCFSLN), an FSL framework for multimodal data (speech, physiological, video). HCFSLN utilizes hyperbolic embeddings to project features into a non-Euclidean space for enhanced separability, a cross-modal attention mechanism to integrate diverse data streams, and an adaptive gating network for robust classification with limited data. The model was trained and evaluated on a newly collected multimodal anxiety dataset from 108 participants and benchmarked against six established FSL baselines.

Key Findings

The HCFSLN model achieved 88% accuracy in multimodal anxiety detection, surpassing the best of six FSL baselines by 14%. Key insights include the proven effectiveness of hyperbolic space in modeling complex anxiety-related speech patterns, leading to superior feature discrimination. The study also validates Few-Shot Learning as a potent and practical paradigm for accurate anxiety classification, particularly advantageous in situations where extensive labeled datasets are scarce.

Abstract

Anxiety disorders impact millions globally, yet traditional diagnosis relies on clinical interviews, while machine learning models struggle with overfitting due to limited data. Large-scale data collection remains costly and time-consuming, restricting accessibility. To address this, we introduce the Hyperbolic Curvature Few-Shot Learning Network (HCFSLN), a novel Few-Shot Learning (FSL) framework for multimodal anxiety detection, integrating speech, physiological signals, and video data. HCFSLN enhances feature separability through hyperbolic embeddings, cross-modal attention, and an adaptive gating network, enabling robust classification with minimal data. We collected a multimodal anxiety dataset from 108 participants and benchmarked HCFSLN against six FSL baselines, achieving 88% accuracy, outperforming the best baseline by 14%. These results highlight the effectiveness of hyperbolic space for modeling anxiety-related speech patterns and demonstrate FSL's potential for anxiety classification.
