Predicting All-Cause Hospital Readmissions from Medical Claims Data of Hospitalised Patients

Published: 2025-10-30
Authors: Avinash Kadimisetty, Arun Rajagopalan, Vijendra SK
Categories: cs.LG, cs.AI

Summary

This research paper developed and evaluated machine learning models (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Support Vector Machines) to predict all-cause hospital readmissions using high-dimensional medical claims data. Principal Component Analysis was employed for dimension reduction, and models were compared using the Area Under Curve (AUC) metric. The Random Forest model achieved the highest predictive performance, offering a valuable tool for identifying at-risk patients and improving healthcare quality while reducing costs.

Medical Relevance

Predicting hospital readmissions is a national priority due to its direct impact on healthcare costs, patient outcomes, and serving as a key benchmark for the quality of care provided by hospitals and healthcare systems.

AI Health Application

Machine learning models (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Support Vector Machines) are applied to medical claims data to predict all-cause hospital readmissions. This AI application aims to identify high-risk patients and crucial factors contributing to readmissions, enabling targeted interventions to reduce readmission rates, improve patient health, and optimize healthcare resource allocation.

Key Points

  • **Objective**: Predict all-cause hospital readmissions to improve healthcare quality and reduce costs.
  • **Data Source**: Utilized high-dimensional medical claims data from hospitalized patients.
  • **Dimension Reduction**: Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was applied to manage the high dimensionality of the health claims data.
  • **Machine Learning Models**: Evaluated Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Support Vector Machines for the prediction task.
  • **Evaluation Metric**: Model performance was rigorously compared using the Area Under Curve (AUC) metric.
  • **Key Result**: Random Forest demonstrated the highest predictive performance, followed by Logistic Regression and Support Vector Machines.
  • **Implication**: The models can identify critical demographic and medical factors and high-risk patients to enable targeted interventions.

Methodology

The study employed supervised machine learning techniques, including Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Support Vector Machines, for the binary classification task of predicting all-cause hospital readmissions. Prior to model building, Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was utilized for dimension reduction on the high-dimensional health claims data. Model performance was rigorously evaluated and compared using the Area Under Curve (AUC) metric.

Key Findings

The Random Forest model demonstrated superior predictive performance for all-cause hospital readmissions, achieving the highest Area Under Curve (AUC) metric compared to Logistic Regression and Support Vector Machines. The analysis also facilitated the identification of crucial demographic and medical factors associated with readmission risk.

Clinical Impact

These predictive models can serve as practical tools for healthcare providers and payers to proactively identify patients at high risk of readmission, enabling targeted interventions, personalized care plans, and optimized resource allocation. This will ultimately lead to improved patient outcomes, enhanced healthcare quality, and significant cost savings for healthcare systems.

Abstract

Reducing preventable hospital readmissions is a national priority for payers, providers, and policymakers seeking to improve health care and lower costs. The rate of readmission is being used as a benchmark to determine the quality of healthcare provided by the hospitals. In thisproject, we have used machine learning techniques like Logistic Regression, Random Forest and Support Vector Machines to analyze the health claims data and identify demographic and medical factors that play a crucial role in predicting all-cause readmissions. As the health claims data is high dimensional, we have used Principal Component Analysis as a dimension reduction technique and used the results for building regression models. We compared and evaluated these models based on the Area Under Curve (AUC) metric. Random Forest model gave the highest performance followed by Logistic Regression and Support Vector Machine models. These models can be used to identify the crucial factors causing readmissions and help identify patients to focus on to reduce the chances of readmission, ultimately bringing down the cost and increasing the quality of healthcare provided to the patients.
