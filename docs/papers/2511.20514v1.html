Summary

This paper introduces a novel interventional ultrasound system designed for real-time 3D tracking and B-mode guidance of a core biopsy needle featuring an embedded photoacoustic beacon. The system aims to overcome limitations of poor needle visibility and 2D guidance in conventional ultrasound, providing clinicians with precise, volumetric visualization of the needle tip's location. Performance was validated in phantom studies, and usability assessed by clinicians, indicating its potential to enhance procedure safety and efficiency.

Medical Relevance

This innovation is highly relevant to medical practice as it directly addresses a major challenge in interventional radiology: ensuring precise needle placement during biopsies and other procedures. By providing real-time 3D guidance, it can significantly reduce adverse events, decrease the need for repeat procedures, and mitigate reliance on ionizing radiation guidance like CT.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical clinical need for clear 3D needle tip localization during interventional ultrasound procedures, particularly in heterogeneous tissues or at steep insertion angles.
  • Proposes a novel 'trackable needle core biopsy tool' incorporating a photoacoustic beacon embedded within its bevel.
  • Determines the 3D needle tip location by measuring the time-of-flight of ultrasound waves generated by the photoacoustic beacon.
  • Utilizes a sparse receiver array, distributed around a conventional curvilinear ultrasound probe, to detect the beacon's ultrasound signals.
  • Provides real-time, quantitative visualization of the 3D needle tip position, superimposed directly onto B-mode ultrasound images.
  • System performance was rigorously assessed in both water and ex vivo tissue phantoms, with ground truth positions established using X-ray computed tomography.
  • Usability of the system was evaluated by 12 clinicians during simulated biopsy procedures on an ex vivo tissue phantom, demonstrating practical applicability.

Methodology

The system integrates a photoacoustic beacon into the bevel of a core biopsy needle. This beacon, when activated, generates ultrasound waves. A sparse receiver array, strategically distributed around a standard curvilinear ultrasound probe, detects these emitted ultrasound signals. The 3D position of the needle tip is then precisely calculated based on the time-of-flight of these ultrasound waves. This calculated 3D location is subsequently superimposed in real-time onto the B-mode ultrasound images. The system's performance was quantitatively assessed in both water and ex vivo tissue phantoms, referencing positions derived from X-ray computed tomography. Its usability was qualitatively evaluated by a cohort of 12 clinicians performing simulated biopsies.

Key Findings

The developed system successfully achieved simultaneous real-time 3D tracking and B-mode ultrasound guidance of a novel, trackable needle. Its localization performance was assessed and validated in both water and ex vivo tissue phantom environments against CT-referenced positions. Furthermore, the system's practical usability was positively evaluated by experienced clinicians during simulated biopsy procedures.

Clinical Impact

This technology holds substantial clinical impact by potentially transforming interventional ultrasound-guided procedures. It promises enhanced procedural safety and accuracy by preventing misplacement and complications, reducing the need for costly and radiation-exposing CT guidance, and minimizing repeat procedures. This could lead to more efficient workflows, improved patient outcomes, and greater accessibility for complex biopsy procedures.

Abstract

We present an interventional ultrasound system capable of simultaneous 3D tracking and B-mode guidance of a novel, trackable needle core biopsy tool. Interventional radiology procedures such as core needle biopsy of focal liver lesions are ideally performed under ultrasound guidance, during which having a clear understanding of the location of the needle tip is essential to avoid adverse events and the need for repeat procedures. However, the 3D nature of the procedure and poor image contrast of the needle in heterogeneous tissue or at steep insertion angles often leads to confusion over the true location of the tip relative to the ultrasound imaging plane. This leads services to rely ionising, slow and expensive X-ray computed tomography guidance and/or senior clinicians for risky or otherwise challenging procedures. Existing methods to enhance needle visibility largely remain limited to 2D, and do not offer real-time volumetric guidance. Our proposed system provides the clinician with a real-time, quantitative visualisation of the 3D location of the needle tip superimposed onto B-mode ultrasound images. The tip location is determined from the time-of-flight of ultrasound generated by a photoacoustic beacon embedded in the needle bevel and received by a sparse receiver array distributed around the imaging system's curvilinear ultrasound probe. The performance of the system was assessed both in water and in an ex vivo tissue phantom, with referenced positions derived from X-ray computed tomography. The usability of the system was assessed by 12 clinicians performing simulated biopsies on a second ex vivo tissue phantom.
