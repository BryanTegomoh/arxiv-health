Real-time 3D Ultrasonic Needle Tracking with a Photoacoustic Beacon

Summary

This paper presents a novel interventional ultrasound system designed for simultaneous real-time 3D tracking and B-mode guidance of a core biopsy needle. The system utilizes a photoacoustic beacon embedded in the needle's bevel and a sparse receiver array to determine the needle tip's 3D location via time-of-flight measurements. This innovation provides clinicians with a quantitative 3D visualization of the needle tip superimposed on B-mode images, addressing the limitations of current 2D methods and poor needle visibility in complex tissues.

Medical Relevance

This technology is highly relevant to medicine as it aims to significantly improve the safety and accuracy of ultrasound-guided interventional procedures, reducing the risk of adverse events, the need for repeat procedures, and reliance on slower, more expensive, and ionizing X-ray CT guidance.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of real-time 3D needle tip tracking in ultrasound-guided procedures (e.g., liver biopsies) where poor contrast and 3D complexity often lead to procedural difficulties.
  • Introduces a novel trackable core biopsy needle featuring a photoacoustic beacon integrated into its bevel.
  • The system employs a sparse ultrasound receiver array, distributed around a standard curvilinear ultrasound probe, to detect ultrasound signals generated by the photoacoustic beacon.
  • Determines the real-time 3D location of the needle tip by calculating the time-of-flight of the ultrasound signals from the beacon to the receiver array.
  • Provides clinicians with a quantitative, real-time 3D visualization of the needle tip, accurately superimposed onto the B-mode ultrasound images for enhanced guidance.
  • Performance validation was conducted in both water and ex vivo tissue phantoms, with precise reference positions derived from X-ray computed tomography.
  • System usability was assessed by 12 clinicians who performed simulated biopsies on an ex vivo tissue phantom, indicating its practical utility.

Methodology

The proposed system integrates a novel core biopsy needle containing a photoacoustic beacon within its bevel. Upon activation, the beacon emits ultrasound pulses, which are detected by a sparse array of ultrasound receivers positioned around a curvilinear ultrasound probe. The 3D coordinates of the needle tip are then computed in real-time based on the time-of-flight of these received signals. System performance was quantitatively evaluated in water and ex vivo tissue phantoms, utilizing X-ray CT as the ground truth reference for needle positions. Usability was further assessed by 12 clinicians performing simulated biopsy procedures on a separate ex vivo tissue phantom.

Key Findings

The study demonstrated the successful implementation and feasibility of real-time, simultaneous 3D needle tip tracking alongside B-mode ultrasound guidance using a photoacoustic beacon. The system accurately determined the 3D position of the needle tip in both controlled water environments and complex ex vivo tissue phantoms. Initial usability assessments by clinicians performing simulated biopsies indicated the potential for practical integration and improved procedural guidance.

Clinical Impact

This system holds substantial clinical impact by offering enhanced, real-time 3D visualization of the needle tip during critical interventional procedures. This precision can potentially lead to a reduction in procedural complications, minimize patient exposure to ionizing radiation from CT, decrease the incidence of repeat procedures, and ultimately enable more confident and efficient execution of challenging biopsies by a broader range of clinicians.

Abstract

We present an interventional ultrasound system capable of simultaneous 3D tracking and B-mode guidance of a novel, trackable needle core biopsy tool. Interventional radiology procedures such as core needle biopsy of focal liver lesions are ideally performed under ultrasound guidance, during which having a clear understanding of the location of the needle tip is essential to avoid adverse events and the need for repeat procedures. However, the 3D nature of the procedure and poor image contrast of the needle in heterogeneous tissue or at steep insertion angles often leads to confusion over the true location of the tip relative to the ultrasound imaging plane. This leads services to rely ionising, slow and expensive X-ray computed tomography guidance and/or senior clinicians for risky or otherwise challenging procedures. Existing methods to enhance needle visibility largely remain limited to 2D, and do not offer real-time volumetric guidance. Our proposed system provides the clinician with a real-time, quantitative visualisation of the 3D location of the needle tip superimposed onto B-mode ultrasound images. The tip location is determined from the time-of-flight of ultrasound generated by a photoacoustic beacon embedded in the needle bevel and received by a sparse receiver array distributed around the imaging system's curvilinear ultrasound probe. The performance of the system was assessed both in water and in an ex vivo tissue phantom, with referenced positions derived from X-ray computed tomography. The usability of the system was assessed by 12 clinicians performing simulated biopsies on a second ex vivo tissue phantom.
