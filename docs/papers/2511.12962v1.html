EndoSight AI: Deep Learning-Driven Real-Time Gastrointestinal Polyp Detection and Segmentation for Enhanced Endoscopic Diagnostics

arXiv ID: 2511.12962v1
Published: 2025-11-17
Authors: Daniel Cavadia
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

EndoSight AI is a deep learning system developed for real-time gastrointestinal polyp detection and segmentation during endoscopic procedures. Leveraging the Hyper-Kvasir dataset, it achieves 88.3% mean Average Precision for detection and up to 69% Dice coefficient for segmentation, all while maintaining real-time inference speeds over 35 FPS on GPU hardware. This integrated AI solution aims to significantly enhance diagnostic accuracy and clinical decision-making for early colorectal cancer prevention.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and real-time detection of gastrointestinal polyps is paramount for the early diagnosis and prevention of colorectal cancer, a significant global health burden. EndoSight AI directly addresses this critical need by providing clinicians with an advanced tool to improve diagnostic precision during endoscopic screening, potentially leading to earlier interventions and better patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

Real-time AI-driven system for automated gastrointestinal polyp detection and segmentation during endoscopic examinations to assist clinicians in early cancer diagnosis and prevention.

Key Points

  • EndoSight AI is a deep learning architecture designed for real-time detection and precise boundary delineation (segmentation) of gastrointestinal polyps.
  • The system was independently developed and evaluated, utilizing the publicly available Hyper-Kvasir dataset for training and testing.
  • It achieved a high mean Average Precision (mAP) of 88.3% for polyp detection, indicating robust localization capabilities.
  • For polyp segmentation, the system demonstrated a Dice coefficient of up to 69%, showcasing its ability for accurate boundary delineation.
  • Crucially, EndoSight AI operates at real-time speeds, exceeding 35 frames per second (FPS) on standard GPU hardware, making it suitable for live endoscopic procedures.
  • The training methodology incorporated clinically relevant performance metrics and a novel thermal-aware procedure to ensure model robustness, efficiency, and practical utility.
  • The solution is engineered for seamless deployment within existing endoscopy workflows, aiming to advance diagnostic accuracy and improve clinical decision-making in gastrointestinal healthcare.

Methodology

The study employed a deep learning architecture, named EndoSight AI, which was developed and independently evaluated. The system was trained on the publicly available Hyper-Kvasir dataset. Key methodological components included the incorporation of clinically relevant performance metrics during training and a novel 'thermal-aware procedure' designed to enhance model robustness and efficiency. Performance was assessed using mean Average Precision (mAP) for detection, Dice coefficient for segmentation, and frames per second (FPS) for real-time inference speed on GPU hardware.

Key Findings

The EndoSight AI system demonstrated a mean Average Precision (mAP) of 88.3% for gastrointestinal polyp detection. For segmentation, it achieved a Dice coefficient of up to 69%. Furthermore, the system exhibited real-time inference capabilities, processing visual data at speeds exceeding 35 frames per second (FPS) on GPU hardware.

Clinical Impact

This AI solution holds significant potential to transform endoscopic diagnostics by providing clinicians with real-time, highly accurate assistance for polyp identification and delineation. This can lead to earlier and more consistent detection of precancerous lesions, reducing missed polyps, improving the quality of endoscopic procedures, and ultimately enhancing clinical decision-making and patient outcomes in the prevention and management of colorectal cancer.
