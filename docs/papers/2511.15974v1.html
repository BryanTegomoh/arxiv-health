KRAL: Knowledge and Reasoning Augmented Learning for LLM-assisted Clinical Antimicrobial Therapy

arXiv ID: 2511.15974v1
Published: 2025-11-20
Authors: Zhe Li, Yehan Qiu, Yujie Chen, Xiang Zhou
Categories: cs.AI

Summary

This paper introduces KRAL (Knowledge and Reasoning Augmented Learning), a novel paradigm designed to address the limitations of Large Language Models (LLMs) in complex clinical antimicrobial therapy decision-making. KRAL proposes a low-cost, scalable, and privacy-preserving approach that leverages teacher-model reasoning, semi-supervised data augmentation, and agentic reinforcement learning to significantly enhance local LLMs' knowledge and reasoning capabilities. It demonstrably outperforms traditional RAG and SFT methods, achieving improved accuracy and reasoning pass rates at a fraction of the long-term training cost.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant as it aims to overcome the current barriers preventing LLMs from being safely and effectively deployed in complex clinical settings like antimicrobial therapy, where decisions require dynamic integration of diverse, critical patient and pharmacological data. By providing a privacy-preserving and cost-efficient solution, KRAL can potentially democratize access to advanced AI for improved diagnostic and therapeutic support.

AI Health Application

The paper describes KRAL, an AI framework designed to enhance Large Language Models (LLMs) for use in clinical antimicrobial therapy. It aims to improve LLMs' knowledge question-answering and reasoning capabilities for clinical diagnostics and decision support, enabling low-cost, high-safety deployment of AI in complex medical settings related to treating infections.

Key Points

  • KRAL addresses fundamental LLM limitations (knowledge gaps, privacy, cost, limited reasoning) in high-stakes clinical antimicrobial therapy, offering a low-cost, scalable, privacy-preserving solution.
  • It employs teacher-model reasoning, using 'answer-to-question reverse generation' to automatically distill complex knowledge and reasoning trajectories.
  • Heuristic learning is utilized for semi-supervised data augmentation, substantially reducing manual annotation requirements by approximately 80%.
  • Agentic reinforcement learning is integrated to jointly enhance medical knowledge and reasoning capabilities while optimizing computational and memory efficiency.
  • Evaluation methodology includes a hierarchical approach using diverse teacher-model proxies to reduce assessment costs, complemented by a modular interface for seamless updates.
  • KRAL significantly improves knowledge question-answering, increasing Accuracy@1 on MEDQA by 1.8% vs. SFT and 3.6% vs. RAG.
  • It also dramatically enhances reasoning capability, boosting Pass@1 on the PUMCH Antimicrobial benchmark by 27% vs. SFT and 27.2% vs. RAG, all achieved at ~20% of SFT's long-term training costs.

Methodology

KRAL employs a multi-faceted approach comprising: 1) teacher-model reasoning distillation via 'answer-to-question reverse generation' for knowledge and reasoning trajectory extraction; 2) heuristic learning for semi-supervised data augmentation to minimize manual annotation; and 3) agentic reinforcement learning to jointly optimize medical knowledge and reasoning while enhancing computational and memory efficiency. A hierarchical evaluation using diverse teacher-model proxies reduces assessment costs, and a modular interface facilitates system updates.

Key Findings

KRAL significantly outperforms both RAG and SFT methods. It achieved a 1.8% increase in Accuracy@1 over SFT and a 3.6% increase over RAG on the MEDQA benchmark (knowledge). For reasoning, Pass@1 on the PUMCH Antimicrobial benchmark increased by 27% over SFT and 27.2% over RAG. Crucially, these improvements were accomplished at approximately 20% of the long-term training costs associated with SFT.

Clinical Impact

KRAL has the potential to significantly enhance clinical antimicrobial therapy by providing local, low-cost, and privacy-preserving LLM-assisted decision support. This could lead to more accurate pathogen profiling, optimized antimicrobial selection, improved patient outcomes, and a reduction in antibiotic resistance through better prescribing practices, making advanced AI accessible for complex medical decisions in resource-constrained or privacy-sensitive environments.

Limitations

The abstract primarily highlights the inherent limitations of general LLMs in clinical decision-making (knowledge gaps, data privacy, high deployment costs, limited reasoning) that KRAL is designed to overcome. It does not explicitly state specific limitations or caveats of the KRAL system itself.

Future Directions

The abstract establishes KRAL as an effective solution for current challenges in LLM-assisted clinical diagnostics, particularly for local LLM deployment. However, it does not explicitly outline specific future research directions for the KRAL framework.

Abstract

Clinical antimicrobial therapy requires the dynamic integration of pathogen profiles, host factors, pharmacological properties of antimicrobials, and the severity of infection.This complexity imposes fundamental limitations on the applicability of Large Language Models (LLMs) in high-stakes clinical decision-making including knowledge gaps, data privacy concerns, high deployment costs, and limited reasoning capabilities. To address these challenges, we propose KRAL (Knowledge and Reasoning Augmented Learning), a low-cost, scalable, privacy-preserving paradigm that leverages teacher-model reasoning to automatically distill knowledge and reasoning trajectories via answer-to-question reverse generation, employs heuristic learning for semi-supervised data augmentation (reducing manual annotation requirements by approximately 80%), and utilizes agentic reinforcement learning to jointly enhance medical knowledge and reasoning while optimizing computational and memory efficiency. A hierarchical evaluation employing diverse teacher-model proxies reduces assessment costs, while modular interface design facilitates seamless system updates. Experimental results demonstrate that KRAL significantly outperforms traditional Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) methods. It improves knowledge question-answering capability (Accuracy@1 on the external open-source benchmark MEDQA increased by 1.8% vs. SFT and 3.6% vs. RAG) and reasoning capability (Pass@1 on the external benchmark PUMCH Antimicrobial increased by 27% vs. SFT and 27.2% vs. RAG), achieved at ~20% of SFT's long-term training costs. This establishes KRAL as an effective solution for enhancing local LLMs' clinical diagnostic capabilities, enabling low-cost, high-safety deployment in complex medical decision support.
