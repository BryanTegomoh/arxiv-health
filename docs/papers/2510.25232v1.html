From Medical Records to Diagnostic Dialogues: A Clinical-Grounded Approach and Dataset for Psychiatric Comorbidity

arXiv ID: 2510.25232v1
Published: 2025-10-29
Authors: Tianxi Wan, Jiaming Luo, Siyuan Chen, Kunyao Lan, Jianhua Chen, Haiyang Geng, Mengyue Wu
Categories: cs.AI, cs.CL
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This research addresses the challenge of psychiatric comorbidity by developing a novel, clinically-grounded approach to generate a large-scale diagnostic dialogue dataset. They constructed 502 synthetic Electronic Medical Records (EMRs) for common comorbid conditions and then used a multi-agent framework, mimicking clinical interview protocols, to generate 3,000 multi-turn diagnostic dialogues, validated by psychiatrists. The resulting dataset, PsyCoTalk, is the first to support comorbidity and aims to improve diagnostic accuracy and treatment planning by enabling the development of AI models for multi-disorder psychiatric screening.

Medical Relevance

Psychiatric comorbidity significantly impacts patient outcomes and treatment complexity; this work provides a crucial resource (PsyCoTalk) to develop advanced AI tools for more accurate and efficient multi-disorder diagnosis, ultimately improving patient care pathways and treatment planning.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development and evaluation of models for multi-disorder psychiatric screening and diagnosis through conversational interfaces. This includes generating clinically relevant synthetic EMRs and diagnostic dialogues to train AI systems that can mimic and potentially assist clinical interview processes to improve diagnostic accuracy and treatment planning for psychiatric comorbidity.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** Psychiatric comorbidity is clinically significant and complex, making diagnosis challenging.
  • **Novel Approach:** Integration of synthetic patient EMR construction with multi-agent diagnostic dialogue generation.
  • **Synthetic EMRs:** Creation of 502 clinically relevant and diverse synthetic EMRs for common comorbid psychiatric conditions.
  • **Multi-Agent Dialogue Framework:** A hierarchical state machine and context tree, based on clinical interview protocols, supports over 130 diagnostic states for dialogue generation.
  • **PsyCoTalk Dataset:** Construction of PsyCoTalk, the first large-scale (3,000 multi-turn dialogues) dialogue dataset specifically designed to support psychiatric comorbidity.
  • **Validation and Fidelity:** Dialogues were validated by licensed psychiatrists for realism and diagnostic validity, exhibiting high structural and linguistic fidelity compared to real-world clinical transcripts.
  • **Enabling AI Development:** The dataset enables the development and evaluation of AI models capable of multi-disorder psychiatric screening within a single conversational interaction.

Methodology

The methodology involves a two-stage process: 1) Construction of 502 synthetic Electronic Medical Records (EMRs) for common comorbid psychiatric conditions, ensuring clinical relevance and diversity. 2) Generation of diagnostic dialogues using a multi-agent framework that translates clinical interview protocols into a hierarchical state machine and context tree, supporting over 130 diagnostic states. The generated dialogues were then validated by licensed psychiatrists for realism and diagnostic validity, and their fidelity was compared against real-world clinical transcripts.

Key Findings

The primary finding is the successful creation of PsyCoTalk, the first large-scale dialogue dataset (3,000 multi-turn dialogues) specifically designed to address psychiatric comorbidity. This dataset demonstrates high structural and linguistic fidelity to real-world clinical transcripts and has been confirmed by licensed psychiatrists for its realism and diagnostic validity. It effectively supports complex multi-disorder psychiatric screening in a conversational format.

Clinical Impact

This dataset and approach have the potential to significantly enhance diagnostic accuracy for complex comorbid psychiatric conditions. It can facilitate the development of AI-powered diagnostic assistants that can screen for multiple disorders in a single patient interaction, leading to earlier and more comprehensive diagnoses, improved treatment planning, and more efficient use of clinical resources in mental healthcare settings.

Limitations

While not explicitly stated as limitations, the dataset is based on *synthetic* EMRs and dialogues. Although validated by psychiatrists for realism and fidelity, synthetic data inherently carries the potential for subtle biases or omissions compared to extremely diverse and unpredictable real-world patient interactions. The abstract does not detail the specific types or ranges of psychiatric comorbidities covered, which could be a nuanced limitation depending on the scope.

Future Directions

The primary future direction highlighted is the use of the PsyCoTalk dataset for the development and evaluation of AI models capable of multi-disorder psychiatric screening. This implies further research into advanced conversational AI, natural language processing, and diagnostic algorithms to leverage this resource for practical clinical applications.

Abstract

Psychiatric comorbidity is clinically significant yet challenging due to the complexity of multiple co-occurring disorders. To address this, we develop a novel approach integrating synthetic patient electronic medical record (EMR) construction and multi-agent diagnostic dialogue generation. We create 502 synthetic EMRs for common comorbid conditions using a pipeline that ensures clinical relevance and diversity. Our multi-agent framework transfers the clinical interview protocol into a hierarchical state machine and context tree, supporting over 130 diagnostic states while maintaining clinical standards. Through this rigorous process, we construct PsyCoTalk, the first large-scale dialogue dataset supporting comorbidity, containing 3,000 multi-turn diagnostic dialogues validated by psychiatrists. This dataset enhances diagnostic accuracy and treatment planning, offering a valuable resource for psychiatric comorbidity research. Compared to real-world clinical transcripts, PsyCoTalk exhibits high structural and linguistic fidelity in terms of dialogue length, token distribution, and diagnostic reasoning strategies. Licensed psychiatrists confirm the realism and diagnostic validity of the dialogues. This dataset enables the development and evaluation of models capable of multi-disorder psychiatric screening in a single conversational pass.
