Two Deep Learning Approaches for Automated Segmentation of Left Ventricle in Cine Cardiac MRI

Summary

This paper introduces LNU-Net and IBU-Net, two novel deep learning architectures based on the U-Net framework, designed for automated left ventricle (LV) segmentation in cine cardiac MRI. These models integrate specialized normalization strategies and data augmentation techniques to enhance performance. The proposed methods experimentally outperform existing state-of-the-art approaches in terms of Dice coefficient and average perpendicular distance on a dataset of 805 MRI images from 45 patients.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated segmentation of the left ventricle from cardiac MRI is clinically critical for the precise quantification of cardiac function parameters (e.g., ejection fraction, ventricular volumes) and is essential for the diagnosis, prognosis, and management of various cardiovascular diseases.

AI Health Application

The paper proposes two novel deep learning architectures (LNU-Net and IBU-Net) to automate the segmentation of the left ventricle in cine cardiac MRI images. This AI application aims to improve the accuracy and efficiency of analyzing cardiac MRI scans, assisting clinicians in the diagnosis, quantification, and monitoring of heart conditions. By automating this crucial step, it can lead to more consistent and potentially faster clinical workflows in cardiology.

Methodology

The study proposes two deep learning architectures, LNU-Net and IBU-Net, both adapted from the U-Net framework. LNU-Net incorporates layer normalization within each convolutional block, while IBU-Net combines instance and batch normalization within its initial convolutional block. The architectures feature down-sampling paths for feature extraction and up-sampling paths for precise localization. Data augmentation techniques, specifically affine transformations and elastic deformations, were applied to the input MRI images. The models were trained and evaluated on a dataset of 805 short-axis cine MRI images from 45 patients, with performance assessed using Dice coefficient and average perpendicular distance, comparing against the original U-Net and other state-of-the-art methods.

Key Findings

The LNU-Net and IBU-Net architectures demonstrated superior performance in left ventricle segmentation, achieving higher Dice coefficients and lower average perpendicular distances compared to the original U-Net and other state-of-the-art approaches when evaluated on the specified cine cardiac MRI dataset.

Clinical Impact

The enhanced accuracy and automation provided by these deep learning approaches can lead to more consistent, efficient, and reliable quantification of left ventricular volumes and function. This advancement has the potential to significantly improve clinical workflows, reduce manual segmentation burden, minimize inter-observer variability, and ultimately provide more precise diagnostic and prognostic information for patients with cardiac conditions.

Abstract

Left ventricle (LV) segmentation is critical for clinical quantification and diagnosis of cardiac images. In this work, we propose two novel deep learning architectures called LNU-Net and IBU-Net for left ventricle segmentation from short-axis cine MRI images. LNU-Net is derived from layer normalization (LN) U-Net architecture, while IBU-Net is derived from the instance-batch normalized (IB) U-Net for medical image segmentation. The architectures of LNU-Net and IBU-Net have a down-sampling path for feature extraction and an up-sampling path for precise localization. We use the original U-Net as the basic segmentation approach and compared it with our proposed architectures. Both LNU-Net and IBU-Net have left ventricle segmentation methods: LNU-Net applies layer normalization in each convolutional block, while IBU-Net incorporates instance and batch normalization together in the first convolutional block and passes its result to the next layer. Our method incorporates affine transformations and elastic deformations for image data processing. Our dataset that contains 805 MRI images regarding the left ventricle from 45 patients is used for evaluation. We experimentally evaluate the results of the proposed approaches outperforming the dice coefficient and the average perpendicular distance than other state-of-the-art approaches.
