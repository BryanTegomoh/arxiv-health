C-GRASP: Clinically-Grounded Reasoning for Affective Signal Processing

Authors: Cheng Lin Cheng, Ting Chuan Lin, Chai Kai Chang
Categories: cs.AI

Summary

C-GRASP introduces a guardrailed RAG-enhanced pipeline to mitigate physiological hallucinations in Large Language Model (LLM) interpretation of Heart Rate Variability (HRV) for affective signal processing. By integrating an individualized Z-score Priority Hierarchy and RSA-aware guardrails, the system achieves superior emotion classification accuracy and high clinical reasoning consistency, fostering transparent, evidence-based clinical decision support.

Medical Relevance

HRV is a crucial noninvasive marker for autonomic nervous system monitoring. This research improves the reliability and clinical applicability of AI for interpreting HRV data, essential for accurate diagnosis, monitoring, and personalized interventions in various health conditions.

AI Health Application

The paper describes C-GRASP, an AI pipeline utilizing Large Language Models (LLMs) with a guardrailed RAG-enhanced architecture to interpret Heart Rate Variability (HRV) for autonomic monitoring and affective signal processing. This application aims to provide individualized, clinically-grounded assessment of physiological states, mitigate common AI pitfalls in medical data (e.g., physiological hallucinations, population bias), and offer transparent, evidence-based clinical decision support for healthcare professionals. It represents a direct application of AI to enhance diagnostic and monitoring capabilities in a medical context.

Key Points

  • LLMs face significant challenges in HRV interpretation, including physiological hallucinations like respiratory sinus arrhythmia (RSA) contamination, short-data instability, and reliance on population norms over individualized baselines.
  • C-GRASP (Clinically-Grounded Reasoning for Affective Signal Processing) is proposed as a RAG-enhanced pipeline that decomposes HRV interpretation into eight traceable reasoning steps.
  • A central feature is the Z-score Priority Hierarchy, which enforces weighting of individualized baseline shifts (Delta Z-scores) over normative statistics, critically anchoring the reasoning to the patient's unique physiological state.
  • The system effectively mitigates spectral hallucinations through automated RSA-aware guardrails, preventing contamination of frequency-domain HRV indices.
  • Evaluated on 414 trials from the DREAMER dataset, C-GRASP, when integrated with high-scale reasoning models (e.g., MedGemma3-thinking), achieved 37.3% accuracy in 4-class emotion classification.
  • It also demonstrated a Clinical Reasoning Consistency (CRC) score of 69.6%, indicating robust and traceable clinical decision-making logic.
  • Ablation studies confirmed that the individualized Delta Z-score module is the critical logical anchor, successfully preventing the 'population bias' inherent in native LLMs.
  • C-GRASP shifts affective computing from black-box classification to transparent, evidence-based clinical decision support, enhancing AI safety in biomedical engineering.

Methodology

C-GRASP utilizes a guardrailed Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)-enhanced pipeline, breaking down HRV interpretation into eight traceable reasoning steps. Its core is a Z-score Priority Hierarchy that prioritizes individualized baseline shifts (Delta Z-scores) over population norms. The system integrates automated RSA-aware guardrails to prevent contamination of frequency-domain indices. Evaluation was conducted on 414 trials from the DREAMER dataset, using high-scale reasoning models like MedGemma3-thinking to assess 4-class emotion classification accuracy and Clinical Reasoning Consistency (CRC).

Key Findings

C-GRASP achieved 37.3% accuracy in 4-class emotion classification and a Clinical Reasoning Consistency (CRC) score of 69.6%. The individualized Delta Z-score module was identified as the critical logical anchor through ablation studies, effectively preventing 'population bias' in LLM interpretations. Automated RSA-aware guardrails successfully mitigated spectral hallucinations.

Clinical Impact

This research provides a framework for transparent, evidence-based clinical decision support for HRV interpretation, moving beyond opaque black-box AI. It enhances the safety and trustworthiness of AI in biomedical applications by ensuring clinically-grounded reasoning, potentially leading to more accurate physiological assessments, personalized stress management, and improved diagnostic tools for autonomic dysfunction.

Abstract

Heart rate variability (HRV) is a pivotal noninvasive marker for autonomic monitoring; however, applying Large Language Models (LLMs) to HRV interpretation is hindered by physiological hallucinations. These include respiratory sinus arrhythmia (RSA) contamination, short-data instability in nonlinear metrics, and the neglect of individualized baselines in favor of population norms. We propose C-GRASP (Clinically-Grounded Reasoning for Affective Signal Processing), a guardrailed RAG-enhanced pipeline that decomposes HRV interpretation into eight traceable reasoning steps. Central to C-GRASP is a Z-score Priority Hierarchy that enforces the weighting of individualized baseline shifts over normative statistics. The system effectively mitigates spectral hallucinations through automated RSA-aware guardrails, preventing contamination of frequency-domain indices. Evaluated on 414 trials from the DREAMER dataset, C-GRASP integrated with high-scale reasoning models (e.g., MedGemma3-thinking) achieved superior performance in 4-class emotion classification (37.3% accuracy) and a Clinical Reasoning Consistency (CRC) score of 69.6%. Ablation studies confirm that the individualized Delta Z-score module serves as the critical logical anchor, preventing the "population bias" common in native LLMs. Ultimately, C-GRASP transitions affective computing from black-box classification to transparent, evidence-based clinical decision support, paving the way for safer AI integration in biomedical engineering.
