FaultDiffusion: Few-Shot Fault Time Series Generation with Diffusion Model

Summary

This paper introduces FaultDiffusion, a novel diffusion model-based framework for few-shot fault time-series generation, primarily in industrial equipment monitoring. It addresses the critical challenge of data scarcity for rare fault events by employing a positive-negative difference adapter and a diversity loss, enabling the synthesis of highly authentic and diverse fault samples that significantly outperform traditional methods.

While the paper focuses on industrial equipment, its core methodology addresses the universal challenge of few-shot data generation for rare events and anomalies in time-series data. This directly translates to medicine, where rare diseases, uncommon adverse drug reactions, or infrequent critical physiological events suffer from data scarcity, thus hindering the development of robust diagnostic and predictive models. The ability to synthetically generate diverse and authentic samples of such rare medical conditions could significantly advance medical AI.

Methodology

FaultDiffusion is built upon a diffusion model architecture. Its key innovations include a positive-negative difference adapter designed to learn the specific deviations between abundant normal data and scarce fault data by leveraging pre-trained normal data distributions. This adapter guides the diffusion process to synthesize accurate fault samples. Furthermore, a diversity loss function is incorporated during training to regularize the generation, preventing mode collapse and ensuring the synthetic fault time series are diverse and representative of the intrinsic variability found in real-world faults, even from a few examples.

Key Findings

The FaultDiffusion model demonstrably achieves state-of-the-art performance on key benchmarks, significantly outperforming traditional time-series generation methods. It excels in synthesizing fault samples that are both highly authentic (accurately reflecting real fault characteristics) and diverse (capturing the full range of variability within fault types), effectively addressing the critical bottleneck of few-shot data in data-driven fault diagnosis.

Abstract

In industrial equipment monitoring, fault diagnosis is critical for ensuring system reliability and enabling predictive maintenance. However, the scarcity of fault data, due to the rarity of fault events and the high cost of data annotation, significantly hinders data-driven approaches. Existing time-series generation models, optimized for abundant normal data, struggle to capture fault distributions in few-shot scenarios, producing samples that lack authenticity and diversity due to the large domain gap and high intra-class variability of faults. To address this, we propose a novel few-shot fault time-series generation framework based on diffusion models. Our approach employs a positive-negative difference adapter, leveraging pre-trained normal data distributions to model the discrepancies between normal and fault domains for accurate fault synthesis. Additionally, a diversity loss is introduced to prevent mode collapse, encouraging the generation of diverse fault samples through inter-sample difference regularization. Experimental results demonstrate that our model significantly outperforms traditional methods in authenticity and diversity, achieving state-of-the-art performance on key benchmarks.
