A benchmark dataset for evaluating Syndrome Differentiation and Treatment in large language models

Summary

This paper introduces TCM-BEST4SDT, a comprehensive, expert-led, clinical case-based benchmark designed to rigorously evaluate Large Language Models (LLMs) in Traditional Chinese Medicine's (TCM) Syndrome Differentiation and Treatment (SDT). It uniquely incorporates a specialized reward model to quantify prescription-syndrome congruence, assessing LLM capabilities beyond basic knowledge to include medical ethics, content safety, and treatment decision-making, with its effectiveness corroborated across 15 mainstream LLMs.

Medical Relevance

This research is critically important for advancing the safe and effective integration of AI, specifically LLMs, into Traditional Chinese Medicine clinical practice. By providing a standardized, expert-validated benchmark, it enables rigorous assessment of AI tools' ability to accurately perform syndrome differentiation and propose appropriate treatments, thereby enhancing patient safety, improving diagnostic accuracy, and fostering trust in AI-assisted TCM care.

AI Health Application

This research develops a benchmark (TCM-BEST4SDT) to evaluate the clinical application capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM). It assesses LLMs' performance in medical diagnosis (Syndrome Differentiation), treatment decision-making, basic TCM knowledge, medical ethics, and content safety. This directly contributes to the development and safe deployment of AI tools (LLMs) designed to assist or simulate aspects of clinical practice in TCM, thereby advancing AI applications in medicine and healthcare.

Key Points

  • Addresses the urgent need for a comprehensive evaluation framework for LLMs in TCM SDT, specifically targeting the individualized, holistic, and diverse nature of TCM practice often neglected by existing knowledge-based benchmarks.
  • Proposes TCM-BEST4SDT, a novel benchmark dataset spearheaded by TCM experts, constructed from clinical cases and following a rigorous data annotation pipeline.
  • Introduces a specialized reward model as a core component, designed to quantify prescription-syndrome congruence, thereby enabling the assessment of treatment decision-making which is a critical gap in current evaluations.
  • The benchmark encompasses four distinct evaluation tasks: TCM Basic Knowledge, Medical Ethics, LLM Content Safety, and the central Syndrome Differentiation and Treatment (SDT).
  • An integrated evaluation framework employs three mechanisms: selected-response evaluation, judge model evaluation, and the specialized reward model evaluation.
  • The effectiveness and utility of TCM-BEST4SDT were empirically corroborated through experiments conducted on 15 mainstream LLMs, covering both general-purpose and TCM-domain-specific models.
  • TCM-BEST4SDT is publicly available to foster and accelerate the development of intelligent research and applications within the TCM domain.

Methodology

The methodology involved the creation of TCM-BEST4SDT, a clinical case-based benchmark dataset, under the guidance of TCM experts and employing a rigorous data annotation pipeline. A specialized reward model was developed to quantify the congruence between proposed prescriptions and differentiated syndromes. The evaluation framework for LLMs integrates three distinct mechanisms: selected-response evaluation (likely for knowledge-based tasks), judge model evaluation (for more open-ended assessments), and the prescription-syndrome congruence reward model evaluation (for treatment decision-making). The benchmark's efficacy was then validated through experiments conducted on 15 mainstream LLMs.

Key Findings

The key finding is the successful development and corroboration of TCM-BEST4SDT as an effective, comprehensive, and clinically relevant benchmark for evaluating LLMs in TCM Syndrome Differentiation and Treatment. The experiments on 15 mainstream LLMs demonstrated its capability to assess not only basic TCM knowledge, medical ethics, and content safety but also, crucially, the accuracy and appropriateness of treatment decision-making through its unique prescription-syndrome congruence reward model.

Clinical Impact

The clinical impact lies in providing a robust, expert-validated tool for developers and researchers to accurately measure and improve the clinical application capabilities of LLMs designed for TCM. This will lead to the creation of more reliable, ethical, and safe AI systems that can genuinely assist TCM practitioners in syndrome differentiation and treatment planning, potentially enhancing diagnostic consistency, treatment efficacy, and ultimately, patient outcomes in TCM settings.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state specific limitations of the TCM-BEST4SDT benchmark itself. It primarily highlights limitations of *existing* benchmarks (e.g., confinement to knowledge-based question-answering, neglecting treatment decision-making) as the motivation for developing TCM-BEST4SDT.

Future Directions

The public availability of TCM-BEST4SDT is intended to "foster the development of intelligent TCM research." This implicitly suggests future directions including its use for continuous evaluation and comparison of new LLM architectures in TCM, guiding further research into improving LLM performance in complex SDT tasks, and potentially expanding the benchmark to cover more TCM specialities or diverse patient populations.

Abstract

The emergence of Large Language Models (LLMs) within the Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) domain presents an urgent need to assess their clinical application capabilities. However, such evaluations are challenged by the individualized, holistic, and diverse nature of TCM's "Syndrome Differentiation and Treatment" (SDT). Existing benchmarks are confined to knowledge-based question-answering or the accuracy of syndrome differentiation, often neglecting assessment of treatment decision-making. Here, we propose a comprehensive, clinical case-based benchmark spearheaded by TCM experts, and a specialized reward model employed to quantify prescription-syndrome congruence. Data annotation follows a rigorous pipeline. This benchmark, designated TCM-BEST4SDT, encompasses four tasks, including TCM Basic Knowledge, Medical Ethics, LLM Content Safety, and SDT. The evaluation framework integrates three mechanisms, namely selected-response evaluation, judge model evaluation, and reward model evaluation. The effectiveness of TCM-BEST4SDT was corroborated through experiments on 15 mainstream LLMs, spanning both general and TCM domains. To foster the development of intelligent TCM research, TCM-BEST4SDT is now publicly available.
