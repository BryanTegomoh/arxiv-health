Skin-R1: Toward Trustworthy Clinical Reasoning for Dermatological Diagnosis

Summary

SkinR1 introduces a novel vision-language model designed for dermatological diagnosis, specifically addressing key limitations of existing models such as data heterogeneity, lack of reasoning rationales, and poor generalization. It integrates deep, textbook-based reasoning with reinforcement learning to provide trustworthy, grounded clinical reasoning. The model demonstrates superior diagnostic accuracy across multiple dermatology datasets.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly advances the development of trustworthy AI for dermatological diagnosis by providing models with explainable, expert-level reasoning. It has the potential to enhance diagnostic accuracy, consistency, and provide valuable decision support for clinicians.

AI Health Application

This research develops SkinR1, a novel dermatological VLM designed to assist healthcare professionals in diagnosing skin conditions. It aims to improve diagnostic accuracy and provide trustworthy, textbook-based clinical reasoning, addressing key challenges in real-world clinical application of AI for dermatology.

Key Points

  • Addresses critical limitations in current VLMs for dermatological diagnosis: data heterogeneity, absence of grounded diagnostic rationales, and limited scalability/generalization.
  • Proposes SkinR1, a VLM that combines textbook-based reasoning with broad generalization capabilities from reinforcement learning.
  • Develops a textbook-based reasoning generator to synthesize high-fidelity, hierarchy-aware, and differential-diagnosis (DDx)-informed trajectories, serving as reliable expert-level supervision.
  • Leverages these generated trajectories for Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) to empower the model with robust and grounded reasoning abilities.
  • Implements a novel Reinforcement Learning (RL) paradigm that incorporates the hierarchical structure of diseases, enabling effective transfer of grounded reasoning to large-scale, sparsely annotated datasets.
  • Achieves superior diagnostic accuracy across multiple dermatology datasets, outperforming existing methods.
  • Ablation studies confirm the crucial role of the reasoning foundation established through Supervised Fine-Tuning in SkinR1's overall performance and trustworthiness.

Methodology

SkinR1 employs a unified, end-to-end framework: (1) a textbook-based reasoning generator synthesizes high-fidelity, hierarchy-aware, and differential-diagnosis (DDx)-informed reasoning trajectories; (2) these trajectories are utilized for supervised fine-tuning (SFT) to imbue the model with grounded reasoning; and (3) a novel reinforcement learning (RL) paradigm, incorporating disease hierarchies, facilitates the transfer of these reasoning patterns to large-scale, sparse datasets.

Key Findings

SkinR1 demonstrated superior diagnostic accuracy across multiple dermatology datasets. An ablation study further confirmed the critical importance of the reasoning foundation instilled by the supervised fine-tuning (SFT) phase for the model's performance.

Clinical Impact

SkinR1's ability to provide grounded, textbook-based reasoning alongside high accuracy could significantly improve the reliability and interpretability of AI in dermatology. This could lead to more confident and consistent diagnoses, potentially aiding clinicians in complex cases, reducing diagnostic errors, and serving as an advanced training tool for medical students and residents.

Abstract

The emergence of vision-language models (VLMs) has opened new possibilities for clinical reasoning and has shown promising performance in dermatological diagnosis. However, their trustworthiness and clinical utility are often limited by three major factors: (1) Data heterogeneity, where diverse datasets lack consistent diagnostic labels and clinical concept annotations; (2) Absence of grounded diagnostic rationales, leading to a scarcity of reliable reasoning supervision; and (3) Limited scalability and generalization, as models trained on small, densely annotated datasets struggle to transfer nuanced reasoning to large, sparsely-annotated ones. To address these limitations, we propose SkinR1, a novel dermatological VLM that combines deep, textbook-based reasoning with the broad generalization capabilities of reinforcement learning (RL). SkinR1 systematically resolves the key challenges through a unified, end-to-end framework. First, we design a textbook-based reasoning generator that synthesizes high-fidelity, hierarchy-aware, and differential-diagnosis (DDx)-informed trajectories, providing reliable expert-level supervision. Second, we leverage the constructed trajectories for supervised fine-tuning (SFT) empowering the model with grounded reasoning ability. Third, we develop a novel RL paradigm that, by incorporating the hierarchical structure of diseases, effectively transfers these grounded reasoning patterns to large-scale, sparse data. Extensive experiments on multiple dermatology datasets demonstrate that SkinR1 achieves superior diagnostic accuracy. The ablation study demonstrates the importance of the reasoning foundation instilled by SFT.
