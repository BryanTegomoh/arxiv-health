A Lightweight Multi-Scale Attention Framework for Real-Time Spinal Endoscopic Instance Segmentation

arXiv ID: 2512.21984v1
Published: 2025-12-26
Authors: Qi Lai, JunYan Li, Qiang Cai, Lei Wang, Tao Yan, XiaoKun Liang
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 0.98 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces LMSF-A, a lightweight multi-scale attention framework designed for real-time instance segmentation in spinal endoscopy, addressing challenges like limited visibility and hardware constraints. By co-designing the backbone, neck, and head for efficiency and accuracy, the framework achieves competitive performance with minimal computational cost. The authors also release a new clinically reviewed PELD dataset and demonstrate the model's exceptional lightness, stability under small-batch training, and strong generalization capabilities.

Medical Relevance

Real-time instance segmentation in spinal endoscopy is paramount for improving surgical safety and precision. By accurately identifying and delineating critical anatomical structures like nerves and ligaments during surgery, this technology directly helps prevent iatrogenic injuries, leading to better patient outcomes and reduced complications in minimally invasive spinal procedures.

AI Health Application

The AI application is a real-time assistive system for spinal endoscopic surgery. It uses instance segmentation to automatically detect and delineate vital anatomical structures, enabling surgeons to protect them and navigate more safely during the procedure, thereby reducing complications and improving patient outcomes.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical need for real-time instance segmentation in spinal endoscopy to identify and protect vital anatomy, overcoming challenges such as narrow fields of view, poor visibility, and limited surgical hardware.
  • Proposes LMSF-A, a novel lightweight multi-scale attention framework meticulously co-designed across its backbone, neck, and head for optimal performance and resource efficiency.
  • The backbone integrates a C2f-Pro module combining RepViT-style re-parameterized convolution (RVB) with Efficient Multi-scale Attention (EMA), enabling multi-branch training for robustness and collapsing into a single fast path for inference.
  • The neck module enhances cross-scale consistency and boundary detail through Scale-Sequence Feature Fusion (SSFF) and Triple Feature Encoding (TFE), which specifically strengthens high-resolution features.
  • A Lightweight Multi-task Shared Head (LMSH) is adopted, utilizing shared convolutions and GroupNorm to significantly reduce parameters and ensure stability during small-batch (including batch-1) training, crucial for surgical deployment.
  • The authors release a new, clinically reviewed PELD dataset (61 patients, 610 images) featuring instance masks for critical spinal structures: adipose tissue, bone, ligamentum flavum, and nerve.
  • LMSF-A demonstrates highly competitive or superior performance across all evaluation metrics, achieving extreme lightness with only 1.8M parameters and 8.8 GFLOPs, and proving generalizability on a public teeth benchmark.

Methodology

The methodology revolves around the LMSF-A framework, consisting of a C2f-Pro backbone that combines RepViT-style re-parameterized convolution (RVB) with Efficient Multi-scale Attention (EMA). The neck features Scale-Sequence Feature Fusion (SSFF) and Triple Feature Encoding (TFE) to refine feature representation and boundary details. The head is a Lightweight Multi-task Shared Head (LMSH) employing shared convolutions and GroupNorm for parameter efficiency and batch-1 stability. The model was trained and evaluated on a newly curated, clinically reviewed PELD dataset specifically for spinal endoscopic imagery.

Key Findings

LMSF-A achieves highly competitive or superior performance compared to existing instance segmentation methods across all evaluation metrics. A primary finding is its exceptional lightness, requiring only 1.8 million parameters and 8.8 GFLOPs, making it suitable for resource-constrained surgical hardware. The framework also demonstrates robust stability during batch-1 training and exhibits strong generalization capabilities when tested on an independent teeth benchmark.

Clinical Impact

This technology has the potential to significantly enhance the safety and efficacy of spinal endoscopic surgeries. By providing surgeons with real-time, precise visual guidance on critical anatomical structures, it can help prevent inadvertent damage to nerves and other vital tissues, optimize surgical trajectory, and improve overall procedural success rates, ultimately benefiting patient recovery and reducing post-operative complications.

Abstract

Real-time instance segmentation for spinal endoscopy is important for identifying and protecting critical anatomy during surgery, but it is difficult because of the narrow field of view, specular highlights, smoke/bleeding, unclear boundaries, and large scale changes. Deployment is also constrained by limited surgical hardware, so the model must balance accuracy and speed and remain stable under small-batch (even batch-1) training. We propose LMSF-A, a lightweight multi-scale attention framework co-designed across backbone, neck, and head. The backbone uses a C2f-Pro module that combines RepViT-style re-parameterized convolution (RVB) with efficient multi-scale attention (EMA), enabling multi-branch training while collapsing into a single fast path for inference. The neck improves cross-scale consistency and boundary detail using Scale-Sequence Feature Fusion (SSFF) and Triple Feature Encoding (TFE), which strengthens high-resolution features. The head adopts a Lightweight Multi-task Shared Head (LMSH) with shared convolutions and GroupNorm to reduce parameters and support batch-1 stability. We also release the clinically reviewed PELD dataset (61 patients, 610 images) with instance masks for adipose tissue, bone, ligamentum flavum, and nerve. Experiments show that LMSF-A is highly competitive (or even better than) in all evaluation metrics and much lighter than most instance segmentation methods requiring only 1.8M parameters and 8.8 GFLOPs, and it generalizes well to a public teeth benchmark.
