Predicting Early and Complete Drug Release from Long-Acting Injectables Using Explainable Machine Learning

arXiv ID: 2601.02265v1
Published: 2026-01-05
Authors: Karla N. Robles, Manar D. Samad
Categories: q-bio.BM, cs.LG
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces a novel data transformation and explainable machine learning (ML) approach to predict early and complete drug release from 321 long-acting injectable (LAI) formulations. The method accurately predicts early drug release and classifies release profiles, while also identifying key material characteristics that modulate drug release dynamics using Shapley additive explanations, providing a quantitative strategy for LAI optimization. It outperforms current time-dependent approaches for complex release curve predictions.

Medical Relevance

Long-acting injectables significantly improve the treatment of chronic diseases by enabling controlled drug delivery, which reduces dosing frequency and enhances patient adherence. This research accelerates the complex development and optimization process for LAI formulations, potentially leading to faster availability of more effective and patient-friendly therapies.

The AI application involves using explainable machine learning to model complex relationships between LAI properties and drug release dynamics. This accelerates the development of new LAI formulations, predicts their performance, and provides actionable insights (via explainability) to pharmaceutical scientists for optimizing drug-release profiles. Key Points

  • Addresses the challenge of extensive optimization required for controlled drug release from polymer-based long-acting injectables (LAIs) by employing ML.
  • Proposes a novel data transformation and explainable machine learning (ML) framework tailored to LAI data, overcoming limitations of previous ML studies.
  • Conducted three main experiments: predicting early drug release (at 24, 48, and 72 hours), classifying release profile types, and predicting complete release profiles using data from 321 LAI formulations.
  • Achieved a strong correlation (>0.65) between true and predicted drug release in 72 hours and an 0.87 F1-score for classifying release profile types.
  • A time-independent ML framework was shown to predict delayed biphasic and triphasic release curves with better performance than current time-dependent approaches.
  • Utilized Shapley additive explanations (SHAP) to reveal the relative influence of specific LAI material characteristics during both early and complete drug release, offering novel, actionable insights.
  • The findings provide a quantitative strategy and recommendations for scientists to efficiently optimize the drug-release dynamics of LAI formulations.

Methodology

The study employed a novel data transformation technique coupled with an explainable machine learning (ML) framework. It analyzed 321 LAI formulations across three distinct prediction tasks: regression for early drug release (24, 48, 72 hours), classification for release profile types, and prediction of complete release profiles. Shapley additive explanations (SHAP) were utilized to interpret the ML models and identify the relative influence of LAI material characteristics on drug release dynamics.

Key Findings

The ML models demonstrated high predictive accuracy, achieving a correlation exceeding 0.65 for 72-hour drug release prediction and an 0.87 F1-score for classifying release profile types. A time-independent ML approach was found to outperform time-dependent methods in predicting complex delayed biphasic and triphasic release curves. Crucially, Shapley additive explanations successfully identified the specific material characteristics that significantly modulate both early and complete drug release profiles, providing actionable insights for LAI design.

By providing a quantitative strategy for understanding and controlling drug release dynamics, it can lead to faster creation of more effective, patient-compliant therapies for chronic diseases, ultimately improving patient adherence and therapeutic outcomes.

Abstract

Polymer-based long-acting injectables (LAIs) have transformed the treatment of chronic diseases by enabling controlled drug delivery, thus reducing dosing frequency and extending therapeutic duration. Achieving controlled drug release from LAIs requires extensive optimization of the complex underlying physicochemical properties. Machine learning (ML) can accelerate LAI development by modeling the complex relationships between LAI properties and drug release. However, recent ML studies have provided limited information on key properties that modulate drug release, due to the lack of custom modeling and analysis tailored to LAI data. This paper presents a novel data transformation and explainable ML approach to synthesize actionable information from 321 LAI formulations by predicting early drug release at 24, 48, and 72 hours, classification of release profile types, and prediction of complete release profiles. These three experiments investigate the contribution and control of LAI material characteristics in early and complete drug release profiles. A strong correlation (>0.65) is observed between the true and predicted drug release in 72 hours, while a 0.87 F1-score is obtained in classifying release profile types. A time-independent ML framework predicts delayed biphasic and triphasic curves with better performance than current time-dependent approaches. Shapley additive explanations reveal the relative influence of material characteristics during early and for complete release which fill several gaps in previous in-vitro and ML-based studies. The novel approach and findings can provide a quantitative strategy and recommendations for scientists to optimize the drug-release dynamics of LAI. The source code for the model implementation is publicly available.
