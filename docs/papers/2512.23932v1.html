A Proof-of-Concept for Explainable Disease Diagnosis Using Large Language Models and Answer Set Programming

arXiv ID: 2512.23932v1
Published: 2025-12-30
Authors: Ioanna Gemou, Evangelos Lamprou
Categories: cs.AI
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces McCoy, a novel proof-of-concept framework that addresses the challenge of creating high-quality knowledge bases for symbolic AI in healthcare by integrating Large Language Models (LLMs) with Answer Set Programming (ASP). McCoy leverages LLMs to translate medical literature into ASP code, combines it with patient data, and uses an ASP solver to produce robust and interpretable disease diagnoses. Preliminary results indicate strong performance on small-scale diagnostic tasks, demonstrating the potential for explainable AI in medicine.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and timely disease diagnosis is critical for patient outcomes, and this work offers an innovative method to enhance diagnostic accuracy and interpretability using AI. By reducing the manual effort in creating medical knowledge bases, it could accelerate the development of sophisticated clinical decision support systems.

AI Health Application

The paper describes McCoy, an AI framework that leverages Large Language Models (LLMs) to interpret medical literature and generate ASP code. This code is then combined with patient data and processed by an ASP solver to provide an explainable disease diagnosis, serving as an interpretable prediction framework for healthcare.

Key Points

  • The core problem addressed is the significant effort required to construct high-quality knowledge bases (KBs) for symbolic AI applications in healthcare, which has limited their widespread adoption.
  • McCoy is a proposed framework that combines the natural language understanding capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) with the logical reasoning and interpretability of Answer Set Programming (ASP).
  • The framework's workflow involves an LLM converting medical literature into structured ASP code, which is then merged with specific patient data.
  • An ASP solver processes the combined medical knowledge and patient data to generate a final, explainable disease diagnosis.
  • McCoy aims to provide a robust and interpretable prediction framework by leveraging the complementary strengths of both data-driven LLMs and knowledge-driven symbolic AI.
  • Preliminary results demonstrate that McCoy achieves strong performance on small-scale disease diagnosis tasks, validating its proof-of-concept.
  • The approach contributes to the field of explainable AI (XAI) in medicine by offering transparent reasoning for diagnostic predictions.

Methodology

The McCoy framework employs an orchestration strategy where a Large Language Model (LLM) is used to translate unstructured medical literature into formal Answer Set Programming (ASP) code, representing medical knowledge. This generated ASP code is then combined with patient-specific clinical data. An ASP solver subsequently processes this integrated knowledge base to logically infer and arrive at a final disease diagnosis.

Key Findings

The primary finding is that the McCoy framework, a novel integration of LLMs and ASP, successfully operates as a proof-of-concept for explainable disease diagnosis. It exhibits strong performance on small-scale diagnostic tasks, demonstrating its capability to leverage medical literature and patient data to produce interpretable predictions.

Clinical Impact

This framework has the potential to significantly improve clinical decision-making by providing highly accurate and, crucially, *explainable* disease diagnoses. This transparency can foster greater trust among clinicians in AI-driven diagnostic tools, reduce diagnostic errors, and streamline the process of building and maintaining medical knowledge bases, ultimately leading to more effective and personalized patient care.

Limitations

The current work is presented as a 'proof-of-concept' and the strong performance is specifically noted on 'small-scale disease diagnosis tasks'. This suggests potential limitations regarding scalability to larger, more complex real-world diagnostic scenarios and the generalizability of the knowledge bases generated by LLMs.

Future Directions

While not explicitly stated, the 'proof-of-concept' and 'small-scale' nature imply future work would involve scaling McCoy to handle larger and more complex diagnostic challenges, validating its performance on broader datasets, and exploring its applicability across diverse medical specialties. Further research could also focus on refining the LLM-to-ASP translation process and enhancing the robustness of the generated knowledge bases.

Abstract

Accurate disease prediction is vital for timely intervention, effective treatment, and reducing medical complications. While symbolic AI has been applied in healthcare, its adoption remains limited due to the effort required for constructing high-quality knowledge bases. This work introduces McCoy, a framework that combines Large Language Models (LLMs) with Answer Set Programming (ASP) to overcome this barrier. McCoy orchestrates an LLM to translate medical literature into ASP code, combines it with patient data, and processes it using an ASP solver to arrive at the final diagnosis. This integration yields a robust, interpretable prediction framework that leverages the strengths of both paradigms. Preliminary results show McCoy has strong performance on small-scale disease diagnosis tasks.
