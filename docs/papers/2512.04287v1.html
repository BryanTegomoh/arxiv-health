Summary

This review paper explores the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into Horizon Scanning for Infectious Diseases, focusing on identifying and responding to emerging threats and opportunities. It demonstrates how AI tools can enhance public health preparedness by improving signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support, while also addressing associated risks and proposing governance strategies.

Medical Relevance

This research is critically important for public health by providing advanced methodologies to proactively anticipate, monitor, and mitigate the impact of emerging infectious disease threats, thereby bolstering global health security and enhancing preparedness against future pandemics and epidemics.

AI Health Application

The AI application described is horizon scanning, signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support specifically for identifying and responding to emerging infectious disease threats and enhancing public health preparedness.

Key Points

  • The paper reviews the application of AI tools specifically for Horizon Scanning in the context of Infectious Diseases.
  • AI is shown to significantly enhance signal detection capabilities for identifying novel or emerging infectious threats.
  • The integration of AI improves data monitoring processes, critical for real-time surveillance of infectious disease patterns.
  • AI tools facilitate more robust and comprehensive scenario analysis, aiding in preparedness planning for potential outbreaks.
  • Decision support systems for public health responses are strengthened through AI integration, enabling more informed and rapid actions.
  • Risks associated with the adoption of AI in public health, such as data biases, ethical considerations, and algorithmic transparency, are addressed.
  • Strategies for effective implementation and robust governance frameworks for AI in infectious disease preparedness are proposed.
  • The review outlines both the considerable potential and the inherent limitations of AI within public health preparedness efforts.

Methodology

This paper constitutes a comprehensive literature review and conceptual exploration. It examines existing and potential applications of Artificial Intelligence within the framework of Horizon Scanning for infectious diseases, analyzing their utility, limitations, associated risks, and proposing implementation and governance strategies.

Key Findings

The review finds that AI tools offer substantial enhancements across critical phases of horizon scanning for infectious diseases, including improved signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support, thereby significantly bolstering public health preparedness. It also emphasizes the imperative of addressing inherent risks and establishing clear implementation and governance frameworks for successful and ethical AI adoption.

Clinical Impact

While not directly impacting individual patient clinical care, the adoption of AI in horizon scanning will profoundly improve public health outcomes by enabling earlier and more accurate detection of novel pathogens, precise forecasting of outbreaks, and better-informed, rapid allocation of resources during infectious disease emergencies, leading to reduced population-level morbidity and mortality.

Limitations

The abstract explicitly mentions addressing the 'risks associated with AI adoption' and demonstrating the 'limitations of AI.' While specific details are not provided, these likely encompass challenges such as data bias, algorithmic opacity, security vulnerabilities, ethical concerns, the need for robust human oversight, and the potential for misinterpretation of AI-generated insights.

Future Directions

Although not explicitly detailing future research, the paper's focus on proposing 'strategies for effective implementation and governance' strongly implies the need for ongoing practical development, rigorous testing, and refinement of AI systems within public health frameworks. This also points towards further research into mitigating identified risks, ensuring ethical deployment, and optimizing AI performance for comprehensive infectious disease preparedness.

Abstract

This review explores the integration of Artificial Intelligence into Horizon Scanning, focusing on identifying and responding to emerging threats and opportunities linked to Infectious Diseases. We examine how AI tools can enhance signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support. We also address the risks associated with AI adoption and propose strategies for effective implementation and governance. The findings contribute to the growing body of Foresight literature by demonstrating the potential and limitations of AI in Public Health preparedness.
