Summary

This review explores the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into Horizon Scanning for infectious diseases, focusing on identifying and responding to emerging threats. It analyzes how AI tools can enhance critical functions like signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support, while also addressing associated risks and proposing strategies for effective implementation and governance.

Medical Relevance

This research is critically relevant for public health preparedness and global health security, as it outlines how AI can significantly improve the early detection, monitoring, and proactive management of emerging infectious disease threats, thereby mitigating their potential impact on populations.

AI Health Application

AI is applied for early signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support to identify and respond to emerging infectious disease threats, thereby enhancing public health preparedness and biosecurity.

Key Points

  • The paper focuses on applying AI in Horizon Scanning specifically for the identification and response to emerging infectious disease threats.
  • It examines how AI tools can significantly enhance signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support processes in public health.
  • The review explicitly addresses the inherent risks associated with the adoption and deployment of AI technologies in this sensitive domain.
  • Strategies for effective implementation and robust governance of AI applications in infectious disease preparedness are proposed.
  • The work contributes to the broader 'Foresight' literature by detailing both the potential capabilities and inherent limitations of AI in Public Health preparedness.
  • The core objective is to improve public health's proactive capacity to anticipate and manage infectious disease risks.

Methodology

This paper is a review that conceptually explores and analyzes the integration of AI tools into Horizon Scanning for infectious diseases. It involves examining the potential applications, associated risks, and proposing strategies for implementation and governance, rather than conducting empirical research or experimental studies.

Key Findings

Key findings indicate that AI tools offer substantial enhancements across signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support systems crucial for infectious disease horizon scanning. However, successful AI integration necessitates proactive management of associated risks and the development of robust implementation and governance frameworks. The paper identifies both the considerable potential and the inherent limitations of AI that must be understood for effective public health preparedness.

Clinical Impact

The potential clinical impact includes earlier and more accurate warning of novel pathogens or outbreaks, enabling faster and more targeted public health interventions. This can lead to improved resource allocation, reduced disease transmission, better patient outcomes through timely clinical responses, and overall enhanced resilience of healthcare systems during infectious disease crises.

Limitations

The abstract explicitly notes that the review addresses the 'risks associated with AI adoption' and demonstrates the 'potential and limitations of AI'. While not detailing specific limitations, it acknowledges that the paper itself identifies and discusses these inherent challenges and constraints within AI applications for public health preparedness.

Future Directions

The paper implicitly suggests a future need for continued research and development in establishing and refining robust strategies for AI implementation and governance in public health. It also points towards ongoing exploration of AI's evolving potential and limitations within the dynamic context of Public Health preparedness and Foresight studies.

Abstract

This review explores the integration of Artificial Intelligence into Horizon Scanning, focusing on identifying and responding to emerging threats and opportunities linked to Infectious Diseases. We examine how AI tools can enhance signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support. We also address the risks associated with AI adoption and propose strategies for effective implementation and governance. The findings contribute to the growing body of Foresight literature by demonstrating the potential and limitations of AI in Public Health preparedness.
