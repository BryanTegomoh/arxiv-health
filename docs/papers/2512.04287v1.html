Summary

This review paper comprehensively examines the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into Horizon Scanning specifically for identifying and responding to emerging infectious disease threats. It details how AI tools can bolster signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support mechanisms within public health preparedness frameworks. The authors also address the inherent risks of AI adoption and propose strategies for its effective implementation and governance, contributing to the understanding of AI's potential and limitations in public health preparedness.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant for public health and infectious disease epidemiology, as it provides a framework for leveraging AI to proactively identify, monitor, and respond to emerging infectious disease threats. By enhancing early warning and decision support systems, it can significantly improve global health security and preparedness against future pandemics.

AI Health Application

Artificial Intelligence is applied to horizon scanning, signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support specifically for identifying and responding to emerging threats from infectious diseases, thereby enhancing public health preparedness and biosecurity measures.

Key Points

  • The paper conducts a review of Artificial Intelligence (AI) applications within the domain of Horizon Scanning for infectious diseases.
  • AI tools are analyzed for their capacity to enhance critical functions such as signal detection, continuous data monitoring, and sophisticated scenario analysis in public health.
  • AI's potential to significantly improve decision support systems for responding to infectious disease threats is a core focus.
  • The authors specifically address and examine the risks and challenges associated with the adoption and deployment of AI technologies in this sensitive context.
  • Proposals for effective implementation strategies and robust governance frameworks for AI in infectious disease preparedness are presented.
  • The review contributes to Foresight literature by outlining both the significant potential and inherent limitations of AI in strengthening Public Health preparedness against emerging threats.

Methodology

This paper employs a comprehensive review methodology, exploring and synthesizing existing literature and conceptual frameworks on the integration of Artificial Intelligence into Horizon Scanning specifically for infectious diseases. The authors qualitatively examine various AI applications, their potential benefits, associated risks, and strategies for governance without conducting empirical data analysis.

Key Findings

The review finds that AI possesses substantial potential to enhance crucial aspects of infectious disease horizon scanning, including advanced signal detection, continuous real-time data monitoring, sophisticated scenario analysis, and robust decision support systems. However, it also highlights that the adoption of AI is accompanied by inherent risks, underscoring the necessity for carefully developed strategies for effective implementation and strong governance frameworks to maximize benefits and mitigate downsides in public health preparedness.

Clinical Impact

The practical impact lies in significantly improving the speed and accuracy of identifying emerging infectious disease threats, enabling more timely and effective public health interventions. This can lead to earlier containment, better resource allocation, reduced disease spread, and ultimately, a decrease in morbidity and mortality associated with novel pathogens, strengthening global health security.

Limitations

The abstract notes that the paper demonstrates the "limitations of AI" within public health preparedness. While specific limitations of the review methodology itself are not detailed, it implies a comprehensive discussion within the paper about the inherent challenges, biases, or technical constraints of AI technologies when applied to complex health threats.

Future Directions

Not explicitly mentioned in the provided abstract, though the discussion on "strategies for effective implementation and governance" suggests a call for practical application and refinement of AI frameworks in public health rather than specific research avenues.

Abstract

This review explores the integration of Artificial Intelligence into Horizon Scanning, focusing on identifying and responding to emerging threats and opportunities linked to Infectious Diseases. We examine how AI tools can enhance signal detection, data monitoring, scenario analysis, and decision support. We also address the risks associated with AI adoption and propose strategies for effective implementation and governance. The findings contribute to the growing body of Foresight literature by demonstrating the potential and limitations of AI in Public Health preparedness.
