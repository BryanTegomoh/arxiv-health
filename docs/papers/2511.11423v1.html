Summary

CURENet introduces a multimodal deep learning framework designed to integrate diverse Electronic Health Record (EHR) data for efficient chronic disease prediction. By leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) and transformer encoders, the model effectively captures complex interactions across clinical notes, lab tests, and time-series visit data. This comprehensive approach achieved over 94% accuracy in predicting top chronic conditions, demonstrating its potential to enhance clinical decision-making.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to healthcare as it provides a more comprehensive and accurate method for predicting chronic diseases by fully utilizing the richness of multimodal EHR data, thereby enabling physicians to make more informed therapeutic decisions and potentially improve patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

CURENet is an AI model designed to predict chronic illnesses by integrating and analyzing multimodal EHR data (clinical notes, lab tests, time-series visits) using advanced AI techniques like LLMs for text processing and transformer encoders for sequential data. This serves as a predictive tool to aid physicians in therapeutic decision-making and ultimately improve patient outcomes.

Key Points

  • Addresses the limitation of most predictive models failing to fully capture interactions, redundancies, and temporal patterns across multiple EHR data modalities.
  • CURENet is a multimodal model specifically designed for efficient chronic disease prediction.
  • Integrates three core EHR data types: unstructured clinical notes, structured lab tests (including textual components), and time-series patient visit data.
  • Utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) for processing clinical text from notes and textual lab test descriptions.
  • Employs transformer encoders to analyze and capture patterns in longitudinal sequential patient visit data.
  • Evaluated on two distinct datasets: the public MIMIC-III and a private FEMH dataset.
  • Achieved high performance, exceeding 94% accuracy in predicting the top 10 chronic conditions within a multi-label classification framework.

Methodology

CURENet is a multimodal deep learning framework. It processes unstructured clinical notes and textual lab tests using Large Language Models (LLMs) to extract semantic representations. Longitudinal sequential patient visit data is handled by transformer encoders to model temporal dependencies. These unified representations from different modalities are then combined to form a comprehensive patient view, enabling multi-label prediction of chronic diseases. The model was trained and validated on MIMIC-III and FEMH datasets.

Key Findings

CURENet demonstrated superior performance, achieving over 94% accuracy in predicting the top 10 chronic conditions when evaluated on both the public MIMIC-III and private FEMH datasets. This highlights its capability to capture intricate interactions between diverse clinical data types for reliable chronic illness prediction.

Clinical Impact

The implementation of CURENet has the potential to significantly enhance clinical decision-making by providing a more reliable and holistic predictive model for chronic illnesses. This could lead to earlier diagnosis, more targeted interventions, personalized treatment plans, and ultimately, improved health outcomes for patients by leveraging a complete understanding of their health status derived from all available EHR data.

Abstract

Electronic health records (EHRs) are designed to synthesize diverse data types, including unstructured clinical notes, structured lab tests, and time-series visit data. Physicians draw on these multimodal and temporal sources of EHR data to form a comprehensive view of a patient's health, which is crucial for informed therapeutic decision-making. Yet, most predictive models fail to fully capture the interactions, redundancies, and temporal patterns across multiple data modalities, often focusing on a single data type or overlooking these complexities. In this paper, we present CURENet, a multimodal model (Combining Unified Representations for Efficient chronic disease prediction) that integrates unstructured clinical notes, lab tests, and patients' time-series data by utilizing large language models (LLMs) for clinical text processing and textual lab tests, as well as transformer encoders for longitudinal sequential visits. CURENet has been capable of capturing the intricate interaction between different forms of clinical data and creating a more reliable predictive model for chronic illnesses. We evaluated CURENet using the public MIMIC-III and private FEMH datasets, where it achieved over 94\% accuracy in predicting the top 10 chronic conditions in a multi-label framework. Our findings highlight the potential of multimodal EHR integration to enhance clinical decision-making and improve patient outcomes.
