Roleplaying with Structure: Synthetic Therapist-Client Conversation Generation from Questionnaires

Summary

This paper introduces SQPsych, an LLM-driven pipeline for generating synthetic therapist-client dialogues based on structured client profiles and psychological questionnaires, addressing the scarcity of real therapy data. Utilizing open-weight LLMs to comply with data privacy regulations, the framework simulates CBT-based conversations for conditions like anxiety and depression. The resulting fine-tuned models, SQPsychLLM, demonstrate strong performance in therapeutic skills, highlighting the potential of synthetic data for scalable and data-secure AI in mental health.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for advancing AI applications in mental health by providing a method to overcome the significant hurdle of scarce, private, and sensitive therapeutic dialogue data. It enables the development and training of AI models that can offer scalable, accessible, and clinically informed mental health support, especially relevant for conditions like anxiety and depression.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of advanced AI models (specifically LLMs) for mental health support, counseling, and therapy. It focuses on generating high-quality synthetic therapeutic conversations from structured clinical input (questionnaires) to train and improve AI agents that can assist with mental health conditions such as anxiety and depression, thereby expanding access to clinically informed support in a data-secure manner.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical data scarcity problem for AI in mental health due to strict privacy regulations and historical lack of recorded therapy dialogues.
  • Presents SQPsych, a novel LLM-driven pipeline for generating synthetic Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)-based therapist-client conversations.
  • Leverages structured client profiles and psychological questionnaires as direct input for the dialogue generation process.
  • Employs open-weight LLMs to overcome data governance and privacy restrictions, making it feasible where proprietary model usage for clinical data is prohibited.
  • Generates a high-quality synthetic dialogue corpus (SQPsychConv), which is validated through both human expert evaluation and LLM-based assessments.
  • Fine-tuned SQPsychLLM models, trained on the SQPsychConv corpus, achieve strong performance on counseling benchmarks, surpassing existing baselines in key therapeutic skills.
  • Highlights the potential of this synthetic data approach to enable scalable, data-secure, and clinically informed AI solutions for mental health support.

Methodology

The SQPsych framework employs open-weight Large Language Models to simulate therapist-client interactions. It takes structured psychological input, derived from client profiles and questionnaires (e.g., related to anxiety and depression), and converts it into natural language dialogues, adhering to Cognitive Behavioral Therapy principles. The generated corpus (SQPsychConv) is validated via human expert review and LLM-based metrics, and subsequently used to fine-tune SQPsychLLM models, which are then evaluated on counseling benchmarks.

Key Findings

The developed SQPsych pipeline successfully generates a high-quality corpus of synthetic therapeutic conversations. Fine-tuned SQPsychLLM models, trained on this corpus, achieve strong performance on counseling benchmarks, demonstrating superior proficiency in key therapeutic skills compared to existing baselines. This indicates the viability of synthetic data generated by open-weight LLMs for developing effective and data-secure AI in mental health.

Clinical Impact

This work provides a critical pathway for the development and widespread adoption of AI-powered mental health interventions, particularly in areas with limited access to human therapists. By generating clinically informed, data-secure training data, it can accelerate the creation of tools for early detection, personalized support, and scalable therapeutic assistance, potentially improving patient outcomes for conditions like anxiety and depression without compromising sensitive patient information.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations. However, inherent challenges with synthetic data include potential biases introduced during generation, the difficulty of perfectly capturing the nuanced unpredictability of real human interaction, and complex clinical scenarios not fully represented by structured questionnaires. The fidelity of simulated CBT principles compared to real-world clinical application also warrants further investigation.

Future Directions

Although not explicitly mentioned, future directions could involve expanding the SQPsych framework to encompass a broader range of therapeutic approaches and clinical disorders, integrating multi-modal client data (e.g., voice, physiological signals), refining dialogue generation for enhanced clinical realism and personalization, and conducting rigorous real-world clinical validation studies to assess the efficacy and safety of AI models trained on such synthetic data.

Abstract

The development of AI for mental health is hindered by a lack of authentic therapy dialogues, due to strict privacy regulations and the fact that clinical sessions were historically rarely recorded. We present an LLM-driven pipeline that generates synthetic counseling dialogues based on structured client profiles and psychological questionnaires. Grounded on the principles of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), our method creates synthetic therapeutic conversations for clinical disorders such as anxiety and depression. Our framework, SQPsych (Structured Questionnaire-based Psychotherapy), converts structured psychological input into natural language dialogues through therapist-client simulations. Due to data governance policies and privacy restrictions prohibiting the transmission of clinical questionnaire data to third-party services, previous methodologies relying on proprietary models are infeasible in our setting. We address this limitation by generating a high-quality corpus using open-weight LLMs, validated through human expert evaluation and LLM-based assessments. Our SQPsychLLM models fine-tuned on SQPsychConv achieve strong performance on counseling benchmarks, surpassing baselines in key therapeutic skills. Our findings highlight the potential of synthetic data to enable scalable, data-secure, and clinically informed AI for mental health support. We will release our code, models, and corpus at https://ai-mh.github.io/SQPsych
