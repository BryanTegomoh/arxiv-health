Summary

This paper introduces a deep learning-based template-fitting pipeline that generates structured quadrilateral (quad) meshes of aortic valves from 3D CT images. By using a common quad mesh template, the method ensures consistent mesh topology and correspondence across patients, leading to high-quality meshes for biomechanical analysis while simplifying the neural network training process.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and consistent geometric modeling of the aortic valve is crucial for patient-specific biomechanical analysis, enabling precise assessment of valve health, progression of disease, and robust preoperative planning for interventions like transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR) or surgical repair.

AI Health Application

The paper applies deep neural networks (a form of AI) to automate the generation of high-quality, consistent finite element meshes of aortic valves directly from 3D CT images. This AI-driven automation is critical for developing patient-specific biomechanical models, which are used to assess aortic valve health, predict disease progression, and facilitate more accurate preoperative planning for interventions or surgeries related to the aortic valve.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of generating accurate, high-quality, and topologically consistent aortic valve meshes from 3D CT images for patient-specific biomechanical analysis.
  • Proposes a novel template-fitting pipeline that leverages deep neural networks to deform a common structured quadrilateral mesh template to fit individual patient aortic valve anatomies.
  • Ensures uniform mesh topology and consistent node-to-node and element-to-element correspondence across different patients by remeshing all valves with a common quad mesh template.
  • The inherent consistency of the structured quad meshes simplifies the deep neural network's learning objective, requiring only two loss terms: a geometry reconstruction term and a smoothness regularization term.
  • Experimental results demonstrate that the approach produces aortic valve surface meshes with improved smoothness and element shape quality compared to traditional irregular triangular meshing methods.
  • The simplified loss function and structured approach reduce the need for multiple explicit regularization terms, enhancing the effectiveness and efficiency of the neural network training.
  • Facilitates more reliable and consistent patient-specific simulations for assessing valve health and informing preoperative planning.

Methodology

The methodology involves a deep neural network-based template-fitting pipeline. A common structured quadrilateral (quad) mesh template is used as a base. This template is then deformed by the deep neural network to accurately match the specific aortic valve geometry extracted from individual patient's 3D CT images. The network is trained with a loss function consisting of a geometry reconstruction term to ensure fidelity to the CT data and a smoothness regularization term to maintain mesh quality.

Key Findings

The proposed approach successfully generates high-quality aortic valve surface meshes characterized by improved smoothness and superior element shape quality compared to traditional methods. A key finding is that using structured quad meshes simplifies the deep learning objective, allowing effective training with only two loss terms and requiring fewer explicit regularization terms, thereby enhancing efficiency.

This could lead to more personalized diagnostic insights, more reliable predictions of valve performance, and optimized preoperative planning for surgical or interventional procedures, ultimately benefiting patient outcomes by tailoring treatments to individual anatomies.

Abstract

Accurate geometric modeling of the aortic valve from 3D CT images is essential for biomechanical analysis and patient-specific simulations to assess valve health or make a preoperative plan. However, it remains challenging to generate aortic valve meshes with both high-quality and consistency across different patients. Traditional approaches often produce triangular meshes with irregular topologies, which can result in poorly shaped elements and inconsistent correspondence due to inter-patient anatomical variation. In this work, we address these challenges by introducing a template-fitting pipeline with deep neural networks to generate structured quad (i.e., quadrilateral) meshes from 3D CT images to represent aortic valve geometries. By remeshing aortic valves of all patients with a common quad mesh template, we ensure a uniform mesh topology with consistent node-to-node and element-to-element correspondence across patients. This consistency enables us to simplify the learning objective of the deep neural networks, by employing a loss function with only two terms (i.e., a geometry reconstruction term and a smoothness regularization term), which is sufficient to preserve mesh smoothness and element quality. Our experiments demonstrate that the proposed approach produces high-quality aortic valve surface meshes with improved smoothness and shape quality, while requiring fewer explicit regularization terms compared to the traditional methods. These results highlight that using structured quad meshes for the template and neural network training not only ensures mesh correspondence and quality but also simplifies the training process, thus enhancing the effectiveness and efficiency of aortic valve modeling.
