Shape Deformation Networks for Automated Aortic Valve Finite Element Meshing from 3D CT Images

Summary

This paper introduces a deep learning-based template-fitting pipeline for automated generation of high-quality, structured quadrilateral meshes of aortic valves from 3D CT images. The method addresses limitations of traditional approaches by ensuring uniform mesh topology and consistent correspondence across patients, simplifying network training, and improving mesh smoothness and element quality.

Medical Relevance

Accurate geometric modeling of the aortic valve is foundational for patient-specific biomechanical analysis and simulations, which are vital for assessing valve health, predicting disease progression, and guiding preoperative planning in cardiology and cardiac surgery.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using deep neural networks (Shape Deformation Networks) to automate and improve the quality of finite element meshing of the aortic valve from 3D CT images. This enhanced meshing facilitates more accurate patient-specific biomechanical simulations, which are crucial for assessing aortic valve health, diagnosing conditions, and informing preoperative planning for interventions or surgeries. It automates a critical step in creating digital twins for personalized medicine in cardiology.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: Traditional aortic valve meshing from 3D CT images often produces triangular meshes with irregular topologies, leading to poorly shaped elements and inconsistent node/element correspondence across patients, which hinders biomechanical analysis.
  • **Proposed Solution**: A template-fitting pipeline leveraging deep neural networks is introduced to generate structured quadrilateral (quad) meshes for aortic valve geometries.
  • **Mechanism for Consistency**: A common quad mesh template is used to remesh all patient aortic valves, ensuring uniform mesh topology and consistent node-to-node and element-to-element correspondence across diverse anatomies.
  • **Simplified Deep Learning Objective**: The inherent consistency of the structured quad meshes allows for a simplified deep neural network loss function, requiring only two terms: geometry reconstruction and smoothness regularization.
  • **Improved Mesh Quality**: Experiments demonstrate the approach produces high-quality aortic valve surface meshes with improved smoothness and shape quality compared to traditional methods.
  • **Enhanced Efficiency and Effectiveness**: The structured quad template not only ensures mesh quality and correspondence but also simplifies the neural network training process by requiring fewer explicit regularization terms, thereby enhancing modeling efficiency.
  • **Foundation for Biomechanical Analysis**: The generated consistent, high-quality meshes are crucial for accurate patient-specific biomechanical simulations and preoperative planning.

Methodology

The methodology involves a template-fitting pipeline that utilizes deep neural networks. A common structured quadrilateral mesh template is employed to remesh individual patient aortic valve geometries, derived from 3D CT images. The deep neural network is trained with a simplified loss function consisting of a geometry reconstruction term and a smoothness regularization term, designed to preserve mesh smoothness and element quality while fitting the template to patient-specific anatomy.

Key Findings

The proposed approach successfully generates high-quality aortic valve surface meshes from 3D CT images. These meshes demonstrate improved smoothness and shape quality compared to traditional methods. The use of a common structured quad mesh template ensures uniform mesh topology and consistent node/element correspondence across patients, which in turn simplifies the deep learning training process by reducing the need for extensive regularization.

Clinical Impact

This technology can significantly advance patient-specific biomechanical modeling of the aortic valve, enabling more accurate and reliable simulations for evaluating valve health (e.g., in aortic stenosis/regurgitation) and planning surgical interventions. It has the potential to improve personalized preoperative planning for valve repair or replacement, leading to better surgical outcomes and enhanced patient care.

Abstract

Accurate geometric modeling of the aortic valve from 3D CT images is essential for biomechanical analysis and patient-specific simulations to assess valve health or make a preoperative plan. However, it remains challenging to generate aortic valve meshes with both high-quality and consistency across different patients. Traditional approaches often produce triangular meshes with irregular topologies, which can result in poorly shaped elements and inconsistent correspondence due to inter-patient anatomical variation. In this work, we address these challenges by introducing a template-fitting pipeline with deep neural networks to generate structured quad (i.e., quadrilateral) meshes from 3D CT images to represent aortic valve geometries. By remeshing aortic valves of all patients with a common quad mesh template, we ensure a uniform mesh topology with consistent node-to-node and element-to-element correspondence across patients. This consistency enables us to simplify the learning objective of the deep neural networks, by employing a loss function with only two terms (i.e., a geometry reconstruction term and a smoothness regularization term), which is sufficient to preserve mesh smoothness and element quality. Our experiments demonstrate that the proposed approach produces high-quality aortic valve surface meshes with improved smoothness and shape quality, while requiring fewer explicit regularization terms compared to the traditional methods. These results highlight that using structured quad meshes for the template and neural network training not only ensures mesh correspondence and quality but also simplifies the training process, thus enhancing the effectiveness and efficiency of aortic valve modeling.
