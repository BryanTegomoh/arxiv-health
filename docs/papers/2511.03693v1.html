Summary

This paper introduces a privacy-preserving federated learning framework for colorectal cancer (CRC) histopathological grading. It leverages a dual-stream ResNetRS50 architecture for multi-scale feature learning and FedProx for stable distributed training, achieving 83.5% overall accuracy and significantly improving Grade III tumor identification with an 87.5% recall, thus enhancing prognostic accuracy while adhering to data privacy regulations.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and consistent colorectal cancer grading is vital for patient prognosis and treatment planning. This work provides a privacy-preserving AI solution that can reduce diagnostic variability and enable collaborative model training across institutions without compromising sensitive patient data, leading to more reliable diagnoses.

AI Health Application

The AI application is a privacy-preserving federated learning system using a multi-scale deep learning model (ResNetRS50) for the automated and accurate histopathological grading of colorectal cancer. This system is designed to analyze digital pathology images from multiple hospitals without sharing raw patient data, thereby enhancing diagnostic consistency, improving prognostic assessment for CRC patients, and facilitating the secure deployment of AI in clinical digital pathology workflows.

Key Points

  • Addresses critical issues in CRC grading: inter-observer variability and privacy constraints for multi-institutional data sharing in AI model training.
  • Proposes a scalable, privacy-preserving federated learning (FL) framework for CRC histopathological grading.
  • Employs a dual-stream ResNetRS50 backbone to concurrently capture fine-grained nuclear detail and broader tissue-level context (multi-scale analysis).
  • Integrates FedProx into the FL system to mitigate client drift, ensuring robustness across heterogeneous data distributions from multiple hospitals.
  • Achieves an overall accuracy of 83.5% on the CRC-HGD dataset, outperforming a comparable centralized model (81.6%).
  • Demonstrates high recall (87.5%) for aggressive Grade III tumors, a key clinical priority to prevent dangerous false negatives.
  • Performance further improves with higher magnification, reaching 88.0% accuracy at 40x, validating the multi-scale approach and generalization benefits.

Methodology

The authors developed a federated learning (FL) framework using a dual-stream ResNetRS50 backbone to extract multi-scale features, capturing both fine nuclear details and broader tissue context. This deep learning architecture is integrated into a robust FL system stabilized with FedProx to mitigate client drift across heterogeneous data from different institutions. The framework's performance was evaluated using the CRC-HGD dataset.

Key Findings

The FL framework achieved an overall accuracy of 83.5%, outperforming a centralized model (81.6%). Critically, it demonstrated a high recall of 87.5% for aggressive Grade III tumors, addressing a major clinical need. Performance improved to 88.0% accuracy at 40x magnification, validating that the federated multi-scale approach enhances model performance, generalization, and preserves patient privacy.

By mitigating inter-observer variability and facilitating multi-institutional collaboration under strict privacy regulations, it can directly inform treatment decisions, reduce diagnostic errors, and ultimately improve patient outcomes. The modular pipeline, including built-in preprocessing and error handling, contributes to its practical readiness for clinical integration.

Abstract

Colorectal cancer (CRC) grading is a critical prognostic factor but remains hampered by inter-observer variability and the privacy constraints of multi-institutional data sharing. While deep learning offers a path to automation, centralized training models conflict with data governance regulations and neglect the diagnostic importance of multi-scale analysis. In this work, we propose a scalable, privacy-preserving federated learning (FL) framework for CRC histopathological grading that integrates multi-scale feature learning within a distributed training paradigm. Our approach employs a dual-stream ResNetRS50 backbone to concurrently capture fine-grained nuclear detail and broader tissue-level context. This architecture is integrated into a robust FL system stabilized using FedProx to mitigate client drift across heterogeneous data distributions from multiple hospitals. Extensive evaluation on the CRC-HGD dataset demonstrates that our framework achieves an overall accuracy of 83.5%, outperforming a comparable centralized model (81.6%). Crucially, the system excels in identifying the most aggressive Grade III tumors with a high recall of 87.5%, a key clinical priority to prevent dangerous false negatives. Performance further improves with higher magnification, reaching 88.0% accuracy at 40x. These results validate that our federated multi-scale approach not only preserves patient privacy but also enhances model performance and generalization. The proposed modular pipeline, with built-in preprocessing, checkpointing, and error handling, establishes a foundational step toward deployable, privacy-aware clinical AI for digital pathology.
