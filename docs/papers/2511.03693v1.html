Colorectal Cancer Histopathological Grading using Multi-Scale Federated Learning

Summary

This paper introduces a privacy-preserving federated learning framework for colorectal cancer (CRC) histopathological grading, effectively addressing data sharing constraints and inter-observer variability. By integrating a dual-stream multi-scale ResNetRS50 architecture within a FedProx-stabilized federated learning system, the framework achieves superior performance (83.5% overall accuracy) compared to centralized models, particularly excelling in identifying aggressive Grade III tumors with high recall.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medical practice as it offers a solution to automate and standardize colorectal cancer grading, a critical prognostic factor, while overcoming significant challenges of data privacy and inter-observer variability among pathologists. It aims to improve diagnostic accuracy and consistency, ultimately aiding in more effective patient management.

AI Health Application

The AI application is an automated, privacy-preserving system for histopathological grading of colorectal cancer. It uses multi-scale federated learning to analyze digital pathology slides from multiple institutions without centralizing sensitive patient data. This system aims to improve the accuracy and consistency of CRC grading, reduce inter-observer variability among pathologists, and aid in the identification of aggressive tumors (Grade III) to prevent false negatives, thereby supporting more effective treatment decisions and patient prognostication. It represents a foundational step towards deployable clinical AI tools in digital pathology.

Key Points

  • **Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning (FL):** The core contribution is an FL framework enabling multi-institutional collaboration for training AI models without sharing raw patient histopathology data, thereby adhering to data governance regulations.
  • **Multi-Scale Feature Learning:** Employs a dual-stream ResNetRS50 backbone to concurrently capture both fine-grained nuclear details and broader tissue-level context from whole slide images, enhancing diagnostic precision.
  • **Robust Distributed Training:** Integrates FedProx stabilization to mitigate client drift and ensure robust model training across heterogeneous data distributions inherent in multi-hospital settings.
  • **Superior Performance:** Achieves an overall accuracy of 83.5% on the CRC-HGD dataset, outperforming a comparable centralized deep learning model which reached 81.6% accuracy.
  • **High Recall for Aggressive Tumors:** Demonstrates significant clinical value by achieving an 87.5% recall for identifying the most aggressive Grade III tumors, a critical factor in preventing dangerous false negatives.
  • **Magnification-Dependent Accuracy:** Performance shows further improvement with higher magnification, reaching 88.0% accuracy at 40x, highlighting the importance of detailed pathological examination.
  • **Foundational for Clinical Deployment:** The proposed modular pipeline includes built-in preprocessing, checkpointing, and error handling, laying a groundwork for deployable, privacy-aware clinical AI tools in digital pathology.

Methodology

The study proposes a federated learning (FL) framework for CRC histopathological grading, incorporating a dual-stream ResNetRS50 deep learning backbone for multi-scale feature extraction (capturing both fine-grained nuclear detail and broader tissue context). The FL system is stabilized using FedProx to address client drift across heterogeneous data distributions from multiple hospitals. The framework includes built-in preprocessing, checkpointing, and error handling, and was evaluated on the CRC-HGD dataset.

Key Findings

The proposed federated multi-scale approach achieved an overall accuracy of 83.5%, surpassing a comparable centralized model (81.6%). Crucially, it demonstrated a high recall of 87.5% for identifying aggressive Grade III tumors. Performance further improved with higher magnification, reaching 88.0% accuracy at 40x, validating its efficacy in preserving patient privacy while enhancing model performance and generalization.

By enabling secure, multi-institutional AI model development, it facilitates the creation of robust diagnostic tools that respect patient privacy, potentially leading to more reliable and timely cancer diagnoses and personalized treatment strategies.

Abstract

Colorectal cancer (CRC) grading is a critical prognostic factor but remains hampered by inter-observer variability and the privacy constraints of multi-institutional data sharing. While deep learning offers a path to automation, centralized training models conflict with data governance regulations and neglect the diagnostic importance of multi-scale analysis. In this work, we propose a scalable, privacy-preserving federated learning (FL) framework for CRC histopathological grading that integrates multi-scale feature learning within a distributed training paradigm. Our approach employs a dual-stream ResNetRS50 backbone to concurrently capture fine-grained nuclear detail and broader tissue-level context. This architecture is integrated into a robust FL system stabilized using FedProx to mitigate client drift across heterogeneous data distributions from multiple hospitals. Extensive evaluation on the CRC-HGD dataset demonstrates that our framework achieves an overall accuracy of 83.5%, outperforming a comparable centralized model (81.6%). Crucially, the system excels in identifying the most aggressive Grade III tumors with a high recall of 87.5%, a key clinical priority to prevent dangerous false negatives. Performance further improves with higher magnification, reaching 88.0% accuracy at 40x. These results validate that our federated multi-scale approach not only preserves patient privacy but also enhances model performance and generalization. The proposed modular pipeline, with built-in preprocessing, checkpointing, and error handling, establishes a foundational step toward deployable, privacy-aware clinical AI for digital pathology.
