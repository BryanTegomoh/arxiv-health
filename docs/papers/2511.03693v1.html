Summary

This paper introduces a privacy-preserving federated learning (FL) framework for colorectal cancer (CRC) histopathological grading, overcoming inter-observer variability and data sharing constraints. The system integrates a dual-stream ResNetRS50 for multi-scale feature extraction and uses FedProx for stability, achieving 83.5% overall accuracy and crucially, an 87.5% recall for aggressive Grade III tumors, outperforming centralized models while preserving patient privacy.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a crucial step towards automated, standardized, and accurate colorectal cancer grading, which can significantly improve prognostic assessment and treatment planning. By preserving patient privacy while enabling multi-institutional data collaboration, it addresses major roadblocks to deploying AI in digital pathology.

AI Health Application

An AI-assisted diagnostic tool for colorectal cancer histopathological grading, leveraging multi-scale federated learning to enhance accuracy, reduce inter-observer variability, and ensure patient data privacy across multiple healthcare institutions for improved clinical prognostics.

Key Points

  • Addresses critical challenges in CRC grading: inter-observer variability, data privacy for multi-institutional data sharing, and the limitations of centralized deep learning models (data governance, neglect of multi-scale analysis).
  • Proposes a scalable, privacy-preserving federated learning (FL) framework for distributed training of CRC histopathological grading models.
  • Employs a dual-stream ResNetRS50 backbone architecture to concurrently capture fine-grained nuclear details and broader tissue-level contextual information.
  • Integrates FedProx into the FL system to mitigate client drift and ensure stability across heterogeneous data distributions from multiple participating hospitals.
  • Achieves an overall accuracy of 83.5% on the CRC-HGD dataset, significantly outperforming a comparable centralized training model (81.6%).
  • Demonstrates high clinical relevance by achieving a high recall of 87.5% for the most aggressive Grade III tumors, a critical factor for preventing dangerous false negatives.
  • Performance further improves with higher image magnification, reaching 88.0% accuracy at 40x, validating the importance of detailed pathological analysis.

Methodology

The study proposes a federated learning (FL) framework for CRC histopathological grading. It uses a dual-stream ResNetRS50 deep learning architecture as its backbone, designed to extract features at both fine-grained (nuclear detail) and broader (tissue-level context) scales simultaneously. The FL system incorporates FedProx for robust training by mitigating client drift across heterogeneous datasets from different institutions. Evaluation was conducted on the CRC-HGD dataset.

Key Findings

The federated multi-scale learning framework achieved an overall accuracy of 83.5% in CRC grading, surpassing a centralized model's 81.6%. Crucially, it demonstrated superior performance in identifying aggressive Grade III tumors with an 87.5% recall. Model accuracy further improved to 88.0% when evaluated at higher 40x magnification.

Clinical Impact

The framework promises more consistent and objective CRC grading, reducing inter-observer variability among pathologists. Its privacy-preserving nature facilitates the pooling of diverse multi-institutional data, leading to more robust and generalizable AI models without compromising patient confidentiality. The high recall for Grade III tumors is clinically vital for early identification of aggressive cancers, enabling timely intervention and potentially improving patient outcomes. The modular design supports future integration into clinical digital pathology workflows.

Limitations

Future Directions

The proposed modular pipeline, with its built-in preprocessing, checkpointing, and error handling, establishes a foundational step toward deployable, privacy-aware clinical AI for digital pathology. Future work likely involves real-world clinical deployment and further generalization across varied datasets and institutions.

Abstract

Colorectal cancer (CRC) grading is a critical prognostic factor but remains hampered by inter-observer variability and the privacy constraints of multi-institutional data sharing. While deep learning offers a path to automation, centralized training models conflict with data governance regulations and neglect the diagnostic importance of multi-scale analysis. In this work, we propose a scalable, privacy-preserving federated learning (FL) framework for CRC histopathological grading that integrates multi-scale feature learning within a distributed training paradigm. Our approach employs a dual-stream ResNetRS50 backbone to concurrently capture fine-grained nuclear detail and broader tissue-level context. This architecture is integrated into a robust FL system stabilized using FedProx to mitigate client drift across heterogeneous data distributions from multiple hospitals. Extensive evaluation on the CRC-HGD dataset demonstrates that our framework achieves an overall accuracy of 83.5%, outperforming a comparable centralized model (81.6%). Crucially, the system excels in identifying the most aggressive Grade III tumors with a high recall of 87.5%, a key clinical priority to prevent dangerous false negatives. Performance further improves with higher magnification, reaching 88.0% accuracy at 40x. These results validate that our federated multi-scale approach not only preserves patient privacy but also enhances model performance and generalization. The proposed modular pipeline, with built-in preprocessing, checkpointing, and error handling, establishes a foundational step toward deployable, privacy-aware clinical AI for digital pathology.
