Medical Imaging and Computational Image Analysis in COVID-19 Diagnosis: A Review

Summary

This review paper examines automated methods for COVID-19 diagnosis using medical imaging, analyzing the disease's characteristics in images and the accuracy of AI-based approaches. It concludes that medical imaging can reveal early COVID-19 signs, even in asymptomatic patients, and automated machine learning methods offer high accuracy while reducing diagnostic time, cost, and error.

Medical Relevance

This review highlights the critical role of medical imaging and computational analysis for rapid, accurate, and cost-effective early diagnosis of COVID-19, including in asymptomatic cases, which is crucial for controlling disease transmission and managing patient care.

AI Health Application

The paper reviews the application of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms for the automated analysis of medical images (e.g., chest X-rays, CT scans) to diagnose COVID-19. This aims to provide faster, more accurate, and cost-effective diagnostic tools for healthcare professionals, especially for early detection and in asymptomatic patients, thereby improving patient management and public health outcomes.

Key Points

  • The study is a comprehensive review focusing on automated medical image analysis for COVID-19 diagnosis, covering literature up to April 2020 from PubMed, Google Scholar, arXiv, and medRxiv.
  • It serves as a tutorial for both clinicians and technologists, reviewing COVID-19 characteristics as presented in medical images.
  • The review evaluates artificial intelligence-based approaches for COVID-19 diagnosis, specifically examining their accuracy and methodologies.
  • Medical images contain discernible signs of COVID-19, enabling early diagnosis, potentially even in asymptomatic individuals.
  • Automated machine learning-based methods can diagnose COVID-19 from medical images with high accuracy.
  • The adoption of automated diagnostic procedures can significantly reduce the time, cost, and human error associated with diagnosis.
  • A key recommendation for future work is the rapid collection of large volumes of imaging data from patients to enhance the performance and robustness of automated diagnostic models.

Methodology

The study conducted a literature review across PubMed, Google Scholar, arXiv, and medRxiv up to April 2020, focusing on papers discussing automatic methods for medical image analysis and diagnosis of COVID-19. The essential points of collected studies were summarized and categorized.

Key Findings

COVID-19 manifests detectable signs in medical images, facilitating early diagnosis even in asymptomatic patients. Automated machine learning-based methods demonstrate high accuracy in diagnosing the disease from these images, leading to reductions in diagnostic time, cost, and potential human error.

Clinical Impact

The findings support the integration of AI-powered medical image analysis into clinical workflows for COVID-19 diagnosis, potentially enabling earlier intervention, improved patient outcomes, and more efficient resource allocation, especially in settings with high patient volumes or limited specialist availability. It could also aid in large-scale screening efforts for asymptomatic carriers.

Limitations

The abstract mentions that one contribution is to express the research limitations in this field and methods to overcome them, but the abstract itself does not explicitly detail these limitations. The review's scope is also limited to studies published by the end of April 2020.

Future Directions

The primary future direction suggested is the urgent collection of large-scale imaging datasets from COVID-19 patients to significantly improve the performance and generalizability of automated diagnostic methods.

Abstract

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus. The disease presents with symptoms such as shortness of breath, fever, dry cough, and chronic fatigue, amongst others. Sometimes the symptoms of the disease increase so much they lead to the death of the patients. The disease may be asymptomatic in some patients in the early stages, which can lead to increased transmission of the disease to others. Many studies have tried to use medical imaging for early diagnosis of COVID-19. This study attempts to review papers on automatic methods for medical image analysis and diagnosis of COVID-19. For this purpose, PubMed, Google Scholar, arXiv and medRxiv were searched to find related studies by the end of April 2020, and the essential points of the collected studies were summarised. The contribution of this study is four-fold: 1) to use as a tutorial of the field for both clinicians and technologists, 2) to comprehensively review the characteristics of COVID-19 as presented in medical images, 3) to examine automated artificial intelligence-based approaches for COVID-19 diagnosis based on the accuracy and the method used, 4) to express the research limitations in this field and the methods used to overcome them. COVID-19 reveals signs in medical images can be used for early diagnosis of the disease even in asymptomatic patients. Using automated machine learning-based methods can diagnose the disease with high accuracy from medical images and reduce time, cost and error of diagnostic procedure. It is recommended to collect bulk imaging data from patients in the shortest possible time to improve the performance of COVID-19 automated diagnostic methods.
