Medical Imaging and Computational Image Analysis in COVID-19 Diagnosis: A Review

Summary

This review paper synthesizes research on the application of medical imaging and computational image analysis, particularly automated AI-based methods, for COVID-19 diagnosis. It highlights that medical images can reveal early signs of the disease, even in asymptomatic patients, and that machine learning approaches can significantly enhance diagnostic accuracy, efficiency, and cost-effectiveness.

Medical Relevance

This review is highly relevant for medical professionals and public health initiatives, demonstrating how advanced medical imaging and AI can facilitate rapid, accurate, and early diagnosis of COVID-19, including in asymptomatic carriers. This capability is crucial for effective disease containment, personalized patient management, and optimizing healthcare resource allocation during infectious disease outbreaks.

AI Health Application

The paper focuses on reviewing automated machine learning and artificial intelligence-based approaches for the diagnosis of COVID-19 from medical images (e.g., chest X-rays, CT scans). The AI application aims to provide early, accurate, and efficient diagnosis, potentially even in asymptomatic patients, thereby reducing diagnostic time, cost, and error in healthcare settings.

Key Points

  • The study provides a comprehensive overview for both clinicians and technologists on the use of automated methods for medical image analysis in COVID-19 diagnosis.
  • It extensively reviews the characteristic radiological manifestations of COVID-19 as depicted in various medical images.
  • Artificial intelligence-based diagnostic approaches are critically examined based on their reported accuracy and the specific methodologies employed.
  • The review identifies existing research limitations within the field and discusses strategies and methods utilized to address these challenges.
  • Medical imaging is identified as a crucial tool for early COVID-19 detection, capable of revealing disease signs even in asymptomatic individuals, which is vital for curbing transmission.
  • Automated machine learning-based methods demonstrate high diagnostic accuracy, offering significant reductions in the time, cost, and potential error associated with traditional diagnostic procedures.
  • A key recommendation for future work is the urgent collection of large-scale imaging datasets from COVID-19 patients to substantially improve the performance and robustness of automated diagnostic systems.

Methodology

This paper constitutes a review study. The authors conducted searches across PubMed, Google Scholar, arXiv, and medRxiv up to the end of April 2020 to identify relevant studies focusing on automatic methods for medical image analysis and COVID-19 diagnosis. The essential findings and methodologies from these collected studies were then summarized.

Key Findings

The review establishes that medical images effectively reveal specific signs of COVID-19, enabling early diagnosis even in asymptomatic patients. Furthermore, automated machine learning-based methods, when applied to these images, can achieve high diagnostic accuracy, leading to significant reductions in the time, cost, and potential human error inherent in diagnostic processes.

Clinical Impact

The potential clinical impact includes accelerated and more precise diagnosis of COVID-19, particularly for asymptomatic individuals who might otherwise go undetected and contribute to disease spread. This can lead to earlier implementation of isolation measures, more efficient patient triaging, reduced diagnostic workload on healthcare systems, and ultimately, improved public health outcomes and resource management.

Limitations

The abstract indicates that the review addresses existing research limitations within the field and discusses methods used to overcome them, although the specific details of these limitations are not explicitly enumerated within the provided abstract.

Future Directions

The authors strongly recommend the swift collection of bulk imaging data from a large cohort of patients to enhance and improve the overall performance, generalizability, and reliability of automated COVID-19 diagnostic methods.

Abstract

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus. The disease presents with symptoms such as shortness of breath, fever, dry cough, and chronic fatigue, amongst others. Sometimes the symptoms of the disease increase so much they lead to the death of the patients. The disease may be asymptomatic in some patients in the early stages, which can lead to increased transmission of the disease to others. Many studies have tried to use medical imaging for early diagnosis of COVID-19. This study attempts to review papers on automatic methods for medical image analysis and diagnosis of COVID-19. For this purpose, PubMed, Google Scholar, arXiv and medRxiv were searched to find related studies by the end of April 2020, and the essential points of the collected studies were summarised. The contribution of this study is four-fold: 1) to use as a tutorial of the field for both clinicians and technologists, 2) to comprehensively review the characteristics of COVID-19 as presented in medical images, 3) to examine automated artificial intelligence-based approaches for COVID-19 diagnosis based on the accuracy and the method used, 4) to express the research limitations in this field and the methods used to overcome them. COVID-19 reveals signs in medical images can be used for early diagnosis of the disease even in asymptomatic patients. Using automated machine learning-based methods can diagnose the disease with high accuracy from medical images and reduce time, cost and error of diagnostic procedure. It is recommended to collect bulk imaging data from patients in the shortest possible time to improve the performance of COVID-19 automated diagnostic methods.
