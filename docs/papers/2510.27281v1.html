HiF-DTA: Hierarchical Feature Learning Network for Drug-Target Affinity Prediction

arXiv ID: 2510.27281v1
Published: 2025-10-31
Authors: Minghui Li, Yuanhang Wang, Peijin Guo, Wei Wan, Shengshan Hu, Shengqing Hu

Summary

HiF-DTA is a novel hierarchical deep learning network designed to improve Drug-Target Affinity (DTA) prediction by addressing limitations in existing sequence-based methods. It achieves superior performance by simultaneously extracting global sequence semantic and local topological features from drugs and proteins, and by modeling drugs with multi-scale representations (atomic, substructural, molecular) fused via bilinear attention. This approach significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, confirming the importance of its global-local extraction and multi-scale fusion components.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and efficient Drug-Target Affinity (DTA) prediction is critical for accelerating early screening in drug discovery, reducing the enormous costs and time associated with experimental validation. This ultimately speeds up the development and availability of new therapeutic drugs for patients.

AI Health Application

This research develops an AI model (HiF-DTA, a hierarchical deep learning network) to predict the binding affinity between drug molecules and target proteins. This application of AI to health aims to significantly accelerate the early stages of drug discovery, reduce the high costs associated with experimental screening, and improve the efficiency of identifying potential drug candidates for various diseases.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: Existing sequence-based DTA methods overlook simultaneous modeling of global sequence semantics and local topological structural features, and represent drugs as flat sequences without multi-scale (atomic, substructural, molecular) information.
  • **HiF-DTA Architecture**: Proposes a hierarchical network with a dual-pathway strategy to extract both global sequence semantic and local topological features from drug and protein sequences.
  • **Multi-scale Drug Representation**: Uniquely models drugs at multiple scales (atomic-level, substructural-level, molecular-level) to learn comprehensive representations.
  • **Feature Fusion Mechanism**: Incorporates a multi-scale bilinear attention module to effectively fuse the diverse learned multi-scale drug representations.
  • **State-of-the-Art Performance**: HiF-DTA significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-art baseline models on well-established DTA benchmark datasets, including Davis, KIBA, and Metz.
  • **Ablation Study Validation**: Ablation experiments confirm the critical importance and contribution of both the global-local feature extraction strategy and the multi-scale feature fusion components to HiF-DTA's superior predictive power.
  • **Objective**: The overall goal is to reduce experimental costs and accelerate early screening in computational drug discovery, thereby streamlining the drug development process.

Methodology

HiF-DTA is a hierarchical deep learning network that employs a dual-pathway strategy to extract both global sequence semantic and local topological features from drug and protein sequences. For drugs, it models multi-scale features (atomic, substructural, molecular levels). These learned representations are then fused using a multi-scale bilinear attention module to predict Drug-Target Affinity.

Key Findings

HiF-DTA achieves superior performance compared to state-of-the-art baselines on Davis, KIBA, and Metz datasets. Ablation studies confirm that both the global-local feature extraction and the multi-scale feature fusion mechanisms are essential components contributing to its enhanced predictive accuracy for Drug-Target Affinity.

This acceleration can lead to quicker identification of promising drug candidates, faster market entry for new therapies, and ultimately, more accessible and effective treatments for various diseases, benefiting patients worldwide.

Abstract

Accurate prediction of Drug-Target Affinity (DTA) is crucial for reducing experimental costs and accelerating early screening in computational drug discovery. While sequence-based deep learning methods avoid reliance on costly 3D structures, they still overlook simultaneous modeling of global sequence semantic features and local topological structural features within drugs and proteins, and represent drugs as flat sequences without atomic-level, substructural-level, and molecular-level multi-scale features. We propose HiF-DTA, a hierarchical network that adopts a dual-pathway strategy to extract both global sequence semantic and local topological features from drug and protein sequences, and models drugs multi-scale to learn atomic, substructural, and molecular representations fused via a multi-scale bilinear attention module. Experiments on Davis, KIBA, and Metz datasets show HiF-DTA outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, with ablations confirming the importance of global-local extraction and multi-scale fusion.
