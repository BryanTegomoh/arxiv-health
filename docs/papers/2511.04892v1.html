LG-NuSegHop: A Local-to-Global Self-Supervised Pipeline For Nuclei Instance Segmentation

Summary

This paper introduces LG-NuSegHop, a novel local-to-global self-supervised pipeline designed for nuclei instance segmentation in histology images, a critical task for disease diagnosis. It addresses the challenges of high nuclei variability and the prohibitive cost of manual data annotation by operating entirely without manually labeled training data or domain adaptation. The pipeline achieves strong generalization and competitive performance against supervised methods while being fully transparent.

Medical Relevance

Automating accurate nuclei segmentation without requiring expensive manual annotations can significantly accelerate and standardize histological analysis, leading to more efficient and consistent disease and cancer diagnoses. Its explainability also fosters greater trust among physicians, facilitating its integration into clinical workflows.

AI Health Application

LG-NuSegHop is a medical AI application designed for automated nuclei instance segmentation in histology images. Its purpose is to assist in disease and cancer diagnosis by providing a more efficient, generalizable, and explainable method for analyzing microscopic tissue samples. By reducing the reliance on extensive manual annotations and improving generalization across different tissues, it aims to streamline diagnostic workflows and potentially enhance diagnostic accuracy, thereby supporting healthcare professionals.

Key Points

  • Nuclei instance segmentation is fundamental for histology image analysis in disease and cancer diagnosis, but faces challenges from nuclei variability and expensive manual annotations.
  • LG-NuSegHop is a self-supervised, local-to-global pipeline that leverages prior knowledge and molecular biology, eliminating the need for manually annotated training data or domain adaptation.
  • The pipeline consists of three distinct modules: (1) local processing for pseudolabel generation, (2) NuSegHop for data-driven feature extraction, and (3) global operations for post-processing.
  • Despite not using any manual annotations, LG-NuSegHop demonstrates good generalization performance across different datasets and unseen organs.
  • Experimental results on three public datasets show that LG-NuSegHop outperforms other self-supervised and weakly supervised methods, and achieves competitive standing among fully supervised methods.
  • A key feature highlighted is the transparency and explainability of every module within LG-NuSegHop, which is crucial for physician trust and adoption in clinical settings.

Methodology

LG-NuSegHop is a self-supervised pipeline comprising three stages: initial local processing operations to generate pseudolabels from raw histology images, followed by NuSegHop, a novel data-driven model for feature extraction. The final stage involves global operations to post-process the predictions from NuSegHop, all developed without reliance on manually annotated training data.

Key Findings

The LG-NuSegHop pipeline achieved superior performance compared to other self-supervised and weakly supervised methods in nuclei instance segmentation. It also demonstrated competitive results against fully supervised approaches across three public datasets, remarkably without requiring any manual annotations or domain adaptation, maintaining strong generalization capabilities.

Clinical Impact

This method has the potential to significantly lower the barrier to entry for AI in digital pathology by reducing the need for costly and time-consuming manual annotations. This could lead to faster, more consistent, and scalable diagnostic workflows for various diseases, particularly cancer, while its inherent transparency can enhance physician confidence and adoption in critical diagnostic tasks.

Abstract

Nuclei segmentation is the cornerstone task in histology image reading, shedding light on the underlying molecular patterns and leading to disease or cancer diagnosis. Yet, it is a laborious task that requires expertise from trained physicians. The large nuclei variability across different organ tissues and acquisition processes challenges the automation of this task. On the other hand, data annotations are expensive to obtain, and thus, Deep Learning (DL) models are challenged to generalize to unseen organs or different domains. This work proposes Local-to-Global NuSegHop (LG-NuSegHop), a self-supervised pipeline developed on prior knowledge of the problem and molecular biology. There are three distinct modules: (1) a set of local processing operations to generate a pseudolabel, (2) NuSegHop a novel data-driven feature extraction model and (3) a set of global operations to post-process the predictions of NuSegHop. Notably, even though the proposed pipeline uses { no manually annotated training data} or domain adaptation, it maintains a good generalization performance on other datasets. Experiments in three publicly available datasets show that our method outperforms other self-supervised and weakly supervised methods while having a competitive standing among fully supervised methods. Remarkably, every module within LG-NuSegHop is transparent and explainable to physicians.
