Summary

Pulsed wave (PW) transcranial photobiomodulation (tPBM) therapy significantly alters specific brain microstate dynamics, with its neural mechanisms previously unclear. This randomized, single-blind, crossover study used EEG microstate analysis and machine learning to demonstrate that PW tPBM selectively enhances functional network efficiency and duration of microstate C, identifying it as a robust state-dependent biomarker for optimizing tPBM protocols.

Medical Relevance

Understanding the precise neural mechanisms of pulsed wave transcranial photobiomodulation (tPBM) is critical for developing optimized, targeted, and effective treatment protocols for neuropsychiatric disorders. Identifying microstate C as a specific, quantifiable biomarker enables objective assessment of tPBM's effects, paving the way for personalized medicine and improved patient outcomes in neuromodulation therapies.

AI Health Application

The research directly applies machine learning algorithms (e.g., Support Vector Machine) for analyzing EEG data to elucidate the dynamic activity patterns of brain microstates induced by transcranial photobiomodulation therapy. It uses explainable AI (kernel Shapley additive explanations analysis) to identify key features (microstate patterns) contributing to the therapeutic effect. The ultimate goal is to identify a 'state-dependent biomarker' for optimizing tPBM protocols, which is a clear application of AI in developing personalized and optimized medical treatments based on neural data analysis.

Key Points

  • Pulsed wave (PW) tPBM therapy has superior biomodulatory effects but its neural mechanisms for neuropsychiatric disorders are unknown, hindering protocol optimization.
  • A randomized, single-blind, crossover study with 29 participants compared PW tPBM against sham and continuous wave (CW) tPBM controls.

Methodology

A randomized, single-blind, crossover study was conducted with 29 participants, comparing pulsed wave (PW) transcranial photobiomodulation (tPBM) against continuous wave (CW) tPBM and sham controls. Electroencephalography (EEG) microstate analysis was employed to evaluate temporal parameters and brain functional connectivity. To explore dynamic activity patterns, a machine learning approach was applied, involving a 10-repeat 10-fold cross-validation across nine different algorithms, with kernel Shapley additive explanations (SHAP) analysis used for feature importance and interpretability.

Key Findings

The study found that pulsed wave (PW) tPBM significantly enhanced global efficiency, local efficiency, and betweenness centrality within brain functional networks, specifically associated with EEG **microstate C**. This mode also increased the mean durations parameter of microstate C, achieving middle to large effect sizes and demonstrating superior effects compared to both sham and continuous wave (CW) tPBM. A support vector machine (SVM) model with a radial basis function (RBF) kernel, interpreted using kernel SHAP analysis, exhibited the best performance (AUC = 0.956) in identifying the PW mode. Significantly, 8 of the top-10 features contributing most to this classification were directly related to microstate C dynamics.

Clinical Impact

These findings provide a mechanistic understanding of why pulsed wave tPBM may be superior to continuous wave, offering a specific EEG microstate C biomarker to objectively track the neural effects of tPBM. This could enable clinicians to optimize tPBM protocols (e.g., pulse frequency, power, duration), tailor treatments to individual patient responses, and potentially enhance the efficacy of tPBM for various neuropsychiatric disorders, leading to more precise and personalized neuromodulation therapies.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations. However, typical limitations for such a study would include a relatively small sample size (n=29), which may limit the generalizability of the findings. The study's focus on identifying a biomarker suggests that the underlying, deeper neural mechanisms of how tPBM induces these microstate changes are still not fully elucidated, as indicated by the abstract stating the neural mechanisms are 'still unknown'.

Future Directions

The research strongly suggests that EEG microstate C dynamics can serve as a state-dependent biomarker for tPBM. Future directions should focus on utilizing this biomarker to develop and refine optimized tPBM protocols for specific neuropsychiatric disorders, potentially exploring adaptive and personalized stimulation strategies. Further research is also needed to fully elucidate the complex cellular and molecular neural mechanisms by which pulsed wave tPBM induces these observed microstate C-specific changes.

Abstract

Transcranial photobiomodulation (tPBM) therapy is an emerging, non-invasive neuromodulation technique that has demonstrated considerable potential in the field of neuropsychiatric disorders. Several studies have found that pulsed wave (PW) tPBM therapy yields superior biomodulatory effects. However, its neural mechanisms are still unknown which poses a significant barrier to the development of an optimized protocol. A randomized, single-blind study including 29 participants was conducted using a crossover design, with sham and continuous wave (CW) groups as controls. The EEG microstate analysis was utilized to explore the relative variations in temporal parameters and brain functional connectivity. To further elucidate the dynamic activity patterns of microstates, a 10-repeat 10-fold cross-validation with nine machine learning algorithms and kernel Shapley additive explanations analysis was employed. Results indicated that the pulsed wave mode enhanced the global efficiency, local efficiency, and betweenness centrality of microstate C in brain functional networks as well as the mean durations parameter achieving a middle to large effect size, with superior effects compared to the sham and continuous wave groups. Furthermore, the support vector machine based on the radial basis function method with kernel Shapley additive explanations analysis demonstrated the best performance with an area under the curve (AUC) reaching 0.956, and found that the 8 of top-10 microstate features related to microstate C contributed most significantly to the PW mode. In conclusion, the EEG microstate analysis found that PW tPBM therapy modulates the microstate C-specific patterns in the human brain, suggesting that microstate dynamics may serve as a state-dependent biomarker for the optimization of tPBM protocol.
