Summary

This paper details UW-BioNLP's approach to the ChemoTimelines 2025 shared task, focusing on automatically generating patient chemotherapy timelines from raw clinical notes using advanced LLM systems. Their method employs a two-step workflow: LLMs extract chemotherapy events from individual notes, followed by algorithmic normalization and aggregation into patient-level timelines. A fine-tuned Qwen3-14B model achieved the best official score of 0.678, demonstrating the efficacy of combining thinking, fine-tuning, and dictionary-enhanced strategies.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated extraction of chemotherapy timelines from clinical notes is crucial for real-time treatment monitoring, clinical research, personalized patient management, and ultimately improving the quality and safety of cancer care.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves utilizing Large Language Models (LLMs) and advanced NLP techniques (fine-tuning, DPO, chain-of-thought) to automatically process unstructured clinical notes within Electronic Health Records (EHRs). The goal is to accurately extract and structure chemotherapy treatment events into patient-level timelines, which can enhance data accessibility for clinicians, researchers, and potentially improve treatment planning and patient outcomes in oncology.

Key Points

  • Addresses ChemoTimelines 2025 Subtask 2: automated extraction of systemic anticancer treatment timelines from electronic health records (EHRs).
  • Utilizes a two-step workflow: an LLM extracts chemotherapy events from individual clinical notes, and then an algorithm normalizes and aggregates these events into patient-level timelines.
  • Evaluated various LLM strategies including Chain-of-Thought (CoT) thinking, Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT), Direct Preference Optimization (DPO), and dictionary-based lookup to improve event extraction.
  • The fine-tuned Qwen3-14B model achieved the highest official score of 0.678 on the test set leaderboard.
  • The research indicates that sophisticated LLM approaches, particularly when fine-tuned, can yield competitive performances for complex clinical NLP tasks.
  • The findings aim to provide valuable insights for future attempts on chemotherapy timeline extraction and the design of similar clinical information extraction tasks.

Methodology

The methodology follows a two-step process: (1) Large Language Models (LLMs) are used to extract specific chemotherapy events (e.g., drug, dosage, start/end dates) from unstructured individual clinical notes. The LLM's performance was optimized through strategies such as Chain-of-Thought prompting, supervised fine-tuning (SFT), direct preference optimization (DPO), and integrating dictionary-based lookups. (2) An algorithmic component then normalizes the extracted events for consistency (e.g., standardizing drug names, resolving temporal ambiguities) and aggregates them chronologically to construct comprehensive patient-level chemotherapy timelines.

Key Findings

The study demonstrated that fine-tuned LLMs are highly effective for extracting chemotherapy timelines, with the fine-tuned Qwen3-14B model achieving the top official score of 0.678. The evaluation of different LLM strategies confirmed that a combination of sophisticated prompting (CoT), task-specific fine-tuning (SFT, DPO), and knowledge-based enhancements (dictionary lookup) significantly improves the accuracy of event extraction from clinical text.

Clinical Impact

Automating chemotherapy timeline construction can drastically reduce manual data abstraction efforts for clinicians and researchers. This improves data consistency for clinical trials, facilitates real-world evidence generation, supports proactive treatment adjustments, enables personalized medicine by providing clear treatment histories, and enhances patient safety through readily accessible and accurate treatment overviews.

Future Directions

The authors suggest that their results and analyses offer valuable insights for future development efforts in the ChemoTimelines task itself, as well as for the broader design and implementation of similar clinical information extraction and timeline construction tasks using LLMs.

Abstract

The ChemoTimelines shared task benchmarks methods for constructing timelines of systemic anticancer treatment from electronic health records of cancer patients. This paper describes our methods, results, and findings for subtask 2 -- generating patient chemotherapy timelines from raw clinical notes. We evaluated strategies involving chain-of-thought thinking, supervised fine-tuning, direct preference optimization, and dictionary-based lookup to improve timeline extraction. All of our approaches followed a two-step workflow, wherein an LLM first extracted chemotherapy events from individual clinical notes, and then an algorithm normalized and aggregated events into patient-level timelines. Each specific method differed in how the associated LLM was utilized and trained. Multiple approaches yielded competitive performances on the test set leaderboard, with fine-tuned Qwen3-14B achieving the best official score of 0.678. Our results and analyses could provide useful insights for future attempts on this task as well as the design of similar tasks.
