UW-BioNLP at ChemoTimelines 2025: Thinking, Fine-Tuning, and Dictionary-Enhanced LLM Systems for Chemotherapy Timeline Extraction

Summary

The UW-BioNLP team participated in ChemoTimelines 2025, focusing on extracting chemotherapy timelines from raw clinical notes using advanced LLM systems. They developed a two-step workflow combining LLM-based event extraction with algorithmic normalization, evaluating strategies such as fine-tuning, chain-of-thought, and dictionary lookups. Their most successful approach, a fine-tuned Qwen3-14B model, achieved the highest official score of 0.678, providing valuable insights for future medical timeline extraction tasks.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated extraction of chemotherapy timelines from clinical notes is critical for oncology research, real-world evidence generation, treatment efficacy monitoring, and personalized patient management, ultimately enhancing the quality and efficiency of cancer care.

AI Health Application

Automated extraction and structuring of chemotherapy treatment timelines from unstructured clinical text data, which can aid in patient care management, clinical research, treatment adherence monitoring, and pharmacovigilance for cancer patients.

Key Points

  • The research addressed ChemoTimelines 2025 subtask 2, aiming to generate patient chemotherapy timelines from unstructured clinical notes within Electronic Health Records (EHRs).
  • A consistent two-step workflow was employed: first, an LLM extracts chemotherapy events from individual clinical notes, and second, a separate algorithm normalizes and aggregates these events into patient-level timelines.
  • Multiple LLM utilization and training strategies were evaluated, including chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting, supervised fine-tuning (SFT), direct preference optimization (DPO), and dictionary-based lookup enhancements.
  • The highest official performance on the test set leaderboard was achieved by a fine-tuned Qwen3-14B model, scoring 0.678.
  • The study demonstrated that various sophisticated LLM-based methodologies can yield competitive results for complex clinical Natural Language Processing (NLP) tasks.
  • The findings and analyses are intended to provide useful insights for future research and development efforts in similar medical information extraction and timeline construction tasks.

Methodology

The methodology involved a consistent two-step process: an LLM was first used to extract specific chemotherapy events from individual raw clinical notes. Subsequently, a dedicated algorithm performed normalization and aggregation of these extracted events to construct comprehensive patient-level chemotherapy timelines. Different strategies were applied to the LLM component, including chain-of-thought reasoning, supervised fine-tuning, direct preference optimization, and dictionary-based lookups, to optimize event extraction accuracy and completeness.

Key Findings

The study's primary finding was that various LLM-based approaches achieved competitive performances in extracting chemotherapy timelines. Specifically, a fine-tuned Qwen3-14B model demonstrated the best official score of 0.678 on the test set, highlighting the efficacy of supervised fine-tuning for this domain-specific task. The research also affirmed that a multi-strategy approach to LLM training and utilization can yield robust solutions for complex clinical NLP challenges.

Clinical Impact

Automating the precise extraction of chemotherapy timelines from vast amounts of unstructured clinical notes has significant clinical impact. It can drastically reduce the manual burden on healthcare professionals, improve the accuracy and completeness of treatment histories for individual patients, facilitate large-scale retrospective analyses for treatment effectiveness, support clinical trial recruitment, and enable more informed, data-driven decisions in personalized oncology care.

Abstract

The ChemoTimelines shared task benchmarks methods for constructing timelines of systemic anticancer treatment from electronic health records of cancer patients. This paper describes our methods, results, and findings for subtask 2 -- generating patient chemotherapy timelines from raw clinical notes. We evaluated strategies involving chain-of-thought thinking, supervised fine-tuning, direct preference optimization, and dictionary-based lookup to improve timeline extraction. All of our approaches followed a two-step workflow, wherein an LLM first extracted chemotherapy events from individual clinical notes, and then an algorithm normalized and aggregated events into patient-level timelines. Each specific method differed in how the associated LLM was utilized and trained. Multiple approaches yielded competitive performances on the test set leaderboard, with fine-tuned Qwen3-14B achieving the best official score of 0.678. Our results and analyses could provide useful insights for future attempts on this task as well as the design of similar tasks.
