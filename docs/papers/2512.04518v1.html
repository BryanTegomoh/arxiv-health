UW-BioNLP at ChemoTimelines 2025: Thinking, Fine-Tuning, and Dictionary-Enhanced LLM Systems for Chemotherapy Timeline Extraction

Summary

This paper presents UW-BioNLP's participation in the ChemoTimelines 2025 shared task, focusing on generating patient chemotherapy timelines from raw clinical notes using advanced LLM-based systems. Their approach involved a two-step workflow combining LLM event extraction with algorithmic aggregation, evaluating strategies like fine-tuning, chain-of-thought, and dictionary lookups. A fine-tuned Qwen3-14B model achieved the best official score of 0.678, demonstrating effective LLM utilization for this critical clinical NLP task.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated extraction of chemotherapy timelines from clinical notes is vital for cancer research, personalized treatment planning, monitoring treatment efficacy, identifying adverse events, and supporting clinical decision systems in oncology, significantly improving the utility of unstructured EHR data.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using Large Language Models (LLMs) and various NLP techniques (fine-tuning, chain-of-thought, dictionary-based lookup) to automatically extract and construct structured chemotherapy treatment timelines from unstructured clinical notes in electronic health records for cancer patients. This automates the process of summarizing critical treatment information, which is vital for clinical care, research, and potentially personalized medicine.

Key Points

  • The research addresses Subtask 2 of the ChemoTimelines 2025 shared task, focused on extracting systemic anticancer treatment timelines from cancer patients' Electronic Health Records (EHRs).
  • A consistent two-step workflow was employed: first, an LLM extracts chemotherapy events from individual clinical notes, followed by an algorithm normalizing and aggregating these into patient-level timelines.
  • Evaluated LLM strategies included Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting, Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT), Direct Preference Optimization (DPO), and dictionary-based lookup to enhance timeline extraction.
  • Multiple distinct approaches demonstrated competitive performance on the test set leaderboard.
  • The highest official score of 0.678 was achieved by a system utilizing a fine-tuned Qwen3-14B Large Language Model.
  • The findings aim to provide valuable insights for future research on the ChemoTimelines task and the broader design of similar medical information extraction challenges.
  • The core innovation lies in applying and comparing various state-of-the-art LLM training and utilization techniques specifically tailored for complex temporal medical event extraction.

Methodology

The study implemented a two-stage pipeline: (1) An LLM component was responsible for identifying and extracting chemotherapy events from individual raw clinical notes. Various LLM configurations were tested, including those employing Chain-of-Thought reasoning, Supervised Fine-Tuning, Direct Preference Optimization, and dictionary-based lookup mechanisms to guide extraction. (2) Subsequently, an algorithmic component processed these extracted events, performing normalization and aggregation to construct a coherent patient-level chemotherapy timeline.

Key Findings

The research successfully demonstrated that LLM-based systems, particularly when fine-tuned, are highly effective for extracting complex chemotherapy timelines from clinical notes. A fine-tuned Qwen3-14B model achieved the highest official score of 0.678, underscoring the competitive performance and potential of these advanced NLP techniques in a challenging real-world medical task.

Clinical Impact

The ability to automatically and accurately synthesize complex chemotherapy histories can significantly reduce manual chart review time for clinicians and researchers. This can lead to more efficient cohort identification for clinical trials, improved precision in treatment planning, better tracking of patient progress and drug exposure, and enhanced support for clinical decision-making, ultimately improving patient outcomes and accelerating oncology research.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations of the UW-BioNLP team's specific methodologies. However, the inherent challenges of extracting nuanced, temporal medical information from raw, often ambiguous or incomplete clinical notes (e.g., handling negation, implicit timings, varying terminologies) are implicit within such a shared task.

Future Directions

The authors suggest that their results and analyses will provide valuable insights for future attempts on the ChemoTimelines task itself. This implies continued refinement of LLM-based approaches, potentially exploring hybrid models, more robust temporal reasoning, and extending these techniques to the design of similar complex medical timeline extraction tasks across various clinical domains.

Abstract

The ChemoTimelines shared task benchmarks methods for constructing timelines of systemic anticancer treatment from electronic health records of cancer patients. This paper describes our methods, results, and findings for subtask 2 -- generating patient chemotherapy timelines from raw clinical notes. We evaluated strategies involving chain-of-thought thinking, supervised fine-tuning, direct preference optimization, and dictionary-based lookup to improve timeline extraction. All of our approaches followed a two-step workflow, wherein an LLM first extracted chemotherapy events from individual clinical notes, and then an algorithm normalized and aggregated events into patient-level timelines. Each specific method differed in how the associated LLM was utilized and trained. Multiple approaches yielded competitive performances on the test set leaderboard, with fine-tuned Qwen3-14B achieving the best official score of 0.678. Our results and analyses could provide useful insights for future attempts on this task as well as the design of similar tasks.
