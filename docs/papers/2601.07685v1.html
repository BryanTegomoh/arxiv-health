Summary

This study developed and evaluated machine learning models for enhancing dementia prediction using patient health data, employing supervised learning algorithms and advanced data handling techniques. The research achieved a notable 98% testing accuracy with the Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA) model, demonstrating its potential for early identification. It also highlighted the importance of model interpretability and identified the APOE-epsilon4 allele and chronic conditions like diabetes as key risk factors.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and early prediction of dementia, including Alzheimer's disease, is crucial for timely clinical interventions, personalized treatment plans, and improved patient management, potentially delaying disease progression and enhancing quality of life through proactive care.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using various supervised machine learning algorithms (K-Nearest Neighbors, Quadratic Discriminant Analysis, Linear Discriminant Analysis, Gaussian Process Classifiers) on patient healthcare data to predict the onset or presence of dementia. The goal is to enhance diagnostic accuracy and potentially facilitate earlier intervention in dementia care, with an emphasis on model interpretability and explainable AI for clinical adoption.

Methodology

The study employed a supervised machine learning approach using patient health data. Several classification algorithms were applied: K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Quadratic Discriminant Analysis (QDA), Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA), and Gaussian Process Classifiers. To improve model performance and handle data characteristics, Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique (SMOTE) was used to address class imbalance, and Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) vectorization was utilized for feature representation.

Key Findings

Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA) emerged as the best-performing model, achieving a high testing accuracy of 98%. The study also highlighted the importance of model interpretability, identifying specific features such as the presence of the APOE-epsilon4 allele and chronic conditions like diabetes as strongly correlated with dementia.

Clinical Impact

The development of highly accurate predictive models, such as the LDA model with 98% accuracy, offers significant potential for earlier identification of individuals at high risk for dementia. This early insight can facilitate proactive clinical interventions, personalized care strategies, and lifestyle modifications, potentially delaying disease onset or mitigating its severity. The identified risk factors reinforce clinical knowledge and can inform targeted screening and risk assessment protocols.

Abstract

Dementia is a complex syndrome impacting cognitive and emotional functions, with Alzheimer's disease being the most common form. This study focuses on enhancing dementia prediction using machine learning (ML) techniques on patient health data. Supervised learning algorithms are applied in this study, including K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Quadratic Discriminant Analysis (QDA), Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA), and Gaussian Process Classifiers. To address class imbalance and improve model performance, techniques such as Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique (SMOTE) and Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) vectorization were employed. Among the models, LDA achieved the highest testing accuracy of 98%. This study highlights the importance of model interpretability and the correlation of dementia with features such as the presence of the APOE-epsilon4 allele and chronic conditions like diabetes. This research advocates for future ML innovations, particularly in integrating explainable AI approaches, to further improve predictive capabilities in dementia care.
