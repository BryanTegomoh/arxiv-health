Validation of Fully-Automated Deep Learning-Based Fibroglandular Tissue Segmentation for Efficient and Reliable Quantitation of Background Parenchymal Enhancement in Breast MRI

Summary

This paper validates a fully-automated deep learning (DL)-based method for segmenting fibroglandular tissue (FGT) in breast MRI to quantify background parenchymal enhancement (BPE), a potential breast cancer risk marker. The DL method outperformed a semi-automated fuzzy c-means approach by yielding higher quality segmentations and stronger correlations with qualitative BPE assessments, promising more efficient and standardized breast cancer risk evaluation.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and quantitative assessment of background parenchymal enhancement (BPE) on breast dynamic contrast-enhanced MRI is vital as it serves as a promising biomarker for breast cancer risk. This study's validated automated deep learning method provides a more precise, objective, and efficient tool than current qualitative or semi-automated methods, potentially leading to more standardized and personalized breast cancer risk stratification.

Methodology

The study involved evaluating an existing open-source, fully-automated deep learning-based method for fibroglandular tissue (FGT) segmentation in breast MRI. This DL method was compared head-to-head with a semi-automated fuzzy c-means method. The comparison utilized breast MRI data from 100 women, assessing parameters such as segmentation agreement, concordance across quantitative BPE metrics, the strength of association with qualitative BPE assessments, and the overall quality of FGT segmentations as scored by a radiologist.

Key Findings

The deep learning-based FGT segmentation method demonstrated good agreement with the semi-automated fuzzy c-means method for quantitative BPE measurements. Significantly, the DL-based quantitative BPE measures exhibited stronger correlations with qualitative BPE assessments made by radiologists. Furthermore, the FGT segmentations generated by the DL method were consistently scored as higher quality by the radiologist, indicating superior performance in terms of accuracy and clinical relevance.

This improvement over qualitative and semi-automated methods can lead to more precise and reliable breast cancer risk assessment, supporting better patient management, personalized screening recommendations, and potentially reducing inter-reader variability in breast MRI interpretation.

Abstract

Background parenchymal enhancement (BPE) on breast dynamic contrast-enhanced magnetic resonance imaging (DCE-MRI) shows potential as a breast cancer risk marker. Clinically, BPE is qualitatively assessed by radiologists, but quantitative BPE measures offer potential for more precise risk evaluation. This study evaluated an existing open-source, fully-automated deep learning-based (DL-based) method for segmenting fibroglandular tissue (FGT) to quantify BPE and compared it to a semi-automated fuzzy c-means method. Using breast MRI examinations from 100 women, we evaluated segmentation agreement, concordance across quantitative BPE metrics, and associations with qualitative BPE. The quality of FGT segmentations from both methods was scored by a radiologist. While the DL-based and semi-automated methods showed good agreement for quantitative BPE measurements, DL-based measures more strongly correlated with qualitative BPE assessments and DL-based segmentations were scored as higher quality by the radiologist. Our findings suggest that DL-based FGT segmentation enhances efficiency for objective BPE quantification and may improve standardized breast cancer risk assessment.
