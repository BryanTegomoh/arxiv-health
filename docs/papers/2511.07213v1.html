DETECT: Data-Driven Evaluation of Treatments Enabled by Classification Transformers

Summary

This paper introduces DETECT, a data-driven framework leveraging Classification Transformers to objectively evaluate the success of chronic pain treatments. By comparing patient Activities of Daily Life (ADL) before and after treatment, using data from public benchmarks and simulated smartphone sensors, DETECT provides an objective and lightweight alternative to subjective self-reported metrics. This novel approach aims to enhance clinical decision-making and personalize patient care.

Medical Relevance

DETECT offers a crucial objective measure for assessing chronic pain treatment efficacy, complementing or potentially replacing subjective patient self-reports. This can lead to more data-driven treatment adjustments and personalized care plans, improving outcomes for millions of individuals suffering from chronic pain.

AI Health Application

Methodology

DETECT is a data-driven framework built upon Classification Transformers. It operates by analyzing and comparing quantitative data representing patient Activities of Daily Life (ADL) collected both before and after a specific clinical treatment. The framework was evaluated using established public benchmark datasets and additional simulated patient data, designed to mimic inputs from smartphone sensors, to demonstrate its efficacy and practicality in objectively measuring functional changes.

Abstract

Chronic pain is a global health challenge affecting millions of individuals, making it essential for physicians to have reliable and objective methods to measure the functional impact of clinical treatments. Traditionally used methods, like the numeric rating scale, while personalized and easy to use, are subjective due to their self-reported nature. Thus, this paper proposes DETECT (Data-Driven Evaluation of Treatments Enabled by Classification Transformers), a data-driven framework that assesses treatment success by comparing patient activities of daily life before and after treatment. We use DETECT on public benchmark datasets and simulated patient data from smartphone sensors. Our results demonstrate that DETECT is objective yet lightweight, making it a significant and novel contribution to clinical decision-making. By using DETECT, independently or together with other self-reported metrics, physicians can improve their understanding of their treatment impacts, ultimately leading to more personalized and responsive patient care.
