Evaluating the Impact of Vaccine Hesitancy on the Allocation of Vital Resources During COVID-19 Pandemic

Published: 2024-03-12
Authors: Hieu Bui, Sandra Eksioglu, Ruben Proano
Categories: physics.soc-ph, math.OC, q-bio.PE

Summary

This paper introduces an enhanced SEIR compartmental model for COVID-19, which uniquely integrates vaccine hesitancy (VH), vaccination rates, and resource availability to more accurately predict disease dynamics, hospitalizations, and fatalities. Through data analysis and scenario simulations, the study demonstrates that reducing VH, ensuring adequate critical healthcare resources, and implementing timely vaccination programs are pivotal for improving public health outcomes and minimizing fatalities during a pandemic.

Medical Relevance

This research provides a more comprehensive epidemiological modeling framework for public health officials to forecast disease burden by integrating behavioral factors like vaccine hesitancy with resource limitations. It offers actionable insights for pandemic preparedness, guiding resource allocation and targeted public health messaging to mitigate the impact of future outbreaks.

  • Proposed an improved SEIR compartmental model for COVID-19, explicitly incorporating the influence of vaccination, vaccine hesitancy (VH), and resource availability on disease dynamics.
  • Addressed a gap in existing epidemiological models by accounting for VH's direct impact on expected hospitalizations and fatalities.
  • Utilized publicly available data to analyze dynamic changes in VH over time and subsequently developed distinct VH scenario paths for simulations.
  • Simulated the proposed model across various VH paths to evaluate the impacts of public attitudes toward vaccination, healthcare resource levels, and the timing of vaccination rollout.

Methodology

The authors developed an enhanced Susceptible-Exposed-Infectious-Recovered (SEIR) compartmental model specifically for COVID-19. This model was improved by integrating parameters representing vaccination rates, the influence of vaccine hesitancy (VH), and the availability of critical healthcare resources. They collected and analyzed publicly available data to understand and characterize the dynamic changes in VH over time, which informed the creation of several VH scenario paths. The proposed compartmental model was then simulated across these different VH paths to analyze the effects of public attitudes, resource levels, and vaccination timing on the epidemic's progression and severity.

By quantifying the impact of behavioral factors and resource limitations, it can optimize vaccine rollout strategies and overall pandemic response to save lives and mitigate healthcare system strain.

Abstract

The COVID-19 pandemic highlighted significant challenges in the allocation of vital healthcare resources. Existing epidemiological models, specifically compartmental models, aimed to predict the spread of the COVID-19 virus and its impact on the population, but they overlooked the influence of \ac{VH} on disease dynamics, including the expected number of hospitalizations and fatalities. We propose improvements to the \ac{SEIR} model for COVID-19 by incorporating the influence of vaccination, \ac{VH}, and resource availability on the disease dynamics. We collect publicly available data and perform data analysis to capture \ac{VH} dynamic changes over time and develop scenario paths for \ac{VH}. We simulate the proposed compartmental model for each \ac{VH} path to explain the impacts of public attitudes toward vaccination, the impacts of healthcare resources on patient outcomes, and the timing of vaccination rollout on the progression and severity of the epidemic. Our analysis demonstrates that reducing \ac{VH} improves health outcomes, reinforcing the importance of addressing \ac{VH} to curb the spread of infectious diseases. Our results show that adequate levels of critical healthcare resources are crucial for minimizing fatalities and also highlight the life-saving impact of timely and effective vaccination programs.
