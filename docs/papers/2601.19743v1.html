Interpretable and backpropagation-free Green Learning for efficient multi-task echocardiographic segmentation and classification

arXiv ID: 2601.19743v1
Published: 2026-01-27
Authors: Jyun-Ping Kao, Jiaxing Yang, C. -C. Jay Kuo, Jonghye Woo

Summary

This paper introduces a novel backpropagation-free multi-task Green Learning (MTGL) framework designed for simultaneous Left Ventricle (LV) segmentation and Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF) classification from echocardiograms. The MTGL model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the EchoNet-Dynamic dataset with superior computational efficiency and interpretability, addressing key limitations of existing Deep Learning approaches in medical image analysis.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and objective assessment of Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF) is crucial for managing heart failure. This research offers an efficient, reliable, and interpretable AI tool that can significantly reduce subjective variability in LVEF measurements, thereby improving diagnostic consistency and guiding therapy more effectively in cardiology.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of an interpretable and efficient multi-task Green Learning (GL) framework for automated and accurate echocardiographic segmentation of the Left Ventricle (LV) and classification of Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF). This aims to assist clinicians in diagnosing and managing heart failure, reducing manual assessment variability, and providing trustworthy AI tools for cardiac image analysis in clinical practice.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed:** High inter-observer variability in manual LVEF assessment and limitations of existing Deep Learning (DL) models (computational intensity, data-hunger, lack of interpretability) in heart failure management.
  • **Proposed Solution:** A backpropagation-free Multi-task Green Learning (MTGL) framework for concurrent LV segmentation and LVEF classification.
  • **Methodology:** The MTGL framework integrates an unsupervised VoxelHop encoder for hierarchical spatio-temporal feature extraction, a multi-level regression decoder for segmentation, and an XG-Boost classifier for LVEF prediction.
  • **State-of-the-Art Performance:** Achieves 94.3% classification accuracy and a Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC) of 0.912 on the EchoNet-Dynamic dataset, outperforming several advanced 3D DL models.
  • **Exceptional Efficiency:** Demonstrates superior computational efficiency with over an order of magnitude fewer parameters compared to advanced 3D DL models.
  • **Interpretability:** The Green Learning (GL) paradigm aims to provide interpretable solutions, addressing the 'black box' nature of many DL models and fostering clinical trust and adoption.
  • **Clinical Trust:** The combination of accuracy, efficiency, and interpretability paves the way for more sustainable and trustworthy artificial intelligence in clinical practice, particularly for critical cardiac assessments.

Methodology

The study proposes a Multi-task Green Learning (MTGL) framework that operates without backpropagation. It comprises an unsupervised VoxelHop encoder responsible for hierarchical spatio-temporal feature extraction from echocardiographic data. These features are then fed into a multi-level regression decoder for Left Ventricle (LV) segmentation and an XG-Boost classifier for LVEF classification, enabling simultaneous multi-task execution.

Key Findings

The MTGL model achieved state-of-the-art performance on the EchoNet-Dynamic dataset, with a 94.3% classification accuracy for LVEF and a 0.912 Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC) for LV segmentation. Crucially, it demonstrated exceptional computational efficiency, utilizing over an order of magnitude fewer parameters than advanced 3D Deep Learning models while significantly outperforming them, and provides an interpretable framework.

Clinical Impact

This model offers a highly accurate, objective, and efficient automated solution for critical echocardiographic analysis, potentially reducing inter-observer variability in LVEF assessment and improving the diagnosis and management of heart failure. Its inherent interpretability can build greater trust among clinicians, accelerating the integration and adoption of AI tools into routine clinical workflow for more sustainable and reliable cardiac care.

Limitations

Not explicitly mentioned in the abstract, but common considerations for such models include generalizability to diverse patient populations and different ultrasound machine vendors, and performance robustness in the presence of various image artifacts.

Future Directions

Not explicitly mentioned in the abstract, but the work suggests broader applicability of the Green Learning paradigm to other complex medical image analysis tasks, paving the way for more interpretable and resource-efficient AI solutions across various clinical domains.

Abstract

Echocardiography is a cornerstone for managing heart failure (HF), with Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF) being a critical metric for guiding therapy. However, manual LVEF assessment suffers from high inter-observer variability, while existing Deep Learning (DL) models are often computationally intensive and data-hungry "black boxes" that impede clinical trust and adoption. Here, we propose a backpropagation-free multi-task Green Learning (MTGL) framework that performs simultaneous Left Ventricle (LV) segmentation and LVEF classification. Our framework integrates an unsupervised VoxelHop encoder for hierarchical spatio-temporal feature extraction with a multi-level regression decoder and an XG-Boost classifier. On the EchoNet-Dynamic dataset, our MTGL model achieves state-of-the-art classification and segmentation performance, attaining a classification accuracy of 94.3% and a Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC) of 0.912, significantly outperforming several advanced 3D DL models. Crucially, our model achieves this with over an order of magnitude fewer parameters, demonstrating exceptional computational efficiency. This work demonstrates that the GL paradigm can deliver highly accurate, efficient, and interpretable solutions for complex medical image analysis, paving the way for more sustainable and trustworthy artificial intelligence in clinical practice.
