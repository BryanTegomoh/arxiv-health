Standardization of Psychiatric Diagnoses -- Role of Fine-tuned LLM Consortium and OpenAI-gpt-oss Reasoning LLM Enabled Decision Support System

Summary

This paper proposes a novel AI-powered decision support system designed to standardize psychiatric diagnoses by mitigating the inherent subjectivity and variability in current clinical practices. It integrates a consortium of fine-tuned Large Language Models (LLMs), specialized in psychiatrist-patient conversational data, with an OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM, orchestrated by LLM agents, to produce robust and highly accurate mental health assessments.

Medical Relevance

This research is crucial for medicine as it targets the fundamental issue of diagnostic variability in mental health, aiming to bring much-needed standardization and objectivity. By providing a reliable AI-powered decision support tool, it can improve diagnostic consistency, accuracy, and ultimately, patient care and treatment efficacy across various psychiatric conditions.

AI Health Application

The paper describes an AI application that uses a consortium of fine-tuned Large Language Models (LLMs) combined with a reasoning LLM to create a decision support system for standardizing and improving the accuracy of psychiatric diagnoses. This system processes conversational data between psychiatrists and patients to assist in the clinical assessment and diagnosis of mental health conditions.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical problem of subjective and variable psychiatric diagnoses, which primarily rely on psychiatrist-patient dialogues, leading to inconsistent clinical outcomes.
  • Proposes a hybrid diagnostic system combining a Fine-Tuned LLM Consortium with an OpenAI-gpt-oss Reasoning LLM-enabled Decision Support System.
  • Leverages individual LLMs fine-tuned on specific conversational datasets capturing psychiatrist-patient interactions related to mental health conditions (e.g., depression).
  • Implements a consensus-based decision-making process to aggregate diagnostic predictions from the LLM consortium, which is then refined by the OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM.
  • Introduces a novel method for deploying LLM agents to orchestrate communication between the LLLLM consortium and the reasoning LLM, ensuring transparency, reliability, and responsible AI practices.
  • Experimental results demonstrate the transformative potential, indicating the system's capability to create a robust and highly accurate diagnostic framework for mental health assessment.
  • A prototype integrating three fine-tuned LLMs with the OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM has been developed in collaboration with the U.S. Army Medical Research Team, marking a first-of-its-kind application.

Methodology

The proposed system integrates multiple fine-tuned Large Language Models (LLMs), individually trained on conversational datasets of psychiatrist-patient interactions focused on specific mental health conditions. Diagnostic predictions from these individual LLMs are aggregated through a consensus-based mechanism. This aggregated output is then refined and validated by an OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM. A novel component is the deployment of LLM agents, which are responsible for orchestrating the communication workflow between the LLM consortium and the reasoning LLM, ensuring a structured, transparent, and reliable diagnostic process. A prototype system was built integrating three fine-tuned LLMs with the reasoning LLM.

Key Findings

Experimental results demonstrate the 'transformative potential' of combining fine-tuned LLMs with a reasoning model. The system successfully creates a 'robust and highly accurate diagnostic system' for mental health assessment, indicating its efficacy in standardizing diagnoses and improving diagnostic reliability.

Clinical Impact

The system holds significant potential to revolutionize clinical psychiatric practice by standardizing diagnoses, thereby reducing inter-clinician and intra-patient variability. This will lead to more consistent and reliable mental health assessments, enabling earlier and more appropriate interventions. It paves the way for advanced AI-powered eHealth systems that can provide critical decision support, improve diagnostic accuracy, and potentially enhance access to quality mental healthcare.

Limitations

Not explicitly mentioned in the abstract, but the description of a 'prototype' suggests the system is in an early stage of development and validation. Further large-scale clinical trials, external validation across diverse patient populations, assessment of generalizability, and evaluation of potential biases in real-world clinical settings would be necessary.

Future Directions

This work is highlighted as 'paving the way for next-generation AI-powered eHealth systems aimed at standardizing psychiatric diagnoses.' This implies future efforts will focus on scaling the system, integrating it into broader healthcare IT infrastructures, rigorous clinical validation, and continuous refinement to achieve widespread adoption and impact in mental healthcare.

Abstract

The diagnosis of most mental disorders, including psychiatric evaluations, primarily depends on dialogues between psychiatrists and patients. This subjective process can lead to variability in diagnoses across clinicians and patients, resulting in inconsistencies and challenges in achieving reliable outcomes. To address these issues and standardize psychiatric diagnoses, we propose a Fine-Tuned Large Language Model (LLM) Consortium and OpenAI-gpt-oss Reasoning LLM-enabled Decision Support System for the clinical diagnosis of mental disorders. Our approach leverages fine-tuned LLMs trained on conversational datasets involving psychiatrist-patient interactions focused on mental health conditions (e.g., depression). The diagnostic predictions from individual models are aggregated through a consensus-based decision-making process, refined by the OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM. We propose a novel method for deploying LLM agents that orchestrate communication between the LLM consortium and the reasoning LLM, ensuring transparency, reliability, and responsible AI across the entire diagnostic workflow. Experimental results demonstrate the transformative potential of combining fine-tuned LLMs with a reasoning model to create a robust and highly accurate diagnostic system for mental health assessment. A prototype of the proposed platform, integrating three fine-tuned LLMs with the OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM, was developed in collaboration with the U.S. Army Medical Research Team in Norfolk, Virginia, USA. To the best of our knowledge, this work represents the first application of a fine-tuned LLM consortium integrated with a reasoning LLM for clinical mental health diagnosis paving the way for next-generation AI-powered eHealth systems aimed at standardizing psychiatric diagnoses.
