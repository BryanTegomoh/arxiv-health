Standardization of Psychiatric Diagnoses -- Role of Fine-tuned LLM Consortium and OpenAI-gpt-oss Reasoning LLM Enabled Decision Support System

Summary

This paper introduces a novel AI-powered decision support system designed to standardize psychiatric diagnoses, which traditionally suffer from subjectivity and variability. The system integrates a consortium of fine-tuned Large Language Models (LLMs) trained on psychiatrist-patient dialogues with an OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM, orchestrated by specialized LLM agents. Experimental results indicate its transformative potential in creating a robust and highly accurate diagnostic platform for mental health.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine as it aims to reduce diagnostic inconsistencies in mental health, a critical challenge leading to variable patient outcomes. Standardizing psychiatric diagnoses can significantly improve the reliability of assessments, ensuring more consistent and effective treatment plans.

AI Health Application

The AI application involves using fine-tuned LLMs trained on psychiatrist-patient conversational data, aggregated through a consensus-based process and refined by a reasoning LLM, to create a decision support system for standardizing and improving the accuracy of clinical diagnoses for mental disorders like depression. This forms an AI-powered eHealth system for mental health assessment.

Key Points

  • Addresses the inherent subjectivity and variability in psychiatric diagnoses through an AI-enabled decision support system.
  • Employs a consortium of fine-tuned LLMs, specifically trained on conversational datasets of psychiatrist-patient interactions related to mental health conditions (e.g., depression).
  • Utilizes a consensus-based decision-making process to aggregate diagnostic predictions from the individual fine-tuned LLMs.
  • Incorporates an OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM to further refine the aggregated consensus, enhancing diagnostic accuracy and reliability.
  • Introduces novel LLM agents responsible for orchestrating communication between the LLM consortium and the reasoning LLM, ensuring transparency, reliability, and responsible AI.
  • Experimental results demonstrate the system's 'transformative potential' and its capability to form a 'robust and highly accurate diagnostic system' for mental health assessment.
  • A prototype integrating three fine-tuned LLMs with the OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM was developed in collaboration with the U.S. Army Medical Research Team.

Methodology

The methodology involves fine-tuning multiple LLMs on extensive conversational datasets capturing psychiatrist-patient interactions for mental health conditions. Predictions from these individual LLMs are then aggregated via a consensus-based mechanism. This consensus is subsequently refined by an OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM. Communication and workflow transparency are managed by novel LLM agents. A prototype incorporating three fine-tuned LLMs with the reasoning LLM was implemented.

Key Findings

The key finding is the demonstration of the 'transformative potential' of combining a fine-tuned LLM consortium with a reasoning model. This integration resulted in a 'robust and highly accurate diagnostic system for mental health assessment,' representing the first such application for clinical mental health diagnosis.

This system has the potential for significant clinical impact by standardizing psychiatric diagnoses, thereby reducing variability across clinicians and improving the consistency and reliability of mental health assessments. This can lead to more accurate early detection, precise treatment planning, and enhanced patient outcomes, laying the groundwork for advanced AI-powered eHealth solutions.

Abstract

The diagnosis of most mental disorders, including psychiatric evaluations, primarily depends on dialogues between psychiatrists and patients. This subjective process can lead to variability in diagnoses across clinicians and patients, resulting in inconsistencies and challenges in achieving reliable outcomes. To address these issues and standardize psychiatric diagnoses, we propose a Fine-Tuned Large Language Model (LLM) Consortium and OpenAI-gpt-oss Reasoning LLM-enabled Decision Support System for the clinical diagnosis of mental disorders. Our approach leverages fine-tuned LLMs trained on conversational datasets involving psychiatrist-patient interactions focused on mental health conditions (e.g., depression). The diagnostic predictions from individual models are aggregated through a consensus-based decision-making process, refined by the OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM. We propose a novel method for deploying LLM agents that orchestrate communication between the LLM consortium and the reasoning LLM, ensuring transparency, reliability, and responsible AI across the entire diagnostic workflow. Experimental results demonstrate the transformative potential of combining fine-tuned LLMs with a reasoning model to create a robust and highly accurate diagnostic system for mental health assessment. A prototype of the proposed platform, integrating three fine-tuned LLMs with the OpenAI-gpt-oss reasoning LLM, was developed in collaboration with the U.S. Army Medical Research Team in Norfolk, Virginia, USA. To the best of our knowledge, this work represents the first application of a fine-tuned LLM consortium integrated with a reasoning LLM for clinical mental health diagnosis paving the way for next-generation AI-powered eHealth systems aimed at standardizing psychiatric diagnoses.
