Are LLMs Truly Multilingual? Exploring Zero-Shot Multilingual Capability of LLMs for Information Retrieval: An Italian Healthcare Use Case

arXiv ID: 2512.04834v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Vignesh Kumar Kembu, Pierandrea Morandini, Marta Bianca Maria Ranzini, Antonino Nocera
Categories: cs.AI, cs.CL, cs.IR
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper investigates the zero-shot multilingual capability of open-source Large Language Models (LLMs) for information extraction from Italian Electronic Health Records (EHRs), specifically focusing on comorbidity extraction. The study reveals that while LLMs show promise, their performance in zero-shot, on-premises settings varies significantly, often struggling to generalize across different diseases and underperforming compared to traditional pattern matching and manual annotations.

Medical Relevance

This research directly addresses the challenge of automating information extraction from complex, multilingual clinical data, which is fundamental for improving diagnostics, personalizing patient care, and enhancing digital healthcare systems. Understanding the current limitations of LLMs in such contexts is crucial for their responsible and effective deployment in medical applications.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the use of multilingual Large Language Models (LLMs) for zero-shot information retrieval and extraction from free-text clinical notes within Electronic Health Records (EHRs), specifically for tasks like comorbidity extraction. This aims to overcome challenges posed by complex clinical language to provide insights that can aid in diagnostics, patient care, and research.

Key Points

  • Explores the zero-shot multilingual capability of open-source LLMs for information retrieval from Italian Electronic Health Records (EHRs).
  • Focuses on the critical and complex task of comorbidity extraction from free-text clinical records.
  • Evaluates LLM performance in an on-premises setting, crucial for adhering to data privacy and security standards in healthcare.
  • Findings indicate that some LLMs struggle significantly in zero-shot scenarios, while others exhibit considerable performance variability.
  • LLMs demonstrated difficulty in generalizing effectively across various diseases, limiting their reliability for broad clinical application.
  • The performance of LLMs in this specific task was found to be inferior to traditional native pattern matching techniques and human expert annotations.
  • Highlights the current limitations of open-source multilingual LLMs for real-time, zero-shot information extraction in complex medical languages like Italian.

Methodology

The study conducted a detailed experimental campaign to assess open-source multilingual LLMs' ability to perform zero-shot comorbidity extraction from Italian Electronic Health Records (EHRs) in an on-premises environment. The LLM performance was systematically compared against established native pattern matching techniques and expert manual annotations.

Key Findings

Open-source multilingual LLMs demonstrated significant limitations for zero-shot comorbidity extraction from Italian EHRs. Specific findings include that some models struggled outright, others showed high variability in performance, and a general difficulty in generalizing accurately across diverse diseases was observed. Overall, their performance was consistently inferior to both established pattern matching methods and human expert annotations.

Clinical Impact

The findings suggest that current zero-shot, multilingual LLM capabilities are not yet robust enough for reliable, real-time comorbidity extraction from Italian EHRs in clinical settings. This implies that direct deployment without further fine-tuning or specialized adaptation could introduce inaccuracies, potentially affecting diagnostic support, treatment planning, patient management, and the overall efficiency and safety of digital healthcare workflows.

Limitations

The study explicitly notes that the LLMs struggled in 'zero-shot, on-premises settings' and exhibited difficulty 'to generalize across various diseases.' This points to a significant limitation in their current ability for broad, unsupervised application in complex multilingual medical contexts.

Future Directions

Not explicitly mentioned in the abstract, but the findings strongly imply a need for further research into fine-tuning, specialized training, or more robust architectural designs for LLMs to enhance their generalization capabilities and performance for complex, multilingual medical information extraction tasks, particularly under zero-shot or few-shot learning paradigms.

Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) have become a key topic in AI and NLP, transforming sectors like healthcare, finance, education, and marketing by improving customer service, automating tasks, providing insights, improving diagnostics, and personalizing learning experiences. Information extraction from clinical records is a crucial task in digital healthcare. Although traditional NLP techniques have been used for this in the past, they often fall short due to the complexity, variability of clinical language, and high inner semantics in the free clinical text. Recently, Large Language Models (LLMs) have become a powerful tool for better understanding and generating human-like text, making them highly effective in this area. In this paper, we explore the ability of open-source multilingual LLMs to understand EHRs (Electronic Health Records) in Italian and help extract information from them in real-time. Our detailed experimental campaign on comorbidity extraction from EHR reveals that some LLMs struggle in zero-shot, on-premises settings, and others show significant variation in performance, struggling to generalize across various diseases when compared to native pattern matching and manual annotations.
