Are LLMs Truly Multilingual? Exploring Zero-Shot Multilingual Capability of LLMs for Information Retrieval: An Italian Healthcare Use Case

arXiv ID: 2512.04834v1
Published: 2025-12-04
Authors: Vignesh Kumar Kembu, Pierandrea Morandini, Marta Bianca Maria Ranzini, Antonino Nocera
Categories: cs.AI, cs.CL, cs.IR
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper investigates the zero-shot, multilingual capability of open-source Large Language Models (LLMs) for extracting information, specifically comorbidities, from Italian Electronic Health Records (EHRs) in an on-premises setting. The study found that while LLMs show potential, many struggled or exhibited significant performance variability and generalization issues, generally underperforming traditional pattern matching and manual annotations for this critical task.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and real-time information extraction from EHRs is fundamental for improving diagnostics, personalizing treatment, automating clinical tasks, and deriving insights in digital healthcare, especially for non-English languages like Italian where specific NLP tools might be limited.

AI Health Application

The AI application is using Large Language Models (LLMs) to perform zero-shot multilingual information retrieval from Electronic Health Records (EHRs) in Italian, specifically for extracting comorbidities. This aims to improve automated understanding and analysis of complex clinical text.

Key Points

  • Explores the zero-shot multilingual capability of open-source LLMs for information extraction from Italian Electronic Health Records (EHRs).
  • Focuses on the challenging and clinically crucial task of comorbidity extraction from free-text clinical notes.
  • The evaluation was conducted in an on-premises setting, simulating practical deployment constraints for healthcare systems.
  • Revealed that some LLMs performed poorly in this zero-shot, non-English (Italian) context, while others showed significant variation in performance.
  • LLMs demonstrated difficulty in generalizing effectively across various diseases when attempting comorbidity extraction.
  • The performance of the tested LLMs was found to be inferior when compared to established native pattern matching techniques and human manual annotations.
  • Highlights the complexity and high inner semantics of free clinical text as a challenge for LLMs even with advanced NLP capabilities.

Methodology

The study involved a detailed experimental campaign evaluating the performance of open-source multilingual LLMs for zero-shot comorbidity extraction from Italian Electronic Health Records. The LLMs were tested in an on-premises environment, and their performance was benchmarked against native pattern matching techniques and manual annotations.

Key Findings

Open-source multilingual LLMs exhibited significant struggles in zero-shot, on-premises settings for extracting comorbidities from Italian EHRs. Many showed substantial performance variation and failed to generalize effectively across different diseases, consistently performing worse than both native pattern matching algorithms and expert manual annotations.

Clinical Impact

The findings suggest that while LLMs hold promise, their current zero-shot application for critical clinical tasks like comorbidity extraction from non-English EHRs may not be sufficiently reliable for direct clinical implementation. Uncritical deployment could lead to inaccurate patient data, compromised diagnostic support, or inefficient clinical workflows, necessitating further research into fine-tuning or more robust multilingual models.

Limitations

The evaluation was limited to a zero-shot, on-premises setting and focused exclusively on comorbidity extraction from Italian EHRs. It did not explore few-shot learning or fine-tuning approaches, nor did it assess performance on a wider range of clinical information extraction tasks or across different languages.

Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) have become a key topic in AI and NLP, transforming sectors like healthcare, finance, education, and marketing by improving customer service, automating tasks, providing insights, improving diagnostics, and personalizing learning experiences. Information extraction from clinical records is a crucial task in digital healthcare. Although traditional NLP techniques have been used for this in the past, they often fall short due to the complexity, variability of clinical language, and high inner semantics in the free clinical text. Recently, Large Language Models (LLMs) have become a powerful tool for better understanding and generating human-like text, making them highly effective in this area. In this paper, we explore the ability of open-source multilingual LLMs to understand EHRs (Electronic Health Records) in Italian and help extract information from them in real-time. Our detailed experimental campaign on comorbidity extraction from EHR reveals that some LLMs struggle in zero-shot, on-premises settings, and others show significant variation in performance, struggling to generalize across various diseases when compared to native pattern matching and manual annotations.
