Summary

PLUTO-4 introduces next-generation pathology foundation models, including compact PLUTO-4S and frontier-scale PLUTO-4G Vision Transformers, designed for diverse histopathology tasks. These models are self-supervisedly pretrained on an extensive multi-institutional whole slide image corpus, achieving state-of-the-art performance across various diagnostic and analytical benchmarks. Notably, PLUTO-4 demonstrates an 11% improvement in dermatopathology diagnosis, positioning it as a transformative tool for translational research and clinical diagnostics.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly advances AI in digital pathology by offering highly accurate and versatile foundation models capable of analyzing complex histopathological images across numerous disease types. It promises to enhance diagnostic precision, speed, and consistency for pathologists, ultimately improving patient outcomes.

AI Health Application

The AI application is to enhance and automate various tasks in diagnostic pathology, including patch-level classification, segmentation, and slide-level disease diagnosis. By leveraging foundation models on vast datasets of medical images, PLUTO-4 aims to provide a robust backbone for more accurate, efficient, and consistent diagnoses, thereby assisting pathologists and improving patient care outcomes across numerous disease types.

Key Points

  • PLUTO-4 extends the Pathology-Universal Transformer (PLUTO) to 'frontier scale' with two distinct Vision Transformer (ViT) architectures.
  • PLUTO-4S is a compact and efficient model optimized for multi-scale deployment using a FlexiViT setup with 2D-RoPE embeddings, ideal for high-throughput practical applications.
  • PLUTO-4G is a frontier-scale model trained with a single patch size to maximize representation capacity and stability, pushing performance boundaries.
  • Both models are pretrained using a self-supervised objective derived from DINOv2 on a massive multi-institutional corpus comprising 551,164 WSIs from 137,144 patients, spanning over 60 disease types and 100 stains.
  • Comprehensive evaluation across public and internal benchmarks confirms that PLUTO-4 achieves state-of-the-art performance on tasks such as patch-level classification, segmentation, and slide-level diagnosis.
  • A significant clinical finding is an 11% improvement in dermatopathology diagnosis, highlighting the model's impact on specific, complex medical domains.
  • PLUTO-4 is presented as a robust backbone for translational research and diagnostic use cases, indicating its potential to transform real-world applications in pathology.

Methodology

The authors developed two Vision Transformer (ViT) architectures, PLUTO-4S (compact, optimized for multi-scale deployment via FlexiViT and 2D-RoPE) and PLUTO-4G (frontier-scale, single patch size). These models were pretrained using a self-supervised objective adapted from DINOv2 on a large, multi-institutional corpus of over 550,000 whole slide images (WSIs). Their performance was then comprehensively evaluated across various public and internal benchmarks, covering patch-level classification, segmentation, and slide-level diagnosis tasks.

Key Findings

PLUTO-4 models achieved state-of-the-art performance across a wide range of histopathology tasks. PLUTO-4S demonstrated high-throughput and robust performance suitable for practical deployment, while PLUTO-4G established new performance frontiers, including a notable 11% improvement in dermatopathology diagnosis.

Clinical Impact

PLUTO-4 has the potential to revolutionize clinical pathology by serving as a highly accurate and versatile AI backbone for automated diagnostic assistance, disease classification, and quantitative analysis of whole slide images. This could lead to faster, more precise, and more consistent diagnoses, particularly in complex subspecialties like dermatopathology, and accelerate translational research for novel biomarker discovery and therapeutic development.

Abstract

Foundation models trained on large-scale pathology image corpora have demonstrated strong transfer capabilities across diverse histopathology tasks. Building on this progress, we introduce PLUTO-4, our next generation of pathology foundation models that extend the Pathology-Universal Transformer (PLUTO) to frontier scale. We share two complementary Vision Transformer architectures in the PLUTO-4 family: a compact and efficient PLUTO-4S model optimized for multi-scale deployment using a FlexiViT setup with 2D-RoPE embeddings, and a frontier-scale PLUTO-4G model trained with a single patch size to maximize representation capacity and stability. Both models are pretrained using a self-supervised objective derived from DINOv2 on a large multi-institutional corpus containing 551,164 WSIs from 137,144 patients across over 50 institutions, spanning over 60 disease types and over 100 stains. Comprehensive evaluation across public and internal benchmarks demonstrates that PLUTO-4 achieves state-of-the-art performance on tasks requiring varying spatial and biological context, including patch-level classification, segmentation, and slide-level diagnosis. The compact PLUTO-4S provides high-throughput and robust performance for practical deployment, while PLUTO-4G establishes new performance frontiers across multiple pathology benchmarks, including an 11% improvement in dermatopathology diagnosis. These diverse improvements underscore PLUTO-4's potential to transform real-world applications as a backbone for translational research and diagnostic use cases.
