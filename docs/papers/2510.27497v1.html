InertialAR: Autoregressive 3D Molecule Generation with Inertial Frames

Summary

InertialAR introduces a transformer-based autoregressive model for 3D molecule generation, addressing challenges of canonical tokenization and hybrid atom-based tokens by utilizing inertial frames and geometric positional encoding. It employs a hierarchical approach to predict atom types and 3D coordinates, achieving state-of-the-art performance in both unconditional and controllable molecule generation, particularly for targeted chemical functionality.

Medical Relevance

This research is highly relevant to medicine and health by providing a powerful computational tool for the de novo design of novel drug-like molecules. Its capability for controllable generation with targeted chemical functionality can significantly accelerate the identification and optimization of therapeutic candidates.

AI Health Application

The AI model (InertialAR) is designed to generate novel 3D molecular structures with specific, targeted chemical functionalities. This directly enables and accelerates the computational design of potential drug candidates, lead compounds, or other biologically active molecules for treating diseases, diagnosing conditions, or developing new biomedical tools.

Key Points

  • **Addressing Fundamental Challenges:** InertialAR tackles the two core issues in 3D molecule generation with transformers: canonical 1D tokenization invariant to SE(3) transformations and atom index permutations, and modeling hybrid atom-based tokens (discrete types with continuous 3D coordinates).
  • **Canonical Tokenization via Inertial Frames:** The model devises a novel tokenization strategy that aligns molecules to their inertial frames and reorders atoms, ensuring invariance to both rigid body transformations (SE(3)) and atom index permutations.
  • **Geometrically Aware Architecture:** The attention mechanism is enhanced with geometric rotary positional encoding (GeoRoPE), providing it with crucial geometric awareness to effectively model the 3D spatial relationships of atoms.
  • **Hierarchical Autoregressive Generation:** InertialAR uses a two-step autoregressive paradigm: first predicting the discrete atom type, and subsequently predicting its continuous 3D coordinates using Diffusion loss.
  • **State-of-the-Art Unconditional Performance:** It achieves state-of-the-art performance on 7 out of 10 evaluation metrics for unconditional molecule generation across widely used datasets like QM9, GEOM-Drugs, and B3LYP.
  • **Superior Controllable Generation:** The model significantly outperforms strong baselines in controllable generation, attaining state-of-the-art results across all 5 metrics when generating molecules with targeted chemical functionality.
  • **Enabling Molecular Design for Drug Discovery:** The ability to controllably generate molecules with desired properties provides a powerful tool for accelerating computational drug discovery and materials science by rapidly proposing novel compounds.

Methodology

InertialAR is a Transformer-based autoregressive model that employs a canonical tokenization by aligning molecules to their inertial frames and reordering atoms for SE(3) and permutation invariance. Its attention mechanism incorporates Geometric Rotary Positional Encoding (GeoRoPE). Molecule generation is hierarchical, predicting discrete atom types followed by continuous 3D coordinates via Diffusion loss.

Key Findings

For controllable generation targeting specific chemical functionalities, it significantly outperformed strong baselines, attaining state-of-the-art results across all 5 metrics.

Abstract

Transformer-based autoregressive models have emerged as a unifying paradigm across modalities such as text and images, but their extension to 3D molecule generation remains underexplored. The gap stems from two fundamental challenges: (1) tokenizing molecules into a canonical 1D sequence of tokens that is invariant to both SE(3) transformations and atom index permutations, and (2) designing an architecture capable of modeling hybrid atom-based tokens that couple discrete atom types with continuous 3D coordinates. To address these challenges, we introduce InertialAR. InertialAR devises a canonical tokenization that aligns molecules to their inertial frames and reorders atoms to ensure SE(3) and permutation invariance. Moreover, InertialAR equips the attention mechanism with geometric awareness via geometric rotary positional encoding (GeoRoPE). In addition, it utilizes a hierarchical autoregressive paradigm to predict the next atom-based token, predicting the atom type first and then its 3D coordinates via Diffusion loss. Experimentally, InertialAR achieves state-of-the-art performance on 7 of the 10 evaluation metrics for unconditional molecule generation across QM9, GEOM-Drugs, and B3LYP. Moreover, it significantly outperforms strong baselines in controllable generation for targeted chemical functionality, attaining state-of-the-art results across all 5 metrics.
