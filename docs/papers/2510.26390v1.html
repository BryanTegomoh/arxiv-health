SPG-CDENet: Spatial Prior-Guided Cross Dual Encoder Network for Multi-Organ Segmentation

Published: 2025-10-30
Authors: Xizhi Tian, Changjun Zhou, Yulin. Yang

Summary

SPG-CDENet introduces a novel two-stage deep learning framework for accurate multi-organ segmentation, specifically addressing challenges posed by variations in organ size and shape. It leverages a spatial prior network to guide a cross dual encoder network that processes global and local features, integrated with a symmetric cross-attention mechanism and a flow-based decoder for maximal feature preservation. The method demonstrates superior performance on public datasets, validating its effectiveness for critical computer-aided diagnostic tasks.

Medical Relevance

Accurate multi-organ segmentation is a foundational task in computer-aided diagnosis, directly impacting disease detection, treatment planning (e.g., radiation therapy), surgical navigation, and monitoring disease progression, thereby enhancing diagnostic precision and patient care outcomes.

AI Health Application

The AI (deep learning) model, SPG-CDENet, is applied to automatically or semi-automatically segment multiple organs from medical images. This application aims to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of computer-aided diagnosis, facilitate precise surgical and radiation therapy planning, and improve general anatomical analysis for various medical conditions.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical challenge of multi-organ segmentation, particularly variations in organ size and shape, using a novel two-stage deep learning paradigm.
  • Employs a spatial prior network as the first stage to generate coarse localization maps, serving as spatial guidance (approximate ROI) for subsequent processing.
  • Features a cross dual encoder network comprising a global encoder (for entire image features) and a local encoder (focused on prior-guided features).
  • Integrates a symmetric cross-attention module across all layers of the global and local encoders to effectively fuse and refine features, enhancing their interaction.
  • Utilizes a flow-based decoder designed to directly propagate high-level semantic features from the final encoder layer to all decoder layers, maximizing feature preservation and utilization.
  • Achieved superior qualitative and quantitative performance on two public datasets compared to existing segmentation methods.
  • Ablation studies confirmed the effectiveness and contribution of each proposed module (spatial prior, dual encoder, symmetric cross-attention, flow-based decoder) to the improved segmentation accuracy.

Methodology

The SPG-CDENet is a two-stage deep learning framework. The initial stage involves a spatial prior network that generates coarse localization maps (ROI). The second stage, a cross dual encoder network, uses these priors and consists of a global encoder (for overall image features) and a local encoder (for prior-specific features). A symmetric cross-attention module is deployed across all encoder layers to facilitate feature fusion and refinement. The network concludes with a flow-based decoder that directly propagates high-level semantic features from the final encoder layer to all decoder layers, ensuring maximum feature utilization. Performance was evaluated through extensive qualitative and quantitative experiments on two public datasets, complemented by ablation studies.

Key Findings

The SPG-CDENet demonstrated superior performance in multi-organ segmentation when compared to existing methods on two public datasets. Key findings include that the novel two-stage paradigm, the spatial prior network, the cross dual encoder architecture with its global and local feature processing, the symmetric cross-attention module, and the flow-based decoder each contribute effectively to the observed improvements in segmentation accuracy, as validated by ablation studies.

Abstract

Multi-organ segmentation is a critical task in computer-aided diagnosis. While recent deep learning methods have achieved remarkable success in image segmentation, huge variations in organ size and shape challenge their effectiveness in multi-organ segmentation. To address these challenges, we propose a Spatial Prior-Guided Cross Dual Encoder Network (SPG-CDENet), a novel two-stage segmentation paradigm designed to improve multi-organ segmentation accuracy. Our SPG-CDENet consists of two key components: a spatial prior network and a cross dual encoder network. The prior network generates coarse localization maps that delineate the approximate ROI, serving as spatial guidance for the dual encoder network. The cross dual encoder network comprises four essential components: a global encoder, a local encoder, a symmetric cross-attention module, and a flow-based decoder. The global encoder captures global semantic features from the entire image, while the local encoder focuses on features from the prior network. To enhance the interaction between the global and local encoders, a symmetric cross-attention module is proposed across all layers of the encoders to fuse and refine features. Furthermore, the flow-based decoder directly propagates high-level semantic features from the final encoder layer to all decoder layers, maximizing feature preservation and utilization. Extensive qualitative and quantitative experiments on two public datasets demonstrate the superior performance of SPG-CDENet compared to existing segmentation methods. Furthermore, ablation studies further validate the effectiveness of the proposed modules in improving segmentation accuracy.
