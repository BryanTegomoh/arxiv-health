SPG-CDENet: Spatial Prior-Guided Cross Dual Encoder Network for Multi-Organ Segmentation

Summary

This paper introduces SPG-CDENet, a novel two-stage deep learning framework for multi-organ segmentation that addresses challenges posed by significant organ size and shape variations. It combines a spatial prior network for coarse localization with a cross dual encoder network featuring global and local encoders, symmetric cross-attention, and a flow-based decoder. The method demonstrates superior segmentation accuracy on public datasets, enhancing computer-aided diagnosis capabilities.

Medical Relevance

Accurate multi-organ segmentation is fundamental for various medical applications, including computer-aided diagnosis, treatment planning (e.g., radiation therapy), and disease progression monitoring. Improving this accuracy can lead to more precise diagnostic insights, better surgical outcomes, and personalized patient care.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the automated and accurate segmentation of multiple organs from medical images (e.g., CT, MRI). This serves to assist clinicians in diagnosis, disease progression tracking, pre-surgical planning, and radiation therapy planning by providing precise anatomical delineations.

Key Points

  • Addresses the challenge of huge variations in organ size and shape, which commonly hinders the effectiveness of deep learning methods in multi-organ segmentation.
  • Proposes SPG-CDENet, a two-stage segmentation paradigm comprising a spatial prior network and a cross dual encoder network.
  • The spatial prior network generates coarse localization maps (approximate ROI) to provide crucial spatial guidance to the subsequent segmentation stage.
  • The cross dual encoder network integrates a global encoder (capturing overall semantic features), a local encoder (focusing on prior-guided features), a symmetric cross-attention module (fusing and refining features across all encoder layers), and a flow-based decoder.
  • The symmetric cross-attention module enhances interaction and feature fusion between the global and local encoder branches, critical for integrating context and fine-grained details.
  • The flow-based decoder is designed to maximize feature preservation by directly propagating high-level semantic features from the final encoder layer to all decoder layers.
  • Extensive qualitative and quantitative experiments on two public datasets confirm SPG-CDENet's superior performance compared to existing segmentation methods, with ablation studies validating the effectiveness of its individual modules.

Methodology

The proposed SPG-CDENet is a two-stage deep learning framework. The first stage involves a spatial prior network that produces coarse localization maps (ROIs). The second stage is a cross dual encoder network, which takes the original image and the prior maps as input. This network includes a global encoder for holistic features and a local encoder for region-specific features, connected by symmetric cross-attention modules across all layers. A flow-based decoder then reconstructs the segmentation masks by propagating high-level features directly to all decoder layers. Performance is evaluated quantitatively and qualitatively on two public datasets, supported by ablation studies.

Key Findings

SPG-CDENet achieves superior performance in multi-organ segmentation compared to existing state-of-the-art methods. The spatial prior network effectively guides the segmentation, and the cross dual encoder network's components, including the symmetric cross-attention and flow-based decoder, are individually validated to significantly improve segmentation accuracy, demonstrating their effectiveness in handling complex organ variations.

Clinical Impact

This research has the potential to significantly enhance the accuracy and reliability of computer-aided diagnostic systems, particularly in scenarios requiring precise delineation of multiple organs (e.g., tumor staging, radiotherapy planning, volumetric analysis). By providing more accurate segmentation masks, it could lead to earlier and more precise disease detection, more effective treatment strategies, and reduced inter-observer variability in clinical practice, ultimately improving patient outcomes.

Abstract

Multi-organ segmentation is a critical task in computer-aided diagnosis. While recent deep learning methods have achieved remarkable success in image segmentation, huge variations in organ size and shape challenge their effectiveness in multi-organ segmentation. To address these challenges, we propose a Spatial Prior-Guided Cross Dual Encoder Network (SPG-CDENet), a novel two-stage segmentation paradigm designed to improve multi-organ segmentation accuracy. Our SPG-CDENet consists of two key components: a spatial prior network and a cross dual encoder network. The prior network generates coarse localization maps that delineate the approximate ROI, serving as spatial guidance for the dual encoder network. The cross dual encoder network comprises four essential components: a global encoder, a local encoder, a symmetric cross-attention module, and a flow-based decoder. The global encoder captures global semantic features from the entire image, while the local encoder focuses on features from the prior network. To enhance the interaction between the global and local encoders, a symmetric cross-attention module is proposed across all layers of the encoders to fuse and refine features. Furthermore, the flow-based decoder directly propagates high-level semantic features from the final encoder layer to all decoder layers, maximizing feature preservation and utilization. Extensive qualitative and quantitative experiments on two public datasets demonstrate the superior performance of SPG-CDENet compared to existing segmentation methods. Furthermore, ablation studies further validate the effectiveness of the proposed modules in improving segmentation accuracy.
