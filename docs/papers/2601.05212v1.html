FlowLet: Conditional 3D Brain MRI Synthesis using Wavelet Flow Matching

arXiv ID: 2601.05212v1
Published: 2026-01-08
Authors: Danilo Danese, Angela Lombardi, Matteo Attimonelli, Giuseppe Fasano, Tommaso Di Noia
Categories: cs.CV

View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces FlowLet, a novel conditional generative framework designed to synthesize age-conditioned 3D Brain MRIs. FlowLet leverages flow matching within an invertible 3D wavelet domain to overcome limitations of existing generative methods, such as slow inference and artifacts from latent compression. The research demonstrates that FlowLet generates high-fidelity volumes efficiently, improving Brain Age Prediction (BAP) model performance for underrepresented age groups and preserving anatomical structures.

Medical Relevance

This work addresses critical challenges in neurological research by enabling the creation of diverse and balanced MRI datasets, which is vital for developing accurate and fair Brain Age Prediction models and studying neurological conditions across varied populations.

AI Health Application

The paper proposes an AI framework (FlowLet) for synthesizing conditional 3D Brain MRIs, specifically for data augmentation to improve Brain Age Prediction (BAP) models. BAP is an AI application that estimates biological brain age from MRI data, which is relevant for early disease detection, understanding neurological aging, and assessing overall brain health. The work aims to make these AI models more robust, fair, and generalizable for clinical and research use by addressing data scarcity and imbalance in medical datasets.

Key Points

  • Existing 3D Brain MRI datasets for Brain Age Prediction (BAP) are demographically skewed and lack diversity, limiting model fairness and generalizability.
  • Current generative methods (e.g., latent diffusion models) for MRI data augmentation are slow at inference, can introduce artifacts due to latent compression, and often lack explicit age conditioning.
  • FlowLet is a conditional generative framework that synthesizes age-conditioned 3D MRIs by employing flow matching within an invertible 3D wavelet domain.
  • The use of an invertible 3D wavelet domain in FlowLet helps to avoid reconstruction artifacts and significantly reduces computational demands compared to latent space methods.
  • Experiments confirm FlowLet's ability to generate high-fidelity 3D MRI volumes rapidly, requiring few sampling steps.
  • Training BAP models with data augmented by FlowLet notably improves prediction performance for age groups that are typically underrepresented in real datasets.
  • Region-based analysis of the synthesized MRIs validates the preservation of critical anatomical structures, ensuring the generated data's biological plausibility.

Methodology

FlowLet proposes a conditional generative framework that synthesizes age-conditioned 3D MRI volumes. It utilizes flow matching, a generative modeling technique, operating within an invertible 3D wavelet domain. This approach allows for direct generation in a transformed space that is computationally efficient and minimizes reconstruction artifacts, unlike latent diffusion models.

Key Findings

FlowLet successfully generates high-fidelity 3D MRI volumes with minimal sampling steps. Importantly, BAP models trained with FlowLet-augmented data exhibit improved performance, particularly for underrepresented age groups. Anatomical structure preservation in the synthesized MRIs was confirmed through region-based analysis.

Clinical Impact

FlowLet can lead to more robust, fair, and generalizable Brain Age Prediction models, aiding in early detection and monitoring of neurological conditions. By mitigating the need for costly and ethically complex real data acquisition, it facilitates more extensive and diverse neurological research, potentially improving diagnostic and prognostic tools in clinical settings.

Limitations

The abstract highlights limitations of *current generative methods* which FlowLet aims to overcome: slow inference speeds, potential introduction of artifacts due to latent compression, and infrequent conditioning on age, all of which negatively impact BAP performance and fairness. Specific limitations of FlowLet itself are not discussed in the abstract.

Abstract

Brain Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) plays a central role in studying neurological development, aging, and diseases. One key application is Brain Age Prediction (BAP), which estimates an individual's biological brain age from MRI data. Effective BAP models require large, diverse, and age-balanced datasets, whereas existing 3D MRI datasets are demographically skewed, limiting fairness and generalizability. Acquiring new data is costly and ethically constrained, motivating generative data augmentation. Current generative methods are often based on latent diffusion models, which operate in learned low dimensional latent spaces to address the memory demands of volumetric MRI data. However, these methods are typically slow at inference, may introduce artifacts due to latent compression, and are rarely conditioned on age, thereby affecting the BAP performance. In this work, we propose FlowLet, a conditional generative framework that synthesizes age-conditioned 3D MRIs by leveraging flow matching within an invertible 3D wavelet domain, helping to avoid reconstruction artifacts and reducing computational demands. Experiments show that FlowLet generates high-fidelity volumes with few sampling steps. Training BAP models with data generated by FlowLet improves performance for underrepresented age groups, and region-based analysis confirms preservation of anatomical structures.
