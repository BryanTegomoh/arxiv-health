Bangla MedER: Multi-BERT Ensemble Approach for the Recognition of Bangla Medical Entity

Authors: Tanjim Taharat Aurpa, Farzana Akter, Md. Mehedi Hasan, Shakil Ahmed, Shifat Ara Rafiq, Fatema Khan

Summary

This paper addresses the significant gap in Medical Entity Recognition (MedER) for low-resource languages by developing a novel approach for Bangla. The researchers propose a Multi-BERT Ensemble model, which, alongside a newly developed high-quality Bangla medical dataset, achieved an impressive 89.58% accuracy, outperforming existing transformer models and marking a substantial 11.80% improvement over a single BERT model.

Medical Relevance

Accurate Medical Entity Recognition is fundamental for developing automated systems in healthcare, which can significantly enhance patient care, streamline medical information processing, and improve clinical outcomes by extracting crucial data from unstructured text.

AI Health Application

The research aims to develop AI models for automatically extracting meaningful medical entities from Bangla medical texts. This application can enable the creation of automated systems for tasks such as structuring patient records, facilitating clinical decision support, improving medical information retrieval, and supporting epidemiological surveillance in low-resource language settings, thereby enhancing patient care and outcomes.

Key Points

  • Addresses the critical need for Medical Entity Recognition (MedER) in low-resource languages, specifically Bangla, where research is underexplored.
  • Proposes a novel Multi-BERT Ensemble approach for Bangla MedER, combining the strengths of multiple transformer architectures.
  • Evaluated various baseline transformer models including BERT, DistilBERT, ELECTRA, and RoBERTa to establish a comparative benchmark.
  • Developed a high-quality, annotated dataset tailored specifically for the Bangla MedER task, overcoming a major challenge in low-resource NLP.
  • The Multi-BERT Ensemble achieved a state-of-the-art accuracy of 89.58%, demonstrating its superior performance.
  • Showcased a significant 11.80% accuracy improvement over a single-layer BERT model, highlighting the effectiveness of the ensemble approach.
  • The findings lay a robust foundation for future advancements in medical Natural Language Processing (NLP) for low-resource languages.

Methodology

The study first examined several transformer models (BERT, DistilBERT, ELECTRA, RoBERTa) as baselines for Bangla MedER. It then proposed and implemented a novel Multi-BERT Ensemble approach. To address data scarcity, a high-quality, custom-annotated dataset specifically for Bangla medical entities was developed. Model effectiveness was rigorously evaluated using multiple performance metrics against this new dataset.

Key Findings

The proposed Multi-BERT Ensemble approach significantly outperformed all baseline transformer models for Bangla MedER, achieving the highest accuracy of 89.58%. This represents a substantial 11.80% accuracy improvement over a single-layer BERT model, demonstrating the robustness and applicability of the ensemble method combined with the newly developed high-quality dataset.

Clinical Impact

This research provides a critical tool for automating the extraction of medical information from Bangla clinical texts and research, potentially leading to more efficient patient record management, improved diagnostic support, better treatment planning, and enhanced public health initiatives in Bangla-speaking regions. It enables the development of sophisticated AI-powered healthcare applications tailored for a large, underserved linguistic community, ultimately improving patient care and outcomes.

Limitations

A major challenge and limitation in MedER for low-resource languages like Bangla, which this study explicitly addresses, is the severe lack of high-quality, annotated datasets. While the study developed one, the inherent scarcity was a significant hurdle to overcome.

Future Directions

The work sets a strong foundation for further advancements in low-resource medical NLP, implying continued research into more sophisticated models, larger datasets, and broader applications within the medical domain for languages like Bangla.

Abstract

Medical Entity Recognition (MedER) is an essential NLP task for extracting meaningful entities from the medical corpus. Nowadays, MedER-based research outcomes can remarkably contribute to the development of automated systems in the medical sector, ultimately enhancing patient care and outcomes. While extensive research has been conducted on MedER in English, low-resource languages like Bangla remain underexplored. Our work aims to bridge this gap. For Bangla medical entity recognition, this study first examined a number of transformer models, including BERT, DistilBERT, ELECTRA, and RoBERTa. We also propose a novel Multi-BERT Ensemble approach that outperformed all baseline models with the highest accuracy of 89.58%. Notably, it provides an 11.80% accuracy improvement over the single-layer BERT model, demonstrating its effectiveness for this task. A major challenge in MedER for low-resource languages is the lack of annotated datasets. To address this issue, we developed a high-quality dataset tailored for the Bangla MedER task. The dataset was used to evaluate the effectiveness of our model through multiple performance metrics, demonstrating its robustness and applicability. Our findings highlight the potential of Multi-BERT Ensemble models in improving MedER for Bangla and set the foundation for further advancements in low-resource medical NLP.
