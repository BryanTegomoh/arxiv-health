Multivariate Gaussian Representation Learning for Medical Action Evaluation

Published: 2025-11-13
Authors: Luming Yang, Haoxian Liu, Siqing Li, Alper Yilmaz
Categories: cs.CV, cs.AI

Summary

This paper addresses the challenges of fine-grained medical action evaluation, particularly for rapid actions, by introducing CPREval-6k, a new multi-view, multi-label benchmark dataset of 6,372 expert-annotated videos. Alongside the dataset, they propose GaussMedAct, a multivariate Gaussian encoding framework that leverages adaptive 3D Gaussians as tokens to learn robust spatiotemporal representations, achieving superior accuracy and efficiency compared to baselines.

Medical Relevance

This research significantly advances the automated evaluation of critical medical actions, like CPR, by providing both a much-needed robust dataset and an efficient, accurate AI model. This can lead to improved training, standardized performance assessment, and potentially real-time feedback systems in clinical settings.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the development of a framework for fine-grained, automated evaluation and analysis of medical actions and motions. This could be used for assessing the performance of medical professionals (e.g., during training or procedures), improving procedural safety and efficacy, or monitoring patient movements in clinical settings for diagnostic or therapeutic purposes. The goal is to provide objective, precise, and real-time evaluation capabilities.

Key Points

  • **CPREval-6k Dataset**: Introduction of a novel, comprehensive medical action benchmark dataset containing 6,372 expert-annotated videos with 22 clinical labels, addressing the lack of sufficient training data in medical vision.
  • **GaussMedAct Framework**: Proposed multivariate Gaussian encoding framework for adaptive spatiotemporal representation learning of medical actions.
  • **Multivariate Gaussian Representation**: Actions are projected to a temporally scaled multi-dimensional space and decomposed into adaptive 3D Gaussians, serving as motion-semantic tokens with anisotropic covariance modeling for noise robustness.
  • **Hybrid Spatial Encoding**: Utilizes a dual-stream Cartesian and Vector strategy to effectively process joint and bone skeletal features, enhancing the utilization of spatiotemporal information.
  • **High Accuracy and Efficiency**: Achieves 92.1% Top-1 accuracy on CPREval-6k, outperforming the ST-GCN baseline by +5.9% accuracy.
  • **Real-time Performance**: Demonstrates real-time inference capabilities with significantly reduced computational cost (only 10% FLOPs compared to ST-GCN).
  • **Robustness**: Cross-dataset experiments confirm the method's superior robustness, indicating generalizability beyond the introduced benchmark.

Methodology

The core methodology involves GaussMedAct, a multivariate Gaussian encoding framework. It projects joint motions into a temporally scaled multi-dimensional space, decomposing actions into adaptive 3D Gaussians that act as motion-preserving tokens with anisotropic covariance modeling for robustness to spatiotemporal noise. A Hybrid Spatial Encoding strategy is employed, featuring a dual-stream approach (Cartesian and Vector) to integrate both joint and bone features from skeletal data.

Key Findings

The proposed GaussMedAct achieved a 92.1% Top-1 accuracy on the new CPREval-6k benchmark, representing a +5.9% improvement over the ST-GCN baseline. It demonstrated real-time inference capabilities with significantly higher efficiency, using only 10% of the FLOPs compared to the baseline. Furthermore, cross-dataset experiments validated its superior robustness and generalization.

It could facilitate standardized performance feedback, identify skill deficiencies, and support the development of AI-powered systems for continuous quality improvement and potentially even real-time assistance during medical emergencies.

Abstract

Fine-grained action evaluation in medical vision faces unique challenges due to the unavailability of comprehensive datasets, stringent precision requirements, and insufficient spatiotemporal dynamic modeling of very rapid actions. To support development and evaluation, we introduce CPREval-6k, a multi-view, multi-label medical action benchmark containing 6,372 expert-annotated videos with 22 clinical labels. Using this dataset, we present GaussMedAct, a multivariate Gaussian encoding framework, to advance medical motion analysis through adaptive spatiotemporal representation learning. Multivariate Gaussian Representation projects the joint motions to a temporally scaled multi-dimensional space, and decomposes actions into adaptive 3D Gaussians that serve as tokens. These tokens preserve motion semantics through anisotropic covariance modeling while maintaining robustness to spatiotemporal noise. Hybrid Spatial Encoding, employing a Cartesian and Vector dual-stream strategy, effectively utilizes skeletal information in the form of joint and bone features. The proposed method achieves 92.1% Top-1 accuracy with real-time inference on the benchmark, outperforming the ST-GCN baseline by +5.9% accuracy with only 10% FLOPs. Cross-dataset experiments confirm the superiority of our method in robustness.
