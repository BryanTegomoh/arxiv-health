PETRA: Pretrained Evolutionary Transformer for SARS-CoV-2 Mutation Prediction

arXiv ID: 2511.03976v1
Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Xu Zou
Categories: cs.LG, cs.AI, q-bio.GN

Summary

This paper introduces PETRA (Pretrained Evolutionary TRAnsformer), a novel deep learning model designed to predict future SARS-CoV-2 mutations. Unlike traditional transformer models that struggle with noisy raw viral RNA sequences, PETRA leverages evolutionary trajectories derived from phylogenetic trees, effectively mitigating noise and capturing the hierarchical structure of viral evolution. The model demonstrates significantly superior predictive performance for both nucleotide and spike amino-acid mutations compared to existing baselines, aiding in real-time variant surveillance and public health preparedness.

Medical Relevance

Accurate prediction of SARS-CoV-2 mutations is critical for public health to anticipate and mitigate future waves of infection, guide the development of updated vaccines, and inform targeted antiviral therapies against emerging variants.

AI Health Application

PETRA is an AI (Pretrained Evolutionary Transformer) model designed to predict future SARS-CoV-2 mutations in real-time. This application of AI provides critical intelligence for public health decision-making, enabling proactive responses to emerging immune-evasive variants, informing the design of next-generation vaccines and therapeutics, and enhancing pandemic preparedness and biosecurity surveillance.

Key Points

  • SARS-CoV-2 exhibits rapid, unpredictable evolution and continuous emergence of immune-evasive variants, posing persistent public health and vaccine development challenges.
  • Traditional generative pre-trained transformers (GPTs) have limited direct application to noisy viral genomic sequences.
  • PETRA utilizes evolutionary trajectories from phylogenetic trees as input, rather than raw RNA sequences, to mitigate sequencing noise and capture hierarchical evolutionary structure.
  • A weighted training framework is employed to address substantial geographical and temporal imbalances prevalent in global SARS-CoV-2 sequence data.
  • PETRA achieves a weighted recall@1 of 9.45% for nucleotide mutations and 17.10% for spike amino-acid mutations, significantly outperforming the best baseline (0.49% and 6.64% respectively).
  • The model demonstrates practical utility by aiding in real-time mutation prediction for major clades such as 24F(XEC) and 25A(LP.8.1).
  • The code for PETRA is open-sourced, facilitating further research and application.

Methodology

PETRA is a transformer-based deep learning model. Its key methodological innovation is using evolutionary trajectories derived from phylogenetic trees as input data instead of raw RNA sequences. This approach helps in mitigating sequencing noise and capturing the inherent hierarchical structure of viral evolution. The model is trained using a weighted framework designed to account for geographical and temporal imbalances in global viral sequence data.

Key Findings

PETRA significantly outperforms existing baselines in predicting future SARS-CoV-2 mutations, achieving a weighted recall@1 of 9.45% for nucleotide mutations and 17.10% for spike amino-acid mutations, compared to 0.49% and 6.64% for the best baseline, respectively. It also demonstrates the ability to support real-time mutation prediction for major viral clades.

Clinical Impact

This model has the potential to enhance global genomic surveillance efforts by providing an early warning system for the emergence of new SARS-CoV-2 variants. This can inform vaccine manufacturers about necessary updates, guide public health officials in preparing for potential outbreaks, and assist in tailoring diagnostic tools and therapeutic strategies to maintain effectiveness against evolving strains.

Limitations

None explicitly mentioned for PETRA itself in the abstract, but the paper highlights that direct applications of traditional GPTs to noisy viral genomic sequences are limited, and global sequence data has substantial geographical and temporal imbalances, which PETRA is designed to address.

Future Directions

Not explicitly mentioned in the abstract, but the open-sourcing of the code suggests potential for broader adoption, further development, and application to other rapidly evolving pathogens.

Abstract

Since its emergence, SARS-CoV-2 has demonstrated a rapid and unpredictable evolutionary trajectory, characterized by the continual emergence of immune-evasive variants. This poses persistent challenges to public health and vaccine development. While large-scale generative pre-trained transformers (GPTs) have revolutionized the modeling of sequential data, their direct applications to noisy viral genomic sequences are limited. In this paper, we introduce PETRA(Pretrained Evolutionary TRAnsformer), a novel transformer approach based on evolutionary trajectories derived from phylogenetic trees rather than raw RNA sequences. This method effectively mitigates sequencing noise and captures the hierarchical structure of viral evolution. With a weighted training framework to address substantial geographical and temporal imbalances in global sequence data, PETRA excels in predicting future SARS-CoV-2 mutations, achieving a weighted recall@1 of 9.45% for nucleotide mutations and 17.10\% for spike amino-acid mutations, compared to 0.49% and 6.64% respectively for the best baseline. PETRA also demonstrates its ability to aid in the real-time mutation prediction of major clades like 24F(XEC) and 25A(LP.8.1). The code is open sourced on https://github.com/xz-keg/PETra
