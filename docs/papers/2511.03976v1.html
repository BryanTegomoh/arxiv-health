PETRA: Pretrained Evolutionary Transformer for SARS-CoV-2 Mutation Prediction

arXiv ID: 2511.03976v1
Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Xu Zou
Categories: cs.LG, cs.AI, q-bio.GN
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

PETRA addresses the challenge of predicting SARS-CoV-2 evolution by introducing a novel transformer model that analyzes evolutionary trajectories derived from phylogenetic trees rather than noisy raw RNA sequences. This innovative approach, coupled with a weighted training framework, significantly outperforms baselines in predicting future nucleotide and spike amino-acid mutations, providing a critical tool for public health and vaccine development.

Medical Relevance

Predicting SARS-CoV-2 mutations is paramount for anticipating future variant emergence, informing timely vaccine updates, guiding therapeutic strategies, and enabling proactive public health interventions to mitigate disease spread and severity.

AI Health Application

PETRA is an AI model (Pretrained Evolutionary Transformer) designed to predict future SARS-CoV-2 mutations based on evolutionary trajectories. This application provides a computational tool for real-time surveillance of viral evolution, aiding in proactive public health responses, informing vaccine development and updates against emerging variants, and contributing to biosecurity by identifying potentially dangerous pathogen changes.

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: SARS-CoV-2's rapid, unpredictable evolution and the emergence of immune-evasive variants pose persistent challenges to public health and vaccine development.
  • **Novel Approach**: PETRA (Pretrained Evolutionary TRAnsformer) is a transformer model that processes "evolutionary trajectories" derived from phylogenetic trees, mitigating sequencing noise and capturing hierarchical viral evolution, instead of directly analyzing raw RNA sequences.
  • **Methodological Advantages**: This approach effectively reduces the impact of sequencing noise common in viral genomics and inherently models the hierarchical structure of viral evolutionary pathways.
  • **Robust Training Framework**: A weighted training framework is utilized to counteract the significant geographical and temporal imbalances present in global SARS-CoV-2 sequence data.
  • **Superior Predictive Performance**: PETRA achieved a weighted recall@1 of 9.45% for nucleotide mutations and 17.10% for spike amino-acid mutations, substantially outperforming the best baseline models (0.49% and 6.64% respectively).
  • **Real-time Application**: The model demonstrated its utility in aiding real-time mutation prediction for major, currently circulating SARS-CoV-2 clades, specifically citing 24F(XEC) and 25A(LP.8.1).

Methodology

PETRA is a transformer-based deep learning model designed to predict SARS-CoV-2 mutations. Its core innovation lies in its input: it analyzes evolutionary trajectories derived from phylogenetic trees rather than raw genomic sequences, which helps mitigate sequencing noise and inherently captures the hierarchical evolutionary structure. The model is trained using a weighted framework specifically designed to address and compensate for the substantial geographical and temporal imbalances prevalent in global viral sequence datasets.

Key Findings

PETRA exhibited significantly enhanced predictive capabilities for future SARS-CoV-2 mutations. It achieved a weighted recall@1 of 9.45% for nucleotide mutations, a marked improvement over the best baseline's 0.49%. For spike amino-acid mutations, PETRA recorded a weighted recall@1 of 17.10%, significantly surpassing the best baseline's 6.64%. Furthermore, the model proved effective in real-time prediction scenarios for important clades such as 24F(XEC) and 25A(LP.8.1).

Clinical Impact

The enhanced accuracy of PETRA in predicting future SARS-CoV-2 mutations offers a critical tool for clinical and public health decision-making. It can enable earlier identification of potentially immune-evasive variants, thereby guiding the timely development and update of vaccines, informing the efficacy of existing therapeutics, and facilitating proactive, targeted public health interventions to control outbreaks and reduce disease burden. This foresight could lead to more effective disease management and resource allocation.

Limitations

The abstract does not explicitly state limitations of the PETRA model itself. However, it highlights an underlying challenge in the field that PETRA aims to address: the "substantial geographical and temporal imbalances in global sequence data," implying that while PETRA mitigates the impact, such data biases remain a systemic hurdle for comprehensive viral surveillance and prediction.

Future Directions

While not explicitly detailed in the abstract, the successful demonstration of PETRA's predictive power for SARS-CoV-2, especially for real-time clade prediction, suggests potential future directions including its application to other rapidly evolving pathogens, further integration with broader epidemiological forecasting models, and continued refinement of its weighted training framework to adapt to evolving data collection landscapes.

Abstract

Since its emergence, SARS-CoV-2 has demonstrated a rapid and unpredictable evolutionary trajectory, characterized by the continual emergence of immune-evasive variants. This poses persistent challenges to public health and vaccine development. While large-scale generative pre-trained transformers (GPTs) have revolutionized the modeling of sequential data, their direct applications to noisy viral genomic sequences are limited. In this paper, we introduce PETRA(Pretrained Evolutionary TRAnsformer), a novel transformer approach based on evolutionary trajectories derived from phylogenetic trees rather than raw RNA sequences. This method effectively mitigates sequencing noise and captures the hierarchical structure of viral evolution. With a weighted training framework to address substantial geographical and temporal imbalances in global sequence data, PETRA excels in predicting future SARS-CoV-2 mutations, achieving a weighted recall@1 of 9.45% for nucleotide mutations and 17.10\% for spike amino-acid mutations, compared to 0.49% and 6.64% respectively for the best baseline. PETRA also demonstrates its ability to aid in the real-time mutation prediction of major clades like 24F(XEC) and 25A(LP.8.1). The code is open sourced on https://github.com/xz-keg/PETra
