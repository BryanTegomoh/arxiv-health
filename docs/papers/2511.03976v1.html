PETRA: Pretrained Evolutionary Transformer for SARS-CoV-2 Mutation Prediction

arXiv ID: 2511.03976v1
Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Xu Zou
Categories: cs.LG, cs.AI, q-bio.GN
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00

Summary

PETRA introduces a novel Pretrained Evolutionary Transformer for predicting future SARS-CoV-2 mutations by analyzing evolutionary trajectories derived from phylogenetic trees, rather than noisy raw RNA sequences. This method, enhanced by a weighted training framework, significantly outperforms existing baselines in predicting both nucleotide and critical spike amino-acid mutations. PETRA offers a powerful tool to aid real-time mutation prediction for emerging clades, thereby supporting public health and vaccine development efforts.

Medical Relevance

This research is profoundly relevant to medicine and public health as it offers a superior method for predicting the emergence of new SARS-CoV-2 variants, particularly those with immune-evasive properties. Such predictive capability is crucial for proactive vaccine adaptation, informing public health strategies, and developing effective therapeutic interventions to combat the ongoing pandemic.

AI Health Application

The AI (Pretrained Evolutionary Transformer, PETRA) predicts future SARS-CoV-2 mutations, including nucleotide and spike amino-acid mutations. This application provides critical foresight for healthcare systems, vaccine developers, and public health agencies to anticipate and respond to emerging variants, thus informing real-time pandemic response and improving medical countermeasures.

Key Points

  • PETRA is a transformer model designed for predicting future SARS-CoV-2 mutations.
  • It utilizes evolutionary trajectories from phylogenetic trees as input, which effectively mitigates sequencing noise and captures the hierarchical structure of viral evolution, unlike direct processing of raw RNA sequences.
  • The model incorporates a weighted training framework to address and compensate for significant geographical and temporal imbalances in global SARS-CoV-2 sequence data.
  • PETRA achieved a weighted recall@1 of 9.45% for nucleotide mutations and a substantial 17.10% for spike amino-acid mutations, outperforming the best baseline models (0.49% and 6.64% respectively).
  • The system demonstrated practical capability in aiding real-time mutation prediction for major circulating clades, including 24F(XEC) and 25A(LP.8.1).
  • Its methodology overcomes the limitations of directly applying general generative pre-trained transformers (GPTs) to inherently noisy viral genomic data.

Methodology

PETRA is a transformer-based deep learning model. Its core methodology involves processing *evolutionary trajectories* derived from *phylogenetic trees* rather than raw, noisy RNA sequences. This approach is designed to effectively mitigate sequencing noise and inherently capture the hierarchical relationships within viral evolution. A crucial component of its training is a *weighted framework* implemented to counteract biases introduced by geographical and temporal imbalances in the global SARS-CoV-2 sequence datasets.

Key Findings

PETRA demonstrated significantly enhanced performance in predicting future SARS-CoV-2 mutations. It achieved a weighted recall@1 of 9.45% for nucleotide mutations and an even more critical 17.10% for spike amino-acid mutations, which are directly relevant to immune evasion. These figures represent substantial improvements over the best baseline models, which yielded only 0.49% and 6.64% respectively. Furthermore, PETRA proved capable of assisting in the real-time prediction of mutations for specific, major viral clades such as 24F(XEC) and 25A(LP.8.1).

Clinical Impact

The enhanced predictive accuracy of PETRA, particularly for spike protein mutations, has significant clinical and public health implications. It enables earlier anticipation of new, potentially more transmissible or immune-evasive SARS-CoV-2 variants, allowing for more proactive and targeted vaccine redesign, therapeutic development, and the implementation of public health measures. This capability can improve pandemic preparedness, optimize surveillance programs, and guide resource allocation for diagnostics and treatments.

Abstract

Since its emergence, SARS-CoV-2 has demonstrated a rapid and unpredictable evolutionary trajectory, characterized by the continual emergence of immune-evasive variants. This poses persistent challenges to public health and vaccine development. While large-scale generative pre-trained transformers (GPTs) have revolutionized the modeling of sequential data, their direct applications to noisy viral genomic sequences are limited. In this paper, we introduce PETRA(Pretrained Evolutionary TRAnsformer), a novel transformer approach based on evolutionary trajectories derived from phylogenetic trees rather than raw RNA sequences. This method effectively mitigates sequencing noise and captures the hierarchical structure of viral evolution. With a weighted training framework to address substantial geographical and temporal imbalances in global sequence data, PETRA excels in predicting future SARS-CoV-2 mutations, achieving a weighted recall@1 of 9.45% for nucleotide mutations and 17.10\% for spike amino-acid mutations, compared to 0.49% and 6.64% respectively for the best baseline. PETRA also demonstrates its ability to aid in the real-time mutation prediction of major clades like 24F(XEC) and 25A(LP.8.1). The code is open sourced on https://github.com/xz-keg/PETra
