AdaGReS:Adaptive Greedy Context Selection via Redundancy-Aware Scoring for Token-Budgeted RAG

Summary

AdaGReS is a novel framework for token-budgeted Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) that addresses the issue of redundant context by optimizing a set-level objective combining query-chunk relevance and intra-set redundancy penalties. It employs greedy selection with an adaptively calibrated trade-off parameter, theoretically guaranteeing near-optimality for context selection. Experimental results, particularly on a high-redundancy biomedical corpus, demonstrate consistent improvements in redundancy control, context quality, and end-to-end answer generation.

Medical Relevance

This framework is highly relevant to medicine and health by ensuring that RAG systems used for medical information retrieval provide concise, non-redundant, and high-quality contextual information within strict token limits. Its proven effectiveness on a "high-redundancy biomedical (drug) corpus" directly demonstrates its utility in high-stakes medical domains where precise and efficient information access is critical.

AI Health Application

AdaGReS can be applied to improve RAG systems designed to synthesize information from vast medical and biomedical texts (e.g., scientific papers, drug interaction databases, electronic health records). This can lead to more accurate answers for clinical queries, assist in drug development by reducing redundancy in research data summaries, and enhance knowledge retrieval for medical professionals and researchers, thereby supporting diagnostics, treatment planning, and medical education.

Key Points

  • Standard RAG's top-k retrieval often returns redundant or near-duplicate chunks, which waste token budget and degrade the quality of downstream generation.
  • AdaGReS introduces a set-level objective function that balances query-chunk relevance with penalties for intra-set context redundancy to optimize context selection.
  • The framework performs greedy context selection under a token-budget constraint, leveraging marginal gains derived from its objective function.
  • A key innovation is a closed-form, instance-adaptive calibration mechanism that dynamically adjusts the relevance-redundancy trade-off parameter based on candidate pool statistics and budget limits, eliminating the need for manual tuning.
  • Theoretical analysis shows that the proposed objective exhibits epsilon-approximate submodularity under practical embedding similarity conditions, providing near-optimality guarantees for the greedy selection process.
  • Experiments were conducted on both open-domain question answering (Natural Questions) and a specialized high-redundancy biomedical (drug) corpus.
  • AdaGReS consistently demonstrated improvements in redundancy control and the overall quality of selected context.
  • These improvements translated into better end-to-end answer quality and increased robustness of the RAG system across various settings.

Methodology

AdaGReS employs a greedy selection strategy to choose context chunks under a token-budget constraint. It optimizes a novel set-level objective function that incorporates both query-chunk relevance and a penalty for redundancy within the selected set. A key methodological innovation is the instance-adaptive, closed-form calibration of the relevance-redundancy trade-off parameter, eliminating the need for manual tuning. The approach is theoretically grounded by demonstrating epsilon-approximate submodularity of the objective, ensuring near-optimal performance of the greedy selection.

Key Findings

The primary findings include significant improvements in redundancy control within the retrieved context and a consequent enhancement in overall context quality. These improvements translated directly into better end-to-end answer quality and increased robustness of the RAG system. The efficacy was demonstrated across different datasets, notably performing well on a challenging high-redundancy biomedical (drug) corpus.

This is crucial for applications like drug interaction checks, treatment guideline lookups, or patient record summarization, where token budgets are often limited and redundant information can hinder rapid, accurate decision-making or even lead to errors.

Abstract

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) is highly sensitive to the quality of selected context, yet standard top-k retrieval often returns redundant or near-duplicate chunks that waste token budget and degrade downstream generation. We present AdaGReS, a redundancy-aware context selection framework for token-budgeted RAG that optimizes a set-level objective combining query-chunk relevance and intra-set redundancy penalties. AdaGReS performs greedy selection under a token-budget constraint using marginal gains derived from the objective, and introduces a closed-form, instance-adaptive calibration of the relevance-redundancy trade-off parameter to eliminate manual tuning and adapt to candidate-pool statistics and budget limits. We further provide a theoretical analysis showing that the proposed objective exhibits epsilon-approximate submodularity under practical embedding similarity conditions, yielding near-optimality guarantees for greedy selection. Experiments on open-domain question answering (Natural Questions) and a high-redundancy biomedical (drug) corpus demonstrate consistent improvements in redundancy control and context quality, translating to better end-to-end answer quality and robustness across settings.
