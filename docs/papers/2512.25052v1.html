AdaGReS:Adaptive Greedy Context Selection via Redundancy-Aware Scoring for Token-Budgeted RAG

Summary

AdaGReS is a novel framework for token-budgeted Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) that addresses the issue of redundant context selection by optimizing a set-level objective combining query relevance and intra-set redundancy penalties. It employs greedy selection with an adaptively calibrated trade-off parameter, theoretically guaranteeing near-optimal context selection, which translates to improved answer quality and robustness across various domains, including biomedical. This framework significantly enhances the efficiency and reliability of RAG by ensuring high-quality, non-redundant contextual information.

Key Points

  • **Redundancy Problem Solved**: Addresses the critical issue in RAG where standard top-k retrieval often returns redundant or near-duplicate chunks, wasting token budget and degrading generation quality.
  • **Set-Level Optimization**: Introduces a set-level objective function that explicitly balances query-chunk relevance with an intra-set redundancy penalty, moving beyond individual chunk scoring.
  • **Adaptive Greedy Selection**: Utilizes a greedy selection approach based on marginal gains from the novel objective, ensuring context selection is optimal given the budget constraint.
  • **Instance-Adaptive Calibration**: Implements a closed-form, instance-adaptive calibration mechanism for the relevance-redundancy trade-off parameter, eliminating the need for manual tuning and adapting to candidate-pool statistics and budget limits.
  • **Theoretical Guarantees**: Provides theoretical analysis demonstrating that the proposed objective exhibits epsilon-approximate submodularity under practical conditions, offering near-optimality guarantees for the greedy selection.
  • **Improved Context Quality & Robustness**: Experiments show consistent improvements in redundancy control and overall context quality, leading to better end-to-end answer quality and increased robustness of the RAG system.
  • **Applicability to Biomedical Data**: Demonstrated effectiveness on a high-redundancy biomedical (drug) corpus, highlighting its relevance for specialized medical information retrieval.

Methodology

AdaGReS operates by performing a greedy selection of context chunks under a token budget. This selection is guided by a novel set-level objective function that simultaneously considers the relevance of each candidate chunk to the query and penalizes redundancy within the already selected context set. A key methodological contribution is a closed-form, instance-adaptive calibration method for the relevance-redundancy trade-off parameter, which automatically adjusts based on the statistical properties of the candidate pool and the token budget, removing the need for manual hyperparameter tuning. The method's effectiveness is further supported by a theoretical analysis demonstrating epsilon-approximate submodularity of its objective, ensuring near-optimal performance of the greedy selection.

This enhanced context quality directly translates to superior end-to-end answer quality and increased robustness of the RAG system. These improvements were consistently observed across diverse datasets, including a high-redundancy biomedical (drug) corpus, underscoring its broad applicability and effectiveness in specialized domains.

Abstract

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) is highly sensitive to the quality of selected context, yet standard top-k retrieval often returns redundant or near-duplicate chunks that waste token budget and degrade downstream generation. We present AdaGReS, a redundancy-aware context selection framework for token-budgeted RAG that optimizes a set-level objective combining query-chunk relevance and intra-set redundancy penalties. AdaGReS performs greedy selection under a token-budget constraint using marginal gains derived from the objective, and introduces a closed-form, instance-adaptive calibration of the relevance-redundancy trade-off parameter to eliminate manual tuning and adapt to candidate-pool statistics and budget limits. We further provide a theoretical analysis showing that the proposed objective exhibits epsilon-approximate submodularity under practical embedding similarity conditions, yielding near-optimality guarantees for greedy selection. Experiments on open-domain question answering (Natural Questions) and a high-redundancy biomedical (drug) corpus demonstrate consistent improvements in redundancy control and context quality, translating to better end-to-end answer quality and robustness across settings.
