Learning from Single Timestamps: Complexity Estimation in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy

arXiv ID: 2511.04525v1
Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Dimitrios Anastasiou, Santiago Barbarisi, Lucy Culshaw, Jayna Patel, Evangelos B. Mazomenos, Imanol Luengo, Danail Stoyanov
Categories: cs.CV

Summary

This paper introduces STC-Net, a novel framework for automated surgical complexity estimation in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC) using the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS), designed to analyze full surgical videos with weak temporal supervision. STC-Net achieves 62.11% accuracy and 61.42% F1-score on a dataset of 1,859 LC videos, significantly outperforming non-localized baselines by over 10%. This scalable approach holds promise for enhancing post-operative analysis and surgical training by providing objective complexity assessments.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated assessment of surgical complexity in LC is paramount for improving patient safety by predicting surgical difficulty, managing operative risks, and optimizing resource allocation, directly contributing to better surgical outcomes and reduced postoperative complications.

AI Health Application

The AI application is the automated estimation of surgical complexity using computer vision and machine learning (STC-Net framework) applied to full surgical videos. This system aims to assist in post-operative analysis (e.g., quality control, research) and enhance surgical training by objectively quantifying the difficulty of a surgical case based on inflammation severity (via the Parkland Grading Scale).

Key Points

  • **Problem Addressed**: The paper tackles the challenge of accurately assessing surgical complexity in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC) due to severe inflammation, which is linked to longer operative times and increased complications, particularly when using the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS) in full, uncurated surgical videos.
  • **Novel Framework (STC-Net)**: The authors introduce STC-Net, a Single Timestamp-based Complexity estimation network, which is designed to directly process complete LC videos under weak temporal supervision, unlike prior methods limited to static images or manually trimmed clips.
  • **Methodological Innovation**: STC-Net employs a unique architecture that jointly performs temporal localization of relevant surgical phases and PGS-based complexity grading through integrated localization, window proposal, and grading modules.
  • **Advanced Loss Formulation**: A key innovation is the introduction of a novel loss function that combines both hard and soft localization objectives, along with background-aware grading supervision, to improve the model's ability to identify and classify specific complex segments within the video.
  • **Performance Superiority**: Evaluated on a private dataset of 1,859 LC videos, STC-Net achieved a classification accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42% for PGS-based complexity grading.
  • **Significant Improvement Over Baselines**: The proposed STC-Net demonstrated a substantial performance gain, outperforming non-localized baseline methods by over 10% in both accuracy and F1-score, thereby highlighting the efficacy of its weak temporal supervision approach.
  • **Clinical Utility**: The framework is positioned as a scalable and effective solution for automated post-operative analysis and surgical training in LC, facilitating better risk assessment and objective skill development.

Methodology

STC-Net is a novel deep learning framework that directly analyzes full Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC) videos to estimate surgical complexity using the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS). It operates under weak temporal supervision, meaning it does not require precise frame-level annotations. The architecture includes modules for temporal localization of relevant events, proposing temporal windows, and then grading the complexity within these windows. A novel loss function integrates hard and soft localization objectives with background-aware grading supervision to enhance its performance.

Key Findings

STC-Net achieved an accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42% for surgical complexity estimation based on the Parkland Grading Scale when evaluated on a private dataset of 1,859 LC videos. This performance represents a significant improvement, outperforming non-localized baseline methods by over 10% in both accuracy and F1-score, confirming the effectiveness of its novel approach with weak temporal supervision.

Clinical Impact

This automated system offers a scalable and objective method for assessing surgical complexity in LC, which can significantly aid surgeons in pre-operative risk stratification, improved patient counseling, and resource planning. Post-operatively, it provides a valuable tool for quality assurance, auditing surgical outcomes, and offering standardized, objective feedback to surgical trainees, ultimately enhancing surgical education and patient safety.

Limitations

The evaluation was conducted on a private dataset of 1,859 LC videos. While substantial, the 'private' nature of the dataset might imply a lack of diversity or potential institutional bias, which could limit the immediate generalizability of the findings to other surgical centers or broader patient populations. No other specific limitations are explicitly mentioned in the abstract.

Future Directions

The authors suggest that STC-Net is 'promising for post-operative analysis and surgical training.' This implies future research and development could focus on integrating this automated complexity estimation into clinical workflows for continuous performance monitoring, personalized surgical skill assessment, and refining pre-operative risk prediction models based on inflammation severity.

Abstract

Purpose: Accurate assessment of surgical complexity is essential in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC), where severe inflammation is associated with longer operative times and increased risk of postoperative complications. The Parkland Grading Scale (PGS) provides a clinically validated framework for stratifying inflammation severity; however, its automation in surgical videos remains largely unexplored, particularly in realistic scenarios where complete videos must be analyzed without prior manual curation. Methods: In this work, we introduce STC-Net, a novel framework for SingleTimestamp-based Complexity estimation in LC via the PGS, designed to operate under weak temporal supervision. Unlike prior methods limited to static images or manually trimmed clips, STC-Net operates directly on full videos. It jointly performs temporal localization and grading through a localization, window proposal, and grading module. We introduce a novel loss formulation combining hard and soft localization objectives and background-aware grading supervision. Results: Evaluated on a private dataset of 1,859 LC videos, STC-Net achieves an accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42%, outperforming non-localized baselines by over 10% in both metrics and highlighting the effectiveness of weak supervision for surgical complexity assessment. Conclusion: STC-Net demonstrates a scalable and effective approach for automated PGS-based surgical complexity estimation from full LC videos, making it promising for post-operative analysis and surgical training.
