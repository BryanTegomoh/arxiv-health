Learning from Single Timestamps: Complexity Estimation in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy

arXiv ID: 2511.04525v1
Published: 2025-11-06
Authors: Dimitrios Anastasiou, Santiago Barbarisi, Lucy Culshaw, Jayna Patel, Evangelos B. Mazomenos, Imanol Luengo, Danail Stoyanov
Categories: cs.CV
Relevance Score: 1.00 / 1.00

Summary

STC-Net is a novel AI framework that automates surgical complexity estimation in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy using the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS) directly from full surgical videos. It employs weak temporal supervision to jointly localize and grade inflammation severity, achieving over 62% accuracy and F1-score and outperforming non-localized baselines by over 10% on a large dataset. This approach offers a scalable solution for post-operative analysis and surgical training.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated assessment of surgical complexity in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy is crucial for predicting operative times, reducing the risk of postoperative complications, and enhancing overall patient safety through improved surgical planning and risk stratification.

AI Health Application

An AI-powered computer vision system (STC-Net) that automatically estimates surgical complexity in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy from full surgical videos, using the Parkland Grading Scale. This AI application is designed to aid in post-operative analysis and enhance surgical training by providing objective, automated assessment of inflammation severity.

Key Points

  • Introduces STC-Net, a novel framework for automated surgical complexity estimation in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC) based on the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS).
  • Designed to operate directly on full, uncurated surgical videos, distinguishing it from prior methods limited to static images or manually trimmed clips.
  • Utilizes weak temporal supervision, performing joint temporal localization and grading through a dedicated localization, window proposal, and grading module.
  • Incorporates a novel loss formulation that combines hard and soft localization objectives with background-aware grading supervision for robust learning.
  • Evaluated on a private dataset comprising 1,859 LC videos, a significant scale for surgical video analysis.
  • Achieved an accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42% for PGS-based complexity estimation.
  • Demonstrated substantial outperformance of over 10% in both accuracy and F1-score compared to non-localized baseline methods, highlighting the effectiveness of its localization strategy.

Methodology

The study developed STC-Net, a deep learning framework designed to process full surgical videos for complexity estimation via the Parkland Grading Scale. It features a multi-module architecture that jointly performs temporal localization of relevant events, proposes precise temporal windows, and applies the complexity grade. A novel loss function was introduced, combining hard and soft localization objectives with background-aware grading supervision, enabling the system to learn effectively from weak temporal annotations.

Key Findings

STC-Net achieved a classification accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42% for PGS-based complexity estimation on a dataset of 1,859 LC videos. This performance represents a significant improvement, outperforming non-localized baseline methods by over 10% in both accuracy and F1-score, thereby validating the efficacy of weak temporal supervision and the integrated localization-grading approach for surgical complexity assessment from full videos.

Clinical Impact

This automated system offers a scalable and objective tool for post-operative analysis of surgical videos, which can aid in standardizing complexity assessment, evaluating surgeon performance, and identifying areas for skill development. It holds significant promise for enhancing surgical training programs by providing quantitative feedback and potentially informing pre-operative planning or intraoperative decision-making by offering an objective measure of inflammation severity.

Limitations

The evaluation was conducted on a private dataset, which may limit the direct generalizability of the reported performance to diverse clinical settings or other patient populations. While weak temporal supervision is a methodological strength for scalability, it intrinsically provides less detailed ground truth than strong supervision, which might set an upper bound on achievable accuracy.

Future Directions

The STC-Net framework is promising for integration into existing post-operative surgical analysis pipelines and could serve as a valuable objective assessment tool within advanced surgical training programs. Future research could focus on validating the system on larger, multi-center datasets to enhance generalizability and exploring its potential for real-time intraoperative applications.

Abstract

Purpose: Accurate assessment of surgical complexity is essential in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC), where severe inflammation is associated with longer operative times and increased risk of postoperative complications. The Parkland Grading Scale (PGS) provides a clinically validated framework for stratifying inflammation severity; however, its automation in surgical videos remains largely unexplored, particularly in realistic scenarios where complete videos must be analyzed without prior manual curation. Methods: In this work, we introduce STC-Net, a novel framework for SingleTimestamp-based Complexity estimation in LC via the PGS, designed to operate under weak temporal supervision. Unlike prior methods limited to static images or manually trimmed clips, STC-Net operates directly on full videos. It jointly performs temporal localization and grading through a localization, window proposal, and grading module. We introduce a novel loss formulation combining hard and soft localization objectives and background-aware grading supervision. Results: Evaluated on a private dataset of 1,859 LC videos, STC-Net achieves an accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42%, outperforming non-localized baselines by over 10% in both metrics and highlighting the effectiveness of weak supervision for surgical complexity assessment. Conclusion: STC-Net demonstrates a scalable and effective approach for automated PGS-based surgical complexity estimation from full LC videos, making it promising for post-operative analysis and surgical training.
