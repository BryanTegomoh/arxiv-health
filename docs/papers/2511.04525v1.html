Learning from Single Timestamps: Complexity Estimation in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy

Summary

This paper introduces STC-Net, a novel deep learning framework for automated surgical complexity estimation in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC) using the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS). Operating directly on full surgical videos with weak temporal supervision, STC-Net jointly performs temporal localization and grading, achieving 62.11% accuracy and 61.42% F1-score, significantly outperforming non-localized baselines.

Medical Relevance

Accurate, automated assessment of surgical complexity in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy is crucial for pre-operative planning, risk stratification, and optimizing surgical outcomes by reducing operative times and mitigating post-operative complications related to severe inflammation.

AI Health Application

The paper describes the development of STC-Net, an AI framework for automated, real-time or post-operative estimation of surgical complexity from full laparoscopic videos. This AI application aims to improve surgical planning, provide objective risk stratification, enhance surgical training by offering automated feedback and assessment, and assist in post-operative analysis for quality improvement and research.

Key Points

  • Addresses the challenge of automating surgical complexity assessment in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC) using the clinically validated Parkland Grading Scale (PGS) from full, uncurated surgical videos.
  • Introduces STC-Net (SingleTimestamp-based Complexity estimation), a novel framework designed to operate under weak temporal supervision, distinguishing it from prior methods limited to static images or manually trimmed clips.
  • STC-Net jointly performs temporal localization of relevant video segments and subsequent PGS-based inflammation severity grading through a modular architecture: localization, window proposal, and grading.
  • A novel loss formulation is employed, combining hard and soft localization objectives with background-aware grading supervision to enhance model learning.
  • Evaluated on a private dataset of 1,859 LC videos, STC-Net achieved an accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42% for complexity estimation.
  • The framework demonstrated superior performance, outperforming non-localized baseline methods by over 10% in both accuracy and F1-score, thereby validating the effectiveness of weak supervision and temporal localization.
  • The findings suggest STC-Net is a scalable and effective approach for automated complexity estimation, holding promise for enhancing post-operative analysis and surgical training.

Methodology

STC-Net is a novel deep learning framework designed for SingleTimestamp-based Complexity estimation in LC via the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS) under weak temporal supervision. It processes full surgical videos by jointly performing temporal localization of relevant segments and complexity grading. The architecture consists of a localization module, a window proposal module, and a grading module. A novel loss formulation integrates hard and soft localization objectives with background-aware grading supervision. The system was developed and evaluated using a private dataset comprising 1,859 LC surgical videos.

Key Findings

STC-Net achieved an accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42% on the 1,859 LC video dataset for PGS-based complexity estimation. This performance significantly surpassed non-localized baseline methods by over 10% in both accuracy and F1-score, demonstrating the efficacy of its weak supervision and temporal localization approach for surgical complexity assessment.

Clinical Impact

This automated system has the potential to significantly enhance clinical practice by providing an objective and scalable method for assessing inflammation severity in LC. It can aid in pre-operative risk assessment, improve surgical planning, optimize resource allocation, and support surgical training by identifying challenging cases. This ultimately contributes to reducing operative times and decreasing the incidence of postoperative complications for patients.

Abstract

Purpose: Accurate assessment of surgical complexity is essential in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC), where severe inflammation is associated with longer operative times and increased risk of postoperative complications. The Parkland Grading Scale (PGS) provides a clinically validated framework for stratifying inflammation severity; however, its automation in surgical videos remains largely unexplored, particularly in realistic scenarios where complete videos must be analyzed without prior manual curation. Methods: In this work, we introduce STC-Net, a novel framework for SingleTimestamp-based Complexity estimation in LC via the PGS, designed to operate under weak temporal supervision. Unlike prior methods limited to static images or manually trimmed clips, STC-Net operates directly on full videos. It jointly performs temporal localization and grading through a localization, window proposal, and grading module. We introduce a novel loss formulation combining hard and soft localization objectives and background-aware grading supervision. Results: Evaluated on a private dataset of 1,859 LC videos, STC-Net achieves an accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42%, outperforming non-localized baselines by over 10% in both metrics and highlighting the effectiveness of weak supervision for surgical complexity assessment. Conclusion: STC-Net demonstrates a scalable and effective approach for automated PGS-based surgical complexity estimation from full LC videos, making it promising for post-operative analysis and surgical training.
