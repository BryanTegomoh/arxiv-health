Learning from Single Timestamps: Complexity Estimation in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy

arXiv ID: 2511.04525v1

Summary

This paper introduces STC-Net, a novel deep learning framework for automated, single-timestamp-based complexity estimation in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC) using the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS). Operating directly on full surgical videos with weak temporal supervision, STC-Net jointly performs temporal localization and grading, achieving 62.11% accuracy and 61.42% F1-score and outperforming non-localized baselines by over 10%.

Medical Relevance

Accurate and automated assessment of surgical complexity in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy directly impacts patient safety by identifying cases prone to longer operative times and increased postoperative complications. This tool can aid in surgical planning, risk stratification, and provide objective feedback for surgeon training.

AI Health Application

The STC-Net framework is an AI application using computer vision to automatically assess the complexity of Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC) procedures from surgical videos, based on the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS). This AI can be applied in healthcare for post-operative analysis to better understand surgical outcomes and risks, and for surgical training to educate and evaluate surgeons, potentially leading to improved surgical quality and reduced complications for patients.

Key Points

  • The research addresses the critical need for automated assessment of surgical complexity in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC) using the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS), which correlates with operative times and complications.
  • STC-Net is introduced as a novel framework for SingleTimestamp-based Complexity estimation, designed to operate on full surgical videos rather than pre-trimmed clips or static images.
  • The model employs weak temporal supervision, combining temporal localization with complexity grading through dedicated localization, window proposal, and grading modules.
  • A new loss formulation is integrated, featuring both hard and soft localization objectives, alongside background-aware grading supervision to enhance model performance.
  • Evaluated on a substantial private dataset of 1,859 LC videos, STC-Net achieved a classification accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42%.
  • STC-Net significantly outperforms non-localized baseline methods by more than 10% in both accuracy and F1-score, underscoring the effectiveness of its weak supervision and localization strategy.
  • The framework demonstrates a scalable and effective approach for automated PGS-based surgical complexity estimation, with promising applications in post-operative analysis and surgical training.

Methodology

The study introduces STC-Net, a deep learning framework that operates on full surgical videos under weak temporal supervision. It integrates three modules: a localization module to identify relevant surgical phases, a window proposal module to generate candidate temporal segments, and a grading module to estimate surgical complexity based on the Parkland Grading Scale (PGS). A novel loss function is employed, combining hard and soft localization objectives with background-aware grading supervision to improve temporal precision and classification accuracy.

Key Findings

STC-Net achieved an accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42% on a private dataset of 1,859 Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy videos. These results represent a significant improvement, outperforming non-localized baselines by over 10% in both metrics, thereby validating the efficacy of its weak supervision and localization approach for surgical complexity assessment.

Clinical Impact

This automated system offers a scalable solution for objective surgical complexity assessment, which can be invaluable for post-operative analysis, informing risk stratification for future patient management, and quality assurance. Furthermore, it provides an objective tool for surgical training, enabling trainees to receive consistent feedback on case difficulty and performance, ultimately enhancing skill development and patient outcomes.

Abstract

Purpose: Accurate assessment of surgical complexity is essential in Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (LC), where severe inflammation is associated with longer operative times and increased risk of postoperative complications. The Parkland Grading Scale (PGS) provides a clinically validated framework for stratifying inflammation severity; however, its automation in surgical videos remains largely unexplored, particularly in realistic scenarios where complete videos must be analyzed without prior manual curation. Methods: In this work, we introduce STC-Net, a novel framework for SingleTimestamp-based Complexity estimation in LC via the PGS, designed to operate under weak temporal supervision. Unlike prior methods limited to static images or manually trimmed clips, STC-Net operates directly on full videos. It jointly performs temporal localization and grading through a localization, window proposal, and grading module. We introduce a novel loss formulation combining hard and soft localization objectives and background-aware grading supervision. Results: Evaluated on a private dataset of 1,859 LC videos, STC-Net achieves an accuracy of 62.11% and an F1-score of 61.42%, outperforming non-localized baselines by over 10% in both metrics and highlighting the effectiveness of weak supervision for surgical complexity assessment. Conclusion: STC-Net demonstrates a scalable and effective approach for automated PGS-based surgical complexity estimation from full LC videos, making it promising for post-operative analysis and surgical training.
