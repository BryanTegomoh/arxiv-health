Global stability of epidemic models with imperfect vaccination and quarantine on scale-free networks

arXiv ID: 1806.03292v1
Published: 2018-06-07
Authors: Shanshan Chen, Michael Small, Xinchu Fu
Categories: q-bio.PE, math.CA, nlin.AO
Relevance Score: 0.95 / 1.00
View on arXiv Download PDF

Summary

This paper introduces and rigorously analyzes novel epidemic models, SVIQR (with permanent immunity) and SVIQS (without permanent immunity), incorporating variable population size, degree-related imperfect vaccination, and quarantine strategies on scale-free networks. The research details the global stability properties of disease-free and endemic equilibria, basic reproduction number, and bifurcation behaviors for both models, providing critical insights for public health planning.

Medical Relevance

This research directly contributes to public health by providing advanced mathematical frameworks to evaluate the long-term effectiveness of critical interventions like vaccination and quarantine in complex social networks. Understanding the stability and dynamics of these models helps policymakers design more targeted and effective disease control strategies, especially for infectious diseases with varying immunity profiles.

AI Health Application

The paper itself does not utilize AI. It develops mathematical models and uses traditional simulation methods. However, the insights derived from these epidemic models (e.g., disease dynamics, effectiveness of interventions) could serve as foundational knowledge or data for AI applications in health, such as AI-driven outbreak prediction systems, optimized resource allocation for vaccination campaigns, or AI models for evaluating the impact of different public health policies.

Key Points

  • Development of two distinct compartmental epidemic models: SVIQR (Susceptible-Vaccinated-Infected-Quarantined-Recovered) for diseases with permanent immunity, and SVIQS (Susceptible-Vaccinated-Infected-Quarantined-Susceptible) for diseases without permanent immunity.
  • Models incorporate key epidemiological factors including variable population size, imperfect vaccination effectiveness, degree-related vaccination strategy (targeting individuals based on network connections), and quarantine measures.
  • The underlying population structure is a scale-free network, reflecting real-world heterogeneous contact patterns, thereby enhancing model realism.
  • Mathematical analysis determined the basic reproduction number (R0) and investigated the global stability of both disease-free and endemic equilibria for both SVIQR and SVIQS models.
  • For the SVIQR model, a forward bifurcation is observed, and both the disease-free equilibrium (DFE) and a unique endemic equilibrium (EE) are proven to be globally asymptotically stable using Lyapunov functions.
  • For the SVIQS model, conditions for the occurrence of multiple endemic equilibria are derived, and it is shown that backward bifurcation cannot occur under certain specified conditions. Global asymptotic stability of the DFE and disease persistence are also proved, with the EE being globally attractive via monotone iterative techniques.
  • The analytical findings from the deterministic mean-field approach are quantitatively supported by stochastic network simulations, validating the theoretical framework.

Methodology

The study employs advanced mathematical methods including the formulation of compartmental epidemic models (SVIQR, SVIQS) on scale-free networks. Analysis includes calculation of the basic reproduction number (R0), identification of disease-free and endemic equilibria, and detailed bifurcation analysis. Global asymptotic stability of equilibria is rigorously proved using Lyapunov functions and monotone iterative techniques. The theoretical findings are then validated against stochastic network simulations.

Key Findings

The SVIQR model (with permanent immunity) exhibits a forward bifurcation where the disease-free and a unique endemic equilibrium are globally asymptotically stable. The SVIQS model (without permanent immunity) shows the possibility of multiple endemic equilibria under specific conditions and does not undergo a backward bifurcation under certain criteria. For SVIQS, the disease-free equilibrium is globally asymptotically stable, disease persistence is demonstrated, and the endemic equilibrium is globally attractive. Importantly, the deterministic mean-field results were consistently corroborated by stochastic network simulations.

Clinical Impact

This research offers a robust theoretical basis for optimizing public health interventions. It enables the prediction of disease outcomes (elimination vs. persistence, potential for multiple endemic states) under varying vaccination coverages and efficacies, and quarantine adherence, particularly considering the heterogeneous contact patterns found in real populations. This can guide resource allocation, inform vaccine rollout strategies (e.g., targeting high-degree individuals), and enhance preparedness for future outbreaks by providing a quantitative understanding of intervention effectiveness.

Abstract

Public health services are constantly searching for new ways to reduce the spread of infectious diseases, such as public vaccination of asymptomatic individuals, quarantine (isolation) and treatment of symptomatic individuals. Epidemic models have a long history of assisting in public health planning and policy making. In this paper, we introduce epidemic models including variable population size, degree-related imperfect vaccination and quarantine on scale-free networks. More specifically, the models are formulated both on the population with and without permanent natural immunity to infection, which corresponds respectively to the susceptible-vaccinated-infected-quarantined-recovered (SVIQR) model and the susceptible-vaccinated-infected-quarantined (SVIQS) model. We develop different mathematical methods and techniques to study the dynamics of two models, including the basic reproduction number, the global stability of disease-free and endemic equilibria. For the SVIQR model, we show that the system exhibits a forward bifurcation. Meanwhile, the disease-free and unique endemic equilibria are shown to be globally asymptotically stable by constructing suitable Lyapunov functions. For the SVIQS model, conditions ensuring the occurrence of multiple endemic equilibria are derived. Under certain conditions, this system cannot undergo a backward bifurcation. The global asymptotical stability of disease-free equilibrium, and the persistence of the disease are proved. The endemic equilibrium is shown to be globally attractive by using monotone iterative technique. Finally, stochastic network simulations yield quantitative agreement with the deterministic mean-field approach.
