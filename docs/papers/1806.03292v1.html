Global stability of epidemic models with imperfect vaccination and quarantine on scale-free networks

Published: 2018-06-07
Authors: Shanshan Chen, Michael Small, Xinchu Fu

Summary

This paper introduces and analyzes novel epidemic models, SVIQR (with permanent immunity) and SVIQS (without permanent immunity), incorporating variable population size, degree-related imperfect vaccination, and quarantine on scale-free networks. It mathematically investigates the global stability of disease-free and endemic equilibria, revealing distinct dynamical behaviors and conditions for disease persistence under various public health interventions. The findings provide a robust framework for understanding and predicting disease spread on complex networks.

Medical Relevance

This research provides advanced epidemic modeling tools that incorporate realistic complexities like network heterogeneity, imperfect vaccination, and quarantine, which are crucial for public health services to design more effective and adaptive strategies for infectious disease control and outbreak management.

Key Points

  • Develops two new epidemic models, SVIQR (Susceptible-Vaccinated-Infected-Quarantined-Recovered) and SVIQS (Susceptible-Vaccinated-Infected-Quarantined-Susceptible), on scale-free networks with variable population size.
  • Models incorporate key public health interventions: degree-related imperfect vaccination (vaccination efficacy may depend on individual's network degree) and quarantine of symptomatic individuals.
  • Employs mathematical methods to study model dynamics, including the calculation of the basic reproduction number (R0) and analysis of the global stability of disease-free and endemic equilibria.
  • For the SVIQR model, it demonstrates a forward bifurcation and proves the global asymptotic stability of both the disease-free and a unique endemic equilibrium using Lyapunov functions.
  • For the SVIQS model, conditions are derived for the occurrence of multiple endemic equilibria, and it's shown that the system does not undergo a backward bifurcation under certain conditions.
  • Proves global asymptotic stability of the disease-free equilibrium and persistence of the disease for the SVIQS model, with the endemic equilibrium shown to be globally attractive via the monotone iterative technique.
  • Validates the deterministic mean-field approach through stochastic network simulations, demonstrating quantitative agreement between the two methods.

Methodology

The authors formulated deterministic mean-field epidemic models (SVIQR and SVIQS) on scale-free networks. They analyzed the dynamics using mathematical techniques such as calculating the basic reproduction number (R0), performing global stability analysis of disease-free and endemic equilibria by constructing Lyapunov functions, and utilizing the monotone iterative technique. The deterministic mean-field results were subsequently validated against stochastic network simulations.

Key Findings

The SVIQR model exhibits a forward bifurcation, with both its disease-free and unique endemic equilibria demonstrating global asymptotic stability. For the SVIQS model, conditions are identified for the existence of multiple endemic equilibria, and it's shown that a backward bifurcation does not occur under certain conditions. The disease-free equilibrium of the SVIQS model is globally asymptotically stable, disease persistence is proved, and the endemic equilibrium is globally attractive. Importantly, stochastic network simulations yielded quantitative agreement with the deterministic mean-field approach.

Clinical Impact

The findings can directly inform public health policy by elucidating the impact of various intervention strategies (imperfect vaccination, quarantine) on disease dynamics within realistically structured populations. Understanding conditions for multiple endemic equilibria or absence of backward bifurcation, especially concerning permanent immunity, allows policymakers to anticipate long-term disease prevalence and optimize resource allocation for sustained control or elimination efforts.

Abstract

Public health services are constantly searching for new ways to reduce the spread of infectious diseases, such as public vaccination of asymptomatic individuals, quarantine (isolation) and treatment of symptomatic individuals. Epidemic models have a long history of assisting in public health planning and policy making. In this paper, we introduce epidemic models including variable population size, degree-related imperfect vaccination and quarantine on scale-free networks. More specifically, the models are formulated both on the population with and without permanent natural immunity to infection, which corresponds respectively to the susceptible-vaccinated-infected-quarantined-recovered (SVIQR) model and the susceptible-vaccinated-infected-quarantined (SVIQS) model. We develop different mathematical methods and techniques to study the dynamics of two models, including the basic reproduction number, the global stability of disease-free and endemic equilibria. For the SVIQR model, we show that the system exhibits a forward bifurcation. Meanwhile, the disease-free and unique endemic equilibria are shown to be globally asymptotically stable by constructing suitable Lyapunov functions. For the SVIQS model, conditions ensuring the occurrence of multiple endemic equilibria are derived. Under certain conditions, this system cannot undergo a backward bifurcation. The global asymptotical stability of disease-free equilibrium, and the persistence of the disease are proved. The endemic equilibrium is shown to be globally attractive by using monotone iterative technique. Finally, stochastic network simulations yield quantitative agreement with the deterministic mean-field approach.
